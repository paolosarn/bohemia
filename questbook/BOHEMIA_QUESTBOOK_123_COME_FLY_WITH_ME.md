# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 123: TELL HIM HE'S NOT A MONSTER AND HE'LL BLOW UP THE ROCKET (Come Fly With Me, Fallout: New Vegas)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the side quest where a cult of irradiated corpses is trying to leave
Earth in pre-war rockets, the engineer who built the rockets is a bald man who believes he's a ghoul, the
prophet has been letting him believe it because he needs the labor, and the single kindest sentence you can
say in the whole quest is the one that turns him into a murderer. Complete event flow, the demons, the
prisoners, the sugar bombs, the navigation console, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY
relevant to our faction web, our conscience system, our PACIFIST LAW, our [READ], our companions, +
despair-with-a-thread-of-hope. Obsidian. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written
here.

Quest: "Come Fly With Me," Fallout: New Vegas (2010). A critic's verdict: "**IT IS THE CHARACTERS THAT SHINE
IN COME FLY WITH ME, BUT THE BOOM OF THE ROCKETS PROVIDES A FITTING AND MEMORABLE END TO **WHAT I THINK IS
THE BEST HOUR AND A HALF IN NEW VEGAS.**"

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE QUEST STARTS AS A LEAD ON SOMEONE ELSE
- "**GO TO NOVAC... BETWEEN THE HOURS OF 9 AM AND 9 PM THERE WILL BE A GUY... HIS NAME'S MANNY, **ASK HIM
  WHERE THE MAN IN THE CHECKERED COAT WENT (BENNY) AND HE WILL TELL YOU TO GO TO A FACILITY INHABITED BY
  GHOULS.**"
- **The main quest sends you here as an errand.** A man with information wants a favor first (cf. our quest
  factory + standing + faction web).
- And his price is a massacre: "**IT SHOULD BE NOTED THAT **YOU CAN KILL ALL THE BRIGHT FOLLOWERS, INCLUDING
  JASON BRIGHT AND CHRIS HAVERSAM, WHICH WILL SATISFY MANNY VARGAS.** IT DOES, HOWEVER, FAIL THE QUEST AND
  COST SOME KARMA.**"
### STAGE 1 — THE PROPHET (and he thinks you were sent)
- "**JASON BRIGHT, A MENTALLY-INTACT GLOWING ONE, INTENDS TO TAKE HIS BROTHERHOOD ON **'THE GREAT JOURNEY' TO
  'THE FAR BEYOND'** BY RIDING THE REPCONN ROCKETS.**"
- "**A GLOWING GHOUL THAT CLAIMS TO KNOW WHAT HIS DEITY IS THINKING AND **BELIEVES YOU ARE A MIRACULOUS GIFT
  SENT TO HELP THE GHOULS FULFIL THEIR DREAMS.**"
- **You are, to him, a piece of evidence that he's right** (cf. our standing + conscience system + faction
  web + AMALGAMATION).
- And the guide's own eye-roll is part of the design: "**TALK TO JASON BRIGHT, **WHO IS NOTHING SHORT OF A
  CULT LEADER**... HE WANTS YOU [TO] KILL THE 'DEMONS' SO THEY CAN CONTINUE ON THEIR 'GREAT JOURNEY'...
  **WHEN YOU GET SICK OF HIS PROSELYTIZING, LOOT THE UPPER LEVEL.**"
### STAGE 2 — HIS ONE MERCY LINE, AND IT'S THE BEST LINE IN THE QUEST
- Jason, about the mindless feral ghouls wandering the facility that are actively trying to kill you:
  "**PLEASE, WANDERER, BEAR IN MIND THAT **EVERY FERAL GHOUL YOU SPARE NOW IS ONE THAT WE CAN SAVE LATER.**"
- And the game honors it mechanically: "**ANY FERAL GHOULS THAT ARE NOT KILLED INSIDE REPCONN **WILL JOIN THE
  'GREAT JOURNEY.'**"
- Punk. **The enemies you didn't shoot get on the rocket.** Restraint isn't scored as karma here — **it's
  scored as passengers** (STARTLINGLY our PACIFIST LAW + combat dial + conscience system + persistent
  consequence + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope; the mercy that shows up in the manifest).
- And the cruel counterpoint, because Obsidian: "**FERAL GHOULS THAT ARE NOT KILLED **OUTSIDE** REPCONN WILL
  STILL BE THERE AND BE HOSTILE.**" The salvation has a property line.
### STAGE 3 — THE DEMONS ARE PEOPLE WITH A DRUG PROBLEM
- "**HE WANTS YOU [TO] CLEAR THE BASEMENT OF THE NIGHTKIN (**OR DEMONS, AS HE CALLS THEM**).**"
- "**THE SUPER MUTANT LEADER, DAVISON, **OFFERS THE PLAYER AN OPPORTUNITY TO HELP HIM FIND A SHIPMENT OF
  STEALTH BOYS FOR HIM AND HIS SUPER MUTANTS. FINDING THE STEALTH BOYS WILL CAUSE DAVISON AND HIS NIGHTKIN TO
  LEAVE THE FACILITY** AND DISAPPEAR FROM THE GAME.**"
- **The demons are squatters who want their supply and will leave if you find it.** The prophet's holy war is
  a logistics dispute (STARTLINGLY our PACIFIST LAW + faction web + logistics + conscience system + MEDICINE
  currency; the enemy faction that leaves if you solve their supply chain).
- And it's fragile: "**DAVISON MAY TURN HOSTILE **DEPENDING ON WHICH NIGHTKIN YOU KILL OR WHETHER OR NOT YOU
  DO SO.**"
### STAGE 4 — AND THERE'S A MAN IN A ROOM WHO WON'T LEAVE
- "**INSIDE THE LARGE ROOM, THERE'S A GHOUL, HARLAND. SPEAK WITH HARLAND AND **ACCEPT HIS QUEST TO FIND HIS
  FRIEND.**" He's fortified: "**A GHOUL NAMED HARLAND HAS PUT UP A DEFENSIVE SPOT HERE AGAINST THE NIGHTKINS...
  **BE WARNED THAT THERE ARE VARIOUS TRIPWIRES LINKED TO SHOTGUNS, AND VARIOUS BEAR TRAPS SPREAD ALL OVER THE

- And the requirement is exact: "**ONE MUST NOT ONLY AGREE TO HELP, **BUT MUST HAVE FOUND HIS COMPANION AS
  WELL TO AVOID HOSTILITIES.**"
- **A man has barricaded himself in a basement with bear traps and he will not move until you find out what
  happened to his friend.** He isn't going to heaven. **He's waiting** (STARTLINGLY our companions +
  entities/spawning + district + PACIFIST LAW + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope).
- "**RETURN TO HARLAN AND GIVE HIM **THE NEWS.**"
### STAGE 5 — THE ROCKETS NEED TWO THINGS AND BOTH ARE SHOPPING
- "**THE ROCKETS REQUIRE TWO THINGS TO FLY: **A SET OF THRUST CONTROL MODULES**... SOLD BY OLD LADY GIBSON
  FOR 500 CAPS (250 WITH SPEECH OR BARTER 50)... **SOME ISOTOPE-239 IGNITING AGENT. DUE TO ITS RAPID DECAY
  THEY CANNOT USE THE LEAKING BARRELS ON THE LAUNCH PAD.**"
- The isotope's source is the best joke in the Mojave: "**THE PLAYER CAN FIND SOME... BY PURCHASING **THE

- "**PROBE CHRIS ABOUT HIS PAST, AND **TRY TO CONVINCE HIM THAT HE ISN'T A GHOUL. THIS REQUIRES A SPEECH

### STAGE 7 — AND THE SABOTAGE IS BOUGHT AT A CONVENIENCE STORE

- **A cult's ascension can be ended with three boxes of breakfast cereal from the gift shop.** And the

### STAGE 9 — THE LAUNCH, AND THE GAME MAKES YOU WATCH
- "**HEAD UP TO THE VIEWING PLATFORM AND COMPLETE THE LAUNCH SEQUENCE.**" A player, on the achievement:
  "**YOU DON'T GET THE ACHIEVEMENT UNTIL AFTER THEY LAUNCH. **IT FORCES YOU TO WATCH.**"

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE QUEST CORRUPTS SAVES: "**MANY CORRUPT SAVES HAVE BEEN REPORTED IN THE REPCONN BUILDING, **AT MANY
  STAGES OF THIS QUEST, AND EVEN HOURS AFTER THE QUEST HAS BEEN COMPLETED.**" The workaround is voodoo:
  "**THE CORRUPT GAME SAVE FILE BUG MAY BE RELATED TO FACTION SCORING. GO AWAY, DO A QUEST THAT ALTERS
  REPUTATION WITH ANOTHER FACTION, AND THEN RETRY.**" LESSON (~3rd confirmation, ours, hard): **the best
  writing in the game is worthless if the faction-scoring system eats the save** — our standing + persistent
  consequence need a regression gate, and our persistence layer needs a corruption test.
- THE KINDNESS IS BEHIND A SPEECH CHECK: "**CONVINCE HIM HE'S NOT A GHOUL BY SUCCEEDING AT A [SPEECH 50]
  CHECK.**" And the console is behind Science 55. LESSON (~10th confirmation): a low-skill run **cannot tell
  a man the truth about himself.** Our [READ] needs a FLOOR.
- YOU CAN MURDER THE ENTIRE CAST AND STILL GET PAID BY SOMEONE: "**YOU CAN KILL ALL THE BRIGHT FOLLOWERS,
  INCLUDING JASON BRIGHT AND CHRIS HAVERSAM, **WHICH WILL SATISFY MANNY VARGAS.**" LESSON (double-edged):
  reactivity this complete is the ceiling AND it means the quest's whole heart is skippable by a rifle.
- THE EXPLOITS ARE ABSURD AND THE WIKI TEACHES THEM: "**ANY GHOULS ONE MAY HAVE KILLED FOR THEIR SPACE SUITS
  **WILL HAVE NEW SPACE SUITS THAT CAN BE SOLD FOR A THOUSAND CAPS**... IN TOTAL, THIS GLITCH CAN NET THE
  PLAYER CHARACTER **APPROXIMATELY FOUR THOUSAND CAPS.**" And: "**ALL FOLLOWERS CAN BE SAFELY KILLED AND
  LOOTED**" after Jason leaves the room. LESSON: if your emotional climax has a farming route, someone
  publishes it.
- THE CUT CONTENT WAS THE BEST IDEA: the crashed rocket "**WAS SUPPOSED TO BE FINDABLE IN THE MOJAVE. **THIS
  WAS CUT DUE TO TIME CONSTRAINTS.**" Also cut: ghoul prisoners you could free, with dialogue still in the
  files — "**A REQUEST THAT DAVISON RELEASE THE GHOUL PRISONERS BECAUSE **'THEY'RE NO THREAT'**.**" LESSON
  (ours): scope kills the payoff first. **Build the consequence before the content** — our FACTORY LAW's job.
- THE LAUNCH MUSIC BREAKS INTO PERMANENT STATIC: "**SOMETIMES AFTER THE ROCKETS LAUNCH, **THE LAUNCH MUSIC
  WILL TURN INTO A CRACKLING NOISE THAT IS IMPOSSIBLE TO GET RID OF.**" LESSON (ours, direct): our music
  repo's most emotional cue must have a **kill path** — SCREECH LAW exists for exactly this.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**JASON BRIGHT** — glowing prophet; wants his flock off Earth and believes you were sent. His one mercy line (stage 2) is the best line in the quest: the request that his people be spared MORE violence. Will never say: doubt about the destination — faith in the DEPARTURE is the whole man. FUNCTION: the prophet whose theology is logistics.

**CHRIS HAVERSAM** — bald, human, believes he's a ghoul; the ONLY engineer the rockets have. The prophet let him believe (needs the labor); the flock lets him believe (kindness or convenience — the quest refuses to rule). Wants: to belong to the people he serves. FUNCTION: the delusion that runs the machine shop — and the quest's live wire: the kindest true sentence ("you're not a ghoul, you're a man") is the one that turns him into a saboteur-murderer (the title beat).

**THE NIGHTKIN DOWNSTAIRS / THE MAN WHO WON'T LEAVE** — the basement's other tenants (stages 3-4): demons who are people with a drug problem, and a holdout in a room. FUNCTION: the dungeon as a rooming house — every "monster" itemizes into a person mid-quest.
