# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 25: FAVOR & WRATH / PLAYING AFTER EVIL WON (Tyranny)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the DUAL-AXIS reputation system (favor AND wrath, not a slider),
the "evil already won" premise, the Fatebinder authority-as-gameplay, the pre-game Conquest choices,
the faction-war structure, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for a
REPUTATION SYSTEM THAT ISN'T A MORALITY SLIDER + AUTHORITY AS THE PLAYER FANTASY + a world SHAPED BY
CHOICES MADE BEFORE THE GAME STARTS. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest here.

Game: Tyranny (Obsidian Entertainment, 2016). The war between good and evil is OVER — evil (the Overlord
Kyros) WON. You are a FATEBINDER, a roaming judge/executioner in Kyros' regime, bringing "the law" to
the last conquered holdouts (the Tiers). One reviewer: more freedom of choice than The Witcher 3.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEAS (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE INVERTED PREMISE: the hero's journey is OVER and the villains WON — you're not a nobody rising to
  power, you're already a HIGH-RANKING officer of a totalitarian empire. The fantasy is AUTHORITY, not
  ascent. You decide how the conquered are ruled: with stability, fear, or your own ambition.
- THE BANALITY OF EVIL: most choices aren't cartoon-evil — they're the mundane administration of an
  unjust order (whose village gets stewardship, who's punished, which law to enforce). "Nothing is black
  or white"; you're stuck in the middle of the everyday machinery of tyranny (like real history).
- CHOICE BEFORE THE GAME (Conquest): before play, a "Conquest" prologue lets you make world-defining
  DECISIONS about how the prior war went — which cities you razed, which factions you favored — and the
  world STARTS already shaped by them. NPCs reference choices you made before the first scene. (A radical
  front-loading of reactivity.)

===============================================================================
## 1. THE FAVOR/WRATH REPUTATION SYSTEM (the signature — the anti-morality-meter)
===============================================================================
- TWO SEPARATE METERS, NOT ONE SLIDER: each faction tracks FAVOR (positive) AND WRATH (negative) as
  INDEPENDENT axes; companions track the same as LOYALTY and FEAR. You don't slide from good to evil —
  you accumulate BOTH toward the same party, independently.
- YOU CAN MAX BOTH AT ONCE: a faction/companion can simultaneously have HIGH favor AND HIGH wrath — they
  can respect and resent you at once. And BOTH unlock abilities: high favor grants some skills, high
  wrath grants OTHERS, and maxing both confers UNIQUE bonuses. (Being intensely FEARED is as mechanically
  powerful as being loved — a genuinely different design from every good/evil bar.)
- FILTERED BY PRINCIPLE, NOT UNIVERSAL: there's no universal "good" — each faction/character judges your
  act by THEIR values. A cruel act earns wrath from one faction and favor from another; a merciful one
  flips it. (Cf. Alpha Protocol's per-person like/respect Q24, but formalized into two always-visible
  meters.)
- COMPANION LOYALTY/FEAR SHAPES ENDINGS: companion reputation affects their reactions, unlocks their
  tier-3 abilities, AND determines their EPILOGUE slides — how their story ends depends on whether they
  served you from love or terror.

===============================================================================
## 2. THE FACTION ARCHITECTURE (choice that reshapes the world)
===============================================================================
- THE TWO PILLARS: the DISFAVORED (Kyros' disciplined, elitist iron legion — "quality," pure-blood
  recruitment, lawful-evil order) vs the SCARLET CHORUS (a chaotic horde that conscripts anyone with a
  pulse — "quantity," second chances, savage). Comically opposite; you're pushed to ally with one and
  earn the enmity of the other (or try to hold the middle as the Fatebinder above both).
- WORLD-ALTERING ALLEGIANCE: side with the Chorus in the prologue and they're GRANTED stewardship of a
  town (grateful); favor shifts who governs where. Your allegiance literally redraws the map's power.
- THE SANCTIONED CIVIL WAR: by Act II the two factions are openly at WAR (with your forces as a possible
  third side), and Kyros DOESN'T intervene; by Act III Kyros makes it official — whichever Archon kills
  or subjugates the others rules the Tiers. The endgame is a power struggle you're central to (you can
  even seek the throne yourself — the Anarchy/own-power path).
- THE HIDDEN MANIPULATOR: the School of Ink and Quill claims to preserve knowledge but is secretly
  playing the realms against each other — a neutral-seeming faction that's the real puppeteer (cf. the
  Network's divide-and-buy; the false-neutral schemer).

===============================================================================
## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- "EVIL" CAN BE OVERSTATED / MUDDY: the marketing "sometimes evil wins" oversells — much of the game is
  grey administration, which some players find less transgressive than promised (the fantasy of BEING
  the villain isn't always delivered; you're often a mediator). LESSON: if you promise a bold premise
  (play the villain / rule the conquered), DELIVER moments that let the player FEEL it, not just admin it.
- LATER ACTS THINNER: widely noted that the ambitious reactivity front-loads; the back half feels more
  rushed/constrained than the rich opening (a budget/scope reality). LESSON: reactivity is expensive;
  budget it so the ENDING pays off the front-loaded promise (don't let Act III collapse the web).
- COMBAT IS SERVICEABLE-NOT-STELLAR: like Alpha Protocol (Q24), the reactivity outshines the combat —
  the systems carry it. (Same lesson: both layers must work.)

===============================================================================
## 4. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. DUAL-AXIS REPUTATION (favor AND wrath, independent): not a good/evil slider — accumulate BOTH toward
    the same party; be respected AND resented at once. The definitive anti-morality-meter design.
W2. FEAR IS AS POWERFUL AS LOVE: high WRATH/FEAR unlocks its OWN abilities + unique bonuses — being
    feared is a mechanically rewarded path, not a penalty. Villainy/hardness is a real build.
W3. FILTERED BY PRINCIPLE, NOT UNIVERSAL: each faction judges acts by THEIR values — one act earns favor
    here and wrath there; there's no global "good." Relativism as a system (cf. Alpha Protocol Q24).
W4. AUTHORITY AS THE FANTASY: you start powerful (a Fatebinder/judge), not a nobody — the fantasy is
    WIELDING authority over the conquered, a fresh inversion of the ascent arc.
W5. THE BANALITY OF EVIL: most choices are the mundane administration of an unjust order (who governs,
    who's punished) — grey, everyday, historically real; more unsettling than cartoon villainy.
W6. CHOICE BEFORE THE GAME (Conquest): a pre-game prologue shapes the STARTING world; NPCs reference
    decisions made before scene one — radical front-loaded reactivity (cf. Alpha Protocol delayed seeds Q24).
W7. ALLEGIANCE REDRAWS THE MAP: siding with a faction grants them territory/stewardship — reputation has
    GEOPOLITICAL, world-state consequences, not just dialogue (cf. NV factions Q02).
W8. THE FORCED-BUT-OPEN ALLIANCE: two opposite factions pull you to pick a side (or hold the middle) —
    mutually-exclusive allegiance with a hard third "stand above both" path (cf. NV's faction endgame).
W9. COMPANIONS SERVE FROM LOVE OR FEAR (and it shows in the epilogue): loyalty/fear shapes their arcs +
    ending slides — HOW you earned their service defines their fate (cf. ME2 loyalty Q06, BG3 Q16).
W10. THE FALSE-NEUTRAL PUPPETEER: a faction claiming neutrality (preserve knowledge) is the real
    manipulator playing sides — the hidden hand (cf. the Network, our Amalgamation's Network handlers).

===============================================================================
## 5. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
Our standing system + faction web + the Network's manipulations map onto Tyranny unusually well — and
the favor/wrath idea is a direct upgrade path for our reputation design.
- W1/W2/W3 (DUAL-AXIS reputation — favor AND wrath, fear as power, principle-filtered): THE big port —
  upgrade Bohemia STANDING from a slider to TWO independent axes per faction/companion (e.g. RESPECT and
  RESENTMENT, or TRUST and FEAR), where BOTH unlock different abilities/options, you can max both, and
  each faction judges acts by THEIR values (the Reds vs the Church vs the Cartel weigh the same deed
  oppositely). Being FEARED is a real, rewarded path — not just the "evil" slider (ties Alpha Protocol
  Q24 like/respect, our Megaton + pacifist laws; a major standing-system upgrade).
- W4/W5 (authority as fantasy + banality of evil): a Bohemia arc where the dynasty holds real AUTHORITY
  over the district and most choices are the MUNDANE administration of scarce power (who eats, who's
  exiled, which law to enforce) — grey, everyday governance, not cartoon villainy (ties our city-builder
  + Q049 verdicts + the mayor-consequence system). The weight is in the ordinary rulings.
- W6 (choice before the game / Conquest): our GENERATIONAL structure IS a "Conquest" engine — the prior
  dynasty's choices shape the heir's STARTING world; NPCs reference deeds from before this dynast was
  born (validated; our fold already does this — lean into NPCs citing pre-birth history).
- W7 (allegiance redraws the map): let Bohemia faction allegiance have GEOPOLITICAL world-state effects
  (side with the Trades -> they're granted a district; favor the Homeless -> they hold the servers'
  ground) — reputation redraws power, not just dialogue (Paolo owns the actual map; Claude does the
  standing->territory plumbing).
- W8 (forced-but-open alliance + stand-above): our faction endgame can pull the dynasty toward one
  bloc with a hard "hold the middle / stand above all" path (our Respect ending is adjacent) — mutually-
  exclusive allegiance with an independent third way.
- W9 (serve from love or fear -> epilogue): our companion/faction EPILOGUE slides (the fold's roll-call,
  Q06/Q050) should reflect HOW their loyalty was earned (love vs fear) — the manner of service defines
  the fate. Bank fear-earned vs trust-earned as distinct epilogue inputs.
- W10 (the false-neutral puppeteer): our NETWORK is exactly this — a faction posing as neutral
  infrastructure/knowledge-keepers while playing the district's factions against each other (validated;
  the School-of-Ink parallel sharpens the Network's "we just keep the lights on" mask over manipulation).
- FLAWS (bank): DELIVER the bold premise (if the dynasty can be a feared tyrant, give beats that FEEL
  it, not just admin); BUDGET reactivity so the ENDGAME pays off (don't let our final generation collapse
  the web Act-III-style); and BOTH layers (combat + city-builder) must stand alone (Alpha Protocol +
  Tyranny agree — reactivity can't rescue weak core loops).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE FATEBINDER (the player)** — wants, per the office: the law delivered. Wants, per the person holding it: whatever the player decides authority is FOR — stability, terror, or a throne. Will trade: verdicts; every ruling spends the office's weight somewhere. Will never say out loud: that being the law and being just are different jobs. FUNCTION: authority as the fantasy (W4). Not a nobody ascending; an officer deciding.

**KYROS (never on screen)** — wants obedience so total that even the civil war is licensed. Will trade: edicts — magic as legislation. FUNCTION: the absent absolute. The Overlord rules the file by not appearing in it; power that never needs the room.

**THE DISFAVORED (Graven Ashe)** — want order, purity, the war fought properly by the properly born. Judge every act by discipline. FUNCTION: lawful-evil's recruiting poster, and one jaw of the vise.

**THE SCARLET CHORUS (the Voices of Nerat)** — want everyone, literally: conscription as digestion; anyone with a pulse can be Chorus, which is mercy and horror in one policy. Judge every act by savage pragmatism. FUNCTION: chaotic-evil's HR department, the other jaw.

**THE COMPANIONS** — want, severally, to serve something that deserves it. Each tracks LOYALTY and FEAR as separate meters, and both work. Will never say out loud: which meter is filling. The epilogue says it for them (W9). FUNCTION: the proof that HOW service is earned is a stat.

**THE SCHOOL OF INK AND QUILL** — wants to be underestimated. Claims to preserve knowledge; actually plays the realms against each other. FUNCTION: the false-neutral puppeteer (W10). Every setting has one; ours is called the Network.

**THE CONQUEST (the pre-game)** — wants answers before scene one: which cities burned, which Archon you favored, what the war cost. FUNCTION: reactivity front-loaded past the title screen (W6). The world is born already remembering you.
