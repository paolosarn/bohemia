**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** The Leviathan — the mid-game capture. Betrayed, imprisoned aboard the enemy's flagship, tortured, the party split and helpless, the player is told by their captor the truth about who they really are: the identity reveal delivered as a weapon, at the player's absolute lowest point.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The party is captured, split, and tortured aboard the enemy flagship, and at the moment of maximum helplessness the captor delivers the truth about who the player really is — not to inform, but to break — and the escape forces the player to keep functioning while their entire identity comes apart.**

Midway through the journey, the party is betrayed and captured aboard the Leviathan, the flagship of the Sith commander Saul Karath. They are separated, imprisoned, and Karath tortures a companion to break the group. The player breaks out and fights toward the bridge — and there, or in the aftermath, the truth lands: the player character is not the ordinary soldier they believe themselves to be. They are Revan, the fallen Sith Lord, mind-wiped and rebuilt by the Jedi, unknowingly leading the enemy to the very superweapon they once sought. The reveal is delivered as a weapon: staged at the lowest point (captured, tortured, betrayed), spoken by the antagonist to shatter the player's sense of self, and immediately followed by a desperate escape that gives no time to process — the player must keep fighting, keep functioning, keep leading the party out, while everything they thought they were dissolves.

The machine is that the WHEN and the WHO of a reveal are as important as the what. The same truth, told safely by a friend, would land as exposition; told by the enemy, at the moment of total helplessness, weaponized to break the player's will, it lands as trauma. And crucially the escape denies catharsis — there is no time to sit with the revelation, no space to grieve the self that just collapsed; the ship is falling apart, the party needs leading, and the player must carry the shattering forward into action. The companions' reactions complete it: Carth, whose family the fallen Revan's war destroyed, must reckon with having trusted Revan; the whole party's trust is retroactively poisoned. The Leviathan is not just the reveal — it is the reveal delivered as an assault and survived under fire.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the truth about who you are hits hardest when your enemy hands it to you at your weakest, and life does not pause to let you absorb it — you carry the collapse forward while the ship is still burning.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### SAUL KARATH — the captor who wields the truth
- **What he wants:** to break the party, and to break the player specifically by telling them what they are.
- **What he'll trade:** the truth, as a weapon — he reveals the player's identity not to inform but to shatter.
- **What he will never say out loud:** that the reveal is his last card; he is losing, and the truth is the cruelest thing he has left.
- **Function:** THE ENEMY WHO DELIVERS THE REVEAL — the identity twist weaponized, spoken to wound.

### THE PLAYER (Revan, unknowing) — the self about to collapse
- **What they want:** to escape, to save the party, to be who they think they are.
- **What they'll trade:** nothing willingly; the truth is taken from them, not given.
- **Function:** THE IDENTITY UNDER ASSAULT — the self shattered at the lowest point and forced to keep functioning.

### CARTH — the companion whose trust is poisoned
- **What he wants:** to trust the player, which the reveal makes agony — the fallen Revan's war killed his family.
- **Function:** THE RELATIONSHIP DETONATED: the reveal retroactively poisons every bond, and Carth carries the sharpest wound.

### THE TORTURED COMPANION — the party broken to set the stage
- Held and hurt by Karath to break the group's will before the reveal.
- **Function:** THE LOWEST POINT, manufactured: the helplessness that makes the truth land as trauma.

### THE ESCAPE — the denial of catharsis
- The ship falling apart, the party needing leading, no time to process.
- **Function:** THE STRUCTURE that weaponizes the reveal: the player must carry the collapse forward under fire.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 1 terminal (the escape survived, the truth carried) — the Leviathan does not branch on OUTCOME; it is a fixed collision whose variable is how the player and party carry the reveal forward, cashing into the endgame's redeem/relapse fork.**

- **THE REVEAL SURVIVED:** the party escapes; the player carries the shattered identity into the rest of the game; the endgame's redeem-or-relapse choice (#32) is now loaded with this collapse. WRITES: the identity known; every prior scene re-read; the bonds poisoned and rebuildable only by conduct.
- **THE COMPANION REACTIONS:** Carth and the party react per their history with Revan — the reveal detonates each bond differently, and rebuilding them becomes the back half's work.
- **THE STRUCTURAL HINGE:** the Leviathan is not a branch, it is a LENS (like #32's office, #34's launch) — the pivot after which the whole game means differently, delivered as an assault and survived under fire.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the quest's only variable is how the collapse is carried, because the truth is fixed — the design's point is that the DELIVERY, not the choice, is the trauma.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE SEEDS ARE UNEVEN, SO THE REVEAL SURPRISES SOME AND BORES OTHERS.** The Leviathan reveal relies on prior seeding (odd competences, NPC reactions, Force aptitude), but the seeding is inconsistent — attentive players see it coming and find the "shattering" flat, while inattentive ones can find it arbitrary. The delivery is masterful but the setup is uneven.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a reveal delivered as trauma must be seeded so it lands as INEVITABLE-in-hindsight for the attentive AND earned for the inattentive — the clues present but not conclusive, per #32's fairness contract — so the delivery's power isn't undercut by a setup that either spoils or blindsides. The seeding is the delivery's foundation.

**F2 — THE ESCAPE'S DENIAL OF CATHARSIS IS ACCIDENTAL, NOT DESIGNED.** The "no time to process" effect — carrying the collapse forward under fire — is the quest's most powerful structural move, but it emerges from generic gameplay pacing (there's an escape sequence because there's always an escape sequence) rather than deliberate design, so the game doesn't fully exploit the trauma-under-fire it stumbles into.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a reveal is followed by forced action that denies catharsis, that denial must be DELIBERATE and shaped — the action's demands echoing the identity collapse, the leadership-under-fire mirroring the theme — so the structure carries the trauma on purpose. An accidental gut-punch is a gift; a designed one is a weapon (the #27 theme-through-mechanic law).

**F3 — THE POISONED TRUST RECOVERS TOO CLEANLY.** The reveal detonates the party's bonds, but the back half lets trust rebuild through fairly standard approval mechanics; Carth's betrayal-wound, so sharp at the Leviathan, heals via ordinary companion-quest beats rather than carrying the specific poison the reveal injected. The detonation lands harder than the aftermath honors.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a bond detonated by a reveal must rebuild in a way that CARRIES the specific wound — the trust reformed as a different, scarred thing, not restored to default — so the reveal's damage is permanent even when the relationship survives. The fold holds the poison; reconciliation is not erasure (the #43 companion-scar law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE REVEAL IS A WEAPON. The truth is delivered by the enemy, at the lowest point, to BREAK the player — not to inform. The same fact told safely would be exposition; told as an assault, it is trauma. The delivery is the design.
W2. THE LOWEST POINT IS MANUFACTURED. Captured, split, tortured, betrayed — the helplessness is built deliberately so the truth lands on a player already broken. The setup is the reveal's amplifier.
W3. THE ESCAPE DENIES CATHARSIS. No time to process; the ship is falling, the party needs leading. The player carries the collapse forward under fire, performing competence through the shattering — the cruelest and truest structure.
W4. THE TRUTH RETROACTIVELY REWRITES THE GAME. Every prior scene re-reads; the player's competences, the NPC reactions, the whole first half reorganizes in hindsight (the #32 lens, delivered as an assault).
W5. THE BONDS DETONATE. Carth, whose family the fallen Revan killed, must reckon with having trusted Revan; the reveal poisons every relationship at once, and the party's faces confirm it before words.
W6. THE ENEMY IS LOSING. Karath plays the reveal as his last card — the truth is the cruelest thing a losing antagonist has left, which makes the delivery feel like desperation, not triumph.
W7. THE PLAYER MUST KEEP FUNCTIONING. The escape forces leadership through the collapse — the player is denied the luxury of falling apart, which is exactly what makes the trauma land as lived, not watched.
W8. THE COMPANIONS' FACES DO THE WORK. The reveal is confirmed by the party's expressions before their dialogue — the detonation is shown in reaction, not just told in exposition.
W9. NO CHOICE, ONLY DELIVERY. The Leviathan doesn't branch; its power is entirely in HOW the fixed truth arrives. The design's thesis: the when and who of a reveal matter more than the what.
W10. IT LOADS THE ENDGAME. The collapse carried out of the Leviathan is cashed at the endgame's redeem-or-relapse fork — the reveal's trauma becomes the weight of the final choice. The delivery pays off hours later.
