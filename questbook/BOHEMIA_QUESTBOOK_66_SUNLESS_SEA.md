# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 66: FUEL, TERROR & A LEGACY TO YOUR HEIR / DREAD-EXPLORATION SURVIVAL (Sunless Sea)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the fuel/food/terror survival triad, terror-as-sanity, the death-
expected legacy-to-heir fold, writing-as-the-star, exploration-as-its-own-reward, the home/homesickness
theme, the story-becomes-a-vending-machine repetition flaw, weak combat, permadeath-vs-Merciful-Mode
accessibility, officers-with-hidden-stories, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's best model for DREAD-
EXPLORATION SURVIVAL where FUEL + SANITY are the real resources + DEATH passes a LEGACY to an heir +
WRITING carries everything. A fold-like inheritance sibling; peerless atmospheric writing. Failbetter Games
(Alexis Kennedy). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Sunless Sea (Failbetter, 2015; iPad/PS4/Switch/Xbox ports). A survival/exploration RPG with roguelike
elements, set in the Fallen London "Neath" (Victorian London dragged underground beside a vast, dark
Unterzee). You captain a tiny steamship into an uncharted, terrifying underground ocean — managing FUEL,
SUPPLIES, and your crew's TERROR — to explore, trade, uncover stories, and (usually) DIE, passing a LEGACY
to your next captain. 350,000+ words; won RPS "Best Words 2014"; a landmark of atmospheric game writing.
Economic-precarity + dread + rebuild-across-heirs = on-theme.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- FUEL + TERROR ARE THE REAL RESOURCES (survival as dread — the defining hook): the tension isn't combat —
  it's FUEL (run out in the dark = death), SUPPLIES (food; starve = death, or cannibalism), and TERROR
  (your crew's sanity — the longer you're in the dark away from light/home, the higher it climbs; too high
  = nightmares, madness, mutiny, self-destruction). Survival is a DREAD-management problem: the dark itself
  is the enemy, and every voyage balances "further out = more discovery + more terror" against the safe pull
  of home (cf. This War of Mine dread-survival Q30, Darkest Dungeon stress Q41, Citizen Sleeper precarity
  Q65; survival AS atmosphere).
- DEATH IS EXPECTED, AND YOU PASS A LEGACY TO YOUR HEIR (the fold — a "chronicle of tragedies"): "Sunless
  Sea is ultimately a chronicle of tragedies." You WILL die (drowned, starved-mad, blasted apart) — and
  success is STACKING A LEGACY across captains: an heir inherits your SEA CHART (the map you explored),
  heirlooms, resources, and advantages, setting sail better-equipped. The unit of progress is the LINE of
  captains, not one life — a fold where each death BUILDS the next run (STARTLINGLY close to our fold;
  cf. Rogue Legacy Q49, Hades Q47, CK3 Q46, our generational fold).
- WRITING IS THE ENTIRE STAR (350k words, atmosphere over everything): the game is driven by STORIES —
  350,000+ words, NO spoken dialogue, every island a "Choose Your Own Adventure" vignette of deftly-written
  dark fantasy. "The best writing in any video game" (RPS Best Words). The prose + a haunting deep-sea
  score carry a simple top-down map — atmosphere + writing ARE the game (cf. Disco prose Q05, Gothic/Qud
  atmosphere Q51/Q56, our writing + music strengths; writing as the load-bearing pillar).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how dread-exploration survival works)
===============================================================================

### EXPLORATION AS ITS OWN REWARD (the map is the content — key craft)
- A hand-illustrated map of an underground ocean; a little steamboat is your avatar. The DRAW is the
  "hypnotic drive to explore — further up and further in": vast dark clumps on the chart invite you to
  penetrate them, each island a NEW story/idea/spin. "Exploration is its own reward"; the best moments are
  unspoilable discoveries. Curiosity vs survival is the central pull (cf. Outer Wilds Q18, Elden Ring Q23,
  our exploration/overmap; discovery as the engine — Paolo owns layout).
### THE HOME / HOMESICKNESS THEME, BUILT INTO THE SYSTEMS (theme through mechanics)
- Kennedy's deliberate design: the theme is HOME + nostalgia/homesickness (saudade, hiraeth) + loneliness,
  surfaced again + again in writing AND systems — the game ENCOURAGES you to return to Fallen London (home
  port) + makes you feel RELIEF at seeing its lights, then the pull to leave again. Terror drops near
  light/home + climbs in the dark. The core loop MECHANICALLY enacts the theme (home vs the lonely dark)
  (cf. Citizen Sleeper mechanic-is-theme Q65, our theme-through-systems; the loop carries the feeling).
### OFFICERS + CREW WITH HIDDEN STORIES (characters drawn out over time)
- You hire unique OFFICERS (the Haunted Doctor, the Irrepressible Cannoneer) — "each has a story to tell,
  if you can draw it out of them" over many voyages. Companions are slow-burn narrative wells, not stat-
  sticks (cf. Citizen Sleeper residents Q65, our companions; characters revealed through time/investment).
### THE PERMADEATH-VS-MERCIFUL-MODE CHOICE (accessibility — with a hard lesson)
- Two save systems: default ROGUELIKE PERMADEATH (die, pass legacy, restart) OR an optional "MERCIFUL MODE"
  (manual save/reload). This is the accessibility gesture — BUT (see flaws) Kennedy calls the dual system
  "the source of very nearly everything that's wrong with the game." Optionality has a COST (cf. Celeste
  Assist Q59, Qud modes Q56; accessibility done imperfectly — a cautionary version).
### THE LEGACY SYSTEM FUSES THE TWO (the fold as the reconciler)
- The LEGACY system (heir inherits chart/heirlooms/resources) is what "helps fuse" permadeath + save-scum
  playstyles together — it softens death (you keep progress) while preserving stakes. Kennedy: it "helps a
  lot, but you can see the joins." The generational inheritance is the mechanism that makes repeated death
  bearable (cf. Rogue Legacy Q49, our fold; the fold as the tension-reconciler).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- "THE STORY BECOMES A VENDING MACHINE" (repetition erodes atmosphere — the central critique): revisiting
  islands, you "stop seeing the writing and start seeing the game" — you pick the option that yields the
  SUPPLIES you need over the one with the interesting story ("I need supplies; I take tea"). Grinding for
  survival OVERWHELMS the storytelling; repetition erodes the atmosphere the game lives on. LESSON (crucial
  for us): if WRITING/atmosphere is your star but SURVIVAL forces repetition, the two FIGHT — design so
  repeated content stays fresh (rotate/gate events; don't make players re-grind past-read vignettes) or the
  magic curdles into a spreadsheet (our procedural texture + FACTORY variety + don't-force-re-grind).
- THE DUAL SAVE SYSTEM IS "NEARLY EVERYTHING WRONG" (accessibility done badly): the permadeath/Merciful-
  Mode split gave an UNEVEN difficulty curve, irritated players re-playing early story, + worsened
  confusion (the game must teach chancy exploration over safe grinding — hard with two modes). Multiple
  reviews said "almost a classic" and blamed exactly this. LESSON (sharp): offer difficulty/death options
  DELIBERATELY + coherently — a bolted-on second mode can break the curve + the teaching; design modes as
  ONE coherent system (contrast Celeste's clean Assist Q59, Qud's clean modes Q56 — the RIGHT way).
- SLOW / PATIENCE-GATED / GRINDY EARLY: "a slow, deliberate game where a journey can take an age"; early
  fuel-scarcity forces cautious grinding before you can afford real exploration. LESSON: a slow-burn dread
  game risks losing the impatient; ease the early economy so exploration (the point) starts sooner (our
  onboarding + early-economy tuning).
- WEAK COMBAT (the underdeveloped system): even post-overhaul, ship combat is dull — line up firing angles
  vs dumb AI; monsters just charge; you circle-strafe pirates to death. "Sunless Sea doesn't need to be a
  great strategy game to tell a great story, but the two could have enhanced each other." LESSON: a weak
  secondary system that you're FORCED through drags the whole; either make combat good or minimize it (our
  combat dial must HOLD — Bloodlines Q39, Into the Breach Q60; don't ship a limp core verb).
- COMPLETION DEPLETES THE MAGIC (GameSpot's critique): the "kinetic thrill of discovery" inevitably erodes
  as you complete/chart the static world — the wonder is finite. LESSON: discovery-driven games face
  entropy as the map fills; layer in REPLAY value (procedural elements, the fold, new-game variation) so
  it doesn't fully deplete (our fold + procedural texture + Amalgamation-arc-over-static-map).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the dread-survival + legacy-fold + writing-star model
===============================================================================
Sunless Sea is a close sibling — dread-exploration survival where FUEL/SANITY are the real resources, DEATH
passes a LEGACY to an heir (our fold), and WRITING carries everything (our strength). Its flaws are sharp,
directly-applicable warnings for a survival-writing game like ours. It's on mobile (platform precedent).
- W1 (fuel + terror are the real resources — survival as dread): bank the model — Bohemia's survival
  tension should come from DREAD-MANAGEMENT (our medicine/electricity/resources + the Amalgamation's
  looming threat + survivor morale/sanity), where the real enemy is scarcity + fear, not just combat.
  A "terror"-like SANITY/morale meter (survivors fray under the Amalgamation's shadow / far from safety)
  fits our conscience/mental-health system + our dread tone (ties This War of Mine Q30, Darkest Dungeon
  stress Q41, Citizen Sleeper Q65, our survival-accounting + conscience system + Amalgamation-dread).
- W2/W7 (death expected + legacy to your heir — OUR FOLD, validated again): Sunless Sea's legacy system is
  a direct cousin of our GENERATIONAL FOLD — death is EXPECTED + an heir inherits the accumulated progress
  (the "chart" explored, heirlooms, resources, standing), each death BUILDING the next run + reconciling
  permadeath-stakes with kept-progress. Bank it as further validation + a model: what our heirs inherit
  (the mapped district, rebuilt infrastructure, standing, knowledge of the Amalgamation) is our "sea chart
  + heirlooms" (ties Rogue Legacy Q49, Hades Q47, CK3 Q46, Mount & Blade Q61, our fold + city-builder;
  the fold as the death/progress reconciler).
- W3/W6 (writing is the star + officers with hidden stories — our strengths): Sunless Sea PROVES atmospheric
  WRITING + a haunting SCORE can be the load-bearing pillar of a survival RPG — a core Bohemia strength
  (our writing + music repo + Bohemia-as-music-promo). Bank: make our island/district vignettes + our
  companions SLOW-BURN narrative wells (stories drawn out over generations), carried by writing + music
  (ties Disco Q05, Gothic/Qud Q51/Q56, Citizen Sleeper Q65, our writing + music pillars + companions).
- W4 (exploration as its own reward): our OVERMAP/exploration should make DISCOVERY its own reward (the
  "further in" pull into dangerous/unknown Vegas), each location a new story, curiosity vs survival the
  central tension — Paolo owns layout, Claude the discovery/hint/story-node data (ties Outer Wilds Q18,
  Elden Ring/Morrowind Q23/Q37, Gothic no-scaling risk-map Q51, our overmap; MAP LAW respected).
- W5 (theme through the systems — home/dread): bank the craft — Bohemia's LOOP should make the player FEEL
  its theme (relief at the safe/rebuilt district vs the pull + dread of the ruined city / the Amalgamation's
  reach), terror/scarcity dropping near safety + rising in danger — theme enacted mechanically (ties Citizen
  Sleeper mechanic-is-theme Q65, our survival-accounting + district-as-home + Amalgamation-dread).
- W8/W9 (Lovecraftian dread + dark comedy + lingering atmosphere): bank a distinctive TONE (dread + weird +
  dark humor + melancholy) that makes horror CHARACTERFUL + lingers after play — fits our post-collapse
  Vegas + Amalgamation existential-horror + our tone (ties Yakuza tonal-range Q13, SOMA/Outer Wilds Q34/Q18,
  our tone + Amalgamation-horror; atmosphere that haunts).
- W10 (gradual eat-what-you-kill scope): Failbetter's fan-base -> Kickstarter -> Early Access -> launch,
  ~15 months, growing scope incrementally = our ALPHA-FIRST/single-file/FACTORY discipline — ship
  incrementally, grow scope as you go (ties our alpha-first + single-file + FACTORY LAW; the disciplined
  solo-studio scope model).
- FLAWS (bank HARD — sharp + directly applicable): (a) DON'T let survival-grind turn WRITING into a
  "vending machine" — if atmosphere is our star but survival forces repetition, they FIGHT; keep repeated
  content FRESH (rotate/gate events, procedural texture, FACTORY variety, don't force re-grinding past-read
  vignettes) — the single most important Sunless Sea lesson for a survival-WRITING game like ours; (b)
  design difficulty/death MODES as ONE coherent system, not a bolted-on second mode (Sunless Sea's dual-
  save was "nearly everything wrong" — do it Celeste/Qud-clean Q59/Q56, our difficulty packages); (c) ease
  the EARLY economy so exploration/story (the point) starts sooner (don't front-load grind — our onboarding
  + early-economy tuning); (d) our COMBAT DIAL must HOLD (don't ship a limp forced core verb — Bloodlines/
  Into the Breach Q39/Q60); and (e) layer REPLAY value (fold + procedural + the Amalgamation arc) so
  discovery doesn't fully DEPLETE as the static map fills (our fold + Amalgamation-arc-over-static-map).
