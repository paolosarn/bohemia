# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #164 — "THE CABOT HOUSE (THE FATHER IN THE CELL, THE SERUM IN THE VEINS)"
**Game:** Fallout 4 (2015)
**Studio:** Bethesda Game Studios
**Quest:** The Secret of Cabot House — a family of immortals has kept their father imprisoned in an asylum for centuries because he is the source of the serum that keeps them young, and he is either a madman or a god, and freeing him ends the family or the world.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Fallout catalogue; corpus anchor Q02/Q03 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia's Amalgamation promises the dead never die and the living never age, and this is the genre's cleanest quest about a family that bought immortality by imprisoning the one it comes from — a father in a cell for centuries so his children can stay young, and the player handed the choice to keep the serum flowing or set the source free. It is SOMA's ARK (#34) as a family secret, the honest-devil (#126) as a locked door, and the "who do you keep imprisoned so you can keep living" question our whole antagonist runs on.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A family has stayed young for four hundred years by drawing a serum from their own father, whom they keep locked in an asylum because he is either insane or something worse — and the player must decide whether to keep the source imprisoned so the family lives, or free him and find out what he is.**

The Cabots are a pre-war Boston family who never aged: Jack, the scientist son; Emogene, the daughter; their mother Wilhelmina; all kept young for centuries. The secret is their father, Lorenzo — an archaeologist who, before the war, found an alien artifact (a crown) that made him immortal and possibly god-touched, and went dangerously insane. Jack keeps Lorenzo imprisoned in the Parsons State Insane Asylum, drawing from him the serum that keeps the whole family young, and has done so for four hundred years. The player arrives as hired help and is drawn into the family's crisis, then handed the choice: help Jack keep Lorenzo caged and the serum flowing (the family survives, the father stays in his cell forever), or free Lorenzo (who rewards the player with the serum himself, then goes to destroy the family that imprisoned him, and is powerful enough to be a genuine threat to more than them).

The machine is a family's immortality that runs on a prisoner, and the player's choice to keep the cell locked or open it. Jack is not a cartoon villain — he has kept his father alive and contained for centuries, sparing the world a possibly-godlike madman while keeping his family young; the imprisonment is monstrous and defensible at once. Lorenzo is not a simple victim — he is genuinely dangerous, possibly insane, possibly right about what he is, and freeing him unleashes something. The serum is the lure: whoever the player sides with, they can be paid in immortality, so the choice is contaminated by the reward — do you keep a father in a cell forever because his imprisonment keeps you young too? The Cabot House is the Amalgamation's bargain in one mansion: someone stays imprisoned so the rest never die, and the player is offered a share of the never-dying to keep the door shut.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** every promise that you never have to age or lose anyone is running on someone kept in a cell to pay for it, and the offer of a share in the not-dying is how they get you to hold the key.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### JACK CABOT — the jailer son
- **What he wants:** the family kept young and safe, and his father contained — a monstrous arrangement he defends as necessary and merciful both.
- **What he'll trade:** the serum, and the family's gratitude, for help keeping Lorenzo caged.
- **What he will never say out loud:** the four hundred years of guilt; that he is his father's warden and his own family's drug supplier.
- **Function:** THE HONEST JAILER: the imprisonment is monstrous and defensible, and he knows both.

### LORENZO CABOT — the father in the cell
- **What he wants:** out, and revenge on the family that caged him — and he is powerful, possibly godlike, possibly insane.
- **What he'll trade:** the serum, freely, to whoever frees him — then he goes to destroy his jailers.
- **What he will never say out loud (clearly):** whether he is a madman, a god, or both; the ambiguity is the danger.
- **Function:** THE IMPRISONED SOURCE: victim and threat at once, the price the family's immortality is drawn from.

### EMOGENE / THE FAMILY — the beneficiaries
- **What they want:** to keep living, young, without asking too hard what it costs.
- **Function:** THE ONES KEPT YOUNG: the family whose never-aging runs on the father in the cell.

### THE SERUM — the contaminating lure
- The drug drawn from Lorenzo that keeps the Cabots young, offered to the player by whichever side.
- **Function:** THE BRIBE: immortality offered to the player, so their choice about the cell is bought.

### THE CROWN / THE ARTIFACT — the source of the immortality
- The alien object that made Lorenzo what he is.
- **Function:** THE ROOT: the never-dying's origin, and the thing that made a father into a prisoner worth keeping.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes — keep Lorenzo caged (family lives, father imprisoned forever) or free him (family destroyed, threat loosed) — both paying the player in serum, so the choice is bought.**

- **KEEP HIM CAGED (side Jack):** the family survives, young; Lorenzo stays in his cell for another four hundred years; the player is rewarded with the serum for holding the door. WRITES: the imprisonment continues; the never-dying flows; the player complicit in the cell.
- **FREE LORENZO:** he rewards the player with the serum, then goes to destroy the Cabots who caged him; the source of the immortality walks free, powerful and unresolved. WRITES: the family ended; a threat loosed; the player paid by the prisoner.
- **THE SERUM CONSTANT:** whichever side, the player can be paid in immortality — the choice about the cell is contaminated by a share in the not-dying, so no branch is disinterested.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** every path answers "who stays imprisoned so the rest never die, and will you take a cut" — the branches are the Amalgamation's bargain in a mansion, and the game bribes both.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — LORENZO'S THREAT IS ASSERTED, NOT DEMONSTRATED.** The whole "keep him caged" case rides on Lorenzo being a world-level danger, but the game never really SHOWS what he'd do free beyond attacking his family — so the player is asked to endorse eternal imprisonment on the strength of an unproven "he's too dangerous," which tilts the choice toward freeing him by default.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if imprisonment is justified by a threat, the THREAT must be demonstrable — witnessed, evidenced, real — so keeping the cell locked is a genuine weighing, not a claim the player takes on faith. An unproven danger makes the jailer look like the villain by default (the #156 make-the-ends-real law).

**F2 — THE FAMILY'S GUILT IS THIN.** Four hundred years of drugging your relatives young off your imprisoned father is a staggering moral weight, but the Cabots wear it lightly — Jack is anxious, not haunted; Emogene is oblivious — so the horror of the arrangement is under-felt, and the player judges an atrocity the perpetrators barely seem to register.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the beneficiaries of an imprisonment-for-immortality must CARRY it visibly — the guilt of the never-dying shown, not shrugged — so the arrangement's horror lands and the player's choice has moral gravity. A family that doesn't feel the cost makes the cost invisible (the #34 SOMA-honesty law: the never-dying must be haunted).

**F3 — THE SERUM BRIBE ISN'T INTERROGATED.** The player is paid in immortality by whichever side, but the game never makes the player RECKON with taking a cut of the not-dying — the serum is a loot reward, not a moral stain, so the "your choice is bought" horror is a mechanic, not a theme.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if the player is bribed with a share of the atrocity's product (immortality, the Amalgamation's promise), taking it must be a recorded MORAL act with weight — the fold marks that the dynast pocketed the not-dying — so the contamination of the choice is felt. A bribe that's just loot launders the complicity (the #31 cruelty-incentivized law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. IMMORTALITY RUNS ON A PRISONER. The family's never-aging is drawn from a father in a cell — the never-dying has a captive source, which is the Amalgamation's exact logic made a family secret.
W2. THE JAILER IS DEFENSIBLE. Jack has kept a possibly-godlike madman contained for four hundred years, sparing the world and keeping his family young — the imprisonment is monstrous and arguably right, the hardest kind of antagonist.
W3. THE PRISONER IS BOTH VICTIM AND THREAT. Lorenzo is caged unjustly AND genuinely dangerous; freeing him is mercy and menace at once, so neither keeping nor opening the cell is clean.
W4. THE SERUM CONTAMINATES THE CHOICE. Both sides pay the player in immortality, so the decision about the cell is bought — you might keep a father imprisoned forever because it keeps you young too.
W5. THE AMBIGUITY IS THE DANGER. Lorenzo won't resolve whether he's a god or a madman; the uncertainty is what makes freeing him a real gamble and caging him a real cruelty.
W6. FOUR HUNDRED YEARS OF A LOCKED DOOR. The sheer duration — centuries of one man holding another in a cell for the family's benefit — is the horror's weight; time makes the arrangement monstrous.
W7. NO SAFE THIRD DOOR. Lorenzo can't be cured and freed; he is kept or unleashed. The removed middle is the quest's honesty: the source of the never-dying admits no gentle resolution.
W8. THE MANSION IS THE AMALGAMATION IN MINIATURE. Someone imprisoned so the rest never die, a share offered to keep the door shut — the whole antagonist's bargain in one house.
W9. THE CHOICE HAS NO INNOCENT PARTY. Family, father, player — all compromised: the jailers, the dangerous victim, the bribed newcomer. The quest refuses anyone clean hands.
W10. THE SERUM RIDES ALONG. However it ends, the player carries the immortality — a permanent reminder that they took a cut of the not-dying, the choice's stain worn in the veins.
