## 0 CORE IDEA

**A terminally ill professional assassin, who abandoned his young son to grief and relatives after the boy's mother was murdered in retaliation for the father's work, discovers that the son — now grown, angry, and adrift — is about to carry out his own first killing, following the exact path the father walked; and the dying man, with little time left, must intervene not to save himself but to stop his son from becoming him, which he can only do by confronting the boy with the truth of the father's own sins, owning the abandonment and the bloody trade that shaped the child, in an atonement he may not live long enough to complete.**

Thane Krios is a drell assassin, one of the best, and he is dying — a terminal illness gives his whole arc the pressure of a closing window. Long ago, while Thane was consumed by his lethal profession, his wife was murdered as retaliation for his contracts, and in his grief and guilt he abandoned their son, Kolyat, leaving the boy to be raised by others while Thane vanished into more killing. Now Kolyat has surfaced on the path to becoming an assassin himself — angry at the world, angry at his absent father, and about to commit his first murder-for-hire, stepping onto the exact road that took everything from the family. Thane, out of time and full of a lifetime's guilt, sets out with the player not to redeem his own soul but to stop his son from inheriting his sins. And the only way to reach Kolyat is through the truth: Thane cannot lecture the boy from a pedestal, because he has no pedestal; he must confront his son by owning what he did — the abandonment, the trade in death, the way he made the child who is now following him. The atonement is not a speech; it is a father finally showing up, admitting he is the template the son is copying, and trying, in whatever time remains, to be the one voice that can say "do not become me" with the authority of having been exactly that.

The machine is that the son is inheriting the father's sins, and only the father — by owning them rather than hiding them — can break the cycle, in an atonement aimed at the next generation, not the self. Thane's tragedy and his only path are the same thing: he made Kolyat, by absence and example, into a boy walking toward the father's life, and he cannot stop it by pretending to be better than he was. The cycle breaks, if it breaks, precisely because the father does the hardest thing — refuses the comfortable distance, shows up dying and guilty, and names his own crimes as the very thing the son must not repeat. The player's role is to help Thane reach Kolyat before the first kill locks the boy onto the path, and to shape whether the confrontation lands as reconciliation, rejection, or something incomplete. And the honesty is that atonement, done right, is not about clearing your own conscience but about interrupting the harm you set in motion before it reaches the next person; that a parent's worst legacy is the template they become; and that the only authority to say "do not become me" belongs to the one willing to fully own having been exactly what the child must not.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the deepest atonement is not for yourself but for the harm you set in motion before it reaches the next generation, and a parent's worst legacy is the template they become — the child follows the sins, not the sermons; the only way to break the cycle is to show up and own having been exactly what the child must not repeat, because "do not become me" carries authority only from the one who admits they were, and refuses the comfortable distance that would let the son keep copying in the dark.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THANE — the dying father atoning for the son, not himself
- **What he wants (stated):** to stop Kolyat before the first kill; to reach the son he abandoned.
- **What he wants (real):** to interrupt the harm he set in motion; to break the cycle before his time runs out.
- **What he'll trade:** his last strength, his pride, the comfortable distance — everything but the truth.
- **What he cannot claim:** a pedestal; he must confront the boy by owning his own sins.
- **Function:** THE ATONING FATHER: the dying man reaching the next generation by naming what he was.

### KOLYAT — the son inheriting the father's sins
- **What he wants (stated):** to become an assassin; to punish the world and the father who left him.
- **What he wants (real, buried):** the father to have shown up; to not be alone with his anger.
- **Function:** THE INHERITED CYCLE: the son walking the father's road, about to lock onto it with a first kill.

### THE FIRST KILL — the point of no return
- Kolyat's impending murder-for-hire; the act that would complete his inheritance of the father's life.
- **Function:** THE CYCLE-LOCKING ACT: the killing that must be interrupted before it fixes the son's path.

### THE PLAYER (Shepard) — the one who helps the father reach the son
- **What they want:** to get Thane to Kolyat before the kill; to shape whether the confrontation lands.
- **Function:** THE ENABLER OF THE ATONEMENT: the one who helps the dying father interrupt the cycle in time.

### THE ABANDONMENT / THE FATHER'S SINS — the template being copied
- Thane's absence, his trade in death; the crimes Kolyat is inheriting by example.
- **Function:** THE TEMPLATE: the father's own sins, which only owning can break, not hiding.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes at the confrontation — reach Kolyat and break the cycle (the son turned back, the atonement landing on the next generation) or fail to reach him (the son lost to the father's road, the cycle unbroken) — with the constant that the son inherits the sins not the sermons, and only the father owning his own crimes, not hiding them, can turn the boy back.**

- **BREAK THE CYCLE:** the truth owned; Kolyat turned back before the first kill; the father's atonement lands on the next generation. WRITES: the cycle interrupted by a dying man showing up and naming his sins; atonement aimed at the son, maybe too late for the self.
- **FAIL TO REACH HIM:** the son locks onto the road; the abandonment completes itself; the cycle unbroken. WRITES: the harm reaching the next generation; the father's sins inherited; the atonement come too late or landing wrong.
- **THE OWN-IT-OR-LOSE-HIM CONSTANT:** in both, the child follows the father's template, and only the father owning his crimes—not a pedestal, not a sermon—could ever break it; the good father he never was is not available.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the branch is reach-him-or-lose-him — break the cycle by owning the sins or watch it complete, and the quest insists atonement is for the next generation not the self, and "do not become me" only carries authority from the one who admits they were.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — KOLYAT'S TURN CAN RESOLVE TOO NEATLY, MAKING THE CYCLE-BREAK FEEL EASY.** If Kolyat is talked back from his first kill in a single scene with a clean reconciliation, the profound difficulty (a lifetime of abandonment doesn't undo in one confrontation) collapses — the cycle-break needs to cost and to feel partial, or "the child follows the sins" loses its weight.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest breaks an inherited cycle, the turn must be COSTLY and partial — the years of harm not erased by one confrontation, the reconciliation incomplete — so the cycle-break is hard-won, not a clean scene (#170's the-cycle-break-must-cost law).

**F2 — THANE'S OWNERSHIP CAN SLIDE INTO SELF-PITY, CENTERING THE FATHER OVER THE SON.** The atonement's power is that it's aimed at Kolyat, not Thane's conscience; if the confrontation becomes Thane's emotional catharsis, it re-centers the father and the "atonement is for the next generation" idea inverts into the father making his son's crisis about himself.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's atonement is aimed at the next generation, the focus must stay on the CHILD, not the parent's catharsis — the owning in service of turning the son, not clearing the father — so atonement doesn't become the parent centering themselves (#208's the-atonement-serves-the-other law).

**F3 — THE FIRST-KILL DEADLINE CAN BE UNDERUSED, DRAINING THE URGENCY.** The cycle-locking act (Kolyat's first murder) gives the quest its clock; if it's not made present and imminent, the race loses tension and the confrontation feels like it could happen anytime, softening the "interrupt it before it locks" stakes.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's stakes are interrupting a point-of-no-return, that threshold must be made IMMINENT — the cycle-locking act close and pressing — so the race to own the truth in time carries real urgency (#206's the-deadline-must-press law).
