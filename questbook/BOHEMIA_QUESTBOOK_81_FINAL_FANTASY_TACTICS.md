# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 81: THE MAN THEY ERASED / HISTORY IS WRITTEN BY THE CHURCH THAT WON (Final Fantasy Tactics)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the Durai Papers frame (the whole game is a suppressed document), the
class-war spine, the two-friends-two-methods split, the death that breaks them both, the church that
erases the actual hero from history, the Faith stat (belief as a mechanic that CUTS BOTH WAYS), the
economic-collapse origin, the bitter ending, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. Matsuno's masterpiece and
the game most fans think Ivalice's real story was told in. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION (who
controls the record), our [READ], our class/faction politics, + our economic-collapse premise. Square
(Yasumi Matsuno). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Final Fantasy Tactics (Square, 1997; War of the Lions 2007; The Ivalice Chronicles 2025). Ivalice,
after the FIFTY YEARS' WAR has "decimated the economy" and left power-hungry lords carving up a feudal
ruin. Two boys — RAMZA, a noble's son, and DELITA, a farmer's son raised in his house — go to war together
and come out as opposites. History remembers Delita as the hero-king who brought peace. History records
Ramza as a heretic. The game is the argument that history is wrong.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE ENTIRE GAME IS A SUPPRESSED DOCUMENT (the frame — and it is our [READ], whole): FFT opens CENTURIES
  LATER, with a scholar, ARAZLAM DURAI, who has found the DURAI PAPERS — "secret historical records HIDDEN
  IN THE VAULTS OF THE CHURCH OF GLABADOS that tell previously unknown secrets of the War of the Lions and
  the celebrated King Delita." He tells the audience that "history has made Delita the ONLY hero of the War
  of the Lions" and asks them "TO CONSIDER IF THIS IS THE TRUTH AND HELP HIM FIND THE TRUE ANSWER."
  - Every battle you fight is a historian's reconstruction of a censored record. "In playing the game, YOUR
    ROLE IS TO HELP DURAI UNCOVER THE TRUTH of Ivalice's history."
  - And the thesis: "nearly EVERY ASPECT OF LIFE IN IVALICE at the time of the War of the Lions is built on
    some sort of FALSEHOOD, MISCONCEPTION, OR OMISSION. The Church of Glabados... ACTIVELY REGULATES WHAT
    PEOPLE HEAR AND BELIEVE" (STARTLINGLY our recorded-vs-unrecorded + [READ]; cf. Morrowind Q74, FFXII Q80;
    the game AS a recovered record).
- THEY ERASED THE HERO, AND THEY KILLED THE MAN WHO WROTE HIM DOWN (the gut-punch): Ramza saves the world.
  Then: Orran Durai, "having found Ramza and Alma alive, SPENT FIVE YEARS writing the Durai Papers,
  chronicling Ramza's struggle... He presented them before the Church of Glabados' Clemensian Council...
  HOPING TO PRESERVE RAMZA'S LEGACY. However, FEARING EXPOSURE OF THEIR CORRUPTION, the church BRANDED
  ORRAN A HERETIC AND EXECUTED HIM, ERASING RAMZA" from history.
  - A man spends five years writing the truth and is burned for it, and the truth sits in a vault for
    CENTURIES until a scholar digs it out. The Church didn't just win the war — it won the RECORD (cf. our
    AMALGAMATION/Network + [READ]; the archivist as martyr).
- IT'S ABOUT CLASS, AND IT WAS BORN FROM AN ECONOMIC COLLAPSE (the origin — directly our premise): Matsuno
  said the story "was originally inspired by THE COLLAPSE OF JAPAN'S BUBBLE ECONOMY, and how it prompted an
  'ERA WHEN MANY WERE ROBBED OF HOPE, WHEN DREAMS WERE MEASURED BY THEIR PRICE TAG'."
  - "Tactics is directly about HOW THE RICH AND POWERFUL VIEW THE POOR AND WEAK AS ALMOST SUBHUMAN,
    practically DELIGHTING in the squalor and suffering normal people go through."
  - "One of the main messages of Tactics truly is THE EFFECTS OF PRIVILEGE, and how even THE MOST
    WELL-INTENTIONED OF US CAN SIMPLY BE BLIND TO THE TRUTH."
  - Matsuno: "the themes I've included in my games have usually reflected the people and the situations I
    was working in at that time" (he studied foreign policy + worked as an ECONOMIC REPORTER before games)
    (STARTLINGLY our post-ECONOMIC-collapse premise; a fantasy built from a real crash).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how a class tragedy is built)
===============================================================================

### THE INEQUALITY IS ESTABLISHED IN THE HOUSE, NOT THE SPEECH (the setup)
- Delita's parents died of plague; Ramza's father took him + his sister TIETRA in and raised them together.
  Ramza thinks they're brothers. But: "Ramza's father makes it PERFECTLY CLEAR ON HIS DEATHBED that DELITA
  IS MEANT TO SERVE AS RAMZA'S STEWARD." The kindest man in the story still knows exactly what Delita is
  for. Even getting Delita into the military academy required pull — "the headmaster BALKING at the idea of
  taking Delita at first."
- And the counter-example: ARGATH, a nobleman from a fallen house, "obsessed with reputation and status,"
  who "openly despises those he deems 'CHATTEL' for their common blood." He is "a PAWN OF THE STATUS QUO
  whose faith in the destructive laws of class defines him" (cf. our faction web + class politics; privilege
  shown in a household, not a monologue).
### THE ENEMY YOU'RE SENT TO CRUSH IS RIGHT (the radicalizing structure)
- Ramza and Delita's first assignment is putting down the CORPSE BRIGADE — a peasant rebellion. "The more
  battles Ramza and Delita see, the more they learn that Ivalice's widespread inequality is due to THE
  GREED OF THE NOBLE CLASS." Delita grows "more and more disillusioned FIGHTING PEOPLE WHOSE GOALS HE
  UNDERSTANDS AND SHARES."
- You spend the opening act killing the poor on behalf of the rich, and the game makes sure you understand
  it (cf. our factions; the mission that indicts the giver).
### THE DEATH THAT BREAKS THEM BOTH (the hinge — and it's an ABSURDITY)
- A Corpse Brigade leader kidnaps TIETRA — Delita's lowborn sister — MISTAKING HER FOR ALMA, Ramza's
  highborn sister. The analysis nails the point: "NOT EVEN A LOWBORN MAN CAN DISTINGUISH HIS CAPTIVE'S
  SOCIAL STATUS, thus making the apparent divide between classes ALL THE MORE ABSURD at face value."
- Ramza's own brother orders ARGATH to shoot the hostage-taker AND Tietra — because a commoner girl is an
  acceptable loss. Argath does it gladly. Delita runs to his sister's body; the fortress explodes; he's
  presumed dead.
- The verdict on Ramza: "Ramza's determination to REMAIN MODERATE in the face of social injustice COST AT
  LEAST ONE OF HIS LOVED ONES THEIR LIVES. This is the point from which Ramza's character truly begins to
  grow." Moderation as complicity (cf. our conscience system + Megaton law; the neutral position that kills).
### TWO FRIENDS, TWO METHODS, ONE GOAL (the game's spine)
- RAMZA renounces his name, becomes a mercenary, and "opposes the power structures by FIGHTING ALONGSIDE
  THE PEOPLE and uncovering dark agendas." He ends the game having saved the world at the rank of SQUIRE,
  branded a heretic, with nothing.
- DELITA "sought to MANIPULATE THE VARIOUS SIDES of the war to achieve his own ends" — he abducts a
  princess, plays every faction against the others, executes his allies for treason, and takes the throne.
  He "walks the path above, trying to portray himself as the hero that the people deserve, WHILE PLOTTING
  SINISTER MOVES BEHIND THE CURTAIN."
- They meet, they understand each other, and they don't stop: "In a classic tale of two people striving to
  achieve peace through different means, Ramza and Delita AGREE TO DISAGREE. The two friends try to
  persuade each other to stand down, EVEN THOUGH BOTH UNDERSTAND ONE ANOTHER'S POINT OF VIEW."
- And the killer read: "Ramza, through his limited conversations with Delita, SEEMINGLY ALLOWS HIMSELF TO
  BE USED BY HIS FRIEND just so they both can get rid of the corrupted factions" (cf. our faction web + our
  finales; two answers, both sincere, both costly).
### EVERYONE IS RUNNING A CON (the political texture)
- "Apart from Ramza, EVERY MAJOR POWER ACTIVE DURING THE GAME IS RUNNING THEIR OWN CON or part of someone
  else's con. Sometimes both. The resulting GAMBIT PILEUP ironically means that these plans RUN INTO EACH
  OTHER, and several parties lose to the others as the game rolls on."
- The church's own cardinal, when exposed, "revealed himself to have become a true god AND TO NOT ACTUALLY
  BELIEVE IN THE DIVINITY HE PROFESSED as a cardinal of the Church of Glabados." Ramza "SCOLDED HIM FOR
  DECEIVING THE PEOPLE AND ABANDONING HIS FAITH" — the heretic defends the faith to the priest (cf. our 13
  factions; the conspiracy pile-up).
### THE FAITH STAT: BELIEF AS A DOUBLE-EDGED MECHANIC (the best stat in RPGs)
- "The Faith stat works as BOTH your magical attack AND INVERSELY as magic defense. So a character with
  high faith is a great spellcaster BUT ALSO VERY VULNERABLE to magic, while someone with very low faith
  can no-sell magic BUT IS USELESS as a caster."
- And the line that makes it literature: "It's double-edged though, as YOU CAN'T NOT BELIEVE IN ATTACK
  MAGIC WITHOUT NOT BELIEVING IN HEALING MAGIC."
- In a game about a church that lies, your openness to belief is EXACTLY your vulnerability to it — and
  closing yourself off costs you every blessing too. The theme is a stat (cf. our conscience system; theme
  as a number, done right).
### THE HERO IS A SQUIRE (the anti-glory design)
- "Ramza saves the world from an Eldritch Abomination and ALTERS THE COURSE OF HISTORY, yet technically
  (in-story) NEVER ROSE ABOVE THE RANK OF SQUIRE." No title, no throne, no statue. Just the work (cf. our
  standing + fold; the hero with no rank).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- WE NEVER SEE IF THE TRUTH ACTUALLY WINS: "While the game makes it clear Delita's reputation and the
  church won't survive the reveal of the papers, WE ACTUALLY NEVER SEE THE FALLOUT OF THE REVEAL, NOR IF
  RAMZA'S PLACE IN HISTORY GETS REASSESSED in Ivalice or not." The whole frame is "help me find the true
  answer" — and the game ends without telling you whether finding it mattered. LESSON: an
  investigation-frame promises a VERDICT; withholding it preserves ambiguity but can read as an unfinished
  thought (our [READ] must decide what recovering the truth actually CHANGES).
- THE CLASS STORY GETS HIJACKED BY DEMONS: the game opens as a razor-sharp materialist tragedy about
  privilege and economics — and resolves as a fight against LUCAVI, literal demons summoned by magic
  stones. The conspiracy is real, but it converts a structural critique into a monster hunt: the nobles
  aren't just greedy, they're POSSESSED. LESSON (sharp, for us): if your theme is systemic, don't let a
  supernatural villain absolve the system — our AMALGAMATION must not become an excuse that lets our
  FACTIONS off the hook for what people did to each other.
- IT'S FAMOUSLY, GLEEFULLY BROKEN: "many gamers who grew up with the game can attest to how fun, in-depth,
  AND YET BROKEN it is." The job system's depth means degenerate builds trivialize the tactics. LESSON:
  deep customization + no guardrails = players optimize the challenge out of existence (our combat dial +
  difficulty packages).
- THE FRAME REQUIRES A LOT OF NAMES, FAST: seven noble houses, two knightly orders, a church, a trading
  company, a dead saint, twelve stones. The prologue is a firehose. LESSON: a political story needs an
  on-ramp — our faction web (13 factions) must be introduced through PEOPLE, not org-charts.
- DELITA'S END IS AMBIGUOUS TO A FAULT: his final scene (and Ovelia) is one of the most-argued in the
  series, and the game gives you almost nothing to adjudicate with. LESSON: ambiguity earns its keep only
  when the player has EVIDENCE to argue from (our [READ]: give them the pieces).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the suppressed record + the collapse that made it
===============================================================================
FFT is the single most on-theme game in the questbook for our RECORD premise: a story whose FRAME is a
censored document, whose villain owns history, whose hero is erased, and whose author was inspired by an
ECONOMIC COLLAPSE. It's FFXII's (Q80) thousand-years-later companion and Morrowind's (Q74) structural twin.
- W1/W2 (the game is a suppressed document + they executed the archivist — our [READ], whole): the top port,
  and it's a STRUCTURAL one. FFT doesn't just have a contested history — the GAME IS THE CONTESTED DOCUMENT.
  A scholar centuries on says: the official record names one hero; help me find out if that's true. Bank
  the frame for Bohemia: our [READ] could present a generation's events as a RECOVERED RECORD the player is
  reconstructing — with the Amalgamation/Network as the institution that "actively regulates what people
  hear and believe," and the gut-punch that someone SPENT YEARS writing the truth and was destroyed for it,
  and it sat in a vault. The archivist as martyr is the most on-theme character type we could build (ties
  our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + Amalgamation + Network + fold, Morrowind Q74, FFXII Q80, Obra Dinn/
  Golden Idol Q19/Q54; a top-tier structural port).
- W3 (born from a real economic collapse — our premise, validated): Matsuno built Ivalice's misery out of
  Japan's bubble bursting: "an era when many were robbed of hope, when dreams were measured by their price
  tag." That IS Bohemia's premise — a post-ECONOMIC (not nuclear) apocalypse. Bank FFT as the proof that a
  fantasy/genre setting built on a REAL financial collapse produces the medium's most enduring political
  story, and as the model for grounding our Vegas in real economics (ties our post-economic-collapse premise
  + faction web + survival-accounting + GDD; our thesis, already proven).
- W4/W5/W6 (privilege in a household + the enemy who's right + moderation is complicity — our class
  politics + conscience system): bank all three. (a) Show inequality in a HOUSE, not a speech — the kind
  father who still tells his son that his brother is a servant; (b) our early quests should send the
  dynasty to crush people whose cause they'd share, and make sure they notice; (c) and bank the hardest
  one: MODERATION IS COMPLICITY — staying neutral got Ramza's loved one killed, and that's where his
  character starts. That's a conscience-system beat with teeth (ties our conscience system + faction web +
  Megaton law + Pacifist Law — note the tension: our Pacifist Law is about REFUSING VIOLENCE, not refusing
  a SIDE; FFT's lesson is that neutrality-as-avoidance has a body count).
- W7 (two friends, two methods, both sincere — our finales): STEAL the spine. Ramza fights beside the
  people and is erased; Delita manipulates every faction and is crowned; both want peace; both are right
  about the other. That's LIBERATE vs BECOME with a face on each — and the devastating grace note is that
  Ramza "seemingly ALLOWS HIMSELF TO BE USED by his friend just so they both can get rid of the corrupted
  factions." Two dynasts/finales that need each other (ties our Liberate/Respect/Become + faction web +
  fold + Megaton law).
- W8 (every power is running a con, and the cons COLLIDE): bank the texture — in Bohemia every faction
  should be running its own play, and the plays should WRECK EACH OTHER without the dynasty's involvement.
  A "gambit pileup" is what a living faction web looks like. Plus the cardinal who doesn't believe: our
  most powerful believer should be the biggest fraud, and the heretic should be the one defending the faith
  (ties our 13 factions + world-model + entities/spawning + Network).
- W9 (the Faith stat is the theme — theme as a NUMBER): the sharpest mechanical steal here. In a game about
  a lying church, FAITH is both your power and your vulnerability, and "you can't not believe in attack
  magic without not believing in healing magic." For Bohemia: a dynast's openness to the Amalgamation (to
  the record, to the dead, to belief) could be BOTH their greatest capability AND exactly what it uses to
  steer them — and armoring against it costs them every benefit too. That's our conscience system with a
  spine (ties our conscience system + Amalgamation + [READ] + standing; theme rendered as a stat).
- W10 (the hero never rises above squire): bank it — our dynasts can save the district and hold NO title.
  Standing without rank. The work without the statue (ties our standing + fold + Arthur's death-scene Q73).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) DECIDE WHAT RECOVERING THE TRUTH CHANGES — FFT's whole frame is "help me find the
  true answer" and it never shows whether the answer landed; our [READ] must cash out (does the record
  change anything, or is that the tragedy? — either is fine, but CHOOSE); (b) DON'T LET THE SUPERNATURAL
  ABSOLVE THE SYSTEM — FFT's materialist class tragedy gets hijacked by literal demons, which risks saying
  the nobles were possessed rather than greedy; our AMALGAMATION must never become the excuse that lets our
  FACTIONS off the hook for what people chose to do to each other (the single most important flaw in this
  teardown for us); (c) deep customization with no guardrails = players optimize the challenge away (our
  combat dial + difficulty packages); (d) introduce a political web through PEOPLE, not org-charts (our 13
  factions need an on-ramp); and (e) ambiguity needs EVIDENCE to argue from (our [READ]: give the pieces).
