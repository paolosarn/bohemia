# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 17: THE FOUR-PILLAR MISSION + SOCIAL BOSS BATTLES (Deus Ex: Human Revolution / Mankind Divided)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the four-pillar immersive-sim design, the multi-path level
structure, the SOCIAL BOSS-BATTLE system (conversation as a winnable fight), the honest flaws, and
Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for EVERY-SPACE-SOLVABLE-FOUR-WAYS + DEBATE-AS-BOSS-FIGHT.
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Games: Deus Ex: Human Revolution (Eidos Montreal, 2011) + Mankind Divided (2016). You play Adam Jensen,
an augmented ex-security officer unraveling a conspiracy. The series' creed: multiple ways through
EVERY scenario, expressed through four "pillars."

===============================================================================
## 0. THE FOUR-PILLAR CREED (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE FOUR PILLARS: COMBAT, STEALTH, HACKING, and SOCIAL — every mission is built so ALL FOUR are
  viable routes to the objective. "No mission in Deus Ex makes exclusive use of one skill set." The
  level is a lock with four different keys, and most players blend them.
- THE DESIGN PROMISE: "There's no better example of choices in Deus Ex than how each quest is
  constructed" — the choice isn't a dialogue menu, it's HOW you physically traverse and resolve a space.
- THE BUILD-EXPRESSES-YOU LOOP: praxis (skill) points unlock augmentations (remote hacking, cloak,
  Icarus dash, the CASIE social enhancer, heavy armor) — your build changes which routes open, but the
  game warns against over-specializing (a pure hacker gets cornered on traversal). Breadth = options.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE MULTI-PATH LEVEL (one objective, four routes) — "Searching for Proof" / the police station
===============================================================================
Representative structure (the early police-station infiltration + the "Searching for Proof" level):
- THE OBJECTIVE: reach a target room/item deep in a guarded space. NO single intended path.
- COMBAT ROUTE: go loud — the space stocks a grenade cache / cover / heavy-weapon options for the
  combat build (Mankind Divided's Titan armor shields a frontal assault... until the energy bar drains).
- STEALTH ROUTE: cloak, rangy jumps (Icarus/Icarus-dash), tiptoe through a crate-strewn storehouse,
  takedowns (lethal or non-lethal) — active camo + traversal augs open a quiet path.
- HACKING ROUTE ("the fourth pillar" that supplements the others): hack turrets to turn them, cameras
  to blind them, doors/keypads to open shortcuts, robots to disable — remote hacking lets you look at a
  device and crack it. Hacking multiplies the OTHER pillars' options.
- SOCIAL / DIEGETIC ROUTE: talk a guard into standing down (the police-station: persuade an old
  colleague, Wayne Haas, to let you in legitimately — a full social path INSTEAD of infiltration).
- THE VENTS/LATERAL: the series' signature air-vent network + ledges + basements — a physical maze of
  alternate routes for the observant (and, crucially, a FAILURE REROUTE — see the social battle below).
- LEVEL-DESIGN CRAFT: diegetic information (a keycode on a nearby monitor, an email hinting a password),
  guiding the eye to key items, multiple ent\exits per room, verticality — the space is authored to be
  read and solved, not funneled.

===============================================================================
## 2. THE SOCIAL BOSS BATTLE (conversation as a winnable fight — the signature)
===============================================================================
The franchise's most distinctive quest-craft: certain confrontations are "SOCIAL BOSS BATTLES" — a
high-stakes CONVERSATION you can WIN or LOSE like a fight, with no violence.
- THE SYSTEM: the opponent has a PSYCHOLOGY. You pick conversational tactics (Empathize / Reason /
  Advise / Confront / Refocus / Placate / Pinpoint / Absolve) turn by turn; the right sequence against
  THAT person's profile wins; the wrong one loses. Responses are partly RANDOMIZED, so it's not pure
  memorization — you must READ the person.
- THE CASIE SOCIAL AUGMENTATION: an optional aug that reads the target's PERSONALITY PROFILE + primes
  pheromones — it TILTS the odds (like Disco's clothing modifiers / dressing for a check, Questbook 05).
  Earnable early with effort; makes reading them explicit.
- THE FLAGSHIP BATTLES:
  - ZEKE SANDERS (hostage crisis, EARLY, usually BEFORE you have CASIE): talk down an unhinged
    hostage-taker with NO assistance — purely your own read of his body language while the hostage sobs
    and he jabs the gun. Cited as one of the most intense, rewarding moments precisely because you're
    UNAIDED. (Empathize -> Empathize -> Reason/Empathize.)
  - DAVID SARIF (your own boss): confront him about a firewall breach; repel his topic-changes (he
    invokes your dead ex, Megan, to destabilize you); stay calm (Refocus/Placate) -> he confesses a
    regretted transgression. You make your BOSS confess.
  - BILL TAGGART (a politician, on LIVE TELEVISION): confront the anti-aug leader mid-press-conference;
    keep CONFRONTING with facts, partially agree then push (Silver Tongue) -> he reveals his aide
    Sandoval's location. Accuse him of being as bad as Sandoval and he nearly loses composure ON AIR
    (damaging his public image). A debate with public-reputation stakes.
  - HUGH DARROW (the finale): out-argue a Nobel-winning super-genius billionaire about the NATURE OF
    MANKIND — win a philosophical debate as a climax (cf. Planescape's argument-as-boss, Questbook 12).
- THE FAILURE REROUTE (crucial): if you LOSE the Taggart social battle, you're not dead-ended — you hop
  the safety line into a bathroom, find VENTS, and reach the target room the immersive-sim way. Failing
  the conversation just routes you to a stealth/traversal solution. (Failure = a branch, not a wall —
  cf. Disco's white checks, Questbook 05.)

## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- BOSS FIGHTS (HR original): the game preaches four pillars but the original HR FORCED lethal combat
  boss fights — a pacifist/stealth/social build hit a WALL (you HAD to kill). A notorious contradiction
  of its own creed (fixed in the Director's Cut). LESSON: don't preach multi-path then force ONE path
  at the climax.
- OVERPOWERED STEALTH/CLOAK (Mankind Divided): players note cloak + hacking make stealth TOO easy /
  trivialize the challenge; non-lethal takedowns feel under-supported. LESSON: balance the pillars so
  none trivializes the others; keep each route TENSE.
- SPECIALIZATION TRAP: over-investing one pillar early corners you (no traversal) — the game punishes
  narrow builds. LESSON (double-edged): breadth should be viable, but don't make specialization a trap
  either — telegraph that some spread is needed.

## 4. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. EVERY SPACE, FOUR KEYS: build every scenario so combat/stealth/hacking/social ALL reach the goal —
    the lock has four keys; players blend them. The choice is HOW you traverse, not a menu.
W2. THE SOCIAL BOSS BATTLE: make a CONVERSATION a winnable fight — read the opponent's psychology, pick
    tactics turn-by-turn, win or lose with no violence. Talking is a boss fight.
W3. READ-THE-PERSON (partly randomized): responses shuffle so you must READ the individual, not
    memorize — the person is the puzzle (cf. Lady Boyle personality-deduction, Questbook 14).
W4. THE ODDS-TILTER AUG (CASIE): an optional tool that reveals the psych profile + primes pheromones —
    tilts the check transparently (dress-for-the-check, Questbook 05); earnable with effort.
W5. THE UNAIDED PEAK (Zeke Sanders): the most intense social battle gives NO assistance — your own read
    vs a sobbing hostage + a jittery gun. Removing the aid RAISES the tension and the reward.
W6. PUBLIC-REPUTATION STAKES (Taggart on TV): a debate where winning DAMAGES the opponent's public
    image (he nearly cracks on air) — social consequence beyond the info you extract.
W7. THE DEBATE-AS-CLIMAX (Darrow): out-argue a genius about the nature of mankind as a FINALE — the
    ultimate boss is a philosophical argument (cf. Planescape, Questbook 12).
W8. FAILURE REROUTES (lose the talk -> take the vents): losing a social battle routes you to a
    stealth/traversal solution — failure is a branch, never a dead end (cf. Disco, Questbook 05).
W9. DIEGETIC INFO IN THE SPACE: keycodes on monitors, passwords in emails — the level teaches its own
    solution environmentally (cf. Vault 22, Questbook 03).
W10. BUILD EXPRESSES YOU (with a breadth warning): your augs open routes; over-specializing corners you
    — options come from breadth. (And the honest flaw: don't make the climax betray the build promise.)

## 5. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
- W1 (every space, four keys): audit Bohemia's key scenarios for OUR four-ish pillars — COMBAT (Dead-Eye),
  STEALTH, our "HACK" equivalent (infrastructure/logistics manipulation — cut power, reroute, sabotage),
  and SOCIAL ([READ]/barter/standing). Every major space solvable multiple ways (ties DOS2/KCD systemic,
  Questbooks 15/08). Our "hacking" pillar = manipulating the CITY systems.
- W2/W3 (the social boss battle + read-the-person): BUILD a Bohemia SOCIAL-BATTLE system — a high-stakes
  conversation won by reading an NPC's psychology and picking tactics turn-by-turn (confront/empathize/
  refocus), partly randomized so you READ them. PERFECT for our summit (Q039), the Amalgamation door
  (Q050 beaten by knowing), faction negotiations, the succession vote — a winnable debate as a set-piece.
- W4 (the odds-tilter): an optional Bohemia tool that reveals an NPC's profile / tilts a social check
  (a companion who knows them, a dossier from the ledger, a favor spent) — transparent soft-modifier.
- W5 (the unaided peak): stage our HARDEST social battle with NO aids — the dynasty's own read vs a
  desperate person (a hostage-taker, a grieving zealot, a cornered defector) — remove the assist to
  spike tension (ties Q049 verdicts, Q017 grief).
- W6 (public-reputation stakes): a Bohemia debate PERFORMED publicly (a district assembly, a faction
  tribunal) where winning shifts STANDING/reputation, not just info — social consequence (ties Q037
  propaganda, our standing system).
- W7 (debate-as-climax): our endgame ALREADY is this (Q050 beaten by knowing; the Amalgamation argued
  down) — Deus Ex + Planescape both validate the PHILOSOPHICAL-DEBATE finale. Bank the social-battle
  MECHANIC for it (tactics vs the Amalgamation's psychology).
- W8 (failure reroutes): losing a Bohemia social battle should ROUTE to another path (blew the
  negotiation -> now sneak/sabotage/fight in) — failure is a branch (our no-brick-wall law + Disco,
  Questbook 05).
- W9 (diegetic info): teach solutions through the SPACE (a code on a terminal, a rota on a wall) — the
  environment as its own hint (ties our [READ]).
- W10 + FLAWS (build expresses you, but don't betray it): keep Bohemia routes build-expressive, but
  NEVER force one pillar at a climax (the HR boss-fight sin) — our Pacifist Path Law already forbids
  this; Deus Ex is the cautionary tale. AND balance pillars so none trivializes the rest (the cloak
  flaw) — keep every route tense (regression-gate our difficulty).
