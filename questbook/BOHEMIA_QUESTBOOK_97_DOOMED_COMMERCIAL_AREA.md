# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 97: THE CURSE IS THE ECONOMY / THE MALIGNANT ENTITY IS A WOMAN MAKING DICE (Investigate Doomed Commercial Area, Disco Elysium)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where a bookstore owner begs you to investigate a supernatural
curse that bankrupts every business in her building, and the curse turns out to be CAPITALISM. Complete event
flow, the curtain, the psychic-investigator lie, the furnace, Neha, the Shivers check that explains
everything, the choice to tell the truth or leave her the ghost, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports.
STARTLINGLY relevant to our POST-ECONOMIC-COLLAPSE PREMISE, our [READ], our conscience system, + our
district. ZA/UM (Robert Kurvitz, Argo Tuulik). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest
written here.

Quest: "Investigate Doomed Commercial Area," a SIDE task in Disco Elysium (2019), Martinaise. The pitch, from
the journal: "**PEOPLE SAY THE COMMERCIAL BUILDING ON THE PLAZA IS CURSED; NO BUSINESS WILL EVER THRIVE THERE
WITHOUT GOING BANKRUPT.**" Objectives include, verbatim: "**SEARCH FOR THE 'MALIGNANT ENTITY.' PLAISANCE SAID
SHE LIVES INSIDE A CHIMNEY—FIND A WAY IN.**"

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE CURSE IS REAL AND IT'S CALLED COMMERCIAL RENT (the joke that isn't a joke — STARTLINGLY our premise):
  every business in this building fails. Every single one. The owner has concluded it's supernatural. She's
  not stupid — she's LOOKING AT REAL DATA. Business after business, bankrupt, in the same square footage,
  for years. That IS an anomaly. There IS a malignant entity destroying these people.
  - It's just not a ghost. It's a market. The building is in a district that lost its economy, and the
    pattern of failure is so consistent and so invisible that the human mind reaches for the occult.
  - Punk: our entire game is set after an ECONOMIC apocalypse. This is the single best short quest ever
    written about the fact that **economic forces feel exactly like curses to the people inside them** (cf.
    our post-economic-collapse premise + district + faction web).
- THE INVESTIGATION IS REAL DETECTIVE WORK ABOUT A FAKE CRIME (the structure): the game treats "is this
  building haunted" with total procedural seriousness. Flashlight. Basement. Furnace. Physical Instrument
  check. Interview the entity. Cross-examine. Check the control case.
  - And the payoff is a **Shivers check** — the skill that lets Harry feel the CITY ITSELF — that answers the
    whole mystery with a fact about real estate (cf. our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded; the supernatural
    investigation that resolves into infrastructure).
- AND YOU GET TO DECIDE WHETHER SHE KEEPS THE GHOST (the choice — no karma, no reward difference): "**DURING
  THIS CONVERSATION, YOU CAN POINT OUT THE TRUE CAUSE OF ALL TENANTS OF THAT BUILDING GOING BANKRUPT, OR LIE
  AND SAY THAT THE CURSE REALLY EXISTS.**"
  - Same XP either way. **70 XP for the investigation.** The game does not pay you differently for the truth
    (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + Megaton law; the choice with no mechanical opinion attached).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE HOOK IS A PIECE OF FABRIC YOU'RE NOT SUPPOSED TO TOUCH
- The bookstore is "west of the Whirling-in-Rags hostel." There are yellow curtains on the ground floor.
- "**INVESTIGATE THE CURTAINS ON THE RIGHT OF THE SHOP. PLAISANCE WILL PLEAD WITH YOU NOT TO DO SO. PERSIST
  IN FIDDLING WITH THE CURTAINS AND PLAISANCE WILL ASK TO TALK TO YOU.**"
- The quest is initiated by IGNORING A WOMAN BEGGING YOU TO STOP. That's the entire character of Harry in
  one interaction, and the game makes you do it to open the content (cf. our conscience system + entities;
  the hook that costs you something to pull).
- "**SHE TELLS YOU THAT IT LEADS TO THE CURSED DOOMED COMMERCIAL AREA.**"
### STAGE 1 — YOU LIE TO GET THE JOB
- "**OFFER TO INVESTIGATE THE CURSE AND THEN MAKE A DRAMA (11) CHECK TO PERSUADE HER THAT YOU ARE A PSYCHIC
  INVESTIGATOR**, initiating Investigate Doomed Commercial Area. **SHE WILL GIVE YOU THE KEY TO BOOKSTORE
  BACK DOOR.**"
- DRAMA is the skill of theatrical lying. To be allowed to solve her problem, you have to become the fraud
  she needs. The quest's first act is Harry inventing a supernatural credential (cf. our [READ] + standing;
  the credential you fabricate to get access).
- Alternative path: "**YOU CAN BREAK INTO THE DOOMED COMMERCIAL AREA WITHOUT TALKING TO PLAISANCE ABOUT THE
  CURSE, IGNORING HER FURTHER WARNINGS.**"
### STAGE 2 — THE DARK (the flashlight is a real object)
- "**YOU'LL NEED TO EQUIP THE FLASHLIGHT FROM KIM'S MOTOR CARRIAGE TO PROCEED IN THE DARK SPACE.**" You have
  to go get it. It's in your partner's car. The game does not hand it to you.
- Kim stops you at the threshold. Harry's Remote Viewer voice answers: "**IT'S THE NETHERWORLD...**"
- What's actually inside: **an abandoned gym.** A shot put ball on the floor. An unsafe 60kg barbell.
  Abandoned offices. A planning board. Somebody's business, mid-collapse, left exactly where it stopped
  (STARTLINGLY our district + prefab factory + HD tile repo; the netherworld is a failed gym).
- And Cuno, if you ask, tells you there's "**A GIANT FRIDGE IN THE BASEMENT OF THE DOOMED COMMERCIAL AREA
  SHAPED LIKE A POLAR BEAR.**" There is.
### STAGE 3 — YOU YELL INTO A FURNACE AND SOMETHING ANSWERS
- The way to reach the "entity" is not a door. It's a chimney. "**HEAD DOWNSTAIRS, FIND THE FURNACE, AND PASS
  A PHYSICAL INSTRUMENT CHECK TO YELL INTO IT.**"
- The dialogue option is perfect: "**I SUMMON THE GHOST OF THIS DOOMED COMMERCIAL AREA...**"
- "**ON A SUCCESS, THE NOVELTY DICEMAKER WILL PULL BACK THE SAFETY CURTAIN**, allowing you to enter the
  upstairs chimney. **THE VOICE WILL TELL YOU TO GO UPSTAIRS.**"
- The ghost answers you. Because she heard you shouting through the pipe. Because she's a person, upstairs,
  working (cf. our district + scheduler; the haunting that has an occupant).
### STAGE 4 — THE MALIGNANT ENTITY (a woman at a workbench, mildly annoyed)
- "**THE WORKSHOP OF NEHA, THE NOVELTY DICEMAKER, WHO ASKS WHETHER YOU ARE HERE TO PICK UP SOME DICE.**"
- The greatest anticlimax in the medium. You have kicked open a door, summoned a spirit through a furnace,
  climbed a chimney, and arrived at a small business owner asking if you're here for your order.
- You can "**ACCUSE HER OF BEING A 'MALIGNANT ENTITY.'**" "**YOU LEARN THAT NEHA IS, INDEED, THE 'ENTITY' BUT
  SHE'S NOT MYSTERIOUS OR CURSED.**"
- "**SPEAKING TO HER WILL REVEAL THAT THERE IS NO CURSE, AND THAT IT'S A HOAX.**" She'll "**TELL YOU ALL
  ABOUT THE AREA AND THE SUPPOSED CURSE.**"
- "**YOU CAN ASK ABOUT THE OTHER BUSINESSES TO LEARN WHY THEY FAILED.**" Each one has a mundane, specific,
  boring, devastating reason.
### STAGE 5 — THE SHIVERS CHECK (the whole quest lands on a control case)
- The masterstroke: "**YOU CAN ATTEMPT A SHIVERS 14 WHITE CHECK TO INQUIRE AS TO WHY HER OWN DICE-MAKING
  BUSINESS HASN'T FAILED.**"
- The answer: "**BECAUSE IT'S NOT IN THE SAME BUILDING AS THE OTHERS.**"
- That's it. That's the curse. Her workshop is technically in a different structure, so it survives. The
  supernatural pattern collapses into a property line.
- "**PASSING THIS CHECK WILL ALLOW YOU TO UNLOCK THE PRECARIOUS WORLD THOUGHT** for your Thought Cabinet."
  The reward for solving a fake haunting is a permanent, installed IDEA about how fragile everything is
  (STARTLINGLY our [READ] + perks; the deduction that becomes a piece of your head).
### STAGE 6 — WHAT YOU TELL HER
- "**AFTER TALKING TO THE NOVELTY DICEMAKER, RETURN TO THE OWNER OF THE BOOKSTORE AND TELL HER WHAT YOU
  MANAGED TO FIND OUT.**"
- "**DURING THIS CONVERSATION, YOU CAN POINT OUT THE TRUE CAUSE OF ALL TENANTS OF THAT BUILDING GOING
  BANKRUPT, OR LIE AND SAY THAT THE CURSE REALLY EXISTS. YOU WILL RECEIVE 70 XP FOR THE INVESTIGATION.**"
- The truth: your business is failing because of economics, and so will the next one, and there is no ghost
  to blame or exorcise. The lie: it's a curse, it's not your fault, it's not the market, it's a monster.
- One of those is TRUE. The other one is KINDER. And the game gives you the same 70 XP for both (cf. our
  conscience system + Megaton law + no-clean-answer).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- IT'S A JOKE THAT ONLY LANDS IF YOU'RE ALREADY LISTENING: the entire thesis rides on a SKILL CHECK
  (Shivers 14) inside an optional sub-conversation of an optional side quest. Fail the check or skip the
  question and it's just "haha the ghost was a lady." LESSON (~9th confirmation this run): the thesis cannot
  live behind a check. If our [READ] hides the conclusion behind a roll, most players get the setup and
  never the punchline.
- THE GATING IS PURE ADVENTURE-GAME NONSENSE: to talk to a woman you can hear, you must find a flashlight in
  a specific car, go to a specific basement, and pass a Physical Instrument check to SHOUT INTO A FURNACE.
  It's characterful and it's also a lock-and-key puzzle wearing a coat. LESSON: our quest factory can use
  ritual absurdity, but the path has to stay legible or players just leave.
- NOTHING CHANGES: whatever you tell Plaisance, the building stays doomed, the businesses keep failing, and
  the district keeps dying. The quest has no mechanical consequence at all. LESSON (double-edged): that's
  the POINT thematically (you can't exorcise an economy), and it's also why some players feel nothing —
  our persistent consequence should let at least the WORLD register what you learned.
- THE BEST LINE IS A DEAD END: you can accuse Neha of being a malignant entity to her face and she just...
  handles it. The scene is glorious and completely inert. LESSON: comedy without a consequence is still
  worth writing, but it's not a quest, it's a bit.
- IT'S THE LEAST-PLAYED GREAT QUEST IN THE GAME: it's behind a curtain, in a bookstore, gated by ignoring a
  woman's pleas, and it's optional. Its premise is the game's actual thesis. LESSON: put the thesis on the
  critical path.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the curse that is a market + the ghost you let her keep
===============================================================================
This is the best short quest ever written about our exact premise: an ECONOMIC apocalypse, experienced from
the inside, by people who can only understand it as a haunting. It's 70 XP, one building, and it's the whole
thesis.
- W1/W2 (the curse IS the economy + the supernatural resolves into real estate — our POST-ECONOMIC-COLLAPSE
  PREMISE + [READ]): the top port, and it's the most direct hit in the whole questbook. Plaisance is not
  stupid. She has real data: every business in that building fails, every time, for years. That IS an
  anomaly and there IS a malignant entity destroying these people — it's just a MARKET, and markets are
  invisible, so the mind reaches for a ghost. Then the whole mystery collapses on one Shivers check: why
  hasn't NEHA's business failed? "**Because it's not in the same building as the others.**" A property line.
  Punk — our Vegas died of an ECONOMIC collapse. Our districts should be full of people who have folk
  explanations for structural forces: the cursed block, the unlucky corner, the building that eats
  businesses. And our [READ] should be the thing that turns a curse back into a cause (ties our
  post-economic-collapse premise + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + district + city-builder + faction web +
  survival accounting + AMALGAMATION; a top-tier port).
- W9/W10 (you choose whether she keeps the ghost + truth and lie pay the same — our conscience system +
  Megaton law): STEAL this exactly. You can tell Plaisance the truth (economics; it will happen again; there
  is nothing to exorcise) or you can tell her the curse is real (it's not your fault; it's a monster; a
  monster can theoretically be beaten). One is TRUE. **The other is KINDER.** And the game pays **70 XP**
  either way — it has no mechanical opinion. That's the Ultima VII/Rust answer (Q83/Q93) at quest scale: no
  karma, no reward differential, just you and what you decided to say to a frightened woman. Our conscience
  system should have dozens of these (ties our conscience system + Megaton law + no-clean-answer + standing +
  [READ] + district + faction web).
- W5/W6/W7 (the netherworld is an abandoned gym + you summon the ghost by yelling into a furnace + the
  entity asks if you're here for your dice — our district + prefab factory + entities): bank all three as
  the staging package. (a) Harry says "**IT'S THE NETHERWORLD...**" and it's a failed gym: a shot put ball, a
  60kg barbell, abandoned offices, a planning board. Somebody's business, stopped mid-sentence. That's
  EXACTLY what our HD tile repo and prefab factory are for — the horror is that it's just a room where
  something ended. (b) The chimney/furnace route is our district's best trick available for free: the
  building has a voice in it because a PERSON is in it, and sound travels through infrastructure. (c) And
  the anticlimax — "**the workshop of Neha, the Novelty Dicemaker, who asks whether you are here to pick up
  some dice**" — is the tone we want: post-apocalyptic Vegas full of people just trying to run their small
  business inside the catastrophe (ties our district + HD tile repo + prefab factory + entities/spawning +
  scheduler + [READ] + ART PHILOSOPHY + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope).
- W3/W4 (you lie to get the job + the hook is ignoring a woman begging you to stop — our standing +
  conscience system): bank both. (a) To be ALLOWED to help her, Harry passes a **DRAMA (11)** check to
  convince her he's a **psychic investigator.** He fabricates the exact credential her worldview requires.
  Our standing system should let the dynasty claim expertise it doesn't have, and the check should be a
  PERFORMANCE skill, not a knowledge one. (b) And the quest OPENS by ignoring a woman pleading with you to
  leave her curtains alone. The content costs a small unkindness to unlock. That's a real design position
  most games are too polite to take (ties our standing + conscience system + entities + quest factory +
  faction web).
- W8 (the reward for solving it is an IDEA — our perks): bank the mechanic. You don't get a gun. You get
  **THE PRECARIOUS WORLD** installed permanently in your head. Our PERKS system is a known gap (never fully
  documented) and this is a shape for it: perks earned by DEDUCTION, not by combat — an idea you understood
  becomes a permanent modifier. Fits [READ] natively (ties our perks + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded +
  standing + conscience system, Caves of Qud Q56, Golden Idol Q54).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) ~9TH CONFIRMATION — THE THESIS CANNOT LIVE BEHIND A CHECK; the entire point of this
  quest is one Shivers 14 roll in an optional sub-conversation, and failing it leaves you with "haha the
  ghost was a lady"; if our [READ] gates the conclusion behind a roll, most players get the setup and never
  the punchline; (b) ritual absurdity is characterful but the PATH has to stay legible (flashlight in a
  specific car → specific basement → shout into a furnace); (c) NOTHING CHANGES — the building stays doomed
  either way, which is thematically correct and mechanically inert; our persistent consequence should let
  the WORLD register what the dynasty learned even when it can't fix it; (d) comedy without consequence is a
  bit, not a quest; and (e) the least-played great quest in the game is the one carrying its thesis — put
  the thesis on the critical path.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**PLAISANCE** — bookstore owner, devout, drowning. Wants the CURSE to be real, because a curse can be exorcised and an economy cannot. Will trade: a small fee and her fragile composure. Will never say: that she has watched the numbers and knows; the curse is the story she can afford. FUNCTION: the client whose real request is for a BETTER EXPLANATION than the true one.

**THE MALIGNANT ENTITY (NEHA)** — a woman at a workbench making dice out of ground bone, mildly annoyed at being called malignant. Wants: to finish her order. FUNCTION: the anticlimax as thesis — the "entity" is a tenant, the horror is the rent.

**THE HARBINGER (the curse itself / the building's failure pattern)** — wants nothing; it is vacancy math wearing a ghost costume. FUNCTION: the antagonist that dissolves under a control case.

**HARRY & KIM** — want the case closed; Kim additionally wants Harry to not bill a grieving shopkeeper for a séance. FUNCTION: the investigator whose SKILLS keep testifying against the supernatural reading (Shivers, the control case) while his heart keeps voting for it.
