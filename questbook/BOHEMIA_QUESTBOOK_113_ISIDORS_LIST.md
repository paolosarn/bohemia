# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 113: YOUR FATHER LEFT YOU A LIST OF CHILDREN / SAVE THEM WITH WHAT YOU HAVE (Isidor's List / The Bound, Pathologic 2)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where your murdered father's legacy turns out to be a scrap of
paper with **eight names** on it, six of them children, and the game spends twelve days making you do
arithmetic on which of them gets the medicine. Complete event flow, the List, the eight, the rival healers'
Bound, the tenth-day infection, Sticky's refusal, the endings, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports.
STARTLINGLY relevant to our fold, our MEDICINE currency, our survival accounting, our companions, our
death-math, + our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope. Ice-Pick Lodge. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No
Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Isidor's List" / the Bound, the through-line of the Haruspex route in Pathologic 2 (2019). You are
Artemy Burakh, a surgeon, returning home to a plague town where your father has been murdered and everyone
thinks you did it. The Haruspex "**BEGINS WITH 30% HEALTH, 20% REPUTATION, 45% HUNGER, AND 55%
EXHAUSTION**" — "**DUE TO HIS LOW STARTING REPUTATION, TOWNSFOLK WILL ATTACK HIM ON SIGHT, AND HE WILL BE
UNABLE TO PURCHASE ITEMS FROM ANY SHOPKEEPERS EXCEPT FOR BAD GRIEF.**"

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE INHERITANCE IS A LIST OF NAMES (the premise — STARTLINGLY our fold + generational persistence):
  "**FOLLOWING ISIDOR BURAKH'S FUNERAL, ASPITY WILL ASK THE HARUSPEX TO RETURN TO HER HOSPICE WITH HER TO
  DISCUSS HIS FATHER. THERE, THE HARUSPEX WILL LEARN OF HIS FATHER'S LIST; **A PIECE OF PAPER WITH EIGHT
  NAMES WRITTEN ON IT.** THE EIGHT NAMES ARE VICTORIA OLGIMSKAYA (KNOWN MORE COMMONLY AS CAPELLA), KHAN,
  STICKY, MURKY, NOTKIN, GRACE, TAYA TYCHEEK, AND **A MYSTERIOUS STEPPE SYMBOL KNOWN ONLY AS THE
  EIGHTH.**"
  - Your father the healer was murdered. What he left you isn't a house, a fortune, or a cure. **It's a
    list of children he'd decided were worth keeping alive**, and no explanation.
  - And nobody can tell you why: "**AS SOME OF THOSE NAMES ON THE LIST ARE FAMILIAR TO THE HARUSPEX, HE GOES
    TO SPEAK WITH EITHER NOTKIN OR GRACE ABOUT THEIR INVOLVEMENT IN THE LIST. **NEITHER OF THEM CAN EXPLAIN
    IT BEYOND THAT THEY USED TO SEE ISIDOR BURAKH FREQUENTLY**" (STARTLINGLY our fold + [READ] +
    generational persistence; the inheritance as an unexplained obligation).
- AND THEN THE PLAGUE PICKS THEM ALL OFF AT ONCE (the trap — direct for our MEDICINE currency): "**ON THE
  TENTH DAY SINCE THE HARUSPEX'S RETURN, STICKY WILL BE INFECTED WITH SAND PLAGUE **ALONGSIDE THE REST OF
  ISIDOR'S LIST.**"
  - Not one at a time. **All of them.** On day ten, your father's entire list gets sick, and you have a
    finite number of doses, and you are one man crossing a town on foot (STARTLINGLY our MEDICINE currency +
    survival accounting + logistics + death-math; the triage that is the quest).
- AND THE OTHER TWO DOCTORS HAVE THEIR OWN LISTS (the structure — STARTLINGLY our faction web + conscience
  system): "**WHEN ALL OF THE HARUSPEX'S OWN BOUND **AND ANOTHER HEALER'S BOUND** ARE ALL CURED, HE WILL
  RECEIVE A LETTER FROM THE POWERS THAT BE.**"
  - "**IF THE HARUSPEX CURES **EVERY SINGLE BOUND**, THEN HE WILL RECEIVE ANOTHER LETTER... INVITING HIM TO
    THE THEATRE. INSIDE THE THEATRE, HE MAY TALK TO TWO PEOPLE **REPRESENTING THE DEVELOPERS OF
    PATHOLOGIC.**"
  - The reward for saving everyone in town is that **the developers come out on stage to talk to you.** Not
    a good ending. Not loot. The authors, in person (cf. our ART PHILOSOPHY + Megaton law).

- The Haruspex has to kill guards to get his own front-door key: "**TWO GUARDS WILL BE STANDING AT THE
  ENTRANCE TO THE TERMITARY BLOCK. ATTEMPTING TO SPEAK WITH EITHER OF THEM CAUSES THEM TO ATTACK. THE

- "**AFTER DEALING WITH THE RATS, THE HARUSPEX FINDS A BOY NAMED STICKY **STANDING ON THE TABLE IN THE FAR

  He has a key you don't: "**HE WAS ABLE TO ACCESS THE HARUSPEX'S LAIR WITHOUT A KEY, IMPLYING THAT HE HAS

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE LOGISTICS ARE THE ENEMY AND THE GAME KNOWS IT: a guide, exhausted — "**THE QUESTS TODAY ARE AN ABSOLUTE
  MESS, REQUIRING YOU TO GO ALL OVER TOWN, OFTEN VISITING THE SAME PLACE MULTIPLE TIMES. **THERE'S A REASON
  PATHOLOGISTICS REFERRED TO THIS DAY AS THE IDITAROD.**" LESSON (ours, hard): "walking across town is the
  theme" is true AND our 120-BPM grid + logistics must make the walk mean something every time, or it's a
  tax.
- THE OPTIMAL PLAY IS SEQUENCE-BREAKING: the same guide brags — "**SIMPLE, EASY, AND YOU CAN DO IT IN 20
  MINUTES OR YOUR STEPPEBUX BACK FREE. **THIS SKIPS COMPLETING ALL THE SIDE QUESTS AND SEQUENCE BREAKS QUEST
  FLAGGING CONSIDERABLY.**" A twelve-hour day of moral triage, speedrun in 20 minutes. LESSON: if your
  quest flags can be outrun, your theme can be outrun.
- IT DEPICTS CHILD DEATH GRAPHICALLY AND WARNS YOU MID-WALKTHROUGH: "**WHEN YOU RETURN TO THE LAIR IN THE
  FACTORY FOR THE FIRST TIME TODAY, YOU'LL BE TREATED TO A VISION... **NOTE THAT THIS INVOLVES A FAIRLY
  GRAPHIC DEPICTION OF CHILD DEATH.**" LESSON (ours, firm): our GDD's line is despair with a thread of hope
  and a life lesson underneath **without preaching** — we can put children at risk without putting the
  camera on it.
- "THERE ARE NO MATERIAL REWARDS FOR THIS QUEST" IS THE MOST COMMON SENTENCE ON THE WIKI: intentional and
  brutal. LESSON (double-edged): a game that never pays you is honest and it also means players quit before
  the part that pays in meaning — our quest factory needs the reward to be SOMETHING, even if it's not loot.
- THE LIST IS NEVER EXPLAINED IN TIME: "**NEITHER OF THEM CAN EXPLAIN IT BEYOND THAT THEY USED TO SEE ISIDOR
  BURAKH FREQUENTLY.**" You spend twelve days protecting people for reasons you learn at the end, if at all.
  LESSON: mystery as motivation works once; our fold needs the dynasty to have at least one reason it can
  ACT on.
- THE BEST ENDING'S REWARD IS A LECTURE: cure every Bound in town and "**HE MAY TALK TO TWO PEOPLE
  REPRESENTING THE DEVELOPERS OF PATHOLOGIC.**" LESSON (sharp, ours): if your maximum-effort reward is the
  authors explaining themselves, you've made the game's thesis into a trophy — our ART PHILOSOPHY says the
  lesson lives UNDER the design, never on a podium.
