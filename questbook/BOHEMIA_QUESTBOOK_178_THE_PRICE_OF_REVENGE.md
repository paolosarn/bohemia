**Game:** Mass Effect 2 (2010)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** Zaeed Massani's loyalty mission ("The Price of Revenge") — an old mercenary drags the squad to a refinery to kill the partner who betrayed him twenty years ago, and when the vendetta puts hostage workers in a fire, the player has to choose whether one man's twenty-year grudge is worth the lives it's burning through right now.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**An old mercenary who has organized twenty years of his life around killing the partner who betrayed and nearly murdered him finally gets his shot — and the shot sets a refinery on fire with workers trapped inside, forcing a choice between finishing the revenge and saving the strangers the revenge is willing to burn.**

Zaeed Massani co-founded the Blue Suns mercenary company with a man named Vido Santiago. Vido betrayed him — shot him in the head, left him for dead, took the company. Zaeed survived, and spent twenty years and every credit hunting the man down. His loyalty mission is the culmination: Vido is at a refinery, and Zaeed intends to kill him. But Zaeed's opening move — his single-minded pursuit — starts a fire, and refinery workers are trapped in the blaze. Vido is escaping. The player cannot both chase Vido and save the workers; the revenge and the rescue are on the same clock. Zaeed does not care about the workers. Twenty years have burned everything human out of the vendetta except the target. He will walk over the hostages to reach Vido, and he wants the player to walk with him.

The machine is that a private grudge has a public body count, and the man carrying the grudge has stopped being able to see it. Zaeed's wound is real — he WAS betrayed, he WAS shot, the revenge is EARNED in every way that matters to him. But earned revenge still spends lives that never agreed to pay for it, and the refinery fire makes the abstract cost concrete: these specific workers, dying now, for a grudge that predates them by decades. The player chooses whether the revenge is worth the strangers, and the choice fractures Zaeed's loyalty on the outcome — save the workers and lose Vido (Zaeed rages, then respects it, if the player's standing is high enough), or chase Vido and let the workers burn (the revenge completed, the price paid by people who never chose to). And the quest refuses to make either clean: the betrayer may live, or the innocents may die, and both are the cost of a grudge that outlived the wound that started it.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** a revenge can be completely earned and still cost more than it's worth, because the debt is owed to you but the price gets paid by whoever's standing between you and the target — and twenty years of grudge can burn the part of you that used to be able to see them.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### ZAEED MASSANI — the man who wouldn't let it go
- **What he wants (stated):** to kill Vido Santiago; the revenge he's spent twenty years and everything he has chasing.
- **What he wants (real):** to close the wound that Vido left — the bullet in the head, the company stolen, the twenty years owed.
- **What he'll trade:** the workers, the mission, his own life — everything is downstream of the target.
- **What he will never say out loud:** that the grudge has eaten the man who started it, and there may be nothing left after Vido dies.
- **Function:** THE GRUDGE-CARRIER whose earned revenge has a body count he can no longer see.

### VIDO SANTIAGO — the betrayer, escaping
- **What he wants:** to get away; to keep the company and the life he stole.
- **Function:** THE TARGET: the earned revenge's object, whose death is worth (to Zaeed) any number of strangers.

### THE REFINERY WORKERS — the price nobody chose
- Hostages trapped in the fire Zaeed's pursuit started; strangers who never agreed to pay for his grudge.
- **Function:** THE BODY COUNT: the concrete cost of the vendetta, dying now for a debt that predates them.

### THE PLAYER (Shepard) — the one who weighs it
- **What they want:** to decide whether the revenge is worth the strangers — and to hold Zaeed's loyalty on the answer.
- **Function:** THE SCALE: the one who puts the earned grudge against the innocent lives and chooses.

### THE FIRE — the clock that forces the choice
- The blaze that makes revenge and rescue mutually exclusive; the same clock for both.
- **Function:** THE FORCING FUNCTION: the mechanism that turns an abstract cost into a specific, timed, either-or.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes — save the workers (Vido escapes) or chase Vido (the workers burn) — with Zaeed's loyalty gated on standing in the save-the-workers branch, the revenge weighed against strangers who never chose to pay for it.**

- **SAVE THE WORKERS:** the hostages pulled from the fire; Vido escapes; the earned revenge denied for the lives it would spend. WRITES: the strangers live; the grudge open; Zaeed's loyalty held only if standing carries the human choice.
- **CHASE VIDO:** the target reached; the workers burn; the revenge completed at the innocents' price. WRITES: the wound closed; the body count paid by people who predated the grudge; the cost banked.
- **THE EARNED-BUT-COSTLY CONSTANT:** in both branches, the revenge is genuinely earned and genuinely expensive — the quest never pretends Zaeed wasn't wronged, only that being wronged doesn't make the price free.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the branch is grudge-versus-strangers — two answers to "what is an earned revenge worth in lives that never agreed to pay," and the fire refuses to let the player have both the vengeance and the clean hands.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE WORKERS ARE ABSTRACT, SO THE BODY COUNT DOESN'T WEIGH ENOUGH.** The refinery hostages are faceless mission-geometry — a number in a fire, not people — so the profound scale (an earned grudge against innocent lives) tips too easily toward whichever the player already preferred, because the cost side has no face.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** when a choice weighs a grudge against strangers, the strangers must have ENOUGH face that their deaths weigh — a name, a plea, a body the player has to walk past — so the scale is real. An abstract cost is a thumb on the scale toward whatever the player wanted anyway (#26's the-ledger-must-have-faces law).

**F2 — ZAEED'S LOYALTY GATING ON STANDING CHEAPENS THE MORAL CHOICE.** Whether Zaeed forgives the player for saving the workers depends on a hidden Paragon/Renegade threshold, so the game turns a genuine ethical fork into a stat check — the "right" choice punished unless the player pre-invested points, which reads the human decision as a skill gate.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a moral choice's relational consequence must follow from the CHOICE and its handling, not a hidden stat threshold — the character responding to what the player did and said, not to an invisible number — so the ethics aren't paywalled behind a build. A conscience gated by points is a stat check wearing a soul (the #169 earned-not-bought law).

**F3 — THE REVENGE COMPLETES TOO CLEANLY IF YOU CHASE VIDO.** Chasing Vido and letting the workers burn closes the grudge with little felt aftermath — the body count is stated, not carried — so the "earned revenge still costs" idea is undercut by a completion that feels mostly like a win, with the price a footnote.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a revenge is completed at innocents' expense, the cost must be CARRIED after — the body count returning, the closed grudge sitting heavier than expected — so the completion isn't a clean win. A revenge whose price vanishes at the credits was never really weighed (#170's the-cost-must-return law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE REVENGE IS EARNED. Zaeed was betrayed, shot, robbed of twenty years — the grudge is legitimate in every way, which is exactly what makes its cost hard to dismiss.
W2. THE COST IS INNOCENT. The refinery workers never agreed to pay for Zaeed's grudge — the body count is strangers, which is the death-math (#26) at its sharpest: whose life the debt is willing to spend.
W3. THE FIRE FORCES THE CHOICE. Revenge and rescue on the same clock — the blaze turns an abstract cost into a specific, timed, either-or the player cannot dodge.
W4. THE GRUDGE HAS EATEN THE MAN. Twenty years have burned the human out of the vendetta; Zaeed can't see the workers, which is the warning the quest carries — what a grudge does to the one who holds it too long.
W5. THE PLAYER IS THE SCALE. Shepard puts the earned revenge against the innocent lives and chooses — the moral weight is handed to the player, not resolved by the story.
W6. NEITHER OUTCOME IS CLEAN. The betrayer lives or the innocents die — the quest refuses a costless win, which is the honesty of an earned-but-expensive revenge.
W7. THE CLEAN GRUDGE CAN'T COEXIST WITH CLEAN HANDS. The removed "have both" verb is the quest's spine: an earned revenge still forces you to choose what it's worth.
W8. THE LOYALTY RIDES THE CHOICE. Zaeed's respect (or rage) follows whether the player forced the human decision — the relationship weighs the ethics, not just the outcome.
W9. THE WOUND IS OLD AND THE VICTIMS ARE NEW. The grudge predates the workers by decades — the price is paid by people who had nothing to do with the wound, which is what makes vendetta so dangerous.
W10. IT IS THE UNRECORDED LEDGER IN ONE MAN. A private revenge with a body count nobody chose — Bohemia's whole question of whose lives a debt is allowed to spend, in one old mercenary's grudge.
