# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #173 — "KARLACH'S HEART (THE ENGINE IN HER CHEST WILL KILL HER EITHER WAY)"
**Game:** Baldur's Gate 3 (2023)
**Studio:** Larian Studios
**Quest:** Karlach's companion arc — a warrior escaped from hell with an infernal engine grafted into her chest that is slowly burning her alive, whose whole quest is the search for a fix that keeps not fully working, ending in a choice between three bad endings for a woman who just wants to live and be touched.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Baldur's Gate 3 catalogue; corpus anchor Q16 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia's death-math and its Liberate ending need the model of a character whose body is a countdown, whose every "cure" is a patch not a fix, and whose ending is choosing which unbearable option hurts least — the terminal-illness dignity of #21/#34/#162 in a companion you love. Karlach is the specimen: a warm, alive, touch-starved woman with an engine that will kill her, offered three endings that are all versions of dying, and the quest is whether the player can give her a good death when they can't give her a life. It is the honest-loss of SOMA (#34) with a heartbeat, and the "no cure, only which cost" fork our hardcore survival core runs on.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A warrior escapes hell with an infernal engine grafted where her heart was — it keeps her alive and is slowly cooking her to death, so hot she cannot be touched without burning — and her whole arc is a search for a fix that keeps almost-working, ending in three choices that are all forms of dying, for a woman whose only wish is to live and be held.**

Karlach was sold to a hell-lord, her heart replaced with an infernal engine, and used as a soldier for a decade before escaping to the surface. The engine keeps her alive and is destroying her: it runs too hot, burning her from the inside, and so hot on the outside that she cannot touch or be touched without harming — a woman starved for contact who is physically unable to have it. Her companion arc is the search for a solution. Each apparent fix is a patch: an upgrade that cools her enough to touch, but not enough to save; a lead that offers hope and delivers a partial reprieve; a mechanic (Dammon the infernal smith) who buys her time, not a life. The engine cannot be truly repaired on the surface — the materials, the infernal engineering, the hell-tech are beyond the world's reach — so every step forward is a slower death, not a stopped one.

The machine is a body that is a countdown, and an ending that offers only versions of loss. At the game's end, Karlach faces three options, all bad: return to hell (where the engine can be maintained, but she goes back to the place that enslaved her, to fight for survival in the nine hells forever); die on the surface (let the engine burn out, and pass in the world she loves, among the people she loves, but pass); or, in an added path, become a mind flayer to survive (keep living, but not as herself). There is no ending where Karlach gets what she wants — to live, whole, on the surface, and be touched. The quest's devastation is that the player, who can fix so much in this game, cannot fix her; the death-math is unbeatable, and the only agency left is helping her choose which unbearable thing to bear, and being with her while she bears it.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** sometimes you cannot give the person you love a life, only a choice between deaths, and the only thing left to give is to be there, honestly, for the one they pick — because the cruelest lie would be pretending there was a cure.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### KARLACH — the woman the engine is killing
- **What she wants:** to live, on the surface, whole, and to be touched — the simplest wish, and the one thing she cannot have.
- **What she'll trade:** anything for a real fix, and, failing that, a good death among the people she loves.
- **What she will never say out loud (easily):** the terror under the cheer; a decade in hell taught her to be loud so no one sees the fear.
- **Function:** THE COUNTDOWN WITH A HEARTBEAT — a warm, alive, touch-starved woman whose body is a terminal clock.

### DAMMON — the smith who buys time, not a life
- **What he wants:** to help Karlach as much as the surface world's reach allows.
- **What he'll trade:** patches — an upgrade that cools her enough to touch, a reprieve, never a cure.
- **Function:** THE PARTIAL FIX: the mechanic whose every success is a slower death, honest about the limit.

### THE INFERNAL ENGINE — the body as a bomb
- The hell-tech grafted where her heart was, keeping her alive and cooking her.
- **Function:** THE DEATH-MATH: the unbeatable clock, the cure that doesn't exist on the surface.

### THE PLAYER — the one who can fix everything but this
- **What they want:** to save Karlach, and cannot; only to help her choose and be with her.
- **Function:** THE HELPLESS LOVE: the agency that fixes so much, meeting the one thing it can't, left only with presence.

### THE THREE ENDINGS — the choice between deaths
- Return to hell, die on the surface, or become a mind flayer.
- **Function:** THE FORK that is all loss: no ending gives her what she wants, only which unbearable thing she bears.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 3 terminal endings — return to hell, die on the surface, become a mind flayer — all forms of loss, with no ending giving Karlach the life she wants, gated by the player's honesty and the choices along the way.**

- **RETURN TO HELL:** Karlach survives, the engine maintained, but goes back to the place that enslaved her, to fight for existence in the nine hells forever. WRITES: alive, and lost to the world she loved; survival as exile.
- **DIE ON THE SURFACE:** Karlach lets the engine burn out and passes among the people she loves, held at last. WRITES: dead, and free, and home; the honest loss (the SOMA/Liberate ending with a heartbeat).
- **BECOME A MIND FLAYER:** Karlach survives by ceasing to be herself. WRITES: alive, and not the woman the player loved; the cost of refusing the death-math.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** every ending is a version of losing her; the branch is which unbearable thing she bears, and the player's real role is honesty about the absence of a cure and presence for the choice she makes.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE "GOOD" ENDING WAS PATCHED IN, EXPOSING THE DESIGN'S HESITATION.** Karlach originally shipped with only the bleak endings; a later patch added a path to more time (the mind-flayer route, the Avernus survival), which relieved players but also revealed the design flinching from its own honesty — the death-math was softened after the fact, so the terminal-loss theme is undercut by the studio's own reluctance to hold it.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a character's arc is honest terminal loss, the design must HOLD it — not patch in a survival route because players hurt — because the courage of the ending is the point. Softening the death-math after the fact tells the player the loss wasn't real. Commit to the honest ending (the #34 SOMA structural-honesty law; the #21 don't-flinch law).

**F2 — THE PATCHES-NOT-A-FIX STRUCTURE ISN'T FELT AS A COUNTDOWN.** Karlach's engine is a terminal clock, but the game never makes the player FEEL the countdown — there's no visible worsening, no sense of time running out, so the "every fix is a slower death" horror is told through dialogue rather than experienced as mounting dread. The bomb doesn't tick.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a body-as-countdown must be FELT — visible worsening, a sense of time draining, the death-math legible as it advances — so the terminal stakes land as dread, not exposition. A clock the player can't hear ticking isn't a countdown (the #21 slow-onset-dread law).

**F3 — THE TOUCH-STARVATION IS UNDERUSED MECHANICALLY.** The engine making Karlach unable to be touched is her most poignant detail — a woman who wants contact and burns anyone who tries — but it's mostly a dialogue note; the game rarely makes the player feel the barrier, so the intimacy-denied tragedy lands lighter than the concept. The distance isn't dramatized.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a character's central deprivation (unable to be touched, unable to be known) must be DRAMATIZED, not just stated — moments where the player feels the barrier, reaches and cannot cross it — so the deprivation is experienced. The tragedy of the distance must be felt across the distance (the #158 specific-deprivation law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. HER BODY IS A COUNTDOWN. The engine keeps Karlach alive and kills her — a terminal clock in a companion you love. The death-math is the arc, and it is unbeatable, which is what makes it devastating.
W2. EVERY FIX IS A PATCH. The cures buy time, never a life; each step forward is a slower death. The hope keeps almost-working, which is crueler than no hope at all.
W3. SHE CANNOT BE TOUCHED. A woman starved for contact who burns anyone who tries — the engine's cruelty is intimate, a deprivation of the simplest human thing.
W4. THE PLAYER CAN FIX EVERYTHING BUT HER. In a game where the player solves so much, the one thing they cannot solve is the person they love — the helplessness is the point, and it lands because everything else is fixable.
W5. THE ENDINGS ARE ALL LOSS. Return to hell, die on the surface, become a mind flayer — no ending gives her what she wants. The fork is not a choice between good and bad but between unbearable and unbearable.
W6. THE HONEST DEATH IS AN OPTION. Dying on the surface, among the loved, held at last — the SOMA/Liberate ending with a heartbeat: refusing the false hope, choosing the honest loss.
W7. THE CHEER IS ARMOR. Karlach is loud and warm because a decade in hell taught her to hide the fear — the coping surface (#162) over a terror the arc lets crack.
W8. THE ONLY GIFT LEFT IS PRESENCE. When you cannot fix her, the only thing to give is to be there, honestly, for the ending she chooses — the quest's answer to helplessness is company, not a cure.
W9. THE KINDEST LIE IS THE PROMISE OF A FIX. "We'll find a cure" is the tempting comfort, and the arc teaches that the honest thing is to stop telling it — the mercy is truth, not false hope.
W10. YOU CHOOSE WHICH LOSS, NOT WHETHER. The agency the quest leaves is helping her pick which unbearable thing to bear — the death-math has no winning setting, and the dignity is in the choosing.
