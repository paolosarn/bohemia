# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 63: THE COMPANION YOU CHOOSE AN ENDING FOR / DIVERGENT BITTERSWEET FINALES (Cyberpunk 2077: Judy + the endings)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the mundane-intimacy-builds-attachment craft, "I was picking an ending
for Judy not V," values-alignment (she stays only if you honor who she is), the Sun/Star/Devil/Temperance/
Tower endings diverging hard, the bittersweet no-clean-win tone, the reactive credits-voicemail epilogue,
the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the MODERN gold standard for a companion relationship that
feels emotionally REAL + endings that diverge hard on WHO you chose to be. DIRECTLY relevant to our
companions, Megaton law, and the Liberate/Respect/Become finales. CD Projekt Red. Reference only; Paolo
does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Cyberpunk 2077 (CDPR, 2020; Phantom Liberty 2023). An open-world RPG in Night City. V, a merc dying
with rockstar-terrorist Johnny Silverhand's ghost in their head, races to save their own life. Judy
Alvarez (a braindance techie of the Mox) is its most beloved romance; the game's 5+ ENDINGS (Sun, Star,
Devil, Temperance, Tower, + secret) diverge hard on V's choices, relationships, and who V became. A modern
narrative-RPG benchmark despite a rocky launch.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- A COMPANION SO REAL YOU CHOOSE THE ENDING FOR THEM, NOT YOURSELF (the emotional peak): the widely-shared
  player experience — "by the end, I wasn't picking an ending for V, I was picking an ending for JUDY." The
  relationship becomes so emotionally real that the player's END-GAME decision is driven by care for the
  COMPANION's fate, not the protagonist's. That's the ceiling of companion writing: the NPC's wellbeing
  outweighs your own avatar's (cf. BG2/DA:O/ME2 companions Q43/Q42/Q06; the modern gold standard).
- ATTACHMENT BUILT FROM MUNDANE INTIMACY (the craft, not melodrama): the bond forms through QUIET, ordinary
  moments — a diving trip, comforting her in a bathroom, "just our day," the "comfortable intimacy of an
  actual relationship." "These small interactions steadily increase affection just like in real life."
  Attachment is engineered through MUNDANE presence + emotional availability, NOT big dramatic set-pieces —
  the everyday IS the romance (cf. RDR2 camp-life Q10, our slow-build relationship design; intimacy from
  the ordinary).
- ENDINGS DIVERGE ON WHO YOU CHOSE TO BE (+ values-alignment): the 5+ endings (Sun/Star/Devil/Temperance/
  Tower) diverge HARD — who lives, who dies, V's reputation, whether relationships survive, whether there's
  hope for a cure. And Judy STAYS only if you honored HER VALUES (she wants to LEAVE Night City — so she
  joins you only in the Star ending that leaves; picking a path against her values LOSES her). The finale
  reflects your accumulated identity + whether you were true to those you love (cf. Chrono choice-endings
  Q50, SH2 behavior-ending Q35, our Megaton/finales; endings as a verdict on who you became).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how a real romance + divergent endings work)
===============================================================================

### THE SLOW BUILD THROUGH THE MAIN PLOT (woven in, not bolted on — key craft)
- Judy enters through the MAIN story (Evelyn's disappearance, the braindance) so EVERY player meets her +
  builds some history — the romance GROWS from shared plot events, not a separate dating minigame. The
  relationship is load-bearing to the narrative, and optional romance DEEPENS an already-real bond (cf.
  BG2 companions-in-the-plot Q43, our companions-woven-into-the-story; integrated, not sidecar).
### GATED BY CONSISTENT, VALUES-BASED CHOICES (you earn it by being right for her)
- The romance requires choices ALIGNED WITH JUDY'S VALUES across her questline (e.g. in Pisces: reject
  Maiko's exploitative deal, don't take the bribe, spare her) — betray her values + you're locked out.
  The bond is EARNED by consistently being someone she'd love, not by picking "romance" from a menu — a
  values-test, not a flirt-counter (cf. our standing/values-based relationships, Alpha Protocol reactivity
  Q24; earned by character-consistency).
### BODY/VOICE-BASED PREFERENCES, IDENTITY-RESPECTFUL (who characters desire)
- Characters have their OWN preferences (Judy romances a feminine-presenting V) — but decoupled from
  lifepath/genitalia in an identity-respectful way. NPCs have AGENCY over who they love; you can't romance
  everyone (cf. our NPCs-as-people-with-agency; characters aren't wish-fulfillment vending machines).
### THE 5+ DIVERGENT ENDINGS (the finale architecture — itemized)
- STAR: leave Night City with the Aldecaldos (+ Judy/Panam if romanced) — the most HOPEFUL, but ambiguous
  (V MIGHT survive, not will).
- SUN: become an Afterlife legend, go to space (V's own voice in the credits voicemail if Judy-romanced).
- DEVIL (Arasaka): trust the corp — secretly the most BITTER/hollow "safe" choice.
- TEMPERANCE: give your body to Johnny — you effectively die; romances ghosted.
- TOWER (Phantom Liberty): cured but at devastating cost — you lose everything/everyone.
- SECRET (Don't Fear the Reaper): storm Arasaka solo — a pure challenge, earned by specific dialogue.
- The endings differ in TONE, survivors, reputation, and which relationships endure — a real
  consequence-web, not a menu of skins (cf. Chrono ~13 endings Q50, our Liberate/Respect/Become).
### EVERY ENDING IS BITTERSWEET (no clean win — the tonal signature)
- "Almost all the endings have an element of tragedy" — even the "best" (Star) is checked with AMBIGUITY
  (V could survive, not will). There is no clean victory — the game's fatalistic theme (a merc's short,
  bright life) is baked into EVERY finale. The bittersweetness is the POINT, earned by the whole game (cf.
  NieR Q22, SOMA Q34, the Phasmid Q62, our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope + finales).
### THE REACTIVE CREDITS-VOICEMAIL EPILOGUE (a cheap, powerful reactivity device)
- During the credits, characters leave VOICEMAILS tailored to your ending + relationships — Judy's varies
  from loving to heartbroken to (in suicide) devastated ("you never realized how many friends you had").
  A lightweight, high-impact way to REFLECT your choices back + give closure per-relationship (cf. Alpha
  Protocol epilogue-reactivity Q24, our reactive epilogue potential; cheap + powerful).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- ROCKY LAUNCH / PROMISES VS DELIVERY: CP2077 launched broken (bugs, cut features, crashes) against
  enormous hype — the narrative excellence was nearly buried by technical failure + overpromising. LESSON
  (paramount for us): a great story dies if the game doesn't SHIP STABLE; do not overpromise, ship solid
  (our single-file/mobile discipline, Bloodlines ship-stable Q39; the cautionary tale of over-scope + over-
  hype).
- LIMITED ROMANCE POOL / BODY-GATED: only 4 romanceable companions, each body/voice-gated — many players
  can't romance their preferred character (Judy = feminine-V only). LESSON: character agency over who they
  love is GOOD, but a thin pool + hard gates leave players out; balance NPC agency with enough options
  (our companion roster breadth).
- HYPERSEXUALIZATION / MARKETING VS CHARACTER: Judy (+ the setting) was heavily hypersexualized in
  marketing ("two of every three images were thirst traps"), at odds with her actual thoughtful writing.
  LESSON (ours): don't let marketing/aesthetic sexualize a character the WRITING treats as a full person;
  respect the character (our tone/wellbeing rules; character over titillation).
- PARASOCIAL INTENSITY / OVER-ATTACHMENT: the romance is SO effective some players form intense parasocial
  attachments (the essay wrestles with "was I one of those men?"). LESSON (wellbeing-relevant): powerful
  digital intimacy can foster unhealthy over-attachment; we design emotional bonds that ENRICH without
  encouraging over-reliance/isolation (our wellbeing rules — don't foster unhealthy parasocial dependency;
  a real line for an emotionally-rich game).
- ENDINGS GATED BY SIDEQUEST COMPLETION (miss the content, miss the ending): which endings are AVAILABLE
  depends on finishing specific questlines (Panam's, the secret dialogue) — players can lock themselves out
  of the best finales unknowingly. LESSON: don't silently gate the best endings behind missable content
  without signposting (our finale-accessibility; make the paths reachable/legible).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the modern companion + divergent-bittersweet-finale model
===============================================================================
Cyberpunk's Judy + endings are the MODERN gold standard for our two goals here: emotionally-REAL companions
+ finales that diverge hard on who you became. It directly informs our companions, our Megaton law, and our
Liberate/Respect/Become finales, and its bittersweet tone matches ours exactly. Its launch is our sharpest
ship-stable cautionary tale.
- W1/W2/W4 (a companion you choose an ending FOR + mundane intimacy + woven into the plot): bank Judy as
  the ceiling for our COMPANIONS — Bohemia bonds should grow through MUNDANE, emotionally-available moments
  (shared district life, quiet beats — RDR2 Q10) woven into the MAIN story (everyone meets them), so real
  that the player's FINALE choice is driven by care for the COMPANION/dynasty, not just the avatar. This is
  the emotional target (ties BG2/DA:O/ME2 Q43/Q42/Q06, RDR2 Q10, our companions + fold; intimacy from the
  ordinary, integrated not sidecar).
- W3/W5 (endings diverge on who you became + values-alignment — our finales + standing): bank the model for
  our LIBERATE/RESPECT/BECOME finales — they should diverge HARD (who survives, the district's fate, the
  dynasty's reputation, which relationships endure) AND reflect whether the player honored their people's/
  companions' VALUES across the arc (a companion/faction stays or leaves based on consistent character, not
  a menu pick). Our multidimensional STANDING is the substrate (ties Chrono choice-endings Q50, SH2
  behavior-ending Q35, our Megaton law + Liberate/Respect/Become + standing; endings as a verdict on who
  the dynasty became).
- W6 (NPCs have agency over who they love): our companions/factions should have their OWN preferences +
  agency (who they'll ally with, love, trust) — not wish-fulfillment vending machines; you can't win
  everyone (ties our NPCs-as-people-with-agency, Gothic/Kenshi living-world Q51/Q55; the Pacifist Law
  means many paths, but people still choose).
- W7/W8 (a real consequence-web + every ending bittersweet — our tone): bank the finale architecture — a
  real consequence-web (not skins) where every ending is BITTERSWEET/ambiguous (no clean win), the
  fatalistic-but-hopeful theme baked into each. This IS our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope + our Liberate
  ("terrible mercy") / Respect ("hardest peace") / Become ("sympathetic damnation") registers — already
  aligned (ties NieR Q22, SOMA Q34, Phasmid Q62, our finales + tone; earn the ambiguity across the whole arc).
- W9 (the reactive credits-voicemail epilogue — steal directly): STEAL the tailored-voicemail epilogue — a
  cheap, high-impact way to REFLECT the player's choices back per-relationship at the end (our companions/
  factions/survivors leave messages tailored to the ending + their bond). Plays to our WRITING + voice
  strengths + our reactive-epilogue potential (ties Alpha Protocol epilogue Q24, our writing/music/voice +
  reactive epilogue; a direct, affordable steal).
- W10 (performance + writing sell it — our strengths): Judy works because of grounded WRITING + a standout
  VOICE performance — a strength-play for us (our writing + music/voice + New Voices Law). Bank: our
  companions' realness rides on writing + voice, our core strengths (ties Hades VO Q47, Disco writing Q05,
  our writing/music/voice pillars).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): SHIP STABLE + don't overpromise (CP2077's broken launch nearly buried its narrative —
  our single-file/mobile discipline + Bloodlines Q39; the sharpest over-scope/over-hype cautionary tale);
  balance NPC romance/alliance AGENCY with enough OPTIONS so players aren't left out (our companion roster
  breadth); don't let aesthetic/marketing SEXUALIZE characters the writing treats as full people (our tone/
  wellbeing rules — character over titillation); design emotional bonds that ENRICH without fostering
  unhealthy PARASOCIAL over-attachment/isolation (our wellbeing rules — a real line for an emotionally-rich
  game; don't encourage over-reliance); and don't silently gate the BEST finales behind missable content —
  make the paths reachable + legible (our finale-accessibility + no-brick-wall).
