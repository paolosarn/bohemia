# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 122: A CORPORATION BOUGHT A MAN'S DEATH AND YOU HOLD THE HAMMER (Sinnerman, Cyberpunk 2077)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the three-quest chain that starts with a contract to murder a man, turns
into dinner with him, and ends with you being handed a hammer and nails on a soundstage while a producer
smokes in the background — where the only real choice is your level of participation, and the game gives you
no way to stop it. Complete event flow, the contract, the dinner, the studio, the hammer, the bonus, the
honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION, our recorded-vs-unrecorded, our
conscience system, our PACIFIST LAW, our faction web, + our ART PHILOSOPHY. CD Projekt Red. Reference only;
Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

NOTE ON SUBJECT MATTER: this chain is about a filmed execution. The teardown is about CRAFT — how a game
constructs complicity and then refuses to let you off. Handled with restraint, per our ART PHILOSOPHY.

Quest chain: "Sinnerman" → "There Is A Light That Never Goes Out" → "They Won't Go When I Go," Cyberpunk 2077
(2020). "**JOSHUA HAS EFFECTIVELY BEEN GRANTED A DAY OF PAROLE BY A MEGACORPORATION **IN EXCHANGE FOR HIS
DEATH IN FRONT OF A CAMERA**, AS WELL AS SOME TWISTED MEASURE OF SALVATION.**"

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE GAME'S BEST CONTENT IS BEHIND SUSTAINED AGREEMENT WITH A SUICIDE: "**PLAYERS WILL HAVE TO 'YES' JOSHUA
  ALL THE WAY TO THE CROSS... **IT'S REMARKABLY EASY TO LOSE THE THREAD AND NOT WITNESS ONE OF THE GAME'S
  MOST MEMORABLE MOMENTS.**" LESSON (~17th confirmation, ours, and the sharpest version yet):
  theme-in-optional is missed — and this one is worse, because **the filter isn't skill or exploration, it's
  whether the player was willing to keep agreeing.** Our quest factory should never make the price of
  admission be *not objecting.*
- THE COMMUNITY CANNOT AGREE WHETHER THE CHOICE PAYS: one guide — "**SHE'LL CALL V AND EVEN GIVE THEM A BONUS
  ON TOP OF THE REGULAR PAY.**" Another — "**WHETHER YOU PARTICIPATE OR NOT DOESN'T CHANGE THE OUTCOME OR
  YOUR REWARDS.**" LESSON (~3rd confirmation with Q119/Q121): **if your consequence is so subtle that two
  guides contradict each other, it isn't a consequence** — our persistent consequence needs a legible tell.
- THERE IS NO SAVE OPTION AND SOME PLAYERS WILL NEVER FORGIVE THAT: "**THERE IS NO OPTION TO SAVE JOSHUA.**"
  LESSON (double-edged, and mostly correct here): removing the verb is honest AND an RPG that has trained
  you for 60 hours to solve things will read this as a bug.
- THE CHAIN NEEDS YOU TO BETRAY A GRIEVING MAN TO EXIST: "**V WILL NEED TO GO WITH JOSHUA AND THE NCPD RATHER
  THAN KILL HIM.**" Bill Jablonsky's grief is a gate. LESSON (ours): our conscience system should never make
  one person's legitimate pain into a *prerequisite* the player must dismiss to see content.
- THE TONE IS A HARD FILTER AND THE STUDIO KNEW: a horror-literate critic — "**I GREW UP ON ROMERO MOVIES...
  AND I CAN ALSO MAKE IT THROUGH THE LAST TEN MINUTES OF MARTYRS WITHOUT COLLAPSING. **BUT CYBERPUNK 2077'S
  'THEY WON'T GO WHEN I GO' QUEST LEFT ME FEELING JUST A LITTLE UNSETTLED.**" LESSON (ours, firm): our ART
  PHILOSOPHY carries weight **without rendering the wound** — we can build the room, the boredom, and the
  paycheck without the hammer.
- THE PAYMENT IS SLOW AS A JOKE AND IT UNDERCUTS THE SCENE: "**REWARD: €$MONEY (**DEPENDING ON WHETHER THE
  BRAINDANCE WAS CONSIDERED SUCCESSFUL OR NOT, RACHEL TAKES A REALLY LONG TIME TO SEND IT TO YOU**).**"
  LESSON: a gag about slow payment terms is *great* satire and it's also the last note of a man's death —
  know which one you want to land.
