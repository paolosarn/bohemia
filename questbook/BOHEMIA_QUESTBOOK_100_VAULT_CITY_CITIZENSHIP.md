# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 100: THE UTOPIA THAT WORKS / DON'T CALL THEM SLAVES (Vault City Citizenship, Fallout 2)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where the only functioning civilization in the wasteland offers
you membership, and the price is agreeing not to notice anything. Complete event flow, the citizenship test
you cannot pass, the servants, Lynette's exile triggers, the Gecko reactor and the repair option she never
asked for, the fake papers you can just buy, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our
FACTION WEB, our standing, our conscience system, our city-builder, + our post-economic-collapse premise.
Black Isle Studios (dialogue: Mark O'Green, Chris Avellone; original design: Jason Anderson). Reference only;
Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: the Vault City citizenship chain, Fallout 2 (1998). "**VAULT CITY IS A LARGE PLACE AND PERHAPS
REPRESENTS A 'UTOPIA' IN THE FALLOUT WORLD. IT IS A HIGHLY ORGANIZED TOTALITARIAN SOCIETY THAT TRACES ITS
ROOTS FROM THE CITIZENS OF VAULT 8.**" You need into their computer archives to find Vault 13 and save your
village. To do that you need to be a **CITIZEN.**

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- IT'S THE BEST PLACE IN THE WASTELAND AND IT'S BUILT ON SLAVERY THEY WON'T NAME (the premise — STARTLINGLY
  our faction web): Vault City has medicine, clean water, agriculture, law, an Auto-Doc, and a functioning
  council. Everything the wasteland lacks. Outside its walls is a shantytown of people who do the work.
  They're called **SERVANTS.**
  - Lynette will exile you for saying the other word. The wiki lists the triggers, verbatim: she "**WILL
    REVOKE THE PLAYER'S DAY PASS, CITIZENSHIP, AND KICK THEM OUT OF THE CITY FOR A VARIETY OF REASONS,
    INCLUDING: BEING RUDE, QUESTIONING VAULT CITY'S SUPERIORITY, QUESTIONING LYNETTE'S DECISION, CALLING
    SERVANTS SLAVES, COMMENTING ON SLAVERY, SUGGESTING COOPERATION BETWEEN GECKO AND VAULT CITY, SUGGESTING
    KILLING THOMAS MOORE, AND MORE.**"
  - **CALLING SERVANTS SLAVES** is a bannable offense. Not doing anything about it. SAYING IT. The utopia's
    immune response is triggered by VOCABULARY (STARTLINGLY our faction web + standing + conscience system;
    the institution defended by a word).
- THE PRIVILEGE IS INVISIBLE TO THE PRIVILEGED (the character writing, and it's brutal): on Lynette —
  "**THIS EXPOSURE ONLY SERVED TO STRENGTHEN HER BELIEFS IN THE INNATE SUPERIORITY OF VAULT CITY CITIZENS. IF
  OTHERS CANNOT ACHIEVE THE SAME AS VAULT CITY, THAT MEANS THEY ARE NOT SMART OR STRONG ENOUGH TO DO SO. THE
  FACT THAT VAULT CITY HAD AN EXTREMELY PRIVILEGED POSITION THANKS TO THE AREA AROUND VAULT 8 AND THE
  G.E.C.K. DOESN'T EVEN FACTOR INTO THE EQUATION FOR HER. THE ACHIEVEMENTS OF VAULT CITY ARE, TO LYNETTE, THE
  RESULT OF HARD WORK AND RESOLVE OF HER FELLOW CITIZENS.**"
  - They started with a **Garden of Eden Creation Kit** — a magic box that builds a functioning settlement —
    and concluded they were better people. That's the whole psychology of inherited advantage, written in
    1998, in a game with a talking deathclaw (cf. our faction web + post-collapse premise + AMALGAMATION).
  - And she's a hypocrite in her pocket: "**THOUGH MENTATS ARE BANNED IN VAULT CITY, ONE CAN USE THE STEAL
    SKILL AND DISCOVER THAT SHE CARRIES SOME. THESE ARE MEANT TO CONVEY TO THE PLAYER THAT SHE IS A
    HYPOCRITE ABOUT CHEM USE.**"
- THE ENTRANCE EXAM IS RIGGED AND THE BACK DOOR IS A WAR CRIME (the structure): "**SHE'LL SAY THAT YOU MUST
  TAKE A CITIZENSHIP TEST (YOU CAN ONLY PASS WITH 10 INTELLIGENCE AND 10 LUCK).**"
  - Ten Intelligence AND ten Luck. Maximum on both. Effectively nobody passes. The meritocracy's test is
    designed to be failed.
  - "**TELL HER THAT YOU WILL TAKE THE TEST, AND SHE'LL MENTION ANOTHER WAY: GET RID OF THE GHOULISH
    INHABITANTS OF GECKO.**"
  - So the legitimate path is impossible and the real path is **ETHNIC CLEANSING** (STARTLINGLY our standing
    + faction web + conscience system + Megaton law; citizenship priced in bodies).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — YOU CAN'T EVEN GET IN THE DOOR
- You need a **Day Pass** to enter the Inner City. Options: high Charisma/Speech to convince Wallace "**THAT
  YOU ARE A MERCHANT WHO WANTS TO TRADE**"; or, better, "**YOU CAN STRIP DOWN TO YOUR VAULT 13 SUIT BEFORE
  SPEAKING TO WALLACE. HE FINDS THIS QUITE UNUSUAL, AND SENDS YOU IN TO SEE THE FIRST CITIZEN.**"
- The vault suit is a passport. You're not a person — you're the RIGHT KIND of person, identifiable by
  clothing (cf. our standing + garment system + faction web).
### STAGE 1 — OR YOU CAN JUST BUY FAKE PAPERS FOR $200
- "**EVEN BETTER THAN EITHER OF THE ABOVE APPROACHES, YOU CAN BRIBE SKEEV HIS ASSISTANT. YOU PAY HIM $200
  FOR FAKE CITIZENSHIP PAPERS (MUCH HANDIER THAN A DAY PASS). THEN, YOU CAN BLACKMAIL HIM FOR $300 TO KEEP
  QUIET! NET: $100 AND A SET OF FAKE PAPERS. I NEVER NEEDED A DAY PASS.**"
- The First Citizen's own assistant sells forged citizenship. And the papers WORK: "**RANDAL IN THE AMENITIES
  OFFICE... ACCEPTS FALSE CITIZENSHIP PAPERS!**"
- The society whose entire identity is that citizenship must be EARNED has a $200 price tag on it, and the
  seller is in the government building (cf. our faction web + standing + conscience system; the meritocracy
  with a black market in itself).
- And you can rat him out later: "**OBTAIN FALSE CITIZENSHIP PAPERS FROM SKEEV. TURN HIM LATER AFTER YOU'VE
  COMPLETED THE GECKO QUEST.**"
### STAGE 2 — THE FIRST CITIZEN (the conversation is a minefield)
- "**GO INSIDE, AND TALK TO LYNETTE, THE FIRST CITIZEN... ASK HER ABOUT THE G.E.C.K, AND SHE'LL SAY THAT IT
  ISN'T MEANT FOR YOU.**"
- Her stated doctrine, verbatim: "**WE NO LONGER CONSIDER OURSELVES TO BE 'VAULT DWELLERS'... WE REFER TO
  OURSELVES AS 'CITIZENS.' IF YOU WISH TO BECOME PART OF OUR COMMUNITY, YOU MUST FIRST PROVE YOUR WORTH.**"
- And the immigration policy: "**THE PROBLEM IS THAT ALL OUTSIDERS WISH TO JOIN OUR SOCIETY, WHICH IS SIMPLY
  NOT FEASIBLE. WE WOULD UNDOUBTEDLY SINK INTO THE SAME ANARCHY WHICH PLAGUES THE OUTSIDE.**"
- The founding myth: "**WE EXITED THE VAULT 120 YEARS AGO AND, WITH THE HELP OF OUR EDEN KIT, BEGAN
  CONSTRUCTION OF VAULT CITY. TEN YEARS LATER, THE OVERSEER RETIRED AND A COUNCIL OF CITIZENS WAS
  ESTABLISHED.**" She says the Eden kit out loud. She just doesn't hear it.
- The player-facing rule from every guide: "**THE PLAYER SHOULD ALWAYS BE POLITE TO LYNETTE, PREFERABLY BY
  ADDRESSING HER AS FIRST CITIZEN EVERY TIME.**" You must perform deference to a woman running a slave state
  or lose the content (cf. our standing + conscience system + faction web).
### STAGE 3 — THE SERVANT ALLOCATION CENTER (the quest that names the thing)
- "**TALK TO THE WORRIED WOMAN, AND ASK HER WHAT IS WRONG. SHE'LL SAY THAT HER HUSBAND IS NOW IN THE SERVANT
  ALLOCATION CENTER AND WAS ARRESTED. OFFER TO FIND JOSHUA FOR HER.**"
- Amanda's husband has been ALLOCATED. There is a building for it. The options to free him: "**EITHER
  CONVINCE HIM THAT JOSHUA HAS CONTRACTED SOME RARE DISEASE, GIVE HIM A BRIBE OF UP TO $1000, TELL HIM THAT
  JOSHUA IS TROUBLESOME AND SHOULD BE KICKED OUT, OR TAKE HIM BY FORCE. HOWEVER YOU DO IT YOU'LL RECEIVE 500
  XP.**"
- Note every non-violent option: you cannot argue that owning him is wrong. You can only argue that he's
  DEFECTIVE MERCHANDISE — diseased, troublesome, not worth keeping. The system has no input for "this is
  wrong," only for "this unit is substandard" (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + faction web; the
  bureaucracy with no field for morality).
### STAGE 4 — THE ERRANDS (the utopia is full of people who need small things)
- A crying child lost a **Mr. Nixon doll** (it's behind the bar, by some boxes). A farmer named Mr. Smith
  needs a **plow.** Valerie needs **a pair of pliers and a wrench.** Lydia needs **10 beers and 10 booze.**
  Dr. Andrew's **Auto-Doc** is broken.
- And the smuggling detail: "**IF YOU HAVE YOUR CITIZENSHIP YOU CAN SIMPLY CARRY THE BOTTLES THROUGH THE MAIN
  GATE. IF NOT YOU CAN GIVE ALL THE BOOZE TO ONE OF YOUR COMPANIONS. THEY WON'T BE SEARCHED.**"
- Citizens aren't searched. Their companions aren't searched. The security state has a class-shaped hole in
  it (cf. our standing + faction web + logistics; the checkpoint that only checks the poor).
### STAGE 5 — GECKO (the quest is a euphemism)
- "**GET THE QUEST FROM LYNETTE TO TAKE CARE OF GECKO'S NUCLEAR PLANT.**" The journal calls it: "**SOLVE THE
  GECKO POWERPLANT PROBLEM.**"
- What she means: "**LYNETTE RECRUITS THE CHOSEN ONE TO 'SOLVE' THE PROBLEM OF RADIATION BEING LEAKED FROM
  THE GECKO POWERPLANT, BUT HER IDEA OF 'SOLVING' THE PROBLEM IS TO SHUT DOWN THE PLANT AND/OR KILLING THE
  GHOULS.**"
- Gecko is a town of ghouls running a leaky reactor. The reactor contaminates Vault City's groundwater. Her
  solution is to end Gecko.
### STAGE 6 — THE THIRD OPTION SHE NEVER ASKED FOR (the best beat in the quest)
- There's a part. There's a repair. Nobody in charge wants it.
- "**BACK IN VAULT CITY LYNETTE WANTS NOTHING TO DO WITH IT. MCCLURE, ALSO IN THE COUNCIL BUILDING, IS MORE
  SYMPATHETIC AND GIVES YOU PERMISSION TO GET THE PART FROM THE AMENITIES OFFICE. TAKE THE PART AND GIVE IT
  TO FESTUS IN GECKO. HE'LL INSTALL THE PART AND ALL WILL BE WELL. YOU CAN ALSO INSTALL IT YOURSELF IF YOU
  HAVE THE NECESSARY SKILL. THIS LEADS ON TO ANOTHER QUEST IN GECKO TO OPTIMIZE THE POWERPLANT.**"
- The whole crisis — the radiation, the groundwater, the genocide-shaped solution — is a **BROKEN PART.**
  Fixable. For free. And the First Citizen "**WANTS NOTHING TO DO WITH IT**," because the problem was never
  the reactor (cf. our [READ] + faction web + Pacifist Law; the war that's a maintenance ticket).
- To even find out, you need the ghouls' side: a **SENTIENT MOLE RAT** in a manhole gives you a **Talisman**;
  showing the Talisman gets Gordon to hand over "**A HOLODISK THAT CONTAINS ECONOMIC DATA CONCERNING THE
  REACTOR.**" Take it to McClure, "**THE SANE VOICE OF VAULT CITY.**" The evidence route runs through the
  people she wants dead (cf. our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded).
### STAGE 7 — AND IF YOU DO WHAT SHE ASKED, SHE HATES YOU FOR IT
- A real player, on Steam, who blew up the reactor exactly as instructed: "**I JUST BLEW UP THE GECKO
  REACTOR, AS LYNETTE ASKED ME TO DO. BUT WHEN I TALK TO HER, TO GET THE CITIZEN STATUS... SHE DRIVES ME OUT
  OF TOWN. I CAN'T SAY ANYTHING ELSE ABOUT THE REACTOR THAN WHAT I REALLY DID.**"
- Her actual response: "**WHAT? YOU DESTROYED THE PLANT?! THE CONTAMINATION! YOU IDIOT. AT LEAST THOSE GHOULS
  ARE GONE... GET OUT OF MY SIGHT.**"
- Read that again. **"At least those ghouls are gone."** She's furious about the contamination and satisfied
  about the genocide. And another player's correction on the same thread is the point: "**SHE ASKED YOU TO
  DEAL WITH THE REACTOR BECAUSE IT WAS CONTAMINATING THE GROUNDWATER. YOU BLEW IT UP, CAUSING A FAR WORSE
  DISASTER THAN THE PRIOR PROBLEM.**"
- The euphemism was load-bearing. She said "solve the problem," you heard permission, and she is not
  responsible for what you heard (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + Megaton law + faction web; the order
  that was never given).
### STAGE 8 — THE BUG THAT ATE THE REWARD
- The final indignity, from the wiki: "**HER SCRIPTING INCLUDES GRANTING THE CHOSEN ONE CITIZENSHIP FOR
  SOLVING THE GECKO POWERPLANT PROBLEM, BUT IT IS INACCESSIBLE DUE TO MUTUALLY-EXCLUSIVE CHECKS.**"
- The reward for the main path is IN THE GAME and unreachable. Troika/Black Isle shipping standards, and a
  free thematic gift: **the citizenship she promised you literally does not exist** (cf. our regression gates
  — and Arcanum Q86's lesson about bugs).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE POLITENESS REQUIREMENT IS A DESIGN TRAP: the guides all say the same thing — "**IT IS IMPORTANT NOT TO
  BE RUDE TO LYNETTE, EVEN QUESTIONING VAULT CITY'S POLICIES AND HER DECISIONS CAN RESULT IN THE CHOSEN ONE
  BEING THROWN OUT OF VAULT CITY FOR GOOD, CUTTING OFF SEVERAL IMPORTANT QUESTS.**" Roleplaying an honest
  person permanently locks content. LESSON (sharp, ours): if the moral response destroys the quest line,
  you haven't written a moral test, you've written a punishment for engagement. Our standing system must let
  the dynasty SAY the true thing and pay a survivable price.
- THE CITIZENSHIP TEST IS UNPASSABLE AND THE GAME NEVER ADMITS IT: "**YOU CAN ONLY PASS WITH 10 INTELLIGENCE
  AND 10 LUCK.**" A first-time player will burn real effort on a door that doesn't open. LESSON: a rigged
  test is great satire and needs an in-fiction TELL, or it just reads as a bug.
- THE CITIZENSHIP REWARD IS UNREACHABLE DUE TO A SCRIPTING BUG: literally in the files, gated behind
  mutually-exclusive checks. LESSON (Arcanum's lesson Q86 again): the theme survives; the QA doesn't. Our
  regression gates exist for this.
- THE ANTI-SLAVERY POSITION HAS NO MECHANICAL PATH: you can free ONE man (Joshua) by claiming he's diseased.
  You cannot free the servants. You cannot argue the policy. The game's only structural answer to slavery is
  to shoot the town. LESSON (ours): if your only lever against an institution is violence, you've made the
  Pacifist Law impossible in that district — build a non-violent lever or admit the institution is scenery.
- LYNETTE IS A CARICATURE AND THE DEVS KNEW IT: from the Fallout Bible, the designers answering a fan asking
  why she's like that — "**SHE WAS SUPPOSED TO EMBODY THE WORST ARROGANCE AND CONDESCENSION THAT VAULT CITY
  HAS TO OFFER (TRAITS THAT ARE NOT PRESENT IN ALL THE CITIZENS, AS MCCLURE AND OTHERS PROVE).**" She's a
  mouthpiece, and McClure exists to prove the point isn't "everyone here is evil." LESSON: if your faction's
  face is a caricature, you need the reasonable member in the same building or the faction becomes a joke.
- IT'S ALL BEHIND A DAY PASS: a player without Charisma, the vault suit, or $200 can bounce off the entire
  city. LESSON: don't gate a main-path settlement behind a stat check with no fallback.
