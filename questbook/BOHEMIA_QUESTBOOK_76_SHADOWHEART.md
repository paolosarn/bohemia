# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 76: A PERSON MADE OF STOLEN MEMORY / WHO ARE YOU WHEN YOUR PAST WAS EDITED (Baldur's Gate 3 — Shadowheart)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the stolen-name/edited-memory premise, the wound her goddess keeps in
her, the Nightsong choice as the identity hinge, the House of Grief + her tortured parents, the final
choice (let them die free, or take the curse yourself), both endings written with equal conviction, the
traits that SURVIVED her conditioning, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. The finest companion arc in
modern RPGs. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION (edited records of people), our recorded-vs-
unrecorded, + our companions-must-be-real law. Larian Studios. Reference only; Paolo does not read it.
No Bohemia quest written here.

Arc: Shadowheart's questline ("Daughter of Darkness," Baldur's Gate 3, Larian, 2023). A cold, guarded
cleric of SHAR (goddess of loss, darkness, + forgetting) carrying a stolen artefact and an unhealing wound.
Her memories were SURGICALLY REMOVED by her own church "so she would not betray the Lady of Sorrows." She
doesn't know her real name. She doesn't know her parents are alive. Over three acts, the player watches a
person discover that her entire identity was WRITTEN FOR HER — and decide, in the end, who she actually is.
The benchmark for companion writing.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- HER PERSONALITY IS A THING THAT WAS *DONE TO HER* (the premise — startlingly on-theme for us): Shadowheart
  isn't secretive because she's cagey; her memories were DELIBERATELY SUPPRESSED by her own faith "so she
  would not betray" it. Her name isn't hers — she was JENEVELLE, raised by devout Selûnite parents in the
  forest outside Baldur's Gate, and TAKEN as a child. The woman you meet is a CONSTRUCTED person built on
  an edited record of a real one. The arc's question: if everything you believe about yourself was
  installed, are you still you? (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION — an entity made of edited records of the
  dead; cf. SOMA Q34, Citizen Sleeper Q65, our recorded-vs-unrecorded; the edited self).
- THE REAL PERSON LEAKS THROUGH THE CONDITIONING (the craft that makes it land): the writing's masterstroke
  is what SURVIVED. Despite Sharran training that prizes cruelty + secrecy, Shadowheart "displays traits
  that are often at odds with her Sharran training: a fondness for FLOWERS and ANIMALS, disapproves of
  indiscriminate violence, prefers peaceful resolutions... often sympathizes with the plights of party
  members." Her favourite flower is a NIGHT ORCHID. She loves animals. She was raised gentle, and the
  conditioning couldn't get all of it. You can SEE the real girl before she can. The evidence of the
  unedited person is in her behavior, not her memory (cf. our [READ]/recorded-vs-unrecorded; the truth that
  survives the edit).
- THE GOD KEEPS A WOUND IN HER SO SHE CAN'T LEAVE (the mechanic of control): Shadowheart carries a
  MYSTERIOUS SHARRAN WOUND that flares with agony at specific moments — near a broken Selûne statue, when
  saving a child. She calls it "an old wound that never properly healed." It isn't. It's a LEASH: "a burden
  imposed on her by Shar," through which "she can somehow feel Shar's influence." Her god literally hurts
  her when she acts like her real self (cf. our Amalgamation's hold on people; control as pain).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (three acts, one identity)
===============================================================================

### ACT 1 — YOU HAVE TO *NOTICE* HER (the arc is opt-in, and the world triggers it)
- Nothing forces the reveal. Her secrets surface from WORLD CONTACT: pray at a Selûnite statue in the
  Owlbear Cave and she snaps that it's "trash at best, dangerous at worst" — pass a DC-15 INSIGHT check on
  why she cares so much, and she confesses she worships Shar. Save a child, find a broken Selûne statue,
  and her wound flares. Her approval gates what she'll say. The player's ATTENTION is the key that opens
  the character (cf. Cyberpunk-Judy Q63, our companions; the arc that requires you to look).
### ACT 2 — THE NIGHTSONG: SHE'S ORDERED TO MURDER PROOF OF THE OTHER LIFE (the hinge)
- The Gauntlet of Shar ends with the NIGHTSONG — a captive immortal, who turns out to be DAME AYLIN,
  daughter of SELÛNE (Shar's twin, the goddess Shadowheart was raised to worship). Shar's command:
  Shadowheart must kill her. It's her final trial; the reward is becoming a DARK JUSTICIAR.
  - THE PLAYER'S ROLE IS *PRESSURE*, NOT COMMAND: you can tell her to kill, or try to stop her — but the
    stop requires a hard CHA/persuasion check (DC 20+; one variant is DC 30, which PC Gamer flags as
    hopeless). The winning line isn't an argument about theology — it's "Throw that spear away. MAKE YOUR
    OWN CHOICE." You don't convert her. You give her permission to choose.
  - AND PUSHING TOO HARD BACKFIRES: PC Gamer's warning — "the first conversation options here are
    misleading. If you want to save Shadowheart from Shar, you might feel tempted to argue with her right
    away. However... pushing too hard will make things worse." The correct play is patience.
  - BOTH OUTCOMES ARE FULLY BUILT: kill the Nightsong and she becomes a Dark Justiciar with unique armour +
    Shar's Spear of Evening — and the cost is real: Aylin's death dooms everyone at the Last Light Inn
    (Isobel's protective magic fails; the refugees die). Spare her and Shar TORTURES Shadowheart with
    "unimaginable agony for her blasphemy" before casting her out — her hair turns silver-white; Aylin
    grants her the Moonlight Glaive.
  - AND YOU CAN LOSE HER ENTIRELY: hand the Nightsong to Balthazar, or leave Shadowheart out of the
    Shadowfell, and she "becomes furious and either attacks or permanently leaves the party." The arc has a
    fail state (cf. our Megaton law + companion-consequence; the choice that's really about permission).
### ACT 3 — THE HOUSE OF GRIEF: HER PARENTS ARE ALIVE, AND BEING TORTURED (the payoff)
- The Sharran cloister in Baldur's Gate, run by VICONIA DEVIR — the Mother Superior who TRAINED her. And
  there she learns the full truth: her parents are ALIVE, held under Shar's curse, tortured for decades to
  keep their daughter in line.
- THE FINAL CHOICE IS A TROLLEY WITH NO GOOD TRACK: free her parents from the curse (which means LETTING
  THEM DIE) — or have SHADOWHEART TAKE THE CURSE ONTO HERSELF to keep them alive. Free them and she loses
  them the moment she finds them. Save them and she inherits their torment forever. The guides recommend
  heeding her own advice + letting them rest — "through this decision, she overcomes her past and gains
  true freedom."
- AND THE PLAYER CAN SELL HER: "the party can agree to SURRENDER SHADOWHEART to Viconia, losing her as a
  companion but gaining the Sharrans as allies for the final battle." A person, traded for an army (cf. our
  Megaton law + no-clean-answer; the trolley with no good track).
### BOTH ENDINGS ARE WRITTEN WITH EQUAL CONVICTION (the anti-"correct path" design)
- SELÛNITE: silver hair, open, gentle, free — and if romanced, the arc's most quietly devastating beat: she
  invites you to SWIM. (She has an aversion to water; Aylin eventually reveals why — a childhood memory the
  church took.) Swimming is her saying she's not theirs anymore. Intimacy as the SYMBOL of an identity
  reclaimed.
- SHARRAN: she becomes the new MOTHER SUPERIOR at the House of Grief, and "word of the Lady of Loss has
  spread far and wide, thanks to the efforts of Shadowheart." If romanced, she ENDS the relationship —
  "she needs to be fully devoted to her goddess. She agrees to be kissed for old times' sake, but remains
  faithful to Shar." And, chillingly, she "expresses sympathy for VICONIA's fate, now able to understand
  what she went through as Mother Superior" — she becomes the woman who broke her.
- A player who's done both: "each feels AUTHENTIC to different aspects of her character. The Sharran path
  reveals the depth of her CONDITIONING and capacity for devotion; the Selûnite transformation showcases
  her RESILIENCE and capacity for change" (cf. our Megaton law; no secretly-correct path).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE GOOD PATH IS GATED BEHIND A DICE ROLL + A BUILD: saving her from Shar requires a DC-20-to-30 CHARISMA
  check. A low-CHA party — a perfectly valid build — CANNOT save her, no matter how well they know her or
  how much they've earned her trust. The most important emotional beat in her arc is gated by a STAT.
  LESSON (sharp): don't gate an EMOTIONAL payoff behind a build-dependent roll — earned relationship should
  be the currency, not Charisma (our standing/relationship design; the check should test what you've DONE,
  not your sheet).
- THE FANDOM DECIDED THERE'S A "BEST" ENDING ANYWAY: guides call Selûnite "the path to the best ending"
  outright. Despite Larian writing both with conviction, the reward structure + presentation (she's
  happier, prettier, romanceable, keeps her parents' memory) makes one path read as CORRECT — so the
  Sharran path becomes "the bad end" in practice. LESSON (our Megaton law): equal writing isn't enough —
  if one branch is obviously better-lit, the choice collapses (balance the FRAMING, not just the text).
- HER APPROVAL SYSTEM FIGHTS HER ARC: to keep Sharran Shadowheart's approval you must do Shar-ish things
  (she "approves of actions that are against Selûne"), but her ARC wants her to escape that. So an
  optimizing player is nudged to reinforce her conditioning. LESSON: an approval meter can work AGAINST
  the character's own growth — approval should track the relationship, not the mask (our companion design).
- THE ARC IS EASY TO MISS ENTIRELY: her reveals are gated behind an Insight check, an optional statue, a
  specific flower, an early conversation you might never have. bg3.wiki even documents a Disguise-Self
  EXPLOIT to retrigger missed dialogue about the night orchid. LESSON (recurring): a deep character arc
  hidden behind unsignposted triggers will be missed by most players (Ranni Q71, our signposting).
- THE CLASS DOESN'T MATCH THE STORY: she's a Trickery Cleric; guides recommend RESPECCING her to Light
  Domain "which has high narrative synergy" if she turns Selûnite. The system doesn't follow the narrative
  — you have to hand-fix it. LESSON: if a character's arc transforms them, the MECHANICS should transform
  too (our fold/upbringing — story change = system change).

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. HER PERSONALITY IS A THING THAT WAS DONE TO HER: memories surgically removed by her own faith "so she
    would not betray" it; even her NAME is stolen — a constructed person built on an edited record of a
    real one (cf. our Amalgamation; the edited self).
W2. THE REAL PERSON LEAKS THROUGH: flowers, animals, mercy, a hatred of pointless cruelty — traits "at odds
    with her Sharran training" that the conditioning couldn't erase. You see her before she does (cf. our
    [READ]; the truth that survives the edit).
W3. THE GOD KEEPS A WOUND IN HER: an "old wound that never healed" is actually a LEASH that flares when she
    acts like her real self (cf. our Amalgamation's hold; control as pain).
W4. THE ARC IS TRIGGERED BY THE *WORLD*, NOT A MENU: a statue, a rescued child, an Insight check — her
    secrets surface from contact with things that hurt (cf. Cyberpunk Q63, our companions).
W5. THE PLAYER'S ROLE IS PERMISSION, NOT PERSUASION: the winning line is "Throw that spear away. MAKE YOUR
    OWN CHOICE" — you don't convert her, you give her the right to choose (cf. our companion-agency design).
W6. PUSHING TOO HARD BACKFIRES: arguing early makes it WORSE; patience is the mechanic (cf. our
    relationship design; restraint as skill).
W7. BOTH PATHS FULLY BUILT, WITH REAL WORLD COST: Dark Justiciar armour + a doomed refugee inn; or divine
    torture + silver hair + freedom — and you can lose her entirely (cf. our Megaton law).
W8. THE FINAL CHOICE IS A TROLLEY WITH NO GOOD TRACK: free her parents (they die) or she takes their curse
    (she suffers forever) — she finds her family and loses them either way (cf. our no-clean-answer ethos).
W9. INTIMACY AS THE SYMBOL OF A RECLAIMED SELF: she invites you to SWIM — the thing the church's stolen
    memory made her fear. Freedom expressed as a small, specific, human act (cf. Cyberpunk-Judy Q63, our
    writing).
W10. THE DARK ENDING IS A REAL PERSON TOO: she becomes Mother Superior, spreads Shar's word, ends the
     romance with a kiss "for old times' sake" — and pities the woman who broke her, "now able to
     understand what she went through." Damnation written with as much care as salvation (cf. our Megaton law).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the edited self + companions who are people
===============================================================================
Shadowheart is the modern benchmark for companion writing AND the closest character-level analogue to our
AMALGAMATION premise: a person constituted by an EDITED RECORD of themselves, who must decide whether the
edit is who they are. That's our recorded-vs-unrecorded, at human scale.
- W1/W2/W3 (the edited self + what leaks through + the wound as a leash — our AMALGAMATION at human scale):
  the top port. Our AMALGAMATION is built from data-portraits — EDITED records of the dead. Shadowheart is
  what that looks like in ONE person: memories removed BY HER OWN SIDE for her own "protection," a name
  that isn't hers, and a god who keeps a wound in her so leaving HURTS. Bank all three: (a) a Bohemia
  character (or an Amalgamation-fragment, or a dynast) whose personality is a thing that was DONE to them;
  (b) the craft rule that the REAL person LEAKS THROUGH the edit in behavior (a flower, an animal, a
  reflex of mercy) — the player sees the truth before the character does, which is EXACTLY our [READ]
  (evidence over testimony); and (c) control as PAIN — the Amalgamation/Network keeps a wound in people so
  they can feel their leash (ties SOMA Q34, Citizen Sleeper Q65, FFX's constructed personhood Q68, our
  Amalgamation + recorded-vs-unrecorded + [READ] + conscience system; a top-tier on-theme port).
- W4/W6 (the world triggers the arc + pushing too hard backfires): bank both — our companions' depths
  should surface from CONTACT with the world (a place, a rescued child, a broken thing) + an attention
  check, not a dialogue menu; and PATIENCE should be a mechanic — pressing too early makes it worse. That's
  cheap for a solo dev and makes characters feel like people with their own timing (ties Cyberpunk-Judy
  Q63, our companions + district; restraint as skill).
- W5 (permission, not persuasion — our companion-agency law): STEAL the line. The winning move isn't
  converting her — it's "MAKE YOUR OWN CHOICE." Our companions/factions should be changed by being GIVEN
  AGENCY, not by winning an argument. This is the anti-Ranni fix (Q71's bond rests on projection; this one
  rests on respect) and it aligns with our Pacifist Law (ties our companions + Pacifist Law + Megaton law).
- W7/W10 (both paths fully built + the dark ending is a real person): bank the standard — our branches must
  BOTH be written with conviction, with real world COST on each (her damnation costs an inn full of
  refugees; her salvation costs her divine torture). And bank W10 specifically: our "bad" outcomes should
  produce a REAL PERSON, not a villain — Sharran Shadowheart pities the woman who broke her, "now able to
  understand what she went through." The damned version is coherent + sympathetic. That's the bar for our
  Liberate/Respect/Become (ties our Megaton law + finales + faction web; damnation written with care).
- W8 (the trolley with no good track): bank it — our fold + Amalgamation should produce choices where BOTH
  tracks cost the same person everything: free the dead (lose them) or hold them (inherit their torment).
  That IS our Amalgamation's central choice, rendered as one family's decision (ties Whispering Hillock Q04,
  Dead Money's let-go Q72, our no-clean-answer ethos + Liberate finale + death-math).
- W9 (intimacy as the symbol of a reclaimed self): bank the craft — a freed character expresses it in a
  small, SPECIFIC, human act (she swims, because the church stole the memory that made her fear water). Our
  characters' liberation should show up in a tiny concrete behavior, not a speech (ties Cyberpunk-Judy's
  mundane intimacy Q63, our writing pillar + no-preaching rule).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) DON'T gate an emotional payoff behind a build-dependent dice roll — earned
  relationship should be the currency (our standing: the check tests what you've DONE, not your sheet);
  (b) equal WRITING isn't enough — if one branch is better-lit (happier, prettier, better-rewarded), the
  fandom declares it correct + the choice collapses; balance the FRAMING (our Megaton law); (c) an approval
  meter can fight the character's own growth (approving of Shar-ish acts nudges you to reinforce her
  conditioning) — approval should track the RELATIONSHIP, not the mask (our companion design); (d) deep
  arcs behind unsignposted triggers get MISSED (Ranni Q71 again — signpost that depth exists); and (e) if
  a character's arc transforms them, the MECHANICS must transform too — don't make the player hand-respec
  the narrative (our fold/upbringing: story change = system change).
