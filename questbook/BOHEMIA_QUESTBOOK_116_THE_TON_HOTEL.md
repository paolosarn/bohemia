# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 116: THE WINDOW KILLS YOUR BROTHER AND NOBODY TELLS YOU (The 'Ton Hotel Raid, Deus Ex)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the mission where the game presents you with a doomed last stand, tells
you to run, opens a window for you, and then kills your brother for taking it — with no warning, no
indicator, and no way to know until hours later when you walk past his corpse. Complete event flow, the
signal, the raid, the window, the closet, the front door, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY
relevant to our conscience system, our persistent consequence, our companions, our fold, our PACIFIST LAW,
+ our ART PHILOSOPHY. Ion Storm / Warren Spector. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest
written here.

Quest: the MJ12 raid on the 'Ton Hotel, Deus Ex (2000). Your brother Paul, a UNATCO agent, has defected. He
has a bomb in his blood. You are in his apartment when the government arrives to kill you both.

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE FLAG IS BUGGED AND HAS BEEN FOR 25 YEARS: "**SOME SORT OF TRIGGER BUG WOULD NOT REGISTER PROPERLY THAT
  PAUL ESCAPED ALIVE AND YOU'D FIND HIS DEAD BODY.**" And there's a whole guide for "**PAUL DENTON
  DISAPPEARED GLITCH**" requiring console commands and manual flag deletion — "**M4_RAIDTELEPORTDONE,
  M4_RAIDBEGAN, PAUL_LEFTHOTEL.**" LESSON (ours, direct): our persistent-consequence flags need a REGRESSION
  GATE and a save-state test, or our best emotional beat becomes a bug report.
- THE GAME NEVER TELLS YOU THE RULE: no warning, no hint, no dialogue. "**I'VE PLAYED IT 5-6 TIMES AND HE
  NEVER SURVIVED EVER... ALWAYS THOUGHT THAT WAS THE ONLY STORY PATH.**" LESSON (double-edged, ~7th
  confirmation): unsignposted consequence is powerful and it means most of your audience never learns the
  system existed — the difference between a secret and an accident is whether the player can find out.
- THE WINDOW IS TAUGHT AS THE SMART ROUTE, THEN PUNISHED: the game explicitly establishes "**THE 'TON CAN BE
  ENTERED THROUGH THE FRONT DOOR OR THROUGH A SIDE WINDOW LEADING INTO PAUL'S ROOM.**" It trains you to use
  it, then kills your brother for it. LESSON (ours, careful): teaching a tool and then making the tool the
  betrayal is a gut-punch **once** — do it twice and players stop trusting the level.
- PAUL IS INVINCIBLE, WHICH DEFLATES THE FIGHT: "**AS WITH OTHER PLOT-CENTRIC, NON-PLAYER CHARACTERS, **PAUL
  REMAINS INVINCIBLE IN THE FIGHT IF THE LATTER CHOICE IS MADE.**" The desperate last stand for your
  brother's life is a scripted scene where he cannot be hurt. LESSON: if the stakes are a flag and not the
  fiction, some players will feel it.
- THE FIGHT IS UNWINNABLE AND THE OPTIMAL PLAY IS SUICIDE: "**THE ONLY WAY TO KEEP PAUL ALIVE IS TO EXIT
  PAUL'S ROOM VIA THE DOOR WITHOUT YOUR WEAPON DRAWN AND LET THE MIB PEOPLE SHOOT YOU TILL YOU'RE DEAD.**"
  LESSON (double-edged): a set piece whose best solution is dying on purpose is either brilliant or broken,
  and the fanbase argued about which for two decades.
- THE HOTEL'S THIRD VISIT IS EMPTY: "**THERE IS NOTHING SIGNIFICANT TO DO (ASIDE FROM GETTING LOOT).**"
  LESSON (double-edged, and I lean the other way): the emptiness IS the content — but if a player doesn't
  feel it, it's just an empty level.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**PAUL DENTON** — wants JC to RESIST (stay, fight the unwinnable raid); asked him, one line, easy to miss. Will never say: "if you take the window I might die" — the stakes are unstated BY DESIGN (and by bug). FUNCTION: the brother whose survival is wired to your obedience to a request the interface undersold.

**THE WINDOW** — wants to be taken; it's marked, lit, and the tutorialized escape verb. FUNCTION: the trap made of training — everything the game taught you says JUMP, and jumping kills your brother.

**THE RAID (MJ12)** — wants Paul; gets him if you leave. FUNCTION: the unwinnable fight that isn't about winning (stage 5) — it's about STANDING THERE.

**THE 'TON HOTEL** — visited three times, different each time (stage 1). FUNCTION: place-as-character; the domestic murder side quest (stage 2) seeds its geography before the raid weaponizes it.
