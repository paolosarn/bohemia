# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 79: A ROCKET TO HEAVEN / COMEDY THAT EARNS SINCERITY (Fallout: New Vegas — Come Fly With Me)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the ghoul cult riding rockets to "the Far Beyond," the bald human
convinced he's a ghoul, the invisible "demons" who can be talked to, the cruel truth under the joke (the
prophet knew Chris couldn't come and used him anyway), the multiple exits, the 12.5%-closer navigation
option, the tone-holds-the-sincerity design, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. Called by PC GamesN "the
best hour and a half in New Vegas" and the finest NV side-quest; it was the FIRST quest designed for the
game. DIRECTLY relevant to our tonal range, our Pacifist Law, + our faction-empathy design. Obsidian
(Joshua Sawyer's team). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: Come Fly With Me (Fallout: New Vegas, Obsidian, 2010). At the REPCONN Test Site outside Novac, a
cult of GHOULS led by a glowing prophet, JASON BRIGHT, is preparing the GREAT JOURNEY to the FAR BEYOND —
a promised land of "safety and healing," away from human bigotry. Their transport: three restored pre-war
ROCKETS. Their engineer: CHRIS HAVERSAM, a human who believes he's a ghoul because he went bald. Their
obstacle: invisible NIGHTKIN ("demons") squatting in the basement. Named for the Sinatra song.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- AN ABSURD PREMISE PLAYED COMPLETELY STRAIGHT (the tonal masterstroke): irradiated zombie-people are
  building rockets to fly to heaven, guided by prophecy, and the game never winks. PC GamesN: it "keeps the
  ridiculous-o-meter dialled all the way up throughout" — AND the ghouls' faith is treated with total
  sincerity. Jason was "shown VISION UPON VISION" of the rockets by "the Creator," who promised his flock
  "a place of safety and healing" + "an escape from the harshness of the wasteland, as well as the violence
  and BIGOTRY FROM HUMANS AGAINST GHOULS." The joke and the ache are the same object: a persecuted people
  building a rocket because there is nowhere on Earth for them (cf. our tonal range + Stardew-surface
  thesis Q69, Yakuza Q13; comedy that EARNS sincerity).
- THE CRUELTY IS HIDDEN INSIDE THE PUNCHLINE (the twist that makes it literature): Chris Haversam thinks
  he's a ghoul because his hair fell out. It's a great gag — "You call this bald, smoothskin? I'M A
  MONSTER!" But: Jason TRIED to tell him he was human, then reconsidered and "saw his arrival as a GIFT
  from the creator to aid in the Great Journey." Chris spent MONTHS restoring their rockets. And Bright
  knew the whole time that a human CANNOT survive the trip — that Chris would be left behind. The prophet
  used a delusional man's labor and let him keep the delusion because it was USEFUL. The funniest character
  in the quest is the victim of the kindest-seeming one (cf. Hearts of Stone's fine print Q70, our faction
  design; the gag with a knife in it).
- EVERY MAJOR OBSTACLE HAS A NON-VIOLENT SOLUTION (the Pacifist Law's ancestor): the "demons" in the
  basement are NIGHTKIN — schizophrenic super mutants — and their leader DAVISON "can be talked to if he
  can be reached undetected." You can resolve the whole confrontation with dialogue. Harland, a ghoul
  barricaded in a room, will turn hostile if you climb his stairs — but you can help him instead (rescue
  his companion). Even Chris's sabotage can be talked down. Nearly every conflict has a peaceful route, and
  they're all missable (cf. our PACIFIST LAW; the peaceful route that exists but must be found).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how the funniest quest becomes the best one)
===============================================================================

### THE HOOK IS A JOKE THAT'S TRUE (the No-Bark intro)
- You can learn about REPCONN from a paranoid drunk, NO-BARK NOONAN, who tells you "a farfetched tale about
  some COMMIE ZOMBIES who want to HIJACK ROCKETS and PAINT LENIN'S FACE ON THE MOON." It sounds like
  nonsense. It's approximately correct. The town lunatic is the most reliable witness in the game (cf. our
  [READ]/testimony-vs-evidence; the unreliable source who's right).
### THE CULT IS WRITTEN WITH RESPECT, NOT MOCKERY (the craft)
- Jason Bright is a GLOWING ONE who "never turned Feral" — a mutation the guide calls "probable and
  extremely rare" — who leads his flock "clad in the vestments of a tribe in the final stages of
  preparation to leave for a 'new world'." He isn't a fraud; he believes. He calls the Courier "a
  miraculous gift sent to help the ghouls fulfil their dreams." And his single most humane line, about the
  mindless FERAL ghouls wandering the site: "Please, wanderer, bear in mind that EVERY FERAL GHOUL YOU
  SPARE NOW IS ONE THAT WE CAN SAVE LATER." He's trying to take the mindless with him. (Mechanically it's
  real: "any feral ghouls that are not killed inside REPCONN will join the Great Journey.") A cult leader
  whose faith is absurd and whose compassion is total (cf. our faction web; belief written with respect).
- Even his NAME is earned, not given: "Jason Bright wasn't his birth name, it is the name that his flock
  started calling him when he began his cult."
### THE ERRAND STRUCTURE IS THE WORLD-TOUR (fetch-quests that are actually a map)
- The rockets need two things, and each is a mini-quest with multiple solutions:
  - THRUST CONTROL MODULES: buy from Old Lady Gibson for 500 caps (250 with Speech/Barter 50), or PICKPOCKET
    her key, or LOCKPICK the box.
  - ISOTOPE-239 IGNITING AGENT: loot it from a dead scavenger ("Mr. RADical") at Clark Field — or realize
    that the REPCONN TOY ROCKETS sold in the Novac gift shop contain the same isotope (a Science 40 check
    confirms it). The children's souvenirs are the fuel.
  - Each errand pulls you across the map + rewards a different build (cf. our quest factory; the fetch that
    teaches the world).
### THE CHOICE ISN'T "GOOD OR EVIL," IT'S "WHICH KINDNESS" (the moral core)
- Tell Chris the truth (Speech 50 or the Black Widow perk) and he SHATTERS — then turns on Bright and wants
  to blow up the rockets, killing everyone. Now you choose:
  - HELP HIM SABOTAGE (he asks for three boxes of SUGAR BOMBS): everyone dies.
  - TALK HIM DOWN: he relents, sees the ghouls off safely, and "he will become a PERMANENT RESIDENT OF
    NOVAC" — a man who lost his religion and got a town.
  - OR NEVER TELL HIM AT ALL: he flies off with his friends, still believing, and dies happy in the upper
    atmosphere. A guide's honest confession: "Do I let this delusional man burn up in the atmosphere? Or do
    you need to be cruel to be kind, forcing him to be left behind? Honestly, NEITHER WAS A GREAT CHOICE in
    my book."
  - Is it kinder to take a man's delusion, or to let it kill him? There's no karma answer that resolves it
    (cf. our Megaton law + Amalgamation's comfort-vs-truth; the choice between two mercies).
### THE LAST BUTTON IS A CHARACTER TEST (the navigation console)
- Before launching, a SCIENCE 55 check opens the navigation data. You can:
  - Modify it so the rockets CRASH INTO EACH OTHER in "a deadly fireworks display" (bad karma).
  - EXAMINE it and correct it so they land 12.5% CLOSER to their destination (good karma) — and it AFFECTS
    THE NOVAC ENDING SLIDES.
  - The best-hidden option in the quest is a small act of competence that nobody will ever thank you for,
    that makes a doomed pilgrimage slightly less doomed. And it's gated behind a skill check most players
    never make (cf. our Pacifist Law + optional-depth; the invisible kindness).
### YOU CAN JUST MURDER THE ENTIRE CULT (the honest alternative)
- The whole thing can be skipped: kill every Bright Follower (this satisfies Manny Vargas, one of the
  quest-givers), fail the quest, take the karma hit. Or exploit the launch: kill ghouls for their SPACE
  SUITS, then note that "any Ghouls one may have killed for their Space Suits will have NEW Space Suits" —
  a bug that nets ~4,000 caps. The game lets you loot a religion (cf. our Megaton law).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- IT'S A FETCH-QUEST WITH GREAT WRITING TAPED TO IT: "a pretty long quest... perhaps a little bit irritating
  due to the amount of BACK AND FORTH that happens throughout its run." Go to REPCONN, go to Gibson's, come
  back, go to Clark Field, come back, go to the platform. The structure is errands; the characters carry
  it. LESSON (recurring, and the honest one): great writing can REDEEM a fetch-chain but doesn't make it
  good structure — our quest factory should make the connective tissue itself interesting (Ranni Q71,
  Morrowind Q74; the sag between the peaks).
- THE PAYOFF IS OFFSCREEN AND UNRESOLVED: the rockets launch and... that's it. The ghouls fly into a sky
  that has no Far Beyond in it. Sawyer confirms they DISCUSSED making the veering rocket crash somewhere
  findable in the Mojave "however, they did not have the time or resources to develop the idea." A cut
  ghoul-prisoner subplot (with Stealth Boys to free them) also exists in the files. LESSON: an ending that
  happens OFFSCREEN protects the ambiguity but costs the payoff — and cut content leaves the seams visible
  (our scope discipline; ship the payoff).
- THE KARMA SYSTEM IS INCOHERENT HERE: "after the rockets are launched, the items in the research lab will
  still result in NEGATIVE KARMA if one takes them despite the fact that the ghouls will not be using them
  anymore." You get punished for looting the possessions of people who have voluntarily left the planet.
  LESSON (again — Tranquility Lane Q78's twin): a global morality ledger applied to a bespoke situation
  produces nonsense; our conscience system must be CONTEXTUAL, not a global score (no karma-as-currency).
- IT'S NOTORIOUSLY BUGGY: "many CORRUPT SAVES have been reported in the REPCONN building, at many stages of
  this quest, and even hours after the quest has been completed"; NPCs fall through floors and make it
  uncompletable; the launch music can degrade into permanent static. LESSON (ours, hard): the most beloved
  quest in the game is also the one most likely to eat your save — ambition without QA costs you the thing
  you built (our regression gates + testing discipline).
- THE BEST CONTENT IS BEHIND SKILL CHECKS: the truth about Chris needs Speech 50 (or a gender-locked perk);
  the 12.5% correction needs Science 55; peaceful Davison needs stealth. A player without those builds sees
  a shooting gallery with a funny hat on. LESSON (the Shadowheart flaw again Q76): don't gate the EMOTIONAL
  content behind stats — or provide multiple keys per door (our Pacifist Law: every door needs several keys).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — comedy that earns sincerity + every door has several keys
===============================================================================
Come Fly With Me is the canon's best proof that COMEDY CAN CARRY THE HEAVIEST MATERIAL — a persecuted people
building a rocket to a heaven that isn't there — and that every conflict can have a talking route. Both are
core Bohemia laws (our tonal range + our Pacifist Law), and this is their best single demonstration.
- W1/W4 (absurd premise played straight + belief written with respect — our tonal range + faction web): the
  top port. Bohemia is post-collapse Vegas: absurdity is native (a casino church, a cargo cult around a
  dead server, a faction that worships the grid). Bank the rule — play it COMPLETELY STRAIGHT. The joke and
  the ache should be the SAME OBJECT: people believe something ridiculous because there is nowhere on
  Earth for them. And write our factions' beliefs with RESPECT, never mockery — Jason Bright is absurd AND
  his compassion is total ("every feral ghoul you spare now is one that we can save later"), and it's
  mechanically true. That's the model for our 13 factions + our CHURCH-adjacent groups (ties DQV's tonal
  contrast Q69, Yakuza Q13, our faction web + tone + ART PHILOSOPHY; comedy that earns sincerity).
- W2 (the cruelty inside the punchline): STEAL it — our funniest Bohemia character should have a knife in
  them. A person whose delusion is a gag, whose labor is being exploited BY the sympathetic party, who was
  never going to be allowed to come. That's our Amalgamation/Network's kindest face doing its coldest math
  (ties Hearts of Stone Q70, Dead Money's Elijah Q72, our Amalgamation + Network + faction web).
- W3/W8 (every obstacle has a non-violent solution, all missable + the invisible kindness — our PACIFIST
  LAW's ancestor): this quest IS our Pacifist Law, shipped in 2010: the "demons" can be talked to, the
  barricaded man can be helped, the saboteur can be talked down. Bank it as the model — every Bohemia
  conflict gets a talking route, and the best outcome is often a small competent act nobody thanks you for
  (the 12.5% correction) that shows up in the ENDING SLIDES. But see the flaw: give every door SEVERAL
  keys, not one skill check (ties our PACIFIST LAW + Megaton law + ending-slides/persistent-consequence).
- W5 (the town lunatic is the reliable witness — our [READ]): bank the device — the least credible source
  in the district tells the literal truth, and the player dismisses it. That's testimony-vs-evidence, which
  is our whole [READ] premise: credibility and accuracy are different axes (ties our [READ] +
  recorded-vs-unrecorded + Caves of Qud's biased history Q56).
- W6 (errands as a world-tour with multiple solutions): bank the redemption of the fetch — if a quest must
  send you across the map, each leg should have 3+ solutions (buy / steal / lockpick / a Science check that
  reveals the CHILDREN'S TOYS are the fuel) and each should teach a different piece of the world (ties our
  quest factory + district + Pacifist Law).
- W7 (the choice between two kindnesses — our AMALGAMATION's central question): the deepest port. "Is it
  kinder to take a man's comforting delusion, or to let him die inside it?" is EXACTLY our Amalgamation's
  question at human scale: the dead are "alive" in the record; is telling people the truth mercy or
  cruelty? Bank it: our best moral choices should be between two KINDNESSES, not good and evil (ties our
  Amalgamation + recorded-vs-unrecorded + death-math + Megaton law + Liberate/Respect/Become, Tranquility
  Lane's memory-wipe debate Q78; a top-tier on-theme port).
- W9/W10 (you can loot a religion + it was the first quest designed): bank both — let the dynasty refuse
  and desecrate, with real costs (our Megaton law); and note the studio's method: the FIRST thing they
  built was a statement of intent (every conflict talkable, every faction human, every joke load-bearing).
  Our first-built content should declare our laws the same way (ties our design pillars + FACTORY LAW).
- FLAWS (bank): (a) great writing REDEEMS a fetch-chain but doesn't fix it — our connective tissue must
  itself be interesting (Ranni Q71, Morrowind Q74); (b) ship the PAYOFF — the cut crashed-rocket left the
  ending offscreen; ambition without resources leaves seams (our scope discipline + FACTORY LAW); (c) a
  GLOBAL morality ledger produces nonsense in bespoke situations (negative karma for looting the belongings
  of people who left the planet) — our conscience system must be CONTEXTUAL (Tranquility Lane's +300-karma
  massacre Q78 is the twin); (d) THE BELOVED QUEST IS THE ONE THAT EATS YOUR SAVE — "many corrupt saves have
  been reported... even hours after the quest has been completed"; ambition without QA destroys the thing
  you built (our regression gates + testing discipline + single-file/persistence hardening); and (e) don't
  gate the EMOTIONAL content behind one stat — every door needs several keys (Shadowheart Q76, our Pacifist
  Law).
