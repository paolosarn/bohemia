# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 109: THE BOUNTY HUNTER WANTS YOU TO PAY HIS BAR TAB / YOUR BODY IS SOMEONE ELSE'S PROPERTY (Pay Ethan's Tab, Citizen Sleeper)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where the man sent to repossess you doesn't kill you — he
makes you his source of income, and then loses his contract, and then needs your help, and then sells you
out anyway. Complete event flow, the tracker, the clock, the tab, the cancellation, Maywick, the gun, the
honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION, our survival accounting, our
standing, our fold, our MEDICINE currency, + our post-economic-collapse premise. Jump Over the Age (**a
one-person studio**, Gareth Damian Martin). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written
here.

Quest: the "Pay Ethan's Tab" / "Gain Ethan's Protection" drive chain, Citizen Sleeper (2022). You are a
**Sleeper**: "**A HUMAN WHOSE MIND HAS BEEN DIGITIZED AND HAS BEEN PUT INTO A ROBOT BODY TO BE CONTROLLED BY
THE ESSEN-ARP CORPORATION.**" You escaped. The corporation's pitch, from the store page, is the entire game:
"**ESSEN-ARP: TO THEM YOU ARE JUST PROPERTY, ONE MORE ASSET IN A PORTFOLIO THAT STRETCHES ACROSS THE
STARS.**"

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- YOU ARE THE UPLOADED DEAD AND SOMEBODY OWNS YOU (the premise — STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + NETWORK): a
  Sleeper is a **copy of a person's mind** running in a corporate frame. The legal argument is the horror:
  "**YOU ARE A CONVENIENT LOOPHOLE. A WAY OF CIRCUMNAVIGATING THE ILLEGALITY OF SENTIENT AI. AFTER ALL, YOU
  ARE AN EMULATION, NOT A TRUE DIGITAL BEING.**"
  - Punk: **AI is illegal, so they upload PEOPLE instead.** A Sleeper isn't a machine mind — it's a scanned
    human, which makes it a product rather than a person. That is the Amalgamation's founding paperwork,
    already written (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic + NETWORK +
    recorded-vs-unrecorded).
- THE BOUNTY HUNTER'S DEMAND IS A BAR TAB (the quest — STARTLINGLY our standing + survival accounting):
  "**THE SLEEPER'S TRACKER ALLOWS THEM TO BE FOUND BY ETHAN, A BOUNTY HUNTER HIRED BY ESSEN-ARP TO RETRIEVE
  ROGUE SLEEPERS. ETHAN AGREES TO LET THE SLEEPER LIVE AS LONG AS THEY PAY HIS BAR TAB EVERY WEEK.**"
  - The drive's own text: "**PAY ETHAN'S TAB: MAYBE IF YOU MANAGE TO PAY HIS TAB ETHAN WILL LEAVE YOU ALONE.
    WISHFUL THINKING PERHAPS, BUT WHAT ELSE CAN YOU DO?**"
  - The man with the legal right to drag you back to slavery decides he'd rather have **a weekly drinking
    allowance.** Your life is now a recurring subscription payment to an alcoholic (cf. our standing +
    survival accounting + faction web; the existential threat converted into a bill).
- AND YOUR BODY IS DESIGNED TO BREAK (the clock under everything — direct for our MEDICINE currency): "**THE
  SLEEPER DISCOVERS THAT THEIR ROBOT BODY IS PROGRAMMED WITH A FORM OF PLANNED OBSOLESCENCE, CAUSING IT TO
  BREAK DOWN OVER TIME WITHOUT THE USE OF A CHEMICAL STABILIZER MANUFACTURED BY ESSEN-ARP.**"
  - **The corporation that owns you built your body to decay without their drug.** Freedom has a
    prescription, and only the slaver has the pharmacy (STARTLINGLY our MEDICINE currency + AMALGAMATION +
    survival accounting; the leash that is a medication).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE CLOCK APPEARS BEFORE THE MAN DOES
- A player's account, and it's the design working perfectly: "**WHILE FENG PROMISED TO HELP ME GET RID OF MY
  TRACKER, THE TASKS THAT I HELP HIM WITH TAKE HIM OUT OF ACTION FOR LONG PERIODS OF TIME. LONG ENOUGH
  PERIODS OF TIME THAT EVENTUALLY A TIMER APPEARS. A TIMER WARNING OF THE INEVITABLE ARRIVAL OF A BOUNTY
  HUNTER, EMPLOYED BY ESSEN-ARP, SURELY TASKED WITH BRINGING ME BACK. **NOT KNOWING IF THE BOUNTY WANTS MY
  SLEEPER DEAD OR ALIVE IS NO SMALL DISCOMFORT.**"
- The antagonist arrives as **a clock on your screen** before he's a person. And the clock runs on cycles you
  spend doing other things (cf. our scheduler + survival accounting + district).
- The rule: "**WANT TO REMOVE THE TRACKER WITHIN YOUR SLEEPER'S FRAME THAT'S SENDING YOUR LOCATION BACK TO
  ESSEN-ARP AT ALL TIMES? YOU'LL NEED TO DO THAT BEFORE THE CLOCK FINISHES AND THEIR BOUNTY HUNTER
  ARRIVES.**"
- And you probably won't: "**TRY AS I DID TO GET MY TRACKER TAKEN CARE OF, I WASN'T SUCCESSFUL BEFORE ETHAN
  SHOWED UP.**"
### STAGE 1 — HE'S NOT A TERMINATOR, HE'S A DRUNK
- The same player: "**A SCRUFFY, BLONDE BOUNTY HUNTER WHO REEKED OF ALCOHOL AND DISREGARD FOR LIFE. I WASN'T
  FULLY SURE IN THOSE FIRST MOMENTS IF THAT DISREGARD WAS RESERVED FOR MY LIFE OR INCLUDED HIS OWN. OVER
  TIME I WOULD COME TO KNOW.**"
- The horror isn't that he's efficient. It's that he's a **guy in a bar with a job he doesn't care about**,
  and your continued existence is a side hustle (cf. our entities + companions + faction web).
### STAGE 2 — THE ARRANGEMENT (extortion as a relationship)
- You pay the tab. Every week. Forever. It's not a boss fight, it's **rent** — and it competes directly with
  your **stabilizer**, your food, and your repairs, because it's all the same currency and the same dice.
- The game's spine: "**EACH CYCLE YOU ROLL YOUR DICE. ASSIGN THEM TO [A] VAST RANGE OF ACTIONS AVAILABLE ON
  THE STATION. WITH EVERY ACTION, CHOOSE WHAT, AND WHO, MATTERS TO YOU.**"
- **Your dice are your day.** Paying a bounty hunter's bar tab means not fixing your body, not helping your
  friend, not earning your passage (STARTLINGLY our survival accounting + THREE CURRENCIES + city-builder;
  the threat that eats your calendar, not your health bar).
### STAGE 3 — THE CANCELLATION (the twist is a corporate email)
- "**HOWEVER, TWO WEEKS LATER, ETHAN DISCOVERS THAT ESSEN-ARP HAS CANCELED HIS CONTRACT AND GIVEN IT TO
  MAYWICK, A RUTHLESS MERCENARY.**"
- A player, live: "**IN THOSE LAST MOMENTS IN THE KOMPRESSOR, ETHAN HAD FOUND OUT THAT HIS CONTRACT TO
  RETRIEVE YOU HAD BEEN CANCELED. HAD I NOT COMPLETED THE WINTER LIGHT I WOULD HAVE BEEN CONFUSED, BUT
  RELIEVED. **KNOWING THAT SOMEONE ELSE WAS OUT THERE, THAT RELIEF NEVER CAME. NOW I KNEW THAT SOMEONE ELSE
  WAS AFTER ME, AND THAT THEY WERE FAR MORE WILLING TO HURT OTHERS TO ACCOMPLISH THEIR TASK.**"
- **The villain gets laid off.** Essen-Arp didn't fire him for failing — they just found a cheaper, meaner
  vendor. Your personal nemesis has been **replaced by a subcontractor**, and now he's unemployed and you're
  still hunted (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + NETWORK + faction web; the enemy who is also labor).
- And the fear it creates isn't for yourself: "**WOULD MY INSISTENCE TO SURVIVE HURT PEOPLE ON THE EYE?**"
### STAGE 4 — HE ASKS YOU FOR HELP (and the drive's title says everything)
- "**HE WARNS THE SLEEPER AND OFFERS TO PROTECT THEM IF THEY HELP HIM REPAY HIS DEBT TO THE BAR OWNER.**"
- The drive is literally named: "**GAIN ETHAN'S PROTECTION: **SOMEHOW YOU ARE ONCE AGAIN HELPING ETHAN.**"
- **"Somehow you are once again helping Ethan."** The quest log is exhausted with you. The man who hunted you
  now needs you to settle his debts so he can afford to defend you from the man hired to replace him. It's
  the gig economy eating its own (cf. our conscience system + faction web + standing + Megaton law).
### STAGE 5 — AND HE SELLS YOU ANYWAY (the design's honesty)
- "**IF THE SLEEPER FAILS TO REMOVE THEIR TRACKER IN TIME, ETHAN WILL TURN THE SLEEPER OVER TO MAYWICK, **EVEN
  IF THE SLEEPER HAS HELPED HIM.**"
- Read that again. **Even if you paid his tab. Even if you cleared his debt. Even if you helped him.** The
  relationship was never the mechanic — **the tracker was.** All that friendship bought exactly nothing
  because you didn't do the technical work.
- Punk: that's the anti-standing lesson. In most RPGs, being kind to a man makes him loyal. Here, being kind
  to a man makes him **sorry** while he hands you over (STARTLINGLY our standing + conscience system +
  Megaton law + no-clean-answer; the relationship that cannot beat the paperwork).
### STAGE 6 — THE ONE VIOLENT MOMENT IN THE GAME
- "**MAYWICK THEN KILLS ETHAN, AND THE SLEEPER USES ETHAN'S GUN TO SHOOT AND KILL MAYWICK.**"
- In a game with **no combat system**, this is the only gunfight. Ethan sells you out, and then the man he
  sold you to kills him for it, and you pick up the dead bounty hunter's weapon and kill his replacement.
- **The gun is Ethan's.** The only violence you commit is with the tool of the man who betrayed you, against
  the man who replaced him, while he bleeds out for nothing (cf. our combat dial + PACIFIST LAW + death-math).
### STAGE 7 — OR HE JUST LEAVES AND THINKS YOU DIED
- "**IF THE TRACKER IS REMOVED, MAYWICK NEVER ARRIVES AND ETHAN LEAVES THE STATION, BELIEVING THE SLEEPER TO
  BE DEAD.**"
- The good ending of this arc: **nothing happens.** The clock stops. Maywick is never hired. Ethan drinks,
  files a report saying you're dead, and gets on a shuttle.
- A player: "**I REMEMBER THE LAST TIME I VISITED THE KOMPRESSOR, ONLY TO FIND OUT THAT ETHAN, MY FORMER
  BOUNTY HUNTER, HAD DISAPPEARED.**"
- **You win by becoming a paperwork problem that resolves itself.** No confrontation, no speech, no closure.
  He's just not there anymore (STARTLINGLY our PACIFIST LAW + ART PHILOSOPHY + [READ]; the victory that is
  an absence).
### STAGE 8 — AND THE STABILIZER RUNS OUT ANYWAY
- The other thread closes on you regardless: "**SABINE GIVES THE SLEEPER SEVERAL VIALS OF STABILIZER, BUT
  WARNS THAT THEY WILL NO LONGER BE ABLE TO SELL ANY MORE, AS YANNICK WAS SOURCING THE VIALS THROUGH HIS
  CORPORATE CONTACTS IN ESSEN-ARP.**"
- You expose the corporate mole inside the gang, and the reward is that **your medicine supply dies with
  him.** Doing the right thing severed your own pharmacy.
- And the writer's read of that walk home, which is our tone exactly: "**WHEN THEY TOOK A SIMILAR WALK
  THROUGH THE LOWEND, HOLDING THE LAST FEW VIALS OF STABILIZER THEY WOULD BE ABLE TO FIND FOR A WHILE, A
  PARTING GIFT FROM A FRIEND. **BUT ESSEN-ARP DOESN'T OWN YOU ANY MORE. THEY CAN'T, BECAUSE THIS PLACE,
  THESE PEOPLE, OWN YOU. THEY ARE WHAT MAKES YOU GET UP EVERY CYCLE, THEY ARE WHAT KEEPS YOU BREATHING.**"

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE THREAT DEFLATES ONCE YOU'RE COMPETENT: a critic, bluntly — "**WITHIN HOURS, MONEY, ENERGY, AND
  CONDITION, YOUR THREE STATS, TAKE CARE OF THEMSELVES. THIS MEANS THE ONLY RESOURCE YOU'RE CONCERNED WITH
  IS TIME... **THE TWO ENTITIES, ETHAN AND THE HUNTER/KILLER, WHO COULD CAUSE YOU HARM ARE SOON RENDERED
  INNOCUOUS.**" LESSON (ours, hard): a survival game whose survival solves itself by hour four has spent its
  premise — our survival accounting must keep biting in act three, or the Amalgamation becomes set dressing.
- THE RISK SYSTEM DISARMS ITSELF: "**EVEN THE RISK TAKING IS SOON MINIMALIZED WITH PERKS SUCH AS KARMA,
  ALLOWING YOU TO REROLL YOUR DICE EACH ROUND, AND PROGNOSTICATION, ALLOWING YOU TO ANTICIPATE OUTCOMES.**"
  LESSON: if your upgrade tree's endpoint is "the dice stop mattering," you've built a countdown to your own
  irrelevance (our difficulty packages).
- THE PUZZLES SOLVE THEMSELVES: "**PUZZLES SOLVE THEMSELVES, AND THE OPTIONS YOU'RE PROVIDED ARE AXIOMATIC...
  I REALLY LIKE CAPTURING A CYBERNETIC ENTITY BY FORCING IT TO SELF-REFERENCE, FOR INSTANCE, **THOUGH I WISH
  THE GAME HAD LET ME FIGURE OUT THE SOLUTION MYSELF.**" LESSON (ours, direct): our [READ] must let the
  player DO the deduction, not watch the protagonist do it.
- THE CHARACTERS DON'T TOUCH EACH OTHER: "**CHARACTERS DON'T INTERACT WITH ONE ANOTHER MUCH... MEANING
  THERE'S NO STRAINS ON RELATIONSHIPS OR LOYALTIES WITH THE WAY YOU SPEND YOUR TIME.**" A game about a
  community whose community members never collide. LESSON: our faction web + companions need the friends to
  have opinions about EACH OTHER, or the station is a hub with kiosks.
- THE BETRAYAL IS UNCONDITIONAL AND THAT MIGHT BE TOO MUCH: "**ETHAN WILL TURN THE SLEEPER OVER TO MAYWICK,
  EVEN IF THE SLEEPER HAS HELPED HIM.**" Thematically brutal and correct; also, a player who did every
  favor gets the identical outcome to one who did none. LESSON (double-edged): if kindness changes nothing
  mechanically, some players will read it as the writer sneering — give it ONE visible effect.
- IT'S A GAME ABOUT PRECARITY WHERE NOBODY IS EVER REALLY CRUEL: "**BY AND LARGE, THE PEOPLE AND FACTIONS
  YOU INTERACT WITH REALLY WANT NOTHING MORE THAN TO LIVE THEIR LIVES IN PEACE.**" Lovely, and it means the
  system is the only villain and the system is offscreen. LESSON: an offscreen antagonist can't be beaten,
  only survived — decide on purpose whether that's your ending.
