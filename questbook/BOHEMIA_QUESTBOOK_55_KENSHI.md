# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 55: THE PURE SANDBOX / A WORLD THAT LIVES WITHOUT YOU (Kenshi)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the no-story pure sandbox, the world-that-lives-without-you, the
weaker-than-everything start, per-limb health/dismemberment/prosthetics, the "narrative abruptly changes"
emergent stories, skill-by-doing, faction dynamics that ignore you, investment-makes-you-care, the honest
flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's MOST EXTREME model for A LIVING WORLD INDIFFERENT TO THE
PLAYER + no-scripted-story emergence + brutal survival consequence. Gothic's (Q51) most extreme cousin;
a near-solo dev's magnum opus. Lo-Fi Games (Chris Hunt). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No
Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Kenshi (Lo-Fi Games, 2018; 12 years in dev, near-solo). A squad-based RPG/RTS/simulation sandbox in
a "sword-punk"/post-apocalyptic desert world. You start as a weak nobody with a few coins + a rusty sword,
dropped into an 870km2 world with ZERO story or guidance, weaker than almost everything. You decide what
to do: trade, build, fight, free slaves, or just survive. 2.3M+ copies, "Overwhelmingly Positive" (95%),
a cult landmark of emergent storytelling. (Economic-collapse-adjacent + post-collapse rebuild = on-theme.)

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE WORLD LIVES WITHOUT YOU (the purest form): "the world of Kenshi is there, it does not wait for your
  actions, it lives on its own." Factions war, trade, patrol, and react to each other INDEPENDENT of the
  player — you are not the center, not the chosen one, just another body in a harsh world. Gothic's (Q51)
  "you're the NPC to them," taken to the EXTREME — the world is fully indifferent (cf. Gothic Q51, RDR
  camp Q10; the ultimate you-don't-matter world).
- NO STORY, PURE EMERGENCE (the world IS a story generator): "no linear narrative; the plot happens
  through emergent narrative." No quests to accept, no arcs to close, no world to save (it's already
  ruined). Stories emerge from SYSTEMS colliding — "the world is dense enough for stories to emerge
  naturally from failure, survival, fear, obsession." You author your own goals (cf. RimWorld/DF Q44/Q45 —
  Kenshi is the 3D squad-scale version; direct-control emergence).
- START AS NOTHING, WEAKER THAN EVERYTHING (brutal earned progression): you begin weaker than almost every
  enemy — "you'll die, get enslaved, lose limbs, crawl through deserts bleeding out." One start is literal
  SLAVERY. Progression is EARNED against a world that will beat you down repeatedly; snowballing from a
  starving nobody to a force is the arc YOU forge (cf. Gothic start-as-nobody Q51, our hardcore survival;
  the extreme version).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how a pure sandbox generates story)
===============================================================================

### PER-LIMB HEALTH + DISMEMBERMENT + PROSTHETICS (consequence on the body — key craft)
- Each LIMB has its own HP: damage a leg -> can't walk; an arm -> can't fight; lose too much -> the limb
  is GONE (permanent) or you die. Lost limbs need PROSTHETICS (a wooden peg to advanced robotics — which
  some factions discriminate against). Injury is PERSISTENT, VISIBLE, and reshapes what a character can
  DO — bodily consequence that drives story (a squad crippled far from safety) (cf. Nemesis scars Q48,
  Darkest Dungeon afflictions Q41, our persistent-consequence + face/rig; the body remembers).
- "THE NARRATIVE ABRUPTLY CHANGES" (systems flip the story): a bounty hunt becomes a desperate survival
  crawl when half your squad is crippled + you can't carry the comatose. The emergent story PIVOTS on
  system state — you don't follow a plot, you RESPOND to a cascading situation (cf. Dwarf Fortress spirals
  Q45, RimWorld Q44; the story is the consequence-chain).

### SKILL-BY-DOING (progression through action)
- Skills level by DOING them — you get better at fighting by fighting, stealth by sneaking, etc. (some
  races better/worse at each). Growth is embodied in ACTIVITY, and higher skill even changes ANIMATIONS.
  You improve by living, not by spending points (cf. Morrowind skill-by-use Q37, our upbringing/skill).

### FACTIONS WITH THEIR OWN AGENDAS (a living political web)
- Many factions (the slaver United Cities, the dying-warrior Shek, the theocratic Holy Nation, anti-
  slavers, Hivers with a caste system) each have laws, economies, and motives, war/trade/patrol on their
  own, and REACT to player interference. The player can ally, betray, or topple them — a living political
  world that CHANGES STATE based on your actions (cf. our faction web Q46/Q43, Gothic camps Q51; the
  world's politics run without you but respond to you).

### SQUAD-SCALE + BASE-BUILDING (RPG -> management shift)
- You grow from one character to a SQUAD (recruit named NPCs + random villagers), then build a BASE — at
  which point the game SHIFTS into a management sim (food, defense, resources, raids from "landowners"
  demanding tax). One seamless system-set spans lone-survivor RPG -> squad tactics -> settlement
  management (cf. our fold: lone-dynast -> settlement -> city-builder; the scale-shift).

### INVESTMENT MAKES YOU CARE (attachment without scripting)
- Kenshi never writes its characters deep — yet losing a long-time squad member is DEVASTATING, because
  of the INVESTMENT you put into leveling them from nobody to hero. Attachment is MANUFACTURED by player
  labor, not authored backstory — "clever way to get you caught up in their fates" (cf. RimWorld/DF Q44/
  Q45, XCOM; our fold — you care because you BUILT them).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE SEAMS SHOW / EMPTY FOR SOME: critics note the simulation's seams "ripple easily" — you can't talk
  to most NPCs, the starting area can feel like "nothing to engage with," and "if I created my own
  narrative it'd be in my head, not the game." LESSON (critical): pure emergence only works if the SYSTEMS
  are dense enough to generate story reliably; a thin sandbox leaves the player doing all the imaginative
  work alone — we need enough AUTHORED texture + hooks that stories emerge IN the game, not just in the
  player's head (our authored spine over the sandbox — our edge over pure Kenshi).
- BRUTAL, UNGUIDED ONBOARDING: "does next to nothing to help the player grasp its mechanics" + weaker-
  than-everything start + no goals = many bounce immediately. LESSON (recurring #1): even a pure sandbox
  needs to teach its systems + offer soft direction, or it filters out all but the hardcore (our SUN-MODE/
  onboarding + soft-goals; Dwarf Fortress accessibility lesson Q45).
- NO GOALS = AIMLESS FOR MANY: "no victory conditions, no failure save losing everyone" — some get bored
  fast ("more fun to watch than play"). LESSON: total openness isn't for everyone; OFFER optional goals/
  arcs (our Amalgamation spine + quests) as a SPINE the free-play hangs on (our edge: authored goals +
  sandbox freedom).
- TECHNICAL ROUGHNESS: dated graphics, poor optimization, frequent load-pauses, stuck-on-load bugs needing
  manual fixes. LESSON (recurring): ambition on a near-solo budget shows in polish; ship STABLE + legible
  even if modest (our single-file/mobile discipline, Bloodlines ship-stable Q39).
- SPARSE SURVIVAL MECHANICS ARE UNEVEN: hunger mostly exists to cap squad size; no thirst; beds mostly
  superfluous — some survival systems feel vestigial. LESSON: every survival system should EARN its place
  + interlock (Slay the Spire Q52); don't ship half-systems (our survival-accounting must be coherent).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the living-world + emergence ceiling, with our authored spine
===============================================================================
Kenshi is the MOST EXTREME model for a living, indifferent world + pure emergence + brutal survival
consequence — the ceiling Gothic (Q51) and RimWorld/DF (Q44/Q45) point toward. Bohemia takes its LIVING-
WORLD + CONSEQUENCE + INVESTMENT-ATTACHMENT but keeps our AUTHORED Amalgamation spine (fixing Kenshi's
"it's all in your head" flaw). Its near-solo origin + post-collapse-rebuild are directly kindred.
- W1/W7 (the world lives without you + factions with agendas): bank the LIVING-WORLD ceiling — our
  district + factions should run their own lives/economies/wars INDEPENDENT of the dynasty + react when
  the dynasty interferes; the player is a participant, not the center (Gothic Q51 taken further). Our
  faction web + world-model + entities are the engine (ties our faction graph Q46/Q43, Gothic Q51, RDR
  Q10; but we keep authored hooks so story emerges IN-game).
- W2/W9 (emergence + investment-attachment — with our fold): our GENERATIONAL FOLD is Kenshi's
  investment-attachment at its most powerful — you care about heirs/survivors because you BUILT them from
  nothing across generations (not because we scripted them). Bank emergence UNDER our authored spine: the
  world generates texture (RimWorld/DF/Kenshi Q44/Q45), the Amalgamation arc gives it MEANING — our EDGE
  over pure-sandbox Kenshi's "it's all in your head" flaw (ties our fold, RimWorld/DF Q44/Q45, Rogue
  Legacy Q49).
- W3 (start as nothing, earn everything): our hardcore survival + earned-standing (Gothic Q51) should
  start the dynasty WEAK against an indifferent world — earn every scrap; the snowball from nobody to
  force is the player's forged arc (ties Gothic Q51, our roguelite/hardcore stance; but our regression-
  gate/no-brick-wall keeps it FAIR — the Kenshi "brutal unguided" flaw is a DON'T).
- W4 (per-limb consequence + prosthetics — on-theme): bank PERSISTENT, VISIBLE bodily consequence — injury
  that reshapes what a survivor can DO, permanent loss, prosthetics with social meaning (some factions
  discriminate) — the body remembers, driving story. Deeply on-theme for a hardcore post-collapse world +
  our face/rig + persistent-consequence systems (ties Nemesis scars Q48, Darkest Dungeon afflictions Q41,
  our death-math/injury; a strong survival-RPG pillar).
- W5 ("the narrative abruptly changes"): design Bohemia's survival so the STORY PIVOTS on system state —
  a supply run becomes a desperate evacuation when the district is raided + half the squad is hurt; the
  player responds to a CASCADE, not a script (ties Dwarf Fortress spirals Q45, RimWorld Q44, our survival-
  accounting + Storyteller Q44; emergent drama under the authored arc).
- W6 (skill-by-doing): consider skill-by-USE for dynasts/survivors (grow by living, animations reflect
  mastery) — embodied progression (ties Morrowind Q37, Gothic trainers Q51, our upbringing).
- W8 (squad-scale -> management shift): our FOLD already IS Kenshi's scale-shift — lone dynast -> settlement
  -> city-builder management; bank the seamless system-set spanning personal survival to settlement macro
  (ties our fold + city-builder + survival-accounting; validated).
- W10 (near-solo magnum opus): Kenshi (near-solo, 12 years, 2.3M copies) + Dwarf Fortress Q45 + Stardew
  PROVE a singular solo vision can become a landmark — direct encouragement for Bohemia's solo ambition +
  our post-collapse-rebuild kinship (ties our solo-dev reality, DF Q45; ship modest-but-stable, not bloated).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): make the SYSTEMS dense + authored enough that stories emerge IN-GAME, not just in the
  player's head (the Kenshi "seams show / it's all in my head" flaw — our authored Amalgamation spine +
  hooks are the fix, our EDGE); TEACH the systems + offer SOFT GOALS (don't ship brutal + unguided —
  accessibility #1, Q45; our SUN-MODE/onboarding); OFFER an authored SPINE the free-play hangs on (our
  Amalgamation arc + quests — total aimlessness bores most); ship STABLE + legible on our platform (single-
  file/mobile discipline, Bloodlines Q39); and make every SURVIVAL system EARN its place + interlock (no
  vestigial half-systems — Slay the Spire elegance Q52, our survival-accounting coherence).
