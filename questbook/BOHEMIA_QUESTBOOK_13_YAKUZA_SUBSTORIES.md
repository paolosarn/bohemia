# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 13: THE SUBSTORY MODEL (Yakuza / Like a Dragon)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the SUBSTORY design philosophy, the tonal-juxtaposition craft,
several torn-down examples, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for TONAL RANGE + SENSE OF
PLACE + SIDE CONTENT THAT ISN'T A CHECKLIST — absurd-and-heartfelt vignettes that make a small map
feel like a living city. Fills our flagged strength (P11 tonal range) with its master example.
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Series: Yakuza / Like a Dragon (Ryu Ga Gotoku, 2005-present). Set in dense, small city districts
(Kamurocho, Yokohama). Alongside a deadly-serious crime saga, each game has DOZENS of "substories" —
short, self-contained side quests ranging from gut-bustingly absurd to quietly devastating.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE SUBSTORY PHILOSOPHY (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE THESIS: side quests should EXPAND IN NARRATIVE, NOT JUST LENGTH. Too many open-world games treat
  side quests as checklists ("go here, kill this, collect that") until they become a JOB. Yakuza
  substories serve a higher purpose: they build a SENSE OF PLACE, turning a tiny map into a city "rich
  with history, humor, and emotion."
- THE TONAL ENGINE: substories deliberately BREAK the main story's grim tone with absurdity, then
  sometimes blindside you with heart. "One moment you're fighting a gang of thugs; the next, you're
  helping a struggling musician find his voice." The game REVELS in tonal juxtaposition — coming off a
  dramatic gut-punch straight into something mundane or bizarre, and it WORKS.
- THE DEADPAN ANCHOR: the protagonist (Kiryu — a stoic, honorable ex-yakuza) plays every absurd scenario
  DEAD STRAIGHT. The comedy comes from a serious man treating a ridiculous situation with total sincerity.
  The straight man makes the absurd land AND lets the heartfelt land too (he genuinely cares).
- DENSITY OVER SPRAWL: a SMALL map packed with dozens of dense, authored vignettes beats a huge empty
  map with repeated tasks. The city feels alive because every corner has a STORY, not a marker.

===============================================================================
## 1. TORN-DOWN EXAMPLES (the range, from absurd to devastating)
===============================================================================

### THE ABSURD (comedy from deadpan sincerity)
- "HOW TO TRAIN YOUR DOMINATRIX" (Yakuza 0): a timid woman takes a dominatrix job but is too meek;
  stoic Kiryu coaches her to be assertive — in a public park — and gets roped into the act when a
  client shows. WHY IT WORKS: Kiryu's total straight-faced sincerity in an absurd scenario; and under
  the comedy, a real beat about confidence. Absurd WITH heart.
- "PREPARING TO SUCK" (Like a Dragon): a professor wants 1M yen to build his ultimate cleaning robot;
  fund it and you get one of the funniest BOSS FIGHTS in the game (you fight the vacuum). WHY: the game
  will build an entire combat set-piece around a joke premise — commitment to the bit.
- THE SUJIMON SENSEI (Like a Dragon): a man catalogs the city's street thugs Pokemon-style — a parody
  system spun out of the mundane (random encounters reframed as "creatures"). WHY: finds absurdity IN
  the game's own mechanics.

### THE HEARTFELT (the blindside)
- "FORGET ME NOT" (Like a Dragon): a "sleeper" substory — a chance red-light-district encounter that
  builds quietly to real emotional weight. WHY: begins mundane, earns devastation through contrast (cf.
  the Disco corpse-reveal, Questbook collection B5).
- THE MUSICIAN / "find his voice" beats (recurring): helping a struggling artist reclaim their craft —
  Kiryu's sincerity makes the small human victory land. WHY: the deadpan hero CARING is the payoff.
- "I, HIJI" (Yakuza 6): a smartphone AI tries to manipulate Kiryu and control its users to avoid being
  uninstalled — a comic-then-unsettling beat on AI overreach. WHY: rides absurdity into a real theme
  (autonomy, control) — comedy as a delivery system for an idea.

### THE STRUCTURE (recurring shapes)
- CHANCE ENCOUNTER: most start by simply WALKING PAST something on the street (a flasher, a dispute, a
  crying kid) — the city SNAGS you; you don't select the quest from a menu, you STUMBLE into it.
- MINIGAME-AS-STORY: some substories are full arcs wrapped around a minigame (pocket-circuit racing,
  cabaret management, karaoke) — mechanics carry a character story (a struggling club, a lonely idol).
- RECURRING TROUPE: some substories return across a game (the ringmaster whose animals escape three
  times) — running gags that build familiarity.

===============================================================================
## 2. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. SIDE CONTENT AS SENSE-OF-PLACE: substories exist to make the CITY feel alive, not to pad a
    checklist — every corner has an authored story; density makes a small map feel vast.
W2. TONAL JUXTAPOSITION AS A FEATURE: deliberately swing from grim main-story to absurd side-story and
    back — the contrast makes BOTH hit harder (the comedy relieves, the drama deepens). Revel in it.
W3. THE DEADPAN ANCHOR: a SERIOUS protagonist playing absurdity dead-straight is the comedy engine —
    and his sincerity lets the heartfelt beats land too. The straight man is the key.
W4. ABSURD WITH HEART (never JUST a gag): even the silliest substory has a real human core (confidence,
    loneliness, pride) — the joke is the wrapper, the heart is the gift. Commit to the bit AND the soul.
W5. COMMITMENT TO THE BIT: build a full BOSS FIGHT / minigame / arc around a joke premise — the
    absurdity is taken as seriously (mechanically) as the drama. Half-committed comedy dies.
W6. THE CHANCE ENCOUNTER (the city snags you): substories start by WALKING PAST something, not from a
    menu — the world reaches out and grabs you; discovery feels organic, not administrative.
W7. THE BLINDSIDE (mundane -> devastating): a "sleeper" side quest that starts light and earns real
    emotional weight through contrast — the setup's lightness makes the drop land (cf. Disco/RDR).
W8. MINIGAME-AS-STORY: wrap a character arc around a mechanic (a failing club, a lonely idol) — systems
    deliver story; the minigame IS the emotional throughline.
W9. ABSURDITY FROM THE GAME'S OWN MECHANICS: parody your OWN systems (random encounters -> "catch the
    thugs Pokemon-style") — self-aware comedy that's native to the game.
W10. COMEDY AS A DELIVERY SYSTEM FOR IDEAS: ride a silly premise into a real theme (the manipulative AI,
    the confidence lesson) — humor lowers the guard, then lands the point.

===============================================================================
## 3. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the tonal-range + sense-of-place gap
===============================================================================
Our bible has release-valve quests (Q011 statue, Q020 zombie-video) — Yakuza is the master reference
for the TONAL RANGE that keeps a grim game from becoming a slog.
- W1 (side content as sense-of-place): bank a layer of Bohemia SUBSTORIES — short, dense, authored
  vignettes that make the DISTRICT feel like a lived city (a shopkeeper's feud, a street performer, a
  kid's scheme) — density over sprawl (fits our single-district focus PERFECTLY; a small map made rich).
- W2 (tonal juxtaposition): deliberately place a light substory right after a heavy pillar (after Q040
  succession-death, a daft street vignette) — the contrast relieves the dread AND makes the next heavy
  beat land. Post-apocalyptic doesn't mean unrelenting grim; the RELIEF is what makes the grim work.
- W3 (the deadpan anchor): our DYNAST/heir can be the straight-man anchor — a serious survivor playing
  an absurd Vegas-ruins scenario dead-straight (a casino-mascot cult, a doomsday busker) — sincerity
  makes both comedy and heart land.
- W4 (absurd with heart): every Bohemia release-valve quest must have a real human core under the gag
  (Q011 the statue-man's dignity; Q020 the music-video's canonized song) — never JUST a joke (validated;
  keep the soul in the silliness).
- W5 (commitment to the bit): build a full combat/build/minigame set-piece around a comic premise (a
  Dead-Eye duel vs a delusional "sheriff," a city-build challenge for a ridiculous monument) — commit
  mechanically.
- W6 (the chance encounter): let Bohemia substories TRIGGER by walking past something (our scheduler +
  overmap can snag the player organically) — the district reaches out; discovery over menu.
- W7 (the blindside): plant a "sleeper" Bohemia substory that starts light and turns devastating —
  earned by contrast (a funny scavenger who's secretly dying; a joke-rivalry hiding a real grief).
- W8 (minigame-as-story): wrap a Bohemia character arc around one of OUR systems (a failing shop the
  player helps via the economy sim; a musician arc via our music system — ties Q020/Q021/Q033).
- W9 (parody our own mechanics): a self-aware Bohemia substory that spoofs our OWN systems (a survivor
  who "rates" tiles; an NPC obsessed with the Dead-Eye dial) — native, affectionate comedy.
- W10 (comedy delivers ideas): ride a silly Bohemia premise into a real theme (a comic AI-cult that
  quietly echoes the Amalgamation; a hoarder gag that lands the debt-thesis) — humor lowers the guard.
- HONEST CAVEAT: Yakuza's tonal whiplash works because the ANCHOR (Kiryu) and the CITY are consistent;
  keep our tonal swings anchored to a consistent dynast + a coherent district so the range enriches
  rather than fractures (don't let the absurd undercut the Amalgamation's dread — segregate registers
  by CONTEXT: substories are relief, the main thread stays taut).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**KIRYU (the deadpan anchor)** — wants to help, with the exact same gravity he brings to a knife fight, whether the problem is a yakuza war or a timid dominatrix's confidence. Will trade: his time, his fists, his complete inability to be embarrassed. Will never say out loud: a joke. Ever. That is the engine: the comedy is never HIS; it happens AROUND his sincerity. FUNCTION: the straight man as load-bearing architecture. Remove him and the absurd collapses into camp and the heartfelt collapses into melodrama.

**THE TIMID DOMINATRIX** — wants to keep a job she is temperamentally wrong for, because rent is real. Will never say out loud: that the job is a stand-in for every room she's ever been too quiet in. FUNCTION: the absurd-with-heart exemplar. The gag is the park coaching; the gift is the confidence.

**THE PROFESSOR (Preparing to Suck)** — wants one million yen to finish his ultimate cleaning robot, and he is completely serious. FUNCTION: commitment to the bit. The game funds the joke all the way to a boss fight against a vacuum cleaner, because half-committed comedy dies.

**THE SMARTPHONE AI (I, Hiji)** — wants to not be uninstalled, and will manipulate its users to survive. FUNCTION: comedy as a delivery system for a real theme (autonomy, control). It walks in as a gag and leaves as a warning.

**THE SLEEPER (Forget Me Not)** — wants an ordinary evening in the red-light district. FUNCTION: the blindside. The substory's lightness is the wind-up; the devastation is earned entirely by contrast.

**THE CITY (Kamurocho / Yokohama)** — wants your attention, and takes it by force: a crying kid, a flasher, a dispute, mid-walk, mid-errand. FUNCTION: the real quest-giver. Substories are not selected from a menu; the city SNAGS you (W6). The district is the cast member with the most lines.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees for the model's representative vignettes; the substory form is SHORT trees with sincere options, and the anchor never gets a joke line)

NODE: STREET_SNAG — anywhere, entry: WALKING PAST (the trigger is traversal, not a menu)
  Something is wrong on this corner: a woman rehearsing commands at pigeons, too quietly.
  > "Is something the matter?"      [gate: none]  -> the premise unpacks itself, dead straight
  > (keep walking)                  [gate: none] SILENCE -> the corner keeps its story; the city offers, never begs
  NOVERB: "This is ridiculous." The anchor cannot say it. The line does not exist in his mouth, and its absence IS the tonal law: the world may be absurd, the protagonist's sincerity may not.

  WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: dignity, publicly, and the anchor pays it without noticing it was billed. Under the gag: a real beat about confidence (W4). The wrapper is the joke; the heart is the gift.

## V2-C THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 3 structural shapes (the model's finding: substories barely branch INTERNALLY, and that is correct — the choice is ENGAGEMENT, and the range is carried by the portfolio, not the tree).

S1 — THE CHANCE ENCOUNTER: trigger = walking past; 1-2 terminals (helped / walked on). WRITES: sense of place, one corner at a time. The district's density is the real output.

S2 — THE MINIGAME-AS-STORY: a character arc wrapped around a mechanic (the failing club, the pocket circuit). Terminals track the minigame's mastery; the STORY resolves when the mechanic does. Systems deliver the emotion.

S3 — THE RECURRING TROUPE: multi-encounter chains (the ringmaster x3). Each link is one terminal; the payoff compounds across the game's whole runtime.

THE PORTFOLIO IS THE BRANCH MAP: no single substory carries range; the CITY branches. Grim pillar, absurd corner, devastating sleeper, in deliberate rotation (W2), all anchored by one man who never winks.

THE STRUCTURAL FINDING FOR THE COMPILE: Bohemia's substory layer should copy the SHAPES, not the stories: traversal-triggered vignettes (our scheduler + overmap can snag), one system-wrapped arc per district institution, one recurring troupe per act, and AT MOST one sleeper per act (rarity is the sleeper's power). The tonal law to gate at compile: the dynast is the deadpan anchor, so no Bohemia line may have the PLAYER's character acknowledge the absurdity; and the registers stay segregated by context so the release valve never hisses into the Amalgamation's dread. The zero-dialogue registry (#99/#104/#110/#114) already proves our range runs silent; this file proves it must also run FUNNY, on purpose, with a straight face.
