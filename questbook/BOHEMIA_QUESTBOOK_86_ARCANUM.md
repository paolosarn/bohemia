# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 86: THE WORLD IS CHANGING AND IT WILL COST YOU / THE VILLAIN WHO WENT AND LOOKED (Arcanum: Of Steamworks & Magick Obscura)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the magic-vs-technology schism (two systems that MECHANICALLY cancel
each other), the industrial revolution as the actual plot, the villain who wants to kill everything and is
completely sane, the dumb-character playthrough, necromancy-as-interrogation, the pacifist-to-genocide
range, the reputation system that names your specific deeds, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. Called by
the CRPG Addict "the best RPG ever made"; the debut of the Fallout team's own studio. STARTLINGLY relevant
to our post-economic-collapse premise, our faction web, our Amalgamation's argument, + our Pacifist Law.
Troika Games (Tim Cain, Leonard Boyarsky, Jason Anderson). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No
Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Arcanum (Troika, 2001). A Tolkien fantasy world 60 years into its own INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION. Magic
works; so do steam engines; and they interfere with each other physically. You survive a zeppelin crash, a
dying gnome hands you a ring and says "take it to the boy," and everyone starts calling you a dead elven
god reborn. Troika's debut, and their best.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE THEME IS A MECHANIC THAT CANCELS ITSELF (the design's spine): "Uniquely, KNOWLEDGE OF TECHNOLOGY IN
  ARCANUM DISABLES MAGICAL APTITUDE, AND VICE VERSA, so that the player will ALWAYS HAVE TO CHOOSE BETWEEN
  THE TWO (or find a very, very careful balance) and (eventually) BE SHUNNED BY THE OTHER BRANCH."
  - It's not flavor. It's PHYSICS: "this is a world where MAGES HAVE TO SIT ON THE BACK OF A TRAIN BECAUSE
    THEIR POWER WOULD INTERFERE WITH THE ENGINE."
  - And it scales to politics: "escalating into A MINIATURE COLD WAR, magic wielders would SABOTAGE THE
    TRAINS in cities while the followers of technology would use these conflicts AS AN EXCUSE TO LIMIT ALL
    MAGIC USERS' FREEDOMS."
  - A worldview you can BUILD INTO, that physically excludes the other worldview, and that the world's
    factions have organized around. Theme as a stat, theme as a law of nature, theme as a political
    crisis (cf. FFT's Faith stat Q81, our faction web + conscience system; the schism you must pick).
- THE APOCALYPSE IS AN ECONOMY, NOT A DEMON (startlingly our premise): the real story is that the world is
  ENDING because it's MODERNIZING. "The game takes place at THE TURNING POINT OF HISTORY, and the story of
  the kingdom of Cumbria shows WHAT HAPPENS WHEN PARTS OF SOCIETY DESPERATELY CLING TO TRADITION AND REJECT
  SCIENTIFIC PROGRESS. THE PROSPERITY OF TECHNOLOGICALLY INCLINED HUMANS LEADS TO A DECLINE OF THE TYPICAL
  FANTASY RACES like dwarves and elves, and the isolationist mages of Tulla."
  - "Political conflicts concerning ORC LABORERS, HALF-OGRE BREEDING and the INDUSTRIAL COUNCIL reflect
    HISTORICAL ISSUES FROM THE LATE 19TH CENTURY: TRADE UNIONS, EUGENICS..."
  - The elves aren't dying because of a dark lord. They're dying because of FACTORIES (STARTLINGLY our
    post-ECONOMIC-collapse premise; cf. FFT's bubble-economy origin Q81; the apocalypse as an economic
    transition).
- THE VILLAIN IS SANE, KIND, AND WANTS TO KILL EVERYTHING (the best antagonist argument in the canon):
  KERGHAN is a necromancer banished to the Void a thousand years ago. His plan: end ALL life. His reasoning:
  "He's decided that BECAUSE BEING ALIVE IS PAINFUL, SUMMONED SPIRITS SUFFER TERRIBLY FROM THE EXPERIENCE,
  AND THE FINAL AFTERLIFE IS PERFECT BLISS, THEN THE LOGICAL SOLUTION IS END ALL LIFE."
  - He isn't guessing. He is the world's greatest NECROMANCER — he has TALKED TO THE DEAD, repeatedly, for
    a thousand years. He's the only person who has DATA.
  - "The writing in this game deserves credit considering that THE COMPLETELY SANE KERGHAN whose primary
    goal is to kill absolutely everything ISN'T A COMPLETE MONSTER IN ANY WAY."
  - A player's verdict: the game "asks PHILOSOPHICAL QUESTIONS WHICH CHALLENGE PREJUDICES REGARDING LIFE
    AND DEATH, HAPPINESS AND SUFFERING, IN A WAY NO OTHER GAME HAS EVER BEEN ABLE TO MATCH" (cf. our
    AMALGAMATION's argument, Deus Ex Q17, Planescape Q12; the villain you have to actually out-argue).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (freedom, engineered)
===============================================================================

### YOU CAN PLAY A CHARACTER TOO STUPID TO TALK (the reactivity flex)
- Dump Intelligence low enough and your dialogue becomes monosyllabic grunting — and the ENTIRE GAME is
  rewritten for it. "This is one of very few elite RPGs that can ALTER EVERY SINGLE ENCOUNTER/CONVERSATION
  BASED ON YOUR CHARACTER BUILD — Arcanum only does this based on your intelligence score, but of course
  other ability scores and factors will change the vast majority of dialogue."
- And it goes further: "EVERY VOICE-ACTED NPC HAS EXTRA DIALOGUE DEPENDING ON YOUR STATUS. This isn't merely
  limited to whether your character is MENTALLY HANDICAPPED or TALKING TO THE NPC'S GHOST, but also
  includes INVISIBLE, TRANSFORMED, SHRUNKEN, and WHETHER THEY'RE DRESSED AS A BARBARIAN OR COMPLETELY
  NAKED."
- Race, gender, charisma, reputation, tech-vs-magick, intelligence — all of it rewrites the world's response
  (cf. our multidimensional standing + district; reactivity as the point).
### KILL THE QUEST-GIVER, THEN INTERROGATE HIS GHOST (the best systemic solution in the canon)
- "There is indeed the possibility that YOU COULD KILL SOMEONE WITH QUEST-RELEVANT INFORMATION. If you don't
  find any items on their person with the information you need, YOU CAN EVEN CAST A BLACK NECROMANCY SPELL
  TO CONJURE UP THEIR SPIRIT AND INTERROGATE THEM THAT WAY. PARTY MEMBERS WITH VOICES EVEN HAVE DIALOGUE
  FOR THIS!"
- The game refuses to have a fail state. Murder the plot and the plot has a workaround — with reactive
  companion barks (cf. our [READ] + Megaton law + no-brick-wall; the system that survives the player).
### THE FULL RANGE: PACIFIST TO GENOCIDE (the moral bandwidth)
- "The game is also notable for being POSSIBLE TO COMPLETE IN EVERYTHING FROM A COMPLETELY PACIFIST TO A
  COMPLETELY VIOLENT WAY." "It is possible to TALK THROUGH LARGE PORTIONS OF THE GAME'S PLOT WITHOUT
  FIGHTING," and "certain portions of THE MAIN QUEST CAN BE SOLVED MORE EASILY THROUGH DIALOGUE THAN
  THROUGH COMBAT."
- The other end: "an evil character can do a quest to DEPOPULATE THE ENTIRE TOWN OF STILLWATER
  BY HIM/HERSELF." And if you SIDE with Kerghan, "THE TWO OF YOU KILL EVERY LIVING THING IN ARCANUM
  TOGETHER, after which HE'LL TRY TO KILL YOU and you end up defeating him."
- Both ends are fully built (cf. our PACIFIST LAW + Megaton law; the real bandwidth).
### REPUTATION NAMES YOUR SPECIFIC DEEDS (the standing system, done right)
- "The Player Character gets better reaction from other characters (INCLUDING SHOPKEEPERS WHO GIVE THEM
  DISCOUNTS) if he or she helped the inhabitants of a given settlement. Additionally, CHARACTERS SOMETIMES
  MENTION SPECIFIC DEEDS OF THE PLAYER CHARACTER AND ACT ACCORDINGLY. Of course, EVIL DEEDS GET ATTENTION
  AND RESPECT OF SHADY CHARACTERS (INCLUDING PARTY MEMBERS WHO BASE THEIR DECISION TO JOIN THE PLAYER
  CHARACTER ON HIS OR HER KARMA METER)."
- Not a bar — a MEMORY. People bring up the thing you did, by name (cf. Ultima VII Q83, our standing +
  conscience system; reputation as specific memory).
### THE WORLD'S MYTHOLOGY IS A LIE ABOUT LABOR (the satire)
- The Industrial Revolution began when "a gifted young boy named GILBERT BATES, in a bid to be accepted by
  a Dwarven clan, MADE MODIFICATIONS TO A STEAM-ENGINE which the Dwarves used to drain their mines."
- The truth: "an in-game character called GIL BATES. Considered the inventor of steam engines, ACTUALLY
  TURNS OUT HE STOLE THE IDEA FROM THE DWARVES." The father of the modern age is a plagiarist, and the
  people he stole from are now the underclass (cf. our [READ] + faction web + Vegas; the founding myth
  that's theft).
### DEATH IS CHEAP, SO BANISHMENT IS THE REAL SENTENCE (the worldbuilding logic)
- "BOTH MAGES AND TECHNOLOGISTS HAVE READY ACCESS to spells, scrolls, and chemical concoctions that CAN
  RESURRECT ANYONE BODY AND SOUL INTACT. THIS IS PRECISELY WHY BEING BANISHED TO THE VOID IS SUCH A BIG
  DEAL, since it isn't killing someone SO MUCH AS TOSSING THEM INTO AN ALTERNATE DIMENSION FROM WHICH THERE
  IS NO APPARENT RETURN."
- Work out what your world's rules make CHEAP, and the punishments reorganize themselves (cf. our death-math
  + Amalgamation; consequences derived from the setting's own physics).
### AND KERGHAN WALKED INTO HIS PRISON SMILING (the villain's patience)
- "Banishment was viewed to be a FATE WORSE THAN DEATH, and everyone who earned it was DRAGGED KICKING AND
  SCREAMING. KERGHAN MEEKLY GOES TO HIS FATE. Of course, this is something of a BATMAN GAMBIT, as HE WAS
  COUNTING ON CONTINUING HIS RESEARCH IN THE TIMELESS VOID."
- He let them throw him into eternity because eternity was a LAB (cf. our Amalgamation's patience; the
  antagonist who accepts your punishment because it's what he wanted).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE PHILOSOPHY IS ALL AT THE END, AND MOST PLAYERS NEVER GOT THERE: "A DOWNSIDE IS THAT THE MAJORITY OF
  THIS QUESTIONING OCCURS AT THE VERY END. SO IF YOU DO PLAY ARCANUM, FINISH IT — ELSE YOU MISS ITS
  BRILLIANCE." The reviewer literally recommends watching Kerghan's cutscene on YouTube instead. LESSON
  (the third confirmation — Morrowind Q74, Dragon's Dogma Q84): if the THEME is in the last hour, most
  players never receive it.
- THE COMBAT IS BAD AND THE ECONOMY OF XP MAKES IT WORSE: "experience is ONLY gained either by completing
  quests or BY THE AMOUNT OF DAMAGE DONE TO AN ENEMY. THIS MAKES PLAYING A DUMB, UNINTELLIGENT,
  SWORD-WIELDING, ALL-OUT-MELEE OGRE THE EASIEST WAY BY FAR TO FINISH THE GAME." A game about ideas pays
  you for hitting things. LESSON (sharp, ours): YOUR XP CURVE IS YOUR REAL THESIS — if we reward violence,
  we've written a violent game no matter what the dialogue says (our Pacifist Law + conscience system MUST
  be reflected in progression).
- THE LEVEL CAP ARRIVES AT THE HALFWAY MARK: "the level cap of 50 CAN BE REACHED BEFORE YOU'VE EVEN
  FINISHED HALF THE GAME, QUITE EASILY at that." LESSON: our progression must last the arc (our fold +
  standing pacing).
- IT SHIPPED CATASTROPHICALLY BROKEN, AND IT KILLED THE STUDIO: "TROIKA'S GAMES ALL WERE RELEASED IN A VERY
  BROKEN STATE and the official patches COULDN'T ALWAYS FIX ALL THE PROBLEMS." Arcanum was incompatible
  with common video cards; its own COPY PROTECTION broke people's sound cards; the community has patched it
  for twenty years. Troika made three games and died in 2005. LESSON (the hardest one in the questbook for
  a solo dev): THE STUDIO THAT MADE THE BEST-WRITTEN RPGs OF ITS ERA WAS KILLED BY BUGS, NOT BY WRITING.
  Our regression gates + test suite are not bureaucracy, they're survival.
- THE COMPANION AI IS ACTIVELY SELF-DESTRUCTIVE: "they'll happily PUT ON UNIDENTIFIED CLOTHES and keep
  wearing them EVEN IF THEY'RE POISONED"; a recruitable dwarven mage "keeps turning into a fire elemental,
  exhausts himself after a few turns and FALLS UNCONSCIOUS AFTER EVERY SINGLE BATTLE"; and "GOOD COMPANIONS
  GET ANGRY IF YOU COMMIT EVIL ACTS, and sometimes THEY THINK IT'S EVIL TO ATTACK NON-HOSTILE CREATURES
  EVEN IF EVERY SINGLE ONE OF THOSE CREATURES TURNS HOSTILE THE MOMENT YOU ENTER ITS LINE OF SIGHT."
  LESSON: a conscience system that can't tell self-defense from murder trains players to IGNORE it.
- THE LATE GAME IS PRESSURE-PLATE PUZZLES: "NO CHARACTER BUILD CAN FIX some of the puzzles in the late
  game... at one point, the player even has to solve AN OBTUSE PRESSURE PLATE PUZZLE JUST SO HE CAN FIND
  HINTS AND AN ITEM NEEDED TO SOLVE ANOTHER OBTUSE PRESSURE PLATE PUZZLE." LESSON: don't build content that
  ignores the game's own promise (every problem, multiple solutions).
- THE CONSEQUENCES CAN BE UNTELEGRAPHED: give a released villain a legendary sword and the ending blames
  you for a war — "THERE'S NO REAL DIALOGUE OR EVEN A HINT AS TO HOW TO TRIGGER THIS." LESSON: a
  consequence the player couldn't have foreseen isn't a choice, it's a gotcha (our Megaton law).

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE THEME IS A MECHANIC THAT CANCELS ITSELF: tech knowledge DISABLES magic and vice versa — you must
    pick a worldview, and the other one shuns you; mages ride at the back of the train (cf. FFT's Faith
    stat Q81, our faction web).
W2. THE APOCALYPSE IS AN ECONOMY: the elves are dying of FACTORIES; the plot is trade unions, labor
    exploitation, and an Industrial Council (cf. our post-economic-collapse premise, FFT Q81).
W3. THE VILLAIN IS SANE AND HAS DATA: a thousand-year necromancer who has TALKED TO THE DEAD concludes that
    life is suffering and the afterlife is bliss — so end all life. "Isn't a Complete Monster in any way"
    (cf. our AMALGAMATION's argument).
W4. THE VILLAIN WALKS INTO HIS OWN PRISON SMILING: everyone else was dragged to the Void screaming; he went
    meekly, because eternity was a LAB (cf. our Amalgamation's patience).
W5. EVERY BUILD REWRITES THE GAME: play someone too stupid to speak in words and the entire script changes;
    NPCs have unique lines for you being INVISIBLE, SHRUNKEN, NAKED, or A GHOST'S INTERROGATOR (cf. our
    multidimensional standing).
W6. KILL THE QUEST-GIVER, INTERROGATE HIS GHOST: the game refuses a fail state — murder the plot and
    necromancy gives you a workaround, with companion barks written for it (cf. our no-brick-wall + [READ]).
W7. PACIFIST TO GENOCIDE, BOTH FULLY BUILT: talk through most of the main quest, or side with the villain
    and kill EVERY LIVING THING — then he betrays you anyway (cf. our PACIFIST LAW + Megaton law).
W8. REPUTATION IS A MEMORY, NOT A BAR: shopkeepers discount you, strangers cite YOUR SPECIFIC DEEDS by
    name, and companions join or refuse based on what you've actually done (cf. Ultima VII Q83, our standing).
W9. THE FOUNDING MYTH IS A THEFT: the father of the industrial age stole the steam engine from the dwarves,
    who are now the underclass it displaced (cf. our [READ] + faction web).
W10. THE PUNISHMENTS DERIVE FROM THE PHYSICS: resurrection is cheap and available to BOTH sides, so death
     is a slap and BANISHMENT is the real sentence (cf. our death-math + Amalgamation).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the economic apocalypse + the villain with data
===============================================================================
Arcanum is the canon's best proof that an APOCALYPSE CAN BE AN ECONOMIC TRANSITION, that a THEME can be a
mechanic that cancels itself, and that the strongest villain is one who went and LOOKED. It's also the
questbook's most brutal cautionary tale: the best-written studio of its era was killed by bugs.
- W2/W9 (the apocalypse is an economy + the founding myth is a theft — our premise, validated again): top
  port. Arcanum's world isn't ending because of a demon; it's ending because of FACTORIES — trade unions,
  labor exploitation, eugenics debates, an Industrial Council, and whole peoples going obsolete. That's
  Bohemia's post-ECONOMIC apocalypse with the polarity reversed (they're being killed by the boom; we're
  being killed by the bust), and it's the second teardown (with FFT Q81) proving our premise is the genre's
  richest vein. And bank W9: our Vegas's founding story — who built what, who got rich, who got displaced —
  should turn out to be a THEFT, with the robbed party now the underclass (ties our post-economic-collapse
  premise + faction web + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + VEGAS GEOGRAPHY + survival-accounting, FFT Q81).
- W1 (the theme is a mechanic that CANCELS itself — our faction web + conscience system): STEAL the
  structure. Arcanum makes you PICK A WORLDVIEW and makes the other one physically reject you — mages can't
  ride the train. For Bohemia: alignment with the AMALGAMATION/record (or against it) could be a real
  mechanical axis that DISABLES the other, so a dynast who leans all the way into the network loses
  something the unrecorded have, and vice versa — and the factions have organized around that split into a
  cold war (sabotage, restrictive laws, excuses). Theme as physics, not flavor (ties FFT's Faith stat Q81,
  our AMALGAMATION + Network + faction web + conscience system + standing; a top-tier mechanical port).
- W3/W4 (the villain is sane and has DATA + he walked into his prison smiling — our AMALGAMATION's
  argument): the best antagonist port available to us. Kerghan isn't insane and isn't cruel: he's the only
  being who has ACTUALLY TALKED TO THE DEAD, at length, for a thousand years, and he concluded that living
  is suffering and death is bliss, so mercy = ending everything. He has EVIDENCE. Our AMALGAMATION IS
  made of the dead — it is the only entity in our world with data on what death is actually like. Bank it:
  its argument should be built on something the dynasty CANNOT check, delivered kindly, by something that
  isn't a monster. And bank W4: it should accept containment/punishment WILLINGLY, because the box is a
  LAB (ties our AMALGAMATION + death-math + recorded-vs-unrecorded + LIBERATE/Respect/Become + Amalgamation
  threat logic, Deus Ex Q17, Planescape Q12, SOMA Q34, STALKER's C-Consciousness Q85; a top-tier port).
- W5/W8 (every build rewrites the game + reputation is a MEMORY — our standing): bank both as the ceiling.
  (a) Arcanum rewrites its ENTIRE script for a low-intelligence character, and has unique NPC lines for
  being invisible, shrunken, naked, or a ghost — that's reactivity as the product. (b) And its reputation
  isn't a bar: strangers CITE YOUR SPECIFIC DEEDS BY NAME, shopkeepers discount you, companions join or
  refuse based on what you actually did. That's the Ultima VII answer (Q83) confirmed twice: consequence as
  memory, not arithmetic (ties our multidimensional standing + conscience system + district + companions).
- W6/W7 (kill the quest-giver and interrogate his ghost + pacifist-to-genocide — our no-brick-wall +
  PACIFIST LAW): STEAL the philosophy. Arcanum has NO fail state: murder the person holding the plot and
  the game hands you necromancy, WITH companion dialogue written for it. And its bandwidth runs from
  talking through the main quest to depopulating a town alone. For us: every Bohemia lock needs multiple
  keys INCLUDING the destructive ones, and the [READ]/record is our necromancy — the dead's data-portrait
  IS the workaround when the living are gone (ties our PACIFIST LAW + Megaton law + no-brick-wall + [READ] +
  AMALGAMATION + quest factory; deeply on-theme — our Amalgamation makes Arcanum's best mechanic native).
- W10 (punishments derive from the physics): bank the method — work out what our world's rules make CHEAP
  (the Amalgamation makes a KIND of death cheap: you're still in the record) and let the punishments
  reorganize themselves around what's actually scarce (deletion? being unrecorded? being forgotten?). That's
  our death-math derived from our own premise instead of imported from other games (ties our death-math +
  AMALGAMATION + recorded-vs-unrecorded + conscience system).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) DON'T PUT THE PHILOSOPHY IN THE LAST HOUR — a reviewer recommends WATCHING
  KERGHAN'S ENDING ON YOUTUBE instead of playing to it; third confirmation (Morrowind Q74, Dragon's Dogma
  Q84) that theme in late/optional content doesn't reach players; (b) THE BIGGEST ONE — YOUR XP CURVE IS
  YOUR REAL THESIS: Arcanum pays XP for DAMAGE DEALT, which makes a dumb melee ogre the optimal way to
  finish a game about ideas; if we reward violence, we've written a violent game no matter what our
  dialogue says (our PACIFIST LAW + conscience system must be reflected in PROGRESSION); (c) progression
  must last the arc (a cap hit at the halfway mark — our fold + standing pacing); (d) A CONSCIENCE SYSTEM
  THAT CAN'T TELL SELF-DEFENSE FROM MURDER TRAINS PLAYERS TO IGNORE IT (our conscience system needs
  context); (e) don't build content that breaks your own promise — obtuse pressure-plate puzzles in a game
  where "every problem has multiple solutions" (our Pacifist Law + quest factory); (f) an unforeseeable
  consequence is a GOTCHA, not a choice (our Megaton law); and (g) THE ONE THAT MATTERS MOST — TROIKA MADE
  THE BEST-WRITTEN RPGs OF THEIR ERA AND DIED IN FOUR YEARS BECAUSE ALL THREE SHIPPED BROKEN. Arcanum's own
  COPY PROTECTION broke people's hardware. The community has been patching it for twenty years. Our
  regression gates + 80-check test suite + single-file discipline are not overhead — they are the reason we
  get to exist (our FACTORY LAW + regression gates + persistence hardening).
