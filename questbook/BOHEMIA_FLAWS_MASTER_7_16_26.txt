### #17  THE FOUR-PILLAR MISSION + SOCIAL BOSS BATTLES (Deus Ex: Human Revolution / Mankind Divided)
  X  BOSS FIGHTS (HR original
     the game preaches four pillars but the original HR FORCED lethal combat boss fights — a pacifist/stealth/social build hit a WALL (you HAD to kill). A notorious contradiction of its own creed (fixed in the Director's Cut). LESSON: don't preach multi-path then force ONE path at the climax.
     >> LAW: don't preach multi-path then force ONE path at the climax.
  X  OVERPOWERED STEALTH/CLOAK (Mankind Divided
     players note cloak + hacking make stealth TOO easy / trivialize the challenge; non-lethal takedowns feel under-supported. LESSON: balance the pillars so none trivializes the others; keep each route TENSE.
     >> LAW: balance the pillars so none trivializes the others; keep each route TENSE.
  X  SPECIALIZATION TRAP
     over-investing one pillar early corners you (no traversal) — the game punishes narrow builds. LESSON (double-edged): breadth should be viable, but don't make specialization a trap either — telegraph that some spread is needed.

LESSON: if deduction is the game, EVERY answer must be fairly clueable — ambiguous art/clues that can't be reasoned out break the contract (a couple of Obra Dinn's slip). Fairness is the whole deal.

  X  BRUTE-FORCE IS STILL POSSIBLE on low-option fates (when only 2 choices remain) — the Rule of Three mitigates but doesn't fully prevent guessing
     LESSON: validation systems reduce but rarely eliminate cheese; design clue-density so guessing is unrewarding.
     >> LAW: validation systems reduce but rarely eliminate cheese; design clue-density so guessing is unrewarding.

### #22  SIDE QUESTS AS PHILOSOPHICAL DESPAIR (NieR: Automata)

LESSON: if your side content carries the theme, the main spine still needs forward drive (don't let the A-plot idle while the sidequests do the philosophy).

### #23  THE NO-MARKER QUESTLINE (Elden Ring / FromSoftware)
  X  MISSABLE-BY-DESIGN AT SCALE
     without markers, on a huge map, players lose whole questlines or must wiki them — the immersion the design protects gets broken by the wiki it necessitates. The core tension. LESSON: cryptic works in SMALL/enfolded spaces; at open-world scale it needs MORE in-world signposting (better dialogue, notes, a way to relocate NPCs) or it becomes a walkthrough dependency.
     >> LAW: cryptic works in SMALL/enfolded spaces; at open-world scale it needs MORE in-world signposting (better dialogue, notes, a way to relocate NPCs) or it becomes a walkthrough dependency.
  X  HIDDEN ORDERING DEPENDENCIES
     doing NPC-B's quest can silently break NPC-A's, with no warning — feels like "unsatisfying nonsense" to players who trip it. LESSON: if order matters, TELEGRAPH it, or make failure legible/recoverable (our no-brick-wall law).
     >> LAW: if order matters, TELEGRAPH it, or make failure legible/recoverable (our no-brick-wall law).
  X  INCONSISTENT QUALITY
     Ranni's is praised (solvable if you listen); others (Nepheli's) are cited as poorly-clued even by fans of the style. LESSON: the approach only works if EVERY quest is fairly clueable in-world (the Obra Dinn fairness contract Q19, restated).
     >> LAW: the approach only works if EVERY quest is fairly clueable in-world (the Obra Dinn fairness contract Q19, restated).
  X  THE "BAND-AID" PATCH
     adding optional markers later pleased new players but, critics argue, removed the thrill WITHOUT fixing scale. LESSON: bolted-on waypoints are a blunt fix; design signposting DIEGETICALLY from the start.
     >> LAW: bolted-on waypoints are a blunt fix; design signposting DIEGETICALLY from the start.

### #24  THE REACTIVITY ENGINE (Alpha Protocol)
  X  REACTIVITY TOP-TIER, MOMENT-TO-MOMENT WEAK
     the near-universal verdict — the choice/consequence model is legendary, but the shooting/stealth gameplay "isn't fun and doesn't really work." The game is carried ENTIRELY by its reactivity + writing. LESSON (double-edged): elite reactivity can make a flawed game beloved — but it can't fully rescue weak core mechanics; you need BOTH. (For us: our combat must stand on its own AND be reactivity-reactive.)
  X  REACTIVITY IS INVISIBLE / UNREWARDED ON ONE PLAYTHROUGH
     much of the brilliance (e.g. maxed CQC breaks Brayko's unblockable combo; 32 endings) is only visible across MULTIPLE playthroughs — "you'd never know unless you played it that way." LESSON: deep reactivity risks going UNSEEN; surface it (let the game acknowledge what you did) so players FEEL the consequence even once (cf. the ME2 roll-call Q06).
     >> LAW: deep reactivity risks going UNSEEN; surface it (let the game acknowledge what you did) so players FEEL the consequence even once (cf. the ME2 roll-call Q06).
  X  SHORT GAME, GROWS ON REPLAY
     rated ~7/10 first run, "+1 per playthrough" — built for replay, a hard sell for one-and-done players (cf. Pathologic Q21, Elden Ring Q23 missable content).

### #27  THE ILLUSION OF CHOICE / PLAYER COMPLICITY (Spec Ops: The Line)
  X  THE LUDONARRATIVE PARADOX (its own critique cuts it
     to critique mandatory shooter-violence, it FORCES you through hours of mandatory shooter-violence — it "is still a linear shooter that forces you to kill droves." Many argue the medium (a AAA cover-shooter) undercuts the message; you must ENDURE the thing it condemns. LESSON: if you critique a mechanic BY making the player do it, the doing must stay MEANINGFUL, not tedious — and be honest that forcing the point can feel manipulative/preachy.
     >> LAW: if you critique a mechanic BY making the player do it, the doing must stay MEANINGFUL, not tedious — and be honest that forcing the point can feel manipulative/preachy.
  X  THE "NO CHOICE" DEBATE
     some argue there WAS a choice (you could stop playing; or the WP scene had no alternative because the game demanded it) — the "you always have a choice" theme is philosophically slippery. LESSON: illusion-of-choice indictment can feel like the game blaming you for ITS railroad — handle the "gotcha" carefully or it breeds resentment, not reflection.
     >> LAW: illusion-of-choice indictment can feel like the game blaming you for ITS railroad — handle the "gotcha" carefully or it breeds resentment, not reflection.
  X  TEDIOUS CORE GAMEPLAY
     widely noted the moment-to-moment shooting is mediocre; the story carries it (cf. Alpha Protocol Q24, Tyranny Q25 — reactivity/theme outshining weak mechanics AGAIN).
  X  CARE NOTE (ours
     this game trades in atrocity, PTSD, and guilt. For Bohemia, any port MUST respect our wellbeing + tone rules — implicate THEMES, never gratuitously traumatize the player; a life-lesson under the decision, never cruelty for its own sake.

  X  THE ALL-LIVE RUN IS THE CANON OF RECORD AND THE DESIGN'S QUIET DEFEAT
     The optimal path erases the finale's entire death-machinery; the masterpiece state is the one where the masterpiece never fires. Banked as the preparation-design paradox: PERFECT AUDITS ARE INVISIBLE.

### #138  "PAMELA'S FATHER"
  X  THE GUARDIAN CAN BE CHEESED
     Pamela's vigilance — the quest's soul — is a patrol-AI with exploitable geometry; speedruns barge past the entire theme. LAW: if the lock is a person's attention, the attention needs senses, not sightlines.
  X  THE FATHER HAS NO POST-REUNION VOICE BUDGET
     Restored, he speaks briefly and generically; the researcher who died-almost for knowledge has no node about what he learned. The quest heals him and forgets to ask him anything.
  X  THE GIBDO MASK'S ORIGIN IS MORALLY UNREMARKED
     The wrappings become the player's tool (commanding gibdos, no less) — wearing the father's condition as equipment, and no one, including the game, notices. The receipt-bearing-relic law (Q131 PORT 4) violated in the softest possible way.
  X  THE UTILITIES CHAIN'S OWN GRIEF IS SPENT OFFHAND
     The river's curse resolves a composer-ghost's torment (Sharp and Flat — a whole sibling tragedy) as a means to the box's end; one family's rescue is powered by another's compressed epilogue.
  X  PAMELA'S AFTERMATH IS UNWRITTEN
     Post-reunion she becomes a generic happy child; the eight-year-old who ran a siege denial operation for weeks has no decompression node. The guard is dismissed without debrief.
  X  THE THREE-DAY CEILING IS UNSIGNALED HERE
     Q128 teaches the rewind tax explicitly; this quest lets players discover the re-broken closet cold — the cruelty is inherited but never locally acknowledged.
  X  THE MISREAD PATH (fighting him) IS UNDER-PRICED IN TEXT
     Attack the father and the quest just... doesn't complete; no scene prices the mistake, no Pamela-node registers the intrusion's violence. The worst thing a player can do here is met with silence that reads as absence, not judgment.
  X  THE HOUSE'S INTERIOR IS ONE ROOM DEEP
     The siege-home — the design's stage — has no domestic texture: no father's research notes, no child's drawings, no lived-in ledger. The family exists in two character models and a closet.
  X  DISTRACTION-AS-KEY GOES UNTAUGHT
     That the music box draws Pamela out is discoverable only by observation; fair, elegant, and the single most missed step in the game's most missed quest.
  X  THE QUEST IS GEOGRAPHICALLY BURIED
     Ikana's late-game gating means most players never meet Pamela at all; the corpus's tenderest quest lives in its least-visited postcode. Placement as quiet erasure (cf. Q128 F1's guide-dependence — the sibling quests share the flaw family).

### #139  THE WHITE PHOSPHORUS MORTAR (THE GATE) (Spec Ops: The Line)
  X  THE THOUSAND UNINDICTED KILLS
     The game's most-repeated criticism, a decade running: the scene indicts one atrocity while the surrounding game has the player gun down hundreds of the same soldiers with zero narrative weight, in combat that plays like the genre it is condemning. If shooting the 33rd man 400 is heroic gameplay and burning man 401 is a war crime, the difference is authored, not moral.
     LAW: the ledger has no genre exemptions. If a death class is meaningful once, it is meaningful every time, and NO DAMAGE BEFORE THE DIAL already encodes this — the dial is the same dial in setpiece and corridor. Never build a scene whose weight depends on the surrounding game agreeing to forget itself.
  X  THE ACCUSATION OF A CHOICE THE MECHANISM MADE
     Second-most-repeated criticism: the game removes the alternative, then blames the player for not taking it. "You could have stopped playing" is real but external — inside the fiction the player is railroaded into the act and then held individually responsible. Critics called it having its cake and shooting it too.
     LAW: never accuse the player of an authored act. If Bohemia wants the player to feel the weight of a deed, the deed must have been available to refuse AT COST, on-screen, with the refusal priced and survivable. The accusation must always be able to survive the reply "I had another option and I know what it was." Verbs-removed is a tool for the HEARER's side of a conversation (the locked law), never for the player's own moral verbs.
  X  MEDIOCRE MOMENT-TO-MOMENT COMBAT
     Broadly noted at launch and after: the shooting the game deconstructs is also just serviceable-at-best shooting. Some argued intentionality; intentional tedium is still tedium (same lesson banked from Undertale's genocide grind, Q28).
     LAW: deconstruction is not an excuse. The loop being critiqued must be GOOD, or the critique lands on the developer. Bohemia's dial combat must be excellent first and meaningful second; a boring system cannot afford a message.

### #140  THE LAST CORRIDOR (THE JUDGEMENT) (Undertale)
  X  THE MESSAGE IS DIFFICULTY-GATED
     The route's full argument (the fight's cheating AS rhetoric, the nothing-turn, the taint) is only witnessed by players skilled and stubborn enough to beat the hardest fight in the game. The audience for the deepest content is, by construction, the players most committed to the conduct being indicted — everyone else watches it on video, which launders the exact complicity the design depends on.
     LAW: never lock the LEDGER-READ behind execution skill. The read must arrive for everyone whose ledger earned it; difficulty may gate victory, never the verdict. In dial terms: losing the finale must still deliver the finale.
  X  CHECKLIST MORALITY AT THE ROUTE GATE
     The no-mercy route demands a strict kill-count checklist per region; miss one and the world quietly reverts to neutral. Widely criticized: it makes the game's darkest moral state something you can FAIL INTO NEUTRAL by sloppiness, and conversely something achievable only with a wiki open — intent laundered through bookkeeping.
     LAW: moral state derives from deeds, never from quota completion. The fold records what happened; it never requires the player to have been thorough about it. If Bohemia ever needs an "everyone is gone" state, it must emerge from the state of the world, not from a counter hitting a target.
  X  THE CULTURAL ECHO FLATTENED THE CRAFT
     A decade of memes turned the corridor's carefully-earned menace into a costume; the character's judgment scene now arrives pre-parodied for many players. Not the game's fault, but a real transmission loss — the design depended on the judge being underestimated, and fame revoked that.
     LAW: the judge figure must be structurally protected from front-loading. Bohemia's equivalent (whoever reads the ledger) should be established by FUNCTION at the moment of the read, not by a marketable persona beforehand. Singleton registry discipline (one Papyrus-class, one Sheogorath-class) already points this way; add: the reader of the finale-ledger is never the mascot.

### #141  THE BOOK OF THE DEAD (THE IDENTIFICATION LOOP) (Return of the Obra Dinn)
  X  THE TAIL DEGRADES INTO ELIMINATION
     The consensus criticism: the final identities (the interchangeable topmen and seamen) hang on evidence thin enough that most players finish by disciplined guess-cycling — the rule of three used as a slot machine with good odds. The game's deepest promise (every row REASONED) is broken by its own last mile.
     LAW: in any identification organ, every single case must carry at least one authored, sufficient evidence path — the mesh may make cases EASIER together, but no case may be closable ONLY by elimination. If the evidence budget runs out, cut the case, never pad the roster. (MECHANISM-MINE / CONTENTS-PAOLO'S: the organ validates evidence-sufficiency per case at build time; which cases exist is canon.)
  X  ONE-SHOT BY CONSTRUCTION
     The game is unrepeatable: knowledge is the only content, so a second run contains nothing. Widely accepted as the honest price of the form — but it is a price, and a Bohemia that spends big authoring budget on knowledge-quests pays it every run UNLESS the knowledge is generated.
     LAW: identification content must be roguelite-native: the ORGAN is authored once (the verbs, the evidence classes, the rule-of-three verification), the CASES are generated per run from the settlement's actual births, deaths, and disappearances. Obra Dinn hand-built sixty rows; Bohemia's sixty rows are whoever actually went missing this generation. The fold already stores the truth; the organ sells the player the chance to LEARN it.
  X  ACCESSIBILITY RIDES ON SENSORY CHANNELS
     Accents carry nationalities, audio carries names, the 1-bit dither carries everything else; players with hearing or visual difficulties lose evidence CLASSES, not just polish. Documented, discussed, only partially mitigated.
     LAW: every evidence class must exist in at least two channels (a spoken name also appears written somewhere; a visual tell also has a testimonial echo). Redundancy is not hand-holding; it is the same mesh principle the game already loves, applied to senses.

### #142  THE WALL AND THE JUDGE (THE ARGUE-THE-GOD FINALE) (Neverwinter Nights 2: Mask of the Betrayer)
  X  THE DEBATE IS STAT-GATED AT ITS PEAK
     The insight/wisdom thresholds lock the K-3 track — the quest's best content — behind build choices made 40 hours earlier. Community consensus and long-standing criticism: a dump-stat character meets the finale with its heart locked out (same wound as Q120's Deionarra check, ~10th confirmation).
     LAW: [READ] needs a FLOOR at finales. The deepest node may open WIDER with earned insight, but a version of the hidden-truth beat must be reachable by every build. Gate the width, never the existence.
  X  THE CRUSADE IS UNDERBUILT RELATIVE TO ITS WEIGHT
     The most consequential branch (ending a cosmology) resolves largely in epilogue text and a boss gauntlet; players who chose it report the war feeling like a summary of itself. The remedy's catastrophe is TOLD more than PLAYED.
     LAW: if a branch's consequence is apocalyptic, its PLAYABLE length must scale with it, or the branch teaches that apocalypse is cheap. Budget rule: the biggest consequence gets the biggest scene, or it gets cut. No obituary-only endings for player-authored catastrophes.
  X  THE HUNGER CLOCK FIGHTS THE TALKING
     The spirit-eater mechanic — the game's thematic spine — actively punishes the player for lingering in dialogue-heavy zones, and the finale is the most dialogue-heavy zone in the game. Widely reported: players metagame the hunger down (or mod it out) precisely to enjoy the writing the hunger was built to weight.
     LAW: a pressure mechanic must never tax the player for engaging the content it exists to make weighty. If Bohemia ever clocks a conversation (and the beat system can), the clock pauses inside load-bearing nodes and says so. Dread between scenes, never metered against reading.

### #143  EDDIE AND THE VERDICT ENGINE (Silent Hill 2)
  X  THE INVISIBLE LEDGER MISFIRES ON HONEST PLAYSTYLES
     The engine cannot distinguish meaning from habit: a cautious player who hoards healing items reads as suicidal; a completionist who re-examines everything reads as grief-obsessed. Two decades of players report endings that felt like misdiagnoses of their intent. The community literally reverse-engineered the algorithm to stop being misread.
     LAW: behavioral evidence must be CORROBORATED before it convicts. The fold may log conduct-class entries, but any verdict assembled from unmarked behavior needs multiple independent signal types agreeing (the Q141 rule-of-three, applied to the player). One habit is a fact; three convergent habits are testimony.
  X  THE MISDIRECTION TAX: PLAYERS GRADE THE WRONG SURFACE
     Because every other game grades dialogue, players assumed the Eddie conversations and endgame choices carried the weight — and communities spent years optimizing scenes that count for nothing. The design's invisibility is its genius AND a usability wound: the game never once signals which register matters.
     LAW: invisible ledgers are legal; UNLEARNABLE ones are not. Somewhere diegetic, at least once, the world must demonstrate that conduct is being read (an NPC who comments on the player's limp; a faction that reacts to self-neglect) so the attentive can learn the register exists without ever seeing the meter. Teach the CLASS of evidence, never the weights.
  X  THE FAILED TWIN IS UNDERBUILT
     Eddie gets three scenes to Angela's five-plus and the fandom's least attention; critics have long noted his arc reads thinner — the bullying backstory arrives mostly in his final monologue, so his kill can land as boss-fight furniture rather than tragedy for players who missed lines in earlier scenes.
     LAW: a mirror character's load-bearing scenes cannot be optional-feeling. If the twin exists to preview the player's verdict, every one of his scenes must be unmissable AND individually weighty — three strong meetings minimum, none of them skippable smalltalk (the format already counts stages; the compile should count MIRROR-stages separately).

### #144  THE CAPTAIN'S WILL (THE LEGACY FOLD) (Sunless Sea)
  X  THE SECOND LIFE REPLAYS TOO MUCH PROSE
     The famous criticism: story content is largely static per port, so an heir re-sails into vignettes the player has already read — the game's greatest asset (the writing) is exactly what repetition cheapens. The pool file banked it as story-becomes-a-vending-machine; at legacy scale it compounds: every death re-serves more known text.
     LAW: the fold must never hand the heir the parent's SCENES. Inherited knowledge changes what the heir can DO, never re-runs what the parent READ — and inherited-state variants (L-3's past-tense officers) must exist precisely so that repeated places speak differently to a second generation. Bohemia's proc-gen city is structurally immune to the geography half; the dialogue half is a compile rule: every heritable NPC needs previous-captain variants.
  X  GRIND CAN OUTWEIGH GRIEF
     Widely reported: mid-game economics turn voyages into port-report milk runs, and death then reads as losing PROGRESS toward a spreadsheet rather than losing a person. When the estate is mostly denominated in tedium, the legacy scene's poetry goes flat.
     LAW: the heritable estate must be denominated in EXPERIENCES (map knowledge, resolved arcs, banked testimony), never in repeatable-labor outputs. If a Bohemia player can grind the fold's value, the fold is mispriced — survival accounting keeps the labor; the fold keeps only what a eulogy could mention.
  X  MERCIFUL MODE EXPOSED THE DESIGN'S DEPENDENCE ON DEATH
     The later-added save-retention mode (and the permadeath debate around it) revealed how much of the game's meaning was load-bearing on loss being real; with death defanged, the legacy machine becomes set dressing, and community consensus split on whether the game survives it.
     LAW: the fold's meaning budget must not sit entirely on permadeath. Bohemia is hardcore by identity, but the design should be tested against its own merciful-mode question: if a run were retried, what would still matter? The answer must be "the ledger and the world's memory," which do not reset — meaning anchored in RECORD, not solely in loss. (The Q28/Q140 taint mechanism is the model: consequence that survives even the undo.)

### #145  THE CONQUEST (THE GAME BEFORE THE GAME) (Tyranny)
  X  THE CONQUEST OUTRUNS THE BUDGET
     The sequence promises a hundred world-states; the shipped game reads back the big axes loudly and the fine grain thinly — players comparing notes found many Conquest differences cash out as a line or two. The front-loaded reactivity is real but SHALLOWER than the authoring interface implies, and the famous unfinished third act compounds it.
     LAW: never let the authoring surface promise more state than the reader consumes. If the player can set it, something in the world must SAY it — rule of thumb from this file: every pre-play choice needs at least one loud, unmissable read-back plus the systemic one, or it gets cut from the authoring surface. (The gate can count read-backs per seed-choice at compile.)
  X  HISTORY WITHOUT WEIGHT AT AUTHORING TIME
     Conquest choices are made in twenty minutes, before the player knows the world, the armies, or the stakes — so the deeds the game spends forty hours holding them to were chosen half-blind. Some players report the read-backs landing as accusations about a stranger's decisions.
     LAW: Bohemia's structure dissolves this — the "Conquest" of generation N is generation N-1's ACTUAL PLAY, chosen with full knowledge, at full weight. But the law still bites for run one: the founder generation's pre-history must be THIN by design (the world-seed authors terrain, never the founder's personal deeds), because unearned guilt reads as the writer's, not the player's. Personal ledgers start empty; only inherited WORLD state arrives pre-shaped.
  X  THE DUAL METER LEAKS INTO A SLIDER AT THE EDGES
     Favor/wrath is the anti-morality-meter, but reviewers and players documented edge cases where content gates check only one axis, quietly collapsing the two-axis idea back into like/dislike where it mattered most.
     LAW: if Bohemia ships dual-axis standing (and the faction graph should — respected-and-resented is the truest post-collapse relationship), every gate that reads the relationship must be WRITTEN two-axis: checks name (favor, wrath) pairs, never a single collapsed value. Validator-checkable at compile once standings enter the .bq gate grammar.

### #146  THE SLAVE'S ESCAPE (THE UNAUTHORED QUEST) (Kenshi)
  X  THE WORLD'S INDIFFERENCE EXTENDS TO THE TRIUMPH
     The escape completes and NOTHING marks it — no reaction, no recognition, not a line. The indifference that made the captivity honest also starves the payoff; players supply the meaning themselves or feel nothing. The pool file banked the general form (investment-makes-you-care carries everything); here it bites hardest, at the arc's climax.
     LAW: the world may stay indifferent; the RECORD may not. Bohemia's fold writes the escape, the settlement's talk reflects it (bark-scale, cheap), and the missing-persons organ (#141) can even close the player's own case. Emergence supplies the story; the ledger supplies the witness. Never ship an emergent arc whose completion no system can see.
  X  THE CURRICULUM IS EXPLOITABLE INTO FARCE
     The same training rules that make the cage a school make it a GYM: veteran players deliberately enslave themselves to speed-train stats, cage-grinding toughness like a treadmill. The institution's horror collapses into an optimization spa the moment the player metagames it — documented endlessly, half-celebrated by the community.
     LAW: condition-states must resist being farmed for their training value: diminishing returns inside institutional states, and fold entries that RECORD voluntary re-entry (the ledger noticing you keep checking into cages is itself the deterrent — and material for the conduct book, #143 PORT 1). Suffering that improves you must not be bookable like a facility.
  X  THE ARC IS BRUTAL BY DEFAULT AND INVISIBLE BY DESIGN
     New players meet the slavery arc with no framing, no content signal, and no difficulty mediation — for some it is the moment Kenshi becomes beloved; for others it is a wall of misery that ends the game unrefunded. The pure sandbox's honesty is also its total abdication of onboarding and consent.
     LAW: Bohemia is hardcore, but conditions of degradation get DIEGETIC telegraphing before they can apply (the valley's reputation, survivor testimony, visible slaver activity — the world warning about itself is world-building, not softening), and the dial governs their intensity. NO DAMAGE BEFORE THE DIAL extends to institutional cruelty: the system exists at full honesty, and the player meets it informed. ### #147  SHARP AND FLAT (THE IKANA SUITE) (The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask)
  X  THE SUITE HIDES ITS OWN FRONT DOOR
     The chain (graveyard authority → night → the specific grave → the song → the unmarked connection to a cave across the canyon) is famously obtuse; generations of players finished the game with Ikana's river mysteriously dry, never knowing the brothers existed. A masterpiece gated behind guide-tier discovery.
     LAW: hidden quests may hide their CONTENT, never their EXISTENCE. The world must visibly carry the wound (the dry river IS visible — good) AND at least one diegetic pointer chain toward its cause (testimony, records, the organ's cases — Bohemia's #141 machinery is exactly this). A drought with no witnesses is content that doesn't exist; a drought people TALK about is a quest hook. (Pairs with #146 F1: the ledger as witness.)
  X  THE RELAY HAS NO STAKES FOR THE COURIER'S CONSCIENCE
     The player carries absolution between brothers whose history (a kingdom's fall, service to the dark, whatever passed between them) is delivered as backstory paint — the quest never lets the courier LEARN enough to have a position, so the reconciliation, gorgeous as staging, is morally weightless for its deliverer. Compare Q126's Hope or #141's letter-writer, where the player's knowledge implicates them.
     LAW: if the player carries a message that settles an account, let them READ enough of the account to be tempted — to edit, withhold, or take a side — and make carrying it CLEAN a chosen discipline rather than the only line (the F-1 NOVERB done Bohemia's way: the adjudication verb exists and costs). A courier who could have meddled and didn't has done something; one who couldn't has done a fetch.
  X  THE RESET ECONOMY CHEAPENS THE REPAIR FOR SOME, DEEPENS IT FOR OTHERS
     The three-day undo of every emotional repair is the game's thesis, but the criticism is real and old: players who experience the resets as futility disengage from exactly the scenes the design most wants to matter, and the game provides no register acknowledging REPEATED tending (the tenth delivery plays like the first).
     LAW: where Bohemia loops (runs, generations), repeated tending must be SEEN by the record: the fold counts the times, and the world's text can reference the count (the keeper who has now fixed the same pump every winter for nine years deserves a line about it). Repetition acknowledged is devotion; repetition ignored is a chore. The mechanism is one integer per repair-class entry.
