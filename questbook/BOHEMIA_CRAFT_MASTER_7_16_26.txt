### #1  THE BLOODY BARON (The Witcher 3)
  W1  TRANSACTION-CARRIES-EMOTION
      the player starts doing an errand for intel, hating the giver; the grief sneaks in under the instrumental frame. (Don't open with "here's a sad story" — open with a job, let the sadness grow underneath.)
  W2  RUMOR -> HUMAN -> TRAGIC RETURN
      flat tyrant (ambassador's description + thug encounter) -> complex human (the quest) -> the flat tyranny returns for real (thugs take over). The arc weaponizes the player's initial contempt.
  W3  THE MIRROR ANTAGONIST
      the Baron = Geralt (two fathers, blood on hands, both searching for lost family, both capable of anything for them). The villain wants what the hero wants.
  W4  THE ISOLATED LOAD-BEARING CHOICE
      the Whispering Hillock — a major outcome decided in a quest that looks unrelated, its weight hidden at decision-time. (The player's "obvious good" dooms Anna.)
  W5  INTERLEAVE / DORMANCY
      the quest pauses and threads THROUGH other quests, so its resolution feels discovered across the world rather than delivered in a line. The family's fate is entangled with the main plot (and Geralt's own search).
  W6  PEREGRINATION (duration AS emotion)
      the botchling carry is deliberately slow and long so grief is felt "physically" through time and distance — pacing as an emotional instrument, not a cutscene.
  W7  SAME WORDS, OPPOSITE SOUL
      the calm vs kill incantations share an opening line, then diverge — mercy (forgiveness, naming, acceptance) vs morbidity (Blood Unborn). One ritual, two moralities.
  W8  REWARD THE LISTENER
      the violet-hollyhock doll — solvable only if the player remembered an offhand line about Anna's favorite flower. Attention is a mechanic.
  W9  ENVIRONMENTAL CONFIRMATION
      the blood/holes/broken-candlestick in the house retroactively verify the abuse reveal — the space testifies.
  W10 OPTIONAL DEPTH
      Tamara's room advances nothing but deepens everything (+ the foreshadowing doll). Faith that some players will look.
  W11 NO CLEAN WIN
      every ending costs — orphans OR Anna's mind OR the Baron's life; and the thugs win regardless. The player debates the "best" ending forever (Sasko's validation: most players FORGAVE the Baron — proof the ambiguity landed).
  W12 DRAMATIZED EXPOSITION
      the Ciri backstory is a PLAYABLE flashback, not a monologue.
  W13 THE LOCATION BIRTHS THE CHARACTER
      the Baron personifies Velen (war/famine/loss). Character as the soul of a place, "deeply ingrained," not placed on top of it. ===============================================================================

### #2  BEYOND THE BEEF (Fallout: New Vegas)
  W1  ONE NODE, MANY QUESTS
      a single incident (missing son) fans into save/expose/drug/rampage/join/ frame/sacrifice — each a genuinely different quest with different verbs, not reskins. The NV "web."
  W2  THE BRANCH-ROUTER NPC
      Mortimer is a single conversation that forks the whole mission (ally vs refuse). Concentrating the fork in one character keeps a sprawling web legible.
  W3  MULTIPLE KEYS TO ONE DOOR
      kitchen access via social (sponsorship) / deception (keys) / skill (lockpick) — the SAME obstacle solved by whatever build you are. (NV Rule #1 in miniature.)
  W4  THE DISARM CONSTRAINT SHAPES SOLUTIONS
      taking your guns at the door pushes players toward stealth/ chems/fists/social/disguise — level design that BREEDS creative solutions by removing the default.
  W5  THE VILLAIN ROUTE IS FULLY BUILT
      you can JOIN the cannibal plot, cook the victim, frame the father, and the game supports it end-to-end (blood-staging, a framed arrest, a linked revenge quest). Evil is a first-class, fully-authored path, not a fail state.
  W6  DISGUISE + TIMED SET-PIECE
      the 7 PM dinner + WGS-attire disguise + intercom-call-the-waiter is a heist-like timed social-stealth beat — a mini-caper inside the quest.
  W7  THE ENDING IS A BRANCH TOO
      the payout conversation forks caps/Fame/Infamy/political-blockade — the reward is a reputation choice, not fixed loot.
  W8  SELF-CONTAINED SANDBOX
      deliberately walled off from cross-faction repercussions so the designers could go MAXIMAL on internal branching without world-state spaghetti. (A lesson in SCOPING reactivity: isolate the sandbox to afford depth.)
  W9  EMERGENT FRAGILITY AS HONEST COST
      the same density that makes it great makes it bug-prone (kill a guard and Mortimer's speech may never fire). The takeaway isn't "don't branch" — it's "test the state-combinations, and isolate the sandbox so failures stay local."
  W10 DELAYED MICRO-CONSEQUENCE
      telling Ted the truth in the freezer forces a Speech check with Heck much later — small early dialogue choices ripple.
  W11 COMPANION-AS-RESOURCE (grim systemic honesty)
      the sandbox lets you sacrifice a companion as the meal — the systems don't flinch from dark player creativity. ===============================================================================

### #3  THERE STANDS THE GRASS / VAULT 22 (Fallout: New Vegas)
  W1  THE DIRTY ERRAND
      the transaction is compromised before you leave (he sent others to die, hid it) — the moral weight is seeded in the HIRE, not just the destination.
  W2  ENVIRONMENTAL WARNING
      the green tint, the mutant flora, the spraypainted "THE PLANTS KILL" sign — the dungeon tells its own horror story from outside; the space is the exposition.
  W3  QUIET-HORROR PACING
      two empty levels before the monsters — dread built by ABSENCE, then released.
  W4  OPTIONAL DEPTH, RESPECTED
      completable as a pure data-fetch; the Keely arc + moral fork are entirely optional. Players who don't dig still finish; players who do get the soul. (Faith in the curious.)
  W5  THE TWO-SIDED KNOWLEDGE PROBLEM
      destroy the data (safe now, but no one learns and it may recur — Science 70) vs preserve it (useful, but the institution WILL misuse it). Both sides have real teeth; there is no clean answer. The quest's intellectual core.
  W6  THE INSTITUTION-WILL-MISUSE-IT BEAT
      the reason "destroy the data" is arguably RIGHT is that the NCR would weaponize the plants regardless of the danger — anti-institutional distrust made a mechanic.
  W7  MULTI-KEY TRAVERSAL
      elevator (Repair) / cave (item) / stairs (effort) — the dungeon opens to any build.
  W8  THE WITNESS WHO KNOWS
      Keely (a survivor/ghoul) carries the moral truth the employer hides — side with the one who's SEEN it, or the one who PAYS you.
  W9  THE GAME MORALIZES THE LIE, NOT THE KILL
      killing Keely costs no Karma; LYING about her death does. A precise, interesting moral model — deception is the sin, not the act. (Debatable, memorable.)
  W10 UNADVERTISED EXCLUSIVITY
      finishing this can silently lock a companion (Cassidy) and break other quests — choices close doors you didn't know were doors. Consequence you feel later.
  W11 STAGING HAZARD AS TENSION
      the gas-ignition can kill YOU if you misread which door to close — the environment demands attention; the set-piece is lethal if rushed. ===============================================================================

### #4  THE WHISPERING HILLOCK (The Witcher 3)
  W1  THE HIDDEN LOAD
      the choice's TRUE stakes (Anna, the Baron, the whole family payoff) are invisible at decision-time. You think you're deciding "save orphans vs kill a monster"; you're actually deciding whether the Baron's wife lives and he lives. The gap between apparent and real stakes is the entire genius.
  W2  THE OBVIOUS-GOOD TRAP
      "save the children" is the intuitive moral choice — and it triggers the worst family outcome (Anna's death, the Baron's suicide). The game punishes the reflexive good deed with tragedy, forcing the player to feel that good intentions ≠ good outcomes.
  W3  NO CLEAN OPTION
      every path kills someone (orphans OR Anna+Baron; Downwarren dies either way-ish). The player weighs incommensurable lives; there's no "right." (Game8: "both choices have necessary evils; weigh it yourself.")
  W4  ZERO REWARD DIFFERENCE
      identical loot/XP — the choice is stripped of gamey motive so it's PURELY ethical. (A masterstroke: reactivity that refuses to bribe you toward any option.)
  W5  DELAYED, DISPLACED CONSEQUENCE
      the payoff lands quests later, in a DIFFERENT quest (Return to Crookback Bog), so cause and effect are separated by hours — the world feels like it has a memory and a life beyond the immediate scene.
  W6  THE AMBIGUOUS PETITIONER
      the spirit is a monster that killed villagers AND the orphans' only savior — you can't tell if freeing it is mercy or catastrophe (it's both). The quest-giver is morally opaque BY DESIGN.
  W7  ORDER-DEPENDENT REACTIVITY (the loophole)
      the SEQUENCE you tackle quests in changes outcomes — deep, hidden state that rewards system-mastery and replay. (The world runs on real conditions, not a script waiting for you.)
  W8  THE CHOICE GALLOPS AWAY
      freeing the spirit = it possesses a horse and RUNS OFF — a staging image of consequence leaving your control. You set something loose; you can't recall it.
  W9  THE DESCENT
      a flooded tunnel down to a heart in the roots — physical descent to a trapped thing; the staging matches the moral (going UNDER to face a buried truth).
  W10 WORSHIP-OF-THE-MONSTER
      Downwarren worships the Crones (their abusers) — killing the spirit "protects" people who venerate the real evil; the moral field is muddied on every axis. ===============================================================================

### #5  DISCO ELYSIUM (the whole-game quest MODEL + The Tribunal)
  W1  DIALOGUE IS THE GAMEPLAY
      no combat; every conflict is conversation + checks. Proof a great quest needs zero fighting — the tension is social, moral, internal.
  W2  THE INTERNAL PARTY (skills as voices)
      externalize the character's competing impulses as VOICES that argue — the player's own psyche is the "party," and the build changes what you perceive.
  W3  FAILURE-AS-CONTENT
      write the FAILURE branch as richer than success wherever possible; teach the player (by making failure fun) that reloading is the worse experience. Failure = new story.
  W4  RED vs WHITE (permanent vs retryable)
      distinguish one-shot story-altering checks from retryable ones; concentrate the biggest divergences in clearly-flagged permanent moments (the Tribunal).
  W5  IDENTITY-AS-LOOT (Thought Cabinet)
      reward engagement with CHARACTER (ideas, self-concept), not gear — and let some rewards make you WORSE, as characterization. The build is a self-portrait.
  W6  DRESS-FOR-THE-CHECK (transparent soft modifiers)
      let players tilt odds via visible, swappable means (clothing/Thoughts) — agency over probability without save-scumming.
  W7  THE UNAVOIDABLE COLLISION
      some climaxes should be INEVITABLE (the Tribunal happens no matter what); agency lives in HOW you meet it, not whether. Fixed event + variable survival.
  W8  THE ODDS ARE THE INVESTIGATION
      everything you did earlier (who you talked to, what you learned, how you built/dressed) silently stacks the climax's checks — the payoff of paying attention.
  W9  PERCEPTION IS BUILD-GATED
      high skills reveal lines/clues invisible to other builds — replay value + the sense of a world bigger than any one playthrough sees.
  W10 MORALIZED FAILURE, NOT PUNISHED
      failing is embedded in the game's THEME (a failure of a man in a failed world) — failure isn't a slap, it's the subject. ===============================================================================

### #6  THE SUICIDE MISSION + LOYALTY SYSTEM (Mass Effect 2)
  W1  THE WHOLE GAME IS THE WIND-UP
      structure the entire experience as PREPARATION for one finale that reads every prior choice — the side content becomes the point.
  W2  SIDE CONTENT = SURVIVAL
      companion (loyalty) missions are optional in name but load-bearing for the ending — the strongest possible answer to "why do side quests?" (your friends live or die).
  W3  LOYALTY EARNED, FRAGILE, LOSABLE
      trust is a live state you can win AND lose (the Jack/Miranda clash), sometimes with no time to repair — relationships have stakes and upkeep.
  W4  ROLE-ASSIGNMENT AS CHARACTER-KNOWLEDGE
      the finale tests whether you KNOW your people (assign the leader to lead, the tech to the vents) — competence-casting; knowing the cast IS the skill check.
  W5  PERMADEATH WITH DIGNITY
      deaths are permanent, carried into the sequel, and STAGED as the character failing at a specific job — not random; each death is legible and earned by a player choice.
  W6  THE HIDDEN CLOCK
      once your people are kidnapped, wandering KILLS them — urgency enforced by world-state, punishing the player who ignores stated stakes (do zero missions = all crew live).
  W7  THE MATH IS INVISIBLE BUT FAIR
      a real survival formula (averages/thresholds) runs under the hood; the player feels the logic (loyal + right-role = live) without seeing the numbers — systemic, legible consequence.
  W8  THE ROLL CALL
      end by TALLYING the survivors — force the player to face exactly who their preparation saved or doomed. The reckoning is the emotional payoff.
  W9  BEATABLE BY UNDERSTANDING, NOT GRINDING
      you can save everyone with smart assignment even under- prepared, or lose people while "completing" everything — mastery is comprehension, not checklist.
  W10 THE NIGHT BEFORE
      a quiet character beat (romance/farewells) right before the point of no return — calm before the storm makes the stakes land. ===============================================================================

### #7  WHODUNIT? (The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion, Dark Brotherhood)
  W1  THE CLOSED BOX
      a sealed location (no exit until done) with a fixed cast — claustrophobic, focused, every variable on one stage. The constraint concentrates the design.
  W2  EASY TO COMPLETE, HARD TO MASTER
      the kill is trivial; the BONUS (no witnesses) is the real game. The challenge is SELF-IMPOSED — the player opts into difficulty for a reward, so mastery is voluntary and satisfying (no forced fail state).
  W3  PLAYER AS PUPPETEER (social manipulation)
      the paranoia gambit lets you turn a group against ITSELF — the marks do your killing. Emergent social systems (disposition -> "X is the killer") produce a player-authored massacre, not a scripted one.
  W4  THE INTELLIGENCE PHASE
      you TALK before you kill — learning personalities, grudges, and schedules IS the gameplay; the murder is the execution of a plan you built from conversation.
  W5  MULTIPLE FULLY-SUPPORTED VERBS
      stealth-isolation / indirect-poison / social-paranoia / brute — each a genuinely different way to play the SAME box. (Multiple solutions, concentrated.)
  W6  ISOLATION-AS-PUZZLE
      the real challenge is SEPARATION — engineering privacy via geography + disposition. Sightlines and who's-where become a spatial-social puzzle.
  W7  DRAMATIC IRONY
      the marks think it's a treasure hunt; you know it's an execution. Every warm conversation plays over that gap — dark comedy + tension from asymmetric knowledge.
  W8  EMERGENT CHAOS
      the AI can spontaneously start killing after the first death — the system produces unscripted situations you must improvise around (a systemic sandbox, not a set script).
  W9  THE INDIRECT KILL (never raise a hand)
      poisoned apples let you murder without an action — the victims kill themselves on your trap. Indirect agency is a distinct, chilling verb.
  W10 CROSS-SYSTEM CONSEQUENCE
      the quest feeds Infamy, which can lock other content — the deed echoes into the wider game's systems. ===============================================================================

### #8  THE IMMERSIVE-SIM QUEST (Kingdom Come: Deliverance)
  W1  GOAL + SIMULATION, NOT BRANCH-MENU
      the quest states an objective and hands you a systemic world; solutions EMERGE (disguise / stealth / bribe-the-guard-with-dice / brute force / secret passage / literacy) rather than being picked from an authored list. Freedom feels discovered, not offered.
  W2  DISGUISE AS MAINTAINED PERFORMANCE
      a disguise isn't a costume toggle — it must be WORN CORRECTLY (helmet on, own clothes off), SUSTAINED (persuasion checks, no caught crimes), and in the monastery, LIVED (attend prayers on time, keep the schedule). Cover is an ongoing discipline.
  W3  THE DISGUISE COSTS YOU (surrender the kit)
      entering the monastery strips your entire inventory and power — the vow ("poverty") is mechanical. A disguise that costs you something real raises the stakes.
  W4  QUEST GATED BEHIND A LIFE SKILL
      you can't enter without LITERACY — a competence earned in the open world. Progression depends on who your character has BECOME, not just quest flags.
  W5  CROSS-QUEST REACTIVITY
      infiltrating the bandits in a PRIOR quest changes THIS quest's difficulty; the monastery arc weaves six side quests. The world remembers and threads quests together.
  W6  DENSITY AS OBSTACLE
      the camp is so crowded that rote stealth-assassination fails — the environment's population forces PATIENCE and opportunism over exploit. The world's texture is the challenge.
  W7  FORCE IS A VALID ROUTE (with a cost)
      you can brute-force the camp or bail the monastery arc by murder-and-flee — the elegant path is optional; the sim supports the blunt one (with lost rewards).
  W8  THE APPROACH IS A MORAL STANCE
      "mercy or annihilation" for the bandits; capture-vs-kill Pious — HOW you solve it is WHO you are, and the game frames it that way.
  W9  DIEGETIC SOCIAL SOLUTIONS
      get the guardian drunk on dice to isolate the mark — a social problem solved through a WORLD ACTIVITY (gambling), not a dialogue check. Systems double as tools.
  W10 REWARD FOR DOING IT RIGHT
      kill Pious OUTSIDE and you miss the "strange die"; the clean method yields a hidden reward — the sim quietly rewards precision/patience.
  W11 THE HONEST FLAW (anti-pattern)
      systemic stealth with INCONSISTENT detection ("seen through walls") turns emergent freedom into unfair failure — LEGIBLE rules are the price of systemic ambition. ===============================================================================

### #13  THE SUBSTORY MODEL (Yakuza / Like a Dragon)
  W1  SIDE CONTENT AS SENSE-OF-PLACE
      substories exist to make the CITY feel alive, not to pad a checklist — every corner has an authored story; density makes a small map feel vast.
  W2  TONAL JUXTAPOSITION AS A FEATURE
      deliberately swing from grim main-story to absurd side-story and back — the contrast makes BOTH hit harder (the comedy relieves, the drama deepens). Revel in it.
  W3  THE DEADPAN ANCHOR
      a SERIOUS protagonist playing absurdity dead-straight is the comedy engine — and his sincerity lets the heartfelt beats land too. The straight man is the key.
  W4  ABSURD WITH HEART (never JUST a gag)
      even the silliest substory has a real human core (confidence, loneliness, pride) — the joke is the wrapper, the heart is the gift. Commit to the bit AND the soul.
  W5  COMMITMENT TO THE BIT
      build a full BOSS FIGHT / minigame / arc around a joke premise — the absurdity is taken as seriously (mechanically) as the drama. Half-committed comedy dies.
  W6  THE CHANCE ENCOUNTER (the city snags you)
      substories start by WALKING PAST something, not from a menu — the world reaches out and grabs you; discovery feels organic, not administrative.
  W7  THE BLINDSIDE (mundane -> devastating)
      a "sleeper" side quest that starts light and earns real emotional weight through contrast — the setup's lightness makes the drop land (cf. Disco/RDR).
  W8  MINIGAME-AS-STORY
      wrap a character arc around a mechanic (a failing club, a lonely idol) — systems deliver story; the minigame IS the emotional throughline.
  W9  ABSURDITY FROM THE GAME'S OWN MECHANICS
      parody your OWN systems (random encounters -> "catch the thugs Pokemon-style") — self-aware comedy that's native to the game.

      remove moral agency (scripted order) but make the player EXECUTE the act (aim + fire) — complicity WITHOUT decision; you did it even though you couldn't choose not to.
  W3  WEAPONIZE THE ABSTRACTION

  W4  THE ACCUSATORY UI

  W5  SYMPATHY-THEN-SLAUGHTER

  W6  THE UNRELIABLE, SELF-DECEIVING HERO

  W7  NAME THE PLAYER'S MOTIVE

  W8  THE VISIBLE DEGRADATION

  W9  FORM = INDICTMENT
      the critique IS the experience (you don't watch atrocity, you commit it) — the strongest version of theme-through-mechanic (cf. Dead Money greed, collection; Pathologic Q21).
  W10 THE HONEST LIMIT

### #125  "TALI: TREASON"
  W1  THE OBJECTIVE IS THE WEAPON
      The quest sends you to find evidence, and the evidence is not a key that opens a door — it is a loaded thing that has a target, and the target is the father of the woman standing next to you. The instant you learn what it says, the fetch quest becomes a moral object. No other quest in the trilogy turns an inventory item into a decision this cleanly. **The item does not unlock the ending. The item IS the ending, and you get to not use it.**
  W2  THE PUNISHMENT ARRIVES BEFORE THE VERDICT
      She is renamed *vas Normandy* in the first thirty seconds, in a friendly greeting, by her aunt. No combat, no roll, no fail state. A word changed and she lost her home. The board's decision is telegraphed by grammar. This is the single most economical piece of writing in the mission: **the trial's outcome is announced by how someone says hello.**
  W3  THE INSULT BECOMES THE IDENTITY
      She keeps *vas Normandy* forever. Through the entire third game, when every other quarian is *vas Rannoch*. The thing they did to shame her became the thing she is. **A punishment the character decides to keep is worth ten speeches about resilience.**
  W4  THE DUNGEON IS A CONFESSION, ORDERED
      The Alarei is not a level, it is an argument delivered in sequence: reckless orders, then the stated goal (retake the homeworld), then the personal logs (he did it for her), then the body, then his last message (a task assignment), then the full technical record. By the time the game asks you the question, it has already answered *why* — and the answer makes the truth harder to tell, not easier. **Structure the discovery so the motive lands BEFORE the crime does.**
  W5  HIS LAST WORDS TO HIS DAUGHTER ARE A WORK ORDER
      "Destroy the main hub, bring the data to Xen and Gerrel." No apology. No I love you that isn't wrapped in an instruction. The most devastating characterization of a father in the trilogy, delivered in one recorded log, and the game never comments on it. **Let the dead reveal themselves through what they thought was urgent.**
  W6  THE HEARING IS A FACADE AND YOU CAN LEARN THAT BY LISTENING
      Four admirals, four agendas, one of them presiding without recusing, none of them primarily interested in whether she did it. War party, slaver party, peace party, family friend. The verdict is a vote about the geth war. **The trial is a proxy fight, and Tali is the object being thrown.**
  W7  THE LEARNING REWRITES THE CLIMAX
      Do the optional walk and the Paragon speech becomes a different speech: not "she's a good person," but "you are abusing this hearing for political ammunition." Same button, same points, different scene. **Optional knowledge that changes the TEXT of the climax rather than unlocking a new one is the cheapest, highest-value reactivity in the genre.**
  W8  THE BEST OUTCOME IS PURCHASED WITH MERCY YOU DIDN'T KNOW YOU WERE BANKING
      Veetor spared from Cerberus in mission two. Reegar ordered to stand down instead of dying heroically. Neither flagged. Both show up in a courtroom hours later and testify — **and they beat the stat check.** You don't need Charm if you were decent to two people who couldn't help you at the time. **The best political capital in the game is people you were kind to when it wasn't useful.**
  W9  EVERY ENDING COSTS SOMETHING REAL AND THE COSTS ARE INCOMPARABLE
      Truth: she keeps her people, loses her father's name, loses you. Lie: she keeps her father's name, loses her people, and thanks you for it, and a species war stays on the rails. Speech: costs a stat, or costs nothing, and that's the flaw. **There is no currency that converts a name into a nation, and the game refuses to invent one.**
  W10 THE SECRET DOESN'T STAY SECRET AND THE GAME TELLS YOU IN AN EMAIL
      Daro'Xen digs through the Alarei herself, finds the research, and sends you a note saying you should have shared it and now you'll watch from a distance. **Your discretion protected a name, not a truth.** The most mature consequence in the mission is a piece of mail confirming that suppression is a delay, not a deletion — and it arrives after the reward screen, when you thought the quest was over. ---

### #126  "RAPHAEL'S DEAL / THE HOUSE OF HOPE"
  W1  THE DEVIL IS THE ONLY HONEST PARTY AND HE HONORS THE CONTRACT
      No trick, no subclause, no monkey's paw.   W2  HE DELIVERS BEFORE YOU PAY

      Saying nothing at the body scene changes the locker's opening — the game files the player's quiet as a position taken. Costless-looking options with downstream text is reactivity at its cheapest and best.
  W9  THE REWARD-ENDING IS A DIAGNOSIS
      MARIA gives the player exactly what their footsteps kept choosing, then adds one cough. Having-it-your-way as the bleakest verdict — judgment delivered inside a gift.
  W10 THE VERDICT SURVIVED THE PLAYERBASE
      The algorithm stayed opaque long enough to generate years of genuine argument about what the game valued — the read was so diegetic that the DISCOURSE became part of the work. Invisibility executed at production quality.

### #144  THE CAPTAIN'S WILL (THE LEGACY FOLD) (Sunless Sea)
  W1  THE WILL IS AUTHORED BY LIVING, NOT BY A MENU
      Every mid-run choice (scion funding, arc-finishing, secret-banking) is secretly testament-drafting. The death screen only READS what the life already wrote — the fold as consequence, never as feature-checklist.
  W2  KNOWLEDGE IS THE ESTATE'S GOLD STANDARD
      The chart — pure geography-knowledge — is the one always-offered inheritance, and the game's best one. What crosses generations best is what was LEARNED. (Twelfth-plus echo of comprehension-as-progression; the fold speaks the same currency.)
  W3  THE REFUSALS MAKE THE LINE REAL
      Unbanked secrets burn, open ambitions die, stranded arcs stay stranded. A generational game earns its deaths by what it will NOT carry — the meaning lives in the non-heritable remainder.
  W4  THE HEIR IS A PERSON, NOT A RESPAWN
      Heir-shapes with different inheritance profiles — raised scion versus claiming rival — make the successor an authored relationship, priced during the predecessor's life. Who inherits is itself a life's-work question.
  W5  THE SCION IS PAID FOR WITH PRESENT STRENGTH
      Funding the heir costs fuel-money NOW: the game mechanizes the parent's dilemma (this life versus the next one) into every port visit. The fold's emotional core, denominated in the survival economy.
  W6  DOOMED RUNS BECOME TESTAMENT RUNS
      Because deaths are slow and legible, endgame play reorganizes around dying WELL — racing to bank, resolve, and bequeath. The game invents an entire late-life genre: playing your own probate.
  W7  INHERITED NPCS SPEAK IN PAST TENSE
      Officers who cross remember the previous captain in their lines, arcs visibly stranded. The cheapest possible reactivity (a text variant) carrying the heaviest possible freight (grief), and the model for every fold-crossing character Bohemia ships.
  W8  THE TRUE ENDINGS ARE EXITS, NOT VICTORIES OVER DEATH
      Completing an ambition ends the line ALIVE — retirement as the rare, expensive alternative to legacy. Death stays the default without becoming the only author.
  W9  THE SECOND LIFE PROVES THE FIRST ONE HAPPENED
      The heir's early game is repriced by inherited geography and the player's own trained dread — progress the interface never has to claim, because the player EXPERIENCES being their own inheritance.
  W10 THE CHRONICLE FRAME
      The game names a full history of play a chronicle of tragedies: the unit of the work is the LINE, not the life. The framing alone does design work — players narrate their dynasties unprompted. Bohemia's three-arc, hundred-year shape is this frame at full scale.

### #145  THE CONQUEST (THE GAME BEFORE THE GAME) (Tyranny)
  W1  THE PAST IS PLAYER-AUTHORED AT SYSTEM RESOLUTION
      The Conquest writes the exact currencies the game consumes — standings, regional states, named flags — so backstory reactivity lands on the player's own signature. Guilt with a receipt.
  W2  AUTHORITY IS THE VERB SET
      Every Conquest option is judicial — attach, counsel, adjudicate, proclaim — teaching the player their role's grammar before teaching them combat. The fantasy is jurisdiction, established in the sequence's verbs alone.
  W3  THE BYSTANDER VERB IS REMOVED FROM THE PAST
      No "I wasn't part of it" exists; presence is jurisdiction. Dirty hands as an initial condition is the boldest opening state in the genre.
  W4  THE EDICT IS A SIGNATURE WITH WEATHER
      Law that persists as terrain until its own wording releases it: the player's counsel becomes a standing feature of the map, and READING THE LAW CLOSELY becomes endgame content. Consequence you can stand in, then lawyer your way out of.
  W5  COMPOUNDING IS VISIBLE AT AUTHORING TIME
      The chronicle re-asks with prior answers echoing, so the player watches their lean harden into a reputation before the game starts — the fold's compounding, compressed into twenty minutes.
  W6  HISTORY FIXED, POSTURE PLAYED
      The main game grades ownership (claim/regret/double down) separately from deeds — the one authoring the past can still choose who its author became. The redemption architecture every guilt system needs.
  W7  MERCY PLANTED BLIND PAYS OFF NAMED
      Conquest sparings return as individuals with memory; the design banks player kindness before the player knows the currency. Q126's mercy-banked pattern, moved to pre-play.
  W8  THE TWO ARMIES PRE-LOAD THE CIVIL WAR
      The main conflict detonates along the attachment line the player drew — the game's central war is the player's own Conquest record, weaponized. Premise and player-state fused.
  W9  WORLD-EDITING WEARS A CHRONICLE'S CLOTHES
      The player is handed regional-content forks (Edict arcs exist or don't) inside a diegetic war summary — content authorship disguised as memory. The trust this extends is repaid in investment.
  W10 THE OPENING IS AN AFTERMATH
      Starting after the ending — evil won, now administrate — remains the sharpest premise inversion in the genre, and the Conquest is what makes it PERSONAL rather than decorative: the aftermath is specifically yours.

### #146  THE SLAVE'S ESCAPE (THE UNAUTHORED QUEST) (Kenshi)
  W1  DEFEAT CONTINUES INSTEAD OF ENDING
      Losing produces a NEW GAME STATE (captivity) rather than a load screen — the single rule doing the most narrative work in the entire design. No continuation, no arc.
  W2  THE INSTITUTION HAS A SCHEDULE
      Feedings, shifts, counts: routine makes captivity LEGIBLE, and legibility turns suffering into a puzzle. The quest's dialogue tree is a timetable.
  W3  PUNISHMENT TRAINS
      Failed escapes teach escaping; beatings teach surviving beatings. The curriculum property — consequence that transfers skill to its victim — is what converts a fail-loop into rising action.
  W4  STATUS IS A FACTION RELATIONSHIP, ENFORCED
      "Slave" is not a cutscene state; the world defends it (recapture, bounty-return, property logic), so exiting it is a real transition with real enemies. States that nothing enforces are costumes.
  W5  THE PROTAGONIST IS MUTE AND THE VILLAIN HAS NO SPEECH
      Intent lives in the player; the antagonist is a price list. With both mouths empty, the player's own narration fills the arc — which is why every testimony sounds authored: it was, by its teller.
  W6  WITNESSES SHARE THE CONDITION
      Other slaves make the state communal: solidarity, betrayal, and the carry-them-out verb all emerge from mere co-presence. One rule (others suffer this too) buys the whole ensemble drama.
  W7  THE CENTRAL GATE IS PURE KNOWLEDGE
      The rotation's gap is stored nowhere but the player's head — no journal, no marker. The game trusts observation absolutely, and the trust is why the break feels EARNED beyond any authored quest's capacity.
  W8  TIME IS THE ONLY FEE
      The camp charges calendar, and the world's other lives visibly spend theirs meanwhile — urgency without timers, dread without music. The indifferent simulation IS the clock.
  W9  THE SEQUEL SELF-GENERATES
      The return-and-burn-it ending is unprompted, unrewarded, near-universal: institutions that teach their victims recruit their destroyers. The design's deepest finding, and it emerged from the systems' shape, not a writer's plan.
  W10 THE ARC IS STATISTICALLY STABLE
      Thousands of testimonies, one beat structure, all different in detail: the mark of an attractor built from rules, which is the only kind of quest a proc-gen world can mass-produce. The existence proof Bohemia's emergent layer needs.

### #147  SHARP AND FLAT (THE IKANA SUITE) (The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask)
  W1  THE QUEST-GIVER IS DEAD AND WANTS A DELIVERY, NOT A DEED
      No vengeance, no rescue — a message. The relay frame makes the player a musician's postal service, and the humility of the role is the scene's dignity.
  W2  UTILITIES AND GRIEF ARE ONE SYSTEM
      The river is dry because a soul is; fixing the relationship fixes the water. Infrastructure as emotional state is the suite's signature and Bohemia's native tongue (survival accounting with a heart under it).
  W3  THE DELIVERABLE IS KNOWLEDGE WITH PROVENANCE
      The song works because of WHO taught it — the same notes from a book would be noise to Sharp. Quest items that are knowledge-with-a-source outclass every fetchable object. (#141's watch, #144's chart, and this song are one family.)
  W4  THE BOSS FIGHT IS STANDING IN PAIN AND ANSWERING CORRECTLY
      Damage falls while the player performs the remedy — courage rendered as holding a song steady under a life-drain. No sword; the whole confrontation is delivery-under-fire.
  W5  THE CURSED ONE IS THE VICTIM, AND BREAKING THE CURSE UNCORKS THE CONFESSION
      The boss's death-rattle slot is filled with weeping and account-giving: violence's usual payout replaced by testimony. (Q135's monster-as-patient, run one screen upstream of #138's domestic version — the suite teaches the machine twice.)
  W6  THE UNMARKED CONNECTION
      Grave to cave, song to brother — no journal, no marker; the link lives in the player's comprehension. The suite trusts understanding as its only thread (twelfth-plus echo of comprehension-as-the-key, and the smallest, cleanest instance in the corpus).
  W7  THE FAILURE STATE IS VISIBLE GEOGRAPHY
      Skip the suite and the dry river stays, walkably, for the whole game — unfinished business as terrain. The world wearing its open quests is reactivity's cheapest, most honest form.
  W8  THE REWARD IS WATER
      The payout is the most survival-denominated resource in the region, cashed downstream by people the player hasn't met yet. Doing right upstream of strangers is the quest's actual moral, delivered mechanically.
  W9  AUTHORITY OVER THE DEAD IS THE DOOR
      Even finding the giver requires out-ranking corpses — the necro-bureaucratic entry fee sets the whole country's tone: in Ikana the dead have chains of command, and the player enters by borrowing one.
  W10 REPAIRS ARE LOOP-LOCAL, KNOWLEDGE PERMANENT
      The world's undo erases the river but not the song: the design states, mechanically, what survives resets — what you learned. The roguelite thesis in miniature, shipped in 2000.
