# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 124: THE CURE IS UNDER HIS OWN HOUSE / HE WAS BUILT TO KILL HIS SISTER (The Underground Facility, NieR Replicant)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where a blindfolded boy asks you to help cure him, the cure is
in a laboratory under his own mansion, and the descent is his memory coming back one document at a time —
until he remembers that he is a weapon, his sister is the weapon he was built to stop, and the only way to
save your friend is to let his sister eat him. Complete event flow, the letter that wasn't from him, the
mailbox at the bottom, Halua on the wall, the fusion, the mother's letter they find too late, the honest
flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our [READ], our AMALGAMATION, our fold, our death-math, +
our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope. Cavia / Toylogic (director: **YOKO TARO**). Reference only; Paolo does not
read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: the Emil / Underground Facility chain, NieR Replicant (2010) / ver.1.22474487139... (2021). Emil is
"**A YOUNG BOY THAT JOINS NIER'S PARTY, INITIALLY CURSED WITH THE ABILITY TO TURN ANYONE WHO SEES HIS EYES TO
STONE.**" He lives alone in a mansion with a butler. He has been blindfolded almost his entire life. He is
maybe ten years old and he is over a thousand.

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE MONSTER UNDER THE HOUSE IS THE HOUSE'S PURPOSE (the premise — STARTLINGLY our district + [READ]): "**THE
  MANOR WAS CONSTRUCTED BY NATIONAL WEAPONS RESEARCH LABORATORY... AND, AS THE NAME IMPLIES, THE GOAL WAS TO
  CREATE HUMAN WEAPONS.**"
  - The mansion isn't haunted. The mansion is the LID. "**RECORDS DATING BACK FROM 2025 INDICATE THAT THE LAB
    WAS BUILT BY THE NATIONAL WEAPONS LABORATORY AS A WAY TO KEEP HALUA IN CHECK, FOLLOWING AN INCIDENT THAT
    NECESSITATED HER LONG-TERM STORAGE.**"
  - Emil lives in a beautiful house that exists to be a roof over the thing that ate his childhood. He does
    not know this. He has lived there for a thousand years (cf. our district + HD tile repo + AMALGAMATION;
    the building that is a containment vessel with furniture on top).
- THE DESCENT IS AMNESIA RUNNING IN REVERSE (the structure — and it's our [READ] as level design):
  "**SOMETHING WITHIN EMIL TRIGGERED INTENSE HEADACHES AS THE PARTY MADE THEIR DESCENT, AND HIS CONFUSION WAS
  COMPOUNDED BY THE DOCUMENTS THEY WOULD FIND AT EACH STAIRWAY.**"
  - "**AS YOU EXPLORE, YOU FIND REPORTS ON PLAN SNOW WHITE, WHICH TRANSFORMED THE 'DONOR BODIES' EMIL AND
    HALUA INTO WEAPONS (NO. 6 AND NO. 7).**"
  - "**WHILE IN THE LABORATORY, EMIL SLOWLY RECOVERS HIS PAST, PIECE BY PIECE.**" Every floor down is a memory
    up. The player reads a document; the boy gets a headache; something surfaces. **The dungeon is his mind
    and you are excavating it with a sword** (STARTLINGLY our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded; the archive
    that hurts the person it's about).
- HE WAS MANUFACTURED AS A FAILSAFE AGAINST HIS OWN SISTER (the reveal — and it's our AMALGAMATION):
  "**HALUA, 'NUMBER 6,' WAS UTILIZED TO CREATE THE 'ULTIMATE WEAPON,' WHILE HE, 'NUMBER 7,' WAS KEPT IN
  RESERVE**... **EMIL WAS DEVELOPED AS A FAIL-SAFE AGAINST HIS SISTER IN THE EVENT SHE LOST CONTROL, WITH
  EMIL EVENTUALLY BEING FORCED TO PETRIFY HER AND THE UNDERGROUND FACILITY.**"
  - "**EMIL WAS UNSUSPECTING OF THE RESEARCHERS WHILE HALUA HAD TO LOOK OUT FOR HIM.**" She protected him
    from the people who were building her, and then they built HIM to stop HER, and he did.
  - The curse he's spent his whole life ashamed of — the eyes that turn people to stone — **is the tool they
    installed in him to use on his sister.** His disability is a weapons system, and it worked exactly once
    (cf. our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic + [READ]; the trauma that is a spec sheet).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE INVITATION IS A FORGERY (the quest starts with a lie told out of love)
- You hear about it sideways: Emil is first mentioned as "**A FRIEND WITH WHOM YONAH EXCHANGES LETTERS.**"
  Nier "**HEARS ABOUT YONAH'S MYSTERIOUS NEW PEN PAL AND WANTS THEIR HELP.**"
- "**IT TURNS OUT THAT THE LETTER JONAH RECEIVED WAS NOT FROM EMIL, BUT FROM A BUTLER WHO WANTED TO SOMEHOW
  CURE HIS PETRIFICATION EYE.**"
- **SEBASTIAN, THE BUTLER, WROTE THE LETTERS.** He forged his employer's child's correspondence to lure a
  swordsman to the house, because the boy has no friends and Sebastian has run out of ideas. The pen pal was
  never real (STARTLINGLY our companions + entities + quest factory; the quest-giver who isn't the person
  who signed it).
### STAGE 1 — FIVE YEARS OF A BUTLER DECODING A PAGE
- After the manor's library fight: "**AFTER DESTROYING GRIMOIRE RUBRUM AT THE MANOR'S STUDY, NIER CHANCES
  UPON A PAGE WITH INFORMATION THAT COULD FINALLY CURE EMIL, ALTHOUGH THE PAGE ITSELF WAS ENCRYPTED.**"
- "**SEBASTIAN SPENT THE NEXT FIVE YEARS DECODING THE PAGE, AND HIS EFFORTS LED HIM TO THE FACILITY'S
  LOCATION.**"
- **FIVE YEARS.** The game timeskips five years for other reasons, and in the background, an old servant sat
  in that house working on a cipher, alone, for half a decade, so a boy could open his eyes.
- "**UP UNTIL NIER'S ARRIVAL AT THE MANSION, EMIL HAD SPENT MOST OF HIS LIFE BLINDFOLDED, AND HONED HIS OTHER
  SENSES TO WORK AROUND THE HANDICAP**" (cf. our companions + [READ] + district; the loyalty measured in
  years of unglamorous work).
### STAGE 2 — THE SWITCH IN THE FOUNTAIN
- "**THE COMPLEX LIES BENEATH EMIL'S MANSION, AND IS ACCESSED BY GOING TO THE COURTYARD AND PRESSING THE
  SWITCH IN THE FOUNTAIN.**"
- The entrance to a human-weapons laboratory is a button in a decorative fountain in a garden a child plays
  in (cf. our district + prefab factory + HD tile repo).
### STAGE 3 — THE CAMERA CHANGES AND THE GAME BECOMES A DIFFERENT GENRE
- "**THE ROOMS BENEATH EMIL'S MANSION CONSIST OF A SERIES OF DUNGEONS VIEWED FROM A DIABLO-LIKE TOP-DOWN
  VIEW.**"
- "**WITH THE EXCEPTION OF HALUA'S ROOM, ALL THREE FLOORS SHIFT THE CAMERA TO AN ISOMETRIC PERSPECTIVE WHEN
  ENTERING THE AREA, EMPHASIZING THE FACILITY'S MAZE-LIKE LAYOUT THAT PROGRESSIVELY BECOMES MORE INTRICATE
  WITH EACH DESCENT.**"
- NieR changes GENRE to change your relationship to space: the manor is a third-person adventure; the
  facility is a cold overhead grid. You're not exploring anymore, you're reading a FLOOR PLAN (cf. our
  district + camera + ART PHILOSOPHY; the perspective shift as a tonal instrument).
- And the layout is the horror: "**THE ROOMS FOUND IN THE LAB ARE A MIX OF BEDROOMS, STORAGE AREAS, AND
  OFFICES WITH TUBES BIG ENOUGH TO FIT CHILDREN IN THEM, ALL SEEMINGLY LAID OUT AT RANDOM.**"
- **Bedrooms. Storage. Offices. Tubes big enough to fit children.** Listed in the same sentence, in the same
  building, at random. Nobody says anything about it (STARTLINGLY our HD tile repo + prefab factory +
  district; the room list as an atrocity).
### STAGE 4 — THE SHADES ARE PROBABLY THE STAFF
- "**NIER HACKS HIS WAY [THROUGH] DOZENS OF SHADES (PRESUMABLY, THE FORMER STAFF OF THE LABORATORY?) AS EMIL
  STRUGGLES WITH STRANGE HEADACHES.**"
- The monsters you kill on the way down are, most likely, the scientists. Nobody confirms it. The game just
  lets you notice that these particular shades are in lab coats' worth of rooms (cf. our AMALGAMATION +
  entities + [READ]).
### STAGE 5 — THE MAILBOX (the loneliest object in the game)
- "**AT THE END OF THE THIRD BASEMENT FLOOR IS A SINGLE MAILBOX AND A LONG BRIDGE THAT LEADS TO HALUA'S
  CONTAINMENT ROOM.**"
- A **MAILBOX.** Three floors underground, at the end of a corridor of child-sized tubes, outside the room
  where they keep the girl. Somebody wanted letters down here. Somebody was WRITING (cf. our [READ] +
  district + recorded-vs-unrecorded; one prop that implies a whole life).
### STAGE 6 — HALUA (the image)
- "**FINALLY, THEY REACH A GIGANTIC CIRCULAR CHAMBER. NAILED AND CHAINED TO THE WALL IN A PSEUDO-CRUCIFIX
  POSE IS A SEMI-SKELETAL FIGURE WITH A GIANT, ROUND HEAD LOCKED INTO A STRANGE SMILE, AND RED EYES. IT'S
  HALUA.**"
- "**EMIL FINALLY REMEMBERS HOW HALUA BECAME THE 'PERFECT' WEAPON—AND HOW, ONCE THEY LOST CONTROL, HE WAS
  CREATED TO PETRIFY HER AND SEAL HER AWAY.**"
- The boss room is his sister, crucified, smiling, because they made her face like that. And he did the
  chaining (cf. our AMALGAMATION + death-math).
### STAGE 7 — HE ASKS YOU TO KILL HIM, AND THEN SHE EATS HIM
- "**AND EMIL REALISES THAT HER MAGIC COULD SAVE KAINÉ, AND EVERYONE ELSE. HE TELLS NIER THAT IF HE SHOULD
  LOSE CONTROL, NIER SHOULD KILL HIM…**"
- "**HALUA SOON AROSE, AND ATE EMIL THE MOMENT SHE WAS UNLEASHED, LEAVING NIER AND GRIMOIRE WEISS NO CHOICE
  BUT TO FIGHT.**"
- The boss fight is: **your ten-year-old friend is inside the monster, and you have to hit the monster.**
- "**ONCE HALUA IS DEFEATED, EMIL SAYS HIS GOODBYES WITHIN HER AND THE TWO FUSE TOGETHER.**"
- Inside: "**IN A WHITE SPACE, EMIL SEES A VISION OF HER ORIGINAL FORM. HE EXCHANGES A FEW WORDS, AND THANKS
  HER… AND THEN IT ALL FADES TO WHITE.**" And what she says: "**HALUA TELLS EMIL IT'S OK; SHE DOESN'T BLAME
  HIM. SHE ALSO TELLS HIM THAT SHE'LL ALWAYS BE WATCHING OVER HIM.**"
- She forgives him for petrifying her, while he kills her again, to save someone he's known for a month.
### STAGE 8 — THE REWARD IS THAT HE LOSES HIS BODY
- "**OUTSIDE THE VISION, EMIL IS SURROUNDED BY AN ENORMOUS MAGIC ORB… AND AS IT FADES AWAY, HE IS IN A NEW
  BODY: LIKE HALUA, HIS HEAD IS A HUGE GRINNING ORB, AND HIS BODY IS A SKELETON.**"
- "**THIS HAS THE UNFORTUNATE SIDE-EFFECT OF TRANSFORMING A DEVASTATED EMIL INTO A SIMILAR SKELETON-LIKE BODY
  THAT FLOATS ABOVE THE GROUND. HOWEVER, NOW HE CAN SEE.**"
- The boy came down here to be CURED. He leaves able to see, able to control his power, able to save Kainé —
  **and he is a skeleton with a grinning skull for a head, forever, at ten years old.** The quest delivers
  exactly what was promised and it is a catastrophe.
- "**NIER TOOK THE TIME TO COMFORT HIM**"; "**BUT NIER PROMISES HE'LL BE THERE, NO MATTER WHAT**"; "**NIER
  ACCEPTS EMIL FOR HIS APPEARANCE REGARDLESS**" (STARTLINGLY our Megaton law + no-clean-answer + companions;
  the successful quest that ruins the client).
### STAGE 9 — THEY GO BACK DOWN TO FIX HIM AND FIND A LETTER INSTEAD
- The beat that finishes it: "**AFTER KAINÉ IS FREED, THE PARTY WOULD RETURN UNDERGROUND TO FIND A WAY TO
  RESTORE EMIL'S BODY. THEY INSTEAD FIND A LETTER FROM A WOMAN WHO USED TO WORK AT THE LAB, WHO TRIED HER
  BEST TO ACT AS A MOTHER TO THE SIBLINGS IN THE MIDST OF THEIR BEING EXPERIMENTED ON.**"
- They go back for a cure. There is no cure. **There is a letter from a staff member who tried to be their
  mom while her colleagues turned them into weapons.**
- That's the mailbox. That's who was writing (cf. our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + AMALGAMATION + fold;
  the record left by the one person who was kind).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE DUNGEON IS AN ISOMETRIC MAZE WITH KEYCARD DOORS: "**SEVERAL DOORS REMAIN LOCKED WHEN INTERACTED WITH,
  AND THEIR RESPECTIVE KEYCARDS—SCATTERED IN THE VICINITY—MUST BE FOUND TO PROGRESS**," through a layout
  that "**PROGRESSIVELY BECOMES MORE INTRICATE WITH EACH DESCENT.**" The most emotionally devastating hour in
  the game is delivered through keycard hunting. LESSON (~9th confirmation of the FETCH-CHAIN LAW): the
  writing redeems it; nothing fixes it.
- THE STORY IS SPLIT ACROSS PLAYTHROUGHS AND SUPPLEMENTAL NOVELS: "**FROM ROUTE B ON, AFTER EMIL DESTROYS THE
  VILLAGE, WE GET A FLASHBACK TO HIS CONFRONTATION WITH HALUA—ESSENTIALLY EMIL'S SIDE OF THE STONE FLOWER**,"
  and "**IN THE REMAKE... SEVERAL STORIES RELATED TO EMIL'S BACKGROUND WERE ADDED IN NOVEL FORMAT.**" His
  trauma — "**BLINDFOLDED AND PUSHED INTO THE CONFRONTATION BY A TERRIFIED MAN WHO STOMPS HIS HEAD INTO THE
  DIRT WHEN HE MAKES THE SLIGHTEST MISTAKE**" — is in a short story. LESSON (~12th confirmation): if the
  thesis is in the supplemental material, you wrote for the fans you already have.
- THE FUSION LOGIC IS INCOHERENT AND PLAYERS NOTICE: a reviewer, plainly — "**I THOUGHT SHE GOT STONED SO IDK
  WHAT IS HAPPENING HERE.**" She's petrified; she wakes up; nobody explains. LESSON: an emotional climax
  built on unexplained mechanics gives the player an exit right when you need them held.
- THE PLAYER HAS NO AGENCY IN THE BEST SCENE: Emil asks you to kill him if he loses control. You cannot. It's
  a cutscene. The promise is never a mechanic. LESSON (ours, sharp): if a character asks the player for
  something, the player should be able to be asked FOR REAL — an unfulfillable promise is a line, not a
  choice (our Megaton law).
- IT'S A TRAGEDY WITH A COMEDY ENDING AND THE TONE WHIPLASH IS REAL: after his "death," "**IT IS REVEALED
  THAT HIS HEAD WAS ALIVE**"; he lands in the desert and starts **rolling** to find his friends; a reviewer:
  "**THE CHEERFUL AND LIVELY BACKGROUND MUSIC AND SEEING EMIL BEAMING BRIGHTLY WHILE SAYING HE MISSES HIS
  COLLEAGUES JUST MADE ME LAUGH.**" LESSON (double-edged): the levity is why the despair lands, and it's a
  hairsbreadth from breaking the spell (our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope).
- THE SHADES-ARE-THE-STAFF READ IS NEVER CONFIRMED: "**PRESUMABLY, THE FORMER STAFF OF THE LABORATORY?**" The
  best detail in the dungeon is a fan's question mark. LESSON: an implication that good deserves one
  confirming document.
