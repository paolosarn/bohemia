# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #125 — "TALI: TREASON"
**Game:** Mass Effect 2 (2010)
**Studio:** BioWare / EA
**Quest:** *Tali: Treason* — Tali'Zorah's loyalty mission. She is charged with smuggling active geth into the Migrant Fleet. Shepard, as captain of her ship, is legally her advocate. The evidence that clears her convicts her dead father.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE
**Filed:** 7/16/26

---

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The quest gives you a fetch objective, and the fetch object is a loaded gun pointed at the person who asked you to fetch it.**

That is the whole machine. Everything else is staging.

Tali is on trial. You go get evidence. You find evidence. The evidence is total and exculpatory: it proves beyond argument that Tali did not smuggle active geth into the fleet. It proves this by proving that her father, Admiral Rael'Zorah, was reactivating geth aboard the Alarei, wiring them into neural networks to push them toward sapience, so he could run weapons tests on them. The geth woke up. They killed the entire crew, including him. His own recorded log explicitly states his daughter knew nothing.

So the evidence is perfect. It acquits her completely, in one document, with no interpretation needed.

And Tali stands next to you and asks you not to use it.

Not because it's false. Because her father is dead and the only thing left of him is his name, and the evidence eats it. She would rather be exiled — stripped of her ship, her people, her right to ever return — than have the fleet learn what he did. She says it out loud, in front of you, before you walk into the room.

**The design move:** the quest establishes an objective, makes you complete the objective, and then makes turning in the objective the cruelest available action. The item in your inventory is not a key. It is a decision that a person you like has already begged you not to make.

**The second move, which is the better one:** you can win without it. There is a path where you never mention the evidence, you look four politicians in the face, and you tell them the truth about *them* — that this hearing is not about Tali, it is about which of them gets to use her as a wedge in an argument about whether to go to war with the geth. And it works. She walks free with her father's name intact.

**The third move, which is the one the quest gets least credit for:** that path is gated. It is gated by a morality bar, or, alternately, by two named people who are only in the room because of what you did in earlier, unrelated missions. If you have neither, your options collapse to two: *destroy the dead man* or *lie and let her be exiled*.

**The thing Bohemia takes:** an inheritance that is simultaneously the proof of your innocence and the proof of your parent's guilt. One artifact. One object. You cannot split it. Filing it saves you and damns him. Burning it damns you and saves him. And the third option — the one where you don't need the artifact at all — exists only if you did the political homework beforehand.

The life lesson underneath, never spoken: **the truth is not neutral. It has a target. Deciding to tell it is deciding who it hits.**

## 1 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 0 — THE SUMMONS
Tali receives a message: she has been charged with treason against the Migrant Fleet and must return to answer it. She does not know the particulars. She asks Shepard to come. Shepard agrees.

Note the framing already: she is not told what she did. She is told to come home and find out.

### STAGE 1 — THE DOCKING (the insult before the trial)
The Normandy is a registered Cerberus vessel. Suspicion at the perimeter; Tali's passphrase clears them. Captain Kar'Danna meets them with a marine detail.

If Legion is in the squad, the marines point weapons at the squad on sight. Shepard can Charm or Intimidate Kar'Danna into allowing a geth aboard the flotilla. If not, Legion goes back to the ship and a different squadmate is picked. **Bringing the accused's alleged crime with you to her trial is a legal option.** The game lets you do it. This is not a joke setup; it changes the reactivity of the room.

Kar'Danna delivers the charge: Tali smuggled active geth into the fleet. She had been sending deactivated geth samples to the fleet **on the orders of her father, Admiral Rael'Zorah.** Tali insists she took every precaution; nothing she sent was dangerous. She is correct. She is also, at this point, unaware that this is true for a reason that will destroy her.

### STAGE 2 — THE NAME
At the garden plaza where the hearing will be held, Admiral Shala'Raan greets her: **"Tali'Zorah vas Normandy."**

Tali hugs her, calls her Auntie Raan, introduces her to Shepard as a friend of her father.

Then she hears it.

Quarian names carry the ship. *vas Neema* was her ship, her home, her place among her people. She has been stripped of it and renamed after a human vessel — a *Cerberus* vessel — before the hearing has even begun.

The rename is not an accident. It is a signal from the board that most of them have already voted. It is also a legal maneuver: because Shepard is the captain of the ship whose name she now carries, **Shepard is her advocate.** They made a human speak for her, in front of a body that despises human interference, and they did it by taking her home away.

Tali realizes all of this in real time, out loud, in about four seconds. The player gets 2 Paragon or 2 Renegade for the reaction.

**The quest has not started and she has already lost something.** No fight caused it. No dice roll. A word changed.

### STAGE 3 — THE ROOM (optional, and this is the trap)
Before the hearing, Shepard can walk the plaza and talk to the four admirals, plus Veetor and Kal'Reegar if they're alive and present.

This is optional. It is a talking segment with no marker, no reward pickup, no XP, in a room you can walk through in eleven seconds.

It is also **the entire mission.**

- **Admiral Han'Gerrel** — wants war. Wants to retake Rannoch by force. Thinks the quarians have gone soft, complacent, comfortable in the fleet. Believes the geth are weakened post-Citadel. Tali is useful to him as a demonstration that the geth are a live threat inside the fleet.
- **Admiral Daro'Xen** — wants the geth as property. Her position is that they can be hacked, retaken, controlled. She is openly fascinated by Legion if you brought it. Tali is useful to her as leverage for expanded AI research.
- **Admiral Zaal'Koris** — sympathizes with the geth. Argues Rael'Zorah was **torturing** them. Wants peace, and Tali's father's conduct is his exhibit A. He is the one who pushed the rename.
- **Admiral Shala'Raan** — Auntie Raan. Family friend. Presiding. Should have recused herself and did not.

You learn, by walking and listening, that **the hearing is a facade.** Tali's guilt or innocence is secondary to every person in the room. Four politicians are having a war argument, and Tali is the object they're throwing at each other.

**Nothing marks this. No quest log entry says "learn the admirals' agendas."** You can skip it entirely and never know.

### STAGE 4 — THE HEARING OPENS, AND IS INTERRUPTED
The hearing begins. Almost immediately: the Alarei — Rael'Zorah's ship, the ship Tali sent the samples to — has been overrun by geth. The crew is presumed dead. Her father is presumed dead.

The board effectively says: *there is your evidence, go get it.*

So the trial pauses, and the game hands you a shooter.

**Structurally this is important and easy to miss.** The combat is not a set piece bolted on for pacing. The combat is **discovery**. You are walking through the crime scene of the crime you're accused of, and the crime scene is your father's workplace, and the bodies are people you knew.

### STAGE 5 — THE ALAREI (the dungeon is a confession, told in order)
The ship is dark and full of geth. You fight through in waves: troopers, hunters, a Geth Prime at the top.

Along the way, **consoles and logs**, all optional, all pickups you can walk past:

- Quarian logs establishing Rael'Zorah ordered his people into increasingly reckless procedure.
- A console confirming his goal: **retake the homeworld.**
- Personal logs establishing he was not a mad scientist. He was a father who wanted his daughter to stand on Rannoch.

The environmental storytelling is doing the entire moral load-bearing job here. By the time you reach the top, the game has already answered the question "why would he do this?" — and the answer is *for her* — before it ever asks you what to do about it.

**Then the body.**

Rael'Zorah, dead on the floor of his own ship. Tali runs to him. She tries to talk herself out of it. She is not composed.

Shepard gets a **Paragon interrupt** to comfort her (+5). Or dialogue: +2 Paragon for "it wasn't his choice," +2 Renegade for "the geth will pay." Further points for noting the geth cut him off, or that he did what he could.

Note the shape of this: **the game gives you a button to hug her and pays you five points for it.** More on this in the flaws.

She finds a message he recorded for her. His last words are instructions: **destroy the main geth hub, bring the data to Admirals Xen and Gerrel.**

Read that again. His final recorded words to his daughter are a task assignment, delivered to the two admirals who want, respectively, geth as slaves and geth as a war. Even dead, even in the last thing he will ever say to her, he is handing her work.

### STAGE 6 — THE GETH PRIME, THEN THE CONSOLE
Final fight: Geth Prime, hunters, troopers, in a confined room. Hard.

Then the loot. And this is a masterpiece of tonal violence that nobody talks about:

- A **Quarian Model Ship** for Shepard's model collection.
- A laptop worth **3,000 credits.**
- A med-kit.

You are standing thirty feet from your friend's father's corpse, and the game gives you a **collectible toy** and pocket money. This is not a criticism. It is a fact about the genre that this quest sits inside and does not escape, and it is worth banking as an artifact of how loot systems corrode tone. (See FLAW F6.)

Then: **the evidence console.**

Rael'Zorah's own record. He was reactivating geth. He was linking them into neural networks to push them toward sapience. He was running weapons tests. He states plainly, on the record, **that Tali had no knowledge of any of it.**

The moment it plays, Tali begs Shepard not to show it. To preserve her father's name.

**Using the console auto-returns you to the Rayya.** No walk back. No time to think in transit. You touch the truth and you are instantly in the room where you have to decide what to do with it.

### STAGE 7 — THE TRIAL, RESUMED
The board resumed without you. Shepard can score +2 Paragon for "you should have pardoned her" or +2 Renegade for "you should have waited."

Raan asks: **do you have new evidence to submit?**

Tali pleads one more time. Then the fork.

**OPTION A — PRESENT THE EVIDENCE (+30 Renegade)**
Shepard plays the recording. Rael'Zorah explains, in his own voice, what he was doing and that his daughter had no part in it. The board **posthumously exiles Rael'Zorah** and clears Tali of all charges. Tali stays in the fleet, keeps her standing, and in ME3 is on the Admiralty Board — which is the political position required to broker geth/quarian peace.

**You lose her loyalty.** In ME2 that has a mechanical price: loyalty is a survival flag for the endgame suicide mission.

She is devastated. And Tali herself notes the reveal causes friction on the board.

**OPTION B — LIE, "WE HAVE NO EVIDENCE" (+30 Paragon)**
Shepard says they found nothing. The board convicts. **Tali is found guilty of treason and exiled from the Migrant Fleet. She has six hours to leave.**

**You gain her loyalty.** She is grateful. She lost her people and she is grateful.

And in ME3 she is not on the Admiralty Board, and the peace path is gone.

**The game pays you thirty PARAGON points for lying under oath in a court.** Bank that. It is one of the most honest things the morality system ever accidentally admitted.

**OPTION C — THE SPEECH (Charm or Intimidate, +30 of either)**
Gated behind a morality score. Shepard does not present evidence. Shepard argues that they **don't need evidence** to know Tali is a loyal person who would never harm her people. Points at her record: she saved the Citadel; she knows more about the geth than any living quarian; she just retook the Alarei.

**If you did Stage 3 — if you talked to the admirals and learned their agendas — the speech changes.** It becomes an accusation: *this trial is being abused to generate political ammunition.* Same points. Different scene. The optional conversation you could have skipped rewrites the climax.

The board exonerates her. Her father's name survives. **You get her loyalty AND the ME3 political position.** This is the best outcome in the trilogy for the quarians and the geth both.

**OPTION D — THE CROWD (no morality score required)**
If, and only if:
- you spoke to the admirals about their motivations, AND
- **Veetor** is present (only if you returned him to the Fleet instead of handing him to Cerberus, back in the game's *second mission*), AND
- **Kal'Reegar** is alive (only if you ordered him to stand down on Haestrom instead of letting him charge a geth Colossus), AND
- you asked both of them for help before leaving for the Alarei

...then you can rally the crowd. Veetor and Reegar speak. They tell the board that Tali did more for the fleet than the admirals ever will.

**Same outcome as Option C. Zero stat requirement.** Purchased entirely with two mercies you performed hours earlier in missions that had nothing to do with this one, for people whose survival the game never told you would matter.

### STAGE 8 — AFTER
If not guilty, Shala'Raan gives Shepard a Multicore Amplifier.

You can talk to Han'Gerrel. He asks what happened on the Alarei. **You can confide in him privately** — tell him what his old friend was doing. He is shocked. He promises to keep it secret. So the truth can be told *once, off the record, to a friend*, without it becoming a verdict.

Daro'Xen asks too. Tali cuts her off immediately: nothing that would help her.

Tali keeps the name **vas Normandy.** She keeps it through the entire third game, when every other quarian has switched to *vas Rannoch*. The insult became the identity.

And if you didn't use the evidence and Legion is loyal, there's a confrontation on the Normandy: Legion has Tali's data and intends to transmit it. Side with either and you lose the other's loyalty. Or pass a check and Legion withholds transmission for "unit cohesion" while Tali volunteers non-classified data instead.

**The final twist of the knife:** afterward, Shepard receives a message from Admiral Daro'Xen. She has been digging through the Alarei herself. She found things from Rael'Zorah's experiments. She notes that had Shepard shared them, humanity might have benefited — and that instead, once her own experiments are complete, humanity will watch from a distance while the quarians reclaim their homeworld.

**The evidence you protected did not stay buried. She got there anyway.** Your discretion bought Tali her father's name. It did not stop the research.

## 2 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE FETCH OBJECT IS OPTIONAL TO THE BEST ENDING.**
The sharpest critique of this quest, and it's fair: the Paragon speech is an argument Shepard could have made **before ever boarding the Alarei.** It contains no information gathered on the Alarei. You do a full combat mission for an object, and the best available outcome is reached by not using the object and giving a character-reference speech instead. A player on Steam nailed it: *smoke and mirrors distraction, that's high morality in the Mass Effect universe.* The evidence is a MacGuffin whose optimal use is to be ignored.
**LAW: if the best ending doesn't use the thing the quest sent you to get, the quest is two quests taped together.**

**F2 — THE MORALITY BAR TURNS THE DILEMMA INTO A BUILD CHECK.**
Same flaw as **Q121 (Legion: A House Divided)**, same game, same year. The dilemma is only a dilemma if you failed to invest in Charm or Intimidate. A maxed Paragon player has no choice to make; they have a button. Flaw Law 4, now with another confirmation: **a moral enforced by a stat is a puzzle, not a dilemma.**

**F3 — THE POINT VALUES ARE OBSCENE AND THE GAME DOESN'T KNOW IT.**
+30 **Paragon** for perjury. +30 **Renegade** for submitting authentic exculpatory evidence in a court proceeding. The scoring system says: lying to protect a dead war criminal's reputation is good; telling the truth to free an innocent woman is bad. That's not a nuanced position the game is arguing. It's the karma system failing to have a vocabulary for the scene it's attached to. **This is the strongest argument in the entire questbook for NO KARMA BAR in Bohemia.** Score the scene and the scene becomes a lie about itself.

**F4 — THEME-IN-OPTIONAL, THE ~18TH CONFIRMATION.**
The revelation that the trial is a political facade lives in **unmarked optional conversations in a plaza you can sprint through.** The entire meaning of the quest is behind a talk you're not told to have. Worse: it's a *precondition* for both good endings (it changes the speech, and it's required for the crowd rally). **Q114 is the fix: content found by WALKING, cannot be missed.**

**F5 — OPTION D IS BUILT ON TWO INVISIBLE FLAGS SET HOURS EARLIER.**
Veetor's fate is decided in **mission two of the game.** Reegar's survival depends on a stand-down order during a firefight where the "heroic" read is to let him fight. Neither moment signals that it will matter. This is glorious *in retrospect* and unfair *in play* — a first-time player has no mechanism for knowing that two acts of mercy are also political capital. Bank it as a virtue with a caveat: **the payoff is one of the best in the trilogy and almost nobody sees it on a blind run.**

**F6 — THE LOOT DESTROYS THE ROOM.**
A model ship collectible and 3,000 credits, harvested from the site of your friend's father's death, thirty seconds before you play his final message. The systems layer and the drama layer are not speaking. This is not fixable by writing better; it's fixable by **not putting a collectible in the room.**
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA: no loot in a scene that ends in a body the player knows by name.**

**F7 — THE +5 HUG.**
The Paragon interrupt over Rael'Zorah's corpse pays five morality points for comforting a grieving friend. The game turned compassion into a pickup. It's a small thing and it's a real one: **the moment you score kindness, the player stops feeling it and starts collecting it.**

**F8 — TALI'S REQUEST IS NEVER SERIOUSLY INTERROGATED.**
Her father's conduct got his entire crew killed. She asks you to bury that to protect his name, and the game gives Shepard no dialogue to say *your father killed those people, and their families are in this room.* Players noticed. The quest wants her request to be sympathetic and so it removes the strongest argument against it. **The game should let the player make the hard argument and still lose the fight.**

**F9 — THE PROXY WAR RESOLVES INTO NOTHING.**
If you asked the admirals to move toward war or toward peace, their replies are **noncommittal, regardless.** The one place the quest could have let you touch the actual political machine, it doesn't. The facade is diagnosed and never operated on.

**F10 — CUT CONTENT WAS THE BETTER QUEST.**
Development data shows a version where **each admiral's argument was individually refutable with evidence found on the Alarei**: Raan's security claim, Koris's torture claim, Gerrel's geth-are-weakened theory, Xen's hacking premise. Four rebuttals, four discoveries, a real trial. Legion could reportedly testify **for Rael'Zorah**. That version is a courtroom. What shipped is a shooter with a cutscene at each end. **Bank the cut version; it's the design Bohemia should actually build.**

## 3 WHY IT WORKS (W1–W10)

W1. **THE OBJECTIVE IS THE WEAPON.** The quest sends you to find evidence, and the evidence is not a key that opens a door — it is a loaded thing that has a target, and the target is the father of the woman standing next to you. The instant you learn what it says, the fetch quest becomes a moral object. No other quest in the trilogy turns an inventory item into a decision this cleanly. **The item does not unlock the ending. The item IS the ending, and you get to not use it.**

W2. **THE PUNISHMENT ARRIVES BEFORE THE VERDICT.** She is renamed *vas Normandy* in the first thirty seconds, in a friendly greeting, by her aunt. No combat, no roll, no fail state. A word changed and she lost her home. The board's decision is telegraphed by grammar. This is the single most economical piece of writing in the mission: **the trial's outcome is announced by how someone says hello.**

W3. **THE INSULT BECOMES THE IDENTITY.** She keeps *vas Normandy* forever. Through the entire third game, when every other quarian is *vas Rannoch*. The thing they did to shame her became the thing she is. **A punishment the character decides to keep is worth ten speeches about resilience.**

W4. **THE DUNGEON IS A CONFESSION, ORDERED.** The Alarei is not a level, it is an argument delivered in sequence: reckless orders, then the stated goal (retake the homeworld), then the personal logs (he did it for her), then the body, then his last message (a task assignment), then the full technical record. By the time the game asks you the question, it has already answered *why* — and the answer makes the truth harder to tell, not easier. **Structure the discovery so the motive lands BEFORE the crime does.**

W5. **HIS LAST WORDS TO HIS DAUGHTER ARE A WORK ORDER.** "Destroy the main hub, bring the data to Xen and Gerrel." No apology. No I love you that isn't wrapped in an instruction. The most devastating characterization of a father in the trilogy, delivered in one recorded log, and the game never comments on it. **Let the dead reveal themselves through what they thought was urgent.**

W6. **THE HEARING IS A FACADE AND YOU CAN LEARN THAT BY LISTENING.** Four admirals, four agendas, one of them presiding without recusing, none of them primarily interested in whether she did it. War party, slaver party, peace party, family friend. The verdict is a vote about the geth war. **The trial is a proxy fight, and Tali is the object being thrown.**

W7. **THE LEARNING REWRITES THE CLIMAX.** Do the optional walk and the Paragon speech becomes a different speech: not "she's a good person," but "you are abusing this hearing for political ammunition." Same button, same points, different scene. **Optional knowledge that changes the TEXT of the climax rather than unlocking a new one is the cheapest, highest-value reactivity in the genre.**

W8. **THE BEST OUTCOME IS PURCHASED WITH MERCY YOU DIDN'T KNOW YOU WERE BANKING.** Veetor spared from Cerberus in mission two. Reegar ordered to stand down instead of dying heroically. Neither flagged. Both show up in a courtroom hours later and testify — **and they beat the stat check.** You don't need Charm if you were decent to two people who couldn't help you at the time. **The best political capital in the game is people you were kind to when it wasn't useful.**

W9. **EVERY ENDING COSTS SOMETHING REAL AND THE COSTS ARE INCOMPARABLE.** Truth: she keeps her people, loses her father's name, loses you. Lie: she keeps her father's name, loses her people, and thanks you for it, and a species war stays on the rails. Speech: costs a stat, or costs nothing, and that's the flaw. **There is no currency that converts a name into a nation, and the game refuses to invent one.**

W10. **THE SECRET DOESN'T STAY SECRET AND THE GAME TELLS YOU IN AN EMAIL.** Daro'Xen digs through the Alarei herself, finds the research, and sends you a note saying you should have shared it and now you'll watch from a distance. **Your discretion protected a name, not a truth.** The most mature consequence in the mission is a piece of mail confirming that suppression is a delay, not a deletion — and it arrives after the reward screen, when you thought the quest was over.

## 4 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE INHERITANCE THAT IS ALSO THE INDICTMENT [core port]
**System:** THE FOLD / dynasty succession / [READ]

Gen 2 inherits a sealed record from Gen 1's era. It is the only document that clears the current dynast of a charge the settlement is actually going to rule on. It is also, in the same file, the proof that the previous dynast did the thing.

**One artifact. Not splittable. Not redactable.** The player cannot present half.

Against **Q98 (Isidor's body)** and **Q113 (Isidor's list)**: Pathologic hands you a corpse and a list of names with no reason attached. Bohemia hands you a corpse with a **reason attached that makes it worse.**

Against **Q120 (Deionarra)**: the dead used as a component. Rael'Zorah isn't engineered to be useful — he's *voluntarily* useful, right up to the last recorded second, and that's the horror.

**Bohemia line, on the record, playable:**
> *"She had no part in it. She sent what I told her to send and she checked every crate twice. Write that down. Then destroy the hub and take the numbers to whoever will use them."*

He clears her and gives her a chore in the same breath.

### PORT 2 — THE VERDICT IS ANNOUNCED BY HOW SOMEONE SAYS HELLO [W2, W3]
**System:** faction reputation / naming / the 13 factions

Bohemia already has factions-as-characters. Give one of them a **naming grammar** — a title that encodes belonging. Then, at the top of a quest, have a friendly NPC greet the dynast with the wrong title, warmly, without malice, because that's what the paperwork now says.

No combat. No stat. **A word changed and you're out.**

And then — **W3, the better half** — if the dynast wins the trial anyway, they can **keep the wrong name.** Permanently. It shows up in every subsequent greeting, every generation, and it means *I was thrown out and I came back and I kept the receipt.*

The Amalgamation, of course, files people by name. **[PENDING, Paolo's call]** — whether a dynast can choose a name the Amalgamation didn't assign, and whether that's an act of freedom or an act of deletion.

### PORT 3 — THE HEARING IS A PROXY WAR [W6, F9]
**System:** the 13 factions / mayor persistence / the Amalgamation-vs-NETWORK argument

A Bohemia tribunal where **four faction reps** each want a different thing and none of them care about the accused:
- one wants a war footing and needs a live threat to prove
- one wants the Amalgamation's dead as a *resource*, and needs research access
- one wants coexistence and needs an atrocity to point at
- one is family and is presiding anyway

**FIX F9 IMMEDIATELY.** Mass Effect diagnoses the facade and then makes every reply noncommittal. In Bohemia the tribunal **is a governance event**: the verdict moves the actual faction war-posture number, permanently, in the direction of whoever's argument you fed. You don't just learn it's a proxy fight — **you get to pick who wins the proxy fight, and the settlement lives with it for a generation.**

Cross-ref **Q107 (Frostpunk Londoners — people you saved organizing against you)** and **Q104 (Vault 11 — a community that gave its atrocity a procedure)**.

### PORT 4 — BUILD THE CUT VERSION [F10]
**System:** [READ] / the tribunal

The cut ME2 design: each admiral's specific claim individually refutable with a specific document found in the dungeon. **That is the quest Bohemia should build**, because Bohemia already has [READ] and already has a record system.

Four claims. Four documents in the ruin. You find zero, one, two, three, or four. **The verdict is a tally, not a switch.** Find two of four and you get a hung tribunal, which is its own outcome with its own consequences.

This also **fixes F1**: the evidence you gather is no longer an optional MacGuffin. It is the mechanism. The dungeon run is load-bearing.

And the father's file is a **fifth** document — the one that refutes *all four at once* and detonates.

### PORT 5 — NO KARMA BAR, AND THIS QUEST IS THE PROOF [F3, F2]
**System:** CONSCIENCE SYSTEM — FINAL

+30 Paragon for lying under oath. +30 Renegade for submitting true evidence that frees an innocent woman.

**Print it on the wall.** This is the single best argument in 125 files for Bohemia's no-karma-bar law. The moment the scene gets a score, the score contradicts the scene, and the player optimizes toward the number instead of the person. **Q121 proved the same thing with "+30 PARAGON" on a button. This is the second conviction on the same charge.**

Bohemia's version: the tribunal outcome writes to **relationships and faction posture**. Nothing writes to a morality total, because there isn't one.

And **F2**: no Charm gate. Which leads directly to —

### PORT 6 — THE MERCY THAT SHOWS UP IN COURT [W8]
**System:** persistent consequence / PACIFIST LAW

Bohemia already has the mechanism from **Q123 (Come Fly With Me)**: mercy shows up in the passenger manifest. This is the same law, aimed at a courtroom.

Two people the dynast spared in Act 1, in situations where sparing them was **inefficient and unrewarded**, walk into the Act 2 tribunal and testify.

**Requirements:** they must have been spared when it cost something. They must have been unable to help at the time. The game must never flag that it will matter.

**And per F2, this path must NOT be a backup for a failed stat check. It must be the PRIMARY path.** Mass Effect made the crowd rally the alternate route for players without Charm. Bohemia inverts it: **there is no Charm. There is only who you were decent to.**

**Bohemia line, from a man the dynast pulled out of a collapse and never saw again:**
> *"I'm not anybody. I dig. But she carried me nine blocks with a busted hip and she didn't know my name either. That's my testimony. Write down that I said it."*

Cross-ref **Q117 (Papyrus)** — a boss who spares you first. Cross-ref **Q114** — a man beaten daily gives you the power to see danger.

### PORT 7 — THE MOTIVE LANDS BEFORE THE CRIME [W4]
**System:** [READ] as level design / ruin generation

**Q124 (Emil) established it:** the dungeon is his mind, every document a memory returning. **Q125 sharpens it:** *order the documents so the sympathetic explanation arrives BEFORE the damning fact.*

In the Bohemia ruin the dynast finds, in this order:
1. an order that was reckless
2. the stated goal, which is noble
3. the personal reason, which is love
4. the body
5. the last recorded words, which are a chore
6. **then** the technical record, which is the crime

By step 6 the player already forgave him. **That is why filing it hurts.**

Cross-ref **Q122 (Sinnerman)**: the horror is the clipboard, not the cross. Same technique. The paperwork lands last and the paperwork is the wound.

### PORT 8 — NO LOOT IN A ROOM WITH A NAMED BODY [F6, F7]
**System:** ART PHILOSOPHY / inventory / production law

**LOCK THIS AS A PRODUCTION LAW.** Bohemia has three currencies (medicine, electricity, resources) and a scavenging loop that touches every room. Therefore, explicitly:

> **NAMED-BODY LAW:** any room containing the corpse of a character the player knows by name contains ZERO scavengeable currency, ZERO collectibles, ZERO containers. The scavenge verb is removed in that room, not just unrewarded.

Same family as **Q118 / Q122 / Q123: the game REMOVES THE VERB.** Q125 says do it for grief, not just for violence.

### PORT 9 — THE SECRET DOESN'T KEEP, AND THE MAIL SAYS SO [W10]
**System:** persistent consequence / the Amalgamation / generational persistence

The dynast buries the record to protect a name. **One generation later**, a message arrives from the faction rep who wanted the research: *we went back to the site ourselves. We found it. You could have shared it and been part of what comes next. Instead you'll watch.*

**Suppression is a delay, not a deletion.** Which is the Amalgamation's thesis stated from the other side — the Amalgamation exists precisely because nothing anybody uploaded was ever really deleted.

Cross-ref **Q119 (Mikhaila's father)**: truth as a two-button PLAY/DELETE decision made alone. Q125 adds: **DELETE doesn't work, and you find out from someone gloating in your inbox.**

Cross-ref **Q116 / Q119** and **FLAW LAW 3**: this is a persistent-consequence flag spanning a generation boundary. **It needs a regression gate in `bohemia_tests.js` on day one.** Q116 shipped a flag bugged for twenty-five years. Bohemia writes the test before the content.

### PORT 10 — THE PRIVATE TRUTH [Stage 8]
**System:** dialogue scenes / relationships

After the tribunal, if the dynast protected the record, they can **tell one person, privately, off the record.** An old friend of the dead. He is shocked. He keeps it.

The truth gets told exactly once, to exactly one person, and it changes nothing legally and everything between two people.

**Bohemia needs this verb.** Not "confess" — **confide**. It is not a choice with a consequence tree. It is a scene where you get to stop being a politician for ninety seconds.

**[PENDING, Paolo's call]** — whether confiding creates a persistent risk (he might tell someone, later, in Gen 3) or whether it's a clean, safe, human moment that the game just gives you. Both are defensible. The first is more Bohemia. The second is rarer and might be worth more.

### PORT 11 — BUILD THE CONSEQUENCE FIRST [F10, method]
**System:** FACTORY LAW

**Q123 established it: BUILD THE CONSEQUENCE BEFORE THE CONTENT.** ME2 cut four evidence-vs-admiral rebuttals and shipped a corridor shooter with the courtroom trimmed to a switch. The consequence got scoped out and the content survived, which is exactly backward.

For the Bohemia tribunal: **write the four verdict states and their generational effects FIRST.** Then write the documents. Then build the ruin. If scope cuts, cut the ruin, not the tribunal.
