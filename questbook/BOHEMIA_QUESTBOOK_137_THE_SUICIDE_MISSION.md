# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #137 — "THE SUICIDE MISSION"
**Game:** Mass Effect 2 (2010)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** The finale where anyone can die and everyone can live: a raid through a one-way relay in which every casualty is computed from two ledgers the player filled all game — the SHIP's upgrades and the CREW's loyalties — plus a final exam in matching specialists to jobs they're actually specialized for.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The finale is an audit: it opens both of the game's ledgers — preparation and relationships — and pays out survival line by line, with zero mercy and zero malice.**

The Omega-4 relay kills everyone who enters. Shepard's counter is a game-long shopping-and-listening list: ship armor, shields, weapons (bought with mined resources); and twelve loyalty missions (each companion's personal wound, resolved or not). Then the mission itself — and the deaths are ARITHMETIC:

- Skipped the ship upgrades? Named crew die in the approach, one per missing system, in scripted order.
- Chose a non-specialist for the vent crawl? They die at the door — the job needed a TECH, and "I like him" is not a qualification.
- Chose a specialist who isn't LOYAL? Sometimes they die anyway — competence without commitment cracks under fire.
- Picked the wrong leader for the second team? THEIR choice-victim dies — leadership as a stat other people pay for.
- Sent no escort with the rescued crew? The crew dies. Sent an escort? That companion survives by leaving — removal as safety.
- Held the line with too weak a rearguard? The math eats the weakest remaining.

**The corpus needs this file because it is the medium's most complete PREPARATION-AS-NARRATIVE machine: no boss difficulty spike, no dice — every death is a receipt for a specific, identifiable, player-authored gap.** And the inverse is the miracle: full ledgers, correct assignments, and ALL FIFTEEN WALK OUT — the "everyone lives" run as an achievable, calculable state.

The life lesson underneath: **people don't die in the crisis; they die in the weeks you didn't spend getting ready for it.**

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**SHEPARD** — wants everyone home; has been told it's impossible. FUNCTION: the auditor-in-chief — the finale grades their game-long attention, not their aim.
**THE TWELVE** — each wants their WOUND CLOSED (a father found, a betrayal answered, a sister saved) — and their survival odds are literally keyed to whether Shepard helped close it. Loyalty as armor, mechanically true. FUNCTION: the ledger with faces.
**THE NORMANDY** — wants parts. Each un-bought system is a named person's death warrant, pre-signed. FUNCTION: the other ledger — steel as survival's first draft.
**THE ILLUSIVE MAN** — wants the base intact (the endgame's second choice: keep or burn the Collector tech). FUNCTION: the employer whose invoice arrives at the finish line.
**THE CREW (Kelly, Chakwas, the nameless)** — want rescue on a TIMER: dawdle after the abduction and they're rendered — the finale's clock starts before the finale. FUNCTION: the cost of side-questing after the alarm — the game's harshest hidden ledger line.
**THE COLLECTORS / THE HUMAN REAPER** — want raw material. FUNCTION: pressure; the enemy is almost irrelevant to the design — the real opponent is the audit.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

STAGE 0 — THE TWO LEDGERS [the whole game]: upgrades bought or not; loyalties earned or not; every hour of play silently filling the finale's actuarial table.
STAGE 1 — THE ABDUCTION CLOCK: the crew taken; every mission spent before the relay converts rescued-crew to corpses on a hidden timer. The finale punishes the completionist instinct it spent a game rewarding — the design's cruelest and most honest reversal.
STAGE 2 — THE APPROACH: un-upgraded systems fail in order; named deaths, scripted, one per gap. The receipts print before the door opens.
STAGE 3 — THE VENT: pick a tech specialist (loyal) or bury someone at a valve. The first assignment exam.
STAGE 4 — THE TEAMS: second-team leader (a LEADER, loyal — or their squad pays); the escort decision (send one home to save the crew, or spend them all forward); the biotic bubble (a loyal biotic or losses in the swarm).
STAGE 5 — THE HOLD/THE BASE: the rearguard math; the final fight; the keep-or-burn choice under the Illusive Man's eye.
STAGE 6 — THE COUNT: the walk to the Normandy under fire — who runs up the ramp is the game's true ending screen. From fifteen alive to Shepard-dies-too (yes: lose enough and the PROTAGONIST's survival fails the audit — the finale will kill its own hero for insufficient bookkeeping, the medium's only such clause).

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

F1 — THE RULES ARE INVISIBLE AND THE WIKI IS MANDATORY. The assignment logic (who counts as "tech," what loyalty covers) is unstated; blind players lose loved companions to hidden rubrics and learn the rules from the internet, after. LAW: the audit's CATEGORIES must be diegetic (dossiers state specialties; the game had the documents and didn't cite them at the node).
F2 — LOYALTY IS BINARY AND FOREVER. One argument mis-handled weeks ago flips a flag that no later devotion can reset; relationships as fuses, not accounts. The model's cleanliness is its falseness.
F3 — THE HIDDEN CREW TIMER PUNISHES THE GAME'S OWN TAUGHT BEHAVIOR. Rewarding thoroughness for 40 hours then executing hostages for it is honest ONCE and vicious always; the reversal deserved one in-world warning (Joker knows; Joker could have said).
F4 — AFFECTION-ASSIGNMENT DEATHS CAN FEEL LIKE GOTCHAS. The trap-of-love is the thesis AND, absent F1's fix, indistinguishable from trivia failure. The same beat is profound or cheap depending entirely on information design.
F5 — SOME DEATHS ARE UNDER-WEIGHTED ON ARRIVAL. Casualties resolve as brief holo-plaques; the funeral budget is one scene for up to a dozen dead. The arithmetic's gravity outruns its ceremony.
F6 — THE ENEMY IS SET DRESSING. The Collectors never adapt to your roster or exploit your gaps; the opponent is inert while the audit does everything. Thematically pure, dramatically thin.
F7 — SHEPARD'S OWN DEATH CLAUSE IS TRIVIA-SHAPED. The hero dies only at near-total-loss thresholds most players never approach; the boldest rule in the design fires for almost no one.
F8 — MINING FUNDED IT. The ship-ledger's currency comes from the game's worst minigame; the finale's steel half is bought with tedium, and the tedium leaks backward into the meaning.
F9 — THE SECOND LEDGER HAS NO PARTIAL CREDIT. Un-loyal but present, half-helped, argued-with-then-reconciled — all compile to the same 0. The human middle states the model can't hold are where most real relationships live.
F10 — THE ALL-LIVE RUN IS THE CANON OF RECORD AND THE DESIGN'S QUIET DEFEAT. The optimal path erases the finale's entire death-machinery; the masterpiece state is the one where the masterpiece never fires. Banked as the preparation-design paradox: PERFECT AUDITS ARE INVISIBLE.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1–W10)

W1. **EVERY DEATH IS A RECEIPT.** No dice, no spikes: each casualty names its cause in player-history — the un-bought plating, the un-heard companion, the wrong name at the vent. Consequence with a paper trail, at scale.
W2. **THE FINALE GRADES ATTENTION, NOT AIM.** Combat skill barely moves the count; listening and logistics move everything. The climax of a shooter is won in conversations and shopping — the medium's quietest genre betrayal, and its best.
W3. **LOYALTY IS ARMOR, LITERALLY.** The wound-closing missions convert to survival odds: being KNOWN by your commander is ballistic protection. The mechanic states the file's thesis without a line of dialogue.
W4. **THE CASTING IS THE CHOICE.** The mission's shape is fixed; the player's authorship is entirely WHO — the manager's finale, where every option list is a roster and every mistake is a staffing decision with a face.
W5. **THE ESCORT LIVES BY LEAVING.** The one guaranteed survival is the assignment that exits the story — presence spent to purchase absence. The design's subtlest line-item.
W6. **THE CLOCK STARTS BEFORE THE FINALE.** The abduction timer converts the pre-mission lull into the first casualty table — readiness includes PUNCTUALITY, and the game means it.
W7. **THE HERO IS SUBJECT TO THE MATH.** Shepard can die of insufficient bookkeeping — the audit exempts no one, including the camera's owner. The medium's only finale with a protagonist-solvency clause.
W8. **THE DEAD STAY DEAD ACROSS A SEQUEL.** The casualty list imports; ME3 is populated by your arithmetic's survivors and haunted by its receipts. The corpus's longest consequence tail (with Q125/Q121 — one studio, one law).
W9. **THE ALL-LIVE RUN EXISTS AND IS EARNED.** Fifteen through the relay and fifteen up the ramp — attainable, calculable, no luck involved. Hope as a spreadsheet you can actually balance; the counter-file to every doomed finale in the corpus.
W10. **THE OPPONENT IS THE AUDIT.** The Collectors are furniture; the antagonist is your own game-long ledger, walking beside you through the base. The finale externalizes nothing — every threat was always you.

### PORT 3 — PARTIAL CREDIT [F2/F9 fixed]
Bohemia's bond-state is a GRADIENT, not a flag — half-helped is half-armor; reconciled-after-rupture carries a scar modifier. The .bq's bond gates take thresholds ([gate: bond:tali>=60]), and the finale's math reads the real number. The human middle states, held.
### PORT 4 — THE ESCORT CLAUSE [W5]
Every Bohemia crisis-event includes one REMOVAL-AS-SAFETY assignment: a companion who survives certainly by exiting the event (escorting evacuees, holding the far gate). Spending presence to purchase a life — the choice's cost is the empty slot beside you for the rest of the fight.
### PORT 5 — PERFECT AUDITS MADE VISIBLE [F10 fixed]
The all-live run's paradox, solved by the Fold: a flawless generational audit writes its OWN entry — the crisis report that lists every death that DIDN'T happen and why ("vent: T. — lived. loyal, qualified, chosen right"). Bohemia makes the invisible masterpiece readable: the dodged bullet gets a receipt too. Gen 3 inherits the list of un-deaths. Nothing in the corpus does this; Bohemia can.
