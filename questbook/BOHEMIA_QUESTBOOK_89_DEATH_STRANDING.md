# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 89: THE ROPE, NOT THE STICK / STRANGERS BUILT THE BRIDGE YOU'RE STANDING ON (Death Stranding)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the SOCIAL STRAND SYSTEM (asynchronous multiplayer where other players'
ladders, bridges and roads appear in your solo game), the Kobo Abe stick-and-rope thesis, terrain as the
antagonist, LIKES as the only currency (no material reward for helping), paths that smooth as people walk
them, the crowdfunded highway, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. The most divisive AAA game of its era
and the one that invented a genre. STARTLINGLY relevant to our fold's inheritance, our city-builder, our
district, + our co-op-with-nobody problem. Kojima Productions (Hideo Kojima). Reference only; Paolo does not
read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Death Stranding (Kojima Productions, 2019; DS2, 2025). You are SAM PORTER BRIDGES, a courier in a
shattered post-apocalyptic America. You carry cargo across a hostile continent to reconnect isolated
settlements to a network. The combat is optional and mediocre on purpose. The actual gameplay is WALKING.
And every ladder, rope, bridge, and road you find was placed by another player you will never meet.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE ROPE, NOT THE STICK (the thesis — and it's the cleanest statement of our PACIFIST LAW's philosophy in
  the canon): "Kojima's whole Strand idea hinges on A METAPHOR FROM KOBO ABE. Essentially, EARLY HUMANS
  USED **STICKS** TO WARD OFF THREATS AND **ROPES** TO BRING THINGS CLOSER. **MOST GAMES, HE ARGUED, ARE
  ABOUT STICKS: VIOLENCE, ENEMIES, COMPETITION. BUT DEATH STRANDING IS A ROPE. IT'S ABOUT CONNECTION AND
  EMPATHY INSTEAD.**"
  - And Kojima FOLLOWED THROUGH mechanically: "Even when there is some combat, IT FEELS A BIT OUT OF PLACE
    IN A WORLD THAT'S DEDICATED ALMOST EXCLUSIVELY TO KINDNESS."
  - "Where other games hand you power and speed, STRAND GAMES ASK YOU TO EARN BOTH, OFTEN BY HELPING
    OTHERS" (cf. our PACIFIST LAW + Arcanum's XP-curve warning Q86; the game where the verb is not violence).
- THE SOCIAL STRAND SYSTEM: OTHER PLAYERS BUILD YOUR WORLD AND YOU NEVER MEET THEM (the landmark mechanic):
  "The game's asynchronous multiplayer... enables online players across the world to INDIRECTLY HELP ONE
  ANOTHER. ROADS, BRIDGES, AND SAFE HOUSES CAN BE BUILT... and players MAY CONTRIBUTE TO THE CONSTRUCTION
  of such structures."
  - Kojima trademarked "Social Strand System" and "Strand Game" in Feb 2018, and explained the naming:
    "Calling it Social Strand System is a SIMILAR CASE TO WHEN I CALLED [Metal Gear] TACTICAL ESPIONAGE
    ACTION BEFORE IT WAS CATEGORIZED AS STEALTH GAME."
  - Game Revolution: "SIMPLE IN CONCEPT, BUT REVOLUTIONARY IN EXECUTION... IT BRIDGED THE DIVIDE BETWEEN
    SINGLE-PLAYER AND MULTIPLAYER GAMING."
  - Crucially: "A PLAYSTATION PLUS SUBSCRIPTION IS NOT REQUIRED to access the Social Strand System's
    features, AND SAID FEATURES ARE OPTIONAL." No server, no subscription, no lobby (cf. Dragon's Dogma's
    Pawns Q84, our fold + district + single-file constraint; multiplayer with nobody).
- THE ONLY REWARD FOR HELPING IS A LIKE, AND IT WORKS ANYWAY (the psychology): "There's NO REWARD for
  leaving behind helpful tools or completing another player's delivery, well, UNLESS YOU COUNT 'LIKES' AS A
  REWARD." Likes buy nothing. They're not currency. They're gratitude.
  - And the emergent proof, from a designer writing about it: "There are points when the player has enough
    resources to construct a bridge that eliminates the need for traversal... HOWEVER, A SINGLE PLAYER DOES
    NOT HAVE ENOUGH RESOURCES TO CREATE IT AT A SINGLE TIME. Thus, PLAYERS CONTRIBUTE SOME AMOUNT TO THAT
    RESOURCE AND MOVE AWAY ON THEIR OWN, the process continues, and SUBSEQUENT WAVES OF PLAYERS HAVE THE
    OPPORTUNITY TO DRIVE ON THAT BRIDGE... **THE PLAYER IS NOT REQUIRED TO GIVE THEIR RESOURCES TO THE
    PARTICULAR STRUCTURE, YET THEY DO SO NEVERTHELESS.**"
  - A crowdfunded highway built by strangers who will never use it together. "Kojima Productions suggests
    that THE GREATEST HOPE WE HAVE IS ONE ANOTHER. **EVEN IF YOU CONSTRUCT THINGS JUST FOR YOURSELF, THEY
    WILL HELP OTHERS**" (STARTLINGLY our city-builder + fold; construction as an act of faith in strangers).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how walking becomes a game)
===============================================================================

### THE TERRAIN IS THE BOSS (the traversal thesis)
- "The game places a UNIQUE EMPHASIS ON BALANCE AND PHYSICS, REQUIRING PLAYERS TO STRATEGIZE EVERY STEP.
  YOU ARE LITERALLY CONTROLLING SAM'S BALANCE as he walks and climbs WITH THE L AND R TRIGGERS."
- "Death Stranding's carefully considered ambulation mechanic TOOK SOMETHING WE NEVER EVEN THINK ABOUT IN
  GAMES (WALKING AROUND) AND TURNED IT INTO SOMETHING THAT REQUIRES OUR ATTENTION."
- "A lot of players saw the wide open spaces and THOUGHT THEY'D BE SKYRIM-ING IT UP by sprinting and
  smashing a jump button." They were wrong, and half of them quit. "Haters labeled it A 'WALKING SIMULATOR'
  but a lot of fans found it to be INTEGRAL to how much they enjoyed exploring the world" (cf. our 120-BPM
  movement law + district; the trivial verb made load-bearing).
### THE PATHS SMOOTH AS PEOPLE WALK THEM (the best small system here)
- "When using the odradek, players may spot FAINT BLUE FOOTPRINTS — TRACES LEFT BY OTHER PORTERS WHO HAVE
  TAKEN THE SAME ROUTE. **IF ENOUGH PLAYERS FOLLOW THE SAME TRAIL, A VISIBLE FOOTPATH WILL GRADUALLY FORM,
  SMOOTHING THE TERRAIN AND MAKING TRAVERSAL EASIER FOR EVERYONE WHO FOLLOWS.**"
- Desire paths, simulated. The route becomes a road because people used it. Nobody decided; everybody
  decided (cf. our district + city-builder + street generation; infrastructure as accumulated use).
### DEATH PUTS YOU IN A ROOM WITH OTHER PEOPLE'S SOULS
- "If Sam dies, HE ENTERS THE SEAM — A LIMINAL SPACE BETWEEN THE WORLD OF THE LIVING AND THE DEAD. Here,
  the player can NAVIGATE SAM'S SOUL BACK TO HIS BODY to restore him to life. **WHILE IN THE SEAM, THE
  FLOATING SOULS OF OTHER PLAYERS CAN ALSO BE SEEN. BY APPROACHING AND TOUCHING THESE SOULS, THE PLAYER CAN
  STRENGTHEN THEIR CONNECTION WITH THOSE INDIVIDUALS** before returning Sam to the living world."
- Even the death screen is a social space. You fail, and you drift among everyone else who failed (cf. our
  death-math + AMALGAMATION; the afterlife as a network — this is almost literally our premise).
### GHOSTS OF STRANGERS HELP YOU IN A FIGHT
- "During battles against BTs, other players can assist Sam in limited but meaningful ways. WHILE THEY
  CANNOT JOIN THE FIGHT DIRECTLY... **WHITE, GHOST-LIKE FIGURES RESEMBLING SAM WILL EMERGE FROM THE TAR TO
  OFFER SUPPORT. THESE FIGURES REPRESENT FELLOW PORTERS AND WILL THROW USEFUL ITEMS** — such as hematic
  grenades or blood bags — to aid Sam."
- The help arrives as an apparition of a person who isn't there (cf. our combat dial + companions).
### THE ANCESTRY IS ACKNOWLEDGED, AND IT MATTERS FOR US
- "Before Kojima named it, other games were already doing Strand-like things. **JOURNEY had anonymous online
  players helping each other WITHOUT WORDS. DARK SOULS used BLOODSTAINS AND HANDWRITTEN WARNINGS to create
  a sense of shared struggle.** Even Deathloop had indirect multiplayer that blurred timelines."
- Death Stranding's addition over Dark Souls: "it builds upon it by **SUBSTITUTING HELPFUL HINTS FOR ACTUAL
  IN-GAME ITEMS.** These items, from ropes to ladders, etc., can help other players traverse the harsh
  conditions."
- A message says "try jumping." A LADDER lets you jump (cf. Dragon's Dogma Q84, our fold; asynchronous help
  as OBJECTS, not text).
### THE PREMISE AGED INTO PROPHECY (the craft note)
- "At launch, it was easy to have a cynical, eye-rolling take on Sam's chosen profession. A DELIVERY GUY?
  The man who brought us Solid Snake... was putting us in the shoes of A GLORIFIED AMAZON DRIVER? We're
  supposed to think these guys are important? **THEN 2020 HAPPENED AND, IN FACT, DELIVERY DRIVERS WERE THE
  MOST IMPORTANT PEOPLE IN THE WORLD.** Suddenly, Kojima's dystopian fantasy FELT A LOT MORE REAL."
- A game about isolation, infrastructure, and the people who keep supply chains alive, shipped four months
  before a pandemic (cf. our post-economic-collapse premise + logistics; the premise that gets realer).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE FIRST TEN HOURS ARE A JOB: "the first five to ten hours of the game are INCREDIBLY TEDIOUS AND
  MONOTONOUS and can only be described as an **'AMAZON DELIVERY SIMULATOR,'** where the player just needs to
  keep walking." The system that makes it sing (other players' structures) doesn't populate until later.
  LESSON: an asynchronous social system is EMPTY AT THE START — for us, a fold-based one has the same
  problem in generation one and must seed itself.
- OTHER PLAYERS' JUNK MISLEADS YOU: IGN's Ogilvie flagged "the POTENTIALLY MISLEADING NATURE OF INCOMPLETE
  OR POORLY PLACED PLAYER OBJECTS," plus "THE LESSENING OF BOSS FIGHTS' DIFFICULTY" from other people's
  donated gear, and "the system's LACK OF CONFIGURATION SETTINGS." LESSON (direct): shared/inherited content
  needs CURATION and a difficulty-integrity check, or generosity becomes noise and trivializes your
  encounters.
- THE STORY IS OPAQUE ON PURPOSE AND IT COST THEM: "the plot is ABSOLUTELY BONKERS and ANY ATTEMPT TO
  SUMMARIZE IT WILL BE FOR NAUGHT." "The vibe is chaotic, with plenty of the ESOTERIC KOJIMA FLAVOR SOME
  SEE AS HIGH ART, AND OTHERS AS SILLY RUBBISH." David Jaffe on the marketing: "MAYBE KOJIMA'S JUST DRANK A
  LITTLE BIT TOO MUCH OF HIS OWN KOOL-AID and thinks that the kind of CRYPTIC, WEIRD SHIT that his hardcore
  fans love... Maybe he's like, 'THAT'S WHAT THEY LOVE!'" LESSON (fifth confirmation): obliqueness costs
  audience; a great system deserves a legible pitch.
- IT WAS THE MOST DIVISIVE GAME OF ITS ERA: "we got a game five years ago that was ALL ABOUT THE IMPORTANCE
  OF CONNECTION AND COMMON GROUND, ONLY FOR IT TO BE ONE OF THE MOST DIVISIVE GAMES OF ITS ERA." Players
  expecting Skyrim bounced off a game about balance. LESSON: if your core verb is unusual, TEACH IT LOUDLY
  AND EARLY — or the wrong audience arrives and leaves angry (our on-ramp, again).
- "LIKES" ARE STILL A LEADERBOARD: an academic critique notes "most methods of gamification employ POINTS,
  LEVELS, AND LEADERBOARDS as if they alone are sufficient to create motivation, WITHOUT UNDERSTANDING THAT
  REAL LIFE... IS ALREADY CONSTRUCTED ON POINTS, LEVELS, AND LEADERBOARDS." And a player admits: "DID I DO
  THAT BECAUSE I WANT TO RECEIVE A LOT OF LIKES? **YES, I WAS LOOKING FOR PERSONAL GRATIFICATION** but the
  game manages to CHANNEL MY EFFORTS IN A DIRECTION I HAVE NEVER SEEN IN THIS MEDIUM." LESSON: the altruism
  is real BUT it's still driven by a number — design the number so the selfish path IS the generous one.
- THE COMBAT IS DELIBERATELY WEAK AND IT SHOWS: combat "feels a bit out of place." Deliberate, but it means
  the game has a whole subsystem that's mediocre by design. LESSON (ours): our Pacifist Law can't mean our
  DIAL is bad — refusing violence as a THEME still requires the violence to feel good when it happens.

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE ROPE, NOT THE STICK: Kobo Abe's metaphor — early humans used STICKS to push threats away and ROPES
    to pull things close; "most games are about sticks... DEATH STRANDING IS A ROPE" (cf. our PACIFIST LAW).
W2. OTHER PLAYERS BUILD YOUR WORLD, ASYNCHRONOUSLY, WITH NO SERVER SUBSCRIPTION: ladders, bridges, roads,
    generators, and safehouses appear in your solo game (cf. Dragon's Dogma Q84, our fold + city-builder).
W3. THE ONLY REWARD IS A LIKE, AND PEOPLE BUILD ANYWAY: no material payoff — "the player is NOT REQUIRED to
    give their resources... YET THEY DO SO NEVERTHELESS" (cf. our city-builder + district).
W4. THE HIGHWAY IS CROWDFUNDED BY STRANGERS: no single player can afford the bridge, so waves of players
    each pay a piece and walk away, and later waves DRIVE ON IT (cf. our city-builder + fold + logistics).
W5. THE TERRAIN IS THE BOSS: you control BALANCE with the triggers; walking is the skill; "took something we
    never even think about in games and turned it into something that REQUIRES OUR ATTENTION" (cf. our
    120-BPM movement law).
W6. PATHS SMOOTH AS PEOPLE WALK THEM: enough porters on one route and "A VISIBLE FOOTPATH WILL GRADUALLY
    FORM, SMOOTHING THE TERRAIN" for everyone after (cf. our district + street generation).
W7. HELP ARRIVES AS OBJECTS, NOT MESSAGES: Dark Souls says "try jumping"; Death Stranding leaves you AN
    ACTUAL LADDER (cf. our fold's inheritance + [READ]).
W8. THE DEATH SCREEN IS A SOCIAL SPACE: you drift in the Seam among the floating souls of other players and
    can TOUCH them to strengthen a connection before returning (cf. our death-math + AMALGAMATION).
W9. STRANGERS' GHOSTS HELP YOU FIGHT: "white, ghost-like figures... EMERGE FROM THE TAR" to throw you blood
    bags and grenades — they represent real players (cf. our combat dial + companions).
W10. THE PREMISE AGED INTO PROPHECY: a game where the hero is a delivery driver shipped four months before
     2020 made delivery drivers "the most important people in the world" (cf. our logistics + premise).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the rope + the thing a stranger left for you
===============================================================================
Death Stranding is the canon's purest statement of our PACIFIST LAW's philosophy (the rope, not the stick),
the best-executed asynchronous-help system ever shipped, and — with Dragon's Dogma (Q84) — the second proof
that a solo game can feel populated with NO server. For a single-file iPhone game whose whole subject is
CONNECTION TO THE DEAD, this teardown is unusually close to home.
- W1 (the rope, not the stick — our PACIFIST LAW's philosophy, stated): the top thematic port. Kobo Abe's
  metaphor is the best articulation of our stance we've found: early humans used STICKS to ward off threats
  and ROPES to bring things closer, and MOST GAMES ARE STICKS. Our Pacifist Law is currently a RULE ("you
  can finish without killing"). Death Stranding makes it a PHILOSOPHY WITH A VERB: the game's actions are
  ropes — carrying, connecting, building, delivering. Bank it: Bohemia's core verbs should be rope-verbs
  (rebuild, supply, connect, remember, carry) and the stick should be the exception that feels wrong. That
  also directly answers Arcanum's XP-curve flaw (Q86) — if the game's VERBS aren't violence, the
  progression can't reward it (ties our PACIFIST LAW + city-builder + logistics + conscience system + ART
  PHILOSOPHY + combat dial, Arcanum Q86, Undertale Q28).
- W2/W7 (asynchronous help with no server + help arrives as OBJECTS — our FOLD): the standout mechanical
  port, and it pairs exactly with Dragon's Dogma's Pawns (Q84) and PZ's Skill Journal (Q88). Three separate
  teardowns now converge on the same answer for us: OUR FOLD'S INHERITANCE SHOULD BE PHYSICAL THINGS LEFT
  IN THE WORLD. Not a stat carryover — a LADDER. Generation one's dynast bolts a walkway across a gap,
  caches medicine in a wall, leaves a note, wires a generator. Generation three FINDS IT and doesn't know
  who left it (or does, and that's the emotion). And the Dark Souls contrast is the design rule: a MESSAGE
  says "try jumping"; an OBJECT lets you jump. Ours should be objects. Zero server cost, fits the single
  HTML file, and it's our whole theme — the dead helping the living (ties our FOLD + generational
  persistence + persistence-v1 + city-builder + district + prefab factory + [READ] + AMALGAMATION +
  single-file-carry, Dragon's Dogma Q84, Project Zomboid Q88; a top-tier port).
- W3/W4 (the only reward is a LIKE + the crowdfunded highway — our city-builder): STEAL the psychology. No
  material payoff exists for helping, and players fund bridges they'll never use because "EVEN IF YOU
  CONSTRUCT THINGS JUST FOR YOURSELF, THEY WILL HELP OTHERS." In our fold, that's literal and free: what
  the first dynasty builds, the third inherits. Bank the structural version — SOME infrastructure should be
  UNAFFORDABLE TO ONE GENERATION, so it's paid for in pieces across a hundred years by people who never see
  it finished. That is Bohemia's premise as a construction mechanic (ties our city-builder + fold +
  generational persistence + logistics + life-support buildings + survival accounting + THREE CURRENCIES;
  a top-tier port).
- W6 (paths smooth as people walk them — our district + street generation): bank it. Desire paths, simulated:
  a route walked enough becomes a ROAD, easing traversal for everyone after. Across our fold that's gorgeous
  and cheap — the routes generation one used become generation three's streets, and the CITY'S SHAPE IS AN
  ACCUMULATED RECORD OF WHAT PEOPLE ACTUALLY DID. Our street generation + tile pipeline could carry this
  natively (ties our district + street generation + city-builder + fold + scheduler + MAP LAW: Paolo owns
  layout, this is emergent plumbing).
- W5 (the terrain is the boss — our 120-BPM movement law): bank the nerve. Kojima made WALKING the skill:
  balance on the triggers, every step considered, "took something we never even think about in games and
  turned it into something that requires our attention." We already made movement a first-class system (the
  120-BPM request law, the grid tick, hold-to-run). That's the same bet. The warning comes with it: half the
  audience expected Skyrim and bounced (ties our 120-BPM law + movement + district + difficulty packages).
- W8 (the death screen is a SOCIAL SPACE — our AMALGAMATION + death-math): bank it, because it's nearly our
  premise already. When Sam dies he enters THE SEAM and drifts among THE FLOATING SOULS OF OTHER PLAYERS,
  and can TOUCH THEM to strengthen a connection before returning. Our AMALGAMATION is an entity made of the
  uploaded dead. What is our death screen? If a dynast dies, do they pass THROUGH the Amalgamation — meet
  what's in there — and get sent back changed? That's a death-math + Amalgamation + fold fusion sitting
  right there (ties our AMALGAMATION + death-math + fold + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded, SOMA Q34,
  Citizen Sleeper Q65).
- W10 (the premise aged into prophecy — our logistics): bank the encouragement. A game about a delivery
  driver, mocked at reveal as "a glorified Amazon driver," shipped four months before 2020 made delivery
  drivers the most important people alive. Our post-ECONOMIC collapse + logistics + supply chains are the
  same bet on the same unglamorous truth: the people who MOVE THINGS are the ones holding it together
  (ties our logistics + post-economic-collapse premise + survival accounting + faction web (CARAVANS,
  TRADES) + district).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) AN ASYNCHRONOUS SOCIAL SYSTEM IS EMPTY AT THE START — DS's first 5-10 hours are an
  "Amazon Delivery Simulator" because nobody's built anything yet; our fold has the identical problem in
  GENERATION ONE and must seed itself (pre-place a predecessor's leavings, or make gen-one's loneliness the
  POINT); (b) SHARED/INHERITED CONTENT NEEDS CURATION — IGN flagged "incomplete or poorly placed player
  objects" misleading players and donated gear TRIVIALIZING BOSSES; our fold's inheritance needs a quality
  gate and a difficulty-integrity check (our FACTORY LAW + regression gates); (c) obliqueness costs audience
  — fifth confirmation ("any attempt to summarize it will be for naught"; "drank a little too much of his
  own kool-aid"); (d) if your core verb is unusual, TEACH IT LOUDLY AND EARLY or the wrong audience arrives
  and leaves angry (our on-ramp, fifth confirmation); (e) the altruism is still driven by a NUMBER —
  players admit they wanted the likes; design so the selfish path IS the generous one; and (f) OUR PACIFIST
  LAW CANNOT MEAN OUR DIAL IS BAD — DS's combat "feels a bit out of place" by design, and it's the game's
  weakest limb; refusing violence thematically still requires violence to feel GOOD when it happens.
