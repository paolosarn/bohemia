# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #170 — "SINS OF THE FATHER (STOP YOUR SON FROM BECOMING YOU)"
**Game:** Mass Effect 2 (2010)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** Thane's loyalty mission — a dying assassin learns his estranged son is about to make his first kill, following the father into the killing life, and must reach him in time to stop the boy from becoming what the father is, before the father dies.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Mass Effect catalogue; corpus anchor Q06 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia's whole engine is the fold — what one generation passes to the next — and this is the genre's cleanest quest about a father racing to break the cycle before it repeats: a killer trying to stop his son from inheriting the killing, dying while he does it. It is the generational inheritance of #42/#67, the "break the cycle or feed it" of #155, and the intervention-that-can-fail of #158, focused on a single father and son. The Amalgamation promises no one is ever lost; this quest asks whether what a parent WAS has to be what the child BECOMES.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A dying assassin, absent his whole son's life, learns the boy is about to make his first kill — following the father into the killing life out of grief and rage — and races to reach him before it happens, to stop his son from becoming what he is, knowing he has no right to ask and no time to earn one.**

Thane Krios is a professional killer, dying of a terminal illness, who abandoned his son Kolyat after the boy's mother was murdered — leaving the child to grief while Thane pursued revenge. Now, years later, Thane learns Kolyat has taken a contract: the boy is about to become an assassin, following his absent father into the only inheritance Thane ever offered. The loyalty mission is a race: Thane and the player must find Kolyat before he makes the kill, through the underworld the father knows too well, to reach a son who hates him and is about to become him. And Thane is dying — he has weeks, maybe less — so this is not a reconciliation with time to heal; it is a father trying, in the little life he has left, to stop the one thing he cannot bear: his son inheriting his sins.

The machine is a parent racing the cycle. Kolyat is becoming an assassin not from calling but from grief and abandonment — the father's absence is the wound, and the killing is the boy reaching for the only thing his father ever was. Thane knows he caused this; he knows he has no standing to ask Kolyat to stop; and he knows he is out of time to earn the standing. The climax is a confrontation where Thane, or the player on his behalf, must reach Kolyat in the moment before the kill — and the intervention can succeed (the boy stopped, the cycle broken, a fragile chance at a father-son ending in the weeks Thane has left) or the reaching can fail in tone, and the son completes the kill, becoming what the father was, the inheritance sealed. The player is often the one who talks Kolyat down, because Thane cannot — the father is the wound, and the wound cannot be the cure.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the worst thing you can pass to your child is the person you were, and if you want to break that inheritance you may have to do it through someone else's mouth, in less time than it takes to deserve, before you run out of both.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THANE KRIOS — the dying father racing the cycle
- **What he wants:** to stop his son from becoming an assassin — to break the inheritance before he dies.
- **What he'll trade:** the little life he has left, and his pride; he knows he has no standing and asks anyway.
- **What he will never say out loud (easily):** the guilt of the abandonment; that the boy's killing is his own absence, coming due.
- **Function:** THE PARENT AT THE CYCLE'S EDGE — trying to break the inheritance he created, out of time to earn the right.

### KOLYAT — the son becoming his father
- **What he wants (stated):** to make the kill, to be someone, to matter.
- **What he wants (real):** his father, and failing that, to become the only thing his father ever was — the killing as a grief reaching for connection.
- **What he'll trade:** the contract, if reached; the inheritance, if not.
- **Function:** THE CHILD AT THE CYCLE'S EDGE — inheriting the sins out of abandonment, one confrontation from becoming them.

### THE PLAYER (Shepard) — the mouth the father can't be
- **What they want:** to help Thane reach Kolyat — and often to be the one who talks the boy down, because the father is the wound.
- **Function:** THE INTERVENTION: the third party who can say what the father can't, the cycle broken through someone else's voice.

### THE CONTRACT / THE KILL — the inheritance about to seal
- The murder Kolyat is about to commit, his first, following his father.
- **Function:** THE CYCLE made a moment: the single act that turns the son into the father, racing the clock.

### THE DYING — the time that is running out
- Thane's terminal illness, the weeks he has left.
- **Function:** THE PRESSURE: the father cannot wait to earn standing; the intervention must happen before he runs out of life.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE T-1 — Thane's confession. Entry: stage 1.
The dying father naming what he did and what it's becoming.
> "You abandoned him." [gate: none] -> he doesn't defend it; the boy's killing is his own absence, coming due
> "Why can't you stop him yourself?" [gate: none] -> because he is the wound; his face is the grief; THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK, paraphrased: I am the reason he is doing this. I cannot be the reason he stops
> "You're dying. There's no time." [gate: none] -> he knows; the intervention must happen before he runs out of both life and standing

### NODE T-2 — Kolyat's grief. Entry: stage 3-4. The son reaching for the father through the killing.
The boy, rage over a wound, about to inherit.
> "You don't have to become him." [gate: none] -> the counter to the inheritance; the boy can be something other than his father's sins
> "He left you. This won't bring him back." [gate: none] -> the killing as grief reaching for connection; naming the real want under the contract
> (let the father speak — and watch it fail) [gate: none] TRAP -> Thane's voice is the wound; the son hears the abandonment, not the plea; the father cannot be the cure
NOVERB "Just have Thane order him to stop" — the father's authority is the poison; commanding the boy seals the inheritance. The removed verb is the quest's truth: the cycle cannot be broken by the one who made it, only through someone else's voice.

### NODE T-3 — the reaching. Entry: stage 4-5. The cycle at its edge.
The player, the mouth the father can't be, in the moment before the kill.
> (talk Kolyat down — reach the grief, not the rage) [gate: read the boy] -> the kill stopped; the cycle broken; a fragile father-son chance in Thane's last weeks
> (fail the tone — meet rage with rage, or let Thane lead) [gate: none] -> the kill completed; the son becomes the father; the inheritance sealed
> WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the reaching must find the grief under the rage; the intervention can fail, and the failure is a boy turned into his father forever.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes — Kolyat stopped (cycle broken, fragile reconciliation) or Kolyat completes the kill (inheritance sealed) — gated on the intervention's tone, with the father unable to be the one who reaches him.**

- **CYCLE BROKEN:** the player reaches Kolyat's grief before the kill; the boy stops; a fragile chance at a father-son reckoning in Thane's last weeks. WRITES: the inheritance refused; the son spared his father's sins; a dying man given a little peace.
- **INHERITANCE SEALED:** the reaching fails in tone (rage met with rage, or the father allowed to lead); Kolyat completes the kill; the son becomes the father. WRITES: the cycle repeated; the boy an assassin; the father's absence made permanent in the child.
- **THE FATHER'S CONSTRAINT:** in both branches, Thane cannot be the one who reaches Kolyat — the parent who made the wound cannot be its cure; the intervention runs through the player's mouth.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the branch is whether the cycle breaks — two answers to "does what the father WAS have to be what the son BECOMES," and the game makes breaking it require a third voice and the right tone under a dying clock.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE INTERVENTION'S SUCCESS LEANS ON A PERSUASION CHECK.** Talking Kolyat down is gated partly behind Paragon/Renegade points — so a player who understands the boy's grief perfectly but lacks the check can fail to reach him, and the cycle-breaking that is the quest's heart is bought with a stat rather than with comprehension.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the cycle-breaking intervention must be gated on UNDERSTANDING the child's wound (reaching the grief, not the rage), not on a raw persuasion number — so a dynast who does the emotional work can always break the inheritance. The check tests knowing the son, not build (the #155/#158 earned-outcome law).

**F2 — THANE'S ABSENCE IS TOLD, NOT LIVED.** The abandonment that drives the whole quest — Thane leaving Kolyat to grief while he pursued revenge — is delivered as backstory, so the player experiences the wound as exposition rather than as something felt; the father's guilt and the son's abandonment land lighter than the concept demands.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the abandonment that creates a cycle must be SHOWN across the fold — the parent's leaving, the child's grief, the years of absence witnessed — so breaking the inheritance carries the weight of a history the player lived. The fold shows the abandonment that told exposition cannot (the #155/#159 show-the-wound law).

**F3 — THE FRAGILE CHANCE ISN'T FOLLOWED THROUGH.** If Kolyat is saved, the "father-son reckoning in Thane's last weeks" is promised but barely delivered — a brief scene, then the thread mostly drops, so the cycle-breaking's payoff is thinner than its stakes. The intervention's success doesn't get the aftermath its weight earned.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** breaking a generational cycle must have an AFTERMATH proportional to its stakes — the reconciled relationship shown growing, the spared child's changed path witnessed, across the fold's time — so the intervention's success is felt, not just noted. A cycle broken deserves more than a closing line (the #47 personal-scale-payoff law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. A PARENT RACES THE CYCLE. The quest's engine is a father trying to stop his son from becoming him — the fold's exact question (what one generation passes to the next) made a race against a single kill. The inheritance at its edge.
W2. THE KILLING IS GRIEF, NOT CALLING. Kolyat becomes an assassin not from talent but from abandonment — the boy reaching for the only thing his father ever was. The cycle is a wound, not a choice.
W3. THE FATHER CANNOT BE THE CURE. Thane is the wound; his face is the grief; he cannot be the one who reaches his son. The cycle can only be broken through someone else's voice — a devastating structural truth.
W4. THE CLOCK IS TERMINAL. Thane is dying; there is no time to earn standing, only to act. The intervention must happen before the father runs out of both life and right.
W5. THE PLAYER IS THE THIRD VOICE. Shepard talks Kolyat down because Thane can't — the intervention runs through a mouth that isn't the wound, which is why it can work.
W6. THE REACHING CAN FAIL. Meet the boy's rage with rage, or let the father lead, and the kill completes — the intervention is genuinely failable, and the failure is a son turned into his father forever.
W7. THE GRIEF UNDER THE RAGE. Reaching Kolyat means finding the grief the rage is defending — the intervention succeeds by addressing the wound, not the anger, which is the truth of every real reconciliation.
W8. THE FATHER OWNS THE CAUSE. Thane does not defend the abandonment; he names it as the source. The parent's honesty about having made the wound is what makes the race for redemption bearable.
W9. THE STAKES ARE ONE FAMILY. Not the galaxy — a father and a son and a single kill. The intimate scale (the #47 lesson) is what makes the cycle's weight land: this is about these two, and it is enough.
W10. THE INHERITANCE IS THE THEME. The quest asks whether what a parent WAS must be what the child BECOMES — the fold's central question, answered by whether the player can break it through the right voice at the right moment. The most Bohemian arc in the game.
