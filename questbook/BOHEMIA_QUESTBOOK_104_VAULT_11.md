# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 104: THE TEST WAS TO SAY NO / EVERYBODY DIED BEING GOOD CITIZENS (Vault 11, Fallout: New Vegas)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest with no quest-giver, no living NPC, and no boss — an empty
hole in the ground where a community held a democratic election every year to choose who would be murdered,
for decades, because a computer told them to, and the correct answer was always **NO**. Complete event flow,
the ruse, the blocs, the blackmail, Katherine Stone, Order 745, the coup, the last five, the congratulations
tape, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our [READ], our faction web, our
AMALGAMATION, our city-builder, + our post-economic-collapse premise. Obsidian (concept: **JOSHUA SAWYER**;
design: **ERIC FENSTERMAKER**), built on Shirley Jackson's "The Lottery." Reference only; Paolo does not read
it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Location: Vault 11, Mojave Wasteland, Fallout: New Vegas (2010). No quest marker sends you. Nobody asks you
to go. You walk in a hole and read a civilization's suicide note, in the order the level designer chose,
and you leave.

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE TEST WAS WHETHER THEY'D REFUSE, AND THEY NEVER DID (the premise — STARTLINGLY our conscience system):
  "**AFTER THE BLAST DOORS SEALED, THE INHABITANTS WERE INFORMED BY THE VAULT COMPUTER THEY WOULD HAVE TO
  SACRIFICE ONE OF THEIR FELLOW VAULT DWELLERS EVERY YEAR OR ELSE EVERYONE IN THE VAULT WOULD BE KILLED.**"
  - "**THIS WAS A RUSE, AS IN TRUTH, IF THE RESIDENTS WERE TO REFUSE, A MESSAGE FROM VAULT-TEC WOULD PLAY
    COMMENDING THE DWELLERS FOR THEIR CHOICE.**"
  - **The door was never locked.** One "no," any year, and it opens. They said yes for decades.
- AND THEY DIDN'T JUST COMPLY, THEY BUILT A GOVERNMENT AROUND IT (the horror — direct for our faction web):
  "**THE RESIDENTS FORMED THE COALITION OF VAULT 11 VOTING BLOCS, A GROUP MADE UP OF THE VAULT'S SIX VOTING
  BLOCS. THIS COALITION CREATED THE VOTING GUIDELINES AND SERVED AS A SUPPOSEDLY IMPARTIAL SET OF
  REPRESENTATIVES THAT WOULD HELP THE REST OF THE INHABITANTS MAKE EDUCATED VOTES.**"
  - Six political parties. Campaign posters. An **OMBUDSMAN.** An **ELECTION GUIDE.** A prepared speech. They
    took a murder and gave it **procedure**, and the procedure is what made it survivable for everyone who
    wasn't picked (STARTLINGLY our faction web + city-builder + conscience system; the atrocity with a
    process).
  - The satire lands because it's real: "**THE ELECTION POSTER READING 'I HATE NATE/KATE' IS A REFERENCE TO
    U.S. PRESIDENT DWIGHT D. EISENHOWER'S 'I LIKE IKE' CAMPAIGN.**"
- THE ENTIRE QUEST IS READING (the form — and it's our [READ], perfected): there is no quest-giver. No
  companion. Nobody alive. The story exists **only** in terminals, holotapes, posters, and the arrangement
  of skeletons. "**ACCORDING TO TERMINAL ENTRIES FOUND THROUGHOUT THE EMPTY VAULT IN 2281...**"
  - The pedigree: Sawyer — "**MY IDEA WAS VAULT 11, BUT WITH LIKE, SHIRLEY JACKSON'S 'THE LOTTERY' AT THE
    HEART OF IT. AND THAT WAS IT. AND EVERYTHING ELSE THAT WAS DESIGNED FOR IT, OR NOT PUT IN, WAS ERIC

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (the order you learn it in)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE FIRST THING YOU FIND IS THE END
- "**THE FIRST AUDIO LOG DISCOVERED BY THE PLAYER AS THEY ENTER THE VAULT RECORDS THE FATE OF THE FINAL
  SURVIVORS.**"
- Five people. The last five. They refused — "**IN AN ACT OF SUICIDE THEY DEFIED THE VAULT COMPUTER AND
  REFUSED TO SEND ANY MORE SACRIFICES, ONLY TO DISCOVER THAT THEIR DEFIANCE WAS EXACTLY WHAT THE VAULT
  COMPUTER WANTED.**"
- You hear the ENDING first, and it means nothing yet. Then you spend an hour learning what it costs (cf.
  our [READ] + quest factory; the level that starts with its own last page).
### STAGE 1 — THE ORIGINAL SIN (they killed the only person who knew)
- "**OF THE ORIGINAL RESIDENTS, ONLY THE OVERSEER HAD ENTERED THE VAULT KNOWING ABOUT THE YEARLY
  SACRIFICE.**"
- "**THE RESIDENTS OF THE VAULT, IN THEIR SHOCK AND ANGER AT DISCOVERING THIS AFTER HAVING ALREADY BEEN
  SEALED INSIDE THE VAULT, SELECTED THE ORIGINAL OVERSEER (WHOM THEY VIEWED AS HAVING BETRAYED THEM) AS THE
  FIRST SACRIFICE.**"
- "**THIS DECISION WOULD MARRY THE POSITIONS OF OVERSEER AND SACRIFICE UNTIL THE END.**"
- Their first act was justice: kill the liar. And that single furious, understandable, RIGHTEOUS decision
  built the machine — from then on, **being elected leader meant being elected to die** (STARTLINGLY our
  faction web + AMALGAMATION + fold; the tradition founded by one act of fair anger).
### STAGE 2 — THE MECHANISM (the ballot is the murder weapon)
- "**AT THE END OF THE OVERSEER'S TERM OF OFFICE, THE OVERSEER WAS REQUIRED TO ENTER A CHAMBER BELOW THE
  OVERSEER'S OFFICE TO BE EXECUTED BY THE VAULT'S COMPUTERS.**"
- "**THE COMPUTER DID NOT REQUIRE THAT THE SACRIFICE SHOULD BE THE OVERSEER**" — they chose that part
  themselves. The computer just wanted a body. **The democracy was their idea.**
- So an election in Vault 11 is a campaign to make somebody ELSE the leader. The posters read "**I HATE
  NATE.**" You campaign AGAINST people. Winning is death (cf. our faction web + standing + city-builder;
  the vote as an execution order).
### STAGE 3 — THE JUSTICE BLOC (power was the point)
- "**ROY GOTTLIEB — PRESIDENT OF THE JUSTICE BLOC AND CHAIRMAN OF THE COALITION — WENT TO KATHERINE STONE AND
  TOLD HER THAT THE JUSTICE BLOC WAS THINKING OF NOMINATING HER HUSBAND, NATHAN.**"
- The motive is petty beyond belief: "**KATHERINE BELIEVED IT WAS BECAUSE NATHAN HAD HAD A LUCKY STREAK IN
  THE POKER GAMES HE'D PLAY WITH SOME OF THE MEMBERS OF THE JUSTICE BLOC.**"
- **They were going to kill a man for winning at cards.**
- And the blackmail: "**ROY KNEW THIS TOO, SO HE OFFERED TO HELP 'SWAY THE BLOC' AWAY FROM ENDORSING NATHAN,
  PROVIDED THAT KATHERINE PERFORMED SEXUAL FAVORS FOR HIM AND HIS FRIENDS IN THE JUSTICE BLOC.**"
- The vault's most powerful faction ran an extortion racket on the machinery of death. The sacrifice wasn't
  a tragedy anymore. It was **LEVERAGE** (STARTLINGLY our faction web + standing + conscience system; the
  atrocity as a business).
- And it's recorded: "**JUSTICE BLOC HQ SECURITY TAPES - GIVEN AFTER READING THE PERSONAL TERMINAL IN THE
  LIVING QUARTERS.**" They TAPED it. They kept it in the office.
### STAGE 4 — KATHERINE (the woman who broke it by winning)
- She paid. They took the favors and endorsed Nathan anyway. So she killed Roy's people — and then:
  "**KATHERINE ALSO KNEW THAT SHE WOULD EVENTUALLY BE FOUND OUT AS THE MURDERER AND HOPED TO BE ELECTED AS
  OVERSEER.**"
- **She campaigned to die.** Being elected Overseer was how she'd escape the consequences of murder, because
  the punishment for leadership was death anyway. That's the most Vault 11 sentence possible.
- "**UNSURPRISINGLY, SHE WON THE ELECTIONS. HER FIRST ACT AS THE NEW LEADER OF VAULT 11 WAS TO CREATE
  OVERSEER ORDER 745. THIS ACT DISSOLVED THE COALITION'S ORIGINAL VOTING RULES AND, INSTEAD, MADE IT SO THE
  NEXT OVERSEER... WOULD BE CHOSEN RANDOMLY USING THE VAULT'S COMPUTER SYSTEMS.**"
- **"THIS, KATHERINE BELIEVED, ENSURED 'COMPLETE IMPARTIALITY AND FAIRNESS' IN THE SELECTION PROCESS.**"
- She didn't stop the killing. She made it FAIR. Even the woman who broke the system couldn't imagine not
  having one (cf. our conscience system + faction web + AMALGAMATION).
### STAGE 5 — THE COUP (fairness was intolerable)
- "**THE JUSTICE BLOC, AGHAST THAT THEY WERE GOING TO LOSE THE ABILITY TO CONTROL VAULT LIFE BY MEANS OF
  ELECTION THREATS, NOT ONLY FEARED THAT ONE OF THEIR OWN MEMBERS WOULD BE SELECTED, BUT ALSO WORRIED THAT
  THE DAMAGE TO THE VAULT'S SOCIAL STRUCTURE WOULD BE SO SEVERE THAT THEY WOULD HAVE PERMANENTLY LOST
  POWER.**"
- "**THEY DECIDED TO STAGE AN ARMED COUP TO FORCE KATHERINE TO REVERSE OVERSEER ORDER 745.**"
- "**THEY STARTED COLLECTING FIREARMS... WHICH THEY USED TO GAIN CONTROL OF THE VAULT'S FOOD, WATER, AND
  POWER SUPPLY. THIS RESULTED IN VIOLENCE ALL ACROSS THE VAULT, WITH ITS INHABITANTS FIGHTING OVER
  RESOURCES.**"

### STAGE 6 — THE OMBUDSMAN'S SPEECH (the one man who asked)
- On a podium in the atrium: "**PREPARED SPEECH OF GUS OLSON, OMBUDSMAN, FOR THE ANNUAL OVERSEER ELECTION.**"
- "**HE EXPRESSES HIS CONFUSION AT WHAT VAULT 11 REQUIRED OF THEM. HE ALSO WONDERS WHETHER THERE'S A REASON

===============================================================================
- THE SKELETON COUNT DOESN'T MATCH THE STORY: the wiki says it plainly — "**VAULT 11'S SACRIFICE CHAMBER ONLY
  HAS A TOTAL OF 16 SKELETONS, BUT GUS OLSON'S PREPARED SPEECH VERY EXPLICITLY SAYS THAT THE SACRIFICES HAVE
  GONE ON FOR DECADES.**" LESSON (ours, sharp): if your environmental story's THESIS is a body count, COUNT
  THE BODIES. Our HD tile repo + prefab factory must make the props match the text.
- IT ENDS IN A ROBOT FIGHT: the most literate level in the game climaxes with **turrets and robots** popping
  out of the walls, because it's an FPS and something had to happen. LESSON (~4th confirmation): when a
  narrative space needs a combat encounter to have an ending, ask whether it needed an ending at all (our
  PACIFIST LAW, Ocean House Q99 — which solved this by having nothing).
- IT'S ENTIRELY OPTIONAL AND UNMARKED: no quest sends you. The best-written content in a 100-hour game is a
  hole in a rock you may never enter. LESSON (~13th confirmation, and the most expensive): THEME-IN-OPTIONAL
  IS MISSED. Vault 11 is New Vegas's thesis about democracy and it is not in New Vegas's main quest.
- THE PLAYER CAN'T DO ANYTHING: you can't fix it, avenge it, or tell anyone. You read it and leave. LESSON
  (double-edged): powerlessness is the POINT and it also means the interactive medium is doing nothing an
  audio drama couldn't. Our [READ] should let the record CHANGE something at least once.

  SACRIFICIAL CHAMBER AND CHOOSING THE LAST OPTION REPEATEDLY.**" You can grind levels in the murder room.
  LESSON: regression gates.
- YOU CAN BREAK AN UNRELATED MAIN QUEST: "**ONCE COMPLETING THE CHAMBER, IT IS POSSIBLE TO FAIL THE HOUSE
  ALWAYS WINS QUESTS.**" LESSON: side content that silently fails main content is a shipping failure.
