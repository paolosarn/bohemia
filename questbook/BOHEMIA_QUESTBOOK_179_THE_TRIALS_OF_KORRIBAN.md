# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #179 — "THE TRIALS OF KORRIBAN (PROVE YOU BELONG TO THE THING THAT MADE YOU EVIL)"
**Game:** Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic (2003)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** The Korriban / Sith Academy arc — to earn the last Star Map, the player must enroll in the Sith academy on the graveyard-world of the Sith, and pass trials that reward cruelty, betrayal, and murder, forcing you to decide how much evil you'll perform to be let deeper into the place, and whether performing it "for the mission" is any different from meaning it.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the KOTOR catalogue; corpus anchor Q08 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia needs the quest where the player must ACT evil to pass through an evil institution, and the game refuses to distinguish undercover cruelty from real cruelty — the murders you commit "to keep cover" leave real bodies, and the academy rewards you exactly as if you meant them. Korriban is the specimen: an institution that grants access by cruelty, a mission that requires the access, and a series of small evils each of which is fully real to its victim regardless of the player's private intentions. It is the "acting the part becomes the part" machine (#94/#172) at institutional scale, and the conduct-book (#143) question our factions can carry: does the ledger record what you did or what you meant.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**To reach the last piece of the map you need, you must enroll in the academy of the Sith on their tomb-world and rise through it — and the academy grants advancement only for acts of cruelty, betrayal, and murder, so the mission requires you to perform real evil on real people while telling yourself it's only cover, and the place rewards you identically whether you mean it or not.**

The final Star Map is on Korriban, the ancient homeworld and graveyard of the Sith, and the only way to reach it is through the Sith Academy — a school that trains dark-side adepts. To gain entry and rise, the player must earn prestige from the academy's masters, and prestige is earned by cruelty: siding with bullies against victims, betraying fellow students, letting people die, murdering to prove ruthlessness, defiling the tombs of the ancient dead. Each individual act has a real victim — a student who trusted you and is now dead, a servant tortured, a rival poisoned. The player may be doing all of it "for the mission," intending to reach the Star Map and leave, but the academy does not grade intention. It grades acts. And the bodies are real regardless of the private story the player tells about why.

The machine is that an institution built to produce evil advances you by exactly the amount of evil you perform, and it cannot tell — and neither, past a certain point, can the victims — the difference between undercover cruelty and the real thing. A murdered student is dead whether you killed them to keep cover or because you wanted to; the servant you tortured for prestige feels it identically either way. The player wants to believe there's a firewall between the acts and the self — that doing evil for a good reason keeps you clean — and Korriban is engineered to erode that belief. The quest lets the player pass through with maximum ruthlessness (fastest, most rewarded, most bodies) or find the narrow, harder paths that get access with less blood (slower, less prestige, some victims spared), and it never lets the player forget that every "for cover" murder is a real murder in the academy's ledger and the victim's grave.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** an institution that rewards evil doesn't care why you do it, and neither does the person you did it to — telling yourself the cruelty was "for the mission" changes your private story and nothing else, because the ledger and the grave both record the act, not the excuse.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THE PLAYER — the one performing evil "for the mission"
- **What they want (stated):** the last Star Map; passage through the academy to the tomb.
- **What they want (real):** to believe the firewall holds — that cruelty for a good reason leaves the self clean.
- **What they'll trade:** the bodies of students and servants, if the mission "requires" it.
- **What they will never say out loud:** that past a certain body count the cover and the self are the same.
- **Function:** THE UNDERCOVER whose real acts the institution rewards identically to real intent.

### THE SITH ACADEMY / ITS MASTERS — the institution that grades acts, not reasons
- **What they want:** to produce ruthless adepts; to advance whoever performs the most cruelty.
- **Function:** THE LEDGER: the machine that grants access by evil and cannot tell cover from conviction.

### THE VICTIMS — the students, servants, and rivals with real graves
- Trusting students, tortured servants, poisoned rivals — each fully harmed regardless of the player's intent.
- **Function:** THE BODIES: the proof that "for the mission" cruelty is real cruelty to the one it lands on.

### THE STAR MAP / THE TOMB — the thing the evil buys access to
- The final map-piece deep in the academy, reachable only by rising through it.
- **Function:** THE PRICE-JUSTIFIER: the mission-goal that "requires" the acts, and tests whether the requirement excuses them.

### THE NARROW PATHS — the harder, less-bloody routes through
- The slower, less-rewarded ways to gain access with fewer victims.
- **Function:** THE ALTERNATIVE: the proof that maximum cruelty was a choice, not a necessity.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal postures through the academy — maximum cruelty (fastest, most prestige, most graves) or the narrow paths (slower, fewer victims, the same map won) — with the constant that the institution and the graves record the acts, not the "for the mission" intent behind them.**

- **MAXIMUM CRUELTY:** the fastest rise; the most prestige; the most bodies; the cover indistinguishable from conviction by the end. WRITES: the map won cheap in effort, expensive in graves; the self merged with the part it played.
- **THE NARROW PATHS:** the slower road; access gained with fewer victims; the firewall partly held. WRITES: the same map won at more cost to the player and less to the world; the proof the cruelty was always a choice.
- **THE LEDGER-DOESN'T-GRADE-INTENT CONSTANT:** in both branches, every "for the mission" act has a real victim the academy rewards and the grave records identically — the excuse never reaches the ledger.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the branch is cover-versus-conviction — two answers to "does doing evil for a good reason keep you clean," and the academy is built to prove the firewall leaks.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE ALIGNMENT SYSTEM SCORES THE ACTS, WHICH LETS THE PLAYER GAME THE FIREWALL.** KOTOR's light/dark meter turns the cruelty into points, so a player can perform academy evil and then "buy back" light-side standing elsewhere — which accidentally proves the opposite of the quest's truth, letting the ledger be balanced with unrelated good deeds instead of holding the acts as permanent.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's point is that cruelty can't be laundered by intent, the system must not let it be laundered by an ALIGNMENT METER either — the specific acts staying on the specific ledger, un-offsettable by unrelated good — so the firewall-leaks truth isn't undone by a score you can rebalance (#143's the-conduct-book-is-per-act law).

**F2 — "FOR THE MISSION" ISN'T CHALLENGED BY THE GAME, ONLY BY THE FICTION.** The quest presents the cover-vs-conviction tension but rarely has an NPC or consequence actually confront the player with "that student is dead and your reason doesn't matter to them" — so the profound idea sits in the setup and is left for the player to feel or ignore, never pressed.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's spine is "your good reason didn't reach the victim," something in the world must PRESS it — a survivor who names the dead, a consequence that treats the act as real — so the player can't coast past the tension on the private excuse. An unchallenged rationalization is a firewall the quest forgot to test (#178's the-cost-must-have-a-face law).

**F3 — THE NARROW PATHS ARE OBSCURE, SO MOST PLAYERS NEVER LEARN THE CRUELTY WAS OPTIONAL.** The less-bloody routes through the academy exist but are easy to miss, so many players experience Korriban as "the game made me do evil" rather than "I chose the bloody road" — which lets the player off the hook the quest means to keep them on.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a cruel institution has a less-bloody path, it must be DISCOVERABLE enough that the player knows the cruelty was a choice — the alternative visible, even if hard — so "the game made me" isn't available as an excuse. A hidden mercy is a choice the player never knew they declined (#175's the-alternative-must-be-seen law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE INSTITUTION GRADES ACTS, NOT REASONS. The academy advances you by exactly the cruelty you perform — the machine that cannot tell cover from conviction, which is the whole engine.
W2. EVERY VICTIM IS REAL. The betrayed student, the tortured servant, the poisoned rival — each fully harmed regardless of the player's intent, which is the proof that "for the mission" cruelty is real cruelty.
W3. THE FIREWALL IS THE PLAYER'S ILLUSION. The belief that evil for a good reason leaves the self clean — Korriban is engineered to erode it, which is the quest's real subject.
W4. THE ACADEMY REWARDS COVER AND CONVICTION IDENTICALLY. The place gives the same prestige whether you meant it — the institutional version of "acting the part becomes the part" (#172).
W5. THE MISSION-GOAL JUSTIFIES THE ACTS. The Star Map "requires" the cruelty — the price-justifier that tests whether a good end excuses evil means.
W6. THE NARROW PATHS PROVE IT WAS A CHOICE. Less-bloody routes exist, which means the maximum cruelty was never forced — the player chose the graves.
W7. THE EXCUSE NEVER REACHES THE LEDGER. The removed "make the murders not count" verb is the spine: the ledger and the grave record the act, not the intent.
W8. THE VICTIMS DON'T CARE WHY. The dead student is dead whether you killed them for cover or conviction — the cruelty is identical to the one it lands on.
W9. IT IS A TOMB-WORLD. Korriban is the graveyard of the Sith — the setting itself is a ledger of the dead, the perfect stage for a quest about what the grave records.
W10. IT IS THE CONDUCT BOOK IN AN INSTITUTION. Does the ledger record what you did or what you meant — Bohemia's whole question of act-versus-intention, staged in a school built to make the answer "what you did."
