# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 08: THE IMMERSIVE-SIM QUEST (Kingdom Come: Deliverance)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the immersive-sim quest PHILOSOPHY + two flagship quests torn
down stage-by-stage — "Nest of Vipers" (systemic infiltration/sabotage) and "Poverty, Chastity and
Obedience" + "A Needle in a Haystack" (the LIVE-THE-DISGUISE monastery arc). This is the medium's
model for SYSTEMIC solution-space, disguise-as-daily-discipline, and reactivity across quests. NOTE:
KCD is set in 1403 BOHEMIA (the historical kingdom) — a real-world touchstone for our own Bohemia.
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE KCD PHILOSOPHY (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
Warhorse's design creed: MAKE FRICTION, DON'T REMOVE IT. Realism is the aesthetic — no fast-teleport
convenience, no magic, disguises that must be MAINTAINED, skills you physically learn (Henry can't even
READ at first). Quests give you a GOAL and a systemic world, then get out of the way; solutions EMERGE
from the simulation rather than from authored branches. The result: unmarked, improvised solutions the
designers didn't script, and delayed consequences that surface "hours later." The trade-off (honestly
noted by players): the same systemic ambition produces janky stealth/detection (guards "see through
walls") — depth and friction come with rough edges.

## 1. FLAGSHIP A — "NEST OF VIPERS" (systemic infiltration + sabotage sandbox)
===============================================================================
PREMISE: Sir Radzig orders Henry to SCOUT and ideally SABOTAGE a bandit/Cuman camp at Pribyslavitz
(two adjoining camps). Objectives: map the camp, burn the arrow caches, poison the cooking pots.

### THE APPROACHES (a systemic multi-solution space, not a branch menu)
A) DISGUISE: get a full Cuman outfit (loot a patrol, or an easy-lock chest near the north entrance) —
   wear ONLY the disguise (including the HELMET; any of your own clothing breaks it) and walk the camp
   unmolested. CAVEAT: Cumans still investigate if you get close -> a PERSUASION check (3 passes
   available, one per stat, each usable once) before the cover blows; and ANY sabotage caught still
   turns the camp hostile.
B) CARRY-OVER REACTIVITY: if you INFILTRATED the bandits in the PRIOR quest (via Morcock), you're
   unsuspicious in regular armor — a previous quest's choice changes THIS quest's difficulty. (Deep
   cross-quest reactivity: the world remembers the earlier infiltration.)
C) STEALTH: sneak at night (enemies sleep, visibility drops); patiently wait for opportunities. NOTE:
   the camps are so densely populated that classic sleep-assassination barely works — the DENSITY
   itself is the obstacle, forcing patience over rote stealth.
D) BRUTE FORCE: ride in and kill everyone (a high-courage warhorse lets you clear the camp mounted;
   most enemies wear only chainmail). Wiping the camp doesn't change the later battle's enemy count
   but removes anyone who could stop your sabotage — force AS a route to the objective.

### THE SABOTAGE (the objective, method-agnostic)
- Burn TWO arrow caches (torch); poison TWO cooking pots (Bane/Dollmaker/Lullaby/Witch potion or
  poison — food items can't be used). Guards investigate a burning cache -> manage timing/sightlines.
- Report back to Radzig; WHAT you accomplished + what you learned shapes the intel you give (and the
  siege that follows). The MORAL FRAME: the game lets you decide if the bandits "deserve mercy or
  annihilation" — the approach IS a moral stance.

### THE HONEST FLAW (banked)
- Players widely cite the sabotage optionals as the game's most frustrating — detection feels
  inconsistent (poison a pot crouched in pitch dark and still aggro; guards "magically know where you
  are"). LESSON: systemic stealth needs LEGIBLE, consistent detection rules or the emergent freedom
  curdles into unfair-feeling failure. (Our craft anti-pattern list: consistency > ambition.)

## 2. FLAGSHIP B — THE MONASTERY ARC (live-the-disguise: "Poverty, Chastity and Obedience"
    + "A Needle in a Haystack" + "In the Cloister")
===============================================================================
PREMISE: Henry must reach PIOUS, a man hiding INSIDE Sasau Monastery, to kill or (per Radzig) secretly
capture him. The monastery is sealed to outsiders. Two ways in (Kozliek names both):
  (1) a SECRET CAVE PASSAGE (tied to the "Saintly Remains" side quest), or
  (2) ENTER LEGALLY AS A NOVICE MONK — impersonate a young noble (Karl) expected to join.

### STAGE 1 — SECURE THE IDENTITY (a mini social-heist)
- Find Karl at the Sasau inn, always shadowed by his guardian MANFRED. To speak to Karl privately,
  REMOVE Manfred: the elegant way is buy him drinks + a game of DICE until he's drunk enough to stagger
  off to sleep (a diegetic social solution). Then Karl, unhappy about monastic life, agrees to let
  Henry take his place — but Henry needs the ACCEPTANCE WRIT (held by Manfred) and Manfred's PURSE (for
  Karl's escape). Options: pickpocket Manfred (risky), CHOKE him unconscious (safer), or SKIP Karl
  entirely and steal the writ from a locked chest in the inn's upper room at night. Multiple keys again.
- MICRO-CHOICE: give Karl the full purse, half (he's upset), or none (he starts a FISTFIGHT) — a small
  social consequence.

### STAGE 2 — THE PREREQUISITE YOU MUST EARN: LITERACY
- The Brother Porter asks if Henry can READ (monks copy books). If Henry never learned literacy (a
  trainable skill), he literally CANNOT enter — a QUEST GATED BEHIND A LIFE SKILL you had to develop in
  the open world. The disguise demands a real competence, not a costume.

### STAGE 3 — SURRENDER THE WORLD (the disguise as vow)
- To enter, Henry must give up ALL worldly goods — store every possession in a gatehouse trunk (he
  temporarily LOSES access to his entire inventory) and don the monk's habit. STAGING: the "poverty,
  chastity, obedience" of the title becomes MECHANICAL — you're stripped of your gear, your money, your
  power, reduced to a novice with nothing. The disguise costs you your whole kit.

### STAGE 4 — LIVE THE ROUTINE (the signature: disguise as DAILY DISCIPLINE)
- Inside ("In the Cloister" + its six sub-quests: Nasty Habits, The Trials of a Novice, Worldly Goods,
  Scattered Pages, Libri Prohibiti, The Abbot's List), Henry must LIVE as a monk to hold cover: attend
  matins/prayers ON TIME, eat supper at the bell, transcribe books, do chores, sleep in the dormitory —
  a strict SCHEDULE enforced by the monastery's routine. Break the routine (miss prayers, wander
  restricted areas, get caught out of place) and suspicion rises. The disguise is not a hat; it's a
  PERFORMANCE you sustain over in-game days. Meanwhile you secretly hunt Pious among the novices.
- THE IDENTIFICATION PUZZLE: Pious hides AS a novice; you must deduce WHICH monk he is (a whodunit-in-
  reverse) via observation and the sub-quests, while maintaining the daily disguise.

### STAGE 5 — THE RESOLUTION (many exits)
- You can: capture Pious and bring him to justice (a FISTFIGHT -> auto-transport to Rattay jail, 1050
  groschen from the bailiff — the "spare/lawful" route Radzig wanted); KILL him (but kill him OUTSIDE
  or you may miss the "strange die" on his corpse — an item reward for doing it right); or, at ANY
  time, murder a novice and FLEE, skipping the whole cloister arc (the blunt shortcut — the sim allows
  you to bail out of the elegant version entirely).
- CROSS-QUEST NOTE: the whole cloister arc can be short-circuited or fulfilled through its six
  intertwined side quests — the "main" and "side" are woven, not separate.

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. GOAL + SIMULATION, NOT BRANCH-MENU: the quest states an objective and hands you a systemic world;
    solutions EMERGE (disguise / stealth / bribe-the-guard-with-dice / brute force / secret passage /
    literacy) rather than being picked from an authored list. Freedom feels discovered, not offered.
W2. DISGUISE AS MAINTAINED PERFORMANCE: a disguise isn't a costume toggle — it must be WORN CORRECTLY
    (helmet on, own clothes off), SUSTAINED (persuasion checks, no caught crimes), and in the monastery,
    LIVED (attend prayers on time, keep the schedule). Cover is an ongoing discipline.
W3. THE DISGUISE COSTS YOU (surrender the kit): entering the monastery strips your entire inventory and
    power — the vow ("poverty") is mechanical. A disguise that costs you something real raises the stakes.
W4. QUEST GATED BEHIND A LIFE SKILL: you can't enter without LITERACY — a competence earned in the open
    world. Progression depends on who your character has BECOME, not just quest flags.
W5. CROSS-QUEST REACTIVITY: infiltrating the bandits in a PRIOR quest changes THIS quest's difficulty;
    the monastery arc weaves six side quests. The world remembers and threads quests together.
W6. DENSITY AS OBSTACLE: the camp is so crowded that rote stealth-assassination fails — the environment's
    population forces PATIENCE and opportunism over exploit. The world's texture is the challenge.
W7. FORCE IS A VALID ROUTE (with a cost): you can brute-force the camp or bail the monastery arc by
    murder-and-flee — the elegant path is optional; the sim supports the blunt one (with lost rewards).
W8. THE APPROACH IS A MORAL STANCE: "mercy or annihilation" for the bandits; capture-vs-kill Pious —
    HOW you solve it is WHO you are, and the game frames it that way.
W9. DIEGETIC SOCIAL SOLUTIONS: get the guardian drunk on dice to isolate the mark — a social problem
    solved through a WORLD ACTIVITY (gambling), not a dialogue check. Systems double as tools.
W10. REWARD FOR DOING IT RIGHT: kill Pious OUTSIDE and you miss the "strange die"; the clean method
    yields a hidden reward — the sim quietly rewards precision/patience.
W11. THE HONEST FLAW (anti-pattern): systemic stealth with INCONSISTENT detection ("seen through walls")
    turns emergent freedom into unfair failure — LEGIBLE rules are the price of systemic ambition.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built — the SYSTEMIC gap-filler)
===============================================================================
This is the deep-dive most aimed at our BIGGEST flagged gap (P7 systemic solution space).
- W1 (goal + simulation): frame Bohemia quests as an OBJECTIVE + our systems (build/logistics/combat/
  social/stealth), and let solutions EMERGE from the sim — not a fixed dialogue-branch menu. Our engine
  (city-builder, resource flows, scheduler, combat) IS a solution space; USE it as one.
- W2/W4 (disguise as lived discipline + gated behind a life skill): a Bohemia INFILTRATION where the
  cover must be LIVED — perform the faction's actual daily labor (work the Trades shift, keep the Church
  routine, live the Homeless camp) to hold it; and gate it behind a real competence the dynasty
  developed (literacy=our [READ]; a trade skill; a faction standing). Ties Q018 cult + Q043 quiet line.
- W3 (the disguise costs you): a Bohemia cover that STRIPS your kit/power (go in as a penitent/prisoner/
  refugee with nothing) — the vulnerability is the tension.
- W5 (cross-quest reactivity): let a PRIOR Bohemia quest's outcome change a LATER quest's difficulty/
  approach (infiltrated a faction before = easier now) — scope-isolated per the sandbox law, but bank
  MORE of these threaded reactivities (our fold does the big version; add tactical ones).
- W6 (density as obstacle): use the WORLD'S texture (a crowded market, a packed camp, a busy plant) as
  the obstacle that forces patience/opportunism over exploit — the environment IS the difficulty.
- W7/W8 (force valid + approach is a stance): keep force a real route WITH a cost, and frame HOW you
  solve a quest as a moral stance (mercy vs annihilation) — our Megaton + pacifist laws, validated.
- W9 (diegetic social solutions): solve social problems through WORLD ACTIVITIES (gamble, trade, share
  a meal, do a chore) not just dialogue checks — our systems double as social tools.
- W10 (reward doing it right): hide rewards behind the CLEAN/patient method (do it unwitnessed/non-
  lethally/in-character -> a secret payoff) — precision rewarded.
- W11 (AVOID the flaw): if we build systemic stealth/detection, make the rules LEGIBLE and consistent
  (show detection state clearly) — ambition without legibility feels unfair (our regression gate).
