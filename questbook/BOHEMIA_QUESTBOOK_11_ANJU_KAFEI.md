# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 11: ANJU & KAFEI / "ANJU'S ANGUISH" (The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: event, staging, the exact three-day timetable, the two-perspective
structure, the requires-two-loops design, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for the LIVING-
CLOCK QUEST — NPCs on real schedules the player must READ and thread, a quest that can be FAILED by
mismanaging TIME, resolving in devotion under an apocalyptic deadline. Fills our flagged gap (P10 living-
system puzzle). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Anju and Kafei" / "Anju's Anguish" (Bombers' Notebook), the longest side quest in Majora's Mask.
The game runs on a repeating 3-DAY cycle (54 real minutes) before the moon destroys Termina; Link loops
time via the Song of Time. This quest spans the ENTIRE cycle and is timed to the minute.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE PREMISE / STAKES
===============================================================================
- WHO: KAFEI (a man cursed into a child's body days before his wedding, hiding in shame under a mask)
  and ANJU (his fiancee, waiting at the Stock Pot Inn, not knowing what became of him). A demon stole
  Kafei's Sun's Mask (needed with Anju's Moon's Mask to make the wedding Couple's Mask).
- THE HOOK: the Mayor's wife (Madame Aroma) asks Link to find her missing son Kafei -> gives Kafei's
  Mask -> opens the quest. Link becomes the SECRET GO-BETWEEN carrying letters, tokens, and news
  between two lovers who cannot reach each other.
- THE STAKES: reunite the lovers BEFORE the moon falls. The reward is the Couple's Mask (needed for the
  ultimate mask) AND a full end-credits scene; fail, and they don't reunite. But the real stakes are
  emotional: two people choosing to face the apocalypse TOGETHER rather than die apart.
- THE CONSTRAINT: everything is on a CLOCK. NPCs move on fixed schedules; miss a window and the chain
  breaks. The quest is a TIME-MANAGEMENT devotion puzzle across a doomed 72 hours.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE THREE-DAY TIMETABLE (the actual schedule — this IS the quest)
===============================================================================
The quest is literally a timetable the player must execute. Key windows (3DS timings):

### DAY 1
- 10:00 AM — Mayor's Residence (East Clock Town): talk to Madame Aroma; agree to find Kafei -> get
  KAFEI'S MASK. (Be quick — if Mr. Gorman arrives first, an argument derails her and the window closes.)
- ~1:50-3:50 PM — Stock Pot Inn: wear the GORON MASK, talk to Anju, agree to stay the night -> ROOM KEY.
- 2:10 PM — the Postman delivers a letter to Anju (leaves 2:18); after he goes, wear KAFEI'S MASK and
  talk to Anju -> she sets a secret meeting for 11:30 PM.
- ~11:30 PM-midnight — Inn KITCHEN: meet Anju -> she gives the LETTER TO KAFEI. Deposit it in a mailbox
  BEFORE dawn of Day 2. (CRITICAL: do NOT stop the thief Sakon in North Clock Town along the way, or the
  chain breaks later.)

### DAY 2
- ~2:00 PM — Laundry Pool: be there for 3:00 PM, when the Postman delivers Link's letter to KAFEI.
- ~3:00 PM+ — enter Kafei's hideout (the door he uses); talk to him -> he tells his story, reveals his
  Sun's Mask was ROBBED by Sakon, and entrusts Link with the PENDANT OF MEMORIES.
- ~5:00 PM — back to the Inn: give Anju the PENDANT (proof Kafei is alive) -> she agrees to WAIT for him
  (otherwise she flees to Romani Ranch on Day 3 and the quest fails).

### DAY 3
- from 6:00 AM — Kafei's place: the Curiosity Shop owner relays a message + gives the KEATON MASK and a
  "Special Delivery to Mama"; names the thief Sakon and his hideout in Ikana Canyon.
- OPTIONAL REWARD FORK: deliver the Special Delivery to Madame Aroma (Milk Bar, wearing Kafei's Mask) ->
  a bottle of Chateau Romani; OR give it to the Postman (West Clock Town), follow him -> the POSTMAN'S
  HAT. (One errand, two mutually-exclusive rewards.)
- ~evening — Ikana Canyon: ambush Sakon's hideout (a stealth beat; the Stone Mask helps); Kafei is
  hiding there too. Recover BOTH masks (Sun's + the stolen goods).
- ~before 5:00 AM (final ~1:30 on the countdown) — Anju's Room, Stock Pot Inn: WAIT. Kafei arrives.
  The reunion cutscene: they combine the SUN'S MASK + MOON'S MASK into the COUPLE'S MASK and give it to
  Link. With the moon minutes from falling, they choose to face the end TOGETHER.

### THE TWO-LOOPS TRUTH (the deep structure)
- To fill EVERY Bombers' Notebook entry, Link must run the sequence TWICE — going back to Day 1 to catch
  the beats he couldn't reach the first cycle. The quest is DESIGNED around the time-loop: you learn the
  schedule on one loop, then execute it cleanly on another. Knowledge of the timetable IS the progression.
- The original N64 version was tighter/harsher; the 3DS remake eased the timing (the designer wanted it
  less punishing) — an honest note that brutal timing can frustrate.

## 2. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. NPCs ON REAL SCHEDULES: Anju, Kafei, the Postman, Sakon, Madame Aroma all live fixed timetables; the
    town is a CLOCKWORK the player reads and threads. The world runs WITHOUT you — you slot into it.
W2. THE QUEST CAN BE FAILED BY TIME: miss a window and the chain breaks / Anju flees. Real stakes from
    time-management — the deadline is teeth, not decoration.
W3. THE PLAYER AS SECRET GO-BETWEEN: you carry letters/tokens between two people who can't meet — a
    structure of DEVOTION-BY-PROXY; you're the thread connecting them, which makes the reunion YOURS too.
W4. KNOWLEDGE IS THE PROGRESSION (loop-mastery): you learn the schedule on one cycle and execute it on
    the next — the time-loop turns FORE-KNOWLEDGE into the core skill. Replaying with knowledge is the
    intended play, not a failure.
W5. THE APOCALYPTIC DEADLINE AS EMOTION: the reunion lands BECAUSE the moon is falling — devotion in the
    face of certain doom. The countdown clock is an emotional instrument (cf. the Baron's "peregrination"
    — time weaponized for feeling, Questbook 01).
W6. TWO PERSPECTIVES, ONE STORY: you experience Anju's side (waiting, hoping) and Kafei's side (ashamed,
    hunting the thief) separately, then converge them — dramatic irony + a reunion the PLAYER engineered.
W7. THE INTERLOCKING DEPENDENCY (don't stop the thief!): a counterintuitive rule — you must NOT stop
    Sakon's robbery early, or a later beat breaks. Events are interdependent; "helping" at the wrong
    time breaks the chain (cf. Whispering Hillock's obvious-good trap, Questbook 04).
W8. MUNDANE TOOLS, PRECISE USE: masks/keys/letters used at exact times/places — the puzzle is SEQUENCING
    known tools against a schedule, not finding new ones. Mastery is timing, not inventory.
W9. THE OPTIONAL REWARD FORK: one errand (the Special Delivery) yields two mutually-exclusive rewards —
    a small choice with real exclusivity inside a tightly-scripted quest.
W10. THE EARNED CUTSCENE: the reunion is a long, tender payoff GATED behind flawless execution across 72
    hours — the emotional climax is EARNED by mastery, so it lands like an achievement AND a story beat.

## 3. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — fills the P10 living-clock gap
===============================================================================
Our engine has a SCHEDULER + a 120 BPM grid + day/night — the substrate for a living-clock quest exists.
- W1 (NPCs on real schedules): a Bohemia quest built on our SCHEDULER where key NPCs live fixed daily
  routines (the plant chief's shifts, a caravan's arrival, a Church service, a Homeless camp's rhythm)
  and the player must READ and THREAD them — the district as clockwork (ties Q047 patience + the KCD
  monastery routine, Questbook 08).
- W2 (failable by time): a Bohemia quest with a real DEADLINE (a caravan leaves, a patient dies, a
  execution proceeds, the Amalgamation moves) where mistiming FAILS it — stakes from the clock (use
  sparingly; pair with our no-hard-brick-wall rule — a failed timed quest should REROUTE, not softlock).
- W3 (secret go-between / devotion-by-proxy): a Bohemia reunion/reconciliation quest where the dynasty
  carries messages/tokens between two people who can't meet (separated families, a divided-faction
  couple, the Underground-railroad Q043 inverted toward love) — the player as the connecting thread.
- W4 (knowledge-as-progression): lean into our GENERATIONAL structure — a timed event an heir can do
  BETTER knowing what the prior dynast learned (fore-knowledge across the fold = the time-loop lesson,
  natively fitting our succession loop). A quest the SECOND dynasty solves cleaner because the first mapped it.
- W5 (apocalyptic deadline as emotion): our endgame (the Amalgamation moving, a district under siege)
  can use a COUNTDOWN as an emotional instrument — devotion/choices under a falling sky (ties the ME2
  hidden clock, Questbook 06, + our Q036 plant siege).
- W6 (two perspectives converging): a Bohemia quest told from TWO sides the dynasty experiences
  separately then converges (two estranged factions, two family branches) — dramatic irony + a
  player-engineered reunion.
- W7 (interlocking dependency / don't help too early): a Bohemia beat where intervening at the WRONG
  time breaks a later payoff — events interdepend; timing IS the puzzle (careful, legible design so it
  doesn't feel like a gotcha — telegraph the dependency).
- W8 (mundane tools, precise sequencing): our systems (build/logistics/social) used at exact times/
  places — the puzzle is SEQUENCING against the schedule, not new mechanics.
- W9 (optional exclusive reward fork): small mutually-exclusive rewards inside tight quests (our Megaton
  at micro-scale).
- W10 (the earned cutscene): gate a tender payoff behind flawless execution — the emotional climax as an
  achievement (our succession reunions, a hard-won district celebration).
- HONEST CAVEAT (from the N64->3DS easing): brutal timing FRUSTRATES; make timed windows GENEROUS +
  legible (show the schedule, forgive near-misses) so the living-clock feels like devotion, not a stopwatch.
