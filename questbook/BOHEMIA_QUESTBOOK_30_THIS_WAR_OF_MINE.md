# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 30: THE CIVILIAN PERSPECTIVE / GUILT AS A SYSTEM (This War of Mine)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the play-as-victims inversion, the Quiet House elderly-couple
dilemma, the mental-health/depression/breakdown engine, no-onboarding + permadeath, the survivor-
remembers-guilt system, the emergent-regret playtest origin, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This
is the medium's model for SURVIVAL GUILT AS A MECHANIC + GREY MORALITY WITH NO RIGHT ANSWER + the
victims'-eye view of catastrophe. 11 bit studios (Frostpunk's sibling, Q29). Uncannily close to
Bohemia's survivor core. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: This War of Mine (11 bit studios, 2014). Inspired by the Siege of Sarajevo. You keep a group of
CIVILIAN survivors alive in a besieged city — scavenge by night, shelter by day, manage bodies AND
minds — as scarcity forces you toward choices that scar the people who make them.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE PERSPECTIVE INVERSION: not soldiers — VICTIMS. Where Call of Duty makes you the gun, TWoM makes
  you the huddled civilian ("your beliefs and opinions are not important; you need to defend your family"
  — the dev's thesis). The whole emotional engine is re-centering war on the powerless.
- GREY MORALITY, NO RIGHT ANSWER: "there are no easy decisions... they simply don't exist." Altruism
  COSTS time + resources (and can kill your own); cruelty FEEDS you but scars your survivors. Every
  scavenging night is a conscience test with no clean option (cf. Pathologic Q21, Pentiment Q26).
- GUILT IS A MECHANIC (the innovation): survivors have MENTAL health. Steal from the vulnerable, kill an
  unarmed civilian, and they slide from "sad" -> "broken" -> a DEPRESSION that can end in suicide or a
  catatonic breakdown (a lost pair of hands). Conversely, helping neighbors LIFTS morale. Conscience is
  a resource you manage — the game mechanizes the psychological cost of survival.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE QUIET HOUSE (the emblematic dilemma, torn down)
===============================================================================
- THE SETUP: a scavenging location — an ELDERLY COUPLE living relatively untouched, their home full of
  FOOD and MEDICINE (the two most precious commodities). They're UNARMED. You can take EVERYTHING without
  resistance. The husband asks what you're doing; his wife is sick; they both look scared; he BEGS you to
  leave. Nothing stops you but your conscience.
- THE CHOICE + THE COST: rob them blind (your people eat for days; the couple may starve; YOUR survivor
  is scarred with guilt -> depression) OR take nothing/some (your people go hungry; you stay human).
  There's no dialogue "resolution," no reward for mercy but morale — the weight is entirely internal.
- THE PLAYTEST ORIGIN (why it exists): during testing, 11 bit watched a player steal ALL the couple's
  food, then REGRET it and RETURN to put half back so they wouldn't starve. That SPONTANEOUS act of
  emergent conscience inspired the team to build EMOTIONAL CONSEQUENCES into the game itself — the guilt
  system exists because a player felt guilty unprompted. (Design lesson: watch for emergent player
  emotion and BUILD THE SYSTEM AROUND IT.)
- THE ACADEMIC READ: TWoM is a "subtractive" + "mirror" experience — it withholds guidance so you draw
  your OWN moral conclusions, then mirrors your choices back as discomfort. "Designed to stimulate
  discomfort, just like Spec Ops: The Line" (Q27) — but WITHOUT forcing the atrocity (you always choose).

===============================================================================
## 2. THE SUPPORTING SYSTEMS (how the dread is enforced)
===============================================================================
- NO ONBOARDING: no tutorial, no coaching — you learn to survive by failing, paralleling a civilian
  thrown into war with no manual. Disorientation is the design (cf. Pathologic's slow-onset Q21).
- PERMADEATH: dead survivors don't come back — permanence simulates war's precariousness + the trauma of
  loss on those who remain (cf. Undertale's unforgetting save Q28, our fold).
- THE DAY/NIGHT SPLIT (two failure surfaces): by DAY you shelter/craft/heal but can't scavenge (snipers);
  by NIGHT one survivor scavenges (risking bandits/soldiers) while those left home are vulnerable to
  RAIDS. Every night is a resource-allocation gamble with bodies (cf. ME2 role-assignment Q06, Pathologic
  triage Q21).
- ATTRITION CASCADES: overexertion -> sickness; untreated injury -> sepsis; unmet needs -> depression ->
  group dysfunction. Neglect compounds; a small deficit snowballs into a death spiral (cf. Frostpunk's
  scarcity Q29). Storytelling/interpersonal care between survivors MITIGATES trauma — community as a
  survival resource.
- NON-PLAYABLE CHILDREN: the presence of kids/infants raises the moral stakes of every scarcity choice
  (a designed vulnerability that weights the ethics — cf. our care rules; handle with weight, not
  exploitation).

===============================================================================
## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- GUILT CAN BE "MANAGED"/GAMED: players learn depression is "easily fixed" with the right survivor setup/
  items, or that there are "no wrong decisions" mechanically — the moral weight can erode into
  optimization ("berserk through it, patch the sadness"). LESSON: if guilt is a meter you can top up, the
  ethics deflate — tie the cost to something that STICKS (permanent scars, a survivor who never fully
  recovers) so conscience isn't just a managed stat (cf. Frostpunk's gameable hope Q29, Undertale's
  permanent taint Q28).
- THE BALANCE OF DISCOMFORT vs FUN: 11 bit themselves note "too much ethical design makes the player feel
  bored" — they had to balance emotional realism against ENTERTAINMENT. LESSON: relentless misery
  alienates (cf. Pathologic Q21); the grimness needs texture, agency, and the occasional win to stay
  playable (our SUN-MODE/tone instincts + difficulty options).
- REPETITION: the day/night loop can grind over a long siege. LESSON: vary the scavenging encounters +
  moral scenarios so the dilemma stays fresh, not rote.

===============================================================================
## 4. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE PERSPECTIVE INVERSION (victims, not soldiers): re-center catastrophe on the POWERLESS — the
    emotional engine of the whole game; you defend family, not conquer (cf. our post-collapse survivors).
W2. GUILT AS A MECHANIC: survivors have MENTAL health that cruelty erodes (sad -> broken -> suicide/
    catatonia) and kindness lifts — conscience is a managed resource; the psychological cost is SYSTEMIZED.
W3. GREY MORALITY, NO RIGHT ANSWER: altruism costs survival, cruelty costs the soul — every choice is a
    real trade with no clean option (cf. Pathologic Q21, Pentiment Q26, Whispering Hillock Q04).
W4. THE EMBLEMATIC DILEMMA (the Quiet House): one concrete, unforgettable moral image (rob the scared,
    unarmed, sick elderly couple) — a vivid scene, not an abstract policy (cf. Frostpunk child-labor Q29).
W5. BUILD THE SYSTEM AROUND EMERGENT EMOTION: the guilt mechanic exists because a playtester felt guilt
    unprompted (returned to un-steal) — WATCH for emergent player feeling and mechanize it. (A profound
    design method: let players teach you what the game is about.)
W6. THE SUBTRACTIVE/MIRROR EXPERIENCE: withhold guidance so players draw their OWN conclusions, then
    mirror choices back as discomfort — discomfort WITHOUT forcing it (the ethical improvement on Spec
    Ops Q27; you always choose, so the guilt is truly yours — cf. Undertale Q28).
W7. NO ONBOARDING + PERMADEATH: learn-by-failing disorientation + irreversible loss simulate war's
    precariousness (cf. Pathologic Q21, Undertale Q28, our fold).
W8. COMMUNITY AS A SURVIVAL RESOURCE: survivors telling stories / caring for each other MITIGATES trauma
    — social bonds are mechanically load-bearing (cf. our companion systems; the anti-isolation).
W9. ATTRITION CASCADES: small neglect snowballs (overexertion->sickness->depression->dysfunction) — the
    death spiral makes early kindness/harshness compound (cf. Frostpunk Q29). Consequences interlock.
W10. THE PACIFIST RUN IS POSSIBLE: you CAN survive as "mister nice guy," never stealing/killing — mercy
     is a viable (harder) path, always your choice (validates our Pacifist Path Law; cf. Undertale Q28).

## 5. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the survivor-core sibling
===============================================================================
TWoM is uncannily close to Bohemia's SURVIVOR heart (post-collapse civilians, scarcity, no-clean-choices)
and pairs with Frostpunk (Q29): TWoM = the GROUND-LEVEL human/guilt view; Frostpunk = the GOVERNANCE view.
Together they map our dual scale (individual survival + district governance).
- W1 (the perspective inversion): OUR core IS this — post-collapse survivors, not conquerors. Validated;
  keep the emotional weight on the powerless rebuilding a life, not a power fantasy (our whole premise).
- W2 (GUILT AS A MECHANIC — the big port): bank a Bohemia CONSCIENCE/mental-health system — survivors/
  companions/the dynast carry a psychological state that CRUEL choices erode (toward breakdown/hardening)
  and merciful ones sustain; conscience is a managed-but-STICKY resource (tie to our upbringing Q003, the
  unrecorded ledger, the succession trait system — a hard choice can permanently scar an heir). Make the
  cost of survival PSYCHOLOGICAL, not just material (ties Pathologic Q21, within our wellbeing rules).
- W3/W4 (grey morality + the emblematic dilemma): give Bohemia vivid, concrete no-right-answer scenes (a
  Quiet-House equivalent — rob the defenseless who have medicine your sick child needs; the game never
  says it's ok) — one felt moral IMAGE per dilemma, not abstract policy (ties Q049 verdicts, our death-
  math, Frostpunk Q29).
- W5 (build the system around emergent emotion): a DESIGN METHOD for Paolo — watch playtesters for
  emergent feeling (a spontaneous mercy, a regretted theft) and BUILD Bohemia systems around what players
  do unprompted; let the players reveal what the game is about (a playtesting law).
- W6 (subtractive/mirror, discomfort without forcing): our unrecorded ledger can MIRROR the dynasty's
  choices back as quiet discomfort (not preaching) — you always CHOSE, so the weight is yours (the
  ethical Undertale/TWoM path, not the Spec Ops railroad; our Pacifist Law + care rules).
- W8 (community as a survival resource): make Bohemia social BONDS mechanically load-bearing — survivors
  who care for each other resist despair; isolation kills (ties our companion systems, the anti-Amalgamation
  "connection vs the archive of the lonely dead" theme).
- W9 (attrition cascades): our survival-accounting/death-math should let small neglect SNOWBALL
  (hunger->sickness->despair->dysfunction) so early kindness/harshness compounds across a life/generation
  (ties Frostpunk Q29, our currencies).
- W10 (the pacifist run is possible): validated AGAIN — a full non-lethal, non-cruel survival path must
  always exist, harder but real (our Pacifist Path Law; TWoM + Undertale Q28 both confirm).
- FLAWS (bank): make conscience STICK (permanent scars, not a toppable meter — or the ethics deflate);
  BALANCE misery with agency/texture/wins so it stays playable (our SUN-MODE/tone/difficulty-package
  instincts); and VARY the moral scenarios so dilemmas stay fresh. Handle child-vulnerability with WEIGHT,
  never exploitation (our care rules, strict).
