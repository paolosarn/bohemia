# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 29: THE BOOK OF LAWS / SOCIETY-SURVIVAL (Frostpunk)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the daily law-passing engine, the Book of Laws slippery slope, the
hope-vs-discontent dual meter, the order-vs-faith trees, "the city must survive" and living with HOW you
survived, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the CLOSEST GENRE-COUSIN to Bohemia's city-builder
side — a survival city-builder where GOVERNANCE is the quest and every law is a moral compromise. From
11 bit studios (This War of Mine). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest here.

Game: Frostpunk (11 bit studios, 2018). "The first society survival game." In an 1886 alt-history global
freeze, you rule the last city, huddled around a coal GENERATOR, and must keep people warm, fed, and
HOPEFUL as the cold escalates — passing laws that steadily define what your society BECOMES.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- GOVERNANCE IS THE GAMEPLAY: the "quest" is running a society under existential scarcity — resource
  management (coal/wood/steel/food) fused with MORAL LAW-MAKING. Every mechanic asks a values question:
  optimization vs empathy, survival vs the soul.
- THE CITY MUST SURVIVE (the tagline) — BUT: survival is never the whole point. The game's sting is the
  ENDING reckoning: the crisis passes, and you + your people are left with the RAMIFICATIONS OF HOW YOU
  SURVIVED. Did you stay human, or become a labor camp? The question isn't "did you win" but "who did you
  become" (the "New Home" finale explicitly judges whether you "crossed the line").
- THEME THROUGH SYSTEMS: 11 bit (This War of Mine) specializes in making the MECHANICS carry the ethics —
  you don't read about hard choices, you MAKE them under a ticking thermometer (cf. theme-through-mechanic
  Q21/Q27; Dead Money greed, collection).

===============================================================================
## 1. THE BOOK OF LAWS (the signature engine)
===============================================================================
- ONE LAW PER DAY (paced moral cadence): roughly every 24 hours you may sign ONE new law from a branching
  "Book of Laws." The slow drip means each law is a deliberate, felt decision — you can't spam your way
  out; society changes one committed step at a time.
- LAWS AS BRANCHING TREES (exclusivity): laws unlock down trees where choices CLOSE others. The
  "adaptation" tree covers survival ethics; then the campaign forces a fork between two IDEOLOGICAL
  tracks — ORDER (cops, watchtowers, staff meetings, loyalty oaths) vs FAITH (churches, houses of prayer)
  — each a different way to manufacture HOPE and suppress DISCONTENT. You pick a path and live its logic.
- THE CHILD-LABOR EXAMPLE (the emblematic compromise): desperate for labor, you can REPEAL child-labor
  limits and put kids to work in "safe" jobs (more hands, everyone's upset) — OR build CHILD SHELTERS /
  insulated daycares (less labor, more hope). One slider between expedience and decency, and the game
  never pretends it's free.
- THE SLIPPERY SLOPE (the masterstroke): each "reasonable" law greases the next. A prison ("empty most of
  my game") unlocks rounding up malcontents -> then a re-education ability -> then a propaganda center
  (hope up, discontent up) -> then loyalty oaths -> "before you know it your settlement has become a
  LABOR CAMP." No single law is monstrous; the CUMULATIVE drift is. The horror is gradualism (cf. the
  banality of evil, Tyranny Q25; the delayed-consequence web).
- YOU DON'T HAVE TO (the quiet judgment): you can survive WITHOUT the dark laws, and the ending suggests
  maybe you SHOULD have — the game withholds an explicit "correct" path but tallies your drift.

===============================================================================
## 2. HOPE vs DISCONTENT (the dual-meter society model)
===============================================================================
- TWO SOCIETY METERS, not one morale bar: HOPE (will to live / faith in the future) and DISCONTENT
  (anger / unrest). They move semi-independently — many acts RAISE BOTH (propaganda: +hope AND +discontent;
  a harsh law: survival up, hope down). If discontent maxes or hope bottoms, you're EXILED and the run
  ends (a political-legitimacy fail-state, distinct from freezing/starving).
- THE BALANCING ACT: you juggle physical survival (heat/food) AGAINST political survival (hope/discontent)
  — a leader can keep everyone ALIVE and still be OVERTHROWN for how they did it. Governance has two
  simultaneous failure axes (cf. Tyranny favor/wrath Q25, Alpha Protocol multidimensional rep Q24).

===============================================================================
## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE "OPTIMAL" PATH CAN FLATTEN THE ETHICS: min-maxers find efficient law orders that trivialize the
  moral weight (it becomes a spreadsheet). LESSON: if survival is solvable "optimally," the moral tension
  drains — keep scarcity tight enough that hard choices STAY hard (our death-math should preserve dilemma,
  not become a solved formula).
- HOPE/DISCONTENT CAN FEEL GAMEY: players learn to "pump hope" with buildings, reducing a profound theme
  to a meter to top up. LESSON: tie the social meters to VISIBLE human consequences (a named family, a
  strike with faces) so they don't feel like abstract bars (cf. Pathologic's personal stakes Q21; our
  unrecorded ledger's "everyone is someone").
- LINEAR SCENARIOS: the campaign is fairly fixed per scenario (limited branching beyond the law trees);
  replay is about trying darker/kinder, not a wholly different story. LESSON: our GENERATIONAL structure
  can give the deeper branching Frostpunk lacks (each dynasty a different society-run).

## 5. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the city-builder's closest cousin
===============================================================================
This is the most DIRECTLY applicable deep-dive to our city-builder + survival + governance layer. We are
literally a post-collapse society-survival RPG city-builder; Frostpunk is the master text for the
GOVERNANCE-AS-MORAL-ENGINE side.
- W1/W2 (governance is the quest + a Book of Laws): bank a Bohemia "BOOK OF LAWS/DECREES" — a paced,
  branching, mutually-exclusive policy engine where the dynasty passes ONE major decree at a meaningful
  cadence (per season/generation), each felt + committed, shaping what the district BECOMES (rationing
  rules, curfews, conscription, how the dead are handled, who gets medicine). Ties our currencies
  (medicine/electricity/resources), Q049 verdicts, the mayor-consequence + succession systems. (Our
  MAP LAW untouched — this is policy/plumbing, not layout.)
- W3 (the slippery slope): design our decrees so each reasonable one GREASES the next — a survival
  measure unlocks a harsher option unlocks a harsher one, until the dynasty drifts into tyranny WITHOUT a
  single monstrous choice. The Amalgamation's "no one may be lost" is the ultimate slope; bank gradualism
  as a horror engine (ties Q018, the banality-of-evil Tyranny Q25, Forgotten-City collective-rule Q20).
- W4 (the emblematic compromise): give Bohemia vivid, concrete moral sliders (not abstract policy) — a
  child-labor-equivalent, a "who eats when there's not enough," a "treat the sick or save the medicine" —
  one felt image per dilemma (ties Pathologic triage Q21, our death-math).
- W5/W10 (dual society meters + legitimacy fail-state): upgrade our standing model toward TWO+ axes at
  the DISTRICT level — e.g. HOPE (will to rebuild) vs DISCONTENT (unrest) — where the dynasty can keep
  people ALIVE yet be OVERTHROWN/deposed for HOW they ruled (a political fail-state distinct from
  starving/freezing). Ties Tyranny favor/wrath Q25, Alpha Protocol Q24, our succession/mayor systems.
- W6 (survive -> who did you become): OUR endgame IS this — the district survives, but Liberate/Respect/
  Become judge HOW + who the dynasty became. Bank Frostpunk's "the crisis passes, live with your methods"
  as an explicit reckoning beat (validated; the city-builder version of our thesis).
- W7 (theme through systems): keep making Bohemia's ethics something the player DOES under pressure
  (allocate, decree, triage), never a sermon (our no-preaching rule + Pathologic/Spec Ops; validated).
- W8 (ideological forks): our FACTIONS give this natural — mutually-exclusive ways to manufacture order/
  hope (Church faith vs Reds order vs Trades commerce), each a different regime the dynasty can lean into
  (ties our faction web + Tyranny Q25).
- W9 (the quiet judgment / dark laws optional): keep the harsh options AVAILABLE but never mandatory, and
  let the unrecorded ledger TALLY the drift without preaching — survivable kindly, tempting cruelly (our
  Pacifist Path Law at the GOVERNANCE scale).
- FLAWS (bank): keep scarcity tight so dilemmas STAY hard (don't let death-math become a solved
  spreadsheet); tie hope/discontent to NAMED human faces (not abstract bars — the ledger); and use our
  GENERATIONAL structure to give the deep branching Frostpunk's linear scenarios lack (each dynasty a
  different society-run).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE CAPTAIN (the player)** — wants the city to survive. The tagline agrees. The ending asks what else got traded for it. Will trade: one law per day, each irrevocable in spirit. Will never say out loud: which law was the one where it stopped being about survival. FUNCTION: the governor as the tested. The player fantasy is authority; the game is the invoice.

**THE GENERATOR** — wants coal. Always, only, more. FUNCTION: the god of the setting: the single point of warmth every moral compromise orbits. Heat is the currency every value gets exchanged into.

**THE PEOPLE (hope and discontent, wearing faces)** — want warmth, food, their children off the coal lines, and a reason to believe in tomorrow. Will trade: obedience for hope, patience for honesty. Will never say out loud, until the meter maxes: that they can depose you. FUNCTION: the dual meter as a cast (W5): a society that can be kept ALIVE and still throw you out for how.

**THE BOOK OF LAWS** — wants signatures. It offers only reasonable pages; the monstrousness is cumulative, never local. FUNCTION: the slippery slope, bound and indexed (W3). No single law is the crime; the drift is.

**THE CHILDREN** — want to not be in this file. FUNCTION: the emblematic compromise (W4): the labor law with faces, the one image that makes "policy" mean something at the signing table.

**ORDER and FAITH (the two trees)** — want, identically: hope manufactured, dissent managed. Differ only in costume: watchtowers or steeples, oaths or prayers. FUNCTION: the ideological fork (W8): the same social control sold under opposite values, and you must buy one.

**LONDON (the ones who want to leave)** — want to go back to a city that no longer exists. FUNCTION: the legitimacy crisis made a faction: hope's failure organizing itself.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees; the machine's honesty: the signature IS the sentence — Frostpunk's dialogue runs through the law book and the meters answer)

NODE: THE_SIGNING — the Book of Laws, entry: one per ~24 hours, forever
  A page turns. Something reasonable is proposed.
  > (sign it)                       [gate: cooldown elapsed] -> the law is SOCIETY now; a new page becomes possible tomorrow (W2, W3)
  > (leave it unsigned)             [gate: none] SILENCE -> the pressure that motivated it does not sign anything either; the cold continues its own legislation
  > (sign the kind alternative)     [gate: the tree's fork] -> costs labor/heat/time; pays hope; the door to the harsh version usually closes
  THE FORM IS THE FINDING: one committed, exclusive choice per day is a moral CADENCE. Nothing can be spammed; everything is felt; the society is a sum of deliberate steps.

NODE: CHILD_LABOR — the emblematic page, entry: hands short, coal low
  > (repeal the limits, "safe jobs") [gate: none] -> more hands today; everyone quietly diminished; discontent seeded in every family with a child (W4)
  > (child shelters instead)         [gate: resources] -> fewer hands, more hope, a city that decided what children are FOR
  NOVERB: "It's only temporary." The book has no clause for it. Laws in Frostpunk do not sunset; the removed verb is the gradualism's engine: every measure passed "for now" is permanent, and the game never pretends otherwise.

NODE: THE_SLOPE — a sequence, not a scene: prison -> rounding up malcontents -> re-education -> propaganda center -> loyalty oaths
  Each node's option list looks like this:
  > (the next reasonable step)      [gate: the previous reasonable step] -> hope up, discontent up, the camp closer (W3)
  > (stop here)                     [gate: none] -> ALWAYS AVAILABLE, never highlighted; the quiet judgment (W9): you don't have to, and the ending will mention it
  THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK is the player's own, weeks later, looking at watchtowers: "when did I sign THAT?" The answer is: never. You signed each step before it.

NODE: ORDER_OR_FAITH — the fork, entry: adaptation tree exhausted, the crisis deepening
  > (Order: watchtowers, patrols, oaths) [gate: exclusive] -> hope as discipline; dissent as crime
  > (Faith: chapels, prayers, processions) [gate: exclusive] -> hope as meaning; dissent as sin
  LOCKS: the other tree, forever this run. The regime's character is chosen here and lived thereafter (W8). Both trees end, if ridden to the last page, somewhere no one at this node intended.

NODE: THE_METERS — no speaker; hope and discontent, moving semi-independently
  > (pump hope with buildings)      [gate: resources] TRAP -> the banked flaw: a profound theme reduced to a bar to top up; the fix is faces (a named family striking, a real funeral), not more architecture
  > (rule honestly through the dip) [gate: nerve] -> slower, human, survivable
  FAIL-STATE: discontent maxed or hope bottomed -> EXILED (W10). The city lives; you are walked to the frost. Political death as a separate axis from physical death.

NODE: THE_RECKONING — the storm passed, entry: the crisis survived
  The game replays your drift: the laws, the line, whether you crossed it.
  > (no options)                    -> the verdict is retrospective and total: THE CITY SURVIVED. DID YOU? (W6)
  WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing, anymore. That is the design: the bill was paid daily, one signature at a time, and this screen is only the receipt.

## V2-C THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 2 fail-terminals + 1 survival-terminal graded by drift (x the Order/Faith regime fork). The map is a GRADIENT, not a fork: the branching happened one page a day.

F1 — THE CITY DIES: frozen, starved. The physical failure.

S1 — THE CITY SURVIVES, graded: the ending reads the Book of Laws back as a character verdict — stayed human / crossed the line — under whichever regime (Order/Faith) you built (W6, W8). No score. A mirror.
