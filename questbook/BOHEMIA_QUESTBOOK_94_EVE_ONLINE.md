Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the Guiding Hand Social Club heist (ten months of infiltration for one
word), the Bloodbath of B-R5RB (a 21-hour, $330,000 war caused by a missed autopay), the permanent WRECK
MONUMENT the developers built on the battlefield, espionage as legitimate play, the player-elected council
that flies to Iceland, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. The largest emergent political simulation ever
run. STARTLINGLY relevant to our faction web, our survival accounting, our persistent consequence, + our
city-builder. CCP Games. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: EVE Online (CCP Games, 2003 → present). A single-shard galaxy of 7,000+ star systems where "**all
market transactions, territorial control, and conflicts emerge from participant decisions RATHER THAN
DEVELOPER-IMPOSED NARRATIVES.**" Player groups aren't guilds — "**IN EVE THEY'RE CALLED CORPORATIONS BECAUSE
THEY ARE QUITE LITERALLY CORPORATIONS.**" Twenty-three years running.

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE BIGGEST WAR IN GAMING HISTORY STARTED WITH A MISSED AUTOPAY (the premise — and it's our
  survival-accounting dream): CCP's own dev blog, verbatim: "In the early hours of January 27th, 2014
  **CONCORD (the NPC 'police force') CAME TO COLLECT THE SOVEREIGNTY BILL** for a dead-end system... called
  B-R5RB... **Unfortunately, when CONCORD tried to extract the ISK that would maintain sovereignty in the
  system for another month, THEY FOUND THAT H A V O C HAD LEFT THEIR AUTOMATIC PAYMENT UNCHECKED. WITHOUT
  THE NECESSARY PAYMENT, SOVEREIGNTY IN THE SYSTEM IMMEDIATELY DROPPED LEAVING THE SYSTEM UP FOR GRABS.**"
  - And then CCP's own understated line: "**MISSED BILL PAYMENTS ARE CERTAINLY NOT NEW TO THE HUMAN
    EXPERIENCE NOR ARE THEY TO EVE ONLINE.**"
  - The result: **21 hours. 7,548 unique characters. 75 Titans destroyed** (previous record: 12). "Total
    losses amounted to over 11 trillion ISK, or **MORE THAN 300 THOUSAND REAL DOLLARS.**"
  - A journalist's summary: "**AND IT ALL COULD HAVE BEEN AVOIDED IF SOMEONE HAD JUST PAID THEIR BILLS**"
    (STARTLINGLY our survival accounting + city-builder + faction web; the bookkeeping error as a
    war trigger).
- TEN MONTHS OF FRIENDSHIP TO EARN ONE BETRAYAL (the Guiding Hand Social Club — the best emergent story ever
  told): an assassin corporation was hired by an anonymous enemy of the Ubiqua Seraph corporation. The fee
  was symbolic — 1 billion ISK — and "**THE ONLY CONDITION OF THE CUSTOMER WAS TO BRING HIM THE COLD CORPSE
  OF THE ENEMY CEO NAMED MIRIAL.**"
  - "**FOR ABOUT TEN MONTHS, THE AGENTS INFILTRATED UBIQUA SERAPH AND ROSE TO THE TOP. ONE OF THEM BECAME
    PRACTICALLY THE SECOND PERSON IN THE CORPORATION**" — the operative Arenis Xemdal, Mirial's trusted
    second-in-command and friend.
  - He "**CONVINCED THE VICTIM TO TAKE THE MOST EXPENSIVE SHIP INTO SPACE FOR SHOW**" — her Navy Apocalypse,
    alongside his own, "**AS A SHOW OF UQS MIGHT.**"
  - "**'NICOLE' WAS THE GO-WORD.**" On the day, the ambush sprang, and simultaneously the other embedded
    agents "**EMPTIED THE CORPORATION'S WAREHOUSES THROUGHOUT THE GALAXY**" — about 30 billion ISK, roughly
    $16,500 real dollars, "**WHICH HAD TAKEN THOUSANDS OF PEOPLE PLAYING THE GAME COUNTLESS HOURS TO GAIN.**"
  - They podded her to get the physical corpse, delivered it, **left notes claiming responsibility**, and
    afterward "**WOULD BOAST SHE HAD FALLEN IN LOVE WITH ONE OF THEIR AGENTS.**"
  - The aftermath: "**GUIDING HAND CEO, ISTVAAN SHOGAATSU, WOULD RECEIVE MULTIPLE DEATH THREATS, AND EVERY
    CEO IN EVERY CORPORATION WOULD THINK TWICE ABOUT WHO THEY TRUSTED**" (cf. our faction web + conscience
    system; the betrayal that recalibrated an entire society's trust).
- ESPIONAGE ISN'T CHEATING, IT'S THE GAME (the design permission that makes it all possible): "**COALITIONS
  HAVE BEEN KEEPING AGENTS IN ENEMY STRUCTURES FOR YEARS, INTERCEPTING ORDERS, LEAKING PLANS. At the
  decisive moment of the Battle of B-R5RB, ONE OF THE SIDES KNEW ABOUT THE ENEMY'S ORDER TO RETREAT, BECAUSE
  THEY HAD THEIR OWN MAN IN THEIR INTELLIGENCE. WHAT IN ANOTHER GAME WOULD BE CALLED CHEATING AND FOR WHICH
  YOU WOULD BE BANNED, IN EVE ONLINE IS SIMPLY PART OF THE METAGAME.**"
  - CCP "**PERMITS THEM ABSENT EXPLOITS, VIEWING THEM AS INTEGRAL TO EMERGENT CONFLICT RATHER THAN
    ABERRATIONS**" (cf. our faction web + Network; the developer's refusal to referee).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (a real society in a spreadsheet)
===============================================================================

### THE CORPORATIONS ARE ACTUALLY CORPORATIONS
- "Groups of players are called guilds and clans in other games, but **IN EVE THEY'RE CALLED CORPORATIONS
  BECAUSE THEY ARE QUITE LITERALLY CORPORATIONS: GROUPS OF PEOPLE ACTING AS A SINGLE ENTITY TO GROW IN
  INFLUENCE AND MAKE MONEY.**"
- Alliances have real overhead: "**FORMATION REQUIRES AN INITIAL 1 BILLION ISK FEE, PLUS A MONTHLY
  MAINTENANCE BILL OF 2 MILLION ISK**," and they "declare formal relations—such as **NON-AGGRESSION PACTS,
  DEFENSE AGREEMENTS, OR WARS—VIA IN-GAME DIPLOMACY TOOLS.**"
- The institution is a MECHANIC with a bill (cf. our 13 factions + city-builder + survival accounting; the
  faction as a legal entity that can go bankrupt).
### THE BANK RUN (the scam that taught everyone about banks)
- "In 2006, a player named **CALLY FOUNDED THE EVE INTERGALACTIC BANK, COLLECTED DEPOSITS WITH THE PROMISE
  OF INTEREST, AND THEN SIMPLY WITHDREW ABOUT 790 BILLION ISK (ABOUT $170,000).**"
- A commentator: "**EVE ONLINE PLAYERS GOT A TASTE OF WHAT IT MEANS TO GET SCREWED OVER BY BANKS.**" Two
  years before 2008 (STARTLINGLY our post-ECONOMIC-collapse premise; the financial institution as a con).
### THE DEVELOPERS BUILT A PERMANENT MONUMENT OUT OF THE WRECKAGE
- After B-R5RB, CCP made "**TITANOMACHY**" — "created using the brand new Titan wreck models... **A PERMANENT
  SITE IN THE GAME**, it will be placed around the seventh planet in the B-R5RB solar system... **THEREAFTER,
  ANY PLAYER WHO PLAYS EVE CAN MAKE THE DANGEROUS PILGRIMAGE THERE AND MARVEL AT THE SCOPE OF
  DESTRUCTION**... we're hard at work placing the wrecks in **A HAUNTINGLY BEAUTIFUL ARRANGEMENT.**"
- The developers turned a player disaster into permanent geography. The map now contains a memorial to a
  bookkeeping error (STARTLINGLY our district + persistent consequence + fold; the world keeping the scar).
### THE PLAYERS HAVE AN ELECTED GOVERNMENT THAT FLIES TO ICELAND
- "**EVE ONLINE PLAYERS HAVE THEIR OWN GOVERNING BODY. THE COUNCIL OF STELLAR MANAGEMENT IS A COUNCIL
  ELECTED BY PLAYERS THROUGH A DIRECT VOTE USING THE STV SYSTEM, WHICH IS USED BY SOME COUNTRIES IN REAL
  LIFE. COUNCIL MEMBERS RECEIVE ACCESS TO THE DEVELOPER'S INTERNAL MATERIALS UNDER NDA AND MEET ONCE A YEAR
  FOR A REAL-LIFE SUMMIT IN ICELAND.**"
- Single transferable vote. NDAs. An annual summit. For a video game (cf. our faction web; governance as
  emergent need, Rust Q93).
### WHY THEY FOUGHT: THE ACADEMIC ANSWER
- A peer-reviewed paper on B-R5RB: the author "**INDUCTIVELY THEORIZES THAT THE BATTLE OF B-R5RB RESULTED
  FROM THE NULL-SEC'S ANARCHIC STRUCTURE, PLAYER COMMITMENT TO THEIR RESPECTIVE CORPORATIONS, MEASURABLE
  ECONOMIC VALUE, AND RISK ASSOCIATED WITH PERMANENT LOSS.**"
- Four ingredients: NO REFEREE, REAL BELONGING, A PRICE TAG, and PERMANENT LOSS. That's the recipe (cf. our
  faction web + survival accounting + death-math + Megaton law; the four conditions for stakes).
### THE GRIEFING IS ORGANIZED, IDEOLOGICAL, AND ANNUAL
- "Groups like **CODE. HAVE CONDUCTED ORGANIZED CAMPAIGNS, INCLUDING 'HULKAGEDDON' EVENTS SINCE 2010, WHICH
  SYSTEMATICALLY ELIMINATE INDUSTRIAL SHIPS TO ENFORCE BEHAVIORAL NORMS AGAINST PERCEIVED INEFFICIENCY.**"
- And "**BURN JITA**" — annual invasions that blockade the main trade hub; in 2023 they "**DESTROYED OVER
  1,000 VESSELS IN A SINGLE DAY TO SOW CHAOS AND DETER COMPLACENCY.**"
- Players formed an organization whose PLATFORM is that mining is morally lazy, and they enforce it with
  violence, annually (cf. our faction web; the faction with an ideology about OTHER PLAYERS' habits).
### THE WAR CHANGED THE ECONOMY FOR MONTHS
- "**RESOURCE SCARCITY EMERGED AS PLAYERS SOUGHT TO REBUILD LOST FLEETS. INCREASED DEMAND FOR CRAFTING
  MATERIALS DROVE PRICES UP, AFFECTING SUPPLY CHAINS**... **ALLIANCES LEARNED THAT INVESTING IN DEFENSIVE
  CAPABILITIES BECAME MORE CRUCIAL, RESHAPING THEIR ECONOMIC PHILOSOPHIES.**"
- Wars in EVE "**BLOSSOM WITH ALL THE SAME PROPAGANDA, DIPLOMACY (AND ESPIONAGE), SUPPLY-CHAIN LOGISTICS AND
  MILITARY TACTICS THAT MIGHT FUEL A REAL-WORLD CONFLICT, TAKING MONTHS TO RESOLVE AND CHANGING ENTIRE
  INDUSTRIAL PATTERNS ACROSS THE STAR MAP**" (cf. our logistics + survival accounting + city-builder).

===============================================================================

  but it's really **AN ONLINE INTERGALACTIC SPREADSHEET MANAGER WITH MORE MICROMANAGEMENT THAN STARSHIP
  BATTLES.**" The legendary stories are the 0.1%; the median hour is inventory math. LESSON (ours): the
  anecdotes a system generates are not the same as the experience it delivers — most of our district's hours
  must be good, not just its highlight reel.
- THE STORIES ARE BETTER THAN THE GAME, AND THAT'S A PROBLEM: every article about EVE is about ~8 incidents
  from 20 years. Most players never see one. LESSON (direct): if our emergent systems only pay off at
  legendary scale, 99% of players get the spreadsheet and none of the legend.
- THE ONBOARDING IS INFAMOUS: "the most fascinating AND INTIMIDATING game ever made." EVE's new-player
  retention is a decades-long unsolved problem. LESSON (ninth confirmation this run): the on-ramp is not
  optional.
- THE BEST CONTENT REQUIRES A SECOND JOB: "**FOR MANY PLAYERS, INVOLVEMENT IN A MASSIVELY MULTIPLAYER ONLINE
  GAME CAN BE LIKE A SECOND JOB**" — the heist took ELEVEN MONTHS of real infiltration work. LESSON: the
  scale that makes it legendary makes it inaccessible.
- THE HEIST WORKED BECAUSE IT COST A REAL PERSON REAL MONTHS: the flip side of "$16,500 in stolen assets" is
  that real humans lost real years of their life's leisure. CCP allowed it. LESSON (sharp, ours): a system
  that permits genuine cruelty gets genuine stories AND genuine casualties — our conscience system operates
  on AI, which means we get to CHOOSE the cruelty level. That's a responsibility, not a limitation.
- IT NEEDS 7,000 REAL PEOPLE, PERMANENTLY: single shard, persistent, 23 years, thousands of humans.
  Everything above is impossible for us to copy directly. LESSON (the Rust lesson Q93 repeated at scale): we
  must MANUFACTURE this out of AI + fold, not borrow it from a server.
