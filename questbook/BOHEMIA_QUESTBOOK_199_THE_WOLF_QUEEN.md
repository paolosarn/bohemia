## 0 CORE IDEA

**A queen who reigned centuries ago — so consumed by ambition that she practiced necromancy on her own subjects and warred against her own family to keep power — was finally put down and her spirit contained, but a cult is now working to resurrect her, and her plan for return is not to build anew but to reach forward across the centuries and take power through the living descendants of her own royal line, so the player must hunt her spirit through the catacombs and stop a dead tyrant from possessing the future of the very bloodline she once ruined.**

Potema, the Wolf Queen, was the most notorious schemer of her age: a monarch whose hunger for power led her to raise the dead, sacrifice her subjects, and turn on her own kin in a civil war that scarred the realm. She was defeated and her spirit sealed. But death did not end her ambition; it only paused it. A necromantic cult labors to bring her back, and Potema, even as a disembodied spirit, is already scheming — her aim is to return to power not as a foreign conqueror but through her own descendants, the current living members of her royal line, using the legitimacy and the bodies of her bloodline to complete the reign death interrupted. The player is enlisted to stop the resurrection: to pursue her spirit through catacombs and crypts, sever the cult's work, and ultimately confront and destroy what remains of her before she can anchor herself in the living family that is, in a sense, her own future self. The dead tyrant is trying to eat her own descendants to keep being queen.

The machine is that the past does not want to rest; it wants to be the future, and the specific horror is an ancestor who would consume her own bloodline to keep reigning. Potema is not a stranger threatening the realm from outside; she is the realm's own history, refusing to stay history, reaching forward to possess the descendants who carry her blood. The quest literalizes the thing every dynasty fears: that the founder's ambition, the ancestor's hunger, does not die with the ancestor but lies in wait to seize the living generations, that the past is not a settled foundation but a predator that wants the future's body. The living descendants are both her victims and her intended vessels — the future she should have protected as an ancestor, targeted instead as a parasite. The player's job is to make the dead stay dead: to defend the present from a past that refuses its place, to stop a bloodline from being devoured by its own root. And the quest's honesty is that some ancestors are not foundations to build on but hungers to be guarded against, and that the most dangerous thing a dynasty can inherit is a forebear who never learned to let the future be its own.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the past does not always want to rest as a foundation — sometimes it wants to be the future, to reach forward and possess the living generations, and the most dangerous inheritance is not a debt or a curse but an ancestor whose hunger never died, who would consume their own descendants to keep reigning; some forebears are not to be honored but guarded against, because they never learned to let the future be its own.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — POTEMA'S "RETURN THROUGH HER DESCENDANTS" PLAN IS UNDERDEVELOPED, SO THE HORROR STAYS ABSTRACT.** The chilling core (an ancestor possessing her own bloodline) is more stated in lore than dramatized in the quest, which plays largely as a dungeon-crawl spirit-hunt — so the specific dread of the past eating its own future can pass the player by, buried under catacomb combat.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's horror is an ancestor consuming their own line, that plan must be DRAMATIZED, not just lore'd — a descendant shown at risk, the possession made felt — so the dread lands as the family's future in danger, not a codex note under a dungeon (#184's the-cycle-must-be-felt law).

**F2 — THE LIVING DESCENDANTS ARE NEARLY ABSENT, SO THE STAKES HAVE NO FACE.** The bloodline Potema targets barely appears as characters, so "she'll consume her own descendants" is an abstraction — the quest would cut far deeper if the player knew a specific living heir whose body and future are on the line.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if the stakes are an ancestor devouring her descendants, at least one descendant must be KNOWN — a specific future at risk — so the horror has a face and the defense has someone to protect (#190's the-stakes-need-a-face law).

**F3 — THE QUEST RESOLVES AS A BOSS KILL, FLATTENING THE PAST-WON'T-REST THEME INTO A GHOST-KILL.** Because Potema is destroyed in a fight, the profound theme (the past reaching to be the future) can reduce to a standard undead boss encounter — the idea that some ancestors must be perpetually guarded against loses to a clean one-time kill that says "handled."
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's theme is that the past refuses to rest, the resolution must carry that it's GUARDED against, not merely killed once — a residue, a vigilance, a sense the hunger could recur — so "the dead stay dead" reads as an ongoing duty, not a closed boss fight (#184's the-cycle-doesn't-fully-close law).
