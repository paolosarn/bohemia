# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 96: HAD TO BE ME / SOMEONE ELSE MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN IT WRONG (Priority: Tuchanka, Mass Effect 3)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where the scientist who helped engineer a species-wide
sterility plague has to decide whether to cure it, and then walks into a tower he knows will kill him,
singing. Complete event flow, the Dalatrass's offer, the truck ride, the Shroud, all four outcomes (cure /
sabotage-and-lie / talk him down / kill him), the "I made a mistake" branch, the honest flaws, and Bohemia
ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION (the atrocity committed by good people with good data), our
MEDICINE currency, our conscience system, + our Megaton law. BioWare (Mac Walters, Patrick Weekes; voice:
Michael Beattie). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Priority: Tuchanka," a MAIN quest in Mass Effect 3 (2012). Background: the GENOPHAGE is a
bioengineered plague that made krogan births fail — deployed centuries ago by the salarians to end a war the
krogan were winning. It didn't sterilize them. It made almost every pregnancy die. Mordin Solus, a salarian
scientist, worked on the team that UPDATED it. He is now on your ship, and he built a cure.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE PERPETRATOR IS THE ONE WHO FIXES IT, AND HE'S NOT SORRY THE WAY YOU WANT HIM TO BE (the character —
  STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION): Mordin helped maintain a plague that kills children before they're born, at
  species scale, for generations. He defends it with data. He also cannot stop thinking about it. His whole
  arc is a man discovering that being RIGHT and being GOOD are different questions, and that he only ever
  audited the first one.
  - The genophage is the best-written atrocity in games because it was committed by careful people, for
    defensible reasons, with a real threat on the other side of the ledger. Nobody in it is a monster (cf.
    our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic + Network; the harm done by competent people with a
    spreadsheet).
- THE QUEST IS A REFERENDUM ON WHETHER YOU CAN AUDIT A GENOCIDE (the structure): the Dalatrass (salarian
  head of state) offers Shepard a deal — **SABOTAGE THE CURE, AND SALARIAN SUPPORT IS YOURS.** "**WHEN THE
  DALATRASS OFFERS YOU THE DEAL TO SABOTAGE THE CURE, ACCEPT IT (OR STAY SILENT).**"
  - So the war-effort math says: keep the plague running, get a fleet. The moral math says: fix what your
    people did. The quest refuses to make either one obviously correct (cf. our Megaton law + faction web;
    the deal that pays in exactly the currency you need).
- AND THE BEST VERSION OF THE QUEST IS WHEN THE GAME PROVES *HIM* WRONG (the branch nobody expects): under
  specific conditions — Eve (the reasonable krogan leader) dead, Wreav (a warlord) in charge — Shepard can
  make a LOGICAL argument, and Mordin, the man who has justified this for years with data, **checks the
  data.**
  - "**Shepard will argue: 'WITH EVE DEAD AND WREAV IN CHARGE, THIS WON'T LEAD TO A RENAISSANCE. IT WILL
    LEAD TO WAR.' Mordin, RELYING ON LOGIC, WILL PAUSE. HE WILL LOOK AT THE DATA. HE WILL REALIZE YOU ARE
    RIGHT.**"
  - And then the line: "**I MADE A MISTAKE. FOCUSED ON BIG PICTURE. BIG PICTURE MADE OF LITTLE PICTURES. TOO
    MANY VARIABLES. I... I AM NOT SURE.**"
  - A character whose entire personality is CERTAINTY, saying *I am not sure* (STARTLINGLY our conscience
    system + [READ]; the man who can be argued out of it, only with evidence).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE SETUP (the cure exists because of a hostage)
- The cure requires a living krogan female who survived salarian experiments. She's called EVE. She is
  reasonable, funny, and dying, and she is the only reason the krogan future looks survivable.
- Mordin has spent the game caring for her. The cure is real. The Shroud — a planet-scale atmospheric
  dispersal tower on Tuchanka — is the delivery mechanism.
### STAGE 1 — THE DALATRASS'S OFFER (the deal is made in front of everyone)
- The salarian head of state tells Shepard the truth as she sees it: the krogan will do this again. Cure
  them and you're handing the galaxy its next war. Her offer: **sabotage the Shroud, and the salarian fleet
  joins the war.**
- "**WHEN THE DALATRASS OFFERS YOU THE DEAL TO SABOTAGE THE CURE, ACCEPT IT (OR STAY SILENT).**" Silence is
  a choice here. The game counts not-objecting as agreeing (cf. our conscience system; the complicity of
  saying nothing).
### STAGE 2 — THE TRUCK (the quest's cruelest design decision)
- On the drive to the Shroud, you are in a vehicle with the krogan. If you took the deal, you now have a
  ride with the people you're about to betray, and the game gives you the OPTION TO TELL THEM.
- "**DO NOT REVEAL THE SABOTAGE TO THE KROGAN IN THE TRUCK.**" Confessing changes everything. Staying quiet
  is an active, sustained lie that takes several minutes of pleasant conversation (cf. our companions +
  conscience system + Megaton law; the betrayal you have to keep choosing).
### STAGE 3 — THE TOWER (the point of no return, at a console)
- "**AT THE FINAL TOWER CONSOLE, MORDIN WILL PREPARE TO DISPERSE THE CURE. YOU MUST TRY TO STOP HIM.**"
- The Shroud is damaged. Dispersing the cure means someone has to go up the tower manually and stay there.
  Mordin knows this. He starts walking.
- **THE LINE.** When Shepard tries to stop him, Mordin's answer is the most quoted thing in the trilogy:
  **"HAD TO BE ME. SOMEONE ELSE MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN IT WRONG."**
  - It's not heroism. It's PEER REVIEW. He is the only person qualified to do the last step correctly, and
    the last step kills you, so he does it. The man's ethic to the end is competence (cf. our AMALGAMATION +
    conscience system; expertise as a death sentence).
- And he dies **singing** — a Gilbert-and-Sullivan patter song he's been humming all game. The tower
  explodes. The cure goes out.
### STAGE 4 — THE FOUR OUTCOMES
- **A — CURE IT.** Mordin climbs, disperses, dies. Krogan future opens. Salarian support is lost.
- **B — SABOTAGE, AND HE FINDS OUT.** Mordin realizes the Shroud has been rigged and goes up anyway to fix
  it. Shepard can **shoot him in the back** at the elevator. He dies knowing, and the game does not let you
  look away from it.
- **C — TALK HIM DOWN (the hardest, the best).** Requires Eve dead and Wreav in charge. Shepard's argument
  is EVIDENCE, not sentiment: "**WITH EVE DEAD AND WREAV IN CHARGE, THIS WON'T LEAD TO A RENAISSANCE. IT
  WILL LEAD TO WAR.**" Mordin **pauses. Looks at the data.** "**I MADE A MISTAKE. FOCUSED ON BIG PICTURE.
  BIG PICTURE MADE OF LITTLE PICTURES. TOO MANY VARIABLES. I... I AM NOT SURE.**"
  - "**HE WILL AGREE TO FAKE THE CURE DEPLOYMENT. THE SHROUD WILL DISPERSE A PLACEBO.**" He lives. He goes
    into hiding. He spends the rest of his life having lied about the one thing he cared about.
  - Note the design: the ONLY way to save him is to convince him that his life's redemption is a bad idea.
    The reward for the "good" outcome is a broken man (cf. our Megaton law + no-clean-answer).
- **D — THE PLACEBO NOBODY KNOWS ABOUT.** The krogan believe they're cured. The lie holds for now. Wrex, if
  alive, finds out eventually.
### STAGE 5 — THE CONDITIONS ARE THE POINT
- "**ONLY UNDER THESE SPECIFIC CONDITIONS WILL MORDIN REALIZE THAT CURING THE GENOPHAGE MIGHT BE A
  CATASTROPHIC ERROR.**"
- You cannot talk Mordin down if Eve is alive. You cannot talk him down if Wrex leads the krogan. The
  argument only works when it's TRUE. The persuasion check is gated on the actual state of the world, not on
  a charisma stat (STARTLINGLY our [READ] + conscience system; the speech check that requires facts).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE BEST BRANCH IS GATED BEHIND CROSS-GAME BOOKKEEPING: talking Mordin down requires Wrex dead (usually a
  Mass Effect 1 decision), Eve dead (a Mass Effect 2 decision about a research lab), and specific ME3
  choices. Most players will never see the "I made a mistake" scene, and can't, because of something they
  did in a different game five years earlier. LESSON (ours): a fold that carries state across generations
  must not bury its best scene behind a combination lock nobody knows they're setting.
- THE MORAL FRAMING QUIETLY REWARDS THE CURE: mechanically, curing gives krogan war assets; sabotaging gives
  salarian ones. But the SCORE is close and the game's sympathies are not — the music, the framing, and
  Mordin's death all argue for the cure. LESSON (the Megaton law): if your presentation makes one branch the
  hero's ending, you don't have a choice, you have a test with a wrong answer.
- "HAD TO BE ME" IS SO GOOD IT ATE THE QUEST: the scene is so famous that the underlying argument — is a
  bioweapon that prevents a war a defensible act? — gets remembered as "the one where Mordin sings." LESSON:
  when a moment is that strong, the THEME rides on it or gets lost behind it.
- THE KROGAN ARE WRITTEN AS RIGHT: Wreav is a cartoon warlord and Eve is a saint, which means the salarian
  position (they'll do it again) is only true if you deliberately kill the good krogan. The game stacks the
  deck and then asks you to weigh it. LESSON (sharp, ours): if a faction's argument is only correct when the
  world is artificially arranged, it's not an argument, it's a puzzle state.
- SILENCE-AS-CONSENT IS UNDER-SIGNPOSTED: "accept it **(OR STAY SILENT)**" — many players didn't realize
  saying nothing to the Dalatrass locked them into complicity. LESSON: our conscience system must make
  passive complicity LEGIBLE, or it's a trap rather than a choice.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the atrocity with a spreadsheet + the expert who has to die to fix it
===============================================================================
This is the single best "good people did this" quest in the medium, and it's aimed straight at our
AMALGAMATION and our MEDICINE currency. It's also the cleanest demonstration that a persuasion check should
require EVIDENCE, not a stat.
- W1/W2 (the perpetrator fixes it + the atrocity was committed by careful people with real reasons — our
  AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic + NETWORK): the top port. The genophage works as fiction because
  NOBODY IN IT IS A MONSTER: the salarians faced a species that was winning a galactic war, they chose a
  measure that ended it without extermination, and they've defended it with data for centuries. Mordin
  maintained it and can justify every step. Our AMALGAMATION is the same shape — built to help, defended
  with real numbers, and the harm is structural rather than malicious. Steal the CHARACTER too: the
  Network's Mordin, the person who helped build the thing and is the only one who could unbuild it, who is
  not sorry the way the player wants him to be, and who is RIGHT about most of it (ties our AMALGAMATION +
  Amalgamation threat logic + NETWORK + faction web + MEDICINE currency + death-math + [READ] +
  Liberate/Respect/Become; a top-tier port).
- W6/W7 (the persuasion check requires FACTS + the certain man says "I am not sure" — our [READ] +
  conscience system): STEAL this outright, it's our whole [READ] thesis in one scene. You cannot talk Mordin
  out of curing the genophage with charisma. You can ONLY do it when the argument is literally true — "**with
  Eve dead and Wreav in charge, this won't lead to a renaissance, it will lead to war**" — and he responds by
  DOING WHAT A SCIENTIST DOES: "**Mordin, relying on logic, will pause. HE WILL LOOK AT THE DATA. He will
  realize you are right.**" Then: "**I MADE A MISTAKE. FOCUSED ON BIG PICTURE. BIG PICTURE MADE OF LITTLE
  PICTURES. TOO MANY VARIABLES. I... I AM NOT SURE.**" Punk — our [READ] system should gate persuasion on
  RECOVERED EVIDENCE, not a stat: you can only change a faction's mind if you're holding something true.
  And "big picture made of little pictures" is a thesis line for our whole game: the Amalgamation optimizes
  the aggregate; the district is where the little pictures live (ties our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded +
  conscience system + AMALGAMATION + faction web + standing + district + no-clean-answer).
- W3/W4/W5 (the deal pays in the currency you need + silence is a choice + the betrayal must be maintained
  in conversation — our survival accounting + conscience system + companions): bank all three as the
  complicity package. (a) The Dalatrass offers A FLEET during an extinction war. In our game, that's the
  faction that offers MEDICINE or ELECTRICITY when the dynasty is dying — the deal that's evil and also
  correct arithmetic. (b) "**Accept it (or stay silent)**" — not objecting IS accepting; our conscience
  system should count passive complicity (with the flaw noted below: make it legible). (c) And the TRUCK
  RIDE is the best staging decision in the quest: after you agree to betray them, you spend several minutes
  riding with them, chatting, able to confess at any moment. The lie isn't a menu click, it's a duration
  you have to sit inside. Our companions + district can do that (ties our survival accounting + THREE
  CURRENCIES + MEDICINE + faction web + conscience system + companions + Megaton law + district).
- W8/W10 (the sacrifice is peer review + you can shoot him in the back — our AMALGAMATION + fold +
  death-math): bank both. (a) "**HAD TO BE ME. SOMEONE ELSE MIGHT HAVE GOTTEN IT WRONG**" is not a hero
  line, it's a COMPETENCE line — he is the only person qualified to do the last step, and the last step
  kills you. A character in our fold who dies because he's the only one who can do it correctly is worth
  more than any noble speech, and our generational structure means the NEXT dynast inherits the job he
  couldn't hand off. (b) And the game's darkest permission: if he learns of the sabotage he goes up ANYWAY,
  and stopping him means shooting a friend in the back at an elevator — no cutaway (ties our AMALGAMATION +
  fold + generational persistence + companions + death-math + conscience system + combat dial).
- W9 (saving him costs him everything — our Megaton law): bank the shape. The only way Mordin LIVES is to
  convince him that his life's one redemptive act is a mistake. He fakes the deployment, the Shroud sprays a
  PLACEBO, and he spends the rest of his life in hiding having lied about the one thing he cared about. The
  "good" outcome produces a broken man and a lie at civilizational scale. That's our Megaton law at full
  strength: every branch pays in a different currency and none of them is clean (ties our Megaton law +
  no-clean-answer + conscience system + persistent consequence + Liberate/Respect/Become + fold).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) A FOLD THAT CARRIES STATE MUST NOT BURY ITS BEST SCENE BEHIND A COMBINATION LOCK —
  the "I made a mistake" branch needs decisions from TWO earlier games; most players can't reach the quest's
  best moment and never know why (our generational persistence + fold: carried state should OPEN content,
  not hide it); (b) if your presentation makes one branch the hero's ending (music, framing, a death scene),
  you don't have a choice, you have a test with a wrong answer (Megaton law); (c) if a faction's argument is
  only correct when the world is artificially arranged (Wreav the cartoon warlord, Eve the saint), it's a
  puzzle state, not an argument — our 13 factions each need a position that's true in the DEFAULT world; (d)
  passive complicity must be LEGIBLE or it's a trap ("accept it, or stay silent"); and (e) when a moment is
  as strong as "had to be me," the theme rides on it or gets lost behind it.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**MORDIN SOLUS** — wants to fix his own arithmetic. Helped modify the genophage with good data and clean logic; has re-run the numbers with a hostage in them and gotten a different answer. Will trade: his life, cheerfully, and will not frame it as a trade. Will never say: "I was wrong" in the language of guilt — he says "I made a mistake" in the language of science, which is somehow worse and better at once. FUNCTION: the atrocity's author as its cure's delivery mechanism.

**THE DALATRASS** — wants the genophage kept, and offers Shepard salarian support to SABOTAGE the cure and lie about it. Will never say: "genocide"; her vocabulary is all "stability." FUNCTION: the deal made in front of everyone (the offer arrives on an open channel of sorts) — temptation with witnesses.

**EVE (BAKARA)** — wants her species to deserve the cure as much as receive it. FUNCTION: the counter-argument to every cynical read; the krogan future, present in the truck, being a person.

**WREX** — wants his people back, and will burn the galaxy's war effort down if he learns of sabotage. FUNCTION: the verification layer (cf. Q3 Keely, Q2 Heck): SOME LIES HAVE WITNESSES WITH GUNS.

**SHEPARD** — holds the war-asset ledger and the friendship ledger and cannot make them one ledger. FUNCTION: the decider who must do it at a console, in front of the man climbing the tower.
