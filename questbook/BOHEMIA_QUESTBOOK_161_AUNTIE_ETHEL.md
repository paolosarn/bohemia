# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #161 — "AUNTIE ETHEL (THE BARGAIN THAT COSTS YOU AN EYE)"
**Game:** Baldur's Gate 3 (2023)
**Studio:** Larian Studios
**Quest:** The Hag / Auntie Ethel — a kindly old woman in the swamp offers to help with your affliction, and the price is your eye, or a girl's freedom, or a bargain you'll spend the rest of the game regretting. The genre's cleanest deal-with-a-monster-who-plays-nice.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Baldur's Gate 3 catalogue; corpus anchor Q16/Q126 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia needs a monster who greets you as a helpful auntie and prices her help in permanent mutilation — the honest-devil machine (#126) with a kindly face and a body-horror invoice. Auntie Ethel is the specimen: she offers to cure what afflicts you, the offer is real, the price is your eye forever, and refusing means a fight to save a girl she's grooming into her next self. It is the seductive bargain of #157 and the fair-terms-catastrophic-outcome of #126, wearing an apron.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A grandmotherly stranger in the swamp offers to fix the horror growing in your skull, and the fix is real — she just wants your eye, forever, and if you say no you learn the sweet old woman is a hag farming a captive girl into her successor.**

The party is afflicted (a parasite in the brain), and Auntie Ethel — warm, folksy, maternal — appears offering to help. She can, genuinely, ease the affliction. The price is an eye: she'll take it, permanently, a real and visible mutilation the character carries for the rest of the game. The player can accept (a permanent debuff, a bargain kept, the help delivered) or refuse. Refusing cracks the disguise: Ethel is a green hag, and in her swamp lair she holds Mayrina, a grieving pregnant woman Ethel is manipulating with the promise of resurrecting her dead husband — and grooming, in the way of hags, toward becoming the next hag. The rescue is a fight through an illusion-maze where Ethel wears the faces of the people the player would hesitate to hurt, including Mayrina herself, threatening to kill the hostage if pushed.

The machine is the kindly monster whose offer is honest and whose price is your body, nested inside a rescue where mercy is weaponized against you. Ethel does not lie about the eye — she is a fair-dealer in the #126 mode, and the horror is that the terms are clear and the cost is permanent and the player might take it anyway because the affliction is real. And when refused, she fights dirty in the exact register Bohemia cares about: illusions of innocents, a hostage held, the player's own reluctance to harm turned into her shield. Mayrina, the captive, is the future the player is trying to prevent — a woman being slowly made into the thing that captured her, her grief the lever.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the help that costs you a piece of your body forever is still help, and the sweetest voice offering it may be farming someone else into the next version of itself while you decide.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### AUNTIE ETHEL — the hag who deals fair
- **What she wants (stated):** to help with your affliction, for a small price.
- **What she wants (real):** your eye (power, a piece of you), and to be left to her swamp and her captive; the help is genuine, the price permanent.
- **What she'll trade:** real relief from the parasite, for a real mutilation — an honest, terrible bargain.
- **What she will never say out loud (until refused):** that she is a hag, that Mayrina is being groomed, that the folksy warmth is the lure.
- **Function:** THE HONEST-DEVIL WITH A KINDLY FACE — fair terms, catastrophic cost, offered with a smile.

### MAYRINA — the captive being made into the next hag
- **What she wants:** her dead husband back, which Ethel dangles.
- **What she'll trade:** her judgment, her freedom, her self — grief is the lever Ethel pulls.
- **What she will never say out loud:** that some part of her knows the deal is poison, and she wants it anyway.
- **Function:** THE FUTURE the player is preventing: a griever being slowly turned into the thing that holds her.

### THE ILLUSION-MAZE — mercy weaponized
- Ethel's lair, where she wears innocent faces and threatens the hostage.
- **Function:** THE DIRTY FIGHT: the player's reluctance to harm turned into the hag's armor.

### THE AFFLICTION — the reason the bargain tempts
- The parasite in the party's heads, real and frightening.
- **Function:** THE PRESSURE that makes a permanent price thinkable — the temptation's fuel.

### THE EYE — the invoice made flesh
- The permanent mutilation Ethel charges.
- **Function:** THE COST, visible and forever: the bargain you carry on your face.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE KINDLY OFFER [MANDATORY]
Auntie Ethel appears, warm and maternal, offering to help with the party's affliction. The monster arrives as a grandmother.

### STAGE 2 — THE PRICE [MANDATORY, forks]
Her terms: an eye, permanently. Accept (real help, real mutilation) or refuse (crack the disguise).

### STAGE 3 — THE DISGUISE CRACKS [MANDATORY if refused]
Ethel is a green hag; her swamp lair holds Mayrina, a grieving pregnant captive being groomed. The auntie becomes the monster.

### STAGE 4 — THE ILLUSION-MAZE [MANDATORY if fought]
Through her lair, Ethel wears innocent faces, threatens to kill Mayrina, turns the player's mercy into her weapon. The dirty fight.

### STAGE 5 — THE RESCUE OR THE BARGAIN [MANDATORY]
Save Mayrina (defeat Ethel without letting the hostage die) — or, on the accept path, walk away eyeless and helped, the captive left to her fate.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 core terminal outcomes — accept the bargain (eyeless, helped, Mayrina abandoned) or refuse and rescue (fight the hag, save the captive) — plus the sub-branch of whether Mayrina survives the fight.**

- **ACCEPT THE BARGAIN:** the eye given, real relief delivered, a permanent visible mutilation carried the rest of the game; Mayrina left to Ethel's farming. WRITES: the honest deal kept; a piece of the player gone forever; a captive abandoned.
- **REFUSE AND RESCUE:** the disguise cracks, the illusion-maze fought, Ethel defeated; Mayrina saved if the player threads the hostage danger. WRITES: the hag stopped; the future un-farmed; no permanent price, but a hard fight.
- **THE HOSTAGE SUB-BRANCH:** push too hard and Mayrina dies in the crossfire — mercy's weaponization made real; the rescue can fail into a killing.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** every path answers "what will you pay, and to whom" — the branches are the honest-devil's bargain taken or the farmed future rescued, and the game prices both.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE EYE'S COST IS MECHANICALLY TRIVIAL.** The permanent mutilation is presented as a grave bargain but amounts to a minor, largely cosmetic debuff — so the "you gave up an eye forever" horror is undercut by the mechanics treating it as a shrug. The narrative price and the systemic price don't match.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a bargain sold as a permanent grave cost must BE one mechanically — a real, felt, lasting consequence the player lives with — or the honest-devil's terms are theatre. If the price is trivial in play, the temptation is fake; match the systemic cost to the narrative one (Q126's honestly-priced-power law, restated).

**F2 — MAYRINA'S GROOMING IS TOLD MORE THAN SHOWN.** The horror that Ethel is farming Mayrina into the next hag is powerful but delivered mostly through implication and lore; the player doesn't witness the grooming's mechanism, so the "future being made" dread lands thinner than the concept.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a captive is being MADE into the thing that holds them, the making must be shown — the mechanism of the grooming witnessed, the grief-lever pulled on screen — so the future-being-farmed horror is felt. The fold can show the slow becoming across time (#155/#159's show-don't-tell law).

**F3 — REFUSING IS OBVIOUSLY CORRECT.** Because the accept-path's cost is trivial-but-permanent and the refuse-path rescues an innocent and stops a monster, refusing is the clearly-superior choice for most players — the "honest bargain" is undercut by there being no real reason to take it. The dilemma tilts.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the honest-devil's bargain must be genuinely TEMPTING — the help must solve something the refuse-path can't, at a cost the player might actually pay — or it is a false choice. Make the bargain a real fork, not an obvious trap (the #156/#157 make-the-ends-tempting law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE MONSTER IS A GRANDMOTHER. Ethel arrives as folksy warmth, not menace — the most disarming disguise, because the player wants to trust the maternal voice offering help.
W2. THE OFFER IS HONEST. She does not lie about the eye; the help is real and the price is real. The horror is the #126 honest-devil: fair terms, permanent cost, taken with full knowledge.
W3. THE PRICE IS YOUR BODY, FOREVER. Not gold, not a favor — a piece of you, visible, permanent. The bargain is written on the character's face for the rest of the game.
W4. THE AFFLICTION MAKES THE PRICE THINKABLE. The parasite is real and frightening, so a permanent mutilation becomes a genuine temptation — pressure is what turns a monstrous price into a considered one.
W5. REFUSAL CRACKS THE DISGUISE. Saying no reveals the hag beneath the auntie — the sweet voice was the lure, and declining is what shows you the monster.
W6. THE CAPTIVE IS THE FUTURE YOU'RE PREVENTING. Mayrina, groomed toward becoming the next hag, is what the player is fighting to stop — a griever being made into her captor, the horror of the cycle.
W7. THE FIGHT WEAPONIZES MERCY. Ethel wears innocent faces and holds the hostage; the player's reluctance to harm becomes her armor — combat that punishes the compassionate.
W8. GRIEF IS THE LEASH. Mayrina is held by the promise of her dead husband's return — the hag farms sorrow, the most reliable lever, which is the Amalgamation's exact method.
W9. THE HOSTAGE CAN DIE. Push too hard and Mayrina is killed in the fight — the rescue can fail into a killing, so mercy's weaponization has real teeth.
W10. THE BARGAIN RIDES ALONG. The eye, if taken, is carried the whole game — the quest hands the player a permanent consequence, not a closed story, a standing reminder of what help cost.
