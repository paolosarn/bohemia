# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 14: LADY BOYLE'S LAST PARTY (Dishonored)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: event, staging, stage-by-stage flow, the randomized-target
deduction, the chaos system, the "non-lethal doesn't mean merciful" beat, and Bohemia ports. This is
the medium's model for the IMMERSIVE-SIM ASSASSINATION SANDBOX + NON-LETHAL-THAT-ISN'T-CLEAN + a
SOCIAL-DEDUCTION target hunt. Widely cited as Dishonored's best mission. Reference only; Paolo does
not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Mission: "Lady Boyle's Last Party," mission 5 of Dishonored (Arkane, 2012). Corvo (a supernatural
assassin) infiltrates a lavish MASKED BALL to identify and eliminate the Lord Regent's secret
mistress/financier — one of three masked sisters, all called "Lady Boyle." Designed under Harvey Smith.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE PREMISE / STAKES
===============================================================================
- WHO: three BOYLE SISTERS — Waverly (paranoid), Esma (seductive), Lydia (mysterious) — one of whom
  secretly FUNDS the corrupt Lord Regent's government. Corvo must find WHICH and eliminate her.
- THE HOOK: from the prior mission's interrogation, you learn only the surname "Boyle" and that the
  target will be at tonight's masked ball. You infiltrate the party to DEDUCE the specific target.
- THE TWIST — RANDOMIZATION: which sister is the target (and which colored dress she wears) is
  RANDOMIZED per playthrough — the canonical target is Waverly, but the game shuffles it, so you must
  actually INVESTIGATE each run, not memorize. The deduction is genuine.
- THE CHAOS FRAME: Dishonored tracks a CHAOS meter (how much death/disorder you cause) that changes the
  world's state and ending. This mission can be done as a bloodbath (HIGH chaos) or a ghost (LOW chaos)
  — the approach is a moral/systemic choice with downstream consequences.
- THE STAKES: a target elimination — but staged as a glittering social space where murder is the CRUDE
  option and the elegant paths are deduction, seduction, and a deeply unsettling non-lethal deal.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE FULL STAGE-BY-STAGE FLOW
===============================================================================

### STAGE 1 — THE FREEDOM (a sandbox, not a corridor)
- You arrive at the Boyle mansion during the party and can MOVE FREELY among the masked guests
  (unarmed-looking, blending in) — the open structure gives you time to formulate a plan. STAGING: the
  whole mansion + grounds is a simulation you explore at will; upstairs is OFF-LIMITS (Walls of Light,
  guards) — cross it wrong and you're hostile. The level trusts you to author your own approach.

### STAGE 2 — THE DEDUCTION (who is the target? — social investigation)
Multiple ways to identify the target, all diegetic:
- GOSSIP: chat with guests; the more DRINKS you buy them, the more they reveal — narrowing the suspects.
- MISS WHITE: bribe her with a drink -> she names two sisters' identities + dress colors (revealing all
  three by elimination) — but NOT which is the target.
- THE DIARIES: sneak upstairs (off-limits) and read the Boyle sisters' DIARIES to learn personalities +
  the target's identity.
- LORD BRISBY: wait for his arrival -> he flatly TELLS you the target's name AND unlocks the non-lethal
  option (see Stage 4).
- THE PERSONALITY-CHECK FLIRT: converse with a sister and correctly GUESS which sister she is -> her
  approval (each rejects a drink offer in a character-specific way: Waverly "unfashionable to drink at
  my own party," Esma "already seeing two of you," Lydia "disappointed at your lack of creativity") —
  reading their personalities IS the deduction.
- THE BRUTE SHORTCUT: you can murder EVERYONE at the party and win by process of elimination — the
  crude, high-chaos "solution" the sim permits (and quietly judges via the chaos meter).

### STAGE 3 — THE ELIMINATION OPTIONS (lethal + the elegant paths)
- LETHAL: kill the target any time, any way — a grenade in the ballroom, a blade in the dark, feed her
  to rats. Fast, loud, HIGH chaos. (If you assassinate in plain sight, the Overseers' music boxes
  BLOCK your magic + hinder you — the space fights back.)
- STEALTH-LETHAL: isolate and kill quietly (converse, lure to a private room, strike) — lower chaos.

### STAGE 4 — THE NON-LETHAL OPTION (the infamous "regrettably dark" beat)
- LORD BRISBY — a partygoer in a creepy scarecrow mask with an UNHEALTHY OBSESSION with the target —
  offers a deal: DELIVER her to him (converse with her, tell her her life's in danger, lead her to the
  cellar, choke her unconscious, place her on his boat) and he'll ensure "she's never seen again."
- THE HORROR: you don't KILL her — you hand her to her deranged stalker to be spirited away forever
  (implied captivity/worse). Designer Harvey Smith calls it "regrettably dark." The COMMUNITY debate is
  fierce: many argue this non-lethal option is MORE cruel than a clean death ("non-lethal doesn't mean
  merciful"). The game refuses to make "spare" = "moral."
- ALT NON-LETHAL: sign the Boyle GUEST LEDGER (reported to the Lord Regent) — a subtler non-lethal
  resolution with its own downstream dialogue next mission.

### STAGE 5 — THE ESCAPE + THE CHAOS TALLY
- Extract past guards to Samuel's boat. The mission's CHAOS level (bodies, alarms) feeds the world-state:
  low chaos -> a less bleak city/ending; high chaos -> more Weepers (plague zombies), grimmer guards,
  a darker finale. Your HOW echoes across the whole game.

===============================================================================
## 2. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE SANDBOX SPACE: a whole mansion + grounds you move through FREELY, blending with guests — the
    open structure lets you author a plan (deduce, seduce, sneak, slaughter). Freedom breeds the fun.
W2. RANDOMIZED TARGET = REAL DEDUCTION: which sister is the target is shuffled per run, so you must
    actually INVESTIGATE, not memorize — the mystery is genuine and replayable.
W3. SOCIAL DEDUCTION VIA DIEGETIC MEANS: identify the target by GOSSIP, DRINKS, DIARIES, personality-
    reading flirts — the investigation happens through the party's social fabric, not a quest-log clue.
W4. MULTIPLE KEYS TO THE ANSWER: gossip / Miss White / diaries / Brisby / personality-checks all lead
    to the target — many diegetic paths to the same knowledge (cf. NV Rule #1, Questbook 02/03).
W5. THE SPACE FIGHTS BACK: kill in plain sight and the Overseers' music boxes NULLIFY your magic — the
    environment has counters to the crude approach, nudging elegance. Systemic pushback.
W6. NON-LETHAL ISN'T CLEAN (the moral masterstroke): the "spare" option (hand her to her stalker) is
    arguably MORE monstrous than killing — the game DENIES the player a clean-conscience easy-out and
    forces them to own the grey. "Non-lethal doesn't mean merciful."
W7. THE CHAOS SYSTEM (HOW echoes): a meter tracking death/disorder changes the WORLD and ending — your
    method has systemic, world-wide consequences (more Weepers, grimmer city). Play-style = world-state.
W8. THE CRUDE SHORTCUT IS ALLOWED (and judged): you CAN murder everyone and win by elimination — the
    sim permits the brute path but quietly costs you via chaos. Freedom includes the ugly option.
W9. PERSONALITY AS THE PUZZLE: each sister rejects your drink in a character-specific line; reading WHO
    they are IS how you solve it — characterization doubles as the deduction mechanic.
W10. THE ELEGANT PATH IS RICHER: designer intent — the subdued methods are "so much more involved and
    exciting long-term" than the grenade; the game REWARDS subtlety with a better experience, not just
    a better score.

===============================================================================
## 3. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
- W1 (the sandbox space): a Bohemia infiltration SANDBOX — a single rich location (a faction gala, a
  Network facility, a Cartel den) you move through FREELY, blending in, authoring your own plan (ties
  the Whodunit closed-box, Questbook 07, + the KCD disguise, Questbook 08).
- W2/W3/W9 (randomized target + social deduction + personality-as-puzzle): a Bohemia target-hunt where
  WHO you're after is revealed by READING the social space (gossip, shared drinks, diaries, reading
  personalities) — and, optionally, randomize the specific target so deduction is genuine + replayable.
  Ties our [READ] + the Amalgamation face-thief (Q022) + the summit saboteur-spotting (Q039).
- W4 (multiple keys to the answer): the target's identity reachable by many diegetic paths (a bribe, a
  document, a personality-read) — investigation with multiple solutions (our reactivity spine).
- W5 (the space fights back): give Bohemia infiltration spaces COUNTERS to the crude approach (the
  Network's servers null your tricks near them; a warded zone; the Overseer-music-box equivalent) —
  systemic pushback that nudges elegance without forbidding force.
- W6 (non-lethal isn't clean): THE big steal — our Pacifist Path Law says non-lethal is ALWAYS
  possible, but Dishonored teaches non-lethal shouldn't always be MERCIFUL. Bank a beat where the
  "spare" option is morally grey or worse (hand a foe to a cruel fate, exile them to something awful) —
  deny the player a clean-conscience easy-out; force them to own the grey (sharpens Q043 betray, Q049
  jail verdicts, Q009 sacrifice). Non-lethal != moral.
- W7 (the chaos system): consider a Bohemia DISTRICT-CHAOS/darkness meter (ties Q019 march tally) where
  cumulative violence/disorder shifts the world-state + ending (grimmer district, warier factions) —
  HOW you play echoes citywide (validate against our fold; scope carefully).
- W8 (crude shortcut allowed + judged): let the sim PERMIT the brute path (slaughter your way to the
  answer) but COST it via the chaos/darkness state — freedom includes the ugly option, with consequence.
- W10 (the elegant path is richer): make our subtle solutions MORE rewarding to play (more content,
  more story, better long-term outcome) than the brute one — reward subtlety with richness, not just a
  cleaner score (our pacifist-bonus philosophy, validated).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE THREE LADIES BOYLE (Esma, Lydia, Waverly)** — one funds the Lord Regent; all three want to survive their own party. Identically masked, identically dressed in different colors, and the TARGET IS RANDOMIZED per playthrough. Each will trade: party flirtation, gossip about her sisters, and — if pressed right — self-incriminating vanity. FUNCTION: a deduction puzzle wearing three identical masks. The cast IS the lock.

**THE GUESTS** — want to be seen at the season's best party while the city dies of plague. Each masked guest is a hint dispenser and an alibi surface. FUNCTION: the social terrain. Notably: several guests will speak to the masked assassin AS a guest — the infiltration is diegetic.

**LORD BRISBY** — wants Lady Boyle. Romantically. Desperately. Will trade: the quest's infamous third option — give her to him, alive, unconscious, forever. Will never say: what her life with him will be, because he has not thought past wanting. FUNCTION: the non-lethal door that is worse than the knife, and knows it ("she will trouble you no more").

**CORVO** — wants the target dead or removed with minimum chaos. FUNCTION: the ghost at the feast.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees)

NODE: THE_FLOOR — entry: invitation accepted, mask on
  A party as a dialogue system: every guest node is optional, unmarked, and cumulative toward IDENTIFYING which sister is the target.
  > (ask a guest about the sisters)  [gate: none] -> hint (color-coded gossip: who drinks, who flirts, who funds)
  > (find the guest list / diary upstairs) [gate: none, trespass risk] -> hard evidence
  > (ask a sister directly, flattering her) [gate: knows:her_vanity] -> she may CONFIRM HERSELF to a charming stranger. The target can be talked into identifying herself at her own death-party. The best single node in the mission.
  THE WALK LOADS THE TALK again (Q9's law) — but here the "walk" is ITSELF conversation. The gossip matrix from Q7, dressed for a ball.

NODE: BRISBY_PROPOSAL — the cellar, entry: he recognizes the assassin's purpose
  > "Why should I trust you?"   [gate: none] -> B_PLEADS (love, he says; the word does a lot of unexamined work)
  > "Deal."                     [gate: none] -> the non-lethal branch opens: deliver her UNCONSCIOUS to his boat.
  > "No."                       [gate: none] -> knife/exile-by-other-means remain.
  NOVERB: "What happens to her after?" — CANNOT BE ASKED. The game withholds the aftermath question at the exact node where a decent person would ask it, and the LOW-CHAOS meter will score the kidnapping as the CLEAN option. The system's arithmetic and the scene's ethics point in opposite directions ON PURPOSE (the designers have owned this reading): "non-lethal" is not "moral," and the score that says otherwise is the indictment. THE QUESTBOOK'S SHARPEST CASE AGAINST OUTCOME-SCORING: even a good meter (chaos, which is systemic, not moral) gets read as a conscience by players, and the game exploits the misread. Bohemia: no meter, so no misread to exploit — Bohemia's version must build the discomfort into the AFTERMATH instead (a later letter in the Fold, unsigned, in her hand). 
  
## V2-C THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 4 × randomized-target multiplier.
B1 — TARGET KILLED, party in panic or none the wiser (staging-dependent). High-or-low chaos by witnesses/bodies.
B2 — WRONG SISTER KILLED: the quest FAILS FORWARD — target survives, consequences propagate, no reload prompt. The deduction had stakes because error is SURVIVABLE and PERMANENT.
B3 — BRISBY: target abducted, low chaos, the "clean" horror. WRITES: boyle_taken.
B4 — GHOST: identified, isolated, choked, hidden — removed without a death (knockout routes). The mastery tier.
STRUCTURAL FINDING: RANDOMIZING THE TARGET converts walkthrough-following into actual play. Bohemia's role-casting (.bq @ROLE) gets this free: cast the tribunal's "presiding," the vault's "witness," the party's "target" per run, and no wiki can pre-chew the deduction. The roguelite justification for the alias system, demonstrated by a 2012 immersive sim.
