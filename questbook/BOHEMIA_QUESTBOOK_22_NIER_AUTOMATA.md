# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 22: SIDE QUESTS AS PHILOSOPHICAL DESPAIR (NieR: Automata)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the side-quests-reinforce-the-theme MODEL, several torn-down
examples, the machines-imitating-humanity engine, the nihilism-into-hope arc, the multi-playthrough
structure, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for SIDE CONTENT THAT
DELIVERS THE THEME (not relief, like Yakuza Q13 — the DARK inverse: side quests that deepen the DREAD
and the central question). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: NieR: Automata (PlatinumGames/Yoko Taro, 2017). Androids 2B, 9S, and A2 fight machine lifeforms
on an Earth abandoned by humanity. Told across multiple REQUIRED playthroughs (routes A/B/C/D/E) that
recontextualize everything. Central question: "WHAT IS IT TO BE HUMAN?" — and whether existence has
meaning when your reason for fighting is a lie.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- SIDE QUESTS AS THEMATIC REINFORCEMENT: NieR's ~60 side quests aren't relief or filler — the good ones
  DEEPEN the game's despair and its central question. The machines (and androids) keep IMITATING human
  behavior — love, family, faith, war, revenge — and it keeps going HORRIBLY WRONG, each side quest a
  small tragedy that asks "is there even a point to existing?" Side content as a philosophy delivery
  system (the DARK inverse of Yakuza's tonal-relief substories, Q13).
- THE MULTI-PLAYTHROUGH RECONTEXT: you replay the same events as different characters (2B's route, then
  9S's, then A2/9S's) — each pass REFRAMES what you did, revealing the enemy's inner life and your own
  complicity. Knowledge across playthroughs changes MEANING, not just difficulty (cf. loop-mastery Q11/
  Q18/Q20 — but here the "loop" is narrative perspective).
- THE ARC: existential NIHILISM (your war is meaningless, humanity is already extinct, everyone imitates
  a dead ideal) that RESOLVES into a hard-won HOPE (Ending E: meaning is something we CHOOSE to assign;
  "we're all in the same boat, so we might as well stick together"). Despair earned, then transcended.

===============================================================================
## 1. TORN-DOWN EXAMPLES (side quests that deliver the theme)
===============================================================================

### "AMNESIA" / THE YORHA BETRAYERS (the reveal that implicates you)
- 9S is asked by a Resistance member to investigate the death of her friend; the woman lost her memories
  in a shockwave and wants only to know what happened to her comrade. Upon regaining her memories, she
  reveals she's a YoRHa E-TYPE — an executioner whose sole purpose is to KILL TRAITORS. The gentle
  amnesiac you pitied is a designed killer. 9S asks 2B about the E-designation; 2B says "some things are
  better left unknown" (a devastating dramatic-irony beat — 2B herself is an E-type who repeatedly kills
  9S). A side quest that quietly detonates the main relationship's central secret.

### "THE WANDERING COUPLE" (love imitated, gone wrong)
- Two machines imitate a married couple; the "husband" machine goes berserk. Machines mimicking human
  love collapse into violence — a micro-tragedy on "can imitation become real, and does it matter?"

### "EMIL'S MEMORIES" / "EMIL'S DETERMINATION" (grief across games)
- A recurring character (a lonely, self-sacrificing weaponized child from the prior game) whose quest
  surfaces centuries of accumulated loss — a long-buried grief the attentive player unearths (cf. the
  recurring mirror figure Q10, foreshadow-in-hindsight).

### THE OPERATOR QUESTS (6O / 21O — the humanity of the "UI")
- Your radio operators (voices that give you orders) get personal quests revealing inner lives — the
  disembodied "interface" characters turn out to be people who fear, hope, and die. Humanizing the
  functional.

### THE PATTERN (why they land)
- Each is a small parable of a lifeform IMITATING humanity (love, family, faith, purpose) and finding
  either tragedy or a fragile, chosen meaning. The side quests are the game ARGUING ITS THESIS in
  miniature, over and over, from different angles (cf. Planescape's quests-as-answers Q12 — here the
  answers are mostly bleak, which is the point).

===============================================================================
## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- MAIN-PLOT PACING SAG: some players find the MAIN story a loop of "follow orders, go here/there,
  unrelated tasks, a big machine attacks, repeat" with weak mid-game progression — the THEMES and SIDE
  content carry it more than plot momentum. LESSON: if your side content carries the theme, the main
  spine still needs forward drive (don't let the A-plot idle while the sidequests do the philosophy).
- REQUIRED REPLAYS ARE A HARD SELL: the meaning depends on replaying routes A->E; players who stop after
  one route miss the whole point. LESSON: gate the payoff on replay ONLY if each pass stands on its own
  AND the game signals there's more (NieR does — but it's still a barrier).
- SOME QUESTS ARE ITEM-COLLECTION FILLER: only ~20 of ~60 side quests are the strong thematic ones; the
  rest are fetch/collect. LESSON: don't dilute — a few dense thematic side quests beat 60 shallow ones
  (the Yakuza density lesson, Q13, restated).

===============================================================================
## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. SIDE QUESTS DELIVER THE THEME (dark inverse of relief): the strong side quests DEEPEN the dread +
    the central question rather than relieving it — side content as a philosophy delivery system.
W2. THE IMITATION ENGINE: every side quest is a lifeform IMITATING humanity (love/family/faith/revenge)
    and it going wrong or bittersweet — one thematic engine generating endless micro-parables.
W3. THE SIDE QUEST THAT DETONATES THE MAIN SECRET: "Amnesia" quietly reveals the E-type executioner
    truth that reframes 2B & 9S's whole relationship — a "side" quest carrying a core revelation.
W4. DRAMATIC IRONY VIA A SIDE BEAT: "some things are better left unknown" lands because WE (may) know
    2B's secret — the side quest weaponizes player knowledge against the characters.
W5. HUMANIZING THE FUNCTIONAL: the radio-operator "UI" characters get inner lives — even the interface
    is a person; nothing is just a system (cf. our unrecorded ledger's "everyone is someone").
W6. RECONTEXT ACROSS PLAYTHROUGHS: replaying as different characters REFRAMES the same events —
    perspective as progression; meaning changes, not difficulty (cf. loop-knowledge Q18/Q20).
W7. NIHILISM -> CHOSEN MEANING (the arc): earn the despair (your war is a lie, everyone imitates a dead
    ideal), then transcend it — meaning is something we ASSIGN; hope after honesty (cf. Planescape's
    regret Q12, our Liberate "let go").
W8. THE ATTENTIVE UNEARTH THE GRIEF: recurring-character quests (Emil) reward the player who digs into
    long-buried loss (cf. foreshadow-in-hindsight Q10/Q11).
W9. SYMPATHY FOR THE "ENEMY": side quests make you care about the machines you're told to kill — the
    designated enemy is humanized until killing feels wrong (cf. monster-is-a-victim, Bloody Baron Q01).
W10. THE THEME IS A QUESTION, NOT A LECTURE: "what is it to be human?" is asked through situations, never
    preached — the player arrives at the despair/hope themselves (cf. Planescape Q12, Forgotten City Q20).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
NieR is the DARK counterpart to Yakuza (Q13): where Yakuza side quests RELIEVE, NieR side quests DEEPEN.
We want BOTH registers — and NieR's "imitation of a lost humanity" engine is uncannily close to our
Amalgamation (data-portraits imitating the dead) and our post-collapse survivors imitating a dead
economy/society.
- W1/W2 (side quests deliver the theme + the imitation engine): bank a set of Bohemia side quests that
  DEEPEN our central question (what survives when a life ends / what do we owe the dead) — micro-parables
  of survivors or Amalgamation-fragments IMITATING the lost world (a family reenacting pre-collapse
  routines; a machine/upload mimicking a dead person; a cult imitating old-world faith) and finding
  tragedy or fragile meaning. One thematic engine -> endless small stories (ties Q018 cult, Q012 mind-
  edits, Q033 the imitation of normalcy).
- W3/W4 (a side quest that detonates the main secret + dramatic irony): plant a Bohemia SIDE quest that
  quietly reveals a core truth (a companion's hidden nature, the Amalgamation's real origin) and
  weaponizes player knowledge against the characters ("some things are better left unknown").
- W5 (humanize the functional): give our "interface"/functional NPCs (a caravan radio voice, a ledger-
  keeper, a plant operator) inner lives + small quests — nothing is just a system; everyone is someone
  (validates the unrecorded ledger's whole premise).
- W6 (recontext across playthroughs/generations): let our GENERATIONAL replays REFRAME earlier events —
  a later dynast re-experiences an ancestor's quest from the OTHER side (the enemy's view, the victim's
  view) — perspective as progression (ties our fold + succession; the Strange-Man-across-generations Q10).
- W7 (nihilism -> chosen meaning): our arc can EARN despair (the collapse, the futility, the dead you
  couldn't save — Pathologic Q21) then transcend into CHOSEN meaning (Liberate: we let the dead go and
  choose to live) — hope after honesty, never cheap uplift (validated by NieR + Planescape).
- W9 (sympathy for the enemy): keep humanizing the Network/Amalgamation until opposing them costs the
  player emotionally (our Amalgamation is already a sympathetic grave; NieR is the master of "the enemy
  is us"). The machines-are-people lesson = our uploads-are-people core.
- W10 (theme as question not lecture): ask "what do we owe the dead" through SITUATIONS, never preach —
  the player arrives at the answer (our life-lesson-under-every-decision rule, validated).
- AVOID (the flaws): keep the MAIN spine driving (don't let side quests carry ALL the theme while the
  A-plot idles); keep thematic side quests DENSE (few strong > many fetch — Yakuza+NieR agree); and if
  our generational "replays" reframe meaning, make each generation stand on its own.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**2B** — wants the mission to stay a mission. Will trade: efficiency, distance, one repeated sentence ("emotions are prohibited"). Will never say out loud: her designation. She is an E-type; her recurring assignment is the boy beside her; the game hides its cruelest fact in a UNIT NUMBER. FUNCTION: the secret the side quests keep almost detonating.

**9S** — wants to KNOW. A scanner: curiosity is his hardware and his death sentence, over and over. Will never say out loud (until route C): what knowing everything did to him. FUNCTION: the audience's proxy, punished for being the audience.

**THE AMNESIAC (the "Amnesia" quest)** — wants her memories back and her friend's death explained. She gets both, and the gentle victim the player pitied stands revealed as a designed executioner, an E-type. FUNCTION: the side quest that detonates the main secret (W3). Her reveal reframes 2B for any player paying attention; 2B's "some things are better left unknown" is dramatic irony with a body count (W4).

**THE WANDERING COUPLE (two machines)** — want to be married, the way the dead species was married. The imitation collapses into violence. FUNCTION: the imitation engine in miniature (W2): love copied without a copier's rights.

**EMIL** — wants to remember why he's still here, centuries after everyone he loved. FUNCTION: the grief the attentive unearth (W8): a recurring child-weapon whose quests are archaeology on loss itself.

**6O / 21O (the operators)** — want, respectively: flowers on a dead Earth, and a family assignment she will never get. FUNCTION: humanizing the functional (W5): the UI turns out to be people; the voice that gives orders can die.

**THE MACHINES (the enemy, collectively)** — want what their makers' enemy's makers wanted: family, faith, a village, a king, peace. FUNCTION: the designated target, humanized until the trigger feels wrong (W9). The war is androids imitating dead humans killing machines imitating dead humans.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees; lines paraphrased, structure exact; route annotations matter — the same node MEANS differently per playthrough, which is the file's whole finding)

NODE: AMNESIA_OPEN — the Resistance camp, entry: route B (9S playable), the woman with no memories
  She asks gently: find out what happened to my friend.
  > "I'll look into it."            [gate: none] -> the investigation: shockwave, records, a designation
  > "What do you remember?"         [gate: scanner] -> fragments; the gentleness is real, which is the trap's whole upholstery

  > "What's an E-type?"  (to 2B, after) [gate: quest done] -> "Some things are better left unknown." THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK: the player who has finished route C hears a confession; the player who hasn't hears a deflection. SAME NODE, TWO MEANINGS (W6).
  NOVERB: 9S cannot ask 2B "Are you one?" The game withholds the direct question because the tragedy requires the not-asking. Cross-ref #126's removed comfort verb: here the removed verb is a PROTECTION, and it protects the executioner.

NODE: COUPLE_TROUBLE — the city ruins, entry: two machines, imitated wedlock
  The "wife" asks for help: her "husband" has changed.
  > (help them reconcile)           [gate: none] -> the imitation deepens; the vocabulary of love arrives without its brakes
  > (the berserk turn, scripted)    -> the fight the quest was always building to; the couple's script had violence in it because the SOURCE MATERIAL did
  > (report what happened)          [gate: none] SILENCE option absent by design: the quest makes you say it out loud to someone
  WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the comfortable read that imitation is cute. The machines are not pretending badly; they are pretending ACCURATELY, and that is the horror.

NODE: OPERATOR_6O — the radio, entry: mid-routes, a voice that has only ever been a function
  She asks for a picture of a desert rose. Off-mission. Personal.
  > (find the flower)               [gate: traversal] -> a voice on a satellite cries over a photograph; the interface has an inner life (W5)
  > (ignore it)                     [gate: none] SILENCE -> the orders continue, voiced by someone the player chose not to know
  THE CRAFT: the game gives its UI a want. After this quest, every mission briefing is read by a PERSON. The cost is paid later, at route C's tower, in full.

NODE: EMIL_DIG — the commercial facility, entry: found the lift, the mask, the song
  > (pursue his memories)           [gate: attention across the whole game] -> centuries of loss, unearthed in pieces (W8)
  > (buy his wares and go)          [gate: none] SILENCE -> he remains a shop with a face; the grief stays buried, and the game does not resent you for it
  LOCKS: nothing. The deepest sorrow in the file is entirely optional, which is why it lands: witnessed grief must be VOLUNTEERED for (the #126 Hope lesson, inverted to archaeology).

NODE: ROUTE_RECONTEXT — no speaker; the structure itself (W6)
  The same boss fight, replayed as 9S: the machine you killed as 2B was SPEAKING, and route A's player couldn't hear it.
  > (replay: hear the enemy)        [gate: route B+] -> meaning changes, not difficulty; complicity arrives retroactively
  > (stop after route A)            [gate: none] TRAP -> the banked flaw: the player who stops holds half a game and believes it whole. The barrier is real; NieR pays it because each route stands, barely, alone

NODE: ENDING_E — the credits, entry: everything lost, all routes done
  The credits themselves attack. Impossible alone, on purpose.
  > (accept help from strangers' save data) [gate: other players' sacrificed saves] -> the thesis, mechanized: meaning is assigned, together; "same boat, stick together" (W7)
  > (offer YOUR save to a stranger) [gate: ending reached] -> the game's final dialogue option deletes your data for someone you will never meet
  THE FINALE IS A LEDGER-READ, SEVENTH CONFIRMATION, and the ledger is OTHER PLAYERS' generosity: the bleakest game in the file bank ends on the most literal mechanical hope in the medium.
