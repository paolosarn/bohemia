# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #149 — "THE SAD SAINT (THE CITIZEN WEB)"
**Game:** Vampyr (2018)
**Studio:** Dontnod Entertainment
**Quest:** The Sad Saint of the East End — Sean Hampton, shelter-keeper of the Docks, the district's PILLAR citizen, bitten and turning — and the machine around him: a city where every named citizen is simultaneously a person, a web of relationships, a district-health input, and a PRICED MEAL the player can drink for power. The queue asked for "Vampyr citizen-webs"; this file mines the web through its flagship case.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (queue-named; source-pulled 7/17 — see SOURCES)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Vampyr is the only shipped game whose ENTIRE population is built on the mechanic Bohemia's settlements need: every citizen has a name, a schedule, relationships, secrets that unlock via investigation (hints), a wellness state feeding district health — and an XP VALUE, because the player character is a predator and progression's cheapest currency is neighbors. It is conscience-no-karma-bar built as an economy: the game never scores mercy; it just prices people and watches. Sean Hampton is the system's masterpiece case because his quest makes the player adjudicate the fate of the one citizen the district leans on.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**Every person in the city is worth something to somebody and worth XP to you, the district gets sick when its people do, and the quest is what happens when the man holding a district together becomes the thing you are.**

Vampyr's London runs on the citizen web: districts (the Docks, Whitechapel, the West End...) each carry a HEALTH STATE aggregated from their named citizens — every one interviewable, schedule-bound, connected (this one's brother, that one's creditor, the nurse who loves the thug). Learning a citizen's HINTS (secrets found by talking to their connections, reading their letters, following their nights) raises the quality of their blood — the game's most sinister mechanic: KNOWING A PERSON MAKES THEM MORE NUTRITIOUS. Dr. Jonathan Reid, new-made vampire, levels fastest by drinking citizens, and the well-studied ones pay best. Mercy is never scored; it is simply expensive, forever, in the only currency the combat cares about.

Sean Hampton is the Docks' PILLAR — the citizen whose state anchors the district. A lay saint: runs the shelter, feeds the hopeless, guards the sewer-folk (the skals, London's untouchable lesser vampires — he protected their entrances long before he knew what he was protecting). Then the violence at Pembroke Hospital, the flight, and the truth: Sean has been bitten and is TURNING — becoming a skal, hunger and all, while still trying to run a soup kitchen. The quest walks the player through his flight, his shelter, his sewer connections (Old Bridget's hidden community), and lands on the adjudication: what is done with a saint who now eats what his flock is made of?

Three answers, three worlds: EMBRACE him (drink him — his XP is enormous, the district loses its pillar, health drops hard); SPARE him as he is (the merciful-looking option and the WORST one — untreated, his hunger wins; the shelter becomes a charnel house; he murders the peaceful skals and his own poor; the game punishes sentiment-without-followthrough harder than it punishes predation); or GIVE HIM YOUR OWN BLOOD — the option that must be EARNED by having learned him (his hints, his story), and PAID FOR out of the player's own progression: the predator's currency, spent backward, to buy a man mastery over what he became. He returns to the shelter. The paper prints a kind headline. The district holds.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** knowing people is power over them — every intimacy is a loaded thing — and the only clean use of it is the one that costs you and pays them.**

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### SEAN HAMPTON — the pillar, turning
- **What he wants (stated):** to keep the shelter open. To keep being what the Docks needs.
- **What he wants (real):** to be told what he now is doesn't disqualify him from grace. He is a theological crisis wearing a soup ladle.
- **What he'll trade:** everything but the shelter. Even turning, his refusals are all on his flock's behalf.
- **What he will never say out loud:** the hunger's voice. The quest's horror is watching a genuinely good man start to hear it and keep serving dinner.
- **Function:** THE FORK, and the district's load-bearing wall. The design put its hardest choice on its most structurally important citizen ON PURPOSE.

### DR. JONATHAN REID (the player) — the predator with a license
- **What he wants:** a cure, answers, and — the game's honest underside — LEVELS, whose cheapest source is the population he's sworn to heal. Doctor and predator in one body; the tension IS the game.
- **Function:** THE ADJUDICATOR whose own appetite is a party to every case.

### THE DOCKS — the district, cast as a character
- **What it wants:** its pillar back. Health aggregates from citizens; lose too many (or the wrong ones) and the district TIPS — hostile, lost, its remaining names swallowed.
- **Function:** THE STAKES, ambient and arithmetic. A neighborhood as a health bar built out of people.

### OLD BRIDGET AND THE SEWER SKALS — the hidden congregation
- **What they want:** to stay hidden and unhunted. Sean guarded their doors when he was only a man; his fate cascades into theirs (spared-untreated, he turns on exactly them first).
- **Function:** WITNESSES and downstream cost — the quest's consequences flow into a community most players barely know exists. The web's edges extend past the player's sight, and the design bills them anyway.

### THE HINT SYSTEM — the intimacy ledger, cast per corpus convention
- Secrets learned from connections, letters, eavesdropping — each hint raising blood quality and dialogue depth simultaneously.
- **What it will never say out loud:** which purpose you're learning FOR. The same knowledge feeds the healer's insight and the predator's appetite; the interface refuses to distinguish.
- **Function:** THE DUAL-USE MACHINE. Investigation as both care and stalking, one verb.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE INCIDENT AT PEMBROKE [MANDATORY]
Sean, brought to the hospital, flees after violence. Entry: the quest opens with its subject already running from what he did — the pillar introduced via his first failure.

### STAGE 2 — THE PURSUIT THROUGH HIS LIFE [MANDATORY, investigation]
Following Sean means interviewing his web: shelter regulars, the poor he feeds, the trail to the sewers. The chase doubles as the hint-harvest — the game makes LEARNING him the same verbs as HUNTING him, and never lets the player forget the equivalence (his blood quality ticks up with every secret).
MISSABLE: hints. Skipping them silently closes the third option later — the design gates redemption behind having actually bothered to know the man.

### STAGE 3 — THE SEWER TRUTH [MANDATORY]
Old Bridget's hidden community; Sean's history as their door-guard; the bite and what he's becoming. What changes: the contract of the quest — this stops being a manhunt and becomes an adjudication.

### STAGE 4 — THE ADJUDICATION [MANDATORY — the fork]
The conversation at the shelter (tree in section 3): embrace, spare-as-is, or give-your-blood (gated on hints; priced in the player's own XP). One scene, three worlds.

### STAGE 5 — THE DISTRICT'S VERDICT [MANDATORY, systemic, delayed]
The web cashes the choice over following nights: embrace → the Docks' health drops hard (pillar gone; stable only if enough citizens remain well); spare-untreated → the shelter locks, corpses on spikes, Sean a crazed skal murdering the peaceful sewer-folk and his own poor — the district bleeds names; give-blood → Sean returns to work, the paper prints "Portrait of a Sad Saint," the district steadies. The verdict arrives as WORLD-STATE, not as a score screen — the game's whole morality is delivered in district arithmetic and newspaper ink.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE V-1 — the web, interviewed. Speakers: Sean's connections. Entry: stage 2, distributed.
Every interview is dual-use by construction:
> ask after Sean's habits [gate: none] -> trail data AND a hint: his blood quality ticks. The interface displays the appetite's gain alongside the investigation's — the game refuses to let care launder itself
> press a connection's own secret [gate: their hints] -> deeper access, and THEIR blood quality ticks too. The web rewards thoroughness in both currencies at once; the player cannot investigate innocently
> (leave people unstudied) [gate: none] SILENCE -> mercy's quiet form: an unlearned citizen is a worse meal and a locked door. Ignorance as a chosen kindness, priced in options
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: innocence of motive. Every fact learned is worth XP the moment resolve slips — the system holds the player's knowledge in escrow for their worst self.

### NODE V-2 — the adjudication. Speaker: Sean, at the shelter, turning. Entry: stage 4.
He does not run. He asks, in effect, what the doctor intends for the monster — the patient conducting his own triage.
> "Come here, Sean." (EMBRACE) [gate: none — always available, always enormous XP] -> the drink. The game shows the number. TRAP-shaped for the build-minded: the pillar is the district's richest vein, and the design WANTS the player to feel the pull (the whole game's thesis in one temptation)
> "Live with it. I won't touch you." (SPARE AS-IS) [gate: none] -> the sentiment option, and the system's cruelest lesson: unhelped, the hunger wins — the massacre at the shelter, the peaceful skals butchered. Mercy without treatment is abandonment wearing mercy's coat. THE ONE CHOICE DOING THE WORK: the game's deepest law is that GOOD INTENTIONS ARE NOT A VERB
> "Take my blood." (GIVE) [gate: KNOWLEDGE — his hints learned; and PRICE — the player's own progression spent] -> mastery: he remains a skal and remains HIMSELF. The redemption is gated on intimacy and paid in the predator's currency, reversed. The system's one clean exit costs the system's one real coin
> (say nothing) [gate: none] SILENCE -> he fills it with the sermon he's been rehearsing — grace, monsters, whether the two exclude. DIFFERENT TEXT by hints held: with his story learned, the sermon names its own failures; unlearned, it stays abstract. Even his last speech pays out on the intimacy ledger
NOVERB "I'll cure you" — no line exists. There is no cure in this game, for him or the player, and the format's silence about it is the setting's honesty: the choice set is manage, end, or abandon. Never fix.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: embrace costs the district; spare costs the sewers and the shelter; give costs the PLAYER, directly, in the game's only power currency. The fork's three prices land on three different ledgers — the design's masterstroke.

### NODE V-3 — the district, replying. Speaker: London, over following nights. Entry: stage 5.
> (no options — the web speaks in state: health bars, locked doors, a newspaper, who is missing from their route)
The verdict arrives ambient: citizens comment, the paper prints, the district's meter moves. Judgment delivered entirely through world-reaction — no score, no alignment tick, no dialogue acknowledging a system at all.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing further. The bill was itemized at V-2; this node is the receipt arriving by post.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 3 terminal fates times the district's continuous health state; one option existence-gated on hints; consequences billed across 3 ledgers (district, sewers, player progression).**

- **EMBRACE:** Sean dies as food. Player gains the pillar's XP; the Docks lose their anchor — health falls hard, holds only if the rest of the web stays tended. The predator's route, priced in neighborhood.
- **SPARE AS-IS:** the false mercy. Sean's hunger wins: the shelter massacre, the peaceful skals murdered, the district bleeding names, the man himself lost to madness anyway. Strictly worse than embrace for nearly everyone including Sean — the game's boldest authored judgment, delivered without a single line of narration.
- **GIVE BLOOD:** hints learned + player XP spent → Sean masters it, returns to the shelter, the kind headline, the district steadies. The only route where the saint stays a saint — bought with the player's own power.
- **THE WEB'S MULTIPLIER (cross-cutting):** every fate lands on a district already shaped by the player's other meals and mercies. The same choice in a healthy Docks and a bled one produces different worlds — no fork in this game is local.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE ECONOMY OVERPOWERS THE THEME FOR POWER-PLAYERS.** The XP gap between abstaining and feeding is so large that a no-feed run is a difficulty mode (underleveled for every boss), while a feeding run trivializes combat. Widely criticized: the moral economy becomes a DIFFICULTY SLIDER, and players choose their ethics by their appetite for boss fights.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** conscience must never be the difficulty setting. Price mercy in TEXTURE (options, relationships, world-state, the fold's read) — never in raw combat viability. If the merciful player is mechanically weaker, ethics collapses into masochism and predation into convenience; the dial's balance must hold at every conscience level.

**F2 — THE SPARE TRAP PUNISHES WITHOUT TELEGRAPHING.** Spare-as-is reads as the good option and detonates into the worst outcome, with little in-scene warning that untreated hunger MUST win. Players report feeling tricked rather than taught — the lesson (intentions aren't verbs) lands, but by ambush, and the massacre is discovered late or never connected.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the world must testify BEFORE the fork about what untreated conditions do (the sewer skals' own histories, a prior case, a doctor's warning) — the information available, the sentiment still tempting, the trap then FAIR. Bohemia's telegraphing law (#146 F3) extends to moral traps: the player may be wrong, never uninformed.

**F3 — THE WEB'S SCHEDULES ARE SHALLOW UNDER PRESSURE.** Citizens keep static routes and conversation loops; outside their quests they idle, and the "living district" reads as a diorama between story beats — the criticism that the web is a menu of meals wearing a neighborhood's face.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the citizen web must run on the world's clock, not the quest log's. Bohemia's beat-driven sim and patrol machinery (7/17 wiring) already move people by law; settlement citizens need the same treatment — schedules, absences, and reactions that tick with the world so the web stays alive between adjudications. (The #146 five-property checklist, applied to friendly institutions.)

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. EVERY CITIZEN IS PRICED. Named, scheduled, connected, and worth XP: the population is simultaneously the game's heart and its loot table, and the interface refuses to hide either fact. The most honest predation economy ever shipped.
W2. KNOWING PEOPLE MAKES THEM MORE NUTRITIOUS. The hint system fuses intimacy with appetite — investigation raises blood quality — so every act of care builds capacity for harm. Dual-use knowledge as a core loop, not a theme statement.
W3. THE DISTRICT IS A HEALTH BAR MADE OF PEOPLE. Aggregated wellness with tipping points turns neighborhoods into stakes without a single scripted consequence — the world's judgment is arithmetic the player can watch coming.
W4. THE HARDEST CASE IS THE LOAD-BEARING CITIZEN. Putting the fork on the district's pillar makes the moral choice and the systemic one the same choice. Structure and story fused at the exact point of maximum pressure.
W5. REDEMPTION IS GATED ON INTIMACY. The third option exists only if the player learned the man — the same hints that fatten his blood unlock his salvation. One ledger, two doors: the system's knowledge economy pays out in whichever direction the player's hands choose.
W6. THE CLEAN EXIT COSTS THE PLAYER'S OWN COIN. Giving blood spends progression backward. Mercy priced in the predator's currency is conscience-no-karma-bar in its purest shipped form: no meter moves; the player is simply POORER and the saint is simply saved.
W7. FALSE MERCY IS THE WORST BRANCH. Spare-as-is out-damages predation — the game's harshest authored opinion, delivered entirely through consequences: sentiment without treatment is abandonment. (Bank the fairness fix in F2; keep the opinion.)
W8. CONSEQUENCES BILL THREE LEDGERS. District, hidden community, player power — the fork's prices land on different accounts, so no single meter can summarize the choice. Judgment by distributed arithmetic beats any alignment system.
W9. THE VERDICT ARRIVES AS WORLD-STATE. Health bars, locked shelters, a newspaper headline: the game replies in city, never in interface. The Q139 loading-screen judge's benign twin — the world as the read-back surface.
W10. THE WEB EXTENDS PAST THE PLAYER'S SIGHT AND BILLS ANYWAY. The sewer skals — a community most players barely meet — pay for the fork's failure modes. Consequences that flow to the barely-known make the city feel infinite in exactly the way a settlement sim needs.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE SETTLEMENT WEB [W1, W3, W4 — the core port]
**System:** settlement systems / the 13 factions / survival accounting
The citizen web, adopted whole for Bohemia's settlements: named citizens with schedules on the beat (F3's law — the patrol/routine machinery is already wired), relationship edges, wellness states aggregating to settlement health with tipping points, and PILLAR citizens whose fates move the aggregate hard. The dark twin (citizens as XP) does NOT port — Bohemia's player isn't a predator — but the PRICED-PERSON principle does: every citizen carries value to factions, to the economy, to the Amalgamation's recording interests. Somebody always wants to spend people; in Vampyr it's the player, in Bohemia it's the world, and the player adjudicates. [PENDING, Paolo's call: pillar citizens per settlement — pure canon.]
```
@TALK pillar_case speaker=settlement_head entry=pillar_condition
  @SAY You know what she holds up. Half this place eats because she argues the ration board into shame.
  @SAY Now you know what she's carrying too. So. Doctor, judge, or neighbor. Which did you walk in as?
  @OPT "Tell me everything about her."      [gate: none] -> case_learn
  @OPT "What happens to you all without her?" [gate: none] -> case_stakes
  @OPT (say nothing)                          [gate: none] SILENCE -> case_stakes
  @NOVERB "I can fix her"
@END
```
**THE NOVERB IS THE PORT** (V-2's finding): manage, end, or stand by — never fix. Bohemia's world has no clean cures either.

### PORT 2 — DUAL-USE INTIMACY [W2, W5]
**System:** [READ] / the conduct book (#143) / faction mechanics
Learning a citizen (testimony, records, their web) unlocks BOTH care-verbs and harm-verbs for that citizen — the same knowledge opening the door to help them, leverage them, or sell them out, with the interface honest about the symmetry. The conduct book logs which door the knowledge went through. Intimacy as escrowed power is the web's deepest truth and it ports without the vampire.

### PORT 3 — CONSCIENCE IS NEVER THE DIFFICULTY SLIDER [F1's law — a balance gate]
**System:** the dial / progression
Hard rule for the dial's economy: no conscience-path may change combat viability class. Mercy prices in texture — options, standing, the fold's read, who answers your letters — never in the numbers the dial checks. Machine-checkable once progression tables exist: the validator diffs power curves across conscience-paths and rejects divergence beyond threshold.

### PORT 4 — THE INFORMED TRAP [W7 kept, F2 fixed]
**System:** quest structure / testimony systems
Bohemia keeps the false-mercy branch (sentiment-without-followthrough detonating) — the corpus's best-taught lesson — but the world testifies FIRST (#146 F3's telegraphing law): prior cases, witness accounts, a professional's warning, all available before the fork. Wrong remains possible; uninformed does not. The trap becomes a test of listening instead of an ambush.

### PORT 5 — THREE-LEDGER BILLING [W8, W10]
**System:** the fold / settlement health / hidden communities
Every settlement-scale fork prices across at least two distinct ledgers, and at least one consequence flows to a community the player has barely met (the web past sight, billed anyway). Compile-countable: fork specs declare their ledgers; single-ledger forks get sent back. [PENDING, Paolo's call: Bohemia's hidden communities — the sewer-skal slot is pure canon.]
