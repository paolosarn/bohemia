# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #144 — "THE CAPTAIN'S WILL (THE LEGACY FOLD)"
**Game:** Sunless Sea (2015)
**Studio:** Failbetter Games (Alexis Kennedy era)
**Quest:** the legacy system played as the quest it secretly is — a captain's death, the will that survives them, and the heir who sets sail with a marked-up chart. Plus the machinery that makes dying WORTH something: ambitions, officers, heirlooms, and the scion.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined from the whole-game teardown, Q66, per the mining-pool rule)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** the pool file called this "STARTLINGLY close to our fold," and it is the only shipped game where the death-to-heir handoff is an AUTHORED NARRATIVE BEAT with player choices inside it, not just a systems transfer. Bohemia's fold, succession, and generational persistence (GDD v4's sole home) need exactly this teardown: what a player chooses AT death, what crosses the line, what is deliberately forbidden to cross, and why the forbidding is where the meaning lives.

---

## 0 CORE IDEA

**Dying is a transaction with your next self, the game makes you negotiate it in advance, and the things that refuse to cross the line are what make the line real.**

Sunless Sea expects you to die. Fuel runs out in the dark, terror climbs until the crew mutinies or the captain's mind goes, the zee eats boats. The game's own framing calls a full history of play a chronicle of tragedies. And instead of softening that, it builds its centerpiece there: the LEGACY. Before or at death, the player shapes a will — an heir is named (a scion raised in advance, a rival, a relict, a debt-collector; each a different inheritance profile), and specific classes of things cross to the next captain: the CHART (the map's explored geography, the single most valuable possession in an exploration game), one heirloom class per legacy type (money, the ship, a weapon, officers' loyalty), and the run's banked story-fragments. Everything else — most money, most cargo, most PROGRESS in open storylines — is deliberately burned.

The design's genius is the refusal. A legacy that carried everything would make death a loading screen; a legacy that carried nothing would make it a punishment clock. Sunless Sea's line is drawn with intent: KNOWLEDGE crosses (the chart, the player's own learned dread of what lives where), RELATIONSHIPS partially cross (an officer will sail with your heir if the paperwork of affection was done), STUFF mostly dies with you. The next captain is poorer than the last and wiser than the first, which is exactly the shape of a real inheritance.

And it is a QUEST, not a menu: ambitions (the captain's chosen life-goal — wealth, a father's bones, the writing of a song) are picked at character creation and mostly die unfinished; the scion option means the player spends money DURING life to raise the child who will inherit; officers carry their own unfinished stories that can strand mid-arc across the line. The will is authored in advance by a hundred small decisions, then read at death by the systems, and the reading is the game's most Bohemian sentence: here is what a life amounted to, transferable portion thereof.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** you do not get to hand your children your victories. You get to hand them your map — and whether anyone who loved you will still answer their letters.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THE DYING CAPTAIN — the testator
- **What they want (stated):** the ambition picked at creation: wealth, the father's bones, the song, the crossing. The game's structural joke: most captains die with the ambition open.
- **What they want (real), by the endgame of a doomed run:** for the deaths to compound into something. The player's want and the character's fuse here — both are optimizing the will.
- **What they'll trade:** the run itself. Veteran play is HARVEST play: sailing to die well — banked secrets, a raised scion, a chart worth inheriting.
- **What they will never say out loud:** the game literally prices this: some knowledge is TOO HEAVY to will forward (see the searing-enigma class of secrets, and the things a captain takes to the grave because passing them on would mark the heir).
- **Function:** THE ESTATE. The character is the quest's raw material.

### THE HEIR — the beneficiary who becomes the player
- **What they want:** depends on who they are, and that IS the legacy choice: the SCION (raised, funded, loving — inherits most, including lineage continuity), the RIVAL (inherits the ship by claiming it, colder profile), the ACCOUNTANT/DEBT-shapes (inherit obligations with the assets).
- **What they will never say out loud:** that they inherit the player's HANDS. The mechanical skill and map-knowledge in the player's head is the true estate, and the game knows it — the chart is its diegetic token.
- **Function:** THE CONTINUITY. The heir is the fold made playable.

### THE CHART — the soul of the estate
- Cast as a character because the game treats it as one: it is the single named thing that ALWAYS crosses (if chosen), and its markings are the previous life's testimony — every island label is a survived mistake.
- **Function:** KNOWLEDGE INCARNATE. The inheritance that cannot be spent, only used.

### THE OFFICERS — the loyalty question
- Each carries a hidden story-arc (the pool file's officers-with-hidden-stories machine). At death, whether one sails for the heir is a function of paperwork done in life.
- **What they will never say out loud:** their arcs' endings, unless a captain finishes the arc — and an arc stranded mid-story at death STAYS stranded for that officer. Some stories die with the wrong captain.
- **Function:** WITNESSES and part-transferable bonds. The estate's emotional line-items.

### THE ZEE — the executor
- The dark that killed the testator is also what makes the estate mean anything: terror, fuel, and hunger price every mile of the chart.
- **Function:** THE COST, ambient and permanent. Nobody inherits calm water.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE AMBITION [MANDATORY, at creation]
Entry: new captain. The player picks the life-goal — the ambition — which quietly authors the will's terms: what this life will chase and, statistically, fail to finish.
MISSABLE: no. But its COMPLETION is the missable — most runs end testate-in-ambition, and the game is built so that this is normal, not failure.

### STAGE 2 — THE ESTATE-BUILDING [THE WHOLE RUN, freeform]
Every voyage adds to or spends the eventual will: chart markings (permanent estate), banked secrets and story-fragments (partially heritable), the scion raised with real money (the heir premium), officer arcs advanced (loyalty paperwork), terror managed (a mutiny or zee-madness death can cost parts of the estate the player assumed were safe).
The design's quiet masterstroke: NONE of this is presented as will-writing. It is presented as living. The will is the shadow the run casts.

### STAGE 3 — THE DEATH [EXPECTED, variably authored]
Fuel-dark, starvation (with the cannibalism ladder before it), terror's endings, combat. The game's deaths are mostly SLOW — the player usually sees the estate-relevant end coming for whole voyages, and the final trips of a doomed captain become de facto testament revisions: race home to bank a secret, marry off an officer's arc, buy the scion one more year.
MISSABLE: a quick death skips the deathbed housekeeping. The estate is whatever was already true.

### STAGE 4 — THE READING OF THE WILL [MANDATORY at death]
The legacy screen — but the pool file's insight is that this is a SCENE wearing a menu's clothes: choose the heir-shape, and the systems read out what crosses under that shape (chart / heirloom class / a fraction of wealth / an officer if bonded). Everything not named burns, on screen, itemized by absence.
Exit: the heir at dock, the inherited chart unrolled, the zee unchanged.

### STAGE 5 — THE SECOND LIFE'S FIRST VOYAGE [MANDATORY, the payoff stage]
The heir sails INTO the previous life's knowledge: the chart's labels, the player's own flinches at certain coastlines. Early-run terror is repriced by inherited geography. The game's fold thesis plays here without a word of text: the second captain is provably not starting over, and everything that proves it was earned by a dead person.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

**Formal note:** Sunless Sea speaks in storylets — prose vignettes with option lists — and the legacy machine's "conversation" is distributed: part storylet, part system screen, part the officers' arcs. The trees below render the actual decision architecture in the format's terms, per the same discipline as #141's book-as-dialogue.

### NODE L-1 — the ambition pick. Speaker: the game's opening prose. Entry: stage 1.
The candidate life-goals, each a different will-shape:
> "Wealth: retire rich or die trying" [gate: none] -> a run that banks MONEY, the least heritable substance in the game. The trap-shaped ambition: the goal whose pursuit builds the worst estate. Authored irony, confirmed by the design's own inheritance math.
> "Your father's bones: a search" [gate: none] -> a run that banks GEOGRAPHY and story-flags — high-heritability life. The sentimental goal builds the best estate; the game's values, stated in mechanics.
> "The song / the word: make something" [gate: none] -> banks story-fragments; partially heritable; finishing it is one of the game's rare true endings — a life that can actually complete.
> (later unlock ambitions gated on prior-life discoveries) [gate: KNOWLEDGE crossing generations] -> the fold feeding the fold: some life-goals can only be CONCEIVED by a dynasty, not a founder.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the run's shape. The ambition is the will's first clause, chosen before the player knows what wills are.

### NODE L-2 — the scion. Speaker: a storylet at port, mid-life. Entry: wealth + a home established.
Raise the heir in advance: recurring costs, small scenes, a child who exists in the world while the captain still sails.
> "Fund the upbringing" [gate: money, repeatedly] -> the heir-premium accrues: the scion inherits MORE (lineage continuity, better transfer profile). Love as compounding paperwork.
> "Not this year" [gate: none] -> the scion's profile stalls; the will's best beneficiary is optional and expensive, which is the point.
> (never raise one) [gate: none] SILENCE -> death offers only the colder heir-shapes: the rival, the collector. A will with no loved one in it is still a will; it just reads that way.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: present-tense strength. Every coin into the scion is fuel not bought. The game makes the player FEEL the actuarial tension between this life and the next one.

### NODE L-3 — an officer's arc, at the line. Speaker: any officer with an unfinished story. Entry: their arc mid-stage as death approaches.
The officers' hidden stories advance by voyages and choices; death can strand them.
> finish the arc while the captain lives [gate: the arc's own conditions — voyages, items, choices] -> the officer's story resolves; bonded officers can cross to the heir with their resolution intact.
> die mid-arc [gate: the zee decides] -> the arc FREEZES. The heir inherits the officer (maybe) but not the momentum; some story-states are simply lost between lives. THE DESIGN'S BOLDEST REFUSAL: narrative progress is mostly non-heritable, on purpose — stories belong to the life that lived them.
> ask the officer about it later, as the heir [gate: inherited officer] -> DIFFERENT TEXT: they speak of the previous captain in past tense, and the arc's stranded state is VISIBLE in their lines. Grief as a dialogue variant. The corpus's cleanest inherited-NPC reactivity.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the illusion that the fold carries everything that matters. It carries what survives a death, which is less, and truer.

### NODE L-4 — THE READING. Speaker: the legacy screen, functioning as executor. Entry: stage 4.
> "The scion inherits" [gate: raised] -> chart + lineage + the warm transfer profile
> "The rival claims the ship" [gate: none] -> the vessel crosses; the warmth does not. Different asset, different sentence.
> "A collector settles the estate" [gate: debts held] -> obligations cross WITH assets; the heir starts owed and owing. The game will let a bad life bequeath its shape.
> "Take the chart" / "let the chart burn" [gate: none — the one always-offered clause] -> the knowledge inheritance, chosen affirmatively. Refusing it exists as an option because a TRUE will requires the possibility of writing a cruel one.
> (unbanked secrets, unspent fortunes, open ambitions) — no option line exists. THE REMOVED VERB: the player cannot will forward what the life didn't finish converting into heritable form. The interface's silence about the burned remainder IS the reading's last clause.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the run. All of it. The screen itemizes what a life amounted to, and the itemization is shorter than the living was — every time, by design.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 4+ heir-shapes times the 3-way estate profile (chart / heirloom class / bonded officers), plus the ambition's rare TRUE COMPLETIONS as run-ending alternatives to death. The real branch surface: which knowledge-class items were banked before the end.**

- **DEATH -> SCION:** the warm fold. Maximum transfer, lineage continuity, the officers most likely to stay. Condition: the upbringing was paid for, in advance, with this life's strength.
- **DEATH -> RIVAL / COLLECTOR / COLD SHAPES:** the estate crosses at a discount; obligations may cross at par. The will a life writes when it never made anyone to love it.
- **AMBITION COMPLETED (rare):** the run ends ALIVE — retirement, the song written, the bones found. The game's true endings are exits from the death-loop, and most players never see one: completion as the alternative to legacy, not its culmination.
- **THE KNOWLEDGE SPECTRUM (cross-cutting):** every branch is repriced by what was banked: chart coverage, secrets converted to heritable form, officer arcs resolved. Two identical deaths with different banking produce different SECOND LIVES — the branch map's real axis runs through the fold, not the funeral.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE SECOND LIFE REPLAYS TOO MUCH PROSE.** The famous criticism: story content is largely static per port, so an heir re-sails into vignettes the player has already read — the game's greatest asset (the writing) is exactly what repetition cheapens. The pool file banked it as story-becomes-a-vending-machine; at legacy scale it compounds: every death re-serves more known text.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the fold must never hand the heir the parent's SCENES. Inherited knowledge changes what the heir can DO, never re-runs what the parent READ — and inherited-state variants (L-3's past-tense officers) must exist precisely so that repeated places speak differently to a second generation. Bohemia's proc-gen city is structurally immune to the geography half; the dialogue half is a compile rule: every heritable NPC needs previous-captain variants.

**F2 — GRIND CAN OUTWEIGH GRIEF.** Widely reported: mid-game economics turn voyages into port-report milk runs, and death then reads as losing PROGRESS toward a spreadsheet rather than losing a person. When the estate is mostly denominated in tedium, the legacy scene's poetry goes flat.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the heritable estate must be denominated in EXPERIENCES (map knowledge, resolved arcs, banked testimony), never in repeatable-labor outputs. If a Bohemia player can grind the fold's value, the fold is mispriced — survival accounting keeps the labor; the fold keeps only what a eulogy could mention.

**F3 — MERCIFUL MODE EXPOSED THE DESIGN'S DEPENDENCE ON DEATH.** The later-added save-retention mode (and the permadeath debate around it) revealed how much of the game's meaning was load-bearing on loss being real; with death defanged, the legacy machine becomes set dressing, and community consensus split on whether the game survives it.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the fold's meaning budget must not sit entirely on permadeath. Bohemia is hardcore by identity, but the design should be tested against its own merciful-mode question: if a run were retried, what would still matter? The answer must be "the ledger and the world's memory," which do not reset — meaning anchored in RECORD, not solely in loss. (The Q28/Q140 taint mechanism is the model: consequence that survives even the undo.)

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE WILL IS AUTHORED BY LIVING, NOT BY A MENU. Every mid-run choice (scion funding, arc-finishing, secret-banking) is secretly testament-drafting. The death screen only READS what the life already wrote — the fold as consequence, never as feature-checklist.
W2. KNOWLEDGE IS THE ESTATE'S GOLD STANDARD. The chart — pure geography-knowledge — is the one always-offered inheritance, and the game's best one. What crosses generations best is what was LEARNED. (Twelfth-plus echo of comprehension-as-progression; the fold speaks the same currency.)
W3. THE REFUSALS MAKE THE LINE REAL. Unbanked secrets burn, open ambitions die, stranded arcs stay stranded. A generational game earns its deaths by what it will NOT carry — the meaning lives in the non-heritable remainder.
W4. THE HEIR IS A PERSON, NOT A RESPAWN. Heir-shapes with different inheritance profiles — raised scion versus claiming rival — make the successor an authored relationship, priced during the predecessor's life. Who inherits is itself a life's-work question.
W5. THE SCION IS PAID FOR WITH PRESENT STRENGTH. Funding the heir costs fuel-money NOW: the game mechanizes the parent's dilemma (this life versus the next one) into every port visit. The fold's emotional core, denominated in the survival economy.
W6. DOOMED RUNS BECOME TESTAMENT RUNS. Because deaths are slow and legible, endgame play reorganizes around dying WELL — racing to bank, resolve, and bequeath. The game invents an entire late-life genre: playing your own probate.
W7. INHERITED NPCS SPEAK IN PAST TENSE. Officers who cross remember the previous captain in their lines, arcs visibly stranded. The cheapest possible reactivity (a text variant) carrying the heaviest possible freight (grief), and the model for every fold-crossing character Bohemia ships.
W8. THE TRUE ENDINGS ARE EXITS, NOT VICTORIES OVER DEATH. Completing an ambition ends the line ALIVE — retirement as the rare, expensive alternative to legacy. Death stays the default without becoming the only author.
W9. THE SECOND LIFE PROVES THE FIRST ONE HAPPENED. The heir's early game is repriced by inherited geography and the player's own trained dread — progress the interface never has to claim, because the player EXPERIENCES being their own inheritance.
W10. THE CHRONICLE FRAME. The game names a full history of play a chronicle of tragedies: the unit of the work is the LINE, not the life. The framing alone does design work — players narrate their dynasties unprompted. Bohemia's three-arc, hundred-year shape is this frame at full scale.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE WILL AS A PLAYED SCENE [W1, W6 — the core port]
**System:** the fold / succession (GDD v4) / dying-generation play
Bohemia's fold currently specifies WHAT persists; this port specifies the SCENE: when a dynast's death becomes legible in advance, the game enters testament play — the dying generation's remaining actions reframed (diegetically, not via UI mode) as estate work: bank testimony into the record, resolve bonds, place the heir. Then the READING: a fold-transfer scene that itemizes what crosses AND names what burns (W3 — the burned remainder must be read aloud; an inheritance with no visible refusals is a save file).
```
@TALK will_reading speaker=record_keeper entry=succession
  @SAY The estate of the second dynast. Sit. This is shorter than the living was. It always is.
  @SAY Crossing: the map as they knew it. Every mark on it is a mistake they survived. Treat it that way.
  @SAY [gate: fold:heir_raised] Crossing: the household's loyalty. Paid for over nine years. It knows your name already.
  @SAY Burning: three secrets never told, one door never opened, the plant deal half-made. Gone with the body. That is not cruelty. That is what a life is.
  @OPT "Read the burned list again."   [gate: none] -> will_burned   @DO set_flag heir_heard_the_cost
  @OPT "Just give me the map."          [gate: none] -> will_map
  @OPT (say nothing)                    [gate: none] SILENCE -> will_map
  @NOVERB "Bring any of it back"
@END
```

### PORT 2 — THE ESTATE'S CURRENCY RULE [W2, F2's law — a compile-time gate rule]
**System:** the fold / survival accounting
Machine-checkable compile rule: fold-heritable classes are KNOWLEDGE (map/case/testimony), BOND-STATES (resolved relationships), and RECORD entries. Repeatable-labor outputs (resources, currency beyond a fixed remnant) are non-heritable by schema. If a proposed fold field can be ground, the validator rejects it. F2's law with teeth; the schema whitelist ships EMPTY of specific items per MECHANISM-MINE / CONTENTS-PAOLO'S.

### PORT 3 — PAST-TENSE VARIANTS FOR EVERY CROSSING NPC [W7, F1's law]
**System:** .bq roles / generational persistence
Compile rule for the questbook's own build queue: any NPC flagged fold-crossing must ship previous-dynast dialogue variants (grief register, stranded-arc register, past-tense register) — the validator counts them like it counts W-points. One line class, highest freight (W7). The .bq role model already casts against whoever exists this run; add the requirement that inherited bonds carry inherited text.

### PORT 4 — THE HEIR-PREMIUM, PAID IN PRESENT STRENGTH [W4, W5]
**System:** survival economy / succession
Raising the successor costs the current generation real, felt resources on a schedule (the L-2 machine): skipped payments produce colder succession profiles, never game-overs. The dilemma must live in the SURVIVAL layer (food, fuel, protection money), so that loving the future is always priced against surviving the present. [PENDING, Paolo's call: what raising a dynast-heir costs per act, and what the cold-succession shapes are in Bohemia's canon.]

### PORT 5 — TESTED AGAINST THE MERCIFUL QUESTION [F3's law — a standing design gate]
**System:** the fold / the dial / hardcore identity
Every fold feature answers one question at review: IF THIS RUN WERE RETRIED, WHAT WOULD STILL MATTER? Anything whose entire meaning evaporates under retry is re-anchored in record (the world remembers, the ledger keeps the entry, the taint survives) or rejected. Bohemia stays hardcore; the meaning stays even where mercy someday intrudes. Add the question to the STATE_OF_PLAY review checklist verbatim — it is a one-line gate a human runs.
