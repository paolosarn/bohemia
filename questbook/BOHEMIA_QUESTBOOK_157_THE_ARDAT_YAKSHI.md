# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #157 — "THE ARDAT-YAKSHI (KILL YOUR DAUGHTER, OR BE SEDUCED BY HER)"
**Game:** Mass Effect 2 (2010)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** Samara's loyalty mission — the hunt for Morinth. A centuries-old justicar has pursued her own daughter, a serial killer who murders through sex, across the galaxy — and the trap requires the player to seduce the daughter while the mother waits to strike, ending in a choice of which of them lives.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Mass Effect catalogue; corpus anchor Q06 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia needs quests where the fork is between two people who are both family and both terrible, and the player is the bait. The Ardat-Yakshi is the genre's cleanest specimen: a mother bound by absolute law to kill her own child, a daughter who is genuinely monstrous and genuinely a person, and a climax where the player must choose which to spare — and can be seduced into choosing the killer. It is the Amalgamation's seduction (#34/#36) made intimate, and the "law vs blood" fork our factions will need.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A mother has hunted her daughter for four hundred years under a law that permits no mercy, and the player is the bait in the trap — required to seduce the monstrous daughter to draw her out, then choose, at the last second, which of the two women dies.**

Samara is a justicar, bound by an absolute code that dictates her every action; she has pursued Morinth, one of her three daughters, for centuries. Morinth is an Ardat-Yakshi — a genetic condition that makes her lethal: she consumes the minds of her lovers at the moment of union, killing them, and she is addicted to it, a serial killer leaving beautiful corpses across the galaxy. The trap: Morinth hunts a specific type — the bold, the artistic, the willing — so the player must pose as her next conquest, draw her to her apartment, and hold her attention until Samara arrives to kill her. But Morinth is not a beast; she is charming, brilliant, seductive, and makes a real case: her mother's law is a prison, her nature is not her fault, and she offers the player power and freedom if they help her kill Samara instead.

The machine is a fork between two family members who are both, in their way, monstrous — Samara bound to a law that would kill her own child without hesitation, Morinth a genuine murderer who is also a genuine person trapped in a lethal body. At the climax, when Samara and Morinth grapple, the player chooses: help Samara kill Morinth (the law wins, the daughter dies, the loyal justicar survives) or help Morinth kill Samara (the killer wins, replaces her mother on the squad, and the player has been seduced into siding with a serial killer). The seduction is the trap's brilliance — the player can be talked into the wrong choice, in the moment, by a monster who feels like the more sympathetic one.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the most dangerous person is not the obvious monster but the one who makes you feel that choosing them is mercy — and a law with no room for mercy will still, sometimes, be the one that was right.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### SAMARA — the justicar bound by absolute law
- **What she wants:** to kill Morinth, as her code demands, though it is her own daughter.
- **What she'll trade:** her loyalty to the squad, earned only if the player helps her fulfill the law.
- **What she will never say out loud:** the grief under the code — she has three daughters cursed with this nature and has spent centuries hunting one; the law is her armor against feeling it.
- **Function:** THE LAW, personified — mercy foreclosed by principle, and the tragedy of a mother who must be her child's executioner.

### MORINTH — the monster who feels like the victim
- **What she wants:** to be free of the hunt, to keep killing (she is addicted), and to replace her mother — and she wants the player to CHOOSE her.
- **What she'll trade:** power, freedom, a seductive vision of a life without her mother's law.
- **What she will never say out loud (honestly):** that she will kill the player too, eventually; the charm is the hunt.
- **Function:** THE SEDUCTION — a genuine person and a genuine serial killer, the trap's living bait for the player's own judgment.

### THE PLAYER-AS-BAIT — the judgment being seduced
- **What they want:** to end the threat, and are placed in exactly the position Morinth's victims occupy.
- **Function:** THE FORK's instrument and target: the player is the lure AND the one being lured, and the climax tests which.

### THE DEAD (Morinth's victims) — the price of the nature
- The beautiful corpses across the galaxy, and the grieving family (a mother whose daughter Morinth killed guides the player in).
- **Function:** THE STAKES made real — the charm has a body count, shown before the seduction.

### THE ARDAT-YAKSHI CONDITION — the curse under the crime
- A genetic nature that makes intimacy lethal; not chosen, not curable.
- **Function:** THE ROOT: the monster is a person born into a killing body, which is what makes Morinth's case land and the fork hard.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE JUSTICAR'S HUNT [MANDATORY]
Samara reveals her centuries-long pursuit of Morinth and the Ardat-Yakshi nature. The player learns the target is her daughter.

### STAGE 2 — THE VICTIMS [MANDATORY]
The trail through Morinth's kills — a grieving mother whose daughter died in Morinth's bed guides the player. The charm's body count is shown first.

### STAGE 3 — BECOMING THE BAIT [MANDATORY]
The player poses as Morinth's type — bold, artistic, willing — at a club, drawing her attention through a seduction the player must perform to draw her out.

### STAGE 4 — THE APARTMENT [MANDATORY]
Alone with Morinth, holding her attention, resisting (or not) her escalating seduction until Samara arrives. The trap is the player's own composure under a monster's charm.

### STAGE 5 — THE FORK [MANDATORY]
Samara and Morinth grapple; the player chooses which to help. The one the player backs kills the other; the survivor joins (or stays with) the squad.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE Y-1 — Samara on the law. Entry: stage 1.
The justicar, explaining the code that will make her kill her own child.
> "She's your daughter." [gate: none] -> the grief under the armor; the law does not bend, and that is its horror
> "Is there no cure?" [gate: none] -> none; the nature is what it is; Morinth is a person and a killer both
> "Why the code?" [gate: none] -> because without it she could not do what must be done. THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK, paraphrased: the law is the only thing that lets me survive being her mother

### NODE Y-2 — Morinth's seduction. Entry: stage 4. The monster making her case.
Alone with the bait, she charms, escalates, and offers a way out of her mother's law.
> "Your mother is hunting you." [gate: none] -> her framing: the law is the prison, she is its victim; a case that lands because it is partly true
> "Tell me what you are." [gate: none] -> the Ardat-Yakshi nature, delivered as tragedy, not confession — the seduction's core: not my fault
> "What are you offering?" [gate: none] -> power, freedom, a life without the law. THE HOOK, paraphrased: help me end her, and I will show you what you could never survive with anyone else
> (resist / hold composure) [gate: WILL / the player's judgment] -> the trap holds; Samara arrives; the fork opens honestly
> (yield to her) [gate: none] TRAP -> the player is being drawn into siding with the killer; the seduction working on the one who was meant to be baiting it
NOVERB "Let's find a way to save you without killing anyone" — no line exists; the Ardat-Yakshi nature admits no cure, and the fork admits no both-live. The removed verb is the tragedy: one of these women dies, and the player only chooses which.

### NODE Y-3 — the fork. Entry: stage 5. Mother and daughter, grappling, the player's hand the deciding weight.
> "Samara, now." (help the mother) [gate: none] -> Morinth dies; the law is fulfilled; the justicar survives, grieving, loyal
> "Do it, Morinth." (help the daughter) [gate: seduced or chosen] -> Samara dies; Morinth replaces her, posing as her mother; the player has sided with a serial killer and armed her
> WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: one of them, always. Back the law and a person born into a killing body dies; back the person and a murderer walks free wearing her mother's face.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes — Morinth dies (Samara loyal) or Samara dies (Morinth joins) — gated by the player's judgment under seduction, and 0 both-live, by the nature's design.**

- **HELP SAMARA:** Morinth dies; the justicar survives and gives loyalty, carrying the grief of executing her own daughter. The law wins; a person born cursed is dead. WRITES: Samara stays; the code held.
- **HELP MORINTH:** Samara dies; Morinth takes her place, imitating her mother to stay on the squad; the player has been seduced into arming a serial killer who will kill again. WRITES: Morinth joins under a false face; the killer won.
- **THE SEDUCTION AXIS:** whether the player is TALKED INTO the wrong choice in the apartment is the real branch — the fork is not just a menu, it is a test the monster can pass on the player. The trap's brilliance: the bait can be baited.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** both endings kill a family member; the branches are two answers to "law or blood, and can you tell mercy from seduction."

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE "HELP MORINTH" PATH IS A LOSING TRAP, NOT A REAL CHOICE.** Siding with Morinth is mechanically inferior (a serial killer imitating a justicar, worse for the squad, no real upside) and the game frames it as the obviously-wrong pick — so the seduction, however well-written, is a choice the player is punished for taking rather than a genuine fork. The dilemma is one-sided.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest offers a seductive-but-wrong choice, the choice must have a REAL upside the player forgoes by refusing it — so that resisting the seduction costs something and the temptation is genuine, not a trap with a loss attached. A seduction you're simply penalized for is a lecture; make the wrong choice genuinely tempting on the merits.

**F2 — MORINTH'S PERSONHOOD DIES WITH HER, UNEXAMINED.** The quest builds Morinth as a genuine person trapped in a lethal nature, then kills her (in the common ending) with no space to sit with what was lost — the "she is a victim too" thread is raised and dropped. The tragedy of the curse is stated but not mourned.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** when a quest kills a humanized monster, it must leave ROOM to mourn what the nature made unavoidable — an aftermath beat where the loss is felt, not just tallied. The fold can carry Morinth's kind forward as a grief, not a closed case (Nature of the Beast's #155 law, restated).

**F3 — THE SEDUCTION REQUIRES A PERFORMANCE THE PLAYER CAN'T FULLY AUTHOR.** To bait Morinth the player must pose as her type via fixed dialogue picks; a player roleplaying differently is railroaded into the seduction script or fails the trap. The bait's performance is a corridor.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a bait/undercover quest must let the player author their OWN cover from their established character, with multiple valid performances reaching the trap — not one scripted seduction. The undercover self should be a choice, not a forced role (KCD's monastery, #08, is the model for lived cover).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE FORK IS TWO TERRIBLE FAMILY MEMBERS. Samara is a mother who will execute her child; Morinth is a child who murders for pleasure — both monstrous, both bound by a nature (law or curse) they didn't fully choose. No clean party.
W2. THE PLAYER IS THE BAIT AND THE TARGET. Posing as Morinth's next victim puts the player in exactly the position of her dead — the trap is set with the player's own body, and the seduction is aimed at them.
W3. THE SEDUCTION CAN WIN. The player can be talked into the wrong choice in the moment — the monster's charm works on the person meant to be baiting it, which is the rarest and best kind of test: your own judgment under pressure.
W4. THE MONSTER HAS A REAL CASE. Morinth's nature isn't her fault; her mother's law is genuinely a prison; the seduction lands because it is partly true. The killer feels, for a moment, like the victim.
W5. THE LAW IS A TRAGEDY, NOT A VIRTUE. Samara's code is what lets her murder her own daughter — the quest shows the cost of absolute principle, not just its correctness.
W6. THE BODY COUNT IS SHOWN FIRST. The grieving mother of a real victim guides the player in, so Morinth's charm arrives already stained — the seduction fights against a corpse the player has seen.
W7. NO BOTH-LIVE. The Ardat-Yakshi nature admits no cure; the fork admits no mercy for both. One family member dies, and the player only chooses which — the tragedy is structural.
W8. THE CURSE IS NOT CHOSEN. Morinth was born into a killing body; the monster is a person the nature made lethal, which is what makes killing her hard and sparing her deadly.
W9. THE SURVIVOR CARRIES THE GRIEF. Samara, if she wins, has executed her daughter and gives loyalty carrying that — the victory is a wound, not a triumph.
W10. THE SEDUCTION OUTLASTS THE MISSION. Players remember being ALMOST talked into siding with a serial killer — the sign the writing worked. A trap that can catch the trapper is unforgettable.
