# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 40: MORAL DISCOMFORT WITH NO EASY EXIT (Cyberpunk 2077: "Sinnerman")
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the escort-that-becomes-something-else structure, the ~50 back-out
points, the forgiveness/complicity/faith moral engine, the crucifixion BrainDance, the dialogue-shapes-
his-spirits mechanic, the honest flaws (offense + "on rails" critiques), and Bohemia ports. This is the
medium's model for A QUEST THAT MAKES YOU DEEPLY UNCOMFORTABLE ON PURPOSE + COMPLICITY YOU CHOOSE STEP
BY STEP + no clean moral exit. CD Projekt RED. Handle per our tone/wellbeing rules. Reference only;
Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Sinnerman" -> "There Is a Light That Never Goes Out" -> "They Won't Go When I Go" (Cyberpunk
2077, 2020). A three-part side-job. You escort a grieving man toward revenge, then get entangled with
JOSHUA STEPHENSON — a death-row murderer who found God and wants to be literally CRUCIFIED on camera as
penance. Named for the Nina Simone song. Widely cited as the game's most disturbing, divisive quest.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- DELIBERATE MORAL DISCOMFORT (the point): Sinnerman is ENGINEERED to unsettle. A CDPR-aware truth from
  the community: "So it makes you uncomfortable? Congratulations, that's what it's SUPPOSED to do." The
  quest refuses catharsis or a clean "good" option — it sits you in genuine ethical unease and makes you
  keep CHOOSING to stay (cf. This War of Mine's guilt Q30, Pathologic Q21, Spec Ops Q27 — but self-
  directed, no forced atrocity).
- COMPLICITY YOU CHOOSE, STEP BY STEP: unlike Spec Ops (which FORCES the atrocity, Q27), Sinnerman gives
  you "about 50 chances to back out" — you can leave at nearly every beat, even BETWEEN individual nail-
  hammer hits. So if you reach the end, you CHOSE every step. The horror is that YOU kept going — agency
  makes the complicity real (the ethical design our Pacifist Law prefers; cf. Undertale chosen-cruelty Q28).
- THE BRAID OF THEMES: revenge, forgiveness, faith, exploitation, spectacle, and redemption tangle with
  no resolution — the studio exploiting Joshua for BrainDance profit, Joshua earnest in his faith, the
  victim's family split (forgiveness vs fury), and YOU caught between. It asks hard questions and answers
  NONE (cf. Whispering Hillock Q04, Vault 22 Q03, Pentiment Q26).

===============================================================================
## 1. THE FULL STAGE-BY-STAGE FLOW
===============================================================================

### STAGE 1 — THE ESCORT THAT ISN'T (the bait-and-switch)
- A fixer (Wakako) hands you a standard revenge job: escort BILL JABLONSKY to kill his wife's murderer.
  You tail a police convoy. Bill approaches with a gun — and is SHOT DEAD by the escorting officer
  before he can act. The "quest" you accepted evaporates in seconds. Then the PRISONER calls you over —
  Joshua, the very murderer — and the REAL quest begins. A standard setup is demolished to launch
  something stranger (cf. New Vegas's ambush-inversion, the subverted premise Q02/Q20).
- IMMEDIATE MORAL WHIPLASH: your CLIENT is dead (his revenge unfulfilled — some players rage that this
  makes it "pointless," which is the POINT: revenge resolves nothing), and you're now aligned with the
  man he wanted dead. The ground shifts under your allegiance.

### STAGE 2 — THE FORGIVENESS VISIT (the excruciating scene)
- Joshua asks you to accompany him (quest "There Is a Light That Never Goes Out"). He goes to the HOME
  of his victim's family — to seek FORGIVENESS. The sister (Zuleikha), through whom he found God, is
  conflicted; the victim's MOTHER (Gloria) arrives, wants nothing to do with her son's killer in her
  home, and orders everyone out. An unbearable scene of a murderer asking absolution from the bereaved —
  no clean feeling available (cf. RDR/Bloody Baron's human-cost Q01/Q10; the discomfort is the content).

### STAGE 3 — THE STUDIO'S EXPLOITATION (the cynical frame)
- A BrainDance studio exec (Rachel) rides along — she thinks Joshua is INSANE but sees a fortune in
  recording his death for "The Passion." She pulls YOU aside, asks your opinion of Joshua, and tries to
  BRIBE you. The sacred (Joshua's faith) and the profane (corporate spectacle profit) collide — is this
  redemption or a snuff film for money? (cf. the exploitation/spectacle theme; media-manufactured
  reality Q09.)

### STAGE 4 — THE DIALOGUE-SHAPES-HIS-SPIRITS MECHANIC (the subtle craft)
- Throughout, your dialogue DICTATES the outcome + Joshua's STATE: ENCOURAGE his conviction/faith and he
  goes to his death in "high spirits"; DISMISS or QUESTION his resolve and he's "incredibly deflated."
  You can also pray WITH him (reciting prayers from several religions — "one of the game's oddly tender
  moments"). Your tone literally shapes a man's final emotional state — an intimate reactivity (cf.
  Alpha Protocol stance-shapes-relationship Q24, BG3 companion-lean Q16).

### STAGE 5 — THE CRUCIFIXION (the ~50 exits + the choice to drive the nails)
- Final quest ("They Won't Go When I Go"): the shoot. You're given option after option to STOP — the
  game "gives you about 50 chances to back out," even BETWEEN nail hits. If you go all the way, Joshua
  asks YOU to be the one to HAMMER HIM to the cross. The player, by choice, drives the nails. The
  culmination is only reached by continuously CHOOSING it — the ultimate "complicity through sustained
  consent" (cf. Spec Ops forced-vs-Sinnerman-chosen Q27; Undertale Q28).
- BRANCHES: you can end it early (kill Joshua for Bill's original contract; talk Joshua down; refuse to
  participate; or see it through). Different exits lock different content. No path feels "clean."

===============================================================================
## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- "ON RAILS" DESPITE THE CHOICE PARADE: a sharp community critique — the quest PARADES choice (50 exits)
  but hard-scripts the spine; try to deviate (run to the car, shoot early) and it BREAKS or funnels you
  back. The freedom is partly illusory. LESSON: if you offer many exits, they must genuinely BRANCH, not
  just pause a fixed rail — false choice breeds resentment (cf. Spec Ops illusion-of-choice Q27, our
  no-brick-wall + real-reactivity standards).
- POTENTIALLY OFFENSIVE / TAKE-DEPENDENT THEME: it stages a literal crucifixion + religious penance;
  some find it profound, others a shallow provocation "mocking a central tenet of Christianity" for shock.
  LESSON (ours): heavy religious/moral content must be handled with GENUINE weight + respect for the
  belief, not edgy provocation — or it reads as hollow (our tone/no-preaching/evenhandedness rules; treat
  faith seriously, from the inside).
- BUGGY / FAIL-SENSITIVE: the chain breaks easily on wrong inputs (kills the client, freezes NPCs) —
  fragile scripting. LESSON: a delicate multi-stage moral quest needs ROBUST state-handling or the
  fragility shatters the mood (our regression-gate/never-lose laws).
- THE DISCOMFORT DIVIDES: some are moved, some enraged, some "cringe" — deliberate-discomfort content is
  POLARIZING by nature. LESSON: bank it as an OPTIONAL, clearly-signposted side-thread (Cyberpunk lets
  you miss it entirely) — don't force the whole audience through your hardest content (cf. Pathologic
  sliders Q21, our difficulty/tone options).

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. DELIBERATE MORAL DISCOMFORT: a quest engineered to unsettle, refusing catharsis or a clean "good"
    option — it sits you in real ethical unease (cf. TWoM Q30, Pathologic Q21, Whispering Hillock Q04).
W2. COMPLICITY THROUGH SUSTAINED CONSENT: ~50 back-out points mean reaching the end is a CHOICE you keep

W3. THE ESCORT THAT ISN'T (bait-and-switch premise): a standard revenge setup is demolished in seconds to
    launch something stranger — subvert the quest's own framing (cf. Forgotten City red-herring Q20, Q02).
W4. THE BRAID WITH NO RESOLUTION: revenge/forgiveness/faith/exploitation/spectacle tangle and the game
    answers NONE — hard questions left open (cf. Pentiment Q26, Vault 22 Q03, Whispering Hillock Q04).
W5. THE EXCRUCIATING HUMAN SCENE: a murderer begging the bereaved for forgiveness — no clean feeling
    available; the discomfort IS the content (cf. Bloody Baron human-cost Q01, RDR Q10).
W6. THE SACRED-VS-PROFANE FRAME: earnest faith exploited for corporate spectacle profit — layering
    cynicism over sincerity so neither is simple (cf. media-manufactured reality Q09).
W7. DIALOGUE SHAPES HIS FINAL STATE: your tone determines a man's emotional state at death (high spirits
    vs deflated) — intimate, weighty reactivity (cf. Alpha Protocol Q24, BG3 Q16).
W8. THE TENDER BEAT INSIDE THE HORROR: praying with him (multiple religions) is "oddly tender" amid the
    dread — a moment of grace that complicates your judgment (cf. Yakuza absurd-with-heart Q13, humanize Q01).
W9. THE OPTIONAL, MISSABLE HARD CONTENT: the whole chain can be avoided/ended early — the hardest content
    is opt-in, clearly a side-thread (cf. Pathologic sliders Q21; respect the player's limits).
W10. EARLY-EXIT BRANCHES WITH REAL LOCKOUTS: quitting early (kill Joshua for the contract) locks later
     quests — your bail-point has consequences; no free do-over (cf. our Megaton law, Elden Ring missable Q23).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — WITHIN our tone/wellbeing/evenhandedness rules
===============================================================================
Sinnerman is the master example of CHOSEN complicity + deliberate discomfort done the ETHICAL way (you
consent at every step) — the opposite of Spec Ops' force (Q27), and aligned with our Pacifist Path Law.
- W1/W2 (deliberate discomfort + complicity through sustained consent): bank a Bohemia quest engineered
  to sit the dynasty in real moral unease with NO clean exit — but built the SINNERMAN way: many genuine
  back-out points so if the dynasty reaches the dark end, they CHOSE every step (agency makes it real;
  the ethical design our Pacifist Law prefers). A verdict/mercy/execution chain (Q049) or an Amalgamation-
  penance quest fits (ties TWoM guilt Q30, Undertale chosen-cruelty Q28; our no-forced-atrocity stance).
- W3 (the escort that isn't): open a Bohemia quest as one thing (a revenge job, a delivery) and DEMOLISH
  the premise to launch something stranger — subvert its own framing (ties Forgotten City Q20, our
  false-dawn Q048).
- W4/W5 (the unresolved braid + the excruciating human scene): braid revenge/forgiveness/faith/
  exploitation and answer NONE; stage a genuinely hard human scene (a wronged party facing the one who
  wronged them; a killer seeking absolution) — the discomfort is the content, handled with weight (ties
  Pentiment Q26, Bloody Baron Q01, our recorded-vs-unrecorded ledger).
- W6 (sacred-vs-profane): layer sincerity with exploitation — the Amalgamation/Network could EXPLOIT a
  survivor's grief/faith for profit or control (a "resurrection" sold as salvation, spectacle over soul)
  — cynicism over sincerity (ties SOMA's false-salvation Q34, our Amalgamation-as-comforting-lie).
- W7 (your tone shapes their final state): bank intimate reactivity where the dynasty's WORDS shape a
  character's emotional state at a pivotal/final moment (encourage vs deflate) — small-scale, weighty
  (ties Alpha Protocol Q24, BG3 Q16, our dialogue systems).
- W8 (the tender beat inside the horror): put a moment of GRACE inside the dread (a shared prayer, a
  kindness) that complicates the player's judgment — don't let dark content be uniformly grim (ties
  Yakuza Q13, humanize Q01; texture over relentlessness).
- W9/W10 (optional missable hard content + real bail-consequences): make our HARDEST content OPT-IN and
  clearly signposted (avoidable/endable early), with bail-points that LOCK consequences (no free do-over)
  — respect player limits AND honor the weight of quitting (ties Pathologic sliders Q21, Megaton law,
  Elden Ring missable Q23).
- FLAWS/CARE (bank HARD): make our many exits GENUINELY branch, not a false-choice rail (Sinnerman's "on
  rails" critique + Spec Ops Q27 — our real-reactivity + no-brick-wall standards); handle faith/moral/
  grief content with GENUINE weight + respect (our evenhandedness + no-preaching + tone rules — treat
  belief seriously from the inside, never edgy provocation); ROBUST state-handling for delicate multi-
  stage quests (our regression-gate/never-lose laws); and NEVER force the discomfort on the whole
  audience — opt-in, per our wellbeing rules.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**JOSHUA STEPHENSON** — a murderer, days from execution, who has found faith and wants his death to MEAN something: a crucifixion BrainDance, broadcast, so others can feel what he feels. Will trade: everything; he has nothing left to hold. Will never say: doubt — and the one moment he almost does is the quest's live wire. FUNCTION: the sincerity that cannot be priced, inside a machine that prices everything.

**RACHEL CASICH** — the studio producer. Wants the recording delivered on schedule with the talent emotionally stable. Will trade: money, access. Will never say: whether she believes any of it; belief is not her department. FUNCTION: the clipboard (Q122's thesis). The horror is her calendar.

**THE WIDOW (ZUZANNA)** — wants to never see Joshua again; is asked, by him, to forgive him at her kitchen table. FUNCTION: the scene the quest is infamous for — a forgiveness visit that the player facilitates and cannot make okay.

**V** — hired as security, kept as witness, promoted (optionally) to executioner's assistant. Wants: initially, the payout. FUNCTION: the ~50 exits. V's presence is re-consented constantly; staying is a choice made over and over.
