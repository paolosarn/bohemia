# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 20: THE GOLDEN RULE / TIME-LOOP MORALITY (The Forgotten City)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the collective-punishment "Golden Rule" engine, the time-loop
knowledge-carry, the four endings, the deduction + moral-philosophy fusion, the argue-the-creator
finale, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for A SINGLE RULE THAT MAKES A WHOLE SOCIETY A
MORAL PRESSURE-COOKER + a loop that carries KNOWLEDGE & INVENTORY + a debate-with-god climax. Fuses the
Anju loop (Q11), Outer Wilds knowledge (Q18), Obra Dinn deduction (Q19), and Planescape argument (Q12)
into ONE quest. Notably: it began as a SKYRIM QUEST MOD (3M+ downloads) before the standalone remake —
proof of pure quest-craft. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: The Forgotten City (Modern Storyteller, 2021; orig. Skyrim mod 2015). A modern character falls
through a portal into a subterranean ROMAN city (~23 inhabitants) trapped under "The Golden Rule."

===============================================================================
## 0. THE PREMISE / STAKES — THE GOLDEN RULE
===============================================================================
- THE RULE: "THE MANY SHALL SUFFER FOR THE SINS OF THE ONE." If ANY single person in the city commits a
  "sin," EVERYONE is instantly killed — turned into GOLD STATUES frozen in their final terror. Inspired
  by the Roman military practice of DECIMATION, multiplied for eternity.
- THE HOOK: you arrive on the very day the Rule is broken. A time-loop portal lets you REWIND to the
  loop's start whenever the Rule is triggered — you keep your MEMORY + INVENTORY. The magistrate
  (Sentius) tasks you to find WHO will break the Rule and stop them.
- THE UNDEFINED "SIN": the game NEVER fixes what counts as sin. Murder/theft/arson clearly break it —
  but what about lying? insulting? deception? blackmail? harmful INTENT? failing to act? The ambiguity
  turns the city into a SURVEILLANCE STATE where everyone lives in fear, and it's the engine of the
  game's moral philosophy: is good/evil universal or society-defined? Are smaller cruelties acceptable
  to preserve the greater good? Can you trust anyone not to sin?
- THE STAKES: save ~23 richly-drawn people, solve why the Rule exists, and escape — while the Rule
  itself indicts collective punishment as a moral horror.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how a loop-morality quest is built)
===============================================================================

### THE LOOP (knowledge + inventory carry)
- Trigger the Rule (by anyone's sin) -> flee to the portal -> rewind to loop-start, keeping MEMORY and
  INVENTORY. You re-sequence events across loops: learn a secret in loop 3, use it in loop 4; carry an
  item forward to solve a problem that was impossible before (cf. Outer Wilds knowledge-carry Q18, Anju
  loop-mastery Q11 — but here the loop is YOURS to trigger, and morality-charged).
- THE DARK EXPLOIT: you CAN break the Golden Rule yourself for personal gain (steal a weapon, steal a
  potion, steal money) — dooming the city THIS loop — then carry the loot into the NEXT loop (e.g. steal
  a cure to save a poisoning victim you couldn't reach in time). The game reinvents "winning": SINNING
  can be a tool, at the cost of everyone's (resettable) lives. A morally-loaded systemic verb.

### THE DEDUCTION + THE RED HERRING (the anti-fetch twist)
- The "find who breaks the Rule" task is a RED HERRING BY DESIGN: DIFFERENT people trigger it depending
  on YOUR actions, and the culprit ultimately barely matters. The magistrate is secretly USING the loops
  to prolong his own life — so keeping you busy chasing a "culprit" serves HIM. The real investigation is
  WHY the Rule exists and WHO built the city. (A quest that interrogates its own premise.)

### CHARACTER-BACKGROUND REACTIVITY (build expresses you)
- You pick an origin (archaeologist, fugitive, etc.): an ARCHAEOLOGIST unlocks unique historical
  dialogue; a FUGITIVE gets a 25% speed boost that opens LEVEL GEOMETRY (routes) no other class can
  reach. Your backstory changes both conversation AND traversal (cf. Deus Ex build-routes Q17, Disco
  perception-gating Q05).

### THE FOUR ENDINGS (escalating engagement -> the canon)
- ENDING 1 (very early): flee alone/selfishly — abandon the city.
- ENDING 2 (~quarter in) & ENDING 3 (~third in): partial resolutions — help some, escape with a few;
  the "many shall suffer for the sins of the one" inscription read literally.
- THE GOLDEN/CANON ENDING (ending 4): the full solve — help enough citizens that Galerius wins the
  election and frees Duli (who gives the cistern key); gather the FOUR TABLETS; reach the Obelisk in the
  Grand Temple; CONFRONT THE CREATOR of the city and ARGUE them into ENDING the Golden Rule. The most
  people survive; the deepest mystery resolves; "the most emotionally satisfying." (Reachable as early
  as loop 4 if you KNOW the path — knowledge collapses the loops.)

### THE ARGUE-THE-CREATOR FINALE (debate as climax)
- The canon ending is won by CONVINCING the city's creator, through accumulated understanding, to end
  the Rule — a philosophical DEBATE resolved by everything you've learned (cf. Planescape's argue-the-
  final-boss Q12, Deus Ex social battles Q17). Knowledge + reason, not force, ends the horror.

## 2. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. ONE RULE MAKES A SOCIETY: a single high-concept law ("the many suffer for the sins of the one")
    turns a whole city into a moral pressure-cooker — every interaction is charged by the shared threat.
    A premise that generates endless situations from ONE idea.
W2. THE UNDEFINED SIN (philosophy as gameplay): refusing to fix "sin" turns the city into a surveillance
    state and forces the player to reason about morality — the ambiguity IS the content (cf. no-clean-
    answer, Q03/Q04).
W3. THE LOOP CARRIES KNOWLEDGE + INVENTORY: re-sequence events with what you know/hold — solve later
    what was impossible earlier (cf. Outer Wilds Q18, Anju Q11); here YOU trigger the loop.
W4. SINNING AS A TOOL (the dark exploit): you can break the Rule for gain and carry loot forward,
    dooming the (resettable) city — a morally-loaded systemic verb that reinvents "winning."
W5. THE RED-HERRING PREMISE: the stated task (find the culprit) is a deliberate misdirection that serves
    a hidden antagonist — the quest interrogates its OWN framing; the real mystery is deeper (cf. the
    MacGuffin-is-a-person twist, Q16).
W6. BACKGROUND REACTIVITY (build opens world): your origin changes dialogue AND physical routes (fugitive
    speed -> unique geometry) — replayable, build-expressive exploration (cf. Deus Ex Q17, Disco Q05).
W7. ESCALATING ENDINGS: four endings from selfish-early to full-canon — engagement depth maps to outcome
    goodness; the best ending demands the most understanding + help (cf. Megaton divergence + ME2
    preparation, Q06).
W8. KNOWLEDGE COLLAPSES THE LOOPS: the canon ending is reachable as early as loop 4 IF you know the path
    — mastery lets you skip the grind (cf. Anju two-loops Q11, Outer Wilds Q18). Knowing = speed.
W9. THE ARGUE-THE-CREATOR CLIMAX: end the horror by DEBATING its author with accumulated understanding —
    reason as the win condition (cf. Planescape Q12, Deus Ex Q17). Comprehension resolves the crisis.
W10. PROVEN AS A MOD FIRST: it began as a Skyrim quest mod (3M+ downloads) — the QUEST-CRAFT stood on its
    own before the graphics/engine. LESSON: a great quest is writing + structure, not production value.

## 3. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)

COLLECTIVE-MORALITY engine that rhymes with the Amalgamation's "no one may be lost."
- W1/W2 (one rule makes a society + undefined sin): bank a Bohemia "GOLDEN RULE" pressure-cooker scenario
  — a district/faction under a single collective-punishment law (the Network's "any dissent and the zone

  charges every interaction and the DEFINITION is contestable. Collective punishment as a moral-horror

  Amalgamation IS a "no one may be lost" rule taken to horror.
- W3/W8 (loop carries knowledge + collapses with mastery): our succession FOLD already carries knowledge
  forward; validate the Forgotten-City sharpening — an heir who KNOWS the path resolves a multi-loop/
  multi-generation crisis FASTER (knowledge collapses the grind; ties Outer Wilds Q18, Anju Q11).
- W4 (sinning as a tool): a Bohemia systemic verb where the dynasty can BREAK a rule for gain at a
  (resettable or heavy) cost — steal the cure now, doom the zone, carry it forward — morally-loaded

- W7 (escalating endings): our Liberate/Respect/Become finale is this — escalating engagement maps to
  outcome; the deepest ending demands the most understanding + help (validated; the canon "help
  everyone" path = our most-assembled Reconstruction).
- W9 (argue-the-creator climax): our endgame ALREADY argues the Amalgamation down (Q050 beaten by
  knowing) — Forgotten City + Planescape + Deus Ex all converge: END THE HORROR BY DEBATING ITS AUTHOR
  with accumulated understanding. Bank this as the canonical shape of our finale.
- W10 (proven as a mod first): the reminder that our quests live or die on WRITING + STRUCTURE, not art

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE OUTSIDER (the player, origin chosen)** — wants out, then wants everyone out, in that order, and the distance between those wants is the game. Will trade: loops. Will never say out loud: that the rewind makes their compassion cheap until the day it doesn't. FUNCTION: the only person in the city whose mistakes are refundable, which is the loudest moral spotlight ever aimed at a protagonist.

**SENTIUS (the magistrate)** — wants the loops to CONTINUE; they are keeping him alive. Will trade: the mission itself ("find who breaks the Rule") — a red herring he manufactures fresh each loop. Will never say out loud: that a solved mystery is his death sentence. FUNCTION: the quest-giver as the hidden antagonist (W5). The task is the trap; the employer is the case.

**THE ~23 CITIZENS** — want, severally: an election won (Galerius), a debt escaped (Duli), a love kept, a shrine tended, a secret buried — and, unanimously, want NOBODY ELSE to sin, because anyone's sin is everyone's execution. Will never say out loud: their own candidate sins; the surveillance state lives in every hushed voice. FUNCTION: the pressure-cooker (W1). One rule makes every small want a public safety issue.

**THE GOLDEN RULE** — wants obedience to a definition it refuses to provide (W2). FUNCTION: the antagonist-as-law. Murder counts. Theft counts. Does a lie? An intent? A failure to act? The ambiguity is the philosophy engine and the fear.

**THE CREATOR** — wants, beneath everything, not to be argued with. FUNCTION: the finale. The horror has an author, the author has reasons, and the reasons can be beaten by a mortal who did the reading (W9).

**THE PORTAL** — wants nothing; it simply always takes you back. FUNCTION: the mercy that makes the cruelty studyable.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees; lines paraphrased, structure exact; the loop is a meta-node every tree can exit through)

NODE: SENTIUS_TASKING — the magistrate's office, entry: loop 1, and re-entry every loop (he remembers nothing; you remember everything)
  Someone will break the Rule today. Find them. Stop them.
  > "Who do you suspect?"           [gate: none] -> a name; a DIFFERENT name if your actions changed the day; the culprit is weather, not plot (W5)
  > "What counts as a sin?"         [gate: none] -> he doesn't know either, and rules the city on it anyway; the ONE LINE DOING THE WORK
  > "Why are you so calm?"          [gate: loop knowledge] -> the crack: a man this serene about doom has an arrangement with it
  TRAP: doing the assignment. Diligently chasing culprits serves Sentius forever; the loop count is his pulse. The quest's stated objective is its own misdirection.

NODE: STREET_OF_FEAR — any citizen, any loop
  Everyone speaks in the soft voice of people who share one noose.
  > (press about the Rule)          [gate: none] -> philosophy, rationed by terror: is evil universal or is it whatever the statues say
  > (press about each other)        [gate: none] -> the surveillance state out loud: everyone is watching everyone, politely
  > (origin line: archaeologist)    [gate: origin] -> the historical read others can't see; NODE-TEXT CHANGES BY WHO YOU WERE BEFORE THE PORTAL (W6)
  NOVERB: "You're all going to be fine." Nobody can promise it, including you, and the game writes no line for the comfort. Cross-ref #126's removed comfort verb: the reassurance that would be a lie does not exist.

NODE: THE_DARK_EXPLOIT — anywhere, any loop; the machine's most honest node
  The cure is behind a merchant's counter. Someone is dying across town. The portal is right there.
  > [steal it]                      [gate: none] -> THE RULE TRIGGERS: the city turns gold screaming, you run for the portal, and next loop THE CURE IS IN YOUR POCKET (W3, W4)
  > (earn it instead)               [gate: time, persuasion, errands] -> slower, cleaner, sometimes impossible this loop
  > (let the victim die this loop)  [gate: none] SILENCE -> the loop resets that too; mercy deferred is refundable here, and the refund is the moral vertigo
  WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing mechanical. Everything else. Sinning is a TOOL, the tool works, and the only witness who persists across loops is you.

NODE: GALERIUS_ELECTION — the plaza, entry: mid-arc
  A good man can win the city, if enough small kindnesses stack.
  > (help citizen after citizen, loop after loop) [gate: accumulated aid] -> Galerius wins -> Duli freed -> the CISTERN KEY: the canon path is paved with errands (W7)
  > (skip it)                       [gate: none] -> endings 1-3 remain; the city keeps its magistrate and its arithmetic

NODE: THE_OBELISK — the Grand Temple, entry: FOUR TABLETS gathered, the cistern passed
  The Creator, at last. The debate the whole game was tuition for.
  > "The Rule is unjust, and here is the evidence." [gate: accumulated understanding: the tablets, the citizens' stories, the Rule's own contradictions] -> THE ARGUMENT LANDS: the Golden Rule ENDS (W9)
  > (argue from anger, not evidence) [gate: none] TRAP -> the Creator has heard anger for millennia; only comprehension is novel
  > (attack)                        [gate: none] -> the horror survives another mortal
  KNOWLEDGE COLLAPSES THE LOOPS (W8): a player who knows this path can reach this node by loop 4. Mastery is speed; the grind was only ever ignorance.
