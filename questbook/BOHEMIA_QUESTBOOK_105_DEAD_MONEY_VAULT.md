# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 105: BEGIN AGAIN / GETTING TO IT ISN'T THE HARD PART (The Sierra Madre Vault, Dead Money)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the heist where the prize is real, sitting on the floor, and the game's
entire thesis is that you have to walk away from it — a casino that never opened, a bomb collar, three
people who cannot let go of anything, and 37 gold bars that weigh more than you can carry. Complete event
flow, Elijah's monologue, the collar, the threes, the vault, the trap, Vera's letter, the honest flaws, and
Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our SURVIVAL ACCOUNTING, our fold, our AMALGAMATION, our
post-economic-collapse premise, our city-builder, + our ART PHILOSOPHY. Obsidian (design: **CHRIS
AVELLONE**). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: Dead Money's finale, Fallout: New Vegas (2010), running from "Sierra Madre Grand Opening" through
"Last Luxuries" and the vault. Avellone gave the team homework: "**HE GAVE THE TEAM A NEAT HOMEWORK
ASSIGNMENT OF WATCHING MOVIES THAT WOULD BE RELEVANT TO THE STORY/DESIGN (BATTLE ROYALE, CUBE, LIFEBOAT,
TREASURE OF THE SIERRA MADRE).**" Four movies. Four locked-room stories about people who can't leave and
won't cooperate.

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE THESIS IS FOUR WORDS AND THE ENTIRE DLC IS A MACHINE FOR PROVING THEM (STARTLINGLY our ART PHILOSOPHY
  + fold): **LET GO. BEGIN AGAIN.**
  - Elijah's opening narration, which is the best cold open in the medium: "**YOU'VE HEARD OF THE SIERRA
    MADRE CASINO. WE ALL HAVE, THE LEGEND, THE CURSES. FOOLISHNESS ABOUT IT LYING IN THE MIDDLE OF THE CITY
    OF THE DEAD, BURIED BENEATH A BLOOD-RED CLOUD. A BRIGHT, SHINING MONUMENT LURING TREASURE HUNTERS TO
    THEIR DOOM... THE OPENING WAS SUPPOSED TO SYMBOLIZE A ROAD TO A BRIGHTER FUTURE, NOT JUST FOR THE
    WORLD... BUT FOR ALL WHO CAME TO ITS DOORS. **A CHANCE FOR ANYONE TO BEGIN AGAIN.** EXCEPT - THE SIERRA
    MADRE NEVER OPENED. THE WAR FROZE IT IN TIME, LIKE A BIG FLASHBULB GOING OFF. THE GRAND OPENING - ONE BIG
    ENDING OF HUMANITY. IT'S STILL OUT THERE, IN THE WASTES, PRESERVED, JUST WAITING FOR SOMEONE TO CRACK IT
    OPEN. **BUT GETTING TO IT. THAT'S NOT THE HARD PART. IT'S LETTING GO.**"
  - **A casino built as a promise that anyone can start over, that never opened, that kills everyone who
    comes looking.** For a post-ECONOMIC-collapse Vegas game, that is our premise handed to us in a
    paragraph (STARTLINGLY our VEGAS GEOGRAPHY + post-economic-collapse premise + AMALGAMATION).
- IT STRIPS YOU TO NOTHING AND MAKES A STIMPAK MATTER AGAIN (the design — direct for our survival
  accounting): "**YOU CAN'T BRING ANY ITEMS INTO THE DLC; THEY ARE ALL REMOVED FOR THE DURATION OF YOUR
  STAY.**"
  - Avellone's stated goal: "**IN SHORT, I WANTED MIRACLE ITEMS LIKE STIMPAKS TO FEEL AMAZING AGAIN RATHER
    THAN CAUSE PLAYERS TO SHRUG.**"
  - A reviewer: "**NEW VEGAS GETS A BIT POWER FANTASY TOWARDS THE MIDDLE-END OF THE GAME... AND DEAD MONEY
    IMMEDIATELY THROWS A WRENCH INTO THIS BY STRIPPING YOU OF BASICALLY EVERYTHING.**"
  - The whole DLC is a hard reset on the player's economy — and it works because scarcity is a FEELING you
    can restore by taking things away (cf. our survival accounting + THREE CURRENCIES + inventory).
- AND THE ART DEPARTMENT WAS BRIEFED ON EMOTIONS, NOT OBJECTS (the method — our FACTORY LAW): "**JOE SANABRIA
  WORKED WITH LEVEL DESIGNERS TO HAVE THE ART AND DESIGN REINFORCE EACH OTHER WHICH FOR DEAD MONEY MEANT
  REINFORCING ISOLATION, TERROR, OBSCURING THE PLAYER'S VIEW, CLAUSTROPHOBIA, AND GREED, WHICH WAS REFLECTED
  IN THE DECAYING CASINO, COMPANION PERSONALITIES, AND GRAFFITI.**"
  - Five words — isolation, terror, obscured view, claustrophobia, **GREED** — and everything from the
    architecture to the companions to the graffiti was checked against them (cf. our FACTORY LAW + ART
    PHILOSOPHY + HD tile repo).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE HOOK IS A RADIO YOU CANNOT RESIST
- "**WITH THE DOOR OPEN, WALK DOWN THE HALLWAY TO THE CURIOUSLY LIT-UP RADIO AT THE END. WHEN YOU GET CLOSE
  TO IT, ALL HELL BREAKS LOOSE AND YOU END UP IN THE SIERRA MADRE.**"
- You are kidnapped by a broadcast. A signal advertising a casino, playing in an empty bunker, that puts you
  to sleep and takes everything you own (cf. our electricity + AMALGAMATION + [READ]).
### STAGE 1 — THE COLLAR (the leash is the radio)
- "**YOUR COLLAR WILL EXPLODE IF YOU GET TOO CLOSE TO RADIOS. THERE ARE DAMAGED RADIOS AND SHIELDED RADIOS;
  FOR DAMAGED RADIOS, YOU CAN JUST BLAST THEM WITH A GUN, BUT FOR SHIELDED RADIOS, YOU'LL NEED TO EITHER
  AVOID THEM COMPLETELY OR TURN THEM OFF VIA A TERMINAL.**"
- The most hated mechanic in Fallout history, and it is thematically perfect: **the thing that kills you is
  the casino's advertising.** Vera's voice, broadcast on a loop from a dead building, detonates your neck.
- Dean explains it: "**VERA'S BEAUTIFUL VOICE DOESN'T RUN ON CASINO POWER, EXACTLY. HER BROADCAST IS TIED
  INTO THE EMERGENCY BROADCAST SIGNAL FOR THE SIERRA MADRE. YOU KNOW, IN CASE OF ATTACK? WELL, GUESS WHAT -
  THE NIGHT OF THE GALA EVENT, THE EMERGENCY SIGNAL WENT OUT ALL RIGHT.**"
- The siren that was supposed to save everyone is the thing that's been killing them for 200 years
  (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + NETWORK + electricity; the safety system that became the hazard).
### STAGE 2 — ELIJAH (the man who found a better weapon than a weapon)
- "**AFTER THE FALL OF HELIOS ONE, FATHER ELIJAH, FORMERLY AN ELDER OF THE MOJAVE BROTHERHOOD OF STEEL, SET
  OUT TO FIND NEW WEAPONS TO ERADICATE THE NCR, AND IN THE PROCESS, HE DISCOVERED THE SIERRA MADRE. IN DOING
  SO, HE LURED OTHERS, INCLUDING THE COURIER, TO THE RESORT, FORCING THEM TO NAVIGATE THE HAZARDS OF THE
  MADRE AND PLAY THE PAWNS IN HIS GAME.**"
- "**IF THE COURIER WISHED TO ESCAPE THIS HELLISH PLACE, THEY WOULD HAVE TO BE CLEVER, AND USE THEIR NEW
  ALLIES AS FATHER ELIJAH DID.**"
- The game's structure IS its indictment: to escape a man who uses people as tools, **you use the same three
  people as tools.** The DLC makes you do Elijah's method to beat Elijah (cf. our conscience system +
  companions + Megaton law).
### STAGE 3 — THE THREES (three people who cannot let go of anything)
- "**THIS DLC ABSOLUTELY LOVES DOING THINGS IN THREES -- THREE SETS OF THREES.**"
- **DEAN DOMINO:** a lounge singer, now a 200-year-old ghoul, who has spent two centuries in a dead casino
  because he cannot stop wanting to rob a man who died in 2077. "**DEAN IS OBSESSED WITH FUCKING OVER THE
  CENTURIES-DEAD FREDERICK SINCLAIR.**" His grudge outlived civilization.
  - His line, and it's the DLC's whole joke: "**GETTING REAL TIRED OF TOURIST SEASON. NO OFFENSE, BUT YOU
    GUYS ARE REALLY LOWERING THE BAR.**"
- **CHRISTINE:** a Brotherhood assassin hunting Elijah, who had her voice cut out. "**CHRISTINE WANTS REVENGE
  ON ELIJAH.**" Her perks are named **VICTORY AND SACRIFICE, UNENDING DUTY,** and **BEGIN AGAIN.**
- **DOG/GOD:** a nightkin with a split personality — "**THE BEAST, DOG, CONSUMED BY HUNGER... AND THE OTHER
  IN REVERSE... CONSUMED BY CONTROL. BOTH WERE DRIVEN BY THE NEED FOR EACH OTHER.**"
- Every companion is a different flavor of not letting go: a grudge, a vendetta, and a man who can't let go
  of **himself** (cf. our companions + conscience system + fold).
### STAGE 4 — THE DIALOGUE IS A TRAP (the skill check that ruins it)
- The walkthrough's warning, and it's the best design detail in the DLC: "**A FEW THINGS TO WATCH OUT FOR:
  FIRST AND FOREMOST, DO *NOT* CHOOSE THE BARTER 50 CHECK IF YOU WANT A GOOD OUTCOME.**"
- **PASSING a skill check gives you the WORSE ending.** Barter — the greed skill, the money skill — is the
  option that damns Dean. The game punishes you for reaching for the transactional solution (STARTLINGLY our
  conscience system + standing; the successful check that's the wrong answer).
### STAGE 5 — THE VAULT (the prize is real and it's on the floor)
- "**INSIDE THE VAULT CONTAINS 17 STACKS OF PRE-WAR MONEY, 37 GOLD BARS, AND VARIOUS EQUIPMENT.**"
- **37 gold bars.** Each one weighs 35 pounds. They're worth about 10,500 caps apiece. They are RIGHT THERE,
  and the game is completely honest about it: this is the treasure, it is real, and you cannot lift it.
- "**IT MAY SEEM TRITE OR OVERBEARING TO HAVE YOUR REWARD FOR A 4-5 HOUR PUZZLE GAUNTLET BE SOME GOLD BARS
  THAT YOU HAVE TO FORSAKE, BUT ON A META LEVEL I APPRECIATE IT. YOU CAN'T TAKE ALL THE GOLD BARS WITH YOU
  WITHOUT EXPLOITS OR CHEATS. YOU HAVE TO MAKE A CHOICE: BE CONTENT WITH LESS, FOR AT LEAST YOU HAVE
  SOMETHING, OR LEAVE EMPTY-HANDED.**"
- **The carry weight IS the moral** (cf. our inventory + survival accounting + logistics; the encumbrance
  system as a thesis statement).
### STAGE 6 — THE TRAP (you have to walk away while he's still talking)
- The escape, from a community guide on stealing everything: "**ONCE YOU FIRST ENTER THE VAULT, PICK UP ALL
  37 OF THE GOLD BARS... THEN SELECT THE DIALOG OPTION THAT GETS FATHER ELIJAH TO COME DOWN INTO THE VAULT.
  AFTER YOU ARE FINISHED SPEAKING TO HIM IN THE VAULT, HIDE BEHIND THE CYLINDER STRUCTURE... AS HE
  APPROACHES THE VAULT, SLOWLY MOVE AROUND THE STRUCTURE TO REMAIN OUT OF HIS FIELD OF VIEW... AFTER MAKING
  IT TO THE EXIT, HE WILL TRIGGER THE ALARM, TRAPPING HIM INSIDE THE VAULT.**"
- **You beat the villain by letting him have the treasure.** He walks into the vault because he cannot not
  walk into the vault, and the door closes. The achievement is called **"SAFETY DEPOSIT BOX."**
- And to trick him you need "**EXPLOSIVES, SCIENCE, REPAIR, LOCKPICK, BARTER, OR SNEAK, AT 75**" — you have
  to be good enough at the heist for him to believe you want it (cf. our conscience system + Megaton law +
  PACIFIST LAW; the final boss defeated by his own greed, without a fight).
### STAGE 7 — VERA'S LETTER (the twist is a love letter, and it's a rejection)
- The vault's real contents: the truth about Sinclair, Vera, and what the Madre was. And a message:
- "**LOVE, LIFE, FAMILY, THOSE TO CARE FOR AND THOSE WHO WILL CARE FOR YOU: TO THOSE WHO KNOW THESE JOYS,
  THE SIERRA MADRE HOLDS LITTLE THEY DON'T ALREADY HAVE. OUT IN THE WORLD, BEYOND THESE WALLS, THAT IS YOUR
  CHANCE TO BEGIN AGAIN. I HOPE THAT YOU WILL RETURN IN HAPPIER TIMES. UNTIL THEN, THE SIERRA MADRE, AND I,
  WILL HOLD YOU IN OUR HEARTS.**"
- The treasure at the bottom of the murder casino is **a note telling you the casino was never worth it**
  (STARTLINGLY our fold + [READ] + ART PHILOSOPHY + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope).
### STAGE 8 — THE ENDINGS (everyone gets to begin again except the ones who don't)
- **Dean**, if he lives: "**ONCE HE LEAVES THE THEATER, THE SIERRA MADRE RECOGNIZES HIM AS A GUEST, AND MANY
  DOORS OPEN TO HIM. HE HAD TO ADMIT, IT HAD BEEN BUILT TO LAST. DURING HIS SEARCH, HE FINDS THE FINAL
  RECORDS OF VERA AND SINCLAIR, AND REALIZES WHAT HAPPENED THE NIGHT THE BOMBS FELL. **HE WAS STRANGELY SAD
  FOR A MOMENT, AND HAD NO IDEA WHY.** SO, SHRUGGING IT OFF, HIS MIND TURNED TO VEGAS.**"
- **Christine stays.** She becomes the Madre's warden, so nobody else finds it. A player, distraught on
  Steam: "**I'VE MANAGED TO STEAL GOLDS.. FOR US.. TO BEGIN AGAIN.. BUT.. I'M ALONE NOW :( WHERE IS SHE..
  ARE YOU STILL THERE CHRISTINE? I WANNA BELIEVE THAT.. WHY HAVE YOU LEFT ME?**"
- **If ALL of them survive**, the ending is the best one and it's not triumphant: "**THEY WILL THINK ABOUT
  GATHERING BY THE FOUNTAIN AND WAITING FOR THE COURIER, BUT THE COURIER'S SILENCE MADE THEM UNEASY, AND
  FEARING OF TURNING ON EACH OTHER MADE THEM HESITATE, LEAVING THE GOODBYES UNSPOKEN. THE MESSAGE AT THE
  FOUNTAIN WAS ENOUGH AND THERE WAS NO NEED TO ADD MORE FAREWELLS ON TOP OF ALL THEY HAD SUFFERED.**"
- Nobody says goodbye. They just go. That's what letting go looks like (cf. our companions + fold +
  no-clean-answer).
- And the worst ending: "**IN THE YEARS FOLLOWING, COMMUNITIES ACROSS THE WEST DIE, AS TRACES OF THE CLOUD
  BEGAN TO DRIFT OVER LANDS HELD BY THE NCR.**"

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE COLLAR IS THE MOST HATED MECHANIC IN THE FRANCHISE: perfect theme, and it converts a horror atmosphere
  into a beeping-management minigame. Players quit over it. LESSON (~2nd confirmation of Ocean House Q99's
  law): your thesis-carrying mechanic can be the reason people never see your thesis.
- PLAYERS FIND IT MISERABLE AND SAY SO: "**DEAD MONEY ISN'T WHAT I'D CALL A 'FUN' DLC. EVEN AT ITS MOST
  GRIPPING I CAN SEE WHY MOST PEOPLE CONSIDER IT A SNOOZEFEST.**" LESSON: "the tedium is the point" is a
  real defense and it is not a shield — our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope needs the THREAD.
- THE THEME IS STATED OVER AND OVER: "**THE ENDLESS REPETITION OF ITS FOUR KEY WORDS: LET GO, BEGIN
  AGAIN.**" LESSON (ours, direct): our GDD's rule is every design decision carries a life lesson underneath
  **WITHOUT THE GAME EVER PREACHING IT** — Dead Money preaches, and it's the one thing to not copy.
- THE PLAYERS BEAT THE MORAL WITH A SPREADSHEET: an entire guide exists titled "**HOW TO STEAL ALL 37 GOLD
  BARS FROM THE SIERRA MADRE VAULT,**" opening with: "**'TO BEGIN AGAIN, YOU NEED TO LET GO' IS THE MESSAGE
  OF THIS DLC, BUT I DIDN'T GO THROUGH THE HELL OF GHOST PEOPLE, BEAR TRAPS, HOLOGRAMS, AND THAT STUPID
  BOMB COLLAR JUST TO BE AWARDED WITH KNOWLEDGE AND WISDOM.**" LESSON (sharp): if your moral is enforced by
  a stat (carry weight), it is a PUZZLE, and players will solve it. A theme enforced by a number isn't a
  theme, it's a lock.
- THE BEST COMPANION EXITS OFFSCREEN: Christine simply vanishes — "**THE GAME JUST REMOVE[S] HER FROM THE
  WORLD SPACE**" — and there's no scene. Players go looking for her for years. LESSON: an offscreen goodbye
  is a bug in an emotional system, even when it's thematically correct.
- THE CROSSOVER THREADS ARE MISSING: "**IT WAS KINDA FRUSTRATING THAT THERE'S NO OPTION TO TELL VERONICA YOU
  FOUND CHRISTINE OR VICE VERSA, YOU'D THINK THAT'D BE KINDA IMPORTANT. WEIRDLY ENOUGH THERE IS AN OPTION TO
  TELL VERONICA YOU FOUND ELIJAH.**" You can report the villain and not the woman she loved. LESSON: build
  the reunion you set up.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**ELIJAH** — wants the Sierra Madre's technology, and has stopped being able to want anything smaller. Will trade: your life, held in a bomb collar, against your usefulness. Will never say: that the casino has already eaten him; he narrates YOUR greed while modeling his own. FUNCTION: the voice on the radio, the collar's other end, and the vault's final trap — his monologue is the timer.

**DOG/GOD, DEAN, CHRISTINE** — three companions, three grips that cannot release: hunger that swallowed a person, vanity that outlived its era, a voice stolen and returned wrong. Each wants ONE thing let go of and cannot. FUNCTION: the thesis in three bodies before the vault states it in gold.

**VERA (dead)** — wanted Sinclair spared the truth. FUNCTION: the letter in the vault — the twist is a love letter and it's a rejection; the treasure room's real content is paper.

**THE GOLD (37 bars)** — wants nothing; weighs everything. FUNCTION: the test object. Carry-weight as moral instrument.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees)

NODE: THE_RADIO_LEASH — throughout, entry: collar on
  Elijah's channel is a conversation you cannot hang up. Options exist inside his calls, but the master gate is spatial: speakers and static ARE dialogue-denial zones. THE MECHANIC: the antagonist controls WHERE talking is survivable. Conversation as territory.

NODE: THE_VAULT_FLOOR — entry: inside, gold in reach
