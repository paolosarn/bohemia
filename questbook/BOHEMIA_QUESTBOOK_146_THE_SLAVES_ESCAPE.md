# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #146 — "THE SLAVE'S ESCAPE (THE UNAUTHORED QUEST)"
**Game:** Kenshi (2018)
**Studio:** Lo-Fi Games (Chris Hunt, near-solo, twelve years)
**Quest:** the game's most famous story, which nobody wrote: capture, enslavement, compliance or defiance, the long learning of the cage, and the escape — produced entirely by systems colliding, experienced by millions of players as if it had been authored, retold in thousands of near-identical testimonies.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined from the whole-game teardown, Q55, per the mining-pool rule)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map (rendered from systems, per the #141 discipline: when the machine has no dialogue, the format documents the machine's actual option space).
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia is a roguelite city-builder whose world simulates beneath the player. The compile needs at least one file that answers: what does a QUEST look like when no quest system exists — what systemic ingredients make players SWEAR there was a story? Kenshi's slavery arc is the medium's cleanest specimen: an unauthored quest so reliable it functions as content, mined here for its load-bearing parts. It is also the ethics test case: a systemic game that includes slavery as a condition the player suffers, escapes, and abolishes — never a power fantasy the player operates. That line matters for a Vegas of the collapse.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**Nobody wrote the quest. The world just has rules, the rules have teeth, and the teeth make stories with the exact shape of authored drama — inciting defeat, captivity, education, escape, and revenge — every time, for everyone, differently.**

Kenshi has no quests. No journal, no objectives, no story flags. What it has is: combat that you lose (the player starts weaker than nearly everything); defeat that maims instead of killing (unconsciousness, bleeding, per-limb damage); factions with economic logic (slavers harvest the fallen); institutions with schedules (slave camps have shifts, foremen, meals, cages); and skills that grow by use — INCLUDING the skills of being a prisoner. Lockpicking trains on your own shackles. Stealth trains on guards who catch you. Toughness trains every time a beating doesn't kill you.

Out of those rules, the same unauthored quest assembles itself for almost every player: you push too far, you fall, you wake shackled in a line of other property. And then the genius nobody scripted: THE CAGE IS A SCHOOL. Compliance keeps you fed while your stats crawl; every failed escape trains the exact skills the next attempt needs. The institution's punishments are the curriculum. Weeks later a player walks out of the camp with maxed stealth, hardened toughness, lockpicking mastery — a character forged by the specific shape of their captivity — and the community's testimonies all carry the same beat structure, independently: the fall, the despair, the learning, the night it finally worked. Some go back to burn the camp and free the rest. The game never asked them to. There is nothing to turn in.

The craft question this file mines: WHICH rules made that story inevitable? Not "simulation" in general — five specific properties, portable, listed in the W's: defeat that continues instead of ending; institutions with legible routine; punishment that trains; property-status as a faction relationship (slavers defend their goods; guards return escapees — the world ENFORCES the state, so exiting it is a real transition); and witnesses (other slaves, so the condition is shared and the escape can be plural).

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the cage teaches you everything you need to leave it, if you survive the lessons — and the ones who learn fastest are the ones who stop pretending they aren't in a cage.**

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**Formal note:** an unauthored quest's cast is a set of ROLES filled by whoever the simulation supplies — precisely the .bq casting model (roles, not names). Documented as such.

### THE FALLEN (the player character) — the property
- **What they want (arc-shaped by the systems):** first survival, then food, then the guard rotation's gaps, then the horizon. The simulation never states a goal; deprivation IS the goal-generator.
- **What they will never say out loud:** nothing — Kenshi's player character never speaks. The muteness is load-bearing: every intention lives in the player's plans, so the story feels self-authored because it literally is.
- **Function:** THE ACCUSED-turned-STUDENT. The quest's protagonist is a stat sheet being reforged by an institution.

### THE SLAVERS — the harvesters
- **What they want:** margin. The fallen are inventory; feeding, shackling, and recapture are business processes. No slaver monologue exists; the ideology is a price list.
- **What they will never say out loud:** also nothing — and the ABSENCE of villain speech is why the arc lands as horror rather than melodrama: the institution is banal, scheduled, and totally uninterested in the player's feelings.
- **Function:** OBSTACLE as economy. The antagonist is a business model.

### THE FOREMEN AND GUARDS — the routine
- **What they want:** shift-end. They patrol, feed, punish, and recapture on legible schedules.
- **Function:** THE CLOCKWORK. Their routine's gaps are the quest's actual dialogue options; learning the rotation is the "conversation."

### THE OTHER SLAVES — the witnesses
- **What they want:** varies by the simulation — some starve, some break, some bolt on their own schedules (the world runs THEIR arcs too, indifferent to yours).
- **Function:** WITNESSES and the plural stakes: their presence turns a private escape into a moral fork (alone, or everyone?), unprompted, every time.

### THE DESERT — the executor
- Escape's second act: the world between the camp and anywhere is what actually kills escapees — thirst, fauna, other factions with their own slavers.
- **Function:** THE COST. Freedom is a geography problem, and the geography does not celebrate you.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

**Emergent, but astonishingly stable across testimonies — the stages below are the arc's statistical skeleton, each stage a SYSTEM STATE, not a script.**

### STAGE 1 — THE FALL [systemically near-guaranteed early]
Entry: any lost fight in slaver range (and the start-as-slave scenario begins here by choice). Defeat = unconsciousness and bleeding, not death; slavers collect the fallen. What changes: faction-state SLAVE applied; gear confiscated; shackles equipped.
MISSABLE: only by never losing — which the stat curves make nearly impossible early. The quest's on-ramp is the game's difficulty itself.

### STAGE 2 — THE INTAKE [institutional]
Transport to the camp; cage assignment; the routine begins (feedings, work shifts, counts). The player learns the map of their captivity the way prisoners do: by schedule, not by exploration.

### STAGE 3 — THE CURRICULUM [the masterpiece stage]
Compliance keeps you alive; defiance TRAINS you. Slip the shackles (lockpicking XP), get caught (stealth lesson, beating, toughness XP), sit back in the cage (assault carries no story penalty — the institution just restores state and the clock resumes). Every failure banks skill; the punishments are the curriculum; the camp is a school that thinks it's a warehouse.
Exit condition: a skills-and-schedule threshold only the player perceives — the night the rotation's gap and the trained stats finally line up.

### STAGE 4 — THE BREAK [player-authored timing]
The run: shackles slipped, gate or fence, guards' cones, the alarm, the desert. Branch-dense (see section 4). A failed break loops to stage 3 WITH INTEREST: better skills, tighter watch.

### STAGE 5 — THE HORIZON, OR THE RETURN [wholly unprompted]
Escape ends the systems' quest — no reward, no chime, nothing acknowledges it (see F1). What the community does next, reliably, is the part no simulation demanded: come back stronger, kill the business, open the cages. The unauthored quest generates its own unauthored sequel, out of nothing but the player's memory of stage 3. THE FINDING: institutions that teach their victims create their own abolitionists, in games as in history.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

**Formal note (the #141 discipline, extreme case):** Kenshi's arc contains almost no dialogue. Its conversations are TRANSACTIONS WITH ROUTINE — and the option lists below are real: they are the actual choice-sets the system state presents, at the cadence a dialogue tree would present lines. This is what a quest's conversation machine looks like when the writer is a scheduler.

### NODE S-1 — the intake. Speaker: the institution, via state change. Entry: stage 2.
The camp presents its standing offer, wordlessly, via mechanics:
> comply: work when hauled, eat when fed [gate: none] -> hunger managed, suspicion low, stats crawl. The survivable default; the trap is its comfort — compliance compounds NOTHING except time
> refuse work [gate: none] -> beatings (toughness XP), starvation risk, suspicion — defiance PRICED as tuition
> slip the shackles, right now, untrained [gate: none — always available, near-certain failure] -> caught, beaten, re-caged. TRAP-SHAPED but secretly the curriculum's enrollment: the failure trains the exact skill that failed
> (do nothing at all; wait) [gate: none] SILENCE -> the routine continues; other slaves' arcs advance around you; the world's indifference IS the reply text
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: time, always time. The camp's only fee is the calendar, and the player watches other characters' lives spending it.

### NODE S-2 — the rotation. Speaker: the guards' schedule. Entry: stage 3, ongoing.
The camp's routine, read as a dialogue whose lines repeat daily:
> observe the count and the shift-change [gate: attention only] -> KNOWLEDGE: the gap exists at a time and place. The quest's central gate is unmarked, unlogged, and stored entirely in the player's head — the purest knowledge-gate in the corpus (harder than #141's book: there isn't even a book)
> test the gap with a small defiance [gate: none] -> the schedule's response teaches its rigidity; suspicion rises; the experiment is priced
> recruit the cage: coordinate with other slaves [gate: their sim-state permits] -> plural escape becomes possible; so does betrayal-by-panic when one bolts early — the systems supply the heist-movie beats without a writer
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: suspicion, the meter the player never sees but learns to feel.

### NODE S-3 — the break. Speaker: the alarm. Entry: stage 4.
> the timed run through the learned gap [gate: KNOWLEDGE + trained stats] -> the desert, and stage 5
> the loud way: fight out with stolen tools [gate: trained combat] -> viable late; writes a body count the faction remembers
> carry a fallen fellow slave out [gate: strength, and the choice] -> slower, plural, the unprompted moral verb the community's testimonies return to again and again — nothing in the code rewards it; everything in the player does
> (fail) [gate: the dice] -> recapture, LOOPS to S-1 with interest: tighter watch, banked skills. The loop IS the difficulty curve of the unauthored quest
NOVERB "surrender your status" — there is no line, lever, or petition by which the world un-slaves you; the state ends by DISTANCE or by the faction's destruction only. The removed verb is the institution's whole truth: it does not negotiate, it only loses custody.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the safety of the routine. The community testimonies are unanimous that the night of the break is the most frightened players ever are in this game — of losing a save state nothing forces them to risk.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: no authored endings exist. The observed arc resolves through 5 stable attractor states, each multiplied by solo/plural and by what the desert does next.**

- **THE CLEAN BREAK:** out through the learned gap, into the desert's second gauntlet. The modal "ending," and only the middle of most testimonies.
- **THE LOUD BREAK:** fought out; faction wrath persists (slavers remember stock that killed staff); the escape buys a permanent enemy. The systems' price for catharsis.
- **THE LONG COMPLIANCE:** never break; the camp as a life. Real, documented, and the community's horror story about ITSELF — the trap ending nobody authored and some players live in.
- **THE RECAPTURE LOOP:** breaks that fail, compounding skills and watchfulness — the difficulty curve as narrative rising action, until it exits into one of the above.
- **THE RETURN:** the unprompted sequel — the freed player destroys the institution and opens the cages. No reward exists; testimony after testimony reports it as the most meaningful act of their run. The map's most important branch is the one the simulation never asked for.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE WORLD'S INDIFFERENCE EXTENDS TO THE TRIUMPH.** The escape completes and NOTHING marks it — no reaction, no recognition, not a line. The indifference that made the captivity honest also starves the payoff; players supply the meaning themselves or feel nothing. The pool file banked the general form (investment-makes-you-care carries everything); here it bites hardest, at the arc's climax.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the world may stay indifferent; the RECORD may not. Bohemia's fold writes the escape, the settlement's talk reflects it (bark-scale, cheap), and the missing-persons organ (#141) can even close the player's own case. Emergence supplies the story; the ledger supplies the witness. Never ship an emergent arc whose completion no system can see.

**F2 — THE CURRICULUM IS EXPLOITABLE INTO FARCE.** The same training rules that make the cage a school make it a GYM: veteran players deliberately enslave themselves to speed-train stats, cage-grinding toughness like a treadmill. The institution's horror collapses into an optimization spa the moment the player metagames it — documented endlessly, half-celebrated by the community.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** condition-states must resist being farmed for their training value: diminishing returns inside institutional states, and fold entries that RECORD voluntary re-entry (the ledger noticing you keep checking into cages is itself the deterrent — and material for the conduct book, #143 PORT 1). Suffering that improves you must not be bookable like a facility.

**F3 — THE ARC IS BRUTAL BY DEFAULT AND INVISIBLE BY DESIGN.** New players meet the slavery arc with no framing, no content signal, and no difficulty mediation — for some it is the moment Kenshi becomes beloved; for others it is a wall of misery that ends the game unrefunded. The pure sandbox's honesty is also its total abdication of onboarding and consent.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** Bohemia is hardcore, but conditions of degradation get DIEGETIC telegraphing before they can apply (the valley's reputation, survivor testimony, visible slaver activity — the world warning about itself is world-building, not softening), and the dial governs their intensity. NO DAMAGE BEFORE THE DIAL extends to institutional cruelty: the system exists at full honesty, and the player meets it informed. ## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. DEFEAT CONTINUES INSTEAD OF ENDING. Losing produces a NEW GAME STATE (captivity) rather than a load screen — the single rule doing the most narrative work in the entire design. No continuation, no arc.
W2. THE INSTITUTION HAS A SCHEDULE. Feedings, shifts, counts: routine makes captivity LEGIBLE, and legibility turns suffering into a puzzle. The quest's dialogue tree is a timetable.
W3. PUNISHMENT TRAINS. Failed escapes teach escaping; beatings teach surviving beatings. The curriculum property — consequence that transfers skill to its victim — is what converts a fail-loop into rising action.
W4. STATUS IS A FACTION RELATIONSHIP, ENFORCED. "Slave" is not a cutscene state; the world defends it (recapture, bounty-return, property logic), so exiting it is a real transition with real enemies. States that nothing enforces are costumes.
W5. THE PROTAGONIST IS MUTE AND THE VILLAIN HAS NO SPEECH. Intent lives in the player; the antagonist is a price list. With both mouths empty, the player's own narration fills the arc — which is why every testimony sounds authored: it was, by its teller.
W6. WITNESSES SHARE THE CONDITION. Other slaves make the state communal: solidarity, betrayal, and the carry-them-out verb all emerge from mere co-presence. One rule (others suffer this too) buys the whole ensemble drama.
W7. THE CENTRAL GATE IS PURE KNOWLEDGE. The rotation's gap is stored nowhere but the player's head — no journal, no marker. The game trusts observation absolutely, and the trust is why the break feels EARNED beyond any authored quest's capacity.
W8. TIME IS THE ONLY FEE. The camp charges calendar, and the world's other lives visibly spend theirs meanwhile — urgency without timers, dread without music. The indifferent simulation IS the clock.
W9. THE SEQUEL SELF-GENERATES. The return-and-burn-it ending is unprompted, unrewarded, near-universal: institutions that teach their victims recruit their destroyers. The design's deepest finding, and it emerged from the systems' shape, not a writer's plan.
W10. THE ARC IS STATISTICALLY STABLE. Thousands of testimonies, one beat structure, all different in detail: the mark of an attractor built from rules, which is the only kind of quest a proc-gen world can mass-produce. The existence proof Bohemia's emergent layer needs.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE UNAUTHORED-QUEST CHECKLIST [W1-W6 — the core port; the emergent layer's spec]
**System:** the dial / factions / the world simulation
The five portable properties, adopted as REQUIREMENTS for any Bohemia condition-state intended to generate stories (captivity, conscription, debt-bondage, quarantine — whichever survive Paolo's canon): (1) defeat CONTINUES into the state, never ends the run at the dial's mercy tiers; (2) the institution runs a LEGIBLE ROUTINE (Bohemia's 120 BPM world makes schedules native — shifts on the beat); (3) consequences inside the state TRAIN survival-relevant capability; (4) the state is a faction relationship the world ENFORCES; (5) the state is SHARED with simulated others. Compile rule: a condition-state missing any of the five is a cutscene wearing handcuffs, and does not ship as emergent content.
```
@TALK camp_routine speaker=foreman entry=state:captive
  @SAY Count's at dusk. Feed's after. You work, you eat. You run, you learn what running costs.
  @SAY That's the whole book of rules. Shortest book in the valley.
  @OPT "Then I work."             [gate: none] -> routine_comply
  @OPT "What happened to the last one who ran?"  [gate: none] -> routine_lesson  @DO set_flag asked_the_price
  @OPT (watch the shift change instead of answering) [gate: none] SILENCE -> routine_observe @DO set_flag reading_the_rotation
  @NOVERB "Let me buy my way out right now"
@END
```
**THE NOVERB IS THE PORT** (S-3's finding): the institution does not negotiate custody mid-scene; exits are earned through the systems. [PENDING, Paolo's call: whether captivity-class states exist, and every word of their canon.]

### PORT 2 — THE LEDGER AS THE MISSING WITNESS [F1's law]
**System:** the fold / settlement barks / the missing-persons organ (#141)
Every emergent arc's completion writes fold entries the world can SEE: the settlement's bark tables react to escape-class entries, the organ can close the player's own disappearance case, and the finale's read (#140/#143) can cite it. Emergence tells the story; the record proves someone was watching. Mechanism mine; bark contents Paolo's.

### PORT 3 — ANTI-FARM DISCIPLINE FOR CONDITION-STATES [F2's law]
**System:** progression / the conduct book (#143 PORT 1)
Training inside condition-states carries diminishing returns, and VOLUNTARY re-entry is itself a recorded conduct signal (three re-entries = the ledger has opinions). The cage can teach; it cannot be a subscription.

### PORT 4 — THE WORLD WARNS ABOUT ITSELF [F3's law]
**System:** rumor/testimony systems / [READ]
Degradation-class dangers are telegraphed diegetically before they can apply: survivor testimony in settlements, visible slaver/pressgang activity on roads, records readable in the organ. The warning layer is world-building that doubles as consent. Mechanism mine, contents empty per MECHANISM-MINE / CONTENTS-PAOLO'S.

### PORT 5 — SCHEDULES ON THE BEAT [W2, W8]
**System:** 120 BPM LAW / I-MOVE-YOU-MOVE / the patrol module
Bohemia's institutions already tick on the beat, and BOH_PATROL already walks owners on schedules — extend the same machinery to institutional routine (counts, feeds, shift-changes as beat-quantized events the player can LEARN the way Kenshi players learn rotations). The knowledge-gate (W7) then works natively: the gap in a routine is real, observable, and stored only in the player's head. The patrol wiring shipped 7/17 is the foundation; routine-actors are its second tenant.
