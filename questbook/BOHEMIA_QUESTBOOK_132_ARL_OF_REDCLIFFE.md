# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #132 — "THE ARL OF REDCLIFFE / CONNOR"
**Game:** Dragon Age: Origins (2009)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** A village besieged nightly by the dead; the source is upstairs — a child possessed by a demon he bargained with to save his father; and every road to saving him runs through someone's death, someone's blood magic, or a journey most players don't know they can afford.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE — FORMAT v2. Mined from the whole-game pool.
**Filed:** 7/16/26

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The monster is a child who made a good-faith deal to save his dying father, and every exit from the situation prices someone the child loves.**

Redcliffe's nights belong to walking corpses; the days to a militia of fishermen and a knight teaching them to hold a chokepoint. Win the night battle and the quest ascends to the castle and inverts: the horror's source is Connor, the arl's son — a secret mage (hidden by his mother from the templars' Circle) who, untrained, accepted a demon's bargain to keep his poisoned father alive. The demon now wears him part-time.

The fork is the corpus's cleanest FOUR-DOOR PRICE MENU:
1. **Kill Connor.** Fast, certain, a child's death by your order or hand.
2. **Let his mother Isolde die instead:** the maleficar Jowan performs a blood-magic ritual powered by her life to send a mage into the Fade after the demon. The mother OFFERS. She is volunteering to fund her son's rescue with herself — paying for a catastrophe her own secrecy built.
3. **The Circle route:** travel to the mage tower, secure lyrium and mages, return, and do the ritual CLEAN — nobody dies. It costs time (with a village and an arl on the clock), a completed other questline, and the knowledge that the option exists at all.
4. **Walk the Fade yourself** (inside routes 2/3): face the desire demon, who OFFERS TERMS — the quest's villain is also a negotiator, and you can leave the child possessed for a price paid to you.

**The design finding the corpus needs: THE GOOD ENDING IS A LOGISTICS PROBLEM.** Door three isn't gated by stats or morality — it's gated by knowing the world well enough to know help exists, and being willing to spend the travel. Mercy is a supply run.

The life lesson underneath: **most tragedies are not caused by evil; they are caused by love that couldn't afford the honest option and didn't know the clean one existed.**

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**CONNOR** — wanted his father to live; a child with unlicensed power and a salesman in his dreams. Surfaces occasionally, terrified, between possessions. FUNCTION: the monster as victim as child — the quest's whole weight in one small body.
**ISOLDE** — wanted her son kept OUT of the Circle (love as concealment) and her husband alive; her two loves compounded into the disaster. Now wants to pay for it: she volunteers for the blood ritual, out loud, insistently. Will never say: that hiding Connor caused this — she says it in everything but words. FUNCTION: the mother as the quest's price-setter and its cause.
**BANN TEAGAN** — wants the village to survive the night and the family to survive the truth; the quest's decent-man load-bearer. FUNCTION: the voice that keeps asking for the least-bad thing.
**JOWAN** — blood mage, poisoner of the arl (under Loghain's orders), now in a cell beneath the mess he helped make. Wants: to fix ONE thing. Will trade: the ritual — his crime's toolset offered as its partial remedy. FUNCTION: the tainted instrument — the quest asks if you'll use the poisoner's magic to save the poisoned man's son.
**THE DESIRE DEMON** — wants to keep the arrangement; failing that, wants a NEW arrangement — with whoever walks into the Fade. Will trade: power, pleasure, secrets, for being left in the child. FUNCTION: the counterparty who never stops being open for business (the fourth honest-ish devil — this one negotiates mid-eviction).
**THE VILLAGE MILITIA** — want dawn. FUNCTION: the night battle's stakes — the quest's first half spends fishermen to earn the right to face the real question.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

STAGE 0 — THE NIGHT BATTLE [MANDATORY]: barricades, oil, a militia of amateurs; the player can strengthen the defense (or not) and villagers live (or don't) by the preparation ledger. Consequence as headcount at dawn.
STAGE 1 — THE CASTLE: the secret passage; the interior's dead; Isolde's confession assembled in fragments — Connor, the demon, the hiding, Jowan.
STAGE 2 — THE PARLOR FORK: the household assembled; the four doors laid out in dialogue with everyone's advocate present (Teagan for least-harm, Isolde for herself, Jowan for his ritual, companions per their natures — Morrigan pragmatic, Wynne appalled, Alistair on the family's side).
STAGE 3A — THE SHORT DOORS: kill Connor, or take Isolde's offer (the ritual, on-screen, her life into the spell).
STAGE 3B — THE LONG DOOR: leave for the Circle with the situation held in fragile stasis; the tower questline; return with clean power.
STAGE 4 — THE FADE: whoever goes (you or a mage) faces the demon in its parlor; fight it, or HEAR ITS TERMS.
STAGE 5 — AFTER: Connor freed (remembers little, or enough), or dead, or quietly still occupied; the arl still poisoned (the quest hands its own sequel forward); the Fold of this family permanently reorganized around what was paid.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 4 doors × Fade-deal sub-branch × night-battle headcount shading × the arl's poisoning carried forward regardless.

B2 — ISOLDE'S RITUAL: mother's life -> Fade access -> demon evicted; the son wakes motherless in a house his survival cost her. Jowan's magic vindicated-ish; the templars' worldview vindicated-ish; nobody says either aloud.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

F1 — THE CLEAN ROUTE IS A KNOWLEDGE CHECK WEARING A FETCH QUEST. Door three requires knowing it exists; blind players in an emergency reasonably conclude the emergency's menu is complete. LAW: if mercy is gated on world-knowledge, the world must have TAUGHT it in reach of the fork (a Circle mention exists — thin, missable, Teagan-dependent).
F2 — STASIS IS FREE. The situation holds indefinitely while you tour the tower; the demon politely pauses. The long door's real price (risk during absence) is waived, hollowing the trade-off it advertises.
F3 — ISOLDE'S CONSENT IS UNDER-INTERROGATED. Her offer is grief and guilt wearing a decision; no node lets anyone ask if expiation-by-suicide-ritual is consent at all. The quest takes her yes at face value because the plot needs the door (cf. Q125 F8's ungiven argument — 2nd confirmation: THE HARD QUESTION THE SCENE NEEDS IS THE ONE IT OMITS).
F4 — CONNOR'S AFTERMATH IS A MEMORY-WIPE CONVENIENCE. Freed, he recalls conveniently little; the child pays the arc's lowest visible psychological bill. The quest spends everyone around him and comps his own room.
F5 — JOWAN'S REDEMPTION-ADJACENCY IS UNPRICED EITHER WAY. Use his ritual or don't, his own arc resolves elsewhere and thinner; the poisoner who offered his crime's tools as remedy never gets the reckoning that offer deserved.
F6 — THE DEMON'S DEALS ARE UNDERWEIGHT DOWNSTREAM. Take the payment and the world barely audits it; the quiet-corruption branch is quiet forever. (Perfect crime, banked — but a SERIES this long should have cashed it somewhere.)
F7 — THE NIGHT BATTLE AND THE PARLOR DON'T SPEAK. Villagers saved or lost at the barricades never enter the castle's moral arithmetic; the quest's two halves keep separate books.
F8 — COMPANION ADVOCACY IS APPROVAL-METER LEGIBLE. The parlor debate doubles as a min-max surface (who to please); the meter leaks into the deliberation (the anti-Q121: here everyone votes and the votes have prices).
F9 — THE TEMPLARS' ARGUMENT WINS SILENTLY. The whole disaster is an argument FOR the Circle's surveillance regime, and the quest never lets anyone say so or push back; its own politics go unexamined at their strongest moment.
F10 — THE ARL STAYS POISONED. The quest resolves the son and hands the father to a sequel-quest; completion that completes nothing the title promised. Banked as structure, flagged as bait.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1–W10)

W1. **FOUR DOORS, FOUR CURRENCIES, NO EXCHANGE RATE.** A child, a volunteer, a supply run, an integrity: the menu prices in incomparables and refuses conversion. The corpus's cleanest architecture of Q125-W9's law.
W2. **THE MONSTER MADE A GOOD-FAITH DEAL.** Connor's bargain was LOVE plus power minus training — the quest's horror has no malice anywhere in its causal chain, only concealment and emergency.
W3. **THE MOTHER SETS THE PRICE AND IS THE CAUSE.** Isolde's hiding built the bomb; Isolde's life is offered as payment — cause and currency in one person, and the quest lets her insist.
W4. **THE GOOD ENDING IS HOMEWORK.** Door three is gated on knowing the world and spending the travel: mercy as logistics, competence as compassion's admission fee. No stat, no alignment — just whether you learned the setting.
W5. **EVERY OPTION HAS A LIVING ADVOCATE IN THE ROOM.** The parlor stages the deliberation with all voices present and arguing — the corpus's best-BLOCKED decision scene; the player chooses inside a functioning argument, not a menu vacuum.
W6. **THE VILLAIN QUOTES PRICES MID-EVICTION.** The desire demon negotiates while being removed — the counterparty that never stops trading converts even the rescue's final room into a market.
W7. **CONSENT DOESN'T CLEAN IT.** Isolde's yes is explicit, repeated, formal — and the ritual is still monstrous, and the scene plays it as both. Pre-consented death, priced honestly.
W8. **THE PERFECT CRIME IS AVAILABLE.** Deal with the demon, walk out a hero, and no system ever checks: the quest trusts the player with an unaudited secret (and the trust is the test).
W9. **THE EMERGENCY'S MENU LIES BY OMISSION.** The fork presents as binary-plus-horror; the clean door exists OFF-MENU for those who know to ask. The quest models how real crises hide their best options behind composure and information.
W10. **THE NIGHT EARNS THE QUESTION.** The militia battle — fishermen, oil, barricades — spends the quest's first act making the player DEFEND the community whose ruling family's secret caused everything. You bleed for the village before learning the castle owed it the apology.
