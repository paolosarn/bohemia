# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 91: THE FIRE HAS TO BE FED WITH PEOPLE / GWYN WAS AFRAID OF US (Dark Souls)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the First Flame (a world running on a fire that must be fueled with the
most powerful beings alive), Gwyn's sacrifice (a god burning himself to postpone an ending he FEARED), the
Undead Curse as the SYMPTOM of a world held past its time, the Way of White (a religion that teaches you to
volunteer), the two serpents who are BOTH kingseekers, the ending where you simply walk out of the room, the
honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION (a system that must be fed to keep
running), our fold, our recorded-vs-unrecorded, + our LIBERATE finale. FromSoftware (Hidetaka Miyazaki).
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Dark Souls (FromSoftware, 2011). The kingdom of Lordran, at the end of the Age of Fire. The First
Flame is dying, and as it dies people stop being able to die — they become UNDEAD, and eventually HOLLOW.
You are one of them. A prophecy says you'll fix it. The prophecy was written by the people who benefit.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE WORLD RUNS ON A FIRE, AND THE FIRE EATS PEOPLE (the premise — startlingly our AMALGAMATION):
  "The Age of Fire [is] an age fueled by the First Flame, but THE FLAME IS FADING. In each Dark Souls game
  WE SET OUT TO RENEW IT. That's our goal, our holy mission. **WE FIGHT OUR WAY TO THE FIRE AND THEN WE
  OFFER OURSELVES UP TO IT, GIVING UP OUR BODIES AS IF WE ARE KINDLING** that helps the fire burn a little
  brighter for a little longer."
  - The fuel is specific and awful: "**THE FUEL FROM THE FIRE STEMS DIRECTLY FROM THE SOULS THE PLAYER HAS
    COLLECTED** and ultimately ACTS LIKE WOOD to kindle the flame."
  - Every era of light is bought by burning the strongest person alive. It's not a metaphor for sacrifice;
    it's a FUEL ECONOMY (cf. our AMALGAMATION + electricity currency + life-support buildings; the
    infrastructure that consumes people).
- THE UNDEAD CURSE IS THE SYMPTOM, NOT THE DISEASE (the diagnosis — the best worldbuilding logic here): "As
  the First Flame began to falter, the world became riddled with a new affliction: THE UNDEAD CURSE... **THE
  UNDEAD CURSE REPRESENTS THE WORLD'S UNNATURAL STATE AS IT STRUGGLES TO MAINTAIN THE AGE OF FIRE. WITH THE
  NATURAL ORDER DISRUPTED, DEATH ITSELF BECOMES DISTORTED.**"
  - People can't die. They just decay: "trapped in an ENDLESS CYCLE of life, death, and resurrection," until
    they go HOLLOW — "a feral state AFTER HOPE AND MEMORY HAVE FADED ENTIRELY."
  - The zombie plague isn't an enemy. It's what a world LOOKS LIKE when it's been kept alive past its time
    (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + death-math + recorded-vs-unrecorded; the dead who can't finish dying).
- GWYN DIDN'T SAVE THE WORLD, HE SAVED *HIS* WORLD (the antagonist's real motive): the primordial serpent
  Kaathe, in the first game, verbatim: "**LORD GWYN TREMBLED AT THE DARK. CLINGING TO HIS AGE OF FIRE, AND
  IN DIRE FEAR OF HUMANS, AND THE DARK LORD WHO WOULD ONE DAY BE BORN AMONGST THEM… LORD GWYN RESISTED THE
  COURSE OF NATURE. BY SACRIFICING HIMSELF TO LINK THE FIRE, AND COMMANDING HIS CHILDREN TO SHEPHERD THE
  HUMANS… GWYN HAS BLURRED YOUR PAST, TO PREVENT THE BIRTH OF THE DARK LORD.**"
  - PopMatters' read: "**SO GWYN KEPT THE WORLD ALIVE FOR HIMSELF AND HIS CHILDREN. In telling his children
    to 'shepherd the humans' HE WAS REALLY TELLING THEM TO CONVINCE US THAT WE TOO NEED TO SACRIFICE
    OURSELVES TO THE FIRST FLAME.**"
  - "Gwyn used his power to CREATE A FABLE THAT WOULD LAST LONG AFTER HIS DEATH, and in doing so, HE
    PREVENTED THE RISE OF THE DARK LORD."
  - The god's noble sacrifice was a delaying action against HIS OWN SUCCESSORS, and the religion built on it
    exists to recruit more volunteers (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + Network + [READ]; the founding myth
    as a recruitment tool).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (a machine that runs on faith)
===============================================================================

### THE OPENING CUTSCENE TELLS YOU EVERYTHING, AND NOBODY NOTICES (the craft note)
- "What's funny about the ingrained perception that Dark Souls throws you into the game WITHOUT ANY
  NARRATIVE ORIENTATION is that **ITS OPENING CUTSCENE SPELLS IT ALL OUT**: the world began with an age of
  twilight, before differentiation had occurred... with the appearance of fire up rose powerful champions
  possessing the souls of lords, who slaughtered the dragons and ushered in the Age of Fire... **TIME HAS
  PASSED AND NOW THE AGE OF FIRE IS DRAWING TO ITS END.**"
- "**THE ONLY THING THE INTRODUCTION DOESN'T SPELL OUT EXPLICITLY IS WHY the Age of Fire is ending OR WHAT
  IS TO BE DONE ABOUT IT.**" They told you the setting and withheld exactly one thing: the MEANING (cf. our
  [READ] + ART PHILOSOPHY; tell them everything, withhold the why).
### THE RELIGION EXISTS TO MAKE YOU VOLUNTEER (the faction design)
- "By the time Dark Souls III was released, **A SOCIETY HAD FORMED AROUND THIS PROCESS. THE WAY OF WHITE is
  a religious covenant for all things related to Gwyn, and IT TEACHES US THE NOBILITY OF SACRIFICE.** We
  give ourselves to the Flame as Gwyn did... Those that survive the burning become LORDS OF CINDER, an
  honorary title that bestows royalty upon them, NO MATTER THE PERSON'S ORIGIN. A prince, a priest, a
  Giant, OR A COMMON MAN can all become Lords of Cinder."
- "**SOCIETY TEACHES US THIS IS RIGHT, AND WE WANT TO BELIEVE IT'S RIGHT. WE NATURALLY WANT TO FIGHT THE
  DARKNESS BECAUSE IT'S AN EASY METAPHOR FOR EVIL AND HORROR.**"
- A church whose one doctrine is that you should climb into the furnace, and whose one reward is a title
  (cf. FFT's Faith Q81, Ultima VII's Fellowship Q83, our CHURCH + Network + faction web).
### BOTH ADVISORS ARE LYING, BECAUSE BOTH WANT A KING
- Kingseeker FRAMPT wants you to link the fire. Darkstalker KAATHE wants you to refuse and become Dark Lord.
  Players assume Kaathe is the honest one because he contradicts the establishment.
- The catch, and it's the best-observed thing in this teardown: "**IT'S WORTH NOTING THAT KAATHE IS A FOIL
  TO FRAMPT AND THAT BOTH OF THE PRIMORDIAL SERPENTS ARE KINGSEEKERS — FRAMPT WANTS TO FIND A SUCCESSOR TO
  GWYN TO LINK THE FIRE, WHEREAS KAATHE WANTS TO CROWN A 'DARK LORD' TO USURP GWYN'S THRONE.**"
  - Both are recruiting a monarch. One wants you in the fire, one wants you on a throne. Neither is on
    YOUR side; they're on the side of there BEING a side (cf. Phantom Liberty's no-honest-brokers Q77, our
    faction web; the rebel who is also a kingmaker).
### THE WHOLE PROPHECY IS AN EMPLOYMENT AD
- "The driving force behind the player's actions is **NEVER QUITE CLEAR — first, it's A VAGUE PROPHECY about
  an undead who will collect souls and end the curse.**" You're told you're the Chosen Undead. Then you meet
  Gwynevere, "the Chosen Undead has HIS DESTINY REVEALED: THEY ARE INDEED THE PROPHESIED CHAMPION OF THE
  GODS."
  - And she's a lie — a giant illusion projected by a god's son to keep the recruitment running (cf.
    Dragon's Dogma's headhunted hero Q84, our AMALGAMATION; the prophecy written by the beneficiary).
### THE ENDING IS TWO BUTTONS AND ONE OF THEM IS "LEAVE"
- LINK THE FIRE: "the player's hand is set ablaze first, WITH THE FLAMES ENGULFING THE PLAYER'S BODY... THE
  CURSE OF THE UNDEAD WILL BE BROKEN, **BUT ONE DAY THE FLAMES WILL FADE AGAIN, AND ALL WILL PLAY OUT AS
  BEFORE.**"
- DARK LORD: "**TRIGGERED BY DEFEATING GWYN, LORD OF CINDER, THEN LEAVING THE BOSS ARENA WITHOUT LIGHTING
  THE FINAL BONFIRE.**"
  - The most radical ending in the medium is implemented as: DON'T PRESS THE BUTTON. WALK OUT. The refusal
    has no cutscene trigger, no dialogue tree, no confirmation. You just leave (cf. our Megaton law +
    LIBERATE finale, Dragon's Dogma Q84; refusal as an absence).
### AND THE GAME REFUSES TO TELL YOU WHICH IS RIGHT (the honest core)
- Fan verdicts, from the wiki, in the same thread: "**LINKING THE FIRE IS JUST REPEATING GWYN'S MISTAKE. THE
  FIRST FLAME WASN'T MEANT TO STAY ALIGHT FOREVER, AND SACRIFICING THE MOST POWERFUL BEINGS IN THE WORLD TO
  KEEP IT FUELED FOR A BIT LONGER IS NOT A LONG-TERM SOLUTION.**"
- "**REKINDLING THE FLAME AGAIN AND AGAIN AND AGAIN only for the Undead Curse to prevail once more and more
  Hollows to appear IS JUST PURE INSANITY. AND COVERING THE WHOLE WORLD IN DARKNESS IS NOT ANY BETTER
  EITHER, BUT IT'S THE CORRECT WAY, IN MY OPINION.**"
- And the most human comment on the page: "**DID I DO A GOOD THING BY LIGHTING THE FIRE? FRANKLY I DON'T
  KNOW**" (cf. STALKER's "I'll never know if I was right" Q85, our no-clean-answer).
### THE SEQUEL'S FINAL BOSS IS EVERY PERSON WHO EVER SAID YES
- DS3's last fight is the SOUL OF CINDER: "**THE AMALGAMATION OF THE ESSENCE OF EVERY CHAMPION WHO HAS
  LINKED THE FIRE, from Gwyn and the Chosen Undead to the INNUMERABLE OTHERS that followed.** The Soul of
  Cinder **SWAPS BETWEEN THE WEAPONS OF EVERY POSSIBLE BUILD PLAYERS COULD HAVE CHALLENGED GWYN WITH**...
  Eventually the flaming sword and the familiar attack patterns come out — **AN ECHO OF GWYN FROM DEEP
  WITHIN.**"
- The final boss is a composite person assembled from everyone who volunteered — and it fights using the
  moves of every PLAYER who ever did (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION — literally an amalgamation of the
  self-sacrificed; the composite made of everyone who said yes).
### THE LORDS ALL QUIT (the detail that reframes the trilogy)
- In DS3, the Lords of Cinder are supposed to return to their thrones and burn again. They didn't. "**ALL
  THE LORDS HAVE SHIRKED THEIR DUTY, BUT MAYBE THEY'RE ON TO SOMETHING.**"
- Except one: "the only Lord who sits on his throne is COURLAND, A COMMON MAN who linked the fire in a
  previous cycle when all hope seemed lost; A TRUE HERO. He's here even before you arrive, but there's a
  curious feature about him: **HE'S MISSING HIS LEGS. EITHER HE'S SO DEVOTED TO THE SACRIFICE THAT HE
  CRAWLED HERE… OR HE SIMPLY NEVER LEFT**" (cf. our fold + faction web; the only loyal one is the one who
  can't leave).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- YOU CAN PICK THE ENDING BY ACCIDENT: from the wiki, real players — "I wanted to put out the fire and
  become the dark lord, **BUT I UNINTENTIONALLY LIT THE FIRE AGAIN**"; "**I LITERALLY JUST KILLED GWYN,
  WALKED AROUND FOR 5 SECONDS THEN SUDDENLY I HAVE ACCIDENTALLY PICKED AN ENDING.**" LESSON (sharp, ours):
  a choice with no confirmation is elegant AND it means some players never knowingly made it. Megaton law:
  the player must KNOW they're choosing (Megaton Q, our conscience system).
- THE BACK HALF IS RUSHED AND EVERYONE KNOWS IT: "I guess I see what people mean when they say **THE BACK
  HALF OF THE GAME WAS RUSHED** lol. Still one of the best games I've ever played though." Lost Izalith and
  the Demon Ruins are notoriously unfinished. LESSON: a masterpiece can ship with a visibly incomplete
  third act and still be a masterpiece — but everyone notices forever.
- NEITHER ENDING HAS A CONCLUSION: "**NEITHER OF THE TWO HAVE A REAL CONCLUSION** (compared to ex: Demon's
  Souls), leaving lot of spaces for interpretation of what actually happens after finishing Gwyn." And
  mechanically: "**NO MATTER WHAT ENDING YOU TRIGGER (LEAVING OR LIGHTING THE BONFIRE), YOU'LL START A NEW
  GAME PLUS.**" The game's thesis is "break the cycle," and both buttons restart the game. LESSON (direct
  for our roguelite fold): if your STRUCTURE is a loop, your THEME can't be escape unless the loop
  acknowledges it (Hades Q47 solved this; DS didn't).
- THE LORE IS ARGUED, NOT TOLD: whether Kaathe is honest, whether the Age of Dark is good, whether Gwyn was
  noble — fifteen years of forum war. Even the wiki page is a debate. LESSON (sixth confirmation): ambiguity
  generates community AND means the theme is optional; put SOME of it on the critical path (Vagrant Story
  Q82, Arcanum Q86).
- THE RETCONS UNDERCUT THE FIRST GAME: "it's kinda funny how this game is trying to make you and Artorias
  look like true heroes for fighting Manus and his Abyss, and **IN DS3 IT'S CONFIRMED THAT THE ABYSS EXISTED
  AS EARLY AS DURING THE WAR WITH DRAGONS** and humans were spawned by it." LESSON: sequels that
  re-explain your mystery can retroactively flatten it (our GDD's canon discipline).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the fire that eats people + the myth that recruits them
===============================================================================
Dark Souls is our AMALGAMATION's structure in mythic form: a system that keeps a world running by consuming
people, a religion that exists to supply it, a founding myth authored by its beneficiary, and an ending
where the only way out is to not participate. Its final boss is literally called an amalgamation of everyone
who volunteered.
- W1/W2/W9 (the fire eats people + the plague is the symptom + the final boss is everyone who said yes —
  our AMALGAMATION): the top port, three fused. (a) Our AMALGAMATION is made of the uploaded dead and the
  NETWORK maintains it — bank the FUEL ECONOMY: what does it CONSUME to keep running, and who decides who
  gets burned? Dark Souls' answer is the most powerful person available, every era, forever. Ours could be
  electricity, medicine, or PEOPLE'S RECORDS. (b) And steal the diagnostic inversion: the Undead Curse isn't
  the enemy, it's what a world LOOKS LIKE when it's held past its time — people who can't finish dying,
  decaying into Hollows "AFTER HOPE AND MEMORY HAVE FADED ENTIRELY." That is our premise: the dead who are
  still in the record and can't be done. (c) And DS3's SOUL OF CINDER is our Amalgamation's boss form
  already written: a composite person assembled from every champion who volunteered, fighting with the
  moves of every player who ever did (ties our AMALGAMATION + Network + death-math + recorded-vs-unrecorded
  + [READ] + electricity/medicine currencies + life-support buildings + LIBERATE finale; a top-tier port).
- W3/W4/W6 (the god's sacrifice was FEAR + the religion recruits + the prophecy was written by the
  beneficiary — our CHURCH + Network + [READ]): the antagonist port. Gwyn didn't save the world; he was
  TERRIFIED of what came after him, so he burned himself to delay his own successors, then had his children
  "shepherd the humans" — i.e. CONVINCE US TO DO IT TOO. The Way of White is a church whose one doctrine is
  volunteering for the furnace. And the prophecy that drives the whole game is a vague employment ad, backed
  by a goddess who's an illusion. For us: our founding myth (who saved Vegas, who built the Amalgamation,
  what the Network is FOR) should be authored by the party that benefits, and the faction that preaches
  sacrifice should be the recruiter. That's our [READ]'s whole job — the record versus what happened (ties
  our CHURCH + NETWORK + AMALGAMATION + faction web + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + standing, Ultima
  VII's Fellowship Q83, FFT Q81, Dragon's Dogma Q84).
- W5 (both advisors are kingseekers — our faction web): STEAL it. Frampt says burn; Kaathe says refuse and
  rule. Players trust Kaathe because he's anti-establishment. But "BOTH OF THE PRIMORDIAL SERPENTS ARE
  KINGSEEKERS" — one wants you in the fire, one wants you on a throne, and neither is on your side. In a
  13-faction game where the dynasty is constantly being courted, that's the shape: the rebel recruiting you
  against the system is ALSO recruiting you (ties our faction web + Network + standing + Megaton law,
  Phantom Liberty Q77, FFXII Q80).
- W8 (the refusal is walking out of the room — our LIBERATE finale): bank it WITH its flaw. The most radical
  choice in the medium is implemented as "don't press the button, leave." No cutscene, no dialogue tree.
  That's beautiful and it belongs in our finale vocabulary — a Bohemia ending you reach by NOT doing the
  thing everyone has spent 100 years telling you to do. BUT see the flaw below: players triggered it BY
  ACCIDENT (ties our LIBERATE/Respect/Become + Megaton law + fold, Dragon's Dogma's Godsbane Q84, Ranni Q71).
- W7 (the opening tells you everything except the WHY — our [READ] + ART PHILOSOPHY): bank the technique.
  DS's cutscene "SPELLS IT ALL OUT" — the ages, the lords, the war, the fading. The ONLY thing withheld is
  WHY it's ending and WHAT TO DO. Everyone remembers it as cryptic; it isn't. It's generous with FACT and
  silent on MEANING. That's exactly the register our [READ] should run in: hand the player the record
  freely, withhold the interpretation (ties our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + ART PHILOSOPHY + quest

- W10 (the only Lord still at his post has no legs): bank the image. Every other Lord of Cinder was supposed
  to return and burn again, and they ALL walked away — "ALL THE LORDS HAVE SHIRKED THEIR DUTY, BUT MAYBE
  THEY'RE ON TO SOMETHING." The one still sitting there is a commoner who's MISSING HIS LEGS: "either he's

  man is a saint or just stuck. Our faction web + fold should have one of those (ties our faction web +

- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) A CHOICE WITH NO CONFIRMATION MEANS SOME PLAYERS NEVER KNOWINGLY MADE IT —
