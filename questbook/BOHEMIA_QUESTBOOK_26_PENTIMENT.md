# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 26: THE UNSOLVABLE ACCUSATION (Pentiment)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the deliberately-unsolvable murder, the time-limited investigation,
the accuse-who-you-believe-OR-who-deserves-it fork, the motive-centered design, the live-with-being-
wrong theme, the multi-decade act structure, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's
model for A MYSTERY WITH NO TRUE ANSWER + CHOICE UNDER INCOMPLETE INFORMATION + YOU LIVE WITH THE
CONSEQUENCE OF MAYBE BEING WRONG. Obsidian; won a Peabody Award. Reference only; Paolo does not read it.
No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Pentiment (Obsidian, 2022). You are Andreas Maler, an artist/illuminator in 16th-century Bavaria
(the town of Tassing + Kiersau Abbey), investigating murders across THREE acts spanning ~25 years. A
2D, illuminated-manuscript-styled narrative RPG about history, faith, class, and the stories we choose
to believe.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE UNSOLVABLE MYSTERY (by design): there is NO true culprit. The game NEVER confirms who really did
  it. Evidence against every suspect is CIRCUMSTANTIAL; motives are all plausible; you accuse based on
  who you BELIEVE did it — or who you think DESERVES punishment — and the game never tells you if you
  were right. "Pentiment is less a narrative OF investigation and more a narrative ABOUT investigation."
- IT BREAKS THE GENRE'S CORE PROMISE: detective fiction is built on FAIRNESS — the murder is solvable,
  there's a definitive answer. Pentiment intentionally eschews this: the deeper truth is that you can
  never fully know, and you must ACT anyway, then LIVE with it. The theme is epistemic humility + the
  weight of judgment under uncertainty.
- HISTORY AS THE REAL SUBJECT: the murders are a lens on a real historical world (the Peasants' War, the
  Reformation, class oppression, how official "truth" is written) — the accusations you make ripple into
  a community's fate across decades. The mystery is a vehicle for a meditation on how history + blame get
  authored by the powerful.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how an unsolvable investigation is built)
===============================================================================

### TIME AS THE CONSTRAINT (you can't chase every lead)
- Each investigation runs on a TIME BUDGET (a handful of day-slots, meals, tasks) before the Archdeacon
  (a State inquisitor) arrives to try your falsely-accused friend. You CANNOT pursue every lead — chasing
  one suspect's thread means NOT chasing another's. You choose whom to investigate (cf. Pathologic
  triage Q21, but for KNOWLEDGE, not lives).
- EACH SUSPECT = A QUESTLINE: Prior Ferenc (via "Occult Hands"), Ottilia (via "The Widow's Curse"), Lucky
  (via "A Stonemason's Anger"), Sister Matilda (via "The Baron and the Nun") — each suspect's guilt is
  investigated through a distinct side quest, each with motive + a findable possible murder weapon. You
  build a case on whoever you had TIME to dig into. (So the "culprit" is partly a function of where you
  spent your hours — a chilling design truth.)

### MOTIVE-CENTERED (the pass/fail spine)
- Dev-explained: the investigation centers on MOTIVE (not just means/opportunity). Andreas must gather
  enough evidence to CONVINCE the Archdeacon of a suspect's guilt — a "rudimentary pass/fail": present a
  persuasive enough case and the accusation sticks. Motive is the through-line the player hunts, giving a
  clear goal inside an unsolvable frame.

### THE ACCUSATION FORK (three ways to end an act)
- At time's end, you present evidence and CHOOSE:
  1) Accuse who you BELIEVE is the killer (build the best-evidenced case).
  2) Accuse who you think DESERVES punishment / impart improvised justice (moral judgment over "truth" —
     take out the one who deserves the gallows regardless of the specific crime).
  3) Bungle it / let fate decide (fail to build a case — someone is condemned anyway).
- CONSEQUENCE: whoever you accuse SUFFERS (execution/exile), their family + the village carry it, and it
  RIPPLES for decades — the accused's kin appear in later acts shaped by your Act-1 choice. And your
  friend may still not be saved. Every accusation is a life you spent on incomplete proof.

### THE MULTI-DECADE STRUCTURE (living with it)
- Act 1 (1518), Act 2 (1525, amid the Peasants' Revolt), Act 3 (a ~20-year skip, a NEW protagonist).
  You RETURN to the same village across 25 years and SEE the long consequences of your judgments — the
  orphans, the grudges, the buried guilt. The game makes you live in the aftermath of your uncertainty
  (cf. our GENERATIONAL fold — you inherit the consequences of prior judgments).

### DEFINE-THE-DETECTIVE (build expresses interpretation)
- Through dialogue you DEFINE Andreas' backstory (his travels, languages, education) — and these change
  which clues he can read + how mysteries resolve (a Latin reader sees things others can't). Who Andreas
  IS shapes what he can KNOW (cf. Disco perception-gating Q05, Deus Ex build-routes Q17).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE TIME LIMIT FRUSTRATES SOME: several reviewers cite the "can't investigate everything" clock as the
  biggest NEGATIVE — the anxiety of missed leads is intentional but alienating; you may accuse "based on
  whoever you investigated the most," which can feel arbitrary rather than deductive. LESSON: unsolvable-
  under-time is POWERFUL theme but frustrates players who want to actually solve it — telegraph that
  it's about JUDGMENT, not solution, and consider tuning the pressure (cf. Pathologic sliders Q21).
- NO CONFIRMATION CAN FEEL UNSATISFYING: some players google for "the answer" and are unsettled to learn
  there isn't one. LESSON: the no-answer must be clearly FRAMED as the point (Pentiment mostly does via
  its themes) or it reads as the game withholding rather than saying something (cf. Dream On Q09 —
  withheld closure only works when it's the subject).

## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE UNSOLVABLE MYSTERY: no true culprit, no confirmation — you act on belief under permanent
    uncertainty. Breaks the genre's fairness promise ON PURPOSE to say something about knowledge + blame.
W2. NARRATIVE *ABOUT* INVESTIGATION: the subject isn't "who did it" but "how we decide who did it, and
    what that costs" — the mechanic embodies the theme (epistemic humility, the weight of judgment).
W3. TIME-TRIAGE ON KNOWLEDGE: you can't chase every lead — where you SPEND time determines what you know,
    so the "culprit" is partly a function of your attention. Investigation as resource allocation (cf.
    Pathologic Q21).
W4. EACH SUSPECT IS A QUESTLINE (with motive + weapon): guilt is built by digging a distinct thread per
    suspect — parallel cases you pick between (cf. Beyond the Beef's web Q02, Obra Dinn's mesh Q19).
W5. ACCUSE-WHO-YOU-BELIEVE *OR* WHO-DESERVES-IT: the fork separates TRUTH from JUSTICE — you can pursue
    the likely killer OR impart moral judgment on who deserves the gallows. Two different games of
    conscience (cf. Vault 22's moralize-the-lie Q03, Whispering Hillock's pure-ethics Q04).
W6. YOU LIVE WITH BEING (MAYBE) WRONG: the accused + their kin carry your choice for DECADES across acts —
    consequence you inhabit, not just trigger (cf. our fold; Whispering Hillock delayed payoff Q04).
W7. MOTIVE AS THE HUNT (a goal inside chaos): centering MOTIVE gives a clear objective + a pass/fail
    inside an unsolvable frame — structure that keeps an open mystery playable (not just vibes).
W8. BUILD DEFINES WHAT YOU CAN KNOW: Andreas' self-defined background gates which clues he reads — who you
    ARE shapes what you can PERCEIVE (cf. Disco Q05, Deus Ex Q17, Forgotten City backgrounds Q20).
W9. HISTORY AS THE REAL SUBJECT: the mystery is a lens on class/faith/power + how official "truth" gets
    authored by the strong — the quest carries a real-world meditation (cf. our life-lesson-under-every-
    decision rule; the recorded-vs-unrecorded theme).
W10. THE DECADES-LONG AFTERMATH: returning across 25 years to see judgments ripen — the game makes you
    INHABIT the long tail of your uncertainty (near-identical to our generational structure).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
Pentiment is the master text on JUDGMENT UNDER UNCERTAINTY + LIVING WITH IT ACROSS TIME — uncannily
aligned with our unrecorded ledger, our verdict quests (Q049), and our generational fold.
- W1/W2/W6 (unsolvable mystery + about-investigation + live-with-being-wrong): bank a Bohemia
  investigation/verdict quest with NO confirmed answer — the dynasty accuses/judges on incomplete
  evidence and NEVER learns if they were right; the accused + kin carry it across GENERATIONS (our fold
  is built for this). Frame it clearly as being ABOUT judgment, not solution (ties Q049 the jailer,
  Q037 whose-truth; the unrecorded ledger records what you DECIDED, maybe wrongly).
- W3 (time-triage on knowledge): a Bohemia investigation on a TIME BUDGET where you can't chase every
  lead — where you spend hours shapes what you know, so your verdict is partly a function of attention
  (ties Pathologic triage Q21; pair with our no-brick-wall + sliders honesty).
- W4 (each suspect a questline w/ motive + means): build parallel suspect-threads, each its own mini-quest
  with motive + findable evidence — the player picks whom to dig into (ties Beyond the Beef Q02, Obra
  Dinn Q19, our [READ]).
- W5 (accuse-who-you-believe OR who-deserves-it): THE sharp port — split TRUTH from JUSTICE in our
  verdict quests: convict the likely-guilty, OR impart moral judgment on who DESERVES it regardless — two
  consciences (ties Q049 verdicts, Q019 tally, Dishonored non-lethal-isn't-clean Q14).
- W7 (motive as the hunt / pass-fail inside chaos): center our investigations on MOTIVE to give a clear
  objective inside an open frame — a persuade-the-authority pass/fail (ties Deus Ex social battle Q17,
  the Rule-of-Three validation Q19).
- W8 (build defines what you can know): our dynast's UPBRINGING (Q003) + skills should gate which clues
  they can read — who the heir IS shapes what they can PERCEIVE (validated across Disco/Deus Ex/Forgotten
  City; a strong replay/succession hook).
- W9 (history as the real subject / who authors truth): PERFECT for us — the recorded-vs-unrecorded
  ledger IS "how official truth gets authored by the powerful"; bank quests where the dynasty's
  accusation BECOMES the recorded history (right or wrong), and the unrecorded truth diverges (ties Q037,
  the Amalgamation's authored record).
- W10 (the decades-long aftermath): our GENERATIONAL fold IS this — return across generations to see
  judgments ripen (the accused's orphaned kin, the buried guilt) — validated as a master structure.
- FLAWS (bank): frame the no-answer as the POINT (not withholding); and TUNE the time pressure / give
  legibility so "judgment under uncertainty" doesn't read as "arbitrary" (Pentiment + Pathologic + Dream
  On agree — the unsolvable only works when it's clearly the SUBJECT).
