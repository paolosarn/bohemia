# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 106: THE INFORMATION IS BEHIND A HANGOVER / YOU HAVE TO PREACH TO THE PEOPLE YOU KEPT AWAKE (Mysterious Ways, Kingdom Come: Deliverance)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the murder investigation where the only witness is a priest who cannot
legally talk, and the solution is not a persuasion check, a bribe, or a threat — it is drinking with him all
night, telling him the truth about yourself, and then delivering his sermon for him while hungover, in front
of the villagers you kept awake by ringing the church bell. Complete event flow, the corpse marked "Judas,"
the swordfighting priest, the honesty test, the blackout, the sermon, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports.
STARTLINGLY relevant to our STANDING, our conscience system, our companions, our [READ], + our
despair-with-a-thread-of-hope. Warhorse Studios (director: Daniel Vávra). Reference only; Paolo does not read
it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Mysterious Ways," the **tenth main quest** in Kingdom Come: Deliverance (2018). Not a side quest.
Not optional. The main story stops dead and requires you to get a priest drunk. Bohemia, 1403. Henry is
tracking the bandits who massacred Neuhof.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE WITNESS CANNOT LEGALLY TELL YOU AND THE GAME MEANS IT (the premise — STARTLINGLY our [READ] +
  standing): "**HE EVENTUALLY REVEALS THAT HE KNOWS WHAT LUBOSH WAS DOING ON THE NIGHT OF THE RAID, BUT MUCH
  TO HENRY'S FRUSTRATION, HE CANNOT BREAK HIS VOWS TO REVEAL IT.**"
  - Father Godwin has the answer. He says so. He will not say it, because of the **seal of confession** — a
    real institution with real weight in 1403 that has nothing to do with whether he likes you.
  - And per gamepressure: "**HE WON'T TELL YOU ANYTHING BECAUSE OF THE SEAL OF CONFESSION. USING PERSUASION
    WON'T HELP YOU EITHER.**" The stat check does not open this door (cf. our [READ] + standing +
    recorded-vs-unrecorded; the informant bound by something bigger than you).
- THE SOLUTION IS A FRIENDSHIP AND IT COSTS YOU A NIGHT (the design — STARTLINGLY our standing +
  companions): the quest's real objective list, verbatim: "**(OPTIONAL) MEET THE PARISH PRIEST IN THE TAVERN
  IN THE EVENING. SIT DOWN WITH THE PRIEST. EARN THE PRIEST'S TRUST. HELP GODWIN. CLIMB THE BELL TOWER. GIVE
  THE SERMON IN CHURCH INSTEAD OF FATHER GODWIN.**"
  - **"EARN THE PRIEST'S TRUST"** is a quest objective. **"CLIMB THE BELL TOWER"** is a quest objective. A
    murder investigation, in the main story, whose objectives include getting drunk and going to church.
- AND THE TRUST TEST IS JUST TELLING THE TRUTH (the mechanic — direct for our conscience system): "**IN ORDER
  TO GAIN THE PRIEST'S TRUST, YOU'LL HAVE TO BE HONEST. CHOOSE THE FIRST ANSWER TO EVERY QUESTION HE ASKS
  YOU ABOUT YOURSELF.**"
  - No Speech skill. No Charisma potion. He asks about your life and you either answer honestly or you
    don't, and the entire main quest hinges on it. He's a priest. He can tell (STARTLINGLY our conscience
    system + standing; the check that reads your character, not your build).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — YOUR LEAD IS ALREADY DEAD AND POSED
- You arrive at Uzhitz to find the limping bandit. "**A LARGE CROWD, INCLUDING THE BAILIFF, HAS GATHERED
  OUTSIDE BY THE TIME HE ARRIVES.**"
- "**SOMEONE GOT TO LIMPY LUBOSH BEFORE YOU DID AND KILLED HIM. HIS BODY WAS PLACED IN SUCH A WAY AS TO MAKE
  A STATEMENT, LEAVING THE WORD 'JUDAS' AS A CLEAR WARNING.**"
- Your witness is a corpse arranged into a message by his own gang. **He talked, so they staged him as a
  lesson.** That's the crime scene: not a body, a bulletin (cf. our [READ] + district + entities +
  faction web).
- "**YOU CAN ALSO ENTER THE HOUSE AND EXAMINE THE CORPSE (EXAMINE VARIOUS BODY PARTS TO HEAR DIFFERENT
  COMMENTS).**" And a detail the guides insist on: "**BE SURE TO ALSO TAKE LIMPY'S ARMOR FROM THE PILE OF HAY
  NEXT TO HIS BODY AND HIS SWORD FROM BEHIND THE WOODPILE ALONG THE WALL BETWEEN THE TWO DOORS, WHICH WILL
  GIVE YOU EXTRA DIALOGUE OPTIONS WITH THE BAILIFF.**"
- **Physical evidence unlocks dialogue.** You find the hidden sword; now you can say a thing you couldn't say
  before (STARTLINGLY our [READ] + inventory; recovered objects as conversation keys).
### STAGE 1 — THE CANVASS (the town remembers something odd)
- "**THROUGH QUESTIONING THE LOCALS, HENRY LEARNS THAT LUBOSH WAS A SMALL-TIME CROOK AND DRUNK WHO MAINLY
  KEPT TO HIMSELF. HOWEVER, SOME REPORT THAT HE HAD RECENTLY BEEN SEEN IN THE COMPANY OF THE LOCAL PRIEST,
  FATHER GODWIN, AND NOBODY REMEMBERS SEEING HIM IN UZHITZ ON THE DAY OF THE RAID.**"
- The clue is a behavior change: "**LUBOSZ RECENTLY STARTED GOING TO CHURCH... THIS WOULD NOT HAVE BEEN
  SURPRISING IN MEDIEVAL BOHEMIA, BUT LUBOSZ WAS NOT A RELIGIOUS MAN.**"
- A career criminal suddenly started attending church. That's the entire lead, and it's inference, not a
  marker: **a man who did something unforgivable went looking for a priest** (cf. our [READ] + district +
  entities; the clue that's a change in habit).
### STAGE 2 — THE PRIEST IS OUTSIDE PRACTICING SWORDPLAY
- "**THE LOCAL PRIEST, FATHER GODWIN, LIVES NEAR THE CHURCH AND IS MOST OFTEN EITHER PRACTICING SWORDPLAY
  OUTSIDE HIS HOUSE OR PRAYING INSIDE THE CHURCH.**"
- Two states: praying, or drilling with a sword in the yard. Character established before he speaks (cf. our
  entities + scheduler + companions; the NPC whose schedule IS the characterization).
- And on the victim: "**GODWIN WILL REMARK THAT HE WAS A LOST SOUL — SIMPLE AND ROUGH, BUT NOT BAD AT
  HEART.**" The priest defends the murdered bandit. He's the only one in the village who does.
- The failure route is a real option: you can "**FIGHT HIM IN AN ATTEMPT TO FORCE HIM TO TELL YOU.**" And the
  wiki's note is the joke of the century: "**IF THE PLAYER WISHES TO DRINK WITH GODWIN REGARDLESS OF STATS,
  THE EASIEST METHOD IS TO PICK THE INTIMIDATION OPTION. THIS WILL ALWAYS FAIL; WHETHER HENRY CONTINUES TO
  FIGHT OR BACK DOWN, GODWIN WILL INVITE HIM TO THE TAVERN.**"
- **You cannot intimidate him. You can only amuse him.** Threatening a swordfighting priest gets you invited
  for a drink (cf. our standing + conscience system + PACIFIST LAW).
### STAGE 3 — THE APPOINTMENT (and you can miss it)
- "**THE MEETING NEEDS TO TAKE PLACE IN THE EVENING SO WAIT UNTIL ABOUT 7:00 PM AND THEN FIND GODWIN IN THE
  TAVERN.**"
- "**FATHER GODWIN CAN BECOME ANGRY AT THE HERO IF YOU MISS THE MEETING. AFTER THAT HE WON'T TELL YOU
  ANYTHING ABOUT THE BANDITS. YOU CAN MEET WITH HIM ON THE NEXT DAY AND APOLOGIZE. NOW, THE ONLY WAY TO
  COMPLETE THIS QUEST IS TO USE THE ALTERNATE SOLUTION.**"
- Stand him up and the friendship is gone. Permanently. The main quest reroutes (cf. our scheduler +
  standing + persistent consequence; the appointment that's real).
### STAGE 4 — THE HONESTY TEST (the best persuasion design in the medium)
- "**IN ORDER TO GAIN THE PRIEST'S TRUST, YOU'LL HAVE TO BE HONEST. CHOOSE THE FIRST ANSWER TO EVERY QUESTION
  HE ASKS YOU ABOUT YOURSELF. HE WILL REACT POSITIVELY TO THIS, AND WHEN YOU TELL HIM IT'S GETTING LATE,
  HE'LL ASK YOU TO STAY AND DRINK WITH HIM.**"
- And what he gives you back isn't information — it's a lecture: "**GODWIN WILL STILL REFUSE TO BREAK THE
  CONFESSIONAL VOW, BUT WILL TELL HENRY ABOUT THE IMPORTANCE OF A GOOD AND ENTERTAINING SERMON, AND BEGINS
  BERATING THE CATHOLIC CHURCH FOR THEIR GREED, HYPOCRISY AND CORRUPTION. HE WILL ALSO ADVISE HENRY TO READ
  THE SERMONS OF JOHN HUS.**"
- Punk: **the drunk priest's rant about how to give a good sermon IS the answer key to a puzzle two hours
  later, and the game never says so.** You either listened to a man talk about his job while drinking, or you
  didn't (STARTLINGLY our [READ] + companions + conscience system; the tutorial disguised as a
  conversation).
- The historical spine: **JAN HUS**, the real Bohemian reformer who was burned at the stake in 1415 — twelve
  years after this game — for exactly the things Godwin is drunkenly saying (cf. our [READ] +
  post-collapse premise + ART PHILOSOPHY).
### STAGE 5 — THE NIGHT (the game just lets go of you)
- "**YOU AND GODWIN CONTINUE DRINKING AND CAVORTING, GETTING STEADILY MORE DRUNK, UNTIL THE BAILIFF
  INTERRUPTS AND HAS YOU THROWN OUT OF THE TAVERN, STARTING A BRAWL. YOU THEN FADE IN AND OUT OF
  CONSCIOUSNESS, REMEMBERING ONLY SNIPPETS OF THE EVENING: CLIMBING THE BELL TOWER, HAVING SEX WITH AN
  ALEHOUSE MAID, PRETENDING TO BE... SHEEP?, AND GREETING THE BEAUTIFUL DAWN BEFORE GODWIN THROWS UP AND YOU
  PASS OUT AGAIN.**"
- The blackout is playable in fragments. You get **snippets**. And one of them is **pretending to be sheep**,
  which the wiki itself punctuates with a question mark.
- And there's a rule inside the chaos: "**WHEN HELPING GODWIN IN THE TAVERN BRAWL, DRAWING HENRY'S WEAPONS
  WILL IMMEDIATELY FAIL THE OBJECTIVE.**" It's a bar fight. Pull a sword and you've lost (cf. our PACIFIST
  LAW + combat dial; the fight where escalation is the failure).
- The achievements are the punchline: "**IF YOU AGREE TO DRINK WITH GODWIN, YOU LOSE THE CHANCE TO EARN THE
  ACHIEVEMENT VIRGIN; HOWEVER, YOU EARN THE ACHIEVEMENT SINNER.**" And: "**BECOMING ADDICTED TO ALCOHOL WILL
  UNLOCK THE ALCOHOLIC ACHIEVEMENT.**" The game tracks your alcoholism (cf. our survival accounting +
  conscience system).
### STAGE 6 — THE MORNING (his concubine wakes you both up)
- "**FINALLY, THE TWO ARE AWAKENED AT GODWIN'S HOUSE BY HIS CONCUBINE, WHO REMINDS THEM THAT A HUNGOVER
  GODWIN HAS TO LEAD MASS - SOMETHING HE IS IN NO FIT STATE TO DO.**"
- The priest has a live-in partner. Nobody comments. It's 1403 and that's just true (cf. our entities +
  district; the historical detail delivered without a speech).
- And the greatest walkthrough line ever written: "**AFTER WAKING UP WITH GODWIN, HENRY WILL HAVE HIS CLOTHING
  UNEQUIPPED. BE SURE TO PUT IT BACK ON UNLESS YOU WANT TO PREACH IN UNDERWEAR.**"
- The timer: "**DON'T GET DISTRACTED HERE, BECAUSE IF GODWIN GETS TO THE CHURCH WITHOUT YOU, HE'LL GO IN
  ALONE AND THE MISSION WILL BE FAILED.**"
### STAGE 7 — THE SERMON (you preach to people you kept awake)
- "**NEXT UP... YOU'LL HAVE TO PREACH A SERMON IN UZHITZ TO PEOPLE WHO HATE YOU FOR KEEPING THEM AWAKE ALL
  NIGHT.**"
- You rang the church bell at 3am while pretending to be livestock. Now those people are in the pews and you
  are in the pulpit, hungover, wearing a priest's authority, about to tell them how to live.
- The correct sermon, in order: "**BEGIN THE SERMON CONFIDENTLY. CHOOSE 'GOD STANDS AT THE HEAD OF THE
  CHURCH'. CONTINUE HARSHLY. SAY 'TO SIN IS HUMAN'. CONTINUE HARSHLY. CRITICIZE THE PRELATES BUT STAND UP
  FOR GODWIN.**"
- Read that sequence. **You confess your own night from the pulpit** — to sin is human — and then you attack
  the church hierarchy while defending your local drunk priest. It's a Hussite sermon, delivered by a
  blacksmith's son, hungover, and it WORKS because it's true (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + standing +
  ART PHILOSOPHY; the performance that only works if you're honest about the disgrace).
- "**THE VILLAGERS LEAVE SATISFIED, AND FATHER GODWIN FINALLY TELLS YOU WHAT LIMPY LUBOSH HAS CONFESSED TO
  HIM.**"
- And Godwin's actual justification, which is the quest's moral spine: "**IF HENRY IS SUCCESSFUL, GODWIN WILL
  ACKNOWLEDGE THAT SAVING INNOCENT LIVES MAY BE WORTH BREAKING THE CONFESSIONAL SEAL.**"
- He doesn't break his vow because you're his drinking buddy. He breaks it because **you demonstrated, in
  public, that you understood what his job was for** (cf. our standing + conscience system + [READ]).
### STAGE 8 — THE FAILURE ROUTE (the bureaucratic one, and it's grim)
- "**IF YOU DON'T SAY THE SERMON, IF YOU MAKE A MESS OF IT, OR IF YOU DIDN'T EARN GODWIN'S TRUST THE PRIOR
  EVENING, THE PRIEST WILL REFUSE TO HELP YOU, AND YOU'LL NEED TO GET THE INFORMATION ANOTHER WAY. IN THAT
  CASE, TALK TO THE BAILIFF (WHO IS VERY UPSET AND THREATENS TO PUT YOU IN THE STOCKS FOR BRAWLING), WHO WILL
  SUGGEST GOING TO SEE THE SCRIBE OF RATTAY AND CHECKING HIS RECORDS.**"
- The alternate path: ride to another town, find a **SCRIBE**, and he won't help because his joints hurt.
  "**IF YOU WANT THE SCRIBE'S HELP READING THE BLACK CHRONICLE, YOU'LL NEED TO GET HIM SOME EMBROCATION FOR
  HIS JOINT PAIN... WHICH YOU CAN PURCHASE FROM THE APOTHECARY DOWNSTAIRS.**"
- **You buy an old man liniment and he reads a book to you**, because Henry is illiterate. Or: "**YOU CAN
  PICK THE VERY HARD LOCK ON THE DOOR OF THE ROOM CONTAINING THE BOOK, BUT YOU'LL NEED TO HAVE LEARNED TO
  READ IN THIS CASE, SINCE THE SCRIBE WON'T BE THERE TO HELP YOU.**"
- Three routes to one fact: **be a friend, buy medicine, or learn to read.** All three are real (STARTLINGLY
  our [READ] + MEDICINE currency + standing + recorded-vs-unrecorded; literacy as a game mechanic).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE SERMON IS A MEMORY QUIZ WITH ONE ANSWER: every guide lists the same six lines in the same order.
  "**BEGIN CONFIDENTLY / GOD STANDS AT THE HEAD OF THE CHURCH / CONTINUE HARSHLY / TO SIN IS HUMAN /
  CONTINUE HARSHLY / CRITICIZE THE PRELATES BUT STAND UP FOR GODWIN.**" A magnificent scene with a single
  correct path. LESSON (ours): if the scene's meaning is "be honest," more than one honest answer should
  work — a one-path dialogue is a lock, not a performance.
- IT AUTOSAVES RIGHT BEFORE THE HARD PART, WHICH DELETES THE STAKES: "**YOU CAN ALWAYS RELOAD YOUR GAME
  AFTER FAILING THE SERMON. THE GAME CREATES A SAVE SHORTLY BEFORE YOU REACH THE CHURCH.**" In a game famous
  for punishing saves, the one scene where failure is interesting is fully undoable. LESSON: a checkpoint in
  front of your best consequence is the consequence's off switch.
- POTIONS BEAT CHARACTER: "**YOU MAY NEED A BARD POTION AND/OR AN AMOR POTION... TO INCREASE YOUR SPEECH OR
  CHARISMA TO CONVINCE FATHER GODWIN**"; and "**A HAIR O' THE DOG POTION MAY BE USED TO NEGATE HENRY'S
  DRUNKENNESS AND/OR HANGOVER BEFORE GIVING HIS SERMON.**" You can chemically erase the hangover that IS the
  scene. LESSON (sharp): if a consumable removes your set piece's central condition, you've made the theme
  optional.
- THE FAILURE PATH IS FLATLY WORSE AND EVERYONE KNOWS IT: "**IF YOU DO GIVE THE SERMON (A MORE INTERESTING
  AND AMUSING CUTSCENE)...**" — the wiki itself says the other path is duller. LESSON: if one branch is
  admitted to be the good one, it isn't a branch, it's a punishment for the players who chose wrong.
- MISSING A 7PM APPOINTMENT PERMANENTLY KILLS THE BEST CONTENT IN THE ACT: no warning, no marker urgency.
  LESSON (double-edged): a real appointment is great world-modeling and a silent content-deleter.
- THE QUEST PUNISHES ABSTINENCE: refuse to drink and you lose the priest, the sermon, the information, and
  the best scene in Act 1. Roleplaying a sober, pious Henry costs you the story. LESSON (ours, careful): our
  conscience system must not make one lifestyle the only door to content.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**GODWIN** — priest, swordsman, drinker, ex-bandit-adjacent; the only witness, sealed by the confessional. Wants: to be spoken to like a man, not a uniform; wants his own doubt kept company. Will trade: the secret — but ONLY inside a friendship that was not performed. Will never say: anything to a flatterer; the flattery routes all fail. FUNCTION: the lock whose key is sincerity, calibrated by a game that tracked what you actually did.

**HENRY** — wants the lead. FUNCTION: the investigator who discovers the only working tool left in the box is the truth about himself.

**THE VILLAGERS OF UZHITZ** — want to sleep; are kept awake by the bell. FUNCTION: tomorrow's congregation, tonight's collateral — the sermon's audience is pre-annoyed by your own night.
