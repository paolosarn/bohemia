# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 107: THEY WANT TO WALK HOME AND DIE / THE MURDER YOU CAUSED THREE DECISIONS AGO (The Londoners Arc, Frostpunk)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest chain inside a city-builder where a faction of your own
citizens decides your city is doomed and starts organizing to march 1,900 miles back to a frozen London —
and every tool you have for stopping them makes you worse. Complete event flow, Winterhome, the thefts, the
rallies, the murder that only exists if you escalate, the banishments, the exile that ends YOUR run, the
honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our CITY-BUILDER, our faction web, our standing,
our conscience system, our survival accounting, + our despair-with-a-thread-of-hope. 11 bit studios (also
This War of Mine). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: the Londoners arc, the second act of "A New Home," Frostpunk (2018). You are the Captain of the last
city on Earth. Temperature starts at **-20C** and ends at **-150C**. A scenario "**lasts around 48 days.**"
And somewhere in the middle, your own people organize against you.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE ANTAGONIST IS A GROUP OF YOUR OWN CITIZENS WITH A POINT (the premise — STARTLINGLY our faction web +
  city-builder): "**THE LONDONERS,' A GROUP OF PEOPLE WHO WANT TO ABANDON YOUR 'DOOMED' COLONY AND RETURN TO
  LONDON, SURGE INTO ACTION. THEY ENGAGE IN PROTESTING, VANDALISM, AND RIOTING, STEAL PRECIOUS FOOD AND
  SUPPLIES, AND HOLD PUBLIC RALLIES WITH THE INTENTION OF ROUSING DISSIDENCE AND RECRUITING MORE OF YOUR
  COLONISTS TO THEIR CAUSE.**"
  - They are not raiders. They are not a rival city. **They are the people you saved**, who have looked at
    the numbers and concluded you're going to kill them all (cf. our faction web + city-builder + standing).
- THE TRIGGER IS FINDING OUT ANOTHER CITY LIKE YOURS ALREADY DIED (the writing — STARTLINGLY our
  AMALGAMATION + fold): "**ONE OF THE PLACES YOU'LL DISCOVER IS WINTERHOME, ANOTHER COLONY VERY SIMILAR TO
  NEW LONDON. THE CITY HAS ALREADY FALLEN, AND THERE ARE FEW SURVIVORS. HOPE WILL IMMEDIATELY TAKE A HUGE
  HIT, WHICH IS A BIG ENOUGH CHALLENGE IN ITS OWN RIGHT. HOWEVER, THE INCIDENT ALSO SPARKS AN UPRISING.**"
  - **The uprising is a data point.** Your people didn't turn on you because they're irrational — they turned
    on you because they saw a city exactly like theirs, run by a captain exactly like you, frozen solid with
    a handful of survivors. The evidence for their position is a ruin (STARTLINGLY our [READ] +
    recorded-vs-unrecorded + AMALGAMATION; the corpse of the other attempt).
- AND THEY ARE COMPLETELY, MURDEROUSLY WRONG (the tragedy — direct for our conscience system): a player,
  witheringly accurate: "**THE ADDED IRONY WITH THE LONDONERS IS THAT IF THEY ACTUALLY TRY THEIR INSANE PLAN,
  THEY'RE GOING TO RUN RIGHT INTO THE STORM, TOO.**"
  - Their plan is "**AN INSANE PLAN TO TRY AND MARCH BACK 1900+ MILES TO LONDON WITHOUT ALL THE EQUIPMENT AND
    SUPPLIES THAT MADE IT POSSIBLE FOR SOMEONE TO SURVIVE THE JOURNEY FROM LONDON IN THE FIRST PLACE.**"
  - They have a real grievance, a rational basis, and a plan that is **mass suicide.** You are right and they
    will not be convinced, and every tool you have for convincing them is a tool that makes you a tyrant (cf.
    our conscience system + Megaton law + no-clean-answer).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE THEFTS (the crisis starts as inventory shrinkage)
- A real player's account, and it's the perfect on-ramp: "**THEN THIEVES BROKE INTO THE FOOD STORES, AND
  BEFORE I MANAGED TO GET MY FIRST GUARDHOUSE SET UP, THERE WAS A SECOND BURGLARY! I HAD GUARDS READY BY THE
  TIME A THIRD THEFT HAPPENED.**"
- The rebellion's first appearance is **a number going down in your food stockpile.** Not a speech. A
  logistics anomaly (STARTLINGLY our survival accounting + logistics + city-builder; the uprising that
  arrives as a supply discrepancy).
- And the reason they steal is beautiful design: "**THEY NEED A LOT OF RESOURCES TO SUCCEED AT THAT.**" They
  aren't stealing to hurt you. **They're provisioning an expedition.** They're doing exactly what you did.
### STAGE 1 — THE FIRST DECISION IS ALREADY A TRAP
- You catch a thief. You have no prison. The player: "**I DIDN'T HAVE A PRISON, AND I WASN'T SURE I EVEN
  WANTED ONE SINCE IF I PUT SOMEONE IN IT I'D HAVE A NON-WORKING MEMBER OF SOCIETY WHO WOULD BE EATING FOOD
  THAT COULD GO TO SOMEONE WHO WASN'T BEING A SELFISH ASS. **SO I EXILED THE THIEF (WHICH WAS PRETTY MUCH A
  DEATH SENTENCE SINCE WHO'S GOING TO SURVIVE OUT THERE WITH NO FOOD OR SUPPLIES).**"
- Read the logic. **A prison is a person who eats and doesn't work.** In a survival economy, mercy has a
  caloric cost, so he chose the option that was cheaper and killed a man. He wasn't cruel. **He was doing
  math** (STARTLINGLY our survival accounting + conscience system + city-builder; the humane option priced
  out of existence).
### STAGE 2 — THE POLICE OPTION (and why it's the wrong one)
- The community's sharpest read: "**OF COURSE STEALING IS BAD AND YOU CAN STOP THEM BY SENDING YOUR
  POLICE-FORCE AFTER THEM. BUT DO YOU REALLY NEED TO DO THAT? IF YOU WERE GOING TO CONVINCE THEM TO STAY
  ANYWAY AND IF YOU ALREADY HAVE ENOUGH RESOURCES (BECAUSE YOU ARE THAT GOOD OF A LEADER) THEN SENDING YOUR
  POLICE WOULD ONLY LEAD TO MORE VIOLENCE (WHICH IT ACTUALLY DOES). THEREFORE NOT ESCALATING THE ENTIRE
  SITUATION AND NOT SENDING YOUR GUARDS/FAITH KEEPERS TO STOP THE THIEFS IS THE BETTER OPTION IN THE LONG
  RUN.**"
- **The correct answer to the theft is to let them steal.** If your city is well-run, you can afford it, and
  enforcement is what turns a shrinkage problem into a body (cf. our PACIFIST LAW + conscience system +
  standing).
### STAGE 3 — THE MURDER THAT ONLY EXISTS BECAUSE YOU MADE IT (the best design in the arc)
- The chain, laid out by a player defending the game: "**IF YOU PICK THE OPTION 'LET HIM GO, HE ISN'T THE
  MURDERER' THEN YOU CAN END THE EVENT-LINE WITHOUT THE MURDERER EVER GETTING BEAT UP... ALSO IF YOU PICK
  THE 'LET HIM GO, HE ISN'T THE MURDERER' DECISION, THEN THE EVENT WHERE YOU DECIDE THE FATE OF THE REAL
  MURDERER ALSO NEVER HAPPENS. MY POINT IS, THERE ARE SO MANY WAYS TO PREVENT THIS FROM HAPPENING WITHOUT
  ANY MORAL PROBLEMS.**"
- "**THE WHOLE EVENT-LINE OF SOMEONE GETTING MURDERED CAN BE ALREADY PREVENTED BY CHOOSING A DIFFERENT
  OPTION EARLIER.**"
- **The murder is downstream of a decision you made two events ago.** The guard who dies, the mob, the
  choice of what to do with the killer — none of it is scripted. It's the accumulated result of you
  escalating. The game never tells you that (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + persistent consequence +
  Megaton law; the tragedy that is your own audit trail).
- And the menu when you get there, verbatim: "**BANISH HIM: THIS PERSON WILL LEAVE THE CITY FOREVER. GIVE HIM
  A CHANCE TO REPENT: THE NUMBER OF LONDONERS WILL FALL. REQUIRES THE PUBLIC PENANCE LAW. LET HIM GO, HE
  ISN'T THE MURDERER: DISCONTENT WILL RISE. OUR PEOPLE WILL DECIDE HIS FATE: DISCONTENT WILL RISE. LET HIM
  GO, BUT NEXT TIME HE'LL HAVE TO REPENT: YOU WILL HAVE 3 DAYS TO SIGN THE PUBLIC PENANCE LAW.**"
- Look at "**OUR PEOPLE WILL DECIDE HIS FATE.**" A player: "**YEAH BUT LETTING HIM DIE IN THE HANDS OF YOUR
  PEOPLE ISN'T THAT GREAT OF AN OPTION TOO.**" You can hand a man to a mob and the UI labels it as a
  discontent modifier.
### STAGE 4 — THE RALLY (three options, all violence)
- "**IS LETTING THE LONDONER SPOUT HIS MOUTH OFF THE WISEST THING TO DO WHEN HE'LL CONVERT MORE PEOPLE TO HIS
  CAUSE, OR WOULD IT BE BETTER TO BREAK UP THE CROWD AND DENY HIS CHANCE TO SPEAK?**"
- The actual menu, from a frustrated player: "**LIKE WHEN YOU CAN CHOOSE TO LET THE LONDONER TALK AND LOSE 15
  PEOPLE, CALL HIM A LIAR AND LOSE 7 PEOPLE, OR 'DISPERSE THE CROWD' AND SOMEHOW BEAT EVERYONE UP? NOT SURE
  WHY TELLING PEOPLE TO GO HOME HAS TO RESULT IN PHYSICAL VIOLENCE.**"
- **Let him speak: lose 15 citizens. Call him a liar: lose 7. Disperse the crowd: a beating.** Free speech
  is priced in population. There is no option that costs nothing (cf. our faction web + standing +
  conscience system + Megaton law).
### STAGE 5 — THE PROMISE SYSTEM (the mechanic that models leadership)
- "**THE RISE IN DISCONTENT FROM IGNORING THE PEOPLE IS MUCH LESS THAN THE RISE IN DISCONTENT IF YOU FAIL TO
  FOLLOW THROUGH.**"
- **It is mechanically better to ignore your people than to promise and fail.** The game rewards silence over
  broken faith. That's a leadership thesis expressed as a number.
- And the trap: "**'WARM ALL HOUSES,' FOR EXAMPLE: IF YOU DO MANAGE TO WARM ALL HOUSES BUT YOU THEN BUILD A
  NEW HOUSE BECAUSE SOME NEW SURVIVORS ARRIVED, YOU'LL SUDDENLY BE BREACHING AS NOW THE NEW HOUSE ISN'T WARM
  AND BAM! YOU'VE FAILED. **TO AVOID THIS, YOU NEED TO LEAVE THE SURVIVORS OUT IN THE COLD FOR THE NIGHT AND
  WORRY ABOUT THEM AFTER THE PROMISE PERIOD HAS PASSED.**"
- Punk: **the optimal play is to let refugees freeze overnight so your promise doesn't technically break.**
  Rescuing people breaks your word. That is the single most damning emergent mechanic in the genre
  (STARTLINGLY our city-builder + survival accounting + conscience system + standing).
- The advice everyone converges on: "**ONLY PROMISE THINGS YOU ARE GUARANTEED TO BE ABLE TO UPHOLD.**"
### STAGE 6 — THE TWO PATHS (purpose as a management tool)
- "**YOU MUST CHOOSE WHETHER YOU TURN TO RELIGION OR REDUCE LIBERTIES IN ORDER TO PRESERVE THE GREATER
  GOOD.**"
- **ORDER:** guards, patrols, and one hope building — "**THE PROPAGANDA CENTRE: THERE CAN ONLY BE ONE IN THE
  CITY... NOTE THAT PASSING THE PROPAGANDA CENTRE LAW AND OWNING THIS BUILDING WILL TRIGGER THE PROPAGANDA
  CENTRE ARC, WHICH WILL RESULT IN SOME CITIZEN'S DEATH.**" The building that manufactures hope has a body
  count in its own quest chain.
- **FAITH:** "**HOUSE OF PRAYER: THIS BUILDING DOESN'T NEED ANY WORKERS AND HAS AN AoE... THE FIRST HAS A
  2-DAY COOLDOWN AND WILL CAUSE HOPE TO RISE SLIGHTLY; THE SECOND COSTS 20 FOOD RATIONS.**"
- **Hope costs 20 food rations.** It's a building with a cooldown and a resource cost. Meaning is a
  production chain (STARTLINGLY our THREE CURRENCIES + city-builder + survival accounting; morale as an
  industry).
- And a player's honesty about the slide: "**I STOPPED AT HAVING PUBLIC PENANCES, WHICH I FELT KIND OF BAD
  ABOUT, BUT HAD BEEN PRESSURED INTO BY AN EVENT (WHICH WOULD GIVE ME A HOPE BOOST IF I PASSED THE LAW). WHEN
  OTHER PEOPLE CAME FORWARD AND SAID THEY DIDN'T LIKE THE PUBLIC PENANCES, I STOPPED DOING THEM. (SADLY, I
  WILL ALWAYS FEEL LIKE THE FAITH KEEPER PROCESSIONS AROUND THE GENERATOR ARE CULTISH, BUT THEY FUNCTION LIKE
  GUARDS DO FOR ORDER SO YOU NEED THEM TO MANAGE THE LONDONERS.)**"
### STAGE 7 — THE PRISON MATH (the ugliest optimization in the game)
- Community advice, delivered as pure strategy: "**ROUNDING UP PEOPLE IN PRISONS LOWERS THE NUMBER OF
  LONDONERS AND LOWERS DISCONTENT WHILE RAISING HOPE.**"
- "**PASSING THE PATROL LAW CAN SEND YOUR GUARDS OUT AND LOWER DISCONTENT AND RAISE HOPE AT THE COST OF 10
  RATIONS.**"
- **Police patrols raise hope. Imprisoning dissidents raises hope.** And you can dismantle it all when you're
  done: "**ARE THINGS LOOKING HOPELESS AND YOUR WORKERS CLOSE TO RIOTING? BUILD A BUNCH OF CHURCHES OR GUARD
  STATIONS. HAVE THINGS SETTLED DOWN? PULL THE WORKERS BACK OUT AND DISMANTLE THEM.**"
- You can build a church, use it to break a strike, and tear it down (cf. our city-builder + faction web +
  conscience system).
### STAGE 8 — THE OTHER WAY TO WIN (and it's the real one)
- The best advice in the search, and it's not about repression at all: "**FROM THERE, ONLY PROMISE THINGS YOU
  ARE GUARANTEED TO BE ABLE TO UPHOLD, AND TRY PUSHING RESEARCH TO ITS LIMITS... AND YOU'LL HAVE BOTH A
  DOWNWARD TREND IN LONDONERS DUE TO YOUR CITY BEING VERY COMFORTABLE AND WELL-MANAGED.**"
- A player: "**ONLY HAD ONE (1) LONDONER LEAVE BECAUSE I MAXED OUT MY HOPE.**"
- Another, on the Faith run: "**THROUGH RELIGIOUS FAITH I HAD CONVINCED ALL THE LONDONERS INTO REJOINING THE
  FOLD.**"
- **The Londoners are a symptom.** Build a city worth staying in and the faction dissolves. The rebellion is
  a readout on your management (STARTLINGLY our city-builder + faction web + standing + survival accounting;
  the insurgency as a performance metric).
### STAGE 9 — AND IF YOU FAIL, THEY EXILE YOU
- "**IF DISCONTENT IS TO RISE FOR TOO LONG, AND NOTHING IS DONE ABOUT IT, THE LEADER WILL BE EITHER EXILED
  OR, IN EXTREME CASES, EXECUTED.**"
- And the game's cruelest mercy: "**INSTEAD, THEY WILL EXILE YOU FROM THE SETTLEMENT, AS EVEN THOUGH YOU WERE
  INCOMPETENT, THEY STILL FELT YOU DESERVED THAT MUCH FOR THE EFFORT YOU'VE PUT IN. CONSIDERING THE FACT
  THAT IT'S A DEATH WORLD OUTSIDE THE WALLS OF YOUR CITY AND THAT YOU'RE BANISHED WITHOUT FOOD OR COAL,
  THOUGH, BEING BOILED TO DEATH BY HOT STEAM ALMOST SEEMED LIKE THE BETTER ALTERNATIVE.**"
- They spare your life out of respect for your effort, by throwing you into -150C without supplies. **The
  mercy is the execution.** And it's exactly what you did to the thief in Stage 1 (cf. our conscience system
  + fold + death-math).
### STAGE 10 — THE GOLDEN ENDING'S FINE PRINT (the accounting is honest and horrible)
- "**A NEW HOME: SURVIVE THE STORM WITHOUT DESCENDING INTO TOTALITARIANISM... THERE IS EVEN AN ACHIEVEMENT
  FOR OBTAINING THIS (THE SAVIOUR), WHERE YOU HAVE TO BEAT THE SCENARIO WITHOUT CAUSING ANY DEATH VIA HARSH
  LAWS, OVERWORK, OR EXPOSURE, ALL WHILE HAVING SAVED EVERYBODY POSSIBLE.**"
- And the exemptions: "**THERE ARE CERTAIN FACTORS THAT DO NOT COUNT TOWARDS OR AGAINST A NEW HOME GOLDEN
  ENDING, SUCH AS **BANISHING PEOPLE FROM THE CITY** OR **LETTING THE LONDONERS LEAVE**, OR SIGNING SPECIFIC
  CONTROVERSIAL LAWS BUT NOT ACTUALLY ENACTING THEM ENOUGH TIMES TO RESULT IN REPERCUSSIONS.**"
- **You can achieve "The Saviour" while banishing people to freeze to death**, because banishment isn't
  scored. The morality system has a loophole and it's the exact loophole real institutions use: **it's not a
  death if you did it outside the walls** (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + Megaton law + faction web;
  the ledger that doesn't count what happens off-property).
- And the epilogue that lands it: "**EVERY TIME YOU COMPLETE A SCENARIO THE GAME RUNS THROUGH A TIMELAPSE
  SHOWING THE GROWTH OF YOUR CITY AND A NARRATIVE COVERING THE DECISIONS YOU MADE. THOUGH MY PEOPLE HAD THEIR
  REGRETS (TURNING AWAY REFUGEES WHO WE COULD NOT FEED) AND DEALT WITH HARDSHIP (SUBSISTING ON SOUP TO
  STRETCH FOOD SUPPLIES), THEY ULTIMATELY LOOKED FORWARD TO A HOPEFUL FUTURE.**"

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE OPTIONS DON'T MATCH THEIR OUTCOMES: "**NOT SURE WHY TELLING PEOPLE TO GO HOME HAS TO RESULT IN PHYSICAL
  VIOLENCE.**" "Disperse the crowd" becoming a beating is the game putting its thumb on the scale to make
  every road lead to tyranny. LESSON (ours, sharp): if your moral system needs to lie about what an action
  IS to make it costly, the dilemma is fake — our conscience system must let a reasonable action have a
  reasonable outcome.
- IT'S ALL SCRIPTED AND PLAYERS FOUND OUT: "**RANDOM? NAH. EVERYTHING IS HEAVILY SCRIPTED IN THIS GAME. USE
  EMERGENCY SHIFT ON THE TECH HOUSE? HAVE A DEAD GUY ON THE FIRST TRY. BUT THEN NOBODY EVER DIES... I
  CHECKED - LOADED THE MAP A FEW TIMES BEFORE EXPLORING TESLA CITY AND EVERY TIME THEY GOT KILLED.**" LESSON:
  a scripted death disguised as a systemic one is a one-time trick; the second playthrough it's a cutscene.
- THE HARSHNESS IS ARGUABLY UNEARNED: a player who ran the numbers — "**PEOPLE LIVED MOST OF THE TIME IN
  ABUNDANCE AND SAFETY WHILE PERILS WERE EASILY SHRUGGED OFF SO HARSH DECISIONS ARE NOT JUSTIFIED.**" And
  another: "**ORDER BEFORE YOU GO PLEDGE OF LOYALTY OR FORCEFUL PERSUASION IS PRACTICALLY LITTLE MORE THAN
  HAVING A POLICE FORCE AND USING POSITIVE PROPAGANDA, THINGS YOU WILL FIND IN EVEN THE MOST LIBERAL OF
  DEMOCRACIES... HARDLY SIGNS OF TYRANNY. ENDED MY ORDER PLAYTHROUGH WITH ALL OF TEN DEATHS.**" LESSON: if
  the game's famous "did you go too far?" ending fires on a police force, players will argue the game is
  moralizing at them rather than testing them.
- THE PROMISE SYSTEM PUNISHES RESCUE: "**TO AVOID THIS, YOU NEED TO LEAVE THE SURVIVORS OUT IN THE COLD FOR
  THE NIGHT.**" Emergent, unintended, and it teaches the player to let people freeze on a technicality.
  LESSON (ours, hard): audit what your systems reward when they collide. Our survival accounting could
  produce this exact monster.
- THE GOLDEN ENDING IGNORES BANISHMENT: you can be "**THE SAVIOUR**" while exiling people into -150C.
  LESSON: if your morality score has an off-property exemption, you've built the loophole into the theme
  instead of about it.
- THE ARC RESOLVES BY DISSOLVING: max your hope and the Londoners just... stop. There's no scene, no
  reckoning, no conversation with their leader. LESSON: the best-run city gets the least story — reward good
  play with CONTENT, not silence.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE LONDONERS** — your own citizens; want to go home to a London they cannot know is dead. Will trade: labor withheld, tools stolen, faith transferred. Will never say: that the march is a grief ritual — hope aimed backward. FUNCTION: the opposition made entirely of people you saved.

**THE FAITHFUL/ORDER APPARATUS (your prior laws)** — want to be used; every escalation tool you built in earlier crises sits in the drawer, humming. FUNCTION: the temptation with your own signature already on it.

**THE CAPTAIN (the player)** — wants the city to survive AND to remain someone the city should survive under. FUNCTION: the governor whose every tool makes them worse (the file's thesis).

**THE PROMISE SYSTEM** — wants to be kept. FUNCTION: leadership modeled as debt — speeches write IOUs the simulation collects.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees; in a city-builder the "nodes" are POLICY EVENTS with option lists — same machine)

NODE: THE_THEFTS — entry: shrinkage detected
  > (post guards)          [gate: none] -> order track opens; tension up
  > (ignore)               [gate: none] SILENCE -> the movement organizes faster in the quiet
  > (address them)         [gate: none] -> the rally comes sooner, on your terms

NODE: THE_MURDER_THAT_ONLY_EXISTS_IF_YOU_ESCALATE — entry: police option taken, pressure applied
  THE ARC'S BEST DESIGN (the file's stage 3): the killing occurs ONLY on the escalation path. On the gentle path there is no body, ever.
  > (investigate it as crime)      [gate: flag:escalated] -> the machinery you built processes the corpse IT produced
  NOVERB: "This wasn't my fault." — no interface surface exists to disown it; the cause chain is three decisions long and the sim remembers every link. THE QUESTBOOK'S CLEANEST CAUSAL-OWNERSHIP MECHANIC: consequence with a paper trail and no narrator to blame.

NODE: THE_RALLY — entry: the movement peaks
  > (disperse by force)    [gate: none] -> violence, obedience, rot
  > (out-argue via promises) [gate: promise-capacity] -> the PROMISE SYSTEM: commitments become sim-tracked debts; breach converts hope to exodus at interest
  > (let it happen)        [gate: none] SILENCE -> the march begins; they die on the ice, offscreen, and the survivors' list arrives like weather

NODE: PURPOSE_AS_MANAGEMENT — the two-path fork (Faith/Order applied to a secession)
  Both paths CAN hold the city; both price it in what the city becomes. The quest's governance thesis: PURPOSE IS A RESOURCE, and manufacturing it has a supply chain (pulpits or patrols).
