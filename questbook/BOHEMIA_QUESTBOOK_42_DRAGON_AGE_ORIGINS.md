# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 42: THE ORIGIN STORY / WHO-YOU-WERE SHAPES EVERYTHING (Dragon Age: Origins)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the six origin stories, how origin reshapes surname/reactions/the
whole opening, the Landsmeet-as-choice-culmination, the Morrigan ritual dilemma, companion approval, the
choices-imported-across-games, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for
CHARACTER-DEFINING BEGINNINGS + a chosen BACKGROUND that ripples through an entire game. Maps DIRECTLY
onto our upbringing system. BioWare. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest here.

Game: Dragon Age: Origins (BioWare, 2009). A dark-fantasy party RPG. You're a Grey Warden fighting a
Darkspawn Blight in Ferelden — but you START as one of SIX different people, each with a unique ~1-hour
origin, before all six converge on the Warden path. A landmark of reactive character-driven RPG design.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- SIX ORIGIN STORIES = SIX DIFFERENT GAMES (for the first hour): before the main plot, you play a bespoke
  opening determined by WHO you chose to be — HUMAN NOBLE, CIRCLE MAGE, CITY ELF, DALISH ELF, DWARF NOBLE,
  or DWARF COMMONER. Each has its own setting, cast, tragedy, and tutorial — a self-contained short story
  that ENDS with your recruitment into the Grey Wardens. Your beginning is authored to your identity.
- ORIGIN RIPPLES THROUGH THE WHOLE GAME (not just the intro): your origin sets your SURNAME, and "affects
  how other characters react to your actions" for the ENTIRE game — nobles defer to a Human Noble, elves
  trust a City Elf, dwarves treat a Dwarf Noble vs Commoner completely differently, mages/templars react
  to a Circle Mage. Characters + quests unique to your origin resurface much later (your origin's villain,
  family, or home reappears). WHO YOU WERE keeps mattering (cf. Bloodlines clan Q39, Fallout build Q38,
  Tyranny Conquest Q25).
- CHOICE AS THE SPINE (rippling forward + across games): Origins is "a franchise about choice" — decisions
  ripple in major/minor ways, tracked and RESPECTED in sequels (imported saves carry ~dozens of flags:
  who ruled, who lived, who you romanced). The world remembers across GAMES, not just acts (cf. ME2
  import Q06, Alpha Protocol reactivity Q24).

===============================================================================
## 1. THE ORIGIN ARCHITECTURE (how six openings converge)
===============================================================================
- THE BESPOKE SHORT STORY: each origin is a complete mini-narrative — e.g. the DWARF COMMONER starts as a
  casteless "brand"-marked underclass criminal in Orzammar (a story about caste + dignity); the CITY ELF
  begins with a wedding shattered by a human noble's assault on the alienage (a story about oppression +
  vengeance); the HUMAN NOBLE opens with your family BETRAYED and slaughtered by Arl Howe (a story about
  loss + a personal nemesis who returns in the main game). Each teaches the world from a DIFFERENT social
  position — you LEARN Thedas through the eyes of your class/race (cf. Forgotten City backgrounds Q20,
  Pentiment define-Andreas Q26).
- THE CONVERGENCE (one path, six colorings): all origins funnel to the Grey Warden Joining, then the same
  main plot — but every step is COLORED by your origin (dialogue options, NPC reactions, unique
  recognitions). Efficient design: author the CORE once, the ORIGIN-SPECIFIC reactions as a reactive
  layer on top (cf. Bloodlines clan-lens Q39).
- THE PERSONAL NEMESIS RETURNS: the Human Noble's betrayer (Arl Howe) is a MAIN antagonist you get
  personal revenge on — your origin's tragedy becomes a main-plot payoff (only for that origin). The
  beginning pays off at the end (cf. our fold/generational seeding).

===============================================================================
## 2. THE CULMINATION QUESTS (where choices cash out)
===============================================================================
- THE LANDSMEET (the choice-culmination set-piece): a political assembly of Ferelden's nobility that is
  "the culmination of your journey, where all your choices thus far show their effects for all to see."
  Accumulated reputation, favors, and alliances (whom you helped, whom you have dirt on) determine who
  backs you, who rules (Anora/Alistair/co-rule/the Warden marries in), and the fate of the traitor
  Loghain (execute, or recruit him to the Wardens — which makes Alistair LEAVE). One set-piece where the
  whole game's reactivity pays out at once (cf. ME2 Suicide Mission Q06, Forgotten City canon-ending Q20,
  our endgame).
- THE MORRIGAN RITUAL (the no-clean-sacrifice dilemma): killing the Archdemon requires a Grey Warden to
  DIE. At the last moment, Morrigan offers a ritual — conceive a child that absorbs the Archdemon's soul,
  so no Warden dies — but with dark, unknown consequences (a tainted god-soul child loose in the world).
  Accept a morally murky escape from sacrifice, or refuse and let someone die a hero. No clean option at
  the climax (cf. Whispering Hillock Q04, SOMA sacrifice-triage Q34, ME2 who-dies Q06).
- THE ARCHITECT (spare-the-enemy, delayed consequence): a sentient Darkspawn (the Architect) offers a
  path that could END the Blights' cause — but freeing him is a gamble whose consequences aren't seen for
  GAMES (later characters warn "we haven't seen the impact of letting the Architect live yet"). Sparing an
  enemy plants a seed that ripens across sequels (cf. RDR recurrence Q10, Alpha Protocol delayed seeds Q24).

===============================================================================
## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- ORIGIN CONTENT IS FRONT-LOADED + UNEVEN: the origins are richest at the START; some get far more
  mid/late callbacks than others (Human Noble's nemesis returns; others' origins fade). LESSON: if you
  promise identity-defining beginnings, SUSTAIN the callbacks across the whole game for EVERY origin, or
  the promise feels hollow past hour one (surface the reactivity — cf. Alpha Protocol Q24, Bloodlines Q39).
- HIGH AUTHORING COST: six bespoke openings + origin-specific reactions throughout is EXPENSIVE — a lot of
  content most players see only once. LESSON: reactivity of this depth is costly; a SOLO dev must pick a
  scope that's sustainable (fewer origins, deeper callbacks > six shallow ones — cf. our FACTORY LAW +
  scope discipline).
- THE RAILS UNDER THE CHOICE: "the overarching narrative follows a specified path; player influence is
  confined to pre-ordained forks" — the freedom is a reactive LAYER over a fixed spine, not true divergence.
  LESSON: be honest about choice-vs-illusion; make the forks that DO exist land hard + be respected (cf.
  Spec Ops/Sinnerman on-rails critiques Q27/Q40).

## 4. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. SIX ORIGIN STORIES (character-defining beginnings): a bespoke ~1hr opening per identity — you LEARN
    the world from your class/race's social position; your beginning is authored to WHO you are (cf.
    Bloodlines clan Q39, Forgotten City backgrounds Q20).
W2. ORIGIN RIPPLES THROUGH THE WHOLE GAME: surname + NPC reactions + unique recognitions persist for the
    ENTIRE game — who you WERE keeps mattering, not just in the intro (cf. Fallout build Q38, Tyranny Q25).
W3. CONVERGE-THEN-COLOR (efficient reactivity): six openings funnel to one core plot, each step COLORED by
    origin — author the core once, layer origin-reactions on top (the scalable pattern; cf. Bloodlines Q39).
W4. THE ORIGIN'S TRAGEDY PAYS OFF LATE: your beginning's nemesis/loss returns as a main-plot payoff — the
    opening seeds the climax (cf. our fold/generational seeding, RDR Q10).
W5. THE CHOICE-CULMINATION SET-PIECE (Landsmeet): one late set-piece where the WHOLE game's accumulated
    reputation/alliances pay out at once — reactivity made visible + decisive (cf. ME2 Q06, Forgotten
    City Q20, our endgame).
W6. THE NO-CLEAN-SACRIFICE CLIMAX (Morrigan ritual): a morally murky escape from a required sacrifice vs
    a clean heroic death — no clean option at the peak (cf. Whispering Hillock Q04, SOMA Q34, ME2 Q06).
W7. SPARE-THE-ENEMY DELAYED CONSEQUENCE (the Architect): sparing a foe plants a seed that ripens across
    SEQUELS — consequence beyond this game (cf. Alpha Protocol Q24, RDR Q10; our fold across generations).
W8. LEARN THE WORLD FROM A SOCIAL POSITION: each origin teaches the setting from a different class/race
    lens (oppression, privilege, caste) — the world gains dimension through WHOSE eyes you see it (cf.
    Disco Q05, Pentiment Q26; our factions/standing).
W9. THE WORLD REMEMBERS ACROSS GAMES: dozens of choice-flags import into sequels — the ultimate "world
    remembers" (cf. ME2 import Q06, our generational fold as the intra-game version).
W10. COMPANION APPROVAL SHAPES OUTCOMES: companions approve/disapprove of choices, gating romance,
     loyalty, who stays/leaves (Alistair leaves if you recruit Loghain) — relationships as consequence
     (cf. ME2 loyalty Q06, BG3 Q16, Alpha Protocol Q24, Tyranny Q25).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**THE WARDEN (the player, six possible people)** — wants, per the plot: the Blight ended. Wants, per the origin: something older — a City Elf wants vengeance for the alienage, a Human Noble wants Howe's head, a Dwarf Commoner wants to stop being a brand-marked nothing. Will trade: the past, which the game refuses to let go of. FUNCTION: the lens with a surname (W1, W2): six openings, one convergence, and the coloring never washes out.

**THE SIX ORIGINS** — want to be more than a tutorial. Each is a complete short story teaching Thedas from a different social position: caste, oppression, privilege, faith. FUNCTION: the character-defining beginning (W1, W8): the world gains dimension through WHOSE eyes it's first seen.

**ARL HOWE (and each origin's nemesis)** — wants the Human Noble's family erased, and returns in the main plot to be answered for. FUNCTION: the origin's tragedy paying off late (W4): the opening seeds the climax — but only for the origin it belongs to (the banked flaw: uneven callbacks).

**THE LANDSMEET** — wants a reckoning: every favor, every dirt-file, every alliance, cashed in one assembly. FUNCTION: the choice-culmination set-piece (W5): the whole game's reactivity made visible and decisive in a single room. Our Q050's blueprint.

**MORRIGAN** — wants a way around the price, and offers one with an unknown cost: a ritual so no Warden dies, at the price of a god-souled child loose in the world. FUNCTION: the no-clean-sacrifice climax (W6): the murky escape versus the clean heroic death.

**THE ARCHITECT** — wants to end the Blights' cause, and asks to be spared to try. FUNCTION: the delayed consequence (W7): sparing him plants a seed that ripens across SEQUELS — the fold's cross-game move, which Bohemia does across generations natively.
