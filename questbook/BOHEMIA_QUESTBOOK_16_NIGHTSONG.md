# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 16: THE NIGHTSONG / SHADOWHEART'S CHOICE (Baldur's Gate 3)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: event, staging, stage-by-stage flow, the companion-tied moral
fork, the playthrough-wide reactivity, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's MODERN model for
REACTIVITY-AS-MEMORY at scale + a COMPANION'S FATE decided by the player + a "relic" that becomes a
PERSON. BG3 is the current benchmark for choices reverberating through a whole game. Reference only;
Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: "Find the Nightsong" -> its Act 2 climax (save/kill the Nightsong) is bound to Shadowheart's
companion arc (Dark Justiciar / Shar vs Selune). Larian, 2023. Ties to the Ketheric Thorm main-boss
thread and the Shadow-Curse.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE PREMISE / STAKES
===============================================================================
- WHO: SHADOWHEART, a secretive cleric of Shar (goddess of loss/darkness) traveling with you; and the
  NIGHTSONG, hyped early as a powerful "relic" to hunt — later revealed to be AYLIN, an immortal
  aasimar daughter of the moon-goddess Selune, held in torturous imprisonment for ~100 years.
- THE HOOK: a bounty/contract to "find the Nightsong relic" appears as early as Act 1 (the goblin camp/
  Emerald Grove) — a treasure-hunt framing. You chase a MacGuffin.
- THE TWIST: the "relic" is a PERSON — a tortured prisoner. And killing her is the final rite for
  Shadowheart to become a Dark Justiciar (fulfilling her god's demand). The treasure hunt becomes a
  moral execution decision bound to a companion's SOUL.
- THE STAKES: (a) Aylin's life; (b) Shadowheart's entire character trajectory + your romance with her;
  (c) the main plot (Aylin is key to lifting the Shadow-Curse and defeating Ketheric Thorm). One choice,
  three levels of consequence — personal, companion, world.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE FULL STAGE-BY-STAGE FLOW
===============================================================================

### STAGE 1 — THE CONTRACT (Act 1: a treasure framing)
- Hear of the Nightsong via a wizard's bounty on a "relic"; get the contract (e.g. from Aradin at the
  Emerald Grove). STAGING: framed as loot to reinforce the later gut-punch (it's a person, not a prize).

### STAGE 2 — THE TRAIL GOES COLD (Act 1: multi-key dungeon)
- The trail leads through the Shattered Sanctum (defeat Priestess Gut, loot a key), a Defiled Temple
  PUZZLE (a Perception check reveals a LEVER that BYPASSES the puzzle entirely — skill shortcut), down
  into the UNDERDARK to an abandoned Selunite outpost — where the trail goes cold. The reveal is
  deliberately DEFERRED to Act 2 (a long-seeded mystery).

### STAGE 3 — THE SHADOW-CURSE APPROACH (Act 2: a soft-timer environment)
- Act 2's cursed lands around Moonrise Towers inflict damage-over-time unless you carry protection
  (moon lanterns / a Pixie blessing) — a SOFT CLOCK / survival-pressure environment gating the approach
  (cf. the ME2/Anju clocks, Questbooks 06/11). Reach the Gauntlet of Shar.

### STAGE 4 — THE GAUNTLET OF SHAR (Act 2: Shadowheart's trial)
- Shadowheart undergoes Shar's trials (the Self-Same trial, the Soft-Step trial, the Faith-Leap "trust
  the invisible bridge" trial) to reach the Nightsong — the dungeon IS her religious test, staging her
  devotion mechanically. Defeat BALTHAZAR (a necromancer who ALSO wants the Nightsong — a rival claimant,
  a la Vault 22's Keely/Hildern tension, Questbook 03).

### STAGE 5 — THE REVEAL + THE FORK (Act 2 climax: the choice)
- The "relic" is revealed: AYLIN, chained, immortal, tortured for a century. Shadowheart raises her
  blade — killing the Nightsong is her lifelong mission from Shar. THE PLAYER'S FORK (persuade
  Shadowheart):
  - URGE HER TO KILL: Shadowheart fully embraces Shar -> becomes a Dark Justiciar (gains a powerful
    armor reward), turns colder/darker, ENDS any romance with you, and DOOMS everyone at Last Light Inn;
    makes lifting the Shadow-Curse harder; in Act 3 she may even kill her own parents. You lose Aylin as
    an ally vs Ketheric.
  - STAY HER HAND (spare Aylin): Shadowheart forsakes Shar, feels abandoned/outcast but grows; Aylin
    lives and becomes a KEY ALLY (lifting the Shadow-Curse, defeating Ketheric); Shadowheart's Act 3 arc
    turns toward healing. Most companions approve.
- REACTIVITY: which way Shadowheart LEANS is shaped by your WHOLE playthrough — good deeds (saving the
  Grove, saving Isobel) incline her to spare; evil choices (siding with Minthara/the Absolute) incline
  her to kill. The choice reads the player's cumulative morality, not just this scene.

### STAGE 6 — THE REVERBERATION (Acts 2-3: the memory)
- The outcome ripples for dozens of hours: Shadowheart's personality, romance, powers, and Act 3
  family-confrontation content all fork here; the main-boss fight (Ketheric) is easier/harder; NPCs and
  factions in Baldur's Gate react to what you did. A "seemingly mundane" early treasure hunt becomes a
  load-bearing hinge for the endgame. (Point-of-no-return note: rushing the Mind Flayer Colony can LOCK
  this and other Moonrise content — order/timing matters.)

## 2. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE MACGUFFIN IS A PERSON: hyped as a "relic" to hunt, revealed as a tortured prisoner — the
    treasure framing makes the moral reveal land harder (you came for loot, you found a soul).
W2. THE CHOICE IS BOUND TO A COMPANION'S SOUL: the fork isn't abstract — it decides who SHADOWHEART
    BECOMES (her faith, personality, romance, powers). Moral weight through a person you care about.
W3. CHOICE READS THE WHOLE PLAYTHROUGH: Shadowheart's lean is shaped by your cumulative good/evil acts —
    the game remembers everything and expresses it HERE (reactivity-as-memory at scale; cf. ME2 loyalty,
    Questbook 06).
W4. THREE LEVELS OF CONSEQUENCE: one choice hits the personal (romance), the companion (her arc), and
    the world (Shadow-Curse, Ketheric, Last Light Inn). Consequence stacked, not flat.
W5. THE DUNGEON IS THE CHARACTER'S TEST: the Gauntlet of Shar's trials ARE Shadowheart's faith
    dramatized (trust-the-invisible-bridge = a leap of faith) — staging that externalizes her inner
    conflict (cf. KCD's live-the-routine, Questbook 08).
W6. DEFERRED REVEAL (long seeding): the "relic" is chased from Act 1 but only revealed in Act 2 — a
    mystery seeded for dozens of hours pays off with maximum weight.
W7. THE RIVAL CLAIMANT: Balthazar also wants the Nightsong -> a third party raises stakes + offers an
    alternative (hand her over) — the fork isn't just save/kill (cf. branch-router NPCs, Questbook 02).
W8. NO CLEAN "CORRECT" (but consequences are real): the game supports evil (Dark Justiciar) as a full,
    rewarded path (powerful armor) — not a fail state; each path has gains AND losses (cf. NV/DOS2).
W9. THE SOFT-TIMER ENVIRONMENT: the Shadow-Curse pressures the approach (damage unless protected) —
    survival tension without a UI clock (cf. Questbooks 06/11).
W10. ORDER/TIMING GATES CONSEQUENCE: rushing a point-of-no-return LOCKS this content — the world moves
    on its own logic; when you act matters (cf. Whispering Hillock timing, Questbook 04).
W11. THE SKILL-SHORTCUT: a Perception check reveals a lever that BYPASSES the temple puzzle — build/
    attention rewarded with a faster path (multiple keys; cf. Questbooks 02/03/15).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**SHADOWHEART** — wants to complete the mission Shar gave her, and wants to not look at why the mission requires her to have no past. Will trade: her secrecy loosens by drops if trust is banked. Will never say (cannot; the memories are literally locked): who she was. FUNCTION: the executioner who is also the case study — the quest asks her to kill the proof that her whole faith is a theft.

**THE NIGHTSONG (DAME AYLIN)** — sold to the player as a RELIC. Is a person: an immortal daughter of a goddess, chained to a rock, farmed for immortality by a man who kills her nightly. Wants: freedom, then vengeance, in that order and with interest. FUNCTION: the object that becomes a person the moment you look directly at it. The whole quest is a bait-and-switch on the word "it."

**KETHERIC THORM** — wants to never bury his daughter again; built an unkillable self on a chained woman's stolen immortality. Will never say: that he knows it's wrong; grief has eaten the part that could. FUNCTION: the boss whose invincibility is a moral statement — you cannot beat him without deciding what to do about the woman.

**SHAR (offstage) / SELUNE (offstage)** — two goddesses running a proxy war through one traumatized cleric. FUNCTION: the Q125 pattern at divine scale: the trial is never about the accused.

**THE PLAYER** — has banked (or not) two acts of trust with Shadowheart. FUNCTION: the only voice in the room she might hear over her god.
