## 0 CORE IDEA

**A woman raised as a lab subject — tortured, experimented on, turned into a weapon as a child — returns to the facility to destroy it as the revenge she has organized her whole life around, and finds records and rooms that contradict her memory: the place was not exactly what she remembers, and the truth complicates the vengeance without erasing the wound.**

Jack, "Subject Zero," was taken as a child and subjected to brutal biotic experiments in a secret facility — pain, isolation, conditioning that made her the most powerful and most broken human biotic alive. Her entire identity is built on that trauma and the rage it forged; her loyalty mission is her demand to go back and blow the place up, the revenge she has waited her whole life to take. But when she arrives, the facility is not the monolithic torture-machine of her memory. Records reveal complications: other children, a warden who may have had a different role than she remembers, experiments that were crueler or less cruel or differently-aimed than the child understood them. The foundational memory — the story Jack built her whole self on — turns out to be partial, distorted by a child's terror and years of rage; the truth is uglier in some ways and less simple in others.

The machine is that the wound is real and the story about it is false, and revenge must be taken (or refused) in that gap. Jack's pain is not in doubt — she WAS tortured, she WAS made a weapon, the facility WAS a horror. But the specific narrative she organized her rage around — who did it, why, what it meant — is contradicted by what she finds, and the player is there as she confronts that her foundational truth was a child's edit. The quest lets her burn the place down anyway (the catharsis of destroying the forge, regardless of the details), or reckon with the complication (a harder, incomplete peace). And the player's role is to help her hold both: that she was genuinely wronged, and that the story she told herself about the wrong was not accurate — and that the revenge is still hers to take or leave, in a truth messier than the rage.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the wound can be real and the story you built on it false at the same time, and finding out your foundational memory was edited by a child's terror does not un-wound you — it only asks whether the revenge you organized your life around is the one you actually needed.

- **What it holds:** records, rooms, and other children that complicate Jack's narrative.
- **Function:** THE CONTRADICTION: the place revisited, whose truth is messier than the memory the survivor built on it.

### THE RECORDS / THE OTHER CHILDREN — the evidence against the memory
- Documents and survivors that reveal the experiment was different than Jack understood.
- **Function:** THE EDIT REVEALED: the proof that the child's terror distorted the foundational truth.

### THE PLAYER (Shepard) — the one who helps her hold both
- **What they want:** to help Jack take or refuse the revenge in the gap between the real wound and the false story.
- **Function:** THE WITNESS to the reckoning: the one who helps her hold that she was wronged AND that the story was wrong.

### THE REVENGE — the catharsis that may not fit the truth
- The destruction of the facility Jack has waited her life to take.
- **Function:** THE CHOICE: burn the forge for the real wound, or reckon with the complicated truth — catharsis or accuracy.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal postures — burn the facility (catharsis) or reckon with the truth (incomplete peace) — both after the foundational memory is revealed as partial, gated by whether the player helps her hold the wound and the false story at once.**

- **BURN IT DOWN:** the facility destroyed; the catharsis of ending the forge, regardless of the complicated details; the revenge taken for the real wound. WRITES: the place gone; the rage spent; the truth acknowledged but the vengeance still claimed.
- **RECKON WITH THE TRUTH:** the complication faced; the rage examined instead of spent; a harder, incomplete peace. WRITES: the forge maybe left standing; the story corrected; the survivor holding a messier truth.
- **THE MEMORY-EDIT CONSTANT:** in both branches, the foundational story is revealed as a child's distortion — the wound real, the narrative false; the reckoning happens regardless of the ending.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** the branch is catharsis-versus-accuracy — two answers to "what revenge is owed when the story was a lie," and the quest refuses to un-wound the survivor or restore the clean story.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE CONTRADICTION IS UNDERPLAYED, SO THE MEMORY-EDIT LANDS LIGHT.** Jack's facility does complicate her memory, but the game states the discrepancies more than it makes the player FEEL the vertigo of a foundational truth collapsing — so the powerful "your story was false, your wound was real" idea reads as a plot note rather than the ground shifting under a survivor.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the collapse of a foundational memory must be FELT as vertigo — the survivor and player experiencing the ground shift, the story they trusted contradicted piece by piece — so the edit-revealed lands as trauma, not exposition. A memory correction the player doesn't feel is a footnote (the #32/#167 reveal-as-experience law).

**F2 — THE "BURN IT ANYWAY" AND "RECKON" PATHS AREN'T DIFFERENTIATED ENOUGH.** Both endings largely lead to the facility's destruction; the "examine the rage instead of spending it" path isn't given a distinct, felt outcome, so the profound choice (catharsis vs accuracy) collapses into a tonal difference rather than a real fork with different consequences.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a survivor's reckoning offers catharsis-vs-accuracy, the two must lead to genuinely DIFFERENT outcomes — the burned forge and the examined rage producing different survivors, different worlds — so the choice matters beyond tone. A fork with one destination is a mood, not a decision (the #25 real-fork law).

**F3 — JACK'S WOUND IS TOLD THROUGH ATTITUDE, WHICH CAN HIDE IT.** Jack's trauma is delivered through her aggression and defensiveness — an effective coping surface — but so consistently that some players read her as just abrasive and never feel the child under the rage, so the reckoning's emotional payoff depends on a wound the player may not have felt.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a survivor's wound is hidden under a coping surface (aggression, deflection), the surface must CRACK before the reckoning — the child under the rage glimpsed — so the memory-collapse has an emotional foundation. The wound must be felt before the story about it is corrected (#162's guilt-cracks-before-the-end law, applied to trauma).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE WOUND IS REAL AND THE STORY IS FALSE. Jack was genuinely tortured, and the narrative she built on it turns out partial — the quest holds both truths at once, which is the rarest and most honest thing to say about trauma.
W2. THE SURVIVOR RETURNS TO THE FORGE. Jack goes back to the place that made her a weapon — the returning-to-the-forge (#165) with a memory about to be tested against the rooms.
W3. THE FOUNDATIONAL MEMORY IS EDITED. The story Jack organized her whole self on was distorted by a child's terror — the recorded-vs-unrecorded self (#26) as a survivor's foundational truth.
W4. THE REVENGE IS COMPLICATED, NOT ERASED. Finding the story was false doesn't un-wound Jack or cancel the revenge — it asks whether the vengeance she needed is the one she's owed, in a truth messier than the rage.
W5. THE PLAYER HELPS HER HOLD BOTH. Shepard's role is to help Jack hold that she was wronged AND that the story was wrong — the witness to a reckoning, not the fixer of it.
W6. CATHARSIS OR ACCURACY. Burn the forge for the real wound, or examine the rage in the complicated truth — the choice is between the satisfaction of revenge and the harder honesty of the facts.
W7. THE CLEAN STORY CAN'T BE RESTORED. Once the memory is revealed as partial, the simple revenge is gone — the removed "give her back the clean story" verb is the quest's honesty: foundational lies don't un-reveal.
W8. THE COPING SURFACE IS AGGRESSION. Jack's rage is armor over a child's wound — the trauma hidden in attitude, cracked at the reckoning (#162).
W9. THE FACILITY TESTIFIES AGAINST THE MEMORY. The rooms and records contradict the story — the space is the evidence, the forge itself correcting the survivor's account of it.
W10. IT IS THE FOLD'S TRUTH IN ONE LIFE. A wound carried forward on a story that turns out false — Bohemia's whole question of what the past really was, and what the living owe a memory, in a single survivor's revenge.
