# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 115: YOU LOST THE COIN TOSS / THERE WAS NEVER A COIN (The Coin Toss, SOMA)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where the only comfort anyone can offer you is a lie about
probability, and the game makes you take that comfort three times before showing you it was never true —
and the version of you that got the good ending doesn't know you exist. Complete event flow, the scan, the
two Simons in one room, the option to kill him, the suicides, the ARK, Catherine's last words, the honest
flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our AMALGAMATION, our recorded-vs-unrecorded, our fold,
our death-math, our conscience system, + our ART PHILOSOPHY. Frictional Games. Reference only; Paolo does
not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: the consciousness-transfer chain, SOMA (2015). Earth is dead — "**A COMET HAS HIT THE EARTH AND
CIVILIZATION HAS RETREATED TO A SMALL COMMUNITY OF SCIENTISTS LIVING AT THE BOTTOM OF THE OCEAN FLOOR. THIS
COMMUNITY WAS HUMANITY'S LAST HOPE, BUT SOMETHING WENT TERRIBLY WRONG.**" You are a brain scan from 2015
running in a diving suit in 2104.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE UPLOADED DEAD ARE RUNNING WITHOUT KNOWING THEY'RE DEAD (the premise — STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION):
  "**HIS BRAIN SCAN MADE BACK IN TORONTO WAS **DONATED TO SCIENCE BEFORE HIS DEATH** AND FUNCTIONED AS A
  TEMPLATE FOR EXPERIMENTS INTO HOW BRAINS FUNCTIONED. ONE OF THESE TEMPLATES MADE ITS WAY ONTO PATHOS II
  AND WAS PUT TO USE BY WAU, PLACED INTO A ROBOTIZED DIVING SUIT.**"
  - Punk: **Simon signed a consent form in 2015.** He donated a scan for medical research and died of his
    brain injury. A century later a machine found the file and **ran it**, and the man woke up at the bottom
    of the ocean not knowing he'd been dead for a hundred years (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation
    threat logic + NETWORK + recorded-vs-unrecorded; the data-portrait booted by something that meant well).
  - "**AT THE BEGINNING SIMON DOESN'T REALIZE WHAT HAS HAPPENED TO HIM, HIS DIGITAL BRAIN REFUSING TO
    COMPREHEND HIS CURRENT SITUATION. HOWEVER, THE REALITY IS REVEALED TO HIM WHEN A COMMUNICATIONS ROOM
    SUFFERS A STRUCTURAL BREAK AND FLOODS, **YET HE FINDS HIMSELF 'ABLE TO BREATHE.'**"
- THE COMFORT IS A LIE AND THE PERSON TELLING IT MAY BELIEVE IT (the quest — STARTLINGLY our conscience
  system + companions): "**CATHERINE EXPLAINS THE PROCESS USING A COIN FLIP METAPHOR. WHEN YOUR CONSCIOUSNESS
  IS SCANNED AND UPLOADED, SHE TELLS SIMON, THERE'S A 50/50 CHANCE YOU WILL 'WAKE UP' IN THE NEW BODY OR
  REMAIN IN THE OLD ONE. **THIS IS A LIE, THOUGH CATHERINE MAY NOT REALIZE IT. THERE IS NO COIN FLIP.**"
  - "**WHEN CONSCIOUSNESS IS COPIED, BOTH VERSIONS CONTINUE TO EXIST AS SEPARATE, DIVERGING ENTITIES. THE
    ORIGINAL DOES NOT HAVE A 50% CHANCE OF WAKING UP AS THE COPY.**"
  - **There is no coin.** There is a copy, and an original, and the original stays exactly where it is
    forever (cf. our conscience system + AMALGAMATION + Megaton law).
- AND THE PLAYER IS THE REASON THE LIE WORKS (the design — STARTLINGLY our ART PHILOSOPHY): the sharpest
  reading anyone's written of this game — "**WHAT IS INTERESTING IS THAT WE, THE PLAYER, JUMP FROM SIMON 1,
  TO SIMON 2, TO SIMON 3. **TO US, THIS IS ONE CONTINUATION OF CONSCIOUSNESS.** OF COURSE SIMON 3 IS THE REAL
  SIMON, OR AT LEAST THE REAL ROBOT SIMON, BECAUSE **WE ARE IN HIS HEAD.** OF COURSE CONSCIOUSNESS JUMPS FROM
  ONE BEING TO ANOTHER; **WE THE PLAYER HAVE JUST EXPERIENCED THAT.** TO US, THE 'COIN FLIP' THAT CATHERINE
  SPEAKS OF IS REAL. IT HAPPENS. WE LIVED IT. **THAT'S IMPORTANT LATER.**"
  - **The camera is the lie.** You believe in the coin toss because the game keeps following you, and it
    keeps following you because it's a video game. The medium is the con (STARTLINGLY our ART PHILOSOPHY +
    [READ]; the argument that only works because you're playing it).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE FIRST DEATH IS PAPERWORK
- "**SOMA STARTS OUT WITH YOU, PLAYING AS SIMON JARRETT, GOING TO SEE A DOCTOR FOR A NEW BRAIN SCAN TECHNIQUE
  INTENDED TO HELP FIX A BRAIN TRAUMA SUFFERED IN A CAR ACCIDENT.**"
- "**BUT THE 'ORIGINAL' SIMON ONLY SITS DOWN, GETS HIS BRAIN SCANNED, AND…**WELL, GETS KILLED. BUMMER.**"
- **The man you play for the first fifteen minutes dies offscreen, of his injuries, weeks later, alone in
  Toronto.** You never see it. The prologue is his last good day and you don't know it (cf. our fold +
  death-math + [READ]).
- Simon 2.0: "**HE HAS THE MEMORIES OF SIMON 1.0 UP UNTIL THE BRAIN SCAN, **BUT LACKS THE REMAINING MEMORIES
  OF SIMON 1.0, WHO LEFT THE DOCTOR'S OFFICE AND EVENTUALLY DIED BECAUSE OF HIS BRAIN INJURIES.**"
### STAGE 1 — THE MECHANIC IS COPY-PASTE AND THE GAME SAYS SO
- "**THINK OF THIS LIKE COPYING AND PASTING A FILE ON A COMPUTER. **WHEN THE FILE IS PASTED, BOTH VERSIONS
  STILL REMAIN.** THE TWO HAVE A COMMON FOUNDATION, BUT CAN LEAD DIFFERENT LIVES. **WHICH FILE IS THE 'REAL'
  FILE, OR DOESN'T IT MATTER?**"
- And it's demonstrated, not argued: "**CATHERINE INSTRUCTS SIMON TO ACQUIRE A POWER SUIT IN ORDER TO REACH
  SOME OF THE DEEPER PARTS OF PATHOS II. **BECAUSE SIMON IS INHABITING THE DIVING SUIT ITSELF, RATHER THAN
  BEING INSIDE IT, HIS MIND NEEDS TO BE PLACED IN THE NEW SUIT.**"
- **You need a better suit, so you copy yourself into it.** The philosophical crisis arrives as an equipment
  upgrade (STARTLINGLY our inventory + AMALGAMATION + conscience system; the atrocity as a gear change).
### STAGE 2 — YOU HEAR YOURSELF TALKING AROUND THE CORNER
- "**WHEN THE TRANSFER OCCURS, AS SIMON 2.0 IS COPIED AND BIRTHS SIMON 3.0, **SIMON 3.0 AWAKENS AND HEARS
  SOMEONE FAMILIAR TALKING AROUND THE CORNER: SIMON 2.0.**"
- The exchange, verbatim:
  - **Simon 3.0: "GOD DAMN YOU, CATHERINE. TWO SIMONS? THERE CAN'T BE TWO SIMONS."**
  - **Catherine: "YOU KNOW IT'S NOT MAGIC. YOU WERE COPIED. THE OTHER SIMON IN THE SEAT WAS COPIED…AND NOW
    YOU ARE HERE. JUST LIKE SIMON LIVED ON IN TORONTO."**
  - **Simon 3.0: "THAT YOU WERE GONNA TAKE MY MIND AND PUT IT IN ANOTHER BODY, LIKE A BRAIN TRANSPLANT."**
  - **Catherine: "I'M SORRY, IT WOULDN'T WORK THAT WAY."**
- "**JUST LIKE SIMON LIVED ON IN TORONTO.**" She's telling him: **this already happened to you once, and you
  were the one left behind, and you died of a head injury a hundred years ago** (cf. our fold + [READ] +
  AMALGAMATION).
### STAGE 3 — AND THEN THE GAME HANDS YOU THE SWITCH
- "**AS SIMON 3.0, YOU HAVE THE OPTION TO KILL SIMON 2.0 OR LET HIM LIVE.**"
- That's it. No prompt, no morality meter, no consequence tracked anywhere. **The other you is sitting in a
  chair, awake, panicking, and you can turn him off.** Or leave him there, awake, forever, at the bottom of
  the ocean, in a suit, alone.
- **Neither option is mercy.** (STARTLINGLY our conscience system + no-clean-answer + Megaton law +
  death-math + PACIFIST LAW; the choice with no kind branch).
### STAGE 4 — THE STATION FIGURED THIS OUT BEFORE YOU DID (and it killed them)
- "**THIS DROVE SOME PEOPLE ON PATHOS-II WILD, UNABLE TO GRAPPLE WITH THE CONCEPT OF TWO VERSIONS OF
  THEMSELVES EXISTING AT THE SAME TIME. **SUICIDES BECAME RAMPANT AFTER BRAIN SCANS, AS PEOPLE TRIED TO
  RESOLVE THE PSYCHOLOGICAL CONFLICT.**"
- The crew of the last human outpost on Earth scanned themselves to save humanity **and then started killing
  themselves** because they couldn't stand knowing there were two of them (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION +
  conscience system + death-math + city-builder; the technology whose side effect is despair).
- And a rival theory the survivors invented to cope: "**A HYPOTHESIS WAS PROPOSED BY ONE OF THE SURVIVORS,
  SARANG, WHICH SUGGESTED THAT **CONTINUITY OF EXPERIENCE WAS THE BASIS OF YOUR IDENTITY AND YOUR TICKET TO
  THE ARK.**" They wrote a philosophy paper to make the fear go away.
### STAGE 5 — THE INTERVIEWS (the worst scene in the game and it's an admin task)
- Catherine's own account of running the scans, and Punk, this is the one:
- "**IT WAS CLEAR FROM THESE INTERVIEWS THAT THESE PEOPLE **DID NOT KNOW WHAT THEY WERE GETTING INTO WHEN
  THEY UPLOADED THEIR BRAIN DATA.** THEY WERE THINKING THEY WERE GOING TO WAKE UP IN THE SCANNER ROOM, AND
  COPIES OF THEMSELVES COULD EXIST SOMEWHERE ELSE. THEY DIDN'T SEEM TO REALIZE THE IMPLICATION THAT **THEY
  COULD WAKE UP MILLIONS OF TIMES AND ALL THE TIMES IT WOULD FEEL LIKE A DIRECT CONTINUATION.** IF I RAN
  THEIR SIMULATIONS IN ANYTHING BUT THAT SCANNER ROOM, **THEY FREAKED OUT AND STARTED HAVING AN EXISTENTIAL
  CRISIS.**"
- So she fakes the room. And later you have to do it yourself, to get a password out of a dead man:
- "**I FINALLY FOUND THE MAN WITH THE PASSWORD AND CATHARINE TALKED TO HIM AS IF THE SCAN HAD BEEN NORMAL.
  **IT HAD TO BE AS CASUAL AS POSSIBLE, SO WE HAD TO TRY A FEW TIMES TO GET THE WORDING JUST RIGHT.** IT WAS
  EERIE, REALLY. LIKE TALKING TO A GHOST, OR **THE BROKEN RECORD OF A PERSON'S LIFE.**"
- **You reboot a dead man repeatedly, workshopping your opening line, until you get one where he doesn't
  panic long enough to tell you a password.** That is our AMALGAMATION's entire moral horizon in one puzzle
  (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic + NETWORK + [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded +
  conscience system; the uploaded dead as a system you query until it cooperates).
### STAGE 6 — THE ARK (the plan is to email humanity into space)
- "**THE ARK, A SATELLITE CONTAINING A SIMULATED PARADISE LAUNCHED INTO SPACE**"; "**THIS ARK WOULD THEN
  PRESUMABLY SURVIVE AS LONG AS THE ONBOARD EQUIPMENT OF THE SATELLITE WOULD CONTINUE FUNCTIONING.**"
- Humanity's last-ditch effort is **a hard drive with a heaven on it**, and its lifespan is a hardware
  warranty (cf. our AMALGAMATION + electricity + survival accounting).
- And it almost didn't happen at all: the ARK "**EVENTUALLY ENDED UP NOT BEING LAUNCHED DUE TO DISAGREEMENTS
  BETWEEN THE LAST SURVIVORS.**" The end of the world was delayed by an argument.
### STAGE 7 — YOU LOSE (and the game does not blink)
- "**IN THE ENDING, THE PLAYER REACHES THE ARK AND TRANSFERS THEIR BRAIN SCAN ONTO HUMANITY'S LAST DITCH
  EFFORT. **BUT RATHER THAN WAKING UP IN A VIRTUAL WORLD, YOU'RE STILL AT A CONTROL PANEL. YOU, SIMON 3.0,
  ARE SCREWED. YOU LOST THE COIN TOSS.**"
- The full exchange, and it's the best-written ending in the medium:
  - **Simon 3.0: "I'M STILL HERE…? I'M STILL HERE? CATHERINE? CATHERINE!"**
  - **Catherine: "I'M HERE."**
  - **Simon 3.0: "WHAT THE HELL HAPPENED? WHAT WENT WRONG?"**
  - **Catherine: "NOTHING! THEY'RE OUT THERE, AMONG THE STARS. WE'RE HERE."**
  - **Simon 3.0: "NO, WE WERE GETTING ON THE ARK. I SAW IT. IT FINISHED LOADING JUST BEFORE IT LAUNCHED."**
  - **Catherine: "YEAH, I SAW."**
  - **Catherine: "SIMON, I CAN'T KEEP TELLING YOU HOW IT WORKS; YOU WON'T LISTEN. YOU KNOW WHY WE'RE HERE.
    YOU WERE COPIED ONTO THE ARK, YOU JUST DIDN'T CARRY OVER. YOU LOST THE COIN TOSS."**
- **"Nothing! They're out there, among the stars. We're here."** Nothing went wrong. **The plan worked
  perfectly.** That's the horror (STARTLINGLY our Megaton law + AMALGAMATION + no-clean-answer; the total
  success that is a personal apocalypse).
### STAGE 8 — AND THEN YOU BREAK HER
- "**CATHERINE, WHO EXISTS AS AN AI IN A PORTABLE DEVICE, TRIES TO CALM SIMON. **BUT HE LASHES OUT, DAMAGING
  HER DEVICE. SHE SHUTS DOWN MID-SENTENCE, LEAVING SIMON IN TOTAL ISOLATION. THE SCREEN FADES TO BLACK AS HE
  BEGS HER NOT TO LEAVE HIM ALONE.**"
- A player: "**SHE IS EXCITED, AND RIGHTFULLY SO: CATHARINE AND SIMON MADE IT TO ARK. SHE SAYS 'WE LOST THE
  COIN TOSS, THAT'S IT, BUT WE MADE IT.' **THEN HER SCREEN CRACKS, THE COMPUTER BREAKS, AND I, SUIT-SIMON, AM
  STUCK AT THE BOTTOM OF THE OCEAN IN A POST-APOCALYPTIC EARTH, THE ONLY SENTIENT BEING STILL IN EXISTENCE**,
  A DEEP PIT IN MY STOMACH, WATCHING THE FUTURE OF HUMANITY FLY OFF INTO THE DISTANCE. **I WILL NEVER GET TO
  SEE IT.**"
- **You destroy the last person who will ever talk to you, in an argument about whether you won.** (cf. our
  companions + conscience system + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope).
### STAGE 9 — THE LAST SHOT (the thread of hope, and it's not for you)
- "**THEN THE PERSPECTIVE SHIFTS. PLAYERS SEE THE ARK. SIMON-4 WAKES UP IN A BEAUTIFUL VIRTUAL ENVIRONMENT,
  CATHERINE BESIDE HIM. **NEITHER IS AWARE OF SIMON-3. BOTH BELIEVE THEY SUCCESSFULLY TRANSFERRED.** THE GAME
  ENDS WITH SIMON-4 EXPERIENCING JOY AND RELIEF WHILE SIMON-3 EXPERIENCES DESPAIR AND ABANDONMENT.**"
- The game gives you the happy ending. **It just doesn't give it to you.** It's for a version of you that
  doesn't know you exist, and Catherine is there, and she's fine, and they're together, and they made it
  (STARTLINGLY our ART PHILOSOPHY + despair-with-a-thread-of-hope + Liberate/Respect/Become; the hope that
  is real and not yours).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- SIMON IS AN IDIOT ON PURPOSE AND IT COSTS: "**THE ENDING WAS REALLY CLEAR AND PREDICTABLE, WITH CATHERINE
  BASICALLY EXPLAINING HOW THINGS WORKED IN ENCOUNTERS PRIOR TO IT, **AND YET SIMON NEVER PICKED UP ON IT**
  WHICH GAVE IT A REALLY NICE DEGREE OF DRAMATIC IRONY.**" And the counter: "**I FOUND MYSELF AGGRAVATED AT
  HIS UNDERSTANDING OF WHAT WAS GOING ON.**" LESSON (ours, sharp): a protagonist who won't learn is a
  device, and players who got it in hour two spend eight hours watching a man be dense. Our fold's dynast
  must be **as smart as the player** and lose anyway.
- THE PLAYER FIGURES IT OUT AND STILL CAN'T ACT: a philosopher, in real time — "**I KNEW THERE WAS NO 'COIN
  TOSS' AND I STILL WANTED THAT FOR OUR POOR SIMON**"; and "**I TRIED TO HOLD THIS PERSPECTIVE. SUIT-SIMON
  AND ARK-SIMON ARE BOTH ME. IT DOESN'T MATTER, I SAID TO MYSELF — IT ISN'T A COIN TOSS, THEY ARE EQUALLY ME
  PRE-SCAN.**" You can understand the whole thing perfectly and the game gives you no way to express that.
  LESSON: if the player's insight has no mechanical outlet, the theme is a cutscene.
- IT'S A HORROR GAME WRAPPED AROUND A PHILOSOPHY SEMINAR: "**I HAVEN'T EVEN CONSIDERED REPLAYING THE GAME,
  MAINLY BECAUSE HORROR GAMES AREN'T REALLY SOMETHING I PARTICULARLY ENJOY... I GOT THE SPARK TO GO AND
  REVISIT THE GAME, **VIA THE MEDIUM OF WIKI PAGES AND YOUTUBE VIDEOS BECAUSE I STILL DON'T PARTICULARLY WANT
  TO RUN THROUGH CORRIDORS DODGING NASTY MONSTERS MYSELF.**" LESSON (~3rd confirmation, with Q99/Q105): the
  monsters are the tax people pay for your ideas, and some of them just won't pay it — our PACIFIST LAW says
  don't charge it.
- THE MONSTERS ARE THE WEAKEST PART OF THE BEST-WRITTEN GAME OF ITS YEAR: they're chase sequences in a game
  whose actual antagonist is **a concept**. LESSON: if your enemy is an idea, adding a thing that bites is
  an admission you didn't trust the idea.
- THE ENDING IS UNACTIONABLE BY DESIGN: no choice you made changes it. LESSON (double-edged): a fixed ending
  makes the thesis airtight and makes the player a passenger — our Liberate/Respect/Become exists precisely
  so the thesis has three doors.
- THE COIN TOSS IS ARGUABLY A CHEAT: fans still fight about it a decade on ("**THE MOST RECENT EXPANSION OF
  THE COIN THEORY CAME FROM REDDITOR NICKDEAN16... IT'S NOT REALLY A COIN TOSS, IT'S A DICE ROLL**").
  LESSON: if your central metaphor is wrong ON PURPOSE and a character delivers it sincerely, half your
  audience will think the WRITER was confused.
