# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 09: DREAM ON / THE PERALEZ ARC (Cyberpunk 2077)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: event, staging, stage-by-stage flow, the deliberate no-resolution
ending, the horror craft, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's model for WITHHELD-CLOSURE HORROR +
MIND-EDITING DREAD + THE INVESTIGATOR-IS-IMPLICATED — and it maps DIRECTLY onto our own Q012 (the
puppet couple / mind-edited council). Written by Patrick Mills under quest director Paweł Sasko (the
same Bloody Baron designer — see Questbook 01). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia
quest written here.

Quest chain: "I Fought the Law" (intro) -> "Dream On" (the payoff). Side jobs in Night City.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE PREMISE / STAKES
===============================================================================
- WHO: Jefferson Peralez (an idealistic mayoral candidate) and his wife Elizabeth — a polished,
  powerful political couple. V first meets them investigating the previous mayor's death ("I Fought
  the Law").
- THE HOOK: later, Elizabeth calls — they can't say what's wrong over the phone (paranoia seeded). At
  the apartment, Jefferson reports a BREAK-IN: he confronted an intruder, fired shots, but "didn't get
  a good look." He asks V to investigate for evidence.
- THE UNRAVELING TRUTH: it's not a burglar. A shadow group (a "Secure Your Soul"-adjacent org / a
  possible ROGUE AI) is SURVEILLING the couple and EDITING THEIR MINDS — wiping memories, implanting
  new ones — to groom Jefferson into a controllable puppet mayor. Their entire reality is being
  authored by someone else.
- THE DREAD: the couple is trapped inside a conspiracy they can't perceive; even their MEMORY of
  events (the "break-in") is manufactured. The horror is epistemic — you cannot trust anyone's
  reality, including the victims' own account of themselves.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE FULL STAGE-BY-STAGE FLOW
===============================================================================

### STAGE 1 — THE CALL + THE UNEASE (the seed)
- After "I Fought the Law," Elizabeth calls (delayed, not immediate — the quest waits, so it surfaces
  organically). They won't talk over the phone. STAGING: the refusal-to-speak plants paranoia before
  you arrive. (Player-noted tell: the couple "never once bickered" — an uncanny too-perfect harmony
  that unnerves before you know why. The unease is authored into their BEHAVIOR.)

### STAGE 2 — THE INVESTIGATION (the apartment as a puzzle-text)
- Elizabeth gives a tour; V uses the SCANNER (yellow-highlighted objects) to examine the scene: a
  blood trail, the "intruder" evidence, the security room. Optional worldbuilding rewards the thorough
  (Jefferson's absurd campaign ad on his PC; emails where Judy personally recommends V — texture that
  makes the couple feel real). The investigation is READING A SPACE for a truth its owners can't see.

### STAGE 3 — THE HIDDEN ROOM (multi-key)
- Scanning reveals a HIDDEN DOOR in the security area. Two keys: force it with a high BODY attribute,
  OR find the code 6709 on the first terminal to unlock the second and open the room (skill vs
  investigation). Inside: cables leading to the roof.

### STAGE 4 — THE TRANSMISSION (the reveal begins)
- Follow the cables to a rooftop SATELLITE DISH transmitting to another antenna blocks away — someone
  is beaming INTO the apartment. A black SURVEILLANCE VAN is parked nearby. The scope of the intrusion
  becomes physical: they aren't robbed, they're BROADCAST INTO.

### STAGE 5 — THE CHASE + THE HIDEOUT (the action beat)
- Leave, pursue the van (a driving chase — LOSE it and the job FAILS; the investigation demands you
  keep up). It leads to a Maelstrom-held hideout. Fight/stealth through; regardless of approach, you
  witness an INVISIBLE (cloaked) AV fleeing — a power far above street-gang level is involved.

### STAGE 6 — THE CONFIRMATION (the worst-case, verified)
- Hack the van's access point. Your worst fear confirmed: the Peralezes' minds are being ALTERED —
  memories wiped, new ones implanted, WITHOUT their knowledge or consent. The "break-in" was a
  glitch/artifact of the editing. The victims don't know who they are anymore; neither do you.

### STAGE 7 — ELIZABETH'S CONFESSION (the double-bind)
- Call Elizabeth; meet at a Japantown ramen shop. She reveals she ALREADY KNEW about the surveillance —
  but the shadow org THREATENED her into silence, forbidding her to tell Jefferson. She hands V the
  choice: TELL Jefferson the truth, or leave him in the manufactured peace.

### STAGE 8 — THE CHOICE (deliberately inconsequential — and that's the point)
- Meet Jefferson. THE FORK:
  - TELL JEFFERSON the truth: he's disturbed — but there's no indication he'll even RETAIN the memory
    (they can just edit it out again); AND Elizabeth then BLOCKS V from ever contacting the couple
    again (you've broken her bargain).
  - DON'T TELL / keep the secret: Jefferson stays in the fabricated calm; you've arguably become
    complicit in the scheme by staying silent.
- CRUCIAL: the choice does NOT resolve the plot. Either way, V is BLOCKED from the couple afterward;
  either way, the mind-editing continues out in the world beyond V's reach. There is no "save them"
  option. The quest ENDS on the unresolved dread.

===============================================================================
## 2. THE DELIBERATE NON-ENDING (the design thesis — confirmed by the devs)
===============================================================================
- Quest director PAWEŁ SASKO and writer PATRICK MILLS CONFIRMED the unresolved denouement is INTENDED.
  Mills: the story "certainly goes on, but our role in it as V is fully concluded" — V sees "a slice"
  of a larger fiction that continues without them. The unsettled feeling is the DESIGNED payoff.
- The questions the quest leaves open (Kotaku): Are you endangering the Peralezes by telling Jefferson?
  Are you complicit if you don't? Will Jefferson even remember? Did you make the right choice? What IS
  the right choice? — The game refuses to answer ANY of them. The ambiguity is the content.
- WHY IT'S NOT A COP-OUT: the withheld closure ENACTS the theme — in a world where reality is edited,
  you (and the victims) can't have certainty; the epistemic helplessness is transmitted to the PLAYER.
  Form = content. (Contrast the anti-pattern: withheld closure is only powerful when it's the SUBJECT;
  a quest that withholds closure for no thematic reason just feels unfinished.)

===============================================================================
## 3. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. THE UNCANNY TELL IN BEHAVIOR: the couple's TOO-PERFECT harmony (never bickering) unnerves the
    player before any reveal — the horror is seeded in CHARACTER BEHAVIOR, not a jump scare.
W2. THE MUNDANE HOOK -> COSMIC DREAD: opens as a routine break-in investigation, escalates to mind-
    editing conspiracy — the ordinary frame makes the reveal land harder (see also Vault 22's dirty
    errand, Questbook 03).
W3. THE SPACE AS PUZZLE-TEXT: you SCAN an apartment to read a truth its own owners can't perceive —
    investigation as reading an environment (ties Obra Dinn / Witcher senses).
W4. EPISTEMIC HORROR (you can't trust reality): the victims' own MEMORIES are manufactured — the dread
    isn't a monster, it's the impossibility of certainty. The scariest thing is edited reality.
W5. THE INVESTIGATOR IS IMPLICATED: staying silent makes YOU complicit; telling may endanger them AND
    changes nothing. There's no clean hands — the player is drawn into the moral trap.
W6. WITHHELD CLOSURE AS THEME (form = content): the unresolved ending ENACTS the powerlessness the
    quest is about; you leave with the same uncertainty the victims live in. (Dev-confirmed intent.)
W7. THE CHOICE THAT "DOESN'T MATTER" — AND THAT'S THE POINT: mechanically the fork changes little
    (you're blocked either way; the plot continues) — but it forces the player to DECIDE what they
    believe is right with no feedback, which is more unsettling than a consequential choice.
W8. A SLICE OF A LARGER FICTION: the world CONTINUES beyond V's involvement — you glimpse a piece of
    something bigger that doesn't revolve around you. The world feels vast and indifferent.
W9. POWER ABOVE YOUR PAY-GRADE: the cloaked AV, the rogue-AI implication — the antagonist is out of
    reach, unbeatable at your level; you can witness but not defeat it. Dread from asymmetry.
W10. DELAYED, ORGANIC TRIGGER: the quest calls you later, mid-other-content — it surfaces like a real
    development, not a menu item, making the world feel alive.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — DIRECT MATCH TO OUR Q012
===============================================================================
Our Q012 ("What the Mirrors Kept" — the mind-edited council / puppet couple) is BUILT on this shape.
This teardown is the master reference for sharpening it AND for the Amalgamation's mind-editing horror.
- W1 (uncanny behavioral tell): render our mind-edited NPCs with a TOO-PERFECT tell (they never
  disagree; they repeat a phrase; their grief is smoothed) — seed the dread in behavior before the
  reveal (our Q012 face-smoothing tech is exactly this; lean into the behavioral uncanny).
- W2 (mundane hook -> cosmic dread): open our Amalgamation-editing quests as an ORDINARY problem (a
  break-in, a memory dispute, a misfiled record) that escalates to mind-editing — the mundane frame
  sharpens the horror.
- W3 (space as puzzle-text): our [READ]/scan investigations should let the player read a SPACE for a
  truth its inhabitants can't see (the servers' influence in a home, the editing artifacts).
- W4 (epistemic horror): THE Amalgamation's core weapon — it edits the LIVING (Q012) and hoards the
  dead (Q018/Q050). Bank epistemic dread as its signature: you can't trust anyone's reality, including
  your own memory (ties the unrecorded ledger — what if a memory was edited?).
- W5 (investigator implicated): our mind-edit quests should trap the dynasty in complicity — expose
  the edit and endanger the victim, or stay silent and enable it; no clean hands (Q012 upgrade).
- W6 (withheld closure AS theme): for the Amalgamation's mind-editing thread specifically, bank the
  DELIBERATE no-clean-resolution ending — the dynasty can't fully save an edited mind, and the
  helplessness IS the point (use ONLY where thematically earned — our Q012 already does this; validated
  by the dev-confirmed master example). Distinguish from quests that SHOULD resolve (most of ours do).
- W7 (the choice that doesn't "matter"): a rare Bohemia fork where the mechanical outcome barely
  changes but the player must decide what's RIGHT with no feedback — unsettling by design (reserve for
  the epistemic-horror thread; contrast our usual Megaton divergence).
- W8/W9 (a slice of a larger fiction + power above your pay-grade): early Amalgamation quests should
  show it as BIGGER than the dynasty can yet touch — you witness, you can't defeat (this is exactly our
  proximity-heat threat-logic and why the endgame requires the WHOLE Reconstruction; validated).
- W10 (delayed organic trigger): let some Bohemia quests CALL the dynasty later, mid-other-content, so
  they surface like real developments (ties our Heartache-style pre-seeding, Questbook collection B3).

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**ELIZABETH PERALEZ** — wants her husband PROTECTED, and has decided that includes from the truth. Will trade: eddies for the investigation she will later ask you to bury. Will never say (until the end): that she chose for him. FUNCTION: the client who becomes the censor. The quest's real antagonist is her love.

**JEFFERSON PERALEZ** — wants to be mayor to FIX things, and wants his own mind to be his. Will never say: "I'm frightened" — until the balcony, where he says exactly that, which is why the scene lands. FUNCTION: the victim who must choose between two unbearable sanities.

**THE UNSEEN EDITORS ("Mr. Blue Eyes" / whoever is behind him)** — want a mayor they authored. Will trade: nothing; they do not negotiate, they REDECORATE. FUNCTION: the antagonist that never gets a scene. The quest's horror is that the enemy has no node tree.

**V** — wants the job done, then wants to have never taken it. FUNCTION: the messenger who must choose what the message is.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees)

NODE: APARTMENT_WALK — entry: hired
  Not a dialogue: an environment with dialogue HOOKS. The scanner finds the repainted wall, the moved couch, the wife's noted inconsistencies. Every find writes a knows: flag that changes LATER nodes.
  THE MECHANIC: investigation as conversation-ammunition, again (cf. Q7's gossip matrix, Q1's bedroom senses). By now this is FLAW-LAW-grade recurrence in reverse: a CRAFT law. Call it: THE WALK LOADS THE TALK.

NODE: JEFFERSON_BALCONY — the closer, entry: investigation done, Elizabeth's instruction in hand
  Elizabeth has asked you to tell him it was nothing. Jefferson asks you, directly, what you found.
  > "Someone is editing your mind. Here's everything." [gate: knows:full_picture] -> J_TRUTH — he spirals; he KEEPS RUNNING for mayor anyway; his last line makes it worse: he thanks you. WRITES: peralez_truth.
  > "Your wife handled it. It was corpo surveillance, nothing more." [gate: none] TRAP-OR-MERCY -> J_LIE — he relaxes. The editors keep him. He will be mayor and he will be theirs. WRITES: peralez_lied.
  > "Ask Elizabeth."   [gate: none] -> J_DEFLECT — the coward's door; the quest ACCEPTS it; Elizabeth's lie proceeds without your fingerprints. WRITES: peralez_deferred.
  NOVERB: "Let me get you out of the city." — RESCUE DOES NOT EXIST. There is no extraction, no safehouse, no fight-the-editors branch. The player's entire agency is EDITORIAL: which sentence does this man live inside. Bank it as the purest instance of the questbook's information-is-the-weapon thesis (cf. Q125's record, Q119's tape): here the weapon has no physical object at all. The evidence is just WORDS YOU CHOOSE TO SAY.
  THE UNANSWERED-BY-DESIGN LAYER: the truth option itself may be wrong — V cannot verify the full picture either. You are choosing between his wife's curated peace and YOUR curated horror. Two edits. No original.

## V2-C THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 3 (and no "solve it" branch exists at any gate, any build, any ending).
B1 — TRUTH: he knows, stays in the race, wife's protection breached, marriage now contains the thing. Later street scenes reference his deterioration.
B2 — LIE: domestic peace, authored mayor, Elizabeth's gratitude, and the player holds the only unedited copy of the night — in their head.
B3 — DEFER: Elizabeth's version stands; V exits the moral chain by choosing to be a courier.
STRUCTURAL FINDING: THE ANTAGONIST HAS NO NODES. You never speak to the editors, never fight them, never see them acknowledged. Bohemia's Amalgamation should reserve this register for its deepest layer: some NETWORK operations must be UNCONFRONTABLE — no boss, no terminal, no verb.
