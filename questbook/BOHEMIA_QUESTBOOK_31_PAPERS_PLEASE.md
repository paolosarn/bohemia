# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 31: THE IRON CAGE / COMPLICITY AS A JOB (Papers, Please)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the bureaucracy-as-gameplay engine, the your-family-survives-on-
your-quota vise, the procedural-vs-semantic (rules vs empathy) clash, the escalating authoritarian
rules, the detain mechanic, the multiple endings, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. This is the
medium's model for COMPLICITY THROUGH MUNDANE WORK + SCARCITY THAT WEAPONIZES YOUR COMPASSION AGAINST
YOU + rules-vs-conscience as the core loop. Lucas Pope (Obra Dinn, Q19). Reference only; Paolo does not
read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Papers, Please (Lucas Pope / 3909, 2013). A "dystopian document thriller." You are an immigration
inspector at the border of the totalitarian state of ARSTOTZKA, stamping passports APPROVED or DENIED —
and your PAY (which feeds/heats your family) depends on processing people correctly and fast, while
your CONSCIENCE begs you to bend the rules for the desperate.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- COMPLICITY THROUGH MUNDANE WORK: you don't fire a gun — you STAMP PAPERS. The game makes you an
  accomplice to a cruel regime one boring, procedural document-check at a time. The banality of the task
  IS the horror; evil is administered, not committed (cf. Tyranny's banality Q25, Spec Ops complicity Q27
  — but here it's PAPERWORK). "Glory to Arstotzka."
- THE IRON CAGE (Weber): the game replicates the MONOTONY of bureaucratic work to trap you in what
  sociologist Max Weber called the "iron cage" — an emotionless system that replaces human judgment with
  rote rule-following. You FEEL the cage: the timer, the quota, the rulebook, the endless queue.
- MORALITY vs RATIONALITY (the central clash): every applicant is a collision between the PROCEDURAL
  (the cold logic of border control — enforce the rules, hit your quota) and the SEMANTIC (this is a
  scared human who needs to cross). The game is built on that clash, and it has NO clean resolution.

===============================================================================
## 1. THE CORE LOOP (how the vise works)
===============================================================================
- THE JOB: inspect each applicant's documents against TODAY'S rules — passport, entry permit, work pass,
  photo, dates, city of issue, name matches. Spot a discrepancy -> INTERROGATE (point out the conflict),
  DETAIN, or DENY. Everything checks out -> APPROVE. You have a booth, a rulebook, a queue, and a CLOCK.
- THE ESCALATING RULEBOOK (complexity as pressure): rules pile up daily — first just a passport, then
  permits, then a specific stamp, then fingerprints, then no-entry from certain nations, then forced
  searches. The bureaucratic burden GROWS until keeping up is frantic; you make mistakes under load,
  and mistakes cost you.
- THE FAMILY VISE (scarcity weaponizes compassion): you're PAID PER CORRECT PROCESSING. At day's end you
  budget wages against RENT, FOOD, HEAT, and MEDICINE for your family (wife, son, uncle, mother-in-law).
  Too many errors -> citations -> docked pay -> a family member goes cold, hungry, or SICK and can DIE.
  So every act of MERCY (waving through a desperate person with bad papers) risks a citation that
  literally STARVES YOUR OWN CHILD. The game weaponizes your compassion against your family — the cruelest
  vise in the medium (cf. This War of Mine's Quiet House Q30, but the victim of your mercy is YOUR kin).
- THE DETAIN MECHANIC (complicity made physical): you can DETAIN applicants (guards drag them off) — for
  a cash BONUS later in the game. So the regime pays you to hand over the very people your conscience
  wanted to help. Cruelty is incentivized, mercy is penalized.

===============================================================================
## 2. THE HUMAN INTRUSIONS (the semantic breaking through)
===============================================================================
- THE RECURRING FACES: applicants have stories that RETURN — a man whose wife follows him the next day
  with invalid papers ("please, my husband just went through — let me follow him"); the Antegrian couple
  fleeing together where one has bad documents. To follow the rules is to SEPARATE families; to bend them
  is to risk your own. The rules become personal (cf. Majora's Anju/Kafei separation Q11, RDR pre-seeding).
- EZIC / THE RESISTANCE (subverting the cage): a shadow order (EZIC) offers you a path to SABOTAGE the
  regime from inside your booth — pass their agents, withhold a stamp, aid an assassination. The moral
  choices become opportunities to SUBVERT the iron cage, at grave personal risk (arrest, execution). You
  can be a cog OR a saboteur (cf. Alpha Protocol reactivity Q24, the villain-route-fully-built Q02).
- 20 ENDINGS: your accumulated choices (obey / bend / resist / flee / aid EZIC) branch to ~20 endings —
  imprisonment, execution, escape to a neighboring state with your family, or the OVERTHROW of Arstotzka.
  Freedom of choice is rewarded with divergent fates (cf. Alpha Protocol's 32 Q24, our Megaton law).

===============================================================================
## 3. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- DELIBERATELY MONOTONOUS: the iron-cage feeling REQUIRES tedium — document-checking is repetitive by
  design, and not everyone tolerates it ("deliberate pacing... won't be for everyone"). LESSON: making
  the player FEEL bureaucratic grind is powerful but alienating; give the monotony RHYTHM + escalating
  variety + human intrusions so it stays gripping, not just dull (cf. Pathologic Q21, TWoM Q30 balance).
- STRESS/BLEAKNESS IS RELENTLESS: "stressful, emotionally draining, deeply sad" — a scar, by design, but
  a hard sell. LESSON: our SUN-MODE/tone/difficulty instincts — let the grimness have texture + the
  occasional small warmth (a family dinner, a saved stranger) so it's endurable.
- CHOICE IS SIMPLE PER-PERSON: each decision is binary-ish (approve/deny/detain) — the DEPTH is in the
  ACCUMULATION + the vise, not any single rich choice. LESSON: moral depth can come from MANY small
  weighted choices under pressure, not one big branching tree (a different model from Witcher/BG3).

===============================================================================
## 4. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. COMPLICITY THROUGH MUNDANE WORK: you administer cruelty via BORING paperwork — the banality is the
    horror; you're an accomplice one stamp at a time (cf. Tyranny Q25, Spec Ops Q27 — the paperwork version).
W2. THE IRON CAGE (felt monotony): the game replicates bureaucratic tedium to TRAP you in a rule-following
    system that replaces judgment — you FEEL the cage (timer/quota/rulebook). Form = the theme.
W3. SCARCITY WEAPONIZES COMPASSION AGAINST YOUR OWN: mercy risks a citation that STARVES YOUR FAMILY —
    the cruelest vise; kindness to a stranger endangers your child. Survival vs conscience, made personal.
W4. THE ESCALATING RULEBOOK: complexity piles up daily until keeping up is frantic — the SYSTEM'S growth
    is the difficulty curve; you err under load (cf. Frostpunk escalating laws Q29).
W5. DETAIN = CRUELTY INCENTIVIZED: the regime PAYS you to hand over the desperate — the systems reward the
    immoral choice, so virtue has a price tag (cf. Dishonored's dark spare Q14, Whodunit bonus Q07 inverted).
W6. RECURRING HUMAN FACES: applicants' stories RETURN (the wife following her husband) — the rules become
    personal; enforcing them separates families you've met (cf. Anju/Kafei Q11, monster-is-a-victim Q01).
W7. SUBVERT THE CAGE (EZIC): you can SABOTAGE the regime from inside your booth at grave risk — cog OR
    saboteur; complicity is a choice you can refuse (cf. get-caught-on-purpose Q15, villain-route Q02).
W8. MANY SMALL WEIGHTED CHOICES > ONE BIG TREE: depth from the ACCUMULATION of tiny pressured decisions,
    not a branching dialogue tree — a different, valid moral-design model (cf. our death-math cadence).
W9. ~20 ENDINGS FROM THE DRIFT: accumulated choices branch to divergent fates (jail/execution/escape/
    revolution) — the sum of small acts is your ending (cf. Alpha Protocol Q24, Frostpunk Q29, Megaton).
W10. THEME THROUGH THE INTERFACE: the rulebook, stamp, and booth ARE the argument — you don't read about
     bureaucracy, you DO it (cf. Pathologic mindmap Q21, Obra Dinn logbook Q19 — same designer).

## 5. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
Papers, Please completes our COMPLICITY trilogy (Spec Ops Q27 = forced atrocity; This War of Mine Q30 =
survival theft; Papers, Please = bureaucratic complicity) — and it's a MECHANICAL model for making the
dynasty's ADMINISTRATION of the district a moral engine.
- W1/W2/W10 (complicity through mundane work + the iron cage + theme-through-interface): bank a Bohemia
  ADMINISTRATION loop where the dynasty PROCESSES the district's daily governance (who gets rations,
  medicine, entry, work, judgment) via a felt bureaucratic UI — and enforcing the Network's/regime's
  rules makes the dynasty COMPLICIT one decision at a time (ties Q049 verdicts, our currencies, the
  mayor-consequence system; the banality-of-evil governance from Tyranny Q25 + Frostpunk Q29).
- W3 (scarcity weaponizes compassion against YOUR OWN — the big port): the cruelest, most on-theme vise —
  make Bohemia MERCY to strangers risk the dynasty's OWN family/heirs (spare the medicine for a dying
  stranger and your own child goes without). Survival vs conscience, personal (ties our death-math,
  succession, TWoM Quiet House Q30 — but the cost lands on YOUR bloodline). Handle within wellbeing rules.
- W4 (the escalating rulebook): let the Network/regime pile ESCALATING rules on the dynasty's
  administration (new restrictions, quotas, searches) until compliance is frantic — the system's growth
  as the pressure curve (ties Frostpunk's slippery slope Q29, the Amalgamation's tightening control).
- W5 (cruelty incentivized / detain): bank beats where the regime PAYS the dynasty to do the immoral
  thing (hand over a dissenter, deny the desperate) — virtue has a price; the systems tempt cruelty (our
  Megaton + Pacifist Law tension; Dishonored dark-spare Q14).
- W6 (recurring human faces): our NPCs/applicants should RETURN with linked stories (the person you
  denied comes back; their family follows) — enforcing rules separates people you've MET (ties our
  pre-seeding, Anju Q11, the unrecorded ledger's "everyone is someone").
- W7 (subvert the cage): our anti-Network resistance path — the dynasty can SABOTAGE the regime from
  inside its own administrative role (pass a forbidden person, withhold a report) at grave risk — cog OR
  saboteur (ties Q027 double-agent, Q043 quiet line, get-caught-on-purpose Q15).
- W8/W9 (many small weighted choices -> divergent endings): validate a MORAL-CADENCE model for Bohemia —
  depth from the ACCUMULATION of many small pressured governance choices (not only big branch-quests),
  summing to divergent dynasty fates (ties our death-math, Frostpunk one-law-a-day Q29, our finales).
- FLAWS (bank): make the administrative grind have RHYTHM + escalating variety + human intrusions so it
  grips rather than bores; give the bleakness TEXTURE + small warmth (our SUN-MODE/tone/difficulty
  instincts); and remember depth can come from MANY small weighted choices, a different tool than the big
  branching quest.
