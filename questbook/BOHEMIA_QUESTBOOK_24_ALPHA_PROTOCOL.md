# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 24: THE REACTIVITY ENGINE (Alpha Protocol)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the Dialogue Stance System, the reputation web (like-you vs
respect-you), boss-altering consequences, the dossier-intel system, the 32 endings, the honest
"top-tier reactivity / weak gameplay" split, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's CULT model for
CHOICE-AND-CONSEQUENCE reactivity — a "web" not a binary, where the game remembers EVERYTHING and pays
it back, often much later. A case study cited by developers for a decade. Reference only; Paolo does not
read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Alpha Protocol (Obsidian Entertainment, 2010). A spy-RPG; you are Michael Thorton, a rookie agent
navigating Saudi Arabia, Moscow, Rome, and Taipei amid betrayals and shifting alliances. Sold poorly,
got no sequel, was delisted — but its REACTIVITY became legendary; "one of the deepest RPGs out there."

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- REACTIVITY AS A WEB, NOT A BINARY: unlike RPGs where choices collapse to Good/Evil, Alpha Protocol
  tracks a WEB of relationships and consequences. Nearly every conversation and mission choice ripples
  across factions, allies, enemies, and future missions — culminating in 32 possible endings. It's the
  reference point for "the game REMEMBERS."
- MORAL AMBIGUITY OVER MORALITY METER: outcomes hinge on PERCEIVED INTENT and REPUTATION, not absolute
  good/evil — mirroring real espionage trade-offs (short-term tactical win vs long-term alliance). No
  karma bar; just consequences.
- THE DELAYED PAYOFF: "while some choices have immediate consequences, many won't become apparent until
  much later" — the game plants seeds that bloom hours later (cf. Whispering Hillock Q04, ME2 Q06).

===============================================================================
## 1. THE DIALOGUE STANCE SYSTEM (the signature mechanic)
===============================================================================
- TIMED, INTENT-BASED: conversations run in REAL TIME; a shrinking bar forces you to pick fast. You DON'T
  see the exact words — only a STANCE/intent label. Miss the timer and the pre-highlighted option fires.
  This models the pressure of a live spy conversation — you commit to an ATTITUDE, not a script.
- THE THREE STANCES ("the three J.B.s" — modeled on Bond, Bauer, Bourne):
  - PROFESSIONAL: straight, clear, respectful (the "Bauer"-ish by-the-book). Usually safe — most NPCs
    react neutrally/positively; wrong-stance usually just costs reputation, no disaster. (The reliable default.)
  - SUAVE: cheeky, sarcastic, charming, dances around the point (the "Bond"). Charms some, infuriates others.
  - AGGRESSIVE: tough, intimidating, threatening (the "Bourne"). Cows some, alienates others.
  - THE SPECIAL 4th OPTION: sometimes a class/intel-gated choice (execute a character on the spot;
    a Tech Specialist can tell an ally how to bypass a boss's security) — unique, often drastic.
- THE POINT: you're role-playing a PERSONA under pressure, and each NPC has TASTES — there's no globally
  "correct" stance; the same line that wins one ally loses another. (Cf. the Deus Ex social battle Q17,
  but here it's a persistent RELATIONSHIP system, not a single fight.)

===============================================================================
## 2. THE REPUTATION WEB (like-you vs respect-you — the deep part)
===============================================================================
- PER-CHARACTER REPUTATION: every major NPC tracks how they FEEL about Thorton, shifted by your stances,
  choices, and mission outcomes. Crucially it's NOT a simple like/dislike — some characters RESPECT you
  MORE when you defy them:
  - Example (Konstantin Brayko, the coked-up '80s-obsessed mobster): he LOVES professional, HATES suave.
  - Example (Konstantin Marburg, the old-guard spy): he respects competence and DISDAINS flippancy; to
    unlock the option to KILL him later, you need LOW reputation with him (earned by defying/needling him)
    + specific suave perks — the game rewards a CONSISTENT antagonistic persona with a unique payoff.
- LIKE vs RESPECT: some allies who LIKE you help softly; some who RESPECT you (even while disliking you)
  give harder-edged aid. Reputation is multidimensional — you can be feared, admired, trusted, or
  resented, each with different mechanical payoffs (cf. NV's faction fame/infamy Q02, but per-PERSON).
- REPUTATION BUYS MECHANICS: being on good terms with the unhinged operative Steven Heck lets you have
  him POISON boss Brayko's cocaine — turning a hard boss fight easy. Relationships literally re-tune
  COMBAT (see below).

===============================================================================
## 3. BOSS-ALTERING CONSEQUENCES (reactivity reaching into combat)
===============================================================================
- CHOICES CHANGE BOSS FIGHTS: the standout design flourish — your relationships/intel alter the
  MECHANICS of boss encounters:
  - Brayko snorts cocaine mid-fight for invulnerability + enhanced attacks — BUT if you're allied with
    Steven Heck, you can have him POISON the coke beforehand, gutting the boss's power.
  - Intel/reputation can let an ALLY disable a boss's security, skip a mission entirely, or open a
    non-lethal capture. Conversations ripple all the way into the shooter layer.
- THE LESSON: reactivity isn't confined to dialogue/endings — the deepest version reaches into the
  MOMENT-TO-MOMENT mechanics (a boss is easier because of a conversation three missions ago).

===============================================================================
## 4. THE DOSSIER / INTEL SYSTEM (preparation as reactivity)
===============================================================================
- DOSSIERS: you collect intelligence on NPCs (files, overheard info, mission findings). Filling a
  dossier UNLOCKS new dialogue options — KNOWING more about someone lets you manipulate them better
  (a bluff you couldn't make without the file). Intel = social power (cf. the CASIE aug Q17, Disco
  "dress for the check" Q05).
- BUY-YOUR-LOADOUT PREP: missions are preceded by vendor prep (gadgets/weapons/intel) — preparation
  shapes options, echoing the ME2 "the whole game is the wind-up" prep philosophy (Q06) at mission scale.

===============================================================================
## 5. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked — the "6/10 with 10/10 reactivity" case)
===============================================================================
- REACTIVITY TOP-TIER, MOMENT-TO-MOMENT WEAK: the near-universal verdict — the choice/consequence model
  is legendary, but the shooting/stealth gameplay "isn't fun and doesn't really work." The game is
  carried ENTIRELY by its reactivity + writing. LESSON (double-edged): elite reactivity can make a
  flawed game beloved — but it can't fully rescue weak core mechanics; you need BOTH. (For us: our combat
  must stand on its own AND be reactivity-reactive.)
- REACTIVITY IS INVISIBLE / UNREWARDED ON ONE PLAYTHROUGH: much of the brilliance (e.g. maxed CQC breaks
  Brayko's unblockable combo; 32 endings) is only visible across MULTIPLE playthroughs — "you'd never
  know unless you played it that way." LESSON: deep reactivity risks going UNSEEN; surface it (let the
  game acknowledge what you did) so players FEEL the consequence even once (cf. the ME2 roll-call Q06).
- SHORT GAME, GROWS ON REPLAY: rated ~7/10 first run, "+1 per playthrough" — built for replay, a hard
  sell for one-and-done players (cf. Pathologic Q21, Elden Ring Q23 missable content).

===============================================================================
## 6. WHY IT WORKS (craft, itemized for stealing)
===============================================================================
W1. REACTIVITY AS A WEB, NOT A BINARY: track relationships + consequences across factions/people, not a
    good/evil bar — 32 endings from a web of small choices. The game REMEMBERS everything.
W2. INTENT OVER SCRIPT (timed stances): pick an ATTITUDE under a clock, not exact words — models
    real-conversation pressure + commits you to a persona (cf. Deus Ex social battle Q17, Mass Effect wheel).
W3. NO UNIVERSALLY-CORRECT CHOICE: the stance that wins one NPC loses another — reputation is per-person
    and taste-based; you can't please everyone (cf. NV factions Q02, but per-individual).
W4. LIKE vs RESPECT (multidimensional reputation): NPCs can respect you MORE for defying them — fear/
    admiration/trust/resentment are distinct axes with distinct payoffs. Antagonism can be a valid,
    rewarded persona.
W5. REPUTATION BUYS MECHANICS (incl. combat): relationships re-tune the game — an ally poisons a boss's
    drugs; intel skips a mission. Consequence reaches into moment-to-moment MECHANICS, not just story.
W6. BOSS-ALTERING CONSEQUENCE: the deepest reactivity changes a BOSS FIGHT based on a past conversation —
    the narrative and mechanical layers fuse (a conversation three missions ago makes this fight easier).
W7. INTEL AS SOCIAL POWER (dossiers): collecting info on an NPC UNLOCKS dialogue/leverage — knowing them
    is manipulating them (cf. CASIE Q17, Lady Boyle deduction Q14).
W8. DELAYED, INVISIBLE-SEED CONSEQUENCE: many choices only pay off hours later — the world has a long
    memory (cf. Whispering Hillock Q04, ME2 Q06, BG3 Q16).
W9. PREP SHAPES OPTIONS: vendor/intel prep before missions determines what's possible — preparation as a
    reactivity input (cf. ME2 wind-up Q06).
W10. SURFACE THE CONSEQUENCE (the FLAW-fix): deep reactivity risks being unseen — ACKNOWLEDGE what the
    player did (an NPC references it, an ending tallies it) so it lands even on one playthrough.

===============================================================================
## 7. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built)
===============================================================================
Our unrecorded ledger + standing + fold are a reactivity engine; Alpha Protocol is the master cult text
on making it a WEB with per-person, multidimensional, mechanics-reaching consequence.
- W1/W8 (web not binary + delayed seeds): our ledger/fold already track a web; bank Alpha Protocol's
  depth target — per-FACTION and per-PERSON consequence, much of it paying off a generation later (our
  Q040->042). Not good/evil; a web of remembered choices.
- W2 (intent over script, timed): consider a Bohemia timed INTENT choice for high-pressure beats (a
  standoff, a negotiation, a betrayal) — pick a STANCE under a clock, commit to a persona (pairs with
  the Deus Ex social-battle mechanic Q17; use sparingly for tension).
- W3/W4 (no universal-correct + like vs respect): make Bohemia standing MULTIDIMENSIONAL — a faction/
  NPC can FEAR, RESPECT, TRUST, or RESENT the dynasty, each with different aid, and the persona that wins
  the Reds may lose the Church. Antagonism can be a VALID rewarded path (defy the Cartel -> they respect
  strength -> a harder-edged alliance). (Ties our faction web + Megaton law; big upgrade to standing.)
- W5/W6 (reputation buys mechanics + boss-altering): THE standout port — let Bohemia relationships/intel
  re-tune COMBAT + city-systems: an ally poisons/sabotages an enemy before a fight; a faction favor cuts
  the power to a Network stronghold mid-mission; a bond opens a non-lethal capture. Reactivity reaching
  into the Dead-Eye layer + the city-builder (ties DOS2 environment-as-toolset Q15, our combat).
- W7 (intel as social power / dossiers): our [READ] + the ledger ARE dossiers — collecting intel on an
  NPC/faction should UNLOCK dialogue/leverage (a bluff you can't make without the file) — knowing is
  power (ties Obra Dinn deduction Q19, the Amalgamation's data).
- W9 (prep shapes options): our city-builder economy IS the prep layer — what you built/stockpiled/allied
  before a crisis shapes your options in it (ties ME2 wind-up Q06, our Reconstruction).
- W10 + FLAWS (surface the consequence + BOTH layers must work): CRUCIAL cautions — (a) our combat +
  city-builder must be FUN on their own (Alpha Protocol's reactivity couldn't rescue weak gameplay; our
  Dead-Eye dial + build loop must stand alone); (b) SURFACE reactivity so it's felt on ONE playthrough
  (NPCs reference the dynasty's deeds; the fold/roll-call tallies them — cf. ME2 Q06) — deep-but-invisible
  reactivity is wasted. Make the world SAY what it remembers.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**MICHAEL THORTON** — wants, nominally, the mission; actually wants to find out which persona is really his. Will trade: stances, under a shrinking timer, forever. FUNCTION: the persona engine. The game never asks what Thorton says; it asks what KIND of man says it, and bills accordingly.

**KONSTANTIN BRAYKO** — wants the '80s back, chemically if necessary. A cocaine mobster in a velvet suit who LOVES professional address and HATES flippancy. Will trade: nothing at the table; everything through the people around him. FUNCTION: the boss whose fight is negotiable three missions early: stay right with Steven Heck and the coke gets poisoned before the first bullet (W5, W6).

**MARBURG** — wants a worthy opponent, and holds flippancy in contempt. Will never say out loud: that your consistent defiance is the only conversation he respects. FUNCTION: the like-vs-respect axis made a man (W4): to earn the option to kill him later you need him to RESPECT and DISLIKE you, cultivated deliberately across the whole game. Antagonism as a rewarded persona.

**STEVEN HECK** — wants chaos, appreciation, and errands that would horrify an oversight committee. FUNCTION: the ally whose friendship is a combat modifier. The most unhinged man in the cast is the most reliable mechanical payoff in the web.

**THE DOSSIERS** — want filling. Every file on every player in the web. FUNCTION: intel as social power (W7): a complete dossier is a dialogue option that didn't exist; knowing a person is leverage on them.

**THE WEB ITSELF** — wants nothing forgotten. 32 endings' worth of remembered stances, spared enemies, defied handlers, poisoned coke. FUNCTION: the antagonist and the reward, the same system: the game that remembers everything, including what you'd forgotten you did.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees; lines paraphrased, structure exact; NOTE THE FORM: options here are STANCES, not scripts — the timer forces commitment to an attitude, and the label is all you get)

NODE: ANY_CONVERSATION — the system's atom, entry: everywhere, always timed
  A shrinking bar. Three stances and sometimes a fourth.
  > [PROFESSIONAL]                  [gate: none] -> the reliable default; most NPCs neutral-to-warm; wrong reads cost reputation, rarely disaster
  > [SUAVE]                         [gate: none] -> charms Bond-lovers, infuriates Marburgs; the highest-variance stance
  > [AGGRESSIVE]                    [gate: none] -> cows some, alienates others; fear is a currency some people bank
  > [SPECIAL: execute / exploit intel] [gate: class, dossier, or reputation] -> the drastic fourth option the build and the web unlock
  > (let the timer expire)          [gate: none] SILENCE -> the highlighted stance fires anyway; in a live conversation, not-choosing IS a choice, mechanized
  THE FORM IS THE FINDING: you commit to INTENT and hear the words after. Regret is native to the system, exactly like talking.

NODE: BRAYKO_COURT — Moscow, entry: the mobster's orbit
  > [PROFESSIONAL]                  [gate: none] -> he warms; the velvet suit approves of straight dealing
  > [SUAVE]                         [gate: none] TRAP -> flippancy at HIS table; reputation bleeds; the fight to come gets its full cocaine
  DOSSIER LINE: know his '80s obsession [gate: dossier filled] -> the flattery only research affords (W7)
  UPSTREAM, THREE MISSIONS EARLY: NODE: HECK_ERRANDS — Taipei, entry: Heck's madness accepted at face value
  > (do his errands, no questions)  [gate: stomach] -> Heck's loyalty -> LATER, UNANNOUNCED: the coke is poisoned; Brayko's invulnerability phase gutted (W5, W6)
  > (judge him)                     [gate: none] -> the boss fight ships at full strength, and no screen ever tells you what you paid
  THE DEEPEST REACTIVITY IN THE FILE IS INVISIBLE, which is both its glory and its banked flaw (W10): surface the consequence or it lands only on the wiki.

NODE: MARBURG_LONG_GAME — Rome, entry: every meeting across the campaign
  > (defy him, consistently, with competence) [gate: persona discipline] -> LOW like, HIGH respect: the axes separate (W4)
  > (flatter him)                   [gate: none] TRAP -> he files you under flippant; the unique payoff dies quietly
  PAYOFF, ENDGAME: [execute Marburg] [gate: low reputation + specific suave perks, cultivated since Act 1] -> the kill option exists ONLY for the player who committed to antagonism as a persona for the whole game
  THE CRAFT: the game rewards a CONSISTENT self, not a correct one. There is no right stance; there is only who you decided to be, kept up under timers, for thirty hours.

NODE: THE_HANDLER_CALLS — between missions, entry: the web adjusting
  Allies reference your stances from missions ago; enemies quote your own words back with interest.
  WRITES: per-person reputation, multidimensional (feared / trusted / respected / resented), each axis buying different aid (W3, W4)
  NOVERB: "Reset. Let me try that again." No take-backs exist anywhere in the system. The timer took your answer and the web filed it. The removed verb is the save-scum, removed at the design layer.

NODE: MISSION_PREP — the vendor screen, entry: before every mission
  > (buy intel)                     [gate: money] -> guard patterns known, dialogue options unlocked, sometimes a mission SKIPPED outright
  > (buy gear instead)              [gate: money] -> the loud answer, funded
  > (go in cold)                    [gate: none] -> the mission at full price
  PREP IS A REACTIVITY INPUT (W9): what you spent before the door opens decides which doors exist.

## V2-C THE BRANCH MAP

COUNT: 32 endings, assembled combinatorially from a per-person web (the count is the marketing number; the finding is that the endings are a TALLY of the web's state, not a fork at the end).

THE WEB'S AXES: per-NPC multidimensional reputation (like x respect x fear), faction postures, who lived, who was defied, which dossiers filled, which seeds (a poisoned stash, a spared rival) planted hours before their bloom (W8).
THE TERMINAL SHAPE: every ending is the web read out loud: who stands with Thorton, who hunts him, which agency exists. The FINALE-IS-A-LEDGER-READ law, eighth confirmation, in its purest combinatorial form.
THE BANKED SPLIT: 10/10 reactivity carrying 6/10 moment-to-moment gameplay. Beloved, delisted, sequel-less. The web could not rescue the shooting.

THE STRUCTURAL FINDING FOR THE COMPILE: Alpha Protocol is the master upgrade path for Bohemia's standing system, in rank order: (1) make standing MULTIDIMENSIONAL per faction and per person: fear, trust, respect, resentment as separate axes with separate aid, so antagonism becomes a valid, rewarded dynasty persona (the Marburg mechanic); (2) let relationships REACH INTO MECHANICS: an ally sabotages the stronghold before the fight, a favor cuts the power mid-mission — reactivity that lands in the Dead-Eye layer and the city-builder, not just in dialogue (the Heck mechanic); (3) [READ] + the ledger ARE dossiers: filled files unlock leverage lines that don't otherwise exist; (4) the timed STANCE choice is banked for high-pressure beats only, paired with #17's social battle. And the two cautions are compile gates: SURFACE THE CONSEQUENCE (the world says what it remembers: NPCs cite the dynasty's deeds; the fold tallies them — deep-but-invisible reactivity is wasted work) and BOTH LAYERS MUST STAND ALONE (the reactivity web cannot rescue weak combat; Alpha Protocol is the tombstone that proves it).
