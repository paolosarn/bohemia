# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #155 — "NATURE OF THE BEAST (THE CURSE IS A WOUND, NOT A MONSTER)"
**Game:** Dragon Age: Origins (2009)
**Studio:** BioWare
**Quest:** Nature of the Beast — the Dalish elves' quest. A werewolf plague terrorizes the forest; the hunt for a cure becomes the discovery that the "monsters" are victims of an old atrocity, and the curse can only end if someone forgives, or someone dies.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Dragon Age catalogue; corpus anchor Q42 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia's whole thesis is that the monster is usually somebody, and the wound outlives the crime — and this is the genre's cleanest quest about a curse that is a grievance in animal form. The werewolves are victims; the elves are wronged; the ancient wound was mutual; and the resolution forces the player to broker a peace, take a side, or wipe out one party — with no cost-free option. It is the Bloody Baron's botchling (#1) scaled to two peoples, and the "let go of the old wound or feed it forever" choice at the heart of our Liberate ending.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A monster hunt becomes a truth commission: the werewolves are cursed humans, the curse was a wronged spirit's vengeance for an elven atrocity, and the plague only ends when someone chooses to forgive a centuries-old wound instead of avenging it again.**

The Dalish elves are dying of a werewolf curse; their keeper sends the player into the forest to kill the beasts' source and lift it. But the forest inverts the hunt: the werewolves can TALK, they are led by a Lady of the Forest who wants peace, and the story underneath is old. Centuries ago, humans wronged the elves; the elves' response summoned Witherfang and the curse; the curse spread to humans and made the werewolves; and the elven keeper, Zathrian, has kept the vengeance alive for centuries, unable to forgive, feeding a wound long after everyone who committed the original crime is dust. The werewolves are not the disease. They are the symptom of a grief no one would put down.

The machine is that every party is both victim and perpetrator, and the resolution is a choice about the WOUND, not the monsters. The player can broker peace — if they can make Zathrian face that his vengeance has outlived its justice and forgive, ending the curse for everyone. Or they can side with the elves and slaughter the werewolves (curing the elves, murdering the victims). Or side with the werewolves and force Zathrian's death (freeing the cursed, dooming the elves he protected). The best outcome requires the hardest thing in the game: convincing the wronged party to stop being wronged, to let a centuries-old atrocity finally be over.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** a grievance you refuse to release becomes a curse you pass to people who never wronged you, and the only cure is the forgiveness the wounded least want to give.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### ZATHRIAN — the keeper who won't forgive
- **What he wants (stated):** the werewolves destroyed and his people cured.
- **What he wants (real):** his ancient vengeance vindicated — he summoned the curse for a real atrocity and has fed it for centuries, unable to let the wound close.
- **What he'll trade:** the cure, but only on his terms — until the player makes him face the truth.
- **What he will never say out loud:** that he is the source; that the "plague" is his grief; that forgiving would cost him the identity of the wronged.
- **Function:** THE WOUND, personified: the victim who became the perpetrator by refusing to heal.

### THE LADY OF THE FOREST — the curse that wants to end
- **What she wants:** peace, and release for the werewolves who are cursed humans, not born monsters.
- **What she'll trade:** the truth, and a path to the cure that doesn't run through slaughter.
- **What she will never say out loud:** that she is bound to Zathrian's grief and cannot end without him facing it.
- **Function:** THE APPEAL FOR PEACE: the monster's leader arguing, reasonably, for mercy.

### THE WEREWOLVES — the victims wearing the crime
- **What they want:** to stop being monsters — to be the humans the curse took.
- **What they'll trade:** their hostility, if peace is real.
- **Function:** THE COST made ambulatory: the "monsters" the elves demand dead are the wounded of an old sin.

### THE DALISH ELVES — the dying wronged
- **What they want:** to survive the curse killing them.
- **Function:** THE OTHER VICTIM: the peace's other half, dying while the player negotiates.

### THE OLD ATROCITY — the crime under everything
- The human wrong that started it, and the elven vengeance that answered — both centuries dead, both still killing.
- **Function:** THE ROOT: a mutual wound no living person committed, still demanding blood.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE HUNT ASSIGNED [MANDATORY]
Zathrian sends the player to kill Witherfang, the werewolves' source, and bring back its heart to lift the curse. The quest opens as a straightforward monster hunt.

### STAGE 2 — THE FOREST INVERTS [MANDATORY]
The werewolves talk; the Lady of the Forest appears; the "beasts" want peace, not blood. The hunt's premise cracks.

### STAGE 3 — THE TRUTH [MANDATORY]
The old story completes: the human atrocity, the elven vengeance, Zathrian's centuries of fed grief, the werewolves as cursed humans. The monster hunt becomes a truth commission.

### STAGE 4 — THE CONFRONTATION [MANDATORY, forks]
Bring the truth to Zathrian. The player can attempt to make him FORGIVE — a hard persuasion, the game's best outcome — or take a side and force the ending in blood.

### STAGE 5 — THE RESOLUTION [MANDATORY]
Peace (Zathrian forgives, dies willingly, curse lifts for all); or slaughter the werewolves (elves cured, victims murdered); or side with the werewolves (Zathrian killed, elves doomed). No cost-free path.

### NODE B-1 — the Lady of the Forest. Entry: stage 2.
The curse's leader, arguing for mercy with unsettling reason.

### NODE B-2 — Zathrian confronted. Entry: stage 4. The wound, cornered by its own truth.
He defends the vengeance; the player must make him face that it has outlived its justice.
> "Everyone who wronged you is dead. This has to end." [gate: PERSUASION / the truth assembled] -> the hard path: he FORGIVES, and the game's best ending opens
> "The curse is you. Admit it." [gate: none] -> he cannot, at first; his identity is the wound; pushing is the quest's real boss fight, fought in words
> "Then we do this your way." [gate: none] -> side with the elves; the werewolves die
> NOVERB "Let's just cure the elves and leave the werewolves alone" — no line exists; the curse is one thing, the elves and werewolves bound to the same wound. The removed verb is the quest's cruelty: you cannot spare both without Zathrian's forgiveness, and forgiveness is the one thing he won't sell.

### NODE B-3 — the resolution. Entry: stage 5.
The wound closes, or is fed again.
> (Zathrian forgives, dies willingly) [gate: persuasion won] -> the curse lifts for ALL; elves and werewolves both live; the grief finally put down
> (slaughter the werewolves) [gate: none] -> elves cured, victims murdered; the wound answered with more wound
> (kill Zathrian, side with werewolves) [gate: none] -> the cursed freed, the elves he protected doomed; the vengeance turned around
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the peace costs Zathrian his life and his grievance; the blood options cost a whole people; there is no exit where nobody pays.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 3 terminal outcomes — peace (both live), elves-win (werewolves die), werewolves-win (elves die) — gated by whether the player can make the wound forgive.**

- **PEACE:** Zathrian faces the truth, forgives, dies willingly; the curse lifts for elves AND werewolves; the centuries-old grief is finally released. The best ending, gated behind the hardest persuasion. WRITES: both peoples live; the wound closes.
- **SIDE WITH THE ELVES:** the werewolves are slaughtered; the elves are cured; the victims of the old sin are murdered to answer it. WRITES: elves survive, the cursed dead.
- **SIDE WITH THE WEREWOLVES:** Zathrian is killed, breaking the curse for the cursed but dooming the elves he sustained. WRITES: the werewolves freed, the Dalish clan devastated.
- **THE THROUGH-LINE:** every ending is about the WOUND, not the monsters — the quest's branches are three answers to "do you feed an old grievance or release it," and only one releases it.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE BEST ENDING IS A SKILL-CHECK, NOT AN EARNED TRUTH.** Peace is gated behind a persuasion stat; a player who understands the situation perfectly but lacks the check is forced into blood. The forgiveness that is the quest's moral heart is bought with a dialogue skill, not with the player's comprehension.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the hardest, best outcome must be gated on ASSEMBLED UNDERSTANDING (evidence, the truth reconstructed, the wound's history known), not a raw stat — so that a player who does the moral work can always reach it, and the check tests knowing, not build. (#38/#17's lesson: earn the argument with the case.)

**F2 — ZATHRIAN'S CENTURIES ARE TOLD, NOT FELT.** The keeper's grief is exposited in a few lines; the player never witnessed the atrocity, the vengeance, or the long feeding of the wound, so his refusal to forgive reads as stubbornness rather than a tragedy the player understands from inside.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a wound the player is asked to help release must be SHOWN across the arc — the original crime witnessed, the grief's long persistence seen (the fold is built for this: a grievance the player watched a prior generation refuse to put down). Told grief cannot carry a forgiveness this heavy.

**F3 — THE WEREWOLVES REVERT TO STAKES AT THE FORK.** After the humanizing reveal, the werewolves become mostly the "other lever" in the ending; their individual personhood (glimpsed in the reveal) doesn't carry into the resolution, so slaughtering them costs less than the setup earned.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a humanized "monster" faction must keep its individual faces through the RESOLUTION — named victims present at the choice, so that wiping them out has the weight the reveal promised. Humanize through the ending, not just the twist.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE MONSTER HUNT IS A TRUTH COMMISSION. The quest opens as a straight beast-kill and becomes the excavation of an old mutual atrocity — the genre's cleanest bait-and-switch from violence to history.
W2. THE CURSE IS A GRIEVANCE IN ANIMAL FORM. The werewolves are a wound wearing fur; the plague is grief that wouldn't die. The monster is literally somebody's refusal to forgive.
W3. EVERY PARTY IS VICTIM AND PERPETRATOR. Humans wronged elves, elves cursed humans, the cursed kill the innocent — no one is clean, and the quest names the whole chain.
W4. THE VILLAIN IS THE WRONGED PARTY. Zathrian is the victim who became the perpetrator by refusing to heal — the hardest, truest kind of antagonist, sympathetic and culpable at once.
W5. THE RESOLUTION IS ABOUT THE WOUND, NOT THE MONSTERS. The three endings are three answers to "feed the grievance or release it" — the monsters are the symptom, the grief is the disease.
W6. THE BEST ENDING IS FORGIVENESS. Peace requires convincing the wronged to stop being wronged — the hardest human act, gated as the game's hardest outcome. Mercy is earned, not given.
W7. NO COST-FREE PATH. Peace costs Zathrian his life; blood costs a whole people. The quest refuses an exit where nobody pays, which is what makes the choice real.
W8. THE MONSTERS ARGUE REASONABLY. The Lady of the Forest makes the case for mercy with unsettling calm — the "beast" is the most morally lucid voice in the forest.
W9. THE CRIME IS CENTURIES DEAD AND STILL KILLING. No living person committed the original wrong, yet it still demands blood — the clearest depiction of how inherited grievance outlives its cause.
W10. THE FORGIVENESS IS A DEATH. Zathrian can only release the wound by dying with it — the peace is not free, it is paid by the one who held the grief. Letting go is the last thing he does.

### PORT 1 — THE CURSE THAT IS A GRIEVANCE [W1, W2, W5 — the core port]

### PORT 2 — FORGIVENESS GATED ON UNDERSTANDING, NOT A STAT [F1's law]
**System:** [READ] / the fold / Liberate adjacency
The best ending — releasing the wound — is gated on the dynast having RECONSTRUCTED the grievance's full history (the crime, the vengeance, the long feeding), not on a persuasion number. A player who does the moral work can always reach the peace; the check tests knowing. This is Liberate's mechanic: you can only make someone let go if you understand what they're holding.

### PORT 3 — THE WOUND SHOWN ACROSS GENERATIONS [F2's law]
**System:** the fold / the ~100-year arc
The grievance the dynast is asked to help release was WITNESSED — a prior generation's atrocity and its refusal to forgive, seen in play, so a later dynast's forgiveness carries the weight of a history they lived. The fold does what Nature of the Beast could only exposit: it lets the player see the centuries.

### PORT 4 — THE HUMANIZED FACTION KEEPS ITS FACES [F3's law]
**System:** entities / faction web
An Amalgamation-plague or cursed faction that the arc humanizes keeps NAMED individuals present through the resolution, so that choosing to wipe them out costs the weight the reveal promised. Humanize through the ending, not just the twist — the fold remembers each face erased.
