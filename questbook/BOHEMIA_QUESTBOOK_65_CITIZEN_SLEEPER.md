# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 65: DICE, CLOCKS & A DECAYING BODY / SURVIVAL AS THE THEME (Citizen Sleeper)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the dice-clocks-drives system, the decaying-body-shrinks-your-dice-
pool survival math, one-unified-mechanic-for-everything, precarity-as-theme, clocks tracking the world
independently, interconnected-stories-no-single-plot, the you-are-property-fighting-to-exist premise, the
back-half-loses-tension flaw, and Bohemia ports. This is the medium's cleanest model for TABLETOP DICE +
CLOCKS AS A SURVIVAL-RPG ENGINE where the MECHANIC IS THE THEME (precarity). DIRECTLY relevant to our
survival-accounting, our dice-driven combat dial, and our sentient-being-fighting-to-exist premise. Jump
Over the Age (Gareth Damian Martin). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Citizen Sleeper (Jump Over the Age / Fellow Traveller, 2022). A tabletop-inspired narrative survival
RPG. You're a SLEEPER — a digitized human consciousness in a corporation-owned artificial body — escaped
onto Erlin's Eye, a ruined space station, hunted by your makers, your body DECAYING. Each "cycle" (day)
you roll dice + spend them to survive + build a life. 93% positive, Game-Awards-nominated; a landmark of
tabletop-to-videogame design + thematic survival. On mobile/Switch (fits our platform).

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- ONE UNIFIED DICE-CLOCKS-DRIVES SYSTEM RUNS THE WHOLE GAME (elegance — the defining craft): a SINGLE
  abstracted system (from tabletop, esp. Blades in the Dark) resolves EVERYTHING — fighting a gangster,
  fixing a ship, babysitting a child, hacking the station. Each cycle you're dealt DICE (rolled at the
  start); you ASSIGN them to actions (higher die = better odds); CLOCKS track multi-step progress (yours +
  the world's); DRIVES are your goals. Designer's point: "a single system for interacting with the world
  that could describe anything meant I could tell a vast variety of stories." One elegant abstraction >
  many bespoke systems (cf. Slay the Spire interlock Q52, Into the Breach Q60, our combat-dial/one-package
  law; abstraction as power).
- THE MECHANIC IS THE THEME (precarity made playable — the masterstroke): the systems ARE the message.
  The decaying body SHRINKS your dice pool as health falls (fewer dice = fewer actions = a death spiral);
  you "never have enough" money/food/medicine; luck (the roll) can "make or break someone living on the
  margins." It's built on "systems that keep people in closed loops — company towns, Uber drivers renting
  their cars — downward spirals of debt." The core loop MECHANICALLY ENACTS precarity, gig-work, chronic-
  illness rhythms — you FEEL the theme by playing it (cf. Papers Please iron-cage Q31, This War of Mine
  Q30, Disco Q05; theme through the core mechanic — the purest survival example).
- YOU ARE PROPERTY, FIGHTING TO EXIST (the on-theme premise): you're a digitized consciousness in a corp-
  OWNED body — "just property, one more asset in a portfolio," your personhood literally DEBATED by
  corporate opponents. The game explores "precarity, personhood, and freedom" — a sentient being fighting
  to be seen as a person + to survive (STARTLINGLY on-theme for our Amalgamation — digitized consciousness,
  personhood, ownership of the self; cf. SOMA Q34, our Amalgamation/uploaded-dead).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how tabletop dice become a survival RPG)
===============================================================================

### THE DICE POOL + THE DAILY ALLOCATION PUZZLE (the core tension — key craft)
- Each cycle you get up to 5-6 PRE-ROLLED dice (rolled at start, spend as you like — an unusual, brilliant
  twist). A high die guarantees a good outcome; a low die risks a bad one. The tension: "do I use my six
  on the objective I NEED, or on the job that nets money for medicine? Do I risk my one die on something
  that could greatly reward me?" A tight daily RESOURCE-ALLOCATION puzzle under scarcity — every die is a
  hard choice (cf. Into the Breach fixed-turns Q60, our combat-dial/resource tension; the daily allocation).
### CONDITION + ENERGY (the interlocking survival meters)
- CONDITION (your body's integrity — decays each cycle, drops your dice count) + ENERGY (food — if it runs
  low it ACCELERATES condition decay). You spend earnings on medicine (condition) + food (energy) + dodging
  bounty hunters — a precarious juggle where neglect COMPOUNDS (a death spiral). Interlocking meters that
  feed each other (cf. our survival-accounting/currencies, This War of Mine Q30, Frostpunk Q29; compounding
  scarcity).
### CLOCKS TRACK BOTH YOU AND THE WORLD (the living-world device — steal this)
- CLOCKS (from Blades in the Dark) are multi-step progress trackers that "create dread, mystery +
  anticipation as they tick" — some are YOUR projects (become a local at the bar; complete a job), some are
  the WORLD's acting on you (a bounty hunter closing in; a faction's plan advancing) whether you engage or
  not. Clocks make the world feel ALIVE + time PRESSURED — a legible, tense representation of parallel
  action (cf. Majora's clock Q11, our scheduler/time-model; clocks as a living-world + tension device).
### "SUCCESS AT A COST" + EMERGENT NARRATIVE (dice drive story, not just combat)
- Unlike D&D dice (resolve combat/skill checks), Citizen Sleeper folds dice into the NARRATIVE thread —
  a low roll can yield "success at a cost" (knowledge but a bad side effect), producing twists +
  complications that generate emergent, personalized story. The dice AUTHOR narrative, not just outcomes
  (cf. our dice combat + narrative potential, Disco checks Q05; dice as a story engine).
### INTERCONNECTED STORIES, NO SINGLE PLOT + UNUSUAL ENDINGS (player-authored shape)
- No single storyline — you STUMBLE onto story ROUTES by exploring + choosing who to help; failing one
  story shows consequences in another; succeeding opens new ones. Endings are unusual: you may not realize
  you're on an ending path; some END the game, others let you CONTINUE; a valid "ending" is simply reaching
  a place where your character is at PEACE. The narrative shape is player-authored + interconnected (cf.
  Kenshi player-authored Q55, our fold + interconnected quests; no railroad).
### INTIMATE, PERSONAL SCALE + CHARACTER (survival that makes you CARE)
- Tighter + more PERSONAL than Disco (Q05) — "less world-changing epic, more intimate survival story."
  Every station resident (salvagers, hackers, bartenders) has a history + is engaging; small illustrative
  portraits + a Tycho-esque score build affection. You care about the people you're surviving alongside
  (cf. Hades personal-stakes Q47, our human-scale/companions; intimacy over epic).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE BACK HALF LOSES ITS TENSION (the central critique): by midgame you accumulate resources/perks/
  mobility until you can "essentially ignore resource management" — hit max wallet, forget upkeep, no dice
  sinks; hacking becomes consequence-free. The SURVIVAL TENSION that IS the game evaporates, and the
  endings feel the loss. LESSON (crucial for us): a survival game whose THEME is scarcity must SUSTAIN the
  scarcity — don't let the economy trivialize itself late; keep meaningful pressure to the end (our
  survival-accounting + difficulty must not collapse into trivial abundance — a direct tuning warning).
- OVERWHELMING FIRST HOUR: "a barrage of new mechanics + systems" up front overwhelms; several reviewers
  nearly bounced before it clicked. LESSON (recurring #1): even an elegant system needs GENTLE onboarding —
  introduce dice/clocks/meters gradually (our SUN-MODE/onboarding, Celeste Q59; don't front-load all systems).
- "PERFECTLY BALANCED" IS A KNIFE-EDGE / BALANCE IS A MYTH: the designer himself says "balance is a bit of
  a myth — you can only balance for one group, excluding another"; he chose a "broad palette" over tight
  balance. The precarity thrills some + feels "unfair + painful" to others. LESSON: survival tension is
  audience-dependent; offer a PALETTE (difficulty options) so scarcity is thrilling-not-punishing per player
  (our difficulty packages, Celeste Assist Q59, Qud modes Q56).
- RNG CAN STILL STING: a run of bad rolls can force a real survival struggle through luck, not misplay —
  the flip side of "luck makes or breaks you." LESSON: luck-as-theme is powerful but must stay FAIR enough
  that losses feel meaningful, not arbitrary (mitigate with the pre-roll-and-allocate agency — our odds-
  must-feel-fair, Into the Breach Q60).
- LIGHT ON MECHANICAL DEPTH / "VISUAL NOVEL WITH DICE": some want more systemic crunch — it's narrative-
  first, mechanically spare. LESSON: elegant-minimal suits the theme but leaves system-hungry players
  wanting; pair our elegance with enough depth for our hardcore audience (our combat dial + city-builder
  depth).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the dice+clocks survival engine where mechanic = theme
===============================================================================
Citizen Sleeper is the cleanest model for a DICE + CLOCKS SURVIVAL-RPG engine where the MECHANIC IS THE
THEME — directly useful for our survival-accounting, our DICE-DRIVEN combat dial, and (startlingly) our
Amalgamation's digitized-personhood premise. It's on mobile (platform precedent) + its precarity-as-play +
intimate scale + tabletop roots fit us. Its back-half-loses-tension flaw is a direct survival-tuning warning.
- W1/W4 (one unified dice system + daily allocation puzzle — our combat dial + resource loops): bank the
  elegance — a SINGLE abstracted system (our DICE-DRIVEN combat dial / one-package law) can resolve varied
  actions, and a daily/turn RESOURCE-ALLOCATION puzzle under scarcity (spend your best "die"/resource on
  what you NEED vs what pays) creates constant hard choices. Our combat dial is already dice/timing-driven —
  Citizen Sleeper validates extending that elegant abstraction across survival actions (ties our combat
  dial + one-package law, Slay the Spire Q52, Into the Breach Q60, our survival-accounting).
- W2/W5 (the mechanic IS the theme + interlocking survival meters — our survival-accounting): bank the
  masterstroke — Bohemia's SURVIVAL mechanics should MECHANICALLY ENACT its theme (the collapse's precarity,
  the struggle to keep people alive) via INTERLOCKING meters (our medicine/electricity/resources +
  survival-accounting) where neglect COMPOUNDS into a death spiral — the player FEELS the collapse by
  playing it (ties Papers Please Q31, This War of Mine Q30, Frostpunk Q29, our survival-accounting +
  currencies + death-math; theme-through-the-core-mechanic, our strongest survival lever).
- W3 (you are property, fighting to exist — the AMALGAMATION resonance): STARTLINGLY on-theme — Citizen
  Sleeper's digitized-consciousness-in-an-owned-body, personhood-debated premise is a cousin of our
  AMALGAMATION (uploaded dead, personhood, ownership of the self). Bank it as a reference for exploring
  precarity/personhood/freedom + whether an uploaded consciousness is a PERSON — deeply on-theme (ties SOMA
  Q34, DDLC self-aware-AI Q57, our Amalgamation/uploaded-dead + recorded-vs-unrecorded; a thematic sibling).
- W6 (clocks track you AND the world — steal the device): STEAL CLOCKS for Bohemia — multi-step trackers
  for the dynasty's projects AND the world's actions (a faction's plan, the Amalgamation's advance, a
  raid) that tick whether the player engages or not — creating dread + a living, time-pressured world.
  Fits our SCHEDULER + time-model + living-world (Gothic/Kenshi Q51/Q55) perfectly (ties Majora clock Q11,
  our scheduler/loop + faction web; a direct, high-value mechanical steal).
- W7 (success at a cost / dice as a story engine): our DICE-DRIVEN combat + actions should fold into
  NARRATIVE — a partial/low roll yields "success at a cost" (you win but something breaks), generating
  twists + emergent story, not just pass/fail (ties our combat dial + Megaton, Disco checks Q05; dice
  author story).
- W8/W9 (interconnected stories + intimate personal scale): our FOLD + quest layer should be interconnected
  (failing one thread shows in another; stumbling onto routes) with valid low-key "at peace" resolutions
  alongside the epic finales; and keep the scale INTIMATE + personal (care about the district's people)
  under the Amalgamation epic (ties Kenshi Q55, Hades personal-stakes Q47, Golden Idol interconnected-cases
  Q54, our fold + companions + Liberate/Respect/Become; intimacy over epic).
- W10 (tabletop as a well + presentation + mobile): bank tabletop (Blades/PbtA — dice/clocks/drives) as a
  rich design well for us, carried by our ART + MUSIC strengths, on MOBILE (Citizen Sleeper's platform
  precedent) (ties our aesthetic/music pillars + single-file/mobile + solo scale).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): SUSTAIN the scarcity/tension to the END — don't let our survival economy trivialize
  itself late (the back-half-loses-tension flaw is a DIRECT warning for our survival-accounting/difficulty
  — keep meaningful pressure, avoid trivial abundance + max-wallet); GENTLE onboarding for dice/clocks/
  meters (don't front-load all systems — SUN-MODE, Celeste Q59); offer a DIFFICULTY PALETTE so precarity
  is thrilling-not-punishing per player (balance-is-a-myth — our difficulty packages, Qud modes Q56,
  Celeste Assist Q59); keep luck FAIR (pre-roll-and-allocate agency so losses feel meaningful — Into the
  Breach Q60, our odds-must-feel-fair); and pair our elegance with enough DEPTH for our hardcore audience
  (combat dial + city-builder crunch — don't be too spare).
