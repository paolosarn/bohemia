# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #147 — "SHARP AND FLAT (THE IKANA SUITE)"
**Game:** The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask (2000)
**Studio:** Nintendo EAD
**Quest:** the composer brothers of Ikana — one dead in a grave beneath the haunted graveyard, one cursed in the cave at the river's source — and the song carried between them that un-poisons a canyon. The quest that is #138's first act: before Pamela's door can be reached, two brothers' grief has to be settled, and the settling is done by delivering music from the dead to the damned.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (the Pamela-sibling file, queued by name; anchored on #138's stage-1 utilities chain and mined with it)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** the handoff queue names the Sharp/Flat pair explicitly as #138's siblings. The machine here is the corpus's purest RELAY OF GRIEF: the quest-giver is a dead man, the deliverable is a song, the target is his brother, and the "boss fight" is standing inside a curse and answering it with the right music. It is also the model INFRASTRUCTURE PRELUDE — a family's rescue (#138) whose first act is fixing the water, and the water is fixed by fixing a relationship between two ghosts. Utilities and grief as one system: the most Bohemian sentence in Zelda.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**Two brothers served a dead kingdom as its musicians. One lies sealed in a grave; one sits cursed at the spring, and the river died with him. The player's job is to be the message: learn the dead brother's song and play it to the living-enough one, inside the storm of his curse, until he remembers who he is.**

Ikana Canyon is Majora's Mask's country of the dead — a kingdom that fell, populated now by its remainders: warring ghosts, mummified families (#138), and the institutional dead who still hold their posts. Among them: the composer brothers, court musicians of the fallen royal family. Flat lies beneath the graveyard, sealed in a grave, patient as sheet music. Sharp is in the cave at the river's source, cursed — sold to the darkness in the kingdom's fall — and his corrupted requiem drains the life of anyone who enters, while the spring he haunts has stopped giving water. The canyon's river is dry BECAUSE a musician's soul is: the environment's failure state and a person's are the same fact, which is the whole design.

The player reaches Flat's grave (the dead must be commanded to open it — the graveyard's own machinery), and the dead brother does not ask for vengeance or rescue. He teaches a SONG — the Song of Storms — and asks, in effect, that it be carried to his brother. The delivery is the boss fight: enter Sharp's cave, stand inside the life-draining requiem with the meter falling, and answer his music with the brother's. The curse breaks. Sharp weeps, remembers, grieves what he did in the kingdom's service and to his brother in its fall. The spring flows. The river runs. Downstream, a music-box house's wheel begins to turn, its gibdo-repelling song starts, and #138 becomes possible.

No sword is drawn in the whole suite. The quest's verbs are: command the dead, learn from the dead, stand in the pain, answer it with the right song. And the reward is WATER — the most survival-denominated payout in the game: fix the family upstream, and the family downstream becomes savable.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** grief between people doesn't stay between people — it dries up rivers, and everyone downstream lives with the drought until someone carries the message neither of them can carry themselves.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### FLAT — the dead brother, the quest-giver in a grave
- **What he wants (stated):** his song carried to his brother.
- **What he wants (real):** for Sharp to be freed from what he became — and, underneath, to be FORGIVEN and to forgive; the song is both message and absolution, encoded so neither brother has to say the words.
- **What he'll trade:** the Song of Storms itself — a permanent player-power, handed over as postage.
- **What he will never say out loud:** blame. The account of the kingdom's fall and his brother's part in it arrives without a single accusatory line; the dead man's discipline IS his characterization.
- **Function:** QUEST-GIVER as WITNESS: the corpus's cleanest case of the giver being dead, patient, and asking for a delivery rather than a deed.

### SHARP — the cursed brother, the boss who is the victim
- **What he wants (as cursed):** nothing — the curse wants; he executes. His requiem drains life because that is what he became in the kingdom's fall: an instrument turned against the living.
- **What he wants (really, underneath):** to be answered. The one thing that reaches him is his brother's music — identity delivered through the ear when every other door is sealed.
- **What he will never say out loud:** what he did — until the curse breaks, and then it is the ONLY thing he can say. The un-cursing uncorks the confession; the boss's death-speech slot is filled by weeping instead.
- **Function:** OBSTACLE and PATIENT in one body (Q135's law; #138's father is its domestic twin one screen downstream — the suite runs the same machine twice at two scales, deliberately).

### THE SONG OF STORMS — the deliverable, cast as a character
- A song that is rain, grief, and cleansing in one instrument — taught by the dead, answered by the cursed, and mechanically the thing that makes water.
- **Function:** THE MESSAGE. The quest item is knowledge that only means something because of WHO it is from. Handed to the player, it is a tune; played in the cave, it is a brother's voice.

### THE RIVER — the stakes, cast as infrastructure
- Dry while Sharp is cursed; flowing when he is answered. Downstream: the music-box house, the gibdos, Pamela, her father (#138).
- **Function:** THE COST MADE GEOGRAPHIC. A relationship's failure state, rendered as drought; its repair, as current. Utilities-as-grief is the suite's signature.

### THE GRAVEYARD DEAD — the door
- The stal-soldiers beneath Ikana's graves, who obey their captain's authority (the player wearing it) and open the grave.
- **Function:** THE LOCK on the quest-giver: even STARTING this quest requires borrowed authority over the dead — Ikana's whole country runs on chain-of-command necromancy, and the player enters the suite by out-ranking corpses.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE DOOR IN THE GROUND [MANDATORY]
Entry: Ikana graveyard, at night, wearing the dead captain's authority.
The player physically does: commands the graveyard's dead to open the sealed grave; descends beneath the headstones into the dark under the cemetery.
MISSABLE: the timing and authority requirements make the door easy to never find — the whole suite hides behind graveyard mechanics, and the canyon's surface gives almost no pointer down. (See F1.)

### STAGE 2 — THE DEAD MAN'S LESSON [MANDATORY]
Beneath: Flat, sealed and waiting. He gives the account — the kingdom's fall, the brother lost to the dark — and teaches the Song of Storms. The player leaves a grave carrying a dead musician's voice.
What changes: a permanent song-power, AND a delivery obligation with no journal entry — the game trusts the player to connect the grave to the cave. (Knowledge-gate, #141-class: the link is stored in the player's understanding or nowhere.)

### STAGE 3 — THE CAVE AT THE SOURCE [MANDATORY]
Entry: the dry river's spring; Sharp's cave.
What happens: the curse's arena — Sharp's requiem plays, the player's life drains on a clock, and the correct verb is to STAND IN IT and play the brother's song. Answering music with music, under damage, is the entire fight.
Exit: the curse breaks; Sharp's confession and grief; the spring flows.

### STAGE 4 — THE RIVER RUNS [MANDATORY, systemic]
Water moves downstream: the music-box house's wheel turns; its gibdo-repelling song starts; the canyon's one defended home comes back online. #138's stage 1 is this stage seen from the house.

### STAGE 5 — THE THREE DAYS [the game's standing condition]
Rewind the world (and the player must), and the river dries again, the curse resumes, the grave re-seals. The suite's repair — like #138's reunion — is written on the world's ice, not its stone; only the SONG persists across resets, because knowledge lives in the player. The one permanent object in the whole suite is what the dead man taught.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE F-1 — Flat, beneath the graveyard. Entry: the grave opened, stage 2.
The dead composer's account: the kingdom fell; his brother went to the dark in its service; he himself lies here. Then the lesson — the song, taught phrase by phrase.
> learn the song [gate: none — the scene's one verb] -> the Song of Storms enters the player permanently
> (there are no other options. The dead man has one request and the format should be honest that the scene is a gift with a freight attached, not a negotiation)
NOVERB "Who was right, you or him?" — the adjudication verb is absent by design. Flat does not ask the player to judge the brothers' history, and no line exists to volunteer for it. The delivery is clean of verdict: the corpus's model for how a messenger role should be written — carry the message, not the case.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing at the grave. The freight is paid in the cave.

### NODE S-1 — Sharp's cave. Entry: stage 3. The conversation conducted entirely in music and damage.
His requiem opens the exchange — the curse's position stated as a life-drain the player stands inside.
> play the Song of Storms [gate: KNOWLEDGE — stage 2 done; the game never marks the connection] -> the answer. The curse breaks mid-phrase; the drain stops; the confession flows
> play anything else / nothing [gate: none] -> the drain continues to death or retreat. Wrong answers are priced in the only currency the cave takes: life
> leave and return prepared [gate: none] SILENCE -> the cave permits retreat; the curse waits. Grief-as-obstacle has no timer except the world's own three days
THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK is not a line: it is the moment the player's song and the requiem overlap — two pieces of music from two brothers fighting for the same air, resolved when the older one wins. The scene's craft is that the "dialogue" is literally contrapuntal.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: standing in the pain while answering it. The player takes real damage during the delivery — mercy priced in hearts, the game's way of saying that carrying someone's grief to their brother is not free.

### NODE S-2 — Sharp, uncursed. Immediately after.
The weeping; the confession (what he served, what it cost, what he did in the fall); the spring's release.
> (no options — the player's role in the reconciliation is COURIER, and the format records the humility: the brothers' scene belongs to the brothers)
DIFFERENT TEXT BY WORLD-STATE: return in the same three days and Sharp keeps the spring, speaks as the restored musician; rewind, and none of it happened — the reset-variant text is the game's standing grief (Q128's paradox, the suite's version).
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the player watches a repair they cannot keep. Three days of river, purchased with a permanent song — the exchange rate of the whole game, in one spring.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 1 authored resolution, 2 failure postures, and the reset-cycle's standing bifurcation — the suite is a CORRIDOR WITH A PRICE, and its branching lives in the cycle, not the quest.**

- **THE DELIVERY MADE:** song learned, curse answered, river running, #138 unlocked. The suite's only completion — and by design: a grief-relay is not a fork; the craft is in the carrying, not the choosing.
- **THE DELIVERY REFUSED (by omission):** never open the grave, never enter the cave — the canyon stays dry, the music-box stays silent, and #138's house survives on borrowed time forever. The failure state is a STANDING DROUGHT, visible to any player who walks the canyon: unfinished quests as geography.
- **THE DELIVERY FAILED (in the cave):** the drain wins; retreat or death; the curse waits. Loops without penalty beyond the world's clock.
- **THE CYCLE BIFURCATION:** completed-this-loop vs reset — the suite's repairs are loop-local, its KNOWLEDGE permanent. Across a full playthrough the player relives the delivery as needed, and the repetition reads as tending, not grinding: some things must be re-done every cycle because the world re-breaks. (The suite's deepest Bohemia relevance: maintenance as meaning.)

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE SUITE HIDES ITS OWN FRONT DOOR.** The chain (graveyard authority → night → the specific grave → the song → the unmarked connection to a cave across the canyon) is famously obtuse; generations of players finished the game with Ikana's river mysteriously dry, never knowing the brothers existed. A masterpiece gated behind guide-tier discovery.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** hidden quests may hide their CONTENT, never their EXISTENCE. The world must visibly carry the wound (the dry river IS visible — good) AND at least one diegetic pointer chain toward its cause (testimony, records, the organ's cases — Bohemia's #141 machinery is exactly this). A drought with no witnesses is content that doesn't exist; a drought people TALK about is a quest hook. (Pairs with #146 F1: the ledger as witness.)

**F2 — THE RELAY HAS NO STAKES FOR THE COURIER'S CONSCIENCE.** The player carries absolution between brothers whose history (a kingdom's fall, service to the dark, whatever passed between them) is delivered as backstory paint — the quest never lets the courier LEARN enough to have a position, so the reconciliation, gorgeous as staging, is morally weightless for its deliverer. Compare Q126's Hope or #141's letter-writer, where the player's knowledge implicates them.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if the player carries a message that settles an account, let them READ enough of the account to be tempted — to edit, withhold, or take a side — and make carrying it CLEAN a chosen discipline rather than the only line (the F-1 NOVERB done Bohemia's way: the adjudication verb exists and costs). A courier who could have meddled and didn't has done something; one who couldn't has done a fetch.

**F3 — THE RESET ECONOMY CHEAPENS THE REPAIR FOR SOME, DEEPENS IT FOR OTHERS.** The three-day undo of every emotional repair is the game's thesis, but the criticism is real and old: players who experience the resets as futility disengage from exactly the scenes the design most wants to matter, and the game provides no register acknowledging REPEATED tending (the tenth delivery plays like the first).
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** where Bohemia loops (runs, generations), repeated tending must be SEEN by the record: the fold counts the times, and the world's text can reference the count (the keeper who has now fixed the same pump every winter for nine years deserves a line about it). Repetition acknowledged is devotion; repetition ignored is a chore. The mechanism is one integer per repair-class entry.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE QUEST-GIVER IS DEAD AND WANTS A DELIVERY, NOT A DEED. No vengeance, no rescue — a message. The relay frame makes the player a musician's postal service, and the humility of the role is the scene's dignity.
W2. UTILITIES AND GRIEF ARE ONE SYSTEM. The river is dry because a soul is; fixing the relationship fixes the water. Infrastructure as emotional state is the suite's signature and Bohemia's native tongue (survival accounting with a heart under it).
W3. THE DELIVERABLE IS KNOWLEDGE WITH PROVENANCE. The song works because of WHO taught it — the same notes from a book would be noise to Sharp. Quest items that are knowledge-with-a-source outclass every fetchable object. (#141's watch, #144's chart, and this song are one family.)
W4. THE BOSS FIGHT IS STANDING IN PAIN AND ANSWERING CORRECTLY. Damage falls while the player performs the remedy — courage rendered as holding a song steady under a life-drain. No sword; the whole confrontation is delivery-under-fire.
W5. THE CURSED ONE IS THE VICTIM, AND BREAKING THE CURSE UNCORKS THE CONFESSION. The boss's death-rattle slot is filled with weeping and account-giving: violence's usual payout replaced by testimony. (Q135's monster-as-patient, run one screen upstream of #138's domestic version — the suite teaches the machine twice.)
W6. THE UNMARKED CONNECTION. Grave to cave, song to brother — no journal, no marker; the link lives in the player's comprehension. The suite trusts understanding as its only thread (twelfth-plus echo of comprehension-as-the-key, and the smallest, cleanest instance in the corpus).
W7. THE FAILURE STATE IS VISIBLE GEOGRAPHY. Skip the suite and the dry river stays, walkably, for the whole game — unfinished business as terrain. The world wearing its open quests is reactivity's cheapest, most honest form.
W8. THE REWARD IS WATER. The payout is the most survival-denominated resource in the region, cashed downstream by people the player hasn't met yet. Doing right upstream of strangers is the quest's actual moral, delivered mechanically.
W9. AUTHORITY OVER THE DEAD IS THE DOOR. Even finding the giver requires out-ranking corpses — the necro-bureaucratic entry fee sets the whole country's tone: in Ikana the dead have chains of command, and the player enters by borrowing one.
W10. REPAIRS ARE LOOP-LOCAL, KNOWLEDGE PERMANENT. The world's undo erases the river but not the song: the design states, mechanically, what survives resets — what you learned. The roguelite thesis in miniature, shipped in 2000.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE GRIEF RELAY [W1, W3, F2's law — the core port]
**System:** the fold / [READ] / the missing-persons organ (#141)
The relay frame, Bohemia-shaped: a dead person's record (the fold holds it; the organ surfaces it) contains a message addressed to a living person the deceased could not face — and the player can carry it. F2's law applied: the player can [READ] enough of the account to be TEMPTED (withhold, edit, take a side), and carrying it clean is a chosen discipline the record notices.
```
@TALK relay_offer speaker=record_of_the_dead entry=organ:case_closed
  @SAY There is a second page. Not for the file. For a person.
  @SAY It says things the dead one never said out loud. You can see who it names.
  @OPT "I'll carry it. Unread."         [gate: none] -> relay_clean    @DO set_flag carried_clean
  @OPT "I'll read it first."            [gate: none] -> relay_read     @DO set_flag read_the_page
  @OPT "Some pages should stay buried." [gate: none] -> relay_refused  @DO set_flag refused_relay
  @OPT (say nothing; take the page)     [gate: none] SILENCE -> relay_clean @DO set_flag carried_silent
  @NOVERB "Tell me what to do with it"
@END
```
