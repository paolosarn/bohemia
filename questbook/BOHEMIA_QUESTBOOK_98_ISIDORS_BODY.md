# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 98: THE EARTH WILL NOT ACCEPT HIM / YOUR FATHER LET THE PLAGUE IN ON PURPOSE (Isidor's Body, Pathologic 2)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest where you come home to inherit your father's work, find you
missed him dying by HOURS, get blamed for his murder by the entire town, and then have to examine his corpse
with the same hands and the same training he gave you — and find out he did it to himself. Complete event
flow, the three clues, the Earth rejecting the grave, the séance that costs a child's lifespan, the wrong
murderers, the truth, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our FOLD (the inheritance
you arrive too late for), our [READ], our AMALGAMATION, + our death-math. Ice-Pick Lodge (Nikolay Dybowski).
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: the Day 1-2 Isidor thread in Pathologic 2 (2019). You are ARTEMY BURAKH, the Haruspex — born into "**A
FAMILY ENDOWED WITH A CASTE RIGHT TO CUT LIVING BEINGS OPEN.**" You've been away ten years studying modern
medicine. Your father's letters called you home, "**PRESAGING OF SOME TRAGEDY.**" You start with **30%
Health, 20% Reputation, 45% Hunger, 55% Exhaustion.** You are already losing.

## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- YOU MISS HIM BY HOURS, AND THE TOWN DECIDES YOU DID IT (the opening — STARTLINGLY our FOLD): "**AS SOON AS
  HE SPEAKS TO ONE OF THE FOUR OPTIONS HE WILL DISCOVER THAT HIS FATHER ISIDOR BURAKH IS DEAD AND HE HAS
  MISSED HIM BY MERE HOURS. WORSE, THE TOWN BELIEVES THAT HIS DEATH - SPECIFICALLY HIS MURDER - WAS CAUSED
  BY THE HARUSPEX.**"
  - "**THE HARUSPEX SOON BECOMES HATED IN THE TOWN. PEOPLE CLOSE THEIR DOORS IN HIS FACE AND HIDE THEIR
    CHILDREN FROM HIM. HE IS AN OUTSIDER TO THE TOWN HE ONCE CALLED HOME.**"
  - The inheritance quest and the murder accusation are THE SAME QUEST. You cannot claim what he left you
    without proving you didn't kill him for it (cf. our FOLD + standing + conscience system).
- THE INHERITANCE IS AN IDENTITY, AND HE NEEDS IT LIKE FOOD (the stakes, in Artemy's own words): "**FROM THIS
  POINT FORWARD, I AM THE ELDEST MEMBER OF THE BURAKH FAMILY. A MENKHU. I AM THE HEIR TO MY FATHER... THE
  INHERITANCE OF MY FATHER WILL NOURISH MY MIND AND MY BODY ALIKE. IT IS A QUESTION OF LIFE AND DEATH FOR
  ME TO CLAIM IT, FOR IT WILL IMBUE MY DISSOLUTE EXISTENCE WITH REASON... WHO WILL I BECOME IF I WILL FAIL
  TO BIND MY DESTINY WITH MY FATHER'S CALLING? THEY SAY I AM A PATRICIDE. THE WHOLE TOWN HAS RISEN AGAINST
  ME.**"
  - The fold's thesis stated by a character: inheritance isn't loot. It's the only thing that makes the
    heir's life mean anything, and he has to EARN it from a town that hates him (STARTLINGLY our FOLD +
    generational persistence).
- AND THE ANSWER IS THAT HE KILLED HIMSELF, SLOWLY, ON PURPOSE, FOR THE TOWN (the truth): the full
  reconstruction — "**ISIDOR SPOKE WITH SIMON KAIN REGARDING THE POSSIBILITY OF A SECOND OUTBREAK of the
  vicious disease that ravaged the Crude Sprawl, AND BOTH OF THEM AGREED THAT THE PLAGUE WAS A NECESSARY
  STEP IN THE EVOLUTION OF THE TOWN. THEY WENT OUT INTO THE STEPPE AND DUG A HOLE THERE IN ORDER TO BRING IT
  UPON THEMSELVES AND CARRY IT BACK TO THE TOWN, FROM WHERE IT WOULD THEN SPREAD.**"
  - Your father, the town's only doctor, the man who taught you to heal, went into the desert and **dug up
    the plague and carried it home in his body** because he decided the town needed the trial.
  - The murder you're accused of is real — Oyun did it — but the CORPSE is the crime scene of something far
    worse: a suicide that took a town with it (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + Amalgamation threat logic;
    the atrocity committed by the person you loved most, for reasons).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (stage by stage)
===============================================================================

### STAGE 0 — THE PROLOGUE IS THE HOUSE, IN A DREAM, AND IT LAUGHS AT YOU
- Before the game starts, on the train, Artemy dreams. "**THE TRAIN HAS STOPPED OUTSIDE OF ISIDOR BURAKH'S
  HOUSE. THE HARUSPEX RETURNS TO HIS CHILDHOOD HOME WITH THE VOICE OF HIS FATHER IN HIS EARS. HE IS ABLE TO
  TAKE ITEMS FROM THE CABINETS, BUT HE WILL HEAR LAUGHTER AS HE DOES SO.**"
- "**WHILE INSIDE, VOICES WILL LAUGH AT THE HARUSPEX, INCREASING IN VOLUME IF HE STOPS TO LOOT ANY ITEMS.**"
- The game's first move is to let you rob your father's house in a dream and have the house MOCK you for it.
  You have not arrived yet and you are already an inheritance-grabber (cf. our fold + conscience system).
### STAGE 1 — YOU ARE ATTACKED AT THE STATION BY PEOPLE WHO KNEW YOU AS A CHILD
- "**UPON ARRIVING AT THE SETTLEMENT, ARTEMY IS ATTACKED AT THE TRAIN STATION BY THREE MEN WHO BELIEVE THAT
  HE IS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE MURDER OF HIS FATHER.**"
- The vigilante's line: "**DON'TCHA SEE? BURAKH THE TURNCOAT! ISIDOR'S SON HIMSELF!**"
- You kill them. You now have blood on your clothes, which every NPC comments on. **Sticky**, a child sitting
  outside your father's house, "**WILL HAVE LITTLE TO SAY FOR HIMSELF ASIDE FROM COMMENTS REGARDING THE
  HARUSPEX'S BLOODY CLOTHES.**" The evidence of your innocence-fight is the evidence of your guilt (cf. our
  standing + conscience system + entities; the self-defense that convicts you).
### STAGE 2 — THE TOWN INVENTS A MONSTER RATHER THAN BELIEVE THE TRUTH
- "**MANY BELIEVE THE KILLER TO BE A SHABNAK-ADYR, A CREATURE MADE OF CLAY WITH BONE LEGS. IT IS A LOCAL
  LEGEND, BROUGHT UP BY A GRIEVING TOWN AS AN EXPLANATION FOR THE ATROCITIES THAT HAD OCCURRED THE NIGHT
  BEFORE.**"
- The crowd outside the house theorizes the clay monster. It's the Doomed Commercial Area's curse (Q97)
  again: when the truth is structural and unbearable, people build a folk creature (cf. our district +
  [READ] + post-collapse premise).
### STAGE 3 — YOU CANNOT GET INTO YOUR OWN HOUSE (the bureaucracy of grief)
- "**A PATROLMAN WILL BE GUARDING THE DOOR. THE PATROLMAN TELLS HIM THAT HE CANNOT ENTER SINCE THE KEY IS
  BEING HELD BY OTHER GUARDS OUTSIDE THE TERMITARY. JUST AS THEIR CONVERSATION ENDS, THE GUARD RECOGNISES
  THE HARUSPEX AS ISIDOR'S SON AND, SINCE EVERYONE BELIEVES HE IS HIS MURDERER, ATTACKS HIM.**"
- "**TWO PATROLMEN WILL BE STANDING AT THE ENTRANCE TO THE TERMITARY BLOCK. ATTEMPTING TO SPEAK WITH EITHER
  OF THEM CAUSES THEM TO ATTACK. THE HARUSPEX MUST KILL THEM TO OBTAIN ISIDOR'S KEY AND CAN FINALLY RETURN
  HOME.**"
- You kill three men to get the key to your dad's front door. And the game notes the alternative: "**DESPITE
  IT BEING HIS FAMILY HOME, SHOULD HE USE A LOCKPICK, ARTEMY'S REPUTATION WILL SUFFER FOR IT.**" Breaking
  into your own house is a crime (cf. our standing + conscience system + district).
- Inside: "**UPON ENTERING, FOUR RATS WILL APPEAR AND ATTEMPT TO BITE HIM. HE MUST ELIMINATE THEM, AS THEIR
  BITES WILL INFECT HIM WITH A DANGEROUS DISEASE.**" Your childhood home is now a disease vector.
### STAGE 4 — THE WITNESS IS A CHILD IN A CLOSET
- "**AFTER DEALING WITH THE RATS, THE HARUSPEX FINDS A BOY NAMED STICKY STANDING ON THE TABLE IN THE FAR
  ROOM OF THE HOUSE.**"
- Sticky's testimony: "**HE WAS HIDING IN THE CLOSET AT NIGHT, HEARD ISIDOR COME AND THEN HEARD SOMEONE
  HEAVY, A STEPPE INHABITANT COME AND THEN ALL WENT SILENT. IT WASN'T THE ILLNESS, WHICH KILLED ISIDOR IN
  THE END...**"
- The only witness to your father's murder is a homeless child who was robbing the house at the time. Your
  entire case rests on him. And he's one of your **Adherents** — the people whose lives you're responsible
  for (cf. our [READ] + companions + district).
### STAGE 5 — THE EARTH REJECTS HIM (the image the whole quest is built around)
- Day 2, the cemetery: "**ON THE MORNING OF THE SECOND DAY, ARTEMY GOES TO SEE HIS FATHER'S BODY IN THE
  CEMETERY. UNFORTUNATELY, HE DISCOVERS THAT THE EARTH WILL NOT ACCEPT ISIDOR'S REMAINS, AS IT IS SAID THAT
  A DEBT IS STILL OWED.**"
- "**COMING TO ISIDOR'S BURIAL REVEALS THAT THE EARTH REJECTS HIM, A SIGN THAT HE DIED WITH AN IMPORTANT
  TASK UNRESOLVED.**"
- The ground will not take him. In a game where the Earth is a character and the Burakhs are the caste that
  "**LINK THE EARTHLY TO THE UNDER-EARTH,**" this is a legal verdict delivered by soil (STARTLINGLY our
  death-math + fold + [READ]; the corpse that won't close).
### STAGE 6 — THE EXAMINATION (three facts, and you have to be the one to find them)
- "**IF THE HARUSPEX DECIDES TO LOOK AT THE BODY, HE CAN NOTICE THREE THINGS:**"
  1. "**THAT THE WOUND WAS NOT CONSISTENT WITH A KNIFE BUT RATHER SOMETHING LIKE A HORN OR CLAW**" — the
     murder weapon was a ritual implement. Kin. Not a townsman.
  2. "**THAT HIS SKIN WAS SUSPICIOUSLY DRY TO THE TOUCH**" — he was sick before he died.
  3. "**THAT THERE WAS DIRT UNDER HIS NAILS**" — he had been DIGGING.
- Three clues on your father's body. Two of them are about the murder. **The third one is about what he did
  in the desert.** And it's the only one that matters (cf. our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded; the detail
  that's answering a different question than the one you asked).
- The whole training is aimed here: "**READING THE FUTURE IN THE ENTRAILS, HE KNOWS THAT A BODY BEARS
  SEMBLANCE TO THE UNIVERSE. HIS SCALPEL FOLLOWS THE LINES OF THE BODY; HIS STEPS FOLLOW THE LINES OF HIS
  KIN'S FORTUNE. A HARUSPEX THAT CAN TELL A TRUE LINE FROM A FALSE ONE IS ENTRUSTED WITH POWER. A HARUSPEX
  WHO IS CONFUSED BY HIS PATH GETS BURIED INTO THE DEEP BLACK FLESH OF THE EARTH.**"
### STAGE 7 — THE SÉANCE COSTS A CHILD YEARS OF HER LIFE
- "**ON DAY 2, THE HARUSPEX CAN 'SPEAK' WITH ISIDOR BY USING GRACE'S POWERS, AT THE EXPENSE OF GRACE'S
  LIFESPAN.**" Grace is a little girl who tends the graves and can talk to the dead. Using her ability
  shortens her life.
- And you get nothing: "**THE CONVERSATION IS ONE-SIDED, AS ISIDOR SEEMS TO BE SPEAKING WITH SOMEONE ELSE AND
  DOES NOT RESPOND TO HIS SON'S ATTEMPTS AT COMMUNICATION.**"
- You burn a child's lifespan to hear your dead father talking to somebody else. That is the single cruelest
  design decision in the medium and it is PERFECT (cf. our conscience system + death-math + companions).
- What Grace can tell you: "**HE SOUNDS AS THOUGH HE WAS SURPRISED BY HIS DEATH, AS THOUGH A FRIEND HAD
  KILLED HIM, BUT THAT NOTHING WOULD HAVE CHANGED EVEN IF ARTEMY HAD ARRIVED ON TIME.**"
- **NOTHING WOULD HAVE CHANGED EVEN IF YOU HAD ARRIVED ON TIME.** The game's answer to the player's guilt
  about missing him by hours: it wouldn't have mattered. That's not comfort. That's worse.
### STAGE 8 — THE WRONG MURDERERS (the town keeps arresting the wrong men)
- "**A MESSENGER FROM ALEXANDER SABUROV, ASKING HIM TO COME TO THE ROD, AS HE HAS ONCE AGAIN FOUND ISIDOR'S
  MURDERER. IT IS STILL THE WRONG MAN. THE SUSPECT WAS A THIEF, BUT HE DID NOT KILL ISIDOR BURAKH.**"
- "Once again." The Mayor keeps producing suspects. They keep being innocent. The justice system is a
  suspect-generator (cf. our faction web + district).
- And the Bachelor, meanwhile, clears you with a THEORY that's also wrong: "**BACHELOR DANKOVSKY HAS MANAGED
  TO UNCOVER THE TRUTH. SIMON KAIN FELL VICTIM TO SOME UNKNOWN DISEASE. SINCE THE CONNECTION BETWEEN THE
  KILLINGS IS EVIDENT, IT IS CLEAR THAT YOUR REVERED FATHER, ISIDOR BURAKH, HAD DIED OF THE SAME CAUSE.**"
- Isidor did NOT die of the disease — Sticky heard him killed. The man who saves your reputation does it by
  being confidently incorrect, and the letter warns: "**WE'D LIKE TO WARN YOU THAT PEOPLE WITH A CERTAIN
  BIAS ARE LIKELY TO DELIBERATELY IGNORE THE FACTS. THEN, THERE ARE THE SIMPLETONS WHO WILL DISREGARD THE
  FACTS OUT OF STUPIDITY.**"
### STAGE 9 — WHY HE DIED (the politics under the murder)
- "**OYUN ONLY ACHIEVED HIS POSITION AS FOREMAN DUE TO COLLUDING WITH THE OLGIMSKYS AND ISIDOR BEING FAR
  LESS CONCERNED WITH KIN POLITICS OVER HIS INTEREST IN HEALING.**"
- Big Vlad consulted a prophet: "**KATERINA... FORETOLD EVERYTHING... THAT ISIDOR WOULD CAUSE SIMON'S DEMISE
  AND FROM THERE CAUSE THOUSANDS MORE DEATHS, AND THAT BURAKH WOULD BECOME THE LEADER OF THE KIN. VLAD
  OLGIMSKY INTERPRETED THIS AS ISIDOR RETURNING AND CAUSING THE KIN TO MASS RIOT... AND THUS ALLOWED OYUN TO
  PROCEED WHEN THE FOREMAN BROUGHT UP THE IDEA OF MAKING ISIDOR 'RESIGN' WITH THE CLEAR IMPLICATION OF
  MURDER.**"
- And the gut-punch: "**BIG VLAD ADMITS HE NOW KNOWS THAT THE PROPHECY WAS TALKING ABOUT THE PLAGUE AND
  ISIDOR'S SON TAKING POWER, NOT HIM.**"
- The prophecy was TRUE and they MISREAD IT, and the misreading is why your father is dead. The prophecy was
  about YOU (cf. our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded + AMALGAMATION; the accurate record, incorrectly
  interpreted, with a body count).
### STAGE 10 — HE TELLS YOU HIMSELF, IN A DREAM, AND HANDS YOU THE DECISION
- "**ISIDOR CAN BE SEEN IN A DREAM NOT LONG AFTER THE ARMY ARRIVES... IN THIS DREAM, HE EXPLAINS HIS
  PERCEPTION OF THE PLAGUE AS A TRIAL FOR THE TOWN, AND HOW HE WAS TORN BETWEEN PRESERVING THE PAST AND
  LOOKING TO THE FUTURE. HE LEAVES THE FINAL CHOICE TO HIS SON, SAYING HE TRUSTS IN HIS JUDGMENT.**"
- Five years earlier: "**THERE WAS AN OUTBREAK OF THE SAND PLAGUE IN THE CRUDE SPRAWL... ISIDOR ORDERED AN
  IMMEDIATE QUARANTINE OF THE AREA, RESULTING IN MASS DEATH AND LEAVING ONLY A HANDFUL OF ORPHANS BEHIND.
  IT IS SAID THAT THIS ACT BY ISIDOR BIRTHED ASPITY AS A REMINDER OF HIS ACTIONS.**"
- He'd done it before. He sealed a neighborhood and let it die. The orphans you meet all game — Murky,
  Sticky, Grace — are the children he made. And then he went and got the plague on purpose (cf. our
  AMALGAMATION + fold + district).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE TRUTH IS ASSEMBLED FROM SIX SCATTERED, MOSTLY-MISSABLE PIECES: three autopsy observations, a séance
  that costs a child's life, a dream after the army arrives, and a Big Vlad confession. "**ALL OF THOSE
  ELEMENTS ALLOW THE HARUSPEX TO PIECE TOGETHER THE EVENTS OF THE NIGHT BEFORE HE ARRIVED.**" Miss two and
  the whole thing stays fog. LESSON (~10th confirmation): if the thesis requires a full collection set, most
  players finish the game not knowing what happened. Our [READ] must have a floor, not just a ceiling.
- THE COMBAT IS THE WORST PART OF ITS OWN BEST QUEST: getting the key to your father's house requires
  killing three guards with Pathologic 2's notoriously bad melee, at 30% health, starving. LESSON (repeat):
  when the emotional scene has a combat tax in front of it, players remember the tax (our Dead Eye Dial).
- "NOTHING WOULD HAVE CHANGED" DEFUSES THE PREMISE: the game's central guilt — you missed him by HOURS — is
  explicitly neutralized by Grace. It's honest and it's also the writer taking the knife back out. LESSON:
  be careful about answering the question your premise is built on.
- THE WHOLE TOWN'S HOSTILITY IS A DIFFICULTY WALL, NOT JUST A STORY: "**UNTIL HAVING RESTORED YOUR
  REPUTATION... YOU WON'T BE ABLE TO BUY ANYTHING AT SHOPS.**" Being hated is thematically perfect and
  mechanically means you starve on Day 1 while learning the systems. LESSON (ours): a low-standing start is
  great narrative and brutal onboarding — gate the SHOPS or the CONTENT, not both.
- THE MURDER IS SOLVED BY A CHILD IN A CLOSET, AND THEN THE GAME MOVES ON: Sticky's testimony is the
  linchpin of the entire accusation, and it has no follow-up scene where you USE it. LESSON: if you build a
  witness, build the moment where the testimony lands.

## V2-A CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

**ARTEMY BURAKH (the player)** — wants to have arrived in time; is hours late forever. Wants the town to stop believing he murdered his father. FUNCTION: the heir performing the inheritance ON the testator: the examination scene uses the exact skills the dead man taught.

**ISIDOR BURAKH (the body)** — wanted the town saved and decided the price was letting the plague in to force the cure question. Will never say anything again; the corpse and the letters are the whole testimony. FUNCTION: the father as EVIDENCE. Q125 PORT 1's inheritance-that-indicts, in its rawest form: examining him clears you and convicts him.

**THE TOWN** — wants a murderer with a face; invents one (you) within hours. Will trade: nothing; mobs don't negotiate, they subside. FUNCTION: the jury that arrived before the facts.

**THE CHILD WITNESS (in the closet)** — wants to un-see. FUNCTION: the testimony that costs its giver; cross-ref the séance beat (information priced in a child's lifespan) — this quest keeps making KNOWLEDGE cost the knower.

**THE EARTH** — refuses the grave. FUNCTION: the image the quest is built around; the land itself files an objection to the burial, and the player must resolve the objection with forensics.

## V2-B THE CONVERSATIONS (node trees)
