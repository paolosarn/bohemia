# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 102: SEVEN DOORS ON ONE ROOF / WRONG CITY, WRONG PEOPLE (Nocturne Op. 55 N. 1, Cyberpunk 2077)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the quest that is one conversation on one rooftop, where a dying man
picks how to die, and every door out of the story is unlocked by a RELATIONSHIP you built forty hours ago
without knowing you were building a key. Complete event flow, all seven endings, the unlock conditions, the
suicide option, the secret run, the Phantom Liberty cure that costs everything, the honest flaws, and
Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our FOLD, our Liberate/Respect/Become, our Megaton law, our
AMALGAMATION, + our companions. CD Projekt Red. Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest
written here.

Quest: "Nocturne Op. 55 N. 1," the point of no return in Cyberpunk 2077 (2020). V has a **Relic** biochip in
their skull overwriting them with the engram of a dead terrorist, Johnny Silverhand. V is dying. The doctors
have said months. The whole game has been the question Dexter DeShawn asked in the first hour: does V
"**LIVE IN PEACE**" or go down in a "**BLAZE OF GLORY**"? And: "**NEARLY EVERY PATH IS SHADOWED BY THE GRIM
PROGNOSIS FROM THE RELIC BIOCHIP: IF V SURVIVES, THEY HAVE ONLY SIX MONTHS TO LIVE.**"

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE ENTIRE FINALE IS A CONVERSATION ON A ROOF, AND YOUR OPTIONS ARE YOUR FRIENDSHIPS (the structure —
  STARTLINGLY our standing + faction web): V goes up to the roof of Misty's Esoterica with Johnny, takes
  some pills, and picks. The dialogue wheel is the game.
  - But the options aren't given. They're EARNED, invisibly, hours earlier: "**PANAM'S PATH ISN'T AVAILABLE
    BY DEFAULT. TO UNLOCK THIS AS AN OPTION, YOU HAVE TO FIRST COMPLETE ALL PANAM'S MISSIONS, INCLUDING THE
    SIDE JOB QUEEN OF THE HIGHWAY. IF YOU'VE TICKED ALL THE BOXES, YOU'LL HAVE THE OPTION TO CALL IN THE
    ALDECALDOS.**"
  - "**TO UNLOCK ROGUE'S PATH, YOU NEED TO COMPLETE ALL OF JOHNNY'S SIDE MISSIONS WITH ROGUE, INCLUDING SIDE
    JOB BLISTERING LOVE.**"
  - And the secret one: "**IN ORDER TO GET THE DON'T FEAR THE REAPER OPTION... V WILL NEED TO PICK THE RIGHT
    DIALOGUE OPTIONS DURING THE CHIPPIN' IN QUEST BEFORE THE POINT OF NO RETURN. IF DONE CORRECTLY, JOHNNY
    SHOULD SUGGEST A WILD SUICIDE RUN.**"
  - The number of ways you're allowed to die is exactly equal to the number of people who'd show up for you
    (STARTLINGLY our standing + faction web + companions + fold; the finale gated on relationships, not
    stats).
- EVERY DOOR COSTS YOU THE PERSON WHO OPENED IT (the thesis): call Panam and Aldecaldos die storming the
  tower. Call Rogue and Rogue dies. Call Arasaka and your friends abandon you. Go alone and **nobody dies
  but you.** Give Johnny the body and V is erased.
  - There is no configuration where everyone walks out. The game's own answer, via Johnny, when you pull off
    the best run: asked about happy endings — "**WRONG CITY, WRONG PEOPLE.**"
- AND THE PRIZE FOR WINNING IS SIX MONTHS (the design's spine): "**IF V SURVIVES, THEY HAVE ONLY SIX MONTHS
  TO LIVE.**" Every "good" ending is a countdown. The Sun leaves you a legend with a clock. The Star leaves
  you in a convoy with a clock. There is exactly ONE ending that removes the clock, and it costs you
  everything else (cf. our death-math + fold + no-clean-answer; the victory with an expiry date).

## 1. THE FULL EVENT FLOW (the seven doors)
===============================================================================

### THE ROOFTOP (the staging is a man on a ledge with a friend who isn't there)
- Misty's rooftop. Pills. A view of Night City. Johnny, who exists only in V's head, sitting beside them.
  The most expensive game of its year ends its story with **two people talking on a roof**, one of whom is
  a hallucination of a dead man.
### DOOR 1 — THE DEVIL (call Arasaka, the people who did this to you)
- "**THE DEVIL: AVENGE THE DEATH OF SABURO ARASAKA.**" "**V GETS HELP FROM TAKEMURA AND ARASAKA. THEY CAN
  CHOOSE TO MERGE WITH MIKOSHI OR SPEND THEIR LAST FEW MONTHS OF LIFE BACK ON EARTH.**"
- The read: "**THE OUTCOME IS A MASTERCLASS IN CORPORATE BETRAYAL. V EITHER BECOMES A DIGITAL GHOST, TRAPPED
  IN MIKOSHI WITH NO GUARANTEE OF A NEW BODY, OR RETURNS TO EARTH AS ARASAKA'S LAB RAT, ONLY TO FIND
  THEMSELVES ABANDONED BY ALL THEIR FRIENDS. YOU LITERALLY SELL YOUR SOUL TO THE DEVIL.**"
- **MIKOSHI** is a corporate soul-storage system. **SECURE YOUR SOUL** is the product name. You can be
  uploaded and shelved, indefinitely, pending a compatible body (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION + NETWORK;
  the afterlife as a subscription).
### DOOR 2 — THE STAR (call Panam, leave the city)
- "**THE STAR: LEAVE NIGHT CITY WITH THE ALDECALDOS.**" "**PANAM HELPS V STORM ARASAKA TOWER. JOHNNY GOES
  WITH ALT, ALLOWING V TO REMAIN IN THEIR BODY. V LEAVES NIGHT CITY WITH THE ALDECALDOS IN SEARCH OF A
  CURE.**"
- "**A COUPLE OF MINOR CHARACTERS WILL DIE, BUT UNLESS YOU'RE PARTICULARLY ATTACHED TO PANAM'S FELLOW
  ALDECALDOS, YOU LIKELY WON'T BE TOO UPSET.**" Note the phrasing — the "good" ending's body count is
  described as forgettable.
- "**THE CLOSEST CYBERPUNK 2077 GETS TO A 'HAPPILY EVER AFTER.'**" And a player's honest counter: "**THE
  STAR ENDING AS WHOLESOME AS IT IS WE ARE LITERALLY ON THE RUN. I AM GOING TO LOSE MY SIGHT, MY HEARING AND
  MY ABILITY TO BREATHE BEFORE I DIE.**"
### DOOR 3 — THE SUN (call Rogue, become the legend, die famous)
- "**THE SUN: BECOME A LEGEND OF THE AFTERLIFE.**" "**V TAKES OVER THE AFTERLIFE AND BECOMES A LEGEND IN
  NIGHT CITY. V ENDS UP ALONE, BUT THE WHOLE CITY KNOWS THEIR NAME, AND IN THE END, THEY SET OFF ON ONE FINAL
  MISSION: A HEIST OF THE CRYSTAL PALACE CASINO, ON BEHALF OF A CLIENT WHO CLAIMS THEY CAN SAVE V'S LIFE.**"
- "**THE GAME ENDS WITH V IN THE MIDDLE OF THE CRYSTAL PALACE HEIST IN SPACE.**" The credits roll on a job
  in progress. You never find out.
- The character read: "**THE V WHO COMMITS TO A LIFE OF VIOLENCE IS SELFISH; THEY FORSAKE THEIR
  RELATIONSHIPS TO ATTEMPT A SUICIDE MISSION, AND EMBRACE THE RESULTING FAME RATHER THAN LEAVE NIGHT CITY
  BEHIND.**"
### DOOR 4 — TEMPERANCE (give Johnny the body; V is erased)
- "**TEMPERANCE: LET JOHNNY SILVERHAND KEEP YOUR BODY.**" "**ALT HAS MANAGED TO SEPARATE JOHNNY AND V'S
  ENGRAMS, BUT THE DAMAGE TO V'S BODY IS TOO SEVERE. SHE GIVES V A CHOICE; RETURN TO THEIR BODY WITH SIX
  MONTHS TO LIVE, OR LET JOHNNY KEEP IT AND KEEP GOING.**"
- "**V'S CONSCIOUSNESS IS ERASED ENTIRELY... WHILE JOHNNY GETS A SECOND CHANCE AT LIFE, THE COST IS
  ABSOLUTE.**"
- And the reaction that makes it: "**MANY OF V'S ALLIES, ESPECIALLY PANAM, HATE JOHNNY FOR THIS OUTCOME,
  CALLING IT 'SICK' THAT JOHNNY WOULD BE WALKING AROUND IN V'S BODY, AND PANAM THREATENS TO KILL HIM AND GET
  REVENGE FOR WHAT IS ESSENTIALLY V'S DEATH.**"
- A player's furious account, and it's the best criticism in the search: "**THIS TIME WENT FOR LETTING JOHNNY
  KEEP MY BODY, WHICH I THOUGHT WOULD BE A GOOD ENDING. DAMN IF I WAS WRONG. JOHNNY PRETTY MUCH ADMITS HE
  GOT EVERYTHING HE WANTED AND SEEMS TO BE FINE WITH V LIVING FOR 6 MONTHS — DOESN'T EVEN BAT AN EYE!**"
- Johnny then "**GETS ONTO A BUS AND LEAVES NIGHT CITY FOR GOOD**" in your body (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION
  + fold; the uploaded dead man who inherits the living).
### DOOR 5 — PATH OF LEAST RESISTANCE (the pills)
- "**THE PATH OF LEAST RESISTANCE: V DECIDES TO KILL THEMSELF AT THE ROOFTOP OF MISTY'S ESOTERICA.**"
- The unlock is chilling in its casualness: "**WHEN YOU'RE ASKED IF YOU ARE SURE, YOU SAY YOU NEED TO
  RECONSIDER. AFTER THAT, SELECT 'COULD ALSO JUST PUT ALL THIS TO REST,' AND THEN THE OPTION THAT THROWS THE
  PILLS AWAY.**"
- "**ONE OF THE MAIN ENDINGS CUTS YOUR STORY SHORT AND PREVENTS YOU FROM ACCESSING THE FINAL MISSION.**"
  Credits. That's it. The game lets you quit, and it counts it as an ending.
### DOOR 6 — DON'T FEAR THE REAPER (the secret run: go alone, nobody dies)
- "**THE SECRET ENDING: V ASSAULTS ARASAKA TOWER, SOLO.**" Unlocked only by "**PICK[ING] THE RIGHT DIALOGUE
  OPTIONS DURING THE CHIPPIN' IN QUEST**" — a conversation about Johnny's dead friends, hours earlier, that
  you didn't know was a test.
- The design: "**GOING IN ALONE IS EXTREMELY RISKY, BUT IT MEANS NOBODY DIES DURING THE FINAL MISSION.**"
- And the community's verdict, after 900 hours: fans "**AGREE IS THE BEST.**" Johnny's line, when you pull it
  off and ask about happy endings: "**WRONG CITY, WRONG PEOPLE.**"
- It's the only run where the answer to "who pays for this" is **you** (cf. our Megaton law + conscience
  system + Pacifist Law; the hardest path is the one where nobody else dies).
### DOOR 7 — THE TOWER (Phantom Liberty: the cure, and what it costs)
- "**KING OF SWORDS: V SURRENDERS SONGBIRD TO SOLOMON REED DURING THE KILLING MOON IN EXCHANGE FOR A CURE.
  UNLOCKS THE DLC SECRET ENDING FOR THE BASE GAME WHERE V IS CURED OF THE RELIC.**"
- "**REED WILL CONTACT V AFTER THE DLC IS OVER AND, WHEN YOU FEEL YOU ARE READY... V WILL BE ABLE TO HAVE
  SURGERY TO CURE THEM OF THEIR TERMINAL DISEASE. THEY WAKE UP AND FIND THAT TWO YEARS HAVE PASSED. ALL
  THEIR CYBERWARE IS GONE, BUT THEY ARE ALIVE.**"
- The bill: "**WHAT MAKES THIS SUPPOSEDLY HAPPY ENDING SO GUT-WRENCHING IS THE FACT THAT JOHNNY IS LOST IN
  THE PROCESS, WITH HIS FINAL GOODBYE LEFT ECHOING IN THE AIR. THAT, COMBINED WITH REALIZING THAT EVERYONE
  ELSE HAD ASSUMED V WAS DEAD AND MOVED ON, IS A VERY SAD CONCEPT, AND SOME MAY ARGUE IT UNDOES A LOT OF THE
  RELATIONSHIPS PLAYERS HAD INVESTED SO MUCH TIME IN.**"
- The only ending that removes the clock: two years gone, all your cyberware stripped, everyone grieved you
  already and moved on, and you can never be a merc again. "**IT OFFERS LIFE, BUT AT THE COST OF EVERYTHING
  THAT MADE THAT LIFE WORTH LIVING**" (STARTLINGLY our fold + death-math + persistent consequence; survival
  as the most expensive option).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE UNLOCK CONDITIONS ARE INVISIBLE AND PERMANENT: Panam's door needs **Queen of the Highway.** Rogue's
  needs **Blistering Love.** The secret needs specific lines in **Chippin' In.** Nothing tells you these are
  keys. Players reach the roof, find two options, and never learn the other five existed. LESSON (~12th
  confirmation, and the sharpest yet): if your finale is gated on relationship state, the game must SHOW
  the player they're building keys — our fold and our standing must make the accumulating consequence
  legible BEFORE the door.
- THE SUN DOESN'T END, IT STOPS: a player's honest read — "**MY BIG ISSUE WITH THE SUN ENDING WAS IT FELT
  LARGELY UNRESOLVED. IN STAR AND TEMPERANCE YOU LEAVE NC SO THE STORY ENDS THERE, BUT IN SUN THERE'S TOO
  MUCH STILL TO BE TOLD.**" Credits roll mid-heist, in space. LESSON: an open ending is a promise; if you
  can't pay it, it's a cliffhanger wearing a theme.
- TEMPERANCE MAKES JOHNNY LOOK LIKE A MONSTER AND THE GAME DOESN'T NOTICE: "**JOHNNY PRETTY MUCH ADMITS HE
  GOT EVERYTHING HE WANTED AND SEEMS TO BE FINE WITH V LIVING FOR 6 MONTHS — DOESN'T EVEN BAT AN EYE!**"
  The sacrifice ending's recipient is unbothered. LESSON: if a sacrifice ending's beneficiary doesn't
  visibly pay, the sacrifice reads as theft.
- THE STAR'S "GOOD" ENDING KILLS PEOPLE AND SHRUGS: "**A COUPLE OF MINOR CHARACTERS WILL DIE, BUT UNLESS
  YOU'RE PARTICULARLY ATTACHED TO PANAM'S FELLOW ALDECALDOS, YOU LIKELY WON'T BE TOO UPSET.**" LESSON
  (ours): if the deaths in your best ending are described as forgettable, they were never characters — our
  companions can't have redshirts.
- THE TOWER MAY HAVE BROKEN THE GAME'S OWN CONTRACT: "**SOME MAY ARGUE IT UNDOES A LOT OF THE RELATIONSHIPS
  PLAYERS HAD INVESTED SO MUCH TIME IN.**" A DLC added an ending that retroactively reframes the base game's
  entire arc. LESSON: adding a finale later can rewrite what the earlier hours MEANT — our GDD's canon
  discipline.
- SEVEN ENDINGS IS A LOT OF ENDINGS: several share a final mission with a swapped epilogue; "**TEMPERANCE IS
  A SLIGHT ALTERATION TO THE SUN AND THE STAR'S ENDINGS.**" Some of the seven are two. LESSON: count real
  branches, not achievement rows.
