# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 92: THE CURE IS THE DISEASE / EVERY FACTION IS A CHARACTER (Bloodborne)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: blood ministration (a miracle medicine that IS the plague), the Healing
Church (the darkest portrait of institutional medicine ever written), factions-as-characters, the
inheritance horror (a game about pregnancy, progeny, and what gets passed down), the three endings, Gehrman
trapped in a dream he can't leave, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our MEDICINE
CURRENCY, our faction web, our fold's inheritance, + our AMALGAMATION. FromSoftware (Hidetaka Miyazaki).
Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Bloodborne (FromSoftware/Japan Studio, 2015). Yharnam, a Victorian gothic city famous for BLOOD
MINISTRATION — a medical treatment that cures anything. You arrive sick, seeking it. You get the
transfusion. You wake into a night of the HUNT, when the city's citizens are turning into beasts. Edge
called it "a DAZZLING WORK OF DARK, ABJECT HORROR that cements Miyazaki as one of the all-time greats."

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE CURE IS THE DISEASE, AND THE INSTITUTION KNOWS (the premise — STARTLINGLY our MEDICINE CURRENCY):
  "Bloodborne takes place in Yharnam, a fictional Victorian city built on **A MIRACLE CURE called the Old
  Blood. This substance, discovered deep beneath the city, COULD HEAL ANY AILMENT. PEOPLE TRAVELED FROM
  ACROSS THE WORLD SEEKING TREATMENT. THE HEALING CHURCH CONTROLLED THE SUPPLY AND GREW INTO THE MOST
  POWERFUL INSTITUTION IN YHARNAM.**"
  - "**BUT THE OLD BLOOD CAME WITH A PRICE. OVER TIME, THOSE WHO USED IT BEGAN TRANSFORMING INTO BEASTS.**
    The streets filled with monsters. The Church, **DESPERATE TO MAINTAIN CONTROL, ESTABLISHED THE HUNT**: a
    night where trained Hunters would purge the beasts from the city."
  - And the knife: "**THE HEALING CHURCH'S GREATEST SIN WASN'T IGNORANCE. THEY KNEW THE BLOOD WAS DANGEROUS.
    Internal documents and hidden experiments show THEY CONTINUED DISTRIBUTING IT** while secretly searching
    for a way to ascend beyond humanity. **THE COMMON PEOPLE WERE EXPENDABLE.**"
  - The institution invented the cure, monopolized it, watched it create the plague, kept selling it, and
    then built an ARMED RESPONSE to the consequences — and sold that too (STARTLINGLY our MEDICINE currency
    + faction web + Network; the treatment that manufactures the patients).
- THE HEALING CHURCH IS THE BEST INSTITUTIONAL VILLAIN IN THE MEDIUM (the faction design): from the deepest
  lore analysis available — "**THERE IS PERHAPS NO FICTIONAL INSTITUTION BETTER EMBODYING THE CORRUPTION,
  ELITISM, AND DEHUMANIZATION IN MODERN MEDICINE THAN THE HEALING CHURCH. Presenting themselves as savior to
  the public while internally JUST CRUNCHING NUMBERS WITH NO PLANS FOR AN ACTUAL CURE, 'HEALTH' FOR THE
  CHURCH IS SIMPLY PROLONGING THE DAMAGE TO KEEP THE DESPERATE DEPENDENT ON 'TREATMENT' ANOTHER DAY.**"
  - "**WHAT ITS MEMBERS MIGHT LACK AS INDIVIDUALS THEY MORE THAN MAKE UP FOR AS THE COLLECTIVE. At the same
    time, THE BUREAUCRACY IS ITS OWN BEAST BEYOND THE SCOPE OF ANY SINGULAR MAN, A SOULLESS IDEOLOGY MADE
    MANIFEST AND RUN AMOK. PUT YOUR FAITH IN MEN'S IDOLS AND YOU WILL FIND YOURSELF DEALING WITH DEVILS.**"
  - No villain. A BUSINESS MODEL (cf. our CHURCH + NETWORK + faction web + medicine currency; the
    institution as the monster).
- FACTIONS *ARE* THE CHARACTERS (the writing method — direct for our 13-faction game): "**BLOODBORNE, WITH
  FEW EXCEPTIONS, FOCUSES MORE ON ITS WEB OF FACTIONS THAN ITS CHARACTERS. But given the writing style of
  Director Hidetaka Miyazaki, THESE FACTIONS IN MANY RESPECTS ARE THIS GAME'S CHARACTERS.**"
  - Byrgenwerth (the college that found the blood and said stop). The Healing Church (the students who
    stole it and sold it). The Choir (the Church's inner circle). The School of Mensis (the splinter that
    went further). The Vilebloods. The Executioners. Each is a POSITION on the same question, and their
    arguments are the plot (cf. our 13 factions; the faction as a protagonist).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (a city that ate its own medicine)
===============================================================================

### THE SCHISM IS THE WHOLE HISTORY (the origin — a lab ethics dispute)
- "Before the events of the game, **SCHOLARS AT THE COLLEGE OF BYRGENWERTH DISCOVER THE ELDRITCH GREAT ONES,
  AND A SUBSTANCE KNOWN AS THE OLD BLOOD. THE HEALING CHURCH, WHICH SPLITS FROM BYRGENWERTH'S RESEARCH,
  SPREADS BLOOD MINISTRATION THROUGHOUT YHARNAM.**"
- "**IF WILLEM WANTED THEM TO TAKE CAUTION WITH BLOOD, THEN THEY WOULD STOP BLINDLY TESTING HYPOTHESES ON
  THEMSELVES. BUT IN ORDER TO PROVE THE THEORY, THEY STILL NEEDED TEST SUBJECTS. ACCORDINGLY, THE
  'TRAITORS' OF BYRGENWERTH WOULD OUTSOURCE THEIR GUINEA PIGS.** Taking the forbidden Great One blood
  brought up from the ruins with them, the runaways **FASHIONED A NEW 'BLOOD TREATMENT' FOR THE MASSES.**"
- The founder said "be careful." The students heard "stop experimenting on OURSELVES." So they found other
  bodies. The entire apocalypse is a lab-ethics disagreement that got a customer base (cf. our Network +
  Amalgamation origin; the founding split).
### THE DOCTORS' OWN CREED IS A CONFESSION
- The White Church Garb description: "**THESE DOCTORS ARE SUPERIORS TO THE BLACK PREVENTATIVE HUNTERS, and
  SPECIALISTS IN EXPERIMENTALLY-BACKED BLOOD MINISTRATION... THEY BELIEVE THAT MEDICINE IS NOT A MEANS OF
  TREATMENT BUT RATHER A METHOD FOR RESEARCH, AND THAT SOME KNOWLEDGE CAN ONLY BE OBTAINED BY EXPOSING
  ONESELF TO SICKNESS.**"
- A piece of clothing states the faction's entire ethics. That's Miyazaki's method: the LORE IS IN THE ITEM
  DESCRIPTIONS, and the item is a thing you're WEARING (cf. our [READ] + inventory + prefab factory; the
  doctrine on the label).
### THE DOCTOR WHO TREATS YOU IS AN IMPOSTOR AND YOU CAN HELP HER
- You wake up in Iosefka's clinic after your first transfusion. Later you find that "Iosefka" is a
  DOPPELGÄNGER: "**INSTEAD OF HELPING PEOPLE, SHE IS EXPERIMENTING WITH THEM. THE PLAYER COULD HELP HER WITH
  HER RESEARCHES BRINGING NEW VICTIMS**, or can kill her."
- You can send survivors you rescued to her clinic "for safety." She experiments on them. The game never
  tells you (cf. our conscience system + Megaton law; the atrocity you commit by being helpful).
### THE GAME IS ABOUT PREGNANCY (the thing nobody expects — and it's our FOLD's horror twin)
- "**BETWEEN UNWANTED PREGNANCIES, UNHOLY CONCEPTIONS, CHILDREN TRAPPED IN A CONSTANT CRYING, HUMANS
  DEVOURING UMBILICAL CORDS TO EVOLVE, THE THEME OF MATERNITY IN THE WORLD OF BLOODBORNE IS DARKER THAN IN
  ANY VIDEOGAME MEDIA BEFORE.**"
- The Great Ones "were searching for centuries for a progeny, **MATING WITH WOMEN UNDER THE BLOOD MOON**,
  while **A HUMAN EATING THEIR UMBILICAL CORDS WOULD EVOLVE INTO A HIGHER ENTITY.**"
- Arianna, after the Blood Moon, hides in a basement: "**SHE GAVE BIRTH TO A DEFORMED CREATURE, a celestial
  progeny, A HORRIBLE GIFT FROM AN UNKNOWN AND ALIEN FORCE. Arianna is SHOCKED AND EMPTY, A SHELL WITH A
  DAMAGED MIND.**"
- And the false doctor, if you left her alive, gets "blessed" too — and she's PROUD: "**God I'm nauseous…
  It's progressing. I can see things… I knew it, I'm different. I'm no beast… I… Oh… God, IT FEELS AWFUL…
  BUT, IT PROVES THAT I'M CHOSEN. DON'T YOU SEE?**"
- The Great Ones "**ALL YEARN FOR A CHILD**" and lost theirs. The cosmic horror isn't tentacles. It's
  INHERITANCE — everyone in this game is trying to have a baby, and the babies are wrong (STARTLINGLY our
  FOLD + generational persistence + AMALGAMATION; the horror of what gets passed down).
### THE THIRD ENDING IS BECOMING THE NEXT GOD'S INFANT
- Two endings are simple: let Gehrman kill you and wake up, or refuse and fight him — and then "**THE
  DREAM'S MASTER, THE MOON PRESENCE, BINDS THEM TO THE HUNTER'S DREAM AS HIS REPLACEMENT.**" You become the
  new Gehrman. The chair, again (cf. Dark Souls Q91, Dragon's Dogma Q84).
- The third: "**IF THE PLAYER LOCATES AND CONSUMES THREE UMBILICAL CORDS from unsuccessful Paleblood
  pregnancies, IT'S POSSIBLE FOR THEM TO BE REBORN AS A PALEBLOOD SQUID BABY (AND FUTURE GREAT ONE)** within
  the Hunter's Dream."
- And the reframe: "**THE HUNTER'S DREAM WAS CREATED BY THE MOON PRESENCE IN ORDER TO SELECT AND NURTURE
  POTENTIAL CANDIDATES CAPABLE OF BECOMING ITS CHILD. The game's title, Bloodborne, therefore refers to the
  player-character's ultimate goal.**"
- The whole game was a god running a SELECTION PROCESS for a baby, and the hunts were the interview (cf.
  Dragon's Dogma's headhunted hero Q84, our fold + Amalgamation).
### GEHRMAN HAS BEEN IN THE CHAIR FOR CENTURIES AND WANTS OUT
- "Gehrman created the art of hunting beasts. He trained the first generation of Hunters... **AT SOME POINT,
  THE MOON PRESENCE TRAPPED HIM IN THE HUNTER'S DREAM**... He's been stuck there **FOR AN UNKNOWN LENGTH OF
  TIME, UNABLE TO DIE OR LEAVE.**"
- He offers to kill you as a KINDNESS — to let you wake up and go home. It's the only mercy he has, and he's
  been unable to accept it himself (cf. Dark Souls' Courland Q91, our fold + faction web; the caretaker who
  can't leave).
### EVERY FACTION THAT TOUCHED THE GODS DIED OF IT
- "**THE PTHUMERIANS WORSHIPPED THE GREAT ONES. BYRGENWERTH SCHOLARS STUDIED THEM. THE HEALING CHURCH TRIED
  TO COMMUNE WITH THEM. EVERY FACTION IN BLOODBORNE'S HISTORY HAS BEEN DRAWN TO THESE BEINGS, AND EVERY
  FACTION HAS BEEN DESTROYED OR CORRUPTED BY THE ATTEMPT.**"
- And the Great Ones aren't evil: "**THEY AREN'T EVIL IN THE TRADITIONAL SENSE. THEY SIMPLY OPERATE ON A
  SCALE THAT MAKES HUMAN CONCERNS IRRELEVANT**" (cf. our AMALGAMATION; the thing that ruins everyone who
  reaches for it, without malice).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE STORY IS BURIED SO DEEP IT NEEDS A 100-PAGE FAN DOCUMENT: the definitive lore analysis ("Paleblood
  Hunt") is a book-length Google Doc, and the community still argues. A reviewer's phrasing: it "**SOMEHOW
  MANAGES TO WITHHOLD EVEN MORE**" than Dark Souls. LESSON (seventh confirmation — Vagrant Story Q82,
  Arcanum Q86, Dark Souls Q91): the theme reaches the players who do homework. Put SOME of it on the
  critical path.
- THE BEST CHARACTER WRITING IS IN CLOTHING DESCRIPTIONS: the Church's entire medical ethic is in a garb
  item. Elegant AND completely missable — most players never read it. LESSON (ours): if our [READ] hides
  the thesis in flavor text, we've written for the wiki, not the player.
- THE FRAME-RATE / LOAD-TIME REALITY: Bloodborne shipped at 30fps with notorious load times, and never got
  a PC port or patch. Ten years later it's still locked to hardware. LESSON (direct, for a single-file
  iPhone game): PLATFORM DECISIONS ARE PERMANENT — the best-written world of its decade is imprisoned by a
  technical choice.
- THE CHALICE DUNGEONS ARE PROCEDURAL FILLER IN A HAND-CRAFTED GAME: the game's one procedural system is
  its most-skipped content, and it's where the deepest lore lives. LESSON (Subnautica's lesson Q90,
  repeated): "you can't teach an algorithm art direction" — and burying critical content in the generated
  layer is worse than not generating it.
- THE INHERITANCE THEME IS ALMOST ENTIRELY OPTIONAL: the Arianna pregnancy, the false Iosefka's
  "blessing," the umbilical cords, the third ending — nearly all of it is missable side content. Most
  players finish never knowing the game is ABOUT birth. LESSON: our fold's theme cannot be optional.
- YOU CAN CAUSE ATROCITIES BY BEING KIND AND NEVER FIND OUT: sending survivors to the "safe" clinic feeds
  the experiments. It's brilliant, and it's also invisible — many players never learn what they did.
  LESSON (the Megaton law, again): a consequence the player never discovers isn't a consequence, it's
  trivia.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the medicine that makes the patients + the child you leave
===============================================================================
Bloodborne is our MEDICINE CURRENCY's darkest possible reading, the best faction-web writing in the medium,
and — under the tentacles — a game about INHERITANCE that pairs directly with our fold.
- W1/W2/W6 (the cure is the disease + the institution is the monster + they sell you both — our MEDICINE
  CURRENCY + CHURCH/NETWORK): the top port, three fused, and it's aimed straight at a currency we already
  have. Our three currencies are MEDICINE, electricity, resources. Bloodborne asks the question we should
  be asking: WHO CONTROLS THE MEDICINE, AND WHAT DOES CONTROLLING IT DO TO THEM? Yharnam's answer: an
  institution that discovered a miracle, monopolized the supply, KNEW it caused the plague, kept
  distributing it because "the common people were expendable," and then sold the ARMED RESPONSE to the
  consequences it created. And the definitive description of it — "'HEALTH' FOR THE CHURCH IS SIMPLY
  PROLONGING THE DAMAGE TO KEEP THE DESPERATE DEPENDENT ON 'TREATMENT' ANOTHER DAY" — is a faction thesis
  we could write tomorrow. In post-collapse Vegas, whoever holds the medicine holds everything (ties our
  MEDICINE currency + survival accounting + CHURCH + NETWORK + faction web (13 factions) + AMALGAMATION +
  death-math + logistics + district + post-economic-collapse premise; a top-tier port).
- W3 (factions ARE the characters — our 13 factions, validated): the writing-method port. Miyazaki wrote a
  game whose CHARACTERS ARE INSTITUTIONS: Byrgenwerth, the Healing Church, the Choir, the School of Mensis,
  the Vilebloods, the Executioners. Each is a POSITION on one question (what do we do with the blood?), and
  their disagreements ARE the plot. We have THIRTEEN factions and a central question (what do we do with the
  Amalgamation / the dead / the record?). Bank the law: each faction should be a coherent ANSWER to that
  question, with a founding, a schism, a doctrine, and a body count — and their argument should BE our story
  (ties our faction web (13 factions) + AMALGAMATION + Network + Church + [READ] + GDD structure).
- W7/W9 (the cosmic horror is BABIES + the whole game was an interview — our FOLD): the port nobody would
  predict. Under the gothic wallpaper, Bloodborne is about PREGNANCY AND PROGENY: gods that "all yearn for a
  child," women impregnated under a blood moon, umbilical cords eaten to ascend, a mother reduced to "a
  shell with a damaged mind," and a final ending where you become an INFANT. And the Hunter's Dream itself
  was "created by the Moon Presence IN ORDER TO SELECT AND NURTURE POTENTIAL CANDIDATES CAPABLE OF BECOMING
  ITS CHILD" — the whole game was an audition to be someone's baby. Punk: our FOLD is three generations and
  our AMALGAMATION is made of the dead. Bloodborne is the horror version of our premise — what if the thing
  that outlives you is a child you didn't choose, shaped by something that was selecting for it the whole
  time? (ties our FOLD + generational persistence + AMALGAMATION + Network + death-math +
  Liberate/Respect/Become, Dragon's Dogma Q84, Dark Souls Q91).
- W4 (the apocalypse is a lab-ethics dispute with a customer base — our AMALGAMATION's origin): STEAL the
  schism. Byrgenwerth's founder said BE CAREFUL WITH THE BLOOD. His students heard "stop testing on
  OURSELVES," so they took the samples, left, and "**OUTSOURCED THEIR GUINEA PIGS**" — to the public, as a
  paid treatment. That's a better origin than any evil plan: the AMALGAMATION/NETWORK founded by people who
  split from a cautious institution and needed test subjects, so they made the public an OFFER (ties our
  AMALGAMATION + Network + faction web + recorded-vs-unrecorded + post-economic-collapse premise, STALKER's
  C-Consciousness Q85).
- W5 (the doctrine is printed on the uniform — our [READ] + inventory): bank the technique WITH its flaw.
  The Healing Church's entire medical ethic ("MEDICINE IS NOT A MEANS OF TREATMENT BUT RATHER A METHOD FOR
  RESEARCH, AND SOME KNOWLEDGE CAN ONLY BE OBTAINED BY EXPOSING ONESELF TO SICKNESS") is a CLOTHING ITEM
  DESCRIPTION. Our garment system, our prefab props, our inventory — every object can carry a faction's
  position. But see the flaw: most players never read it (ties our [READ] + inventory macro/micro + garment

- W8 (the victim is PROUD — our CHURCH + conscience system): bank the line. The false doctor, forcibly

  That's the most disturbing thing in the teardown and it's a CHARACTER BEAT our Church/Network could carry

  conscience system + faction web, Dark Souls' Way of White Q91).
- W10 (every faction that reached for the gods died of it, and the gods aren't evil — our AMALGAMATION):
  bank the shape. "The Pthumerians WORSHIPPED them. Byrgenwerth scholars STUDIED them. The Healing Church
  tried to COMMUNE with them. EVERY FACTION HAS BEEN DESTROYED OR CORRUPTED BY THE ATTEMPT." And: "THEY
  AREN'T EVIL IN THE TRADITIONAL SENSE. THEY SIMPLY OPERATE ON A SCALE THAT MAKES HUMAN CONCERNS
  IRRELEVANT." Three different postures toward our AMALGAMATION — worship (CHURCH), study (NETWORK), use
