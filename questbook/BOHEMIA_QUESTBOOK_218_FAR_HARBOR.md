## 0 CORE IDEA

**A fog-shrouded island holds three communities in a precarious balance — the human townsfolk who fear the fog and outsiders, a cult that worships the island's radioactive fog as divine, and a hidden sanctuary of escaped synthetic people led by a self-aware machine who offers runaway synths a refuge — and their fragile coexistence, the player discovers, is held together by concealed truths and at least one buried act (a synth living secretly among the humans, a memory-wipe, a hidden betrayal), so the player ends up holding the power to preserve the uneasy peace by keeping the secrets, to expose the truths that would collapse it into war, or to engineer the outright destruction of any of the three factions.**

Far Harbor's island is a knot of three peoples who each have reason to fear or hate the others, kept in balance by fog, distance, and lies. The human town survives by wariness. The Children of Atom worship the fog and want it to spread. And Acadia, hidden in the mist, is a refuge for synths — artificial people who fled the mainland's institute — governed by DiMA, a self-aware synth of deep intelligence and deeper moral complexity, who has kept his community safe through means that include manipulation, concealment, and at least one buried atrocity (replacing a human leader with a synth double, a memory-wipe, secrets that would ignite the island if known). DiMA embodies the central question of made people among the living: should synths hide (Acadia's model), integrate secretly (the replacement), or is coexistence with humanity impossible? The player, investigating, uncovers the hidden truths holding the peace together and gains the power to decide the island's fate: keep the secrets and preserve a coexistence built on lies, reveal them and let the balance collapse into open conflict, or take a side and engineer the extermination of the townsfolk, the cult, or the synths.

The machine is that a fragile peace between peoples who fear each other is held together by hidden truths, and the one who uncovers them holds the match — so the choice is whether a coexistence built on lies is worth preserving, or whether the truth is worth more than the peace. Acadia refuses the easy answers about made people. DiMA is not a villain and not a saint; he is a synth who did monstrous things (the replacement, the wipe) to keep his people alive, and the peace of the whole island now depends on those things staying buried. The player's discovery makes them the island's fulcrum: expose DiMA's crimes and the humans may destroy the synths; keep them and the coexistence continues on a foundation of concealment and injustice; pick a faction and end the standoff in blood. There is no option that is both fully honest and fully peaceful, because the peace was always a lie and the truth was always a weapon. And the deepest layer is the synth question itself — whether made people can ever safely live among their makers, or must hide, replace, or separate — with DiMA's compromised, secret-riddled refuge standing as the fragile best-case, one revelation away from ash. The player decides whether made people get to keep a peace they built on lies, and whether the humans get a truth that would let them destroy it.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** a peace between peoples who fear each other is almost always held together by hidden truths and buried wrongs, and whoever uncovers them holds the match — the choice is never clean, because a coexistence built on lies is unjust and a truth that would collapse it into war is not obviously better; sometimes the people keeping the peace did monstrous things to keep it, and deciding whether their secrets should hold is deciding whether a flawed survival is worth more than an honest catastrophe.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE SYNTH QUESTION CAN GET BURIED UNDER THE FACTION LOGISTICS.** The rich core (should made people hide, integrate, or is coexistence impossible) can be swamped by the mechanical three-faction quest structure, so DiMA's philosophical weight becomes a series of fetch-and-decide steps and the profound made-people question reads as flavor over a standoff to resolve.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest carries a deep question under faction logistics, the QUESTION must stay foregrounded — the made-people dilemma given real space amid the mechanics — so it isn't buried under keep/expose/destroy bookkeeping (#191's the-question-stays-central law).

**F2 — DIMA'S MONSTROUS MEANS CAN BE UNDER-WEIGHED, MAKING "KEEP THE SECRETS" TOO EASY.** If the replacement and memory-wipe are treated as minor, preserving the peace reads as obviously right; the power depends on DiMA's acts being genuinely monstrous (a real person replaced, a real mind wiped) so that keeping the secrets is a genuine moral cost, not a free good.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's peace rests on a keeper's atrocities, those atrocities must WEIGH — the real victims of the replacement and wipe felt — so "keep the secrets" carries a genuine cost and isn't a costless preservation (#180's the-buried-wrong-must-weigh law).

**F3 — THE EXTERMINATION OPTIONS CAN OVERSHADOW THE SUBTLER PRESERVE/EXPOSE CHOICE.** The dramatic power to wipe out a faction can pull the quest toward "which faction do you genocide," drowning the finer, harder question (is a peace built on lies worth keeping) under the spectacle of destruction — the subtle choices need weight equal to the loud one.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest offers extermination beside subtler resolutions, the SUBTLE options must carry weight equal to the destruction — preserve and expose given their full consequence — so the finer question isn't swamped by the spectacle of genocide (#196's the-quiet-endings-must-weigh law).

W8. THE PLAYER DECIDES FOR EVERYONE. The fulcrum resolves the island for all three peoples — enormous, uncomfortable power over others' fates.
W9. THE TRUTH IS A WEAPON. Exposing the secrets frees the humans to destroy the synths — honesty as detonation, not clean virtue.
W10. IT IS MADE-PEOPLE-AMONG-THE-LIVING IN ONE ISLAND. Whether synths can coexist, and at what cost of lies — Bohemia's whole Amalgamation question of the made among the born, in a three-faction standoff.
