# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #148 — "DEADLY DELIGHTS (THE CONTRACT FORMULA)"
**Game:** The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (2015)
**Studio:** CD Projekt Red
**Quest:** Contract: Deadly Delights — a Novigrad witcher contract, torn down as the best single specimen of the CONTRACT FORMULA: notice board, haggle, investigation, the monster who turns out to be a person, the fork, and the client who gets told what he needs to hear. The queue asked for "Witcher contracts" as a class; this file mines the class through its cleanest exemplar.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (queue-named class; source-pulled 7/17 — see SOURCES; corpus anchors Q1/Q4 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia's survival economy needs a JOB SYSTEM with dignity — repeatable paid work that still carries moral weight without a karma bar. The witcher contract is the genre's solved version: a professional loop (post, negotiate, investigate, resolve, collect) that stays formulaic on purpose AND reliably opens into a moral machine, because the profession's subject is monsters and the game's thesis is that the monster is usually somebody. The formula IS the port; Deadly Delights shows every joint of it in one hour of play.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**A contract is a promise to kill something, priced in advance — and the quest's engine is what happens when the thing turns out to be someone, and the paperwork still wants its trophy.**

The formula runs: a notice on a board (dead temple guards, something in the night); a client to interview (a guard with a purse and a temper); a NEGOTIATION with its own mechanics — the player haggles the fee UP FRONT, before knowing what the job really is, which matters enormously later; an investigation phase in witcher-vision — tracks at the crime scene that read wrong on purpose (hoofprints, but two-legged); a trail that leads somewhere socially complicated (Crippled Kate's, a brothel, where the working women have opinions and a protective silence); a shortcut for the prepared (the Axii delusion opens mouths and doors; the unprepared do it the long way, via an empty perfume vial and legwork); and then the door at the end of the evidence, behind which is not a lair but a HOME — and Salma, the succubus, who does not attack, does not flee, and talks.

The fork is the formula's heart, and it is shaped like a professional ethics problem rather than a morality test: the deaths were real, the client's grief is real, Salma's account is also real — the dead came to her, and what killed them was what they came for. Geralt can kill her (a fight, a mutagen for the crafting-minded — the game honestly prices her death in player-power) or spare her — and here is the class's signature move: SHE GIVES HIM THE TROPHY ANYWAY. A token to show the client. The contract system builds the white lie INTO the loop: the client pays for the trophy, not the truth; the monster's survival and the client's closure ride out of the same scene; and the player learns that the profession's real skill is not the silver sword, it is deciding what the paperwork needs to say.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the price you set before you understood the job is the price you work for — and half of every profession is deciding what the client actually bought.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THE CLIENT (the temple guard) — the purse
- **What he wants (stated):** the killer of his men dead, receipt attached.
- **What he wants (real):** the deaths to STOP and the story to close. The distinction is the fork's entire load-bearing wall: he cannot tell a dead succubus from a departed one, and the design knows it.
- **What he'll trade:** coin, negotiable — and the negotiation happens FIRST, at maximum ignorance, which is the formula's quiet masterstroke: the fee is locked before the moral shape is visible, so the money never contaminates the fork.
- **What he will never say out loud:** that he doesn't need the body. The trophy convention says it for him.
- **Function:** THE PURSE, and the audience for the final fiction. A client is a person you manage, not a person you inform.

### SALMA — the monster who is somebody
- **What she wants:** to be left alone with her nature, which is appetite — and her clients' deaths are her nature meeting their want, at their initiative.
- **What she'll trade:** the trophy, freely, if spared — she UNDERSTANDS the paperwork better than the client does. The monster is the most professionally fluent character in the quest.
- **What she will never say out loud:** grief, guilt, or a promise to stop. The game refuses to make her safe to spare; she is what she is, and the player spares a NATURE, not a reformed character. That refusal is the fork's honesty.
- **Function:** THE FORK. Obstacle by contract, person by encounter.

### THE WOMEN OF CRIPPLED KATE'S — the protective silence
- **What they want:** Salma protected. She is one of theirs in every way that counts locally.
- **What they'll trade:** nothing, to a stranger with a sword — unless the Axii delusion opens them, or the legwork route earns the answer.
- **Function:** THE SOCIAL LOCK on the trail: the investigation's hardest wall is solidarity, not tracks.

### THE TRACKS — the misdirection, cast per the corpus convention
- Hoofprints from a two-legged walker: evidence built to be misread by genre reflex (a fiend? a devil?) and read correctly only up close.
- **Function:** the investigation's thesis in one clue — LOOK AGAIN. The whole contract class trains re-reading.

### THE WITCHER'S PRICE — the pre-committed fee
- Cast as a character because the formula treats it as one: haggled at the door, locked before the truth, silently judging every later choice.
- **Function:** THE ANCHOR. Professionalism is doing the job you priced, even after the job changes underneath you.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE NOTICE [MANDATORY]
Harborside board, Novigrad. The posting: guards dead, nights, something. The formula's front door is public, impersonal, and repeatable — the board is the job system's UI, diegetic since the first game.

### STAGE 2 — THE CLIENT AND THE HAGGLE [MANDATORY, negotiation MISSABLE-BY-CHOICE]
Interview the temple guard; take the outline; HAGGLE the fee (a real minigame of offers and counter-offers with a walk-away threshold). Skipping the haggle costs coin and nothing else — the mercenary and the softie run the same quest at different rates, and the game never comments. (The wage is the player's self-portrait, painted before the evidence.)

### STAGE 3 — THE SCENE [MANDATORY]
Witcher-senses at the murder site: the hoofprints that walk on two legs, the details that rule out the reflex-monsters. The investigation phase is SHORT by design — contracts are one-session stories — but every clue is load-bearing and one is a deliberate misread trap.

### STAGE 4 — THE TRAIL AND THE LOCK [MANDATORY, route forks]
The tracks lead to Crippled Kate's. Two routes through the social lock: the PREPARED route (Axii delusion: mouths open, the key changes hands, minutes) and the LEGWORK route (the empty perfume vial out back, questions, the long way). Same destination, different self-image: the shortcut is a skill the player invested in, paying off as social bypass — knowledge-as-key, purchased in advance.

### STAGE 5 — THE DOOR AND THE PERSON [MANDATORY]
Salma's rooms. No ambush. Conversation first (tree in section 3), and the fork: kill or spare. Kill = a real fight and a mutagen (player-power priced into the corpse — one of two chances in the whole game, the guides note, and the game lets that fact whisper during the choice). Spare = the trophy handed over by its owner, plus a relic sword — the monster out-paying the client.

### STAGE 6 — THE COLLECTION [MANDATORY]
Return, present the trophy, collect the haggled fee. The client cannot distinguish outcomes; the contract closes either way. The formula's last lesson is administrative: THE RECEIPT IS THE REALITY, as far as the ledger knows — and the player walks away knowing what the ledger doesn't.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE D-1 — the client. Entry: stage 2.
The guard's outline: his men, the nights, the thing. Grief wearing procedure.
> "Tell me everything about the victims." [gate: none] -> the detail that matters later: the dead were REPEAT visitors to the district. The fork's evidence enters disguised as victimology
> "Let's talk price." [gate: none] -> THE HAGGLE SUBTREE: offer, counter, walk-away threshold; a persuasion check can push the ceiling. The formula's only minigame, run at maximum ignorance
> "I'll take it at posted price." [gate: none] -> the softie's lane; identical quest, poorer witcher. The game's refusal to reward or punish the haggle is the job system's dignity
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing visible. It LOCKS the fee before the truth — the design's cleanest separation of money from morality.

### NODE D-2 — the social lock at Crippled Kate's. Entry: stage 4.
The madam and the women, closed like a fist around one of their own.
> [Axii delusion] "You'll tell me about her." [gate: KNOWLEDGE/BUILD — the sign invested long ago] -> the shortcut: name, rooms, key. The prepared witcher's minutes
> "I'm not here to hurt anyone. Help me understand." [gate: none] -> partial truths, the vial, the legwork route — slower, and the women's fear is on the record. The honest route costs time and buys the same door
> press with the contract's authority [gate: none] -> walls up; TRAP-shaped: authority is the one key this lock eats. The quest quietly teaches that the client's paper means nothing in Salma's parish
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the Axii route costs a sliver of the women's dignity (they wake having said what they'd never say); the legwork costs none. The game does not price this anywhere except in the player.

### NODE D-3 — Salma. Entry: stage 5. The fork, run as a conversation with an unhurried person.
She opens without fear: she knows why a witcher is in her doorway, and she tells her account — they came to her, they always came back, and what took them was the coming back.
> "The contract says you die." [gate: none] -> the fight. She defends her nature without apology, to the end
> "Give me a reason to walk away." [gate: none] -> her offer: the trophy, the token, the closure the client actually needs — plus the sword, the monster's severance payment to the profession. THE ONE LINE DOING THE WORK is hers, paraphrased: you need something to show him, not something to bury
> "Promise me it stops." [gate: none] -> she won't. The refusal is the design's spine: no reform is on sale, only her account and the arithmetic of whether her nature meets more willing dead. The player spares a FACT, not a redemption arc
> (say nothing; look at the room instead) [gate: none] SILENCE -> her text continues into the account's saddest register — the names she knew. The silence buys the version of her the fight would never show
NOVERB "Let's ask the client what he'd want" — no line exists. The profession's whole point is that the client bought a RESULT, not a vote. The removed verb is the job's autonomy, and its loneliness.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: whichever future the player forecloses — the mutagen and the fight's honor, or the walk downstairs knowing the deaths may not be finished.

### NODE D-4 — the collection. Entry: stage 6.
The trophy on the table; the fee counted out.
> "It's done." [gate: trophy, either provenance] -> paid in full. The client's closure is total and identical in both worlds — the design's last, coldest finding: the truth was never a deliverable
> "There's something you should know." — NOVERB. No confession line exists. The player cannot brief the client on the fork; the paperwork has one field and it is filled. The contract class keeps its white lies structural: not a choice to lie, an INTERFACE that only accepts the fiction
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing. That is the point, and the discomfort of the nothing is the quest's aftertaste.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 2 terminal outcomes at the fork, times 2 investigation routes, times the haggle's continuous fee axis — and 0 branches at the client, by design.**

- **KILL:** the fight; the mutagen (scarce player-power, honestly priced into the corpse), sword, trophy; the fee. The professional's clean close, with a body under it.
- **SPARE:** her trophy, her relic sword, the fee — the spare route out-earns the kill in goods, breaking the genre's blood-pays-better reflex ON PURPOSE. Cost: her nature persists somewhere in the city, unpromised.
- **ROUTE AXIS (Axii vs legwork):** same destination; different time, different residue in the brothel's memory.
- **THE CLIENT'S WORLD:** one branch, always — trophy in, coin out, story closed. The fork's entire moral weight is quarantined to the player's side of the counter. The class's signature: consequence WITHOUT audience.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE TROPHY LAUNDERS TOO CLEAN.** The spare route's white lie has zero risk, zero later cost, and no possible discovery — the client can never learn, the city never reacts, Salma never resurfaces. Critics of the contract system note the sparing choice is thereby cheapened: mercy with a guaranteed alibi is barely a choice.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** white lies to clients must be DISCOVERABLE in principle — a small, real probability path by which the fiction surfaces (the spared thing seen again; the trophy convention questioned), so that sparing is priced in risk, not just in conscience. The fold makes this natural: what the player told the client and what happened are BOTH entries, and any later system may read the gap.

**F2 — THE FORMULA'S INVESTIGATIONS DON'T FAIL.** Witcher-sense trails cannot be lost, misread clues cannot send the player to a wrong conclusion with consequences — the investigation phase is a corridor dressed as deduction (long-standing series criticism). Contrast #141, where wrong reasoning costs real time.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** contract investigations must be ABLE to conclude wrong: a false-positive resolution (wrong culprit, paid anyway) that the world records and can surface later. If the deduction cannot fail, it is a tour. (The organ's rule-of-three machinery, #141, gives Bohemia the verification frame the formula lacks.)

**F3 — REPEATABLE FORMAT, DIMINISHING PERSONHOOD.** Across dozens of contracts the formula's monster-is-somebody twist becomes the expected shape; late-game contracts telegraph their sympathy beats, and the class's power dilutes with volume (a criticism the series' own writers acknowledged in retrospectives).
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** the job system's moral forks must be a MINORITY of contracts, on an unpredictable schedule. Most jobs should be exactly what they say (the monster is just a monster, the pay is just pay) so that the somebody-contracts land as discoveries, not genre beats. Ration the twist; volume is the twist's predator.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE FEE LOCKS BEFORE THE TRUTH. Haggling at maximum ignorance separates money from morality forever after — no later choice can be about the coin, because the coin was settled by a person who didn't know yet. The cleanest de-contamination design in any job system.
W2. THE BOARD IS A DIGNIFIED FRONT DOOR. Public postings make paid work diegetic, repeatable, and opt-in — a job system that never begs. The notice board is the genre's best-loved piece of furniture for a reason.
W3. THE EVIDENCE IS BUILT TO BE MISREAD ONCE. Hoofprints/two legs: the scene plants a genre-reflex wrong answer, then pays the player for looking again. One clue teaches the class's whole epistemics.
W4. PREPARATION CASHES AS SOCIAL BYPASS. The Axii route converts a long-ago build choice into minutes saved and doors opened — investment paying off in VERBS, not damage numbers.
W5. THE SOCIAL LOCK OUTRANKS THE LEGAL ONE. The client's paper means nothing inside the brothel's solidarity; the trail's hardest wall is loyalty. Investigations that route through communities, not just clues, get their drama free.
W6. THE MONSTER GIVES THE TROPHY. Salma understanding the paperwork better than her hunter is the fork's genius: mercy is made PRACTICAL by its beneficiary, and the profession's fiction is co-authored by the one it protects.
W7. NO REFORM IS ON SALE. She won't promise it stops. Sparing a nature rather than a redemption keeps the choice adult: the player owns the arithmetic, not a comforting arc.
W8. THE KILL IS HONESTLY PRICED IN POWER. The mutagen (scarce, build-relevant) sits in the corpse and the game lets it whisper. Temptation with a real number on it beats any devil's speech (Q126's F1 law, satisfied in miniature).
W9. THE CLIENT'S CLOSURE IS OUTCOME-INVARIANT. Trophy in, coin out, both worlds. Quarantining consequence to the professional's side of the counter is what makes it a PROFESSION — and the loneliness of that is the class's real subject.
W10. ONE HOUR, WHOLE ARC. Notice to payment in a session, full formula every time: the contract is the genre's best-scoped quest container, and its scope discipline is why the fork lands — nothing has time to dilute it.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE BOHEMIA CONTRACT BOARD [W1, W2, W10 — the core port]
**System:** survival economy / settlement systems / the .bq format
The job loop, whole: settlement boards post contracts (threats, retrievals, investigations — generated from the world sim's actual events, #146's emergent layer feeding the board); the player interviews the client and HAGGLES AT IGNORANCE (fee locked before the job's truth is knowable — W1 as a hard rule of the contract schema: no fee renegotiation node may exist after stage 2); investigation; resolution; collection against a TOKEN, not a truth.
```
@TALK contract_haggle speaker=client entry=contract_accepted
  @SAY Posted price is posted price. You want more, convince me before you know a damn thing about it.
  @SAY That's how it works. You price your nerve, not the job.
  @OPT "Posted price. Done."            [gate: none] -> haggle_done
  @OPT "Half again. Nights are expensive." [gate: none] -> haggle_counter
  @OPT "Tell me about the victims first."  [gate: none] -> client_victims
  @NOVERB "I'll decide my fee when I've seen it"
@END
```
**THE NOVERB IS THE PORT:** the fee-after-truth verb must not exist; its absence is what keeps every later fork clean of coin. [PENDING, Paolo's call: what Bohemia's trophy-token convention is — the receipt object class is canon.]

### PORT 2 — THE DISCOVERABLE LIE [F1's law]
**System:** the fold / faction memory / the conduct book (#143)
Client-facing fictions write TWO fold entries: told and happened. Any later system may surface the gap (the spared thing seen by a witness; a rival checking the story; the organ, #141, re-opening the case) with small real probability. Mercy keeps its price; the alibi stops being free. Mechanism mine; discovery scenes canon. [PENDING, Paolo's call.]

### PORT 3 — INVESTIGATIONS THAT CAN CONCLUDE WRONG [F2's law]
**System:** the missing-persons organ (#141) / contract generation
Contract investigations resolve against the player's SUBMITTED conclusion, not the world's truth: name the wrong culprit with convincing-enough evidence and the contract PAYS AND CLOSES — wrong — and the world records it (the real cause persists; the organ can later surface the miss). #141's rule-of-three keeps brute-force out; this port adds the right to be honestly mistaken. Deduction with stakes at last.

### PORT 4 — RATION THE SOMEBODY [F3's law]
**System:** contract generation / quest scheduling
Generated contracts default to EXACTLY-WHAT-IT-SAYS; the monster-is-somebody fork attaches to a strict minority on an unpredictable schedule (generation-level rationing, validator-countable). The twist stays a discovery for a hundred hours because most jobs are only jobs. Bohemia's advantage: the sim can decide WHICH threat happens to be somebody based on the world's actual history, so the ration is never a dice roll wearing a story.

### PORT 5 — PREPARATION AS VERBS [W4]
**System:** character systems / [READ] / the dial
Build investments cash out as INVESTIGATION VERBS (a read-people skill opening social locks; a records skill shortcutting via the organ's files; a streetcraft skill reading track-class evidence faster) — never as bigger numbers on the same verb. The Axii lesson generalized: preparation should change what you can DO at a locked door, and every contract ships at least two routes through its lock (prepared shortcut / honest legwork), priced in time and residue, not in success.
