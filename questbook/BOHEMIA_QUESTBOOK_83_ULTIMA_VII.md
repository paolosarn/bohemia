# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 83: EVERY PERSON HAS A BED / THE SELF-HELP CULT THAT ATE A KINGDOM (Ultima VII: The Black Gate)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the NPC-schedule simulation nobody has surpassed, the Fellowship (a
Scientology-modeled self-help cult that adopts the poor), the Guardian (the fourth-wall villain who is
secretly YOU), the murder-mystery investigation spine, the object-physics immersive-sim roots, the
virtue-simulation without a morality meter, the honest flaws, and Bohemia ports. The foundational open-world
RPG — PC Gamer asked in 2017 whether ANY RPG has surpassed it. STARTLINGLY relevant to our district's
living world, our faction web (esp. the CHURCH), our Amalgamation, + our [READ]. Origin Systems (Richard
Garriott, Raymond Benson). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Game: Ultima VII: The Black Gate (Origin Systems, 1992, MS-DOS). The Avatar returns to Britannia after 200
years. There's no war and no dark lord. There's a ritual murder in Trinsic, a popular new self-help church
called THE FELLOWSHIP that's taken over care of the poor, magic quietly dying, and a red face that appears
on YOUR computer screen calling itself the Guardian and promising to be "your companion... your provider...
and your master." "Widely lauded as a high point in the series" and a genre milestone.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- EVERY SINGLE NPC HAS A LIFE THAT ISN'T ABOUT YOU (the simulation — still unmatched): "In most RPGs
  non-playable characters are there to supply information... but have no lives of their own. The NPCs in
  Ultima VII, on the other hand, LIVE THEIR OWN LIVES. MOST OF THEM HAVE NOTHING TO DO WITH YOU AND WITH
  YOUR QUEST. THEY ARE THERE BECAUSE THEY ARE."
  - "The inhabitants of Britannia pursue their own schedules. YOU CAN STALK ANY CHARACTER AND WATCH HIS
    MOVEMENTS — it can be a fascinating process. You can witness how he wakes up in the morning. Then he
    probably goes to his shop... In the evening you can meet him in the local tavern drinking some beer and
    talking with friends. Then he goes back home and sleeps at night."
  - And the kicker: "I'm talking of EVERY SINGLE NPC in the game here, not just some 'chosen' characters who
    have something to do with the plot."
  - Dialogue follows the same law: "If you talk to an NPC, he will talk to you about WHAT IS IMPORTANT TO
    HIM, like any normal person, INSTEAD OF IMMEDIATELY GIVING YOU INFORMATION connected to the game."
  - The verdict, thirty years on: "YOU CAN COUNT ON ONE HAND THE AMOUNT OF GAMES THAT PAID ATTENTION TO NPC
    ROUTINES AFTER ULTIMA VII" (cf. our district + entities/spawning + scheduler; the world that isn't
    about you).
- THE VILLAIN IS A SELF-HELP CULT THAT DOES ACTUAL GOOD (the antagonist design — our CHURCH's blueprint):
  the Fellowship is "a spiritual prophet named Batlin" running "a religious organization... which HELPS
  BRITANNIA'S PEOPLE ACHIEVE SOME SENSE OF PERSONAL PEACE AND HOPE FOR A BETTER FUTURE; the group is
  popular enough to have spread throughout Britannia, ESPECIALLY IN THE POORER REGIONS."
  - "A new sect called 'The Fellowship' EXPANDS ITS MEMBERSHIP IN THE LOWER CLASSES and SECURES INFLUENCE
    THROUGH THEIR PROMINENT MEMBERS WHO ASCEND TO LEADING POSITIONS IN SOCIETY."
  - They "ADOPT THE POOR AND DISENFRANCHISED THAT THE REST OF THE REALM CAN'T SEEM TO HELP." The kingdom
    failed these people. The cult didn't.
  - Garriott confirmed in 1992 that the Fellowship is modeled on SCIENTOLOGY — "I'm serious" — down to the
    hierarchy, the triangle medallion, and the in-box recruitment book (STARTLINGLY our CHURCH + faction
    web + Network; the cult that fills a vacuum the state left).
- THE GUARDIAN IS YOU (the twist that reframes the whole trilogy): a red face appears ON THE PLAYER'S OWN
  SCREEN, breaking the fourth wall, addressing you directly, promising to be your "companion... provider...
  and master."
  - Throughout the game he "constantly mocks and chides you," gives "dangerous advice, SPRINKLED WITH JUST
    ENOUGH TRUTH TO KEEP YOU GUESSING," and specifically "will sometimes appear and MOCK THE PLAYER AFTER
    THEY COMMIT A CRIMINAL ACT."
  - And the trilogy's eventual revelation: "The Guardian is IN FACT THE ALTER-EGO, THE SUBCONSCIOUS MIRROR,
    TO THE AVATAR HIMSELF."
  - The thing watching you commit crimes and laughing IS you (STARTLINGLY our AMALGAMATION — an entity built
    from people that reflects them back; cf. Hearts of Stone's O'Dimm Q70, our conscience system).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how you build a world in 1992)
===============================================================================

### THE HOOK IS A MURDER, NOT A PROPHECY (the investigation spine)
- No chosen one, no invasion. You arrive and "the whole town is shocked due to a RITUALISTIC MURDER that
  occurred the preceding night — the body of the blacksmith Christopher was found in the stable." The mayor
  asks you to investigate. That's it.
- "Most of the game is composed of the Avatar's INVESTIGATION of the Fellowship and the Trinsic murders."
  The player is confronted with "graphic crime scenes, drug use, racism and political corruption."
- And the structure of the investigation is our [READ]: "It is OBVIOUS TO THE PLAYER FROM THE START that
  this group is hiding a nefarious agenda, but the Avatar MUST FIND CONCRETE PROOF of wrongdoing BEFORE
  they are able to make any connections." Suspicion isn't enough. You need evidence (cf. our [READ] +
  recorded-vs-unrecorded; testimony vs proof).
### THE SICKNESS IS AMBIENT, NOT ANNOUNCED (world-as-symptom design)
- "Though there are no wars being fought, no great ethical quests to solve, and seemingly only mundane
  problems, THIS IS ONLY A FACADE. Wizards are inexplicably losing their power, racial tensions between the
  Britannians and the Gargoyles are at a resounding high, the Moongates aren't functioning correctly, THE
  POPULATION FEELS SPIRITUALLY EMPTY FOR REASONS NOBODY CAN EXPLAIN, and study of the virtues seems to have
  fallen SOLELY INTO THE HANDS of an organization called the Fellowship."
- The apocalypse is a mood. Nobody can name what's wrong. That's the horror (cf. our post-collapse Vegas +
  district; the collapse as atmosphere).
### THE CUBE: A TRUTH-DETECTOR AS A QUEST ITEM (the best mechanic here)
- The Avatar acquires the CUBE GENERATOR — carry it, and people CANNOT LIE to you. The CRPG Addict:
  "The Fellowship is a great faction to hate, and an interesting antagonist, ESPECIALLY ONCE YOU HAVE THE
  CUBE AND CAN UNCOVER SO MANY OF THEIR LIES (A GREAT, GREAT, GREAT IDEA BY THE DESIGNERS, BTW)."
- An item that converts the whole world's dialogue into evidence. Every conversation you've already had
  becomes re-askable, and the answers change (cf. our [READ]; the tool that turns testimony into proof).
### YOU GO UNDERCOVER IN THE CULT (the investigation's best move)
- "The Avatar himself GOES UNDERCOVER BY ENTERING THE FELLOWSHIP" — joins, takes their tests, reads their
  book, wears their medallion. The game shipped a real FELLOWSHIP MEDALLION and THE BOOK OF FELLOWSHIP in
  the box, so the player was physically recruited too (cf. our faction web + standing; join to investigate).
### VIRTUE IS SIMULATED, NOT SCORED (the anti-karma design — directly ours)
- "Ultima VII features LESS EMPHASIS ON BEHAVING VIRTUOUSLY than other installments. The Avatar CAN STEAL
  AND MURDER TO HIS HEART'S CONTENT, without making the game impossible to finish."
- BUT: "the game SIMULATES A SOCIETY that sticks to some of the virtues: NPCs — either the party members or
  the people of Britannia — REACT TO KILLINGS OF INNOCENTS AND STEALING WHENEVER THEY BECAME AWARE OF IT;
  PARTY MEMBERS MAY LEAVE THE GROUP if they witness too much bloodshed and larceny AND WILL REFUSE TO JOIN
  THE PARTY UNTIL THE PLAYER REDEEMS THEMSELF. THEY MAY EVEN OPENLY REVOLT AND ATTACK THE AVATAR."
- No karma number. No good/evil bar. Just PEOPLE WHO SAW WHAT YOU DID and respond like people. In 1992
  (STARTLINGLY our conscience system — the direct answer to Tranquility Lane's +300-karma massacre Q78 and
  Come Fly With Me's incoherent ledger Q79).
### THE OBJECT PHYSICS (the immersive-sim seed)
- It "pioneered immersive sim elements such as INTERACTIVE OBJECT PHYSICS — allowing players to STACK CRATES
  FOR MAKESHIFT PATHS or manipulate the environment creatively." Every object is real: "you can move around
  just about anything you want — steal books for reference, MOVE LORD BRITISH'S FORK TO HIS KNIFE PLACE."
  It's the ancestor of Deus Ex (Q17), Dishonored (Q14), and Divinity (Q15) — Larian's whole design lineage
  traces here.
- Also a DEED SYSTEM: "the Avatar must BUY AND CARRY A TITLE to any horse-and-carriage or ship" — property
  law, in 1992 (cf. our prefab/inventory + city-builder; the world as manipulable stuff).
### THE VILLAIN IS A SHOT AT THE PUBLISHER (the craft note)
- Origin's slogan was "WE CREATE WORLDS"; the Guardian is "the DESTROYER OF WORLDS." The three Generators
  are shaped like a CUBE, SPHERE, and TETRAHEDRON — the old Electronic Arts logo. The Fellowship's two
  murderous enforcers are named ELIZABETH and ABRAHAM: E.A. Origin's conflict with EA is encoded in the
  villain (cf. our themes; the enemy made from your real life).
### THE ENDING STRANDS YOU (the cost)
- You destroy the Black Gate in time. But "as the moongates had been rendered useless by the destruction of
  one of the generators, THE AVATAR HAS TO REMAIN IN BRITANNIA, WITH NO MEANS TO RETURN TO EARTH." Winning
  costs you the way home (cf. our finales; the victory that exiles you).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE INVESTIGATION IS A FETCH-CHAIN IN A TRENCHCOAT: "Finding the Fellowship guilty is done by A STRING OF
  SEEMINGLY NEVER-ENDING FETCH QUESTS, where NPCs in the different towns will give the Avatar what he needs,
  BUT ONLY AFTER he ventures to a local dungeon to retrieve the requested item." And the plot's spine is
  "chasing around a brother and sister duo from city to city, A WEAK BUT FORGIVABLE WAY TO ENSURE THE
  PLAYER GETS TO EVERY CITY." LESSON (the third time this exact flaw has landed — Ranni Q71, Morrowind Q74,
  Come Fly With Me Q79): a great world + great writing REDEEM a fetch-chain but never fix it; our quest
  factory must make the connective tissue itself carry weight.
- ITEM ANXIETY / UNMARKED CRITICAL LOOT: "often you DON'T GET ENOUGH CLUES as to which items are really
  important; even worse, you could get an important item BEFORE YOU ACTUALLY KNOW YOU NEED IT, AND THEN
  THROW IT OUT. Imagine the frustration of going through the entire game and suddenly realizing that you've
  thrown out something needed to complete it... THIS FORCES YOU TO KEEP AND CARRY ALL KINDS OF WEIRD ITEMS
  — HALF OF WHICH ARE PROBABLY PURE JUNK — IN FEAR." LESSON (direct, for our inventory + prefab systems):
  full object-simulation without legibility creates HOARDING ANXIETY, not immersion.
- THE COMBAT IS A DISASTER AND THE MAGIC IS POINTLESS: critics "echo original complaints about CHAOTIC
  COMBAT, USELESS MAGIC SYSTEMS." Fights are a scrum you barely control. LESSON: a world-sim's combat can't
  be an afterthought — ours is the DIAL, and it's the one thing that must stay sharp.
- THE PLOT LOSES PLAYERS: "the plot isn't really that compelling. It's alright, but YOU CAN LOSE TRACK OF
  WHAT YOU NEED TO BE DOING NEXT. OR LOSE INTEREST." And after full exploration "what more will you do?"
  LESSON: an investigation needs a legible next-step (our quest factory + [READ]'s spine).
- THE ART IS FLAT: "every other NPC falls into a SMALL GROUP OF REUSED SPRITES, and the locations all end up
  looking VERY SIMILAR." The world is deep and looks samey. LESSON (ours, for the tile/pack pipeline):
  simulation depth doesn't cover for visual sameness — our HD tile repo + variation matter.
- ITS INFLUENCE WAS *NOT* ABSORBED, AND THAT'S A WARNING: the CRPG Addict's striking observation —
  "HARDLY ANYONE IS LEARNING FROM ULTIMA'S ATTENTION TO NPCs, OPEN GAME WORLDS, SANDBOX ENGINES, OR
  ORIGINAL PLOTS." A generation of RPGs copied the interface and ignored the ideas. LESSON: the best
  design in the world doesn't propagate on merit — it propagates when it's CHEAP TO COPY. Our systems must
  be affordable, or they die with us.

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the living district + the cult that fills the vacuum
===============================================================================
Ultima VII is the foundational text for everything we're building: a district where every person has a bed
and a job, a faction that's evil because it's GOOD at the thing the state failed at, an investigation where
proof beats suspicion, and a conscience system with no numbers. PC Gamer asked in 2017 whether any RPG has
surpassed it. Most of what's below is us trying.
- W1/W2 (every NPC has a life that isn't about you + they talk about what matters to THEM — our district):
  the top port. Bank the LAW: our DISTRICT's people should have schedules (wake, work, tavern, home, sleep)
  and most of them should have NOTHING to do with the dynasty. "They are there because they are." And their
  dialogue leads with THEIR business — name, job, trouble, family — not our quest. This is our scheduler +
  entities/spawning + 120-BPM tick doing narrative work, and Ultima VII proves it's the single most
  durable design decision in the genre's history (ties our district + city-builder + entities/spawning +
  scheduler + world-model + writing pillar, Gothic/Kenshi/RDR2 Q51/Q55/Q10; MAP LAW: Paolo owns layout,
  Claude owns the plumbing).
- W3 (the self-help cult that actually helps — our CHURCH + faction web): STEAL the Fellowship whole. It
  isn't evil because it's cruel — it's dangerous because it ADOPTS THE POOR AND DISENFRANCHISED THAT THE
  REST OF THE REALM CAN'T HELP, and then places its members in leading positions. In post-collapse Vegas
  the state IS gone, so a faction that actually feeds people and gives them meaning has enormous, earned
  power — and that's what makes it terrifying, not a secret torture basement. Garriott modeled it on
  Scientology and said so out loud. That's our CHURCH's blueprint and arguably the NETWORK's (ties our
  faction web (13 factions) + CHURCH + NETWORK + Amalgamation + HOMELESS/VOLUNTEERS + survival-accounting;
  a top-tier faction port).
- W4/W5 (suspicion isn't enough + the Cube — our [READ], mechanized): bank both. (a) The player should KNOW
  a faction is dirty in hour one and still have to find CONCRETE PROOF — that gap IS our [READ]. (b) And
  STEAL the CUBE: a portable device/authority that makes people UNABLE TO LIE, which retroactively converts
  every conversation in the game into evidence. Re-ask everything; the answers change. For us that's an
  Amalgamation/Network tool, a record-reader, a verified-testimony device — and it's the single best
  mechanical expression of recorded-vs-unrecorded we've found (ties our [READ] + recorded-vs-unrecorded +
  Amalgamation + faction web, Golden Idol/Obra Dinn Q54/Q19; a top-tier mechanical port).
- W6 (virtue is SIMULATED, not scored — our conscience system, answered): the most important flaw-fix in the
  questbook. Q78 (Tranquility Lane's +300-karma massacre) and Q79 (New Vegas's incoherent karma ledger) both
  taught us NOT to price morality. Ultima VII, in 1992, shows the ALTERNATIVE: no bar, no score — the
  dynasty can do anything, and PEOPLE WHO SAW IT respond. Companions leave. New ones refuse to join until
  you redeem yourself. Eventually they REVOLT AND ATTACK YOU. That's our conscience system: consequence as
  relationships, not arithmetic (ties our conscience system + companions + standing + district + Megaton law
  + Pacifist Law; THE answer we were looking for).
- W7 (the collapse is ambient): bank it — our post-economic apocalypse shouldn't be announced by a
  narrator. It should be a MOOD nobody can name: things quietly failing, tensions up, and "the population
  feels spiritually empty for reasons nobody can explain." That's the vacuum the CHURCH/NETWORK fill (ties
  our post-economic-collapse premise + district + faction web + ART PHILOSOPHY).
- W8 (the fourth-wall villain who watches your crimes and is secretly YOU — our AMALGAMATION): bank it. The
  Guardian appears specifically WHEN YOU DO SOMETHING WRONG, gives advice "sprinkled with just enough truth
  to keep you guessing," and is ultimately the Avatar's own subconscious mirror. Our AMALGAMATION is built
  from people — it should reflect them, comment on the dynasty's worst acts, and be RIGHT often enough to
  be trusted (ties our Amalgamation + conscience system + Network, O'Dimm's mirror Q70, our GDD's
  Amalgamation-design convergence).
- W9 (object physics + property law): bank both — Ultima VII's stack-the-crates physics is the seed of the
  entire immersive-sim tree, and its DEED system (buy a title before you can board a ship) is a
  city-builder idea in an RPG. Our prefab/inventory/city-builder should let the world be MOVED and OWNED
  (ties our prefab factory + inventory + city-builder + district).
- W10 (you go undercover, and so does the player): bank the move — joining a faction to investigate it, and
  handing the PLAYER the faction's own literature (our version: the Network's onboarding, the Church's
  pamphlet, an in-game readable that recruits you) (ties our faction web + standing + [READ]).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) THE FETCH-CHAIN FLAW IS NOW A LAW — this is the FOURTH teardown where a
  masterpiece's connective tissue is errands (Ranni Q71, Morrowind Q74, Come Fly With Me Q79, and here,
  where the plot literally chases two people city to city to force a tour); our quest factory must make the
  TISSUE carry weight; (b) OBJECT-SIM WITHOUT LEGIBILITY = HOARDING ANXIETY — players carried junk in fear
  of discarding a critical item; our inventory/prefab systems must SIGNAL what matters (a direct warning
  for our macro/micro inventory); (c) a world-sim's combat can't be an afterthought — U7's is "chaotic" and
  its magic "useless"; the DIAL is our one sharp thing and it stays sharp; (d) simulation depth doesn't
  cover visual sameness ("a small group of reused sprites... locations all end up looking very similar") —
  our HD tile repo + variation pipeline is load-bearing; and (e) THE WARNING THAT MATTERS MOST — great
  design does NOT propagate on merit. "Hardly anyone is learning from Ultima's attention to NPCs, open game
  worlds, sandbox engines, or original plots." The genre copied its INTERFACE and ignored its IDEAS for a
  decade. Our systems must be cheap enough to survive us.
