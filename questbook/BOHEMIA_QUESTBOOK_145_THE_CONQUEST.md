# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #145 — "THE CONQUEST (THE GAME BEFORE THE GAME)"
**Game:** Tyranny (2016)
**Studio:** Obsidian Entertainment
**Quest:** the Conquest — the pre-game sequence where the player adjudicates, region by region, how the Overlord's already-won war WENT: which banner they marched under, which cities burned, which laws were proclaimed, who surrendered and on what terms. The game proper then opens into a world those answers already shaped, and spends forty hours reading them back.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined from the whole-game teardown, Q25, per the mining-pool rule)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia is a roguelite with generations: every run BEGINS downstream of prior events (the fold, the parent generation, the world-seed). The Conquest is the medium's only full-scale machine for making pre-play history a PLAYED, OWNED thing rather than a lore dump — the exact tool a generational game needs for the moment an heir steps into a world their predecessor bent. It is also the banality-of-evil engine in miniature: authority exercised as paperwork, before a single sword is drawn on screen.

---

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The character creator asks what you look like. The Conquest asks what you did — and then the whole game holds you to it.**

Tyranny opens after the ending: evil won, the continent is conquered, and the player is a Fatebinder — a judge in the winner's legal machine. Before the first playable scene, the game runs the Conquest: an interactive map-chronicle of the three-year war of subjugation, in which the player makes region-by-region decisions AS the war unfolds in summary. Which of the two rival armies (the disciplined Disfavored, the feral Scarlet Chorus) did you attach your authority to in each campaign? Did the city yield to siege, trickery, or the Edict — the Overlord's reality-warping proclamation-magic — and did you counsel the Edict's use or argue against it? Who did you spare on the courthouse steps; who did you make an example of?

None of it is played as combat. All of it is played as JUDGMENT — option lists over a moving map, each answer phrased as something the Fatebinder did, said, or signed. Twenty minutes later the game starts, and the Conquest is not backstory: it is STATE. The rival armies' opinion of you opens pre-loaded from your campaign attachments. Cities you burned are ash on the map with survivors who remember the name of the judge who ordered it. An Edict you invoked is still executing — a permanent storm parked over a region because you once said the word — and one of the main game's arcs is going back to the weather you signed.

The design solves the oldest problem in role-playing: every RPG protagonist arrives with a past the player didn't author, so reactivity to backstory always reads as the writer's, not the player's. Tyranny makes the past PLAYER-AUTHORED at the resolution the systems actually consume — faction standings, regional states, named grudges — so when an NPC spits at you about the siege of a city, the accusation lands on a decision the player remembers making. Guilt with a receipt.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** you are never starting fresh. You are always arriving in the aftermath of choices — and the only question is whether they were yours, and whether you'll claim them either way.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THE FATEBINDER (the player, past tense) — the signature
- **What they want (in Conquest):** whatever the player decides they wanted — order, terror, advancement, restraint. The sequence is the one place the game lets the player author motive retroactively.
- **What they will never say out loud:** that they could have counseled differently. The game proper is full of NPCs saying it FOR them.
- **Function:** THE ESTATE (same slot as Q144's dying captain): the Conquest-self is a predecessor whose ledger the played-self inherits — the two are the same person, which is the design's twist on inheritance.

### THE DISFAVORED and THE SCARLET CHORUS — the two hands of the winner
- **What they want:** the Disfavored: order, hierarchy, the war remembered as discipline. The Chorus: growth by absorption, the war remembered as glorious appetite. Each wants the Fatebinder's Conquest record to prove the OTHER army was the problem.
- **Function:** the DUAL METER made flesh — every Conquest attachment pre-loads favor with one and wrath with the other, and the main game's civil war detonates along the line the player drew before playing.

### KYROS' EDICTS — the proclamations, cast as characters
- **What they want:** execution. An Edict is law as weather: proclaimed once, it persists — storms, fire, silence — until its written terminal condition is met.
- **What they will never say out loud:** their loophole. Every Edict ends by its own exact wording, and reading the law closely is endgame content.
- **Function:** THE SIGNATURE MADE PERMANENT. The Conquest lets the player counsel Edicts onto regions; the game makes them walk into the standing weather of their advice.

### THE CONQUERED — the remainder
- Cities, holdouts, named rebels whose Conquest fates (spared, razed, made example of) the game reads back through survivors.
- **Function:** WITNESSES with pre-play memory. The corpus's only NPCs who hold grudges from before the first scene.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE APPOINTMENT [MANDATORY]
Character creation proper, then the frame: you were there for the war; now say how it went. The interface is a chronicle-map with years advancing.

### STAGE 2 — THE CAMPAIGNS [MANDATORY, three years compressed]
Region by region: which army carried the campaign (attachment choice), how the stronghold fell (assault, subterfuge, siege, Edict), what the Fatebinder personally adjudicated (sparings, executions, proclamations read aloud). Each answer writes faction favor/wrath, regional state, and named flags. OPTIONAL nothing — every region resolves — but each region offers materially different verbs, and several outcomes exist only via specific combinations (the Edict-scarred regions, the city that never fell).
MISSABLE: entire world-states. A Conquest without the storm Edict produces a game without the storm region's arc as authored — the content budget openly forks at pre-play.

### STAGE 3 — THE READING BACK [THE ENTIRE MAIN GAME]
The game opens; the Conquest is now terrain, standings, and grudges. NPCs cite it. The rival armies weigh it. The Edicts you counseled still execute. Act-scale arcs (breaking an Edict by satisfying its wording) exist BECAUSE of stage 2 answers.

### STAGE 4 — THE OWNERSHIP FORK [DISTRIBUTED]
Dialogue throughout lets the player claim, regret, or double down on Conquest deeds — the past is fixed, the POSTURE toward it is played. The game grades posture separately from history: you cannot unburn the city; you can decide who the burner is now.

## 3 THE CONVERSATIONS (THE ACTUAL MACHINE)

### NODE C-1 — a campaign's attachment. Speaker: the chronicle. Entry: each region, stage 2.
The year advances; the armies move on a stronghold; the Fatebinder's authority must ride with someone.
> "March with the Disfavored" [gate: none] -> writes Disfavored favor, Chorus wrath, and the region remembers DISCIPLINE: occupation, order, resentment of the boot
> "Attach to the Scarlet Chorus" [gate: none] -> the inverse writes, and the region remembers APPETITE: absorption, chaos, resentment of the maw
> "Hold your own line between them" [gate: some regions only] -> both armies' wrath, independence flags — the costliest lane, priced honestly
LOOPS: each region re-asks with the standings SO FAR visible in the chronicle's tone — early attachments make later neutrality read as betrayal, and the interface lets the player feel the compounding before the game ever starts.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the other army's war. Both cannot be yours; the civil war to come is being loaded, region by region.

### NODE C-2 — the fall of a city. Entry: a stronghold resists.
> "Counsel patience: siege it out" [gate: none] -> slow fall; the city survives diminished; survivors remember hunger, not fire
> "Find the traitor inside" [gate: prior flags in some regions] -> the gate is EARLIER CONQUEST ANSWERS — pre-play choices gating pre-play choices; the sequence has its own knowledge economy
> "Counsel the Edict" [gate: none — and the interface makes it FEEL like the biggest button on the page] -> the proclamation falls; the region is TERRAIN-SCARRED for the whole game. TRAP in the honest register: maximally effective, permanently costly, and the game will make you visit.
> "Let it stand unconquered" [gate: one region's specific path] -> the rarest state: a place the war never finished. The main game treats it as a wound in the winner's story — and yours.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: the region's future content shape. The Conquest is openly a WORLD EDITOR wearing a chronicle's clothes, and the game trusts the player with it.

### NODE C-3 — the adjudications. Entry: interleaved; the personal scale.
Named individuals surface — a rebel commander taken alive, a mayor who bargained, a deserter.
> spare / conscript / execute / make an example [gates: none] -> each writes a NAMED flag; several of these people appear in the main game with the memory, and one Conquest mercy is famously repaid forty hours later
NOVERB "It wasn't my decision" — no such line exists in the Conquest, because the frame is judicial: the Fatebinder's presence IS jurisdiction. The sequence quietly removes the bystander verb from the player's past, which is the whole moral architecture: you were not a witness to the war; you were an officer of it.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: innocence by construction. The game proper can be played toward rebellion or mercy, but never from a clean start — the Conquest guarantees dirty hands as the initial condition.

### NODE C-4 — the reading back (main game, distributed). Speaker: survivors, armies, the weather.
> "I did what the law required." [gate: none] -> the loyalist posture; the armies read it as reliability
> "I was wrong at [the city]." [gate: the deed's flag] -> regret, priced: some listeners soften, the armies log weakness
> "I'd do it again." [gate: the deed's flag] -> the example-maker's posture; fear economy engaged
> (say nothing when cited) [gate: none] SILENCE -> the accuser's text CONTINUES, and several such scenes read silence as confession — DIFFERENT TEXT downstream. The Conquest's deeds cannot be outrun by quiet.
WHAT THIS NODE COSTS: nothing mechanical — everything tonal. History is fixed; the self who owns it is the remaining playable object.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: the studio's own stated space runs to hundreds of distinct Conquest world-states (attachment × fall-method × adjudications per region); the mechanically load-bearing axes are 3 per region (who carried it, how it fell, who was spared/made example), and the game reads every axis back somewhere.**

- **ATTACHMENT AXIS:** cumulative Disfavored/Chorus lean → opening standings, which army's civil-war overtures arrive warm, which arrive armed.
- **FALL-METHOD AXIS:** per-region terrain and arc shape; Edict-counseled regions carry standing proclamations whose BREAKING is main-game content. The branch that generates quests.
- **ADJUDICATION AXIS:** named survivors with pre-play memory; individual paybacks and grudges.
- **THE POSTURE LAYER (main game):** claim / regret / double down per cited deed — history fixed, ownership played. The Conquest's real ending is who the player decides the Conquest-self was.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE CONQUEST OUTRUNS THE BUDGET.** The sequence promises a hundred world-states; the shipped game reads back the big axes loudly and the fine grain thinly — players comparing notes found many Conquest differences cash out as a line or two. The front-loaded reactivity is real but SHALLOWER than the authoring interface implies, and the famous unfinished third act compounds it.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** never let the authoring surface promise more state than the reader consumes. If the player can set it, something in the world must SAY it — rule of thumb from this file: every pre-play choice needs at least one loud, unmissable read-back plus the systemic one, or it gets cut from the authoring surface. (The gate can count read-backs per seed-choice at compile.)

**F2 — HISTORY WITHOUT WEIGHT AT AUTHORING TIME.** Conquest choices are made in twenty minutes, before the player knows the world, the armies, or the stakes — so the deeds the game spends forty hours holding them to were chosen half-blind. Some players report the read-backs landing as accusations about a stranger's decisions.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** Bohemia's structure dissolves this — the "Conquest" of generation N is generation N-1's ACTUAL PLAY, chosen with full knowledge, at full weight. But the law still bites for run one: the founder generation's pre-history must be THIN by design (the world-seed authors terrain, never the founder's personal deeds), because unearned guilt reads as the writer's, not the player's. Personal ledgers start empty; only inherited WORLD state arrives pre-shaped.

**F3 — THE DUAL METER LEAKS INTO A SLIDER AT THE EDGES.** Favor/wrath is the anti-morality-meter, but reviewers and players documented edge cases where content gates check only one axis, quietly collapsing the two-axis idea back into like/dislike where it mattered most.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if Bohemia ships dual-axis standing (and the faction graph should — respected-and-resented is the truest post-collapse relationship), every gate that reads the relationship must be WRITTEN two-axis: checks name (favor, wrath) pairs, never a single collapsed value. Validator-checkable at compile once standings enter the .bq gate grammar.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE PAST IS PLAYER-AUTHORED AT SYSTEM RESOLUTION. The Conquest writes the exact currencies the game consumes — standings, regional states, named flags — so backstory reactivity lands on the player's own signature. Guilt with a receipt.
W2. AUTHORITY IS THE VERB SET. Every Conquest option is judicial — attach, counsel, adjudicate, proclaim — teaching the player their role's grammar before teaching them combat. The fantasy is jurisdiction, established in the sequence's verbs alone.
W3. THE BYSTANDER VERB IS REMOVED FROM THE PAST. No "I wasn't part of it" exists; presence is jurisdiction. Dirty hands as an initial condition is the boldest opening state in the genre.
W4. THE EDICT IS A SIGNATURE WITH WEATHER. Law that persists as terrain until its own wording releases it: the player's counsel becomes a standing feature of the map, and READING THE LAW CLOSELY becomes endgame content. Consequence you can stand in, then lawyer your way out of.
W5. COMPOUNDING IS VISIBLE AT AUTHORING TIME. The chronicle re-asks with prior answers echoing, so the player watches their lean harden into a reputation before the game starts — the fold's compounding, compressed into twenty minutes.
W6. HISTORY FIXED, POSTURE PLAYED. The main game grades ownership (claim/regret/double down) separately from deeds — the one authoring the past can still choose who its author became. The redemption architecture every guilt system needs.
W7. MERCY PLANTED BLIND PAYS OFF NAMED. Conquest sparings return as individuals with memory; the design banks player kindness before the player knows the currency. Q126's mercy-banked pattern, moved to pre-play.
W8. THE TWO ARMIES PRE-LOAD THE CIVIL WAR. The main conflict detonates along the attachment line the player drew — the game's central war is the player's own Conquest record, weaponized. Premise and player-state fused.
W9. WORLD-EDITING WEARS A CHRONICLE'S CLOTHES. The player is handed regional-content forks (Edict arcs exist or don't) inside a diegetic war summary — content authorship disguised as memory. The trust this extends is repaid in investment.
W10. THE OPENING IS AN AFTERMATH. Starting after the ending — evil won, now administrate — remains the sharpest premise inversion in the genre, and the Conquest is what makes it PERSONAL rather than decorative: the aftermath is specifically yours.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE SUCCESSION CHRONICLE [W1, W5, F2's law — the core port]
**System:** the fold / succession / generational persistence
Bohemia's generation N+1 opens with a Conquest-shaped scene that is READ, not authored: the chronicle of the parent generation's actual recorded play — attachments, adjudications, standing "edicts" (the deals and laws the dynast signed that still execute) — presented as the heir's briefing, with the POSTURE layer played live (claim / regret / disown each cited deed, writing the heir's opening stance flags). Tyranny authors the past in a menu; Bohemia authors it by having been played. The fold is the Conquest done honestly.
```
@TALK succession_briefing speaker=record_keeper entry=generation_start
  @SAY Before you walk out that door as the name, hear what the name did.
  @SAY [gate: fold:razed_entry] The east block. Your predecessor ordered it cleared. People remember the order and the voice. Some of them are still here.
  @OPT "The order stands. I'd have given it too."   [gate: none] -> posture_claim   @DO set_flag heir_claims
  @OPT "That was theirs, not mine. Say so."          [gate: none] -> posture_disown  @DO set_flag heir_disowns
  @OPT "It was wrong. Put that in the record."       [gate: none] -> posture_regret  @DO set_flag heir_regrets
  @OPT (say nothing)                                 [gate: none] SILENCE -> posture_silence @DO set_flag heir_silent
  @NOVERB "Change what happened"
@END
```
**THE NOVERB IS THE PORT:** history is fixed; only ownership is playable (W6). [PENDING, Paolo's call: what the record keeper IS — clerk, Amalgamation surface, or family rite.]

### PORT 2 — STANDING EDICTS IN THE VALLEY [W4]
**System:** territory systems / the survival economy / quest generation
The signed-thing-that-keeps-executing: dynast-level decisions (a water treaty, a quarantine, a bounty proclamation) persist as WORLD STATE with written terminal conditions, and later generations can end them only by satisfying or lawyering the original wording — the document itself becomes a quest object under [READ]. Mechanism mine; which proclamations exist is canon. [PENDING, Paolo's call.]

### PORT 3 — THE FOUNDER STARTS THIN [F2's law, made structural]
**System:** run structure / world-seed
Run one, generation one: the world-seed shapes TERRAIN and factions, never the founder's personal ledger. No pre-authored deeds, no inherited guilt for the first life — the conduct and fold books open empty, so that every read-back forever after lands on player-authored history only. This is a compile-time invariant worth a gate line: generation-1 fold ledgers must initialize empty.

### PORT 4 — TWO-AXIS STANDING, ENFORCED IN THE GRAMMAR [F3's law]
**System:** the 13 factions / faction graph / .bq gates
Adopt favor+wrath as separate faction axes (the graph's edges already carry typed relationships), and enforce F3's law at the format level: when standings enter the .bq gate grammar, gates name the axis explicitly (favor>=X, wrath>=Y, both), and the validator rejects any collapsed reputation check. Respected-and-resented must be a reachable, mechanically distinct state — it is the post-collapse default between survivors.

### PORT 5 — MERCY BANKED ACROSS THE LINE [W7]
**System:** the fold / NPC memory
Adjudication-class fold entries (spared, conscripted, made example) persist on NAMED NPCs across generations, and at least some repayments must skip a generation — the person the grandmother spared answers the grandchild's need. Tyranny pays mercy back in forty hours; Bohemia can pay it back in forty years, which no other game can do. [PENDING, Paolo's call: the canon cases.]
