# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #153 — "A NIGHT TO REMEMBER (THE BLACKOUT RECONSTRUCTION)"
**Game:** The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim (2011)
**Studio:** Bethesda Game Studios
**Quest:** A Night to Remember — Sanguine's Daedric quest. A drinking contest with a stranger ends in a total blackout; the player wakes across the province and must RECONSTRUCT the missing night by interviewing the people it wronged. The genre's best comedic quest that is secretly a detective story.
**Type:** INDIVIDUAL-QUEST DEEP DIVE (mined fresh 7/17 from the Skyrim catalogue; corpus anchor Q37/Q75 for the game's larger machines)
**FORMAT:** v2 — cast + conversation node trees + branch map.
**Filed:** 7/17/26
**Why pulled:** Bohemia needs a RELEASE-VALVE quest that is comedy on the surface and reconstruction underneath — the Yakuza tonal-range port (#13) given a concrete specimen. A Night to Remember is the model: the player is the culprit, the night is the mystery, and the humor comes from a straight investigation into your own off-screen chaos. It also rhymes hard with our themes — a blackout is an EDITED MEMORY you did to yourself, and the quest is the player piecing together what the record won't show.

## 0 CORE IDEA

**The player is both the detective and the criminal, and the crime scene is a night they cannot remember — comedy generated by treating your own blackout with total forensic sincerity.**

Sam Guevenne, a stranger in a tavern, offers a drinking contest. Accept, and the screen cuts to black. The player wakes in a ruined temple across the map with a splitting head, a priest demanding they leave, and no memory of how they got there or what the wedding ring on the altar has to do with anything. The quest is the reconstruction: follow the thread of your own wreckage backward — a hagraven who says you sold her a goat, a wedding you apparently agreed to and abandoned, a debt at a tavern you trashed, a giant you may have insulted — each witness handing you one more piece of a night the player experienced as a single cut to black.

The machine is dramatic irony inverted: normally the audience knows what the character doesn't; here the WORLD knows what the PLAYER doesn't, and every NPC is a witness who saw the version of you that you can't access. The humor lands because the quest never winks — the priest, the goat-seller, the jilted bride all treat the aftermath as a real grievance, and the player's straight-faced "I did WHAT?" is the joke. Sam turns out to be Sanguine, Daedric Prince of revelry, and the whole ruinous night was his idea of a good time and his job interview for you.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** you are responsible for the version of you that showed up when you weren't watching, and the only way to face it is to go ask the people it hurt.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### SAM GUEVENNE / SANGUINE — the stranger who is a god
- **What he wants (stated):** a drinking buddy and a good time.
- **What he wants (real):** to WATCH — the whole night was staged entertainment, and the player's mortified reconstruction is the show he stayed for.
- **What he'll trade:** the Sanguine Rose (the reward), freely, once the night is fully re-walked. He is not withholding; he is enjoying.
- **What he will never say out loud:** that he engineered every grievance. He lets the witnesses tell it.
- **Function:** THE AUTHOR of the chaos and its audience — the release-valve's engine, a god whose whole quest is a bender with a punchline.

### THE PRIEST OF DIBELLA (Witchmist Grove waking) — the first witness
- **What he wants:** the hungover stranger OUT of his ruined shrine.
- **What he'll trade:** the first clue — where the night's thread runs next.
- **Function:** THE OPENING WITNESS: the quest hands the player a grievance before it hands them a goal.

### THE JILTED BRIDE (Ysolda / the wedding thread) — the abandoned promise
- **What she wants:** the wedding the player apparently agreed to, or an explanation.
- **What she will never say out loud:** how much she meant it, played straight under the farce.
- **Function:** THE HUMAN COST inside the comedy — the one witness whose grievance has a real ache, keeping the quest from pure slapstick.

### THE WITNESSES (the goat-seller, the tavern, the giant) — the reconstruction
- **What they want:** restitution, an apology, or just the stranger gone.
- **What they'll trade:** each their fragment of the night.
- **Function:** THE CRIME SCENE, distributed across people: the night is reassembled from testimony, not flashback.

### THE PLAYER'S OWN BLACKOUT SELF — the absent culprit
- Cast as a character because the quest is about them: the person who showed up when the player wasn't driving.
- **Function:** THE MYSTERY and the mirror — you are investigating yourself.

## 2 FULL EVENT FLOW (STAGE BY STAGE)

### STAGE 1 — THE CONTEST [MANDATORY]
A tavern. Sam offers a drinking contest with a "staff" as the prize. Accept the drink; the screen cuts to black. The quest's inciting action is a VOLUNTARY loss of control — the player agrees to the blackout.

### STAGE 2 — THE WAKING [MANDATORY]
Wake in Witchmist Grove, a temple of Dibella across the map, hungover, a priest furious. No memory, no map marker to the truth — only the priest's grievance as the first thread.

### STAGE 3 — THE RECONSTRUCTION [MANDATORY, witnesses in sequence]
Follow the thread witness to witness: the abandoned wedding, the sold goat, the trashed tavern and its debt, the offended giant. Each interview is a comic beat AND a clue; the player assembles the night backward.

### STAGE 4 — THE THREAD'S END [MANDATORY]
The reconstruction leads to Sanguine's revelation: Sam was the Prince, the night was his sport, and the player passed the audition.

### STAGE 5 — THE REWARD [MANDATORY]
The Sanguine Rose, handed over with a god's approval. The bender was the job interview; the reconstruction was the résumé.

He wants the stranger out of his profaned shrine and is not interested in their confusion.

The jilted party, holding a grievance the player authored blind.

## 4 THE BRANCH MAP

**COUNT: 1 terminal (the Rose earned), times the witness-order the player walks, times how much restitution they choose to offer — and 0 branches on the outcome, by design: the night happened, and the only variable is how the player carries it.**

- **THE RECONSTRUCTION:** always completes; the humor and the reward are invariant. WRITES: the Sanguine Rose, and a player who has faced their off-screen self.
- **THE RESTITUTION AXIS:** at each witness, the player may make amends or shrug — no mechanical consequence, only self-portrait. The comedy has a conscience slot the game never forces.
- **THE HUMAN NOTE:** the wedding thread carries a real ache under the farce; whether the player treats it kindly is unrecorded and entirely theirs.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE RECONSTRUCTION IS A CORRIDOR.** The witnesses come in a fixed order and the player cannot investigate wrong, reach a false conclusion, or miss a beat — the "detective" work is a guided tour of pre-written comedy. The mystery cannot fail, so it is a delivery mechanism, not a deduction.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** a reconstruction quest must be ABLE to assemble wrong — the player accuses their own blackout self of something they didn't do, or misses a witness and carries a false version — with the world recording the gap. A blackout you can only reassemble one correct way is a cutscene with steps.

**F2 — THE HARM HAS NO WEIGHT.** The jilted bride, the sold goat, the trashed tavern — none of it persists; the world resets, no witness stays wronged, the wedding thread's ache evaporates on quest completion. The comedy is consequence-free, which is safe and also thin.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** at least one grievance from a blackout must STICK — a person who stays hurt, a debt that stays owed, a reputation the district remembers. Release-valve comedy earns its lightness by letting one thing not be funny.

**F3 — THE VOLUNTARY BLACKOUT ISN'T INTERROGATED.** The player CHOOSES to drink into oblivion and the quest never asks why or costs them the choosing — the loss of control is pure setup, never a theme. For a game about edited memory, that is a missed depth.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a Bohemia quest offers a voluntary blackout (drink, a memory edit, a fold-skip), the CHOICE to lose control should itself be a recorded act with a cost — the player elected not to be present, and something happened in their absence they now own.

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE PLAYER IS THE CRIMINAL. The mystery is your own off-screen night, so the detective work is self-confrontation — the rarest and best investigation subject: yourself, unwatched.
W2. THE WORLD KNOWS WHAT YOU DON'T. Inverted dramatic irony: every NPC is a witness to a you that you can't access, and their straight-faced grievances are the comedy engine.
W3. SINCERITY IS THE JOKE. The quest never winks; the priest and the bride treat the aftermath as real, and the player's mortified reconstruction lands because nobody in it is laughing.
W4. THE CRIME SCENE IS TESTIMONY. The night is reassembled from people, not flashback — a social reconstruction that makes the witnesses the level design.
W5. THE BLACKOUT IS UNRECOVERABLE DIRECTLY. No flashback verb exists; the player must go ask, which turns a memory gap into a tour of the people it touched.
W6. THE HUMAN NOTE UNDER THE FARCE. The jilted wedding keeps the comedy from weightlessness — one witness whose grievance has an ache, played almost straight.
W7. THE BENDER IS A JOB INTERVIEW. Sanguine was auditioning the player through chaos; the reconstruction was the résumé — the reward reframes the whole night as a test passed.
W8. SCOPE DISCIPLINE. Contest to Rose in one arc; the comedy never dilutes because the container is tight — the release valve does one thing perfectly.
W9. RESTITUTION IS OPTIONAL AND UNJUDGED. At each witness the player may make amends or not, with no mechanical push — the conscience slot is real and entirely theirs.
W10. THE TONE IS A COUNTERWEIGHT. Placed among Skyrim's grim Daedric quests, the levity relieves and, by contrast, sharpens the dread of the darker ones. Range is the point.

## 7 BOHEMIA PORTS

### PORT 1 — THE BLACKOUT RECONSTRUCTION QUEST [W1, W4, W5 — the core port]
**System:** the release-valve quest layer / the unrecorded ledger / dialogue scenes
A Bohemia quest where the dynast loses a night (drink, a fever, a fold-skip, an Amalgamation edit) and must RECONSTRUCT it by interviewing the people it touched — comedy on the surface, a memory-gap detective story underneath, deeply on-theme for a game about the edited record.
```
@TALK blackout_witness speaker=neighbor entry=night_lost
  @SAY You don't remember. Of course you don't. You were three sheets and singing.
  @SAY You owe Mira a door and the whole row an apology, so start there.
  @OPT "I did what, exactly?"        [gate: none] -> witness_account
  @OPT "Let me make it right."        [gate: none] -> witness_amends
  @OPT "That doesn't sound like me."  [gate: none] -> witness_insist
  @NOVERB "Let me just remember it myself"
@END
```
**THE NOVERB IS THE PORT:** no flashback verb; the lost night is reassembled SOCIALLY or carried as a gap. The blackout as an edited memory the player did to themselves — the Amalgamation's horror in a comic key. [PENDING, Paolo's call: what a Bohemia blackout's trigger is.]

### PORT 2 — THE RECONSTRUCTION THAT ASSEMBLES WRONG [F1's law]
**System:** the missing-persons organ (#141) / the fold
The reconstruction can conclude FALSE — the dynast pieces together a version of their lost night that didn't happen, accuses their own blackout self of the wrong grievance, and the fold records both the believed version and the true one. Any later system may surface the gap. A blackout you can only reassemble correctly is a corridor; ours can be honestly misremembered.

### PORT 3 — ONE GRIEVANCE STICKS [F2's law]
**System:** faction memory / the conduct book (#143)
Most of a blackout's harm is comic and resets; ONE grievance persists — a person who stays hurt, a debt the district remembers, a reputation dinged. The release valve earns its lightness by letting exactly one thing not be funny, and the fold carries it.

### PORT 4 — CHOOSING TO NOT BE PRESENT [F3's law]
**System:** the fold / the dial / conscience
When a Bohemia quest offers a VOLUNTARY loss of control (drink to oblivion, elect a memory edit, skip a fold beat), the choosing is itself a recorded act with a cost: the dynast elected absence, and something happened while they were gone that they now own. The voluntary blackout as a moral act, not just a setup.
