# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 74: THE PROPHECY MIGHT BE A LIE / EARNING A DESTINY BY CONVINCING PEOPLE (Morrowind — the Main Quest)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the deliberately-ambiguous chosen-one, the Dagoth Ur "are you REALLY
Nerevar?" dialogue with four valid answers, "each Event is preceded by Prophecy — but without the Hero,
there is no Event," the contested history (the victors narrate), the Cavern of the Incarnate's dead
predecessors, mantling, and the structure where you EARN a destiny by CONVINCING people. The greatest
"is the prophecy real, or are you being used?" arc in RPG history. DIRECTLY relevant to our chosen-one
skepticism, our [READ]/contested-history, + our faction politics. Bethesda (Ken Rolston, Michael Kirkbride,
Kurt Kuhlmann). Reference only; Paolo does not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

Quest: The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind's main quest (Bethesda, 2002). You are a PRISONER — "born on a
certain day to uncertain parents" — released from an Imperial jail on the Emperor's personal orders,
shipped to Vvardenfell with 87 gold, common clothes, and a sealed package for a man you've never heard of.
The island is dying of a Blight plague spread by DAGOTH UR, a god-thing under a volcano. Local legend says
the ancient hero-king Indoril Nerevar will be REBORN to save Morrowind. The Empire thinks that might be
you. So might you. Maybe.

===============================================================================
## 0. THE CORE IDEA (why it's in the canon)
===============================================================================
- THE PROPHECY MIGHT BE COMPLETELY FAKE — AND IT DOESN'T MATTER (the thesis): "In Morrowind, it's possible
  that you are SOME SCHMUCK WHO GOT REALLY LUCKY. The ancient prophecy might be little more than a LIE or
  local superstition. And even if that were the case, IT STILL MIGHT NOT MATTER." The game presents
  multiple CONFLICTING interpretations of your relationship to the prophecy and deliberately refuses to
  resolve it. Compare: "Consider Skyrim. You are the goddamn Dragonborn. You have magic no one else has."
  Morrowind gives you a maybe (cf. our chosen-one skepticism + our dynasty's self-made legitimacy; the
  anti-chosen-one).
- "EACH EVENT IS PRECEDED BY PROPHECY. BUT WITHOUT THE HERO, THERE IS NO EVENT." (the epigraph = the
  design): the game's opening quote IS its thesis — prophecy doesn't MAKE the hero; the hero makes the
  prophecy true by DOING it. TV Tropes reads it exactly: "The Chosen One's success is a matter of the
  hero's OWN ABILITY, rather than being The Chosen One." Destiny is a description written after the fact
  (cf. our earned-standing/self-made dynasty; prophecy as retroactive narration).
- YOU'RE NOT THE FIRST — THE CAVERN OF THE INCARNATE IS FULL OF THE ONES WHO FAILED (the gut-punch): the
  quest takes you to a cavern holding the GHOSTS OF PREVIOUS "Nerevarines" — other people who were ALSO
  chosen, who ALSO believed, and who ALL DIED. "You are not the only 'chosen one' at the Cavern of the
  Incarnate; all who failed even though they were The Chosen One, because their skills (or reasoning) was
  not enough." The prophecy has a body count of believers. You might just be the next corpse (cf. our
  fold — generations who tried and failed before; the failed predecessors).

## 1. THE ARCHITECTURE (how you earn a destiny)
===============================================================================

### THE OPENING GIVES YOU NOTHING (the anti-tutorial — key craft)
- You are released with: 87 gold, common clothes, a hand-written NOTE with DIRECTIONS to a man named Caius
  Cosades, and a sealed PACKAGE of CIPHERED TEXT you can't read. The note "does not give a clue to who
  Caius Cosades might be." UESP's own summary: "With these two documents, 87 gold, and the common clothes
  on your back, YOU MAY SET OFF IN ANY DIRECTION, FREELY IGNORING THE REMAINDER OF THE MAIN QUEST." The
  main quest is OPTIONAL, unmarked, and starts with a piece of paper (cf. Q37's Morrowind directions
  teardown; the world as its own tutorial).
### YOUR HANDLER IS A SPY WITH A DRUG HABIT (the deflating mentor)
- Caius Cosades — the leader of the Emperor's secret police on the island — is a shirtless, skooma-addicted
  man in a shack in Balmora. He doesn't know if you're the Nerevarine either. His "errands" are him
  "tugging on threads to get to the bottom of the Nerevarine Prophecy." Your mentor is investigating YOU,
  in real time, with no more certainty than you have. And eventually he's RECALLED to the Imperial City —
  leaving you in charge, mid-prophecy, with no boss (cf. our faction handlers; the mentor who's guessing).
### THE HISTORY IS CONTESTED, AND THE VICTORS WROTE IT (our [READ], in 2002)
- The central historical event — how Nerevar died — has TWO irreconcilable accounts:
  - THE TRIBUNAL TEMPLE (the ruling church) says: Dagoth Ur BETRAYED Nerevar by seizing the god-making
    tools, and Nerevar died of his wounds.
  - THE ASHLANDER PROPHECY (the outlawed tribal faith) says: Nerevar's own ADVISORS betrayed + MURDERED
    him, then used the tools to make themselves GODS — and those advisors are the Tribunal, who now rule
    as living deities and outlaw the prophecy.
  - The guide's own line: "As for which is true, THE VICTORS OF WAR ARE THE ONES WHO NARRATE HISTORY."
  - You uncover the truth in-game by digging through suppressed texts (the "Lost Prophecies," Mehra Milo's
    hidden library). The state religion is BUILT ON A LIE it enforces (cf. our recorded-vs-unrecorded/
    [READ] + the Amalgamation's edited record; contested history as the plot).
### YOU BECOME THE CHOSEN ONE BY *CONVINCING PEOPLE* (the structural masterstroke)
- The prophecy has SEVEN trials, but the middle of the game is pure POLITICS: to be the Nerevarine you must
  be NAMED so by the four Ashlander tribes (Urshilaku, Ahemmusa, Zainab, Erabenimsun) AND named HORTATOR
  (war-leader) by all three Great Houses (Redoran, Hlaalu, Telvanni). Seven separate factions must each be
  personally persuaded — by quests, gifts, murder, bribery, blackmail, or diplomacy, each with its own
  price. Nobody hands you a destiny. You go door to door and ARGUE for it. Your "prophecy" is a political
  campaign (STARTLINGLY our standing-as-progression-spine; cf. Gothic/Mount&Blade Q51/Q61; destiny as
  faction politics).
### THE CORPRUS TWIST (the curse that's the cure)
- To fulfill the prophecy you must deliberately CONTRACT CORPRUS — Dagoth Ur's incurable divine disease
  that turns people into mindless, deformed monsters. A hermit cures the mind-rot but NOT the body's
  changes — leaving you with the "positive" effects: immunity to all disease and NO AGING. You become
  functionally IMMORTAL by catching the villain's plague on purpose. The prophecy's requirement is to take
  the enemy's corruption INTO YOURSELF and survive it (cf. our Amalgamation-bargain/temptation; the poison
  that makes you able to face the poisoner).
### THE MOMENT: DAGOTH UR ASKS YOU IF YOU'RE REAL (the best dialogue in the game)
- At the end, the villain — an old friend of the man you might be — asks: are you TRULY Nerevar reborn?
  Kotaku: "This moment is the moment that makes Morrowind great." Your options:
  - YES, I am Nerevar reborn.
  - NO — I'm a loyal agent of the EMPIRE (you're a tool of an occupying government).
  - NO — I'm my OWN, SELF-MADE hero (the prophecy has no claim on me).
  - I DON'T KNOW.
  - "All it took was a simple dialog tree." The game's entire thesis is resolved by letting the PLAYER
    decide what they believe — and the ending happens regardless (cf. our finales + Megaton law; the
    identity question answered by the player, not the plot).
### MANTLING: "WALK LIKE THEM UNTIL THEY MUST WALK LIKE YOU" (the metaphysical loophole)
- The lore's own escape hatch: "In completing the main quest, it is entirely possible that you acted like
  Nerevar SO HARD that you BECAME him regardless" of whether you started as him. Doing the deeds MAKES the
  identity real. Even the "fake" answer produces a true Nerevarine (cf. our BECOME finale + upbringing;
  identity as accumulated action).
### AZURA FREES YOU AT THE END (the reward is release, not glory)
- After Dagoth Ur falls, the goddess Azura tells the Nerevarine they are FREE FROM THE BURDEN OF PROPHECY.
  The final prize isn't a throne — it's being let out of the story (cf. our finales; freedom from destiny
  as the reward).
### THE FAILSAFE: "THE THREAD OF PROPHECY IS SEVERED" (you can break the world)
- Kill an essential NPC (Vivec, etc.) and the game prints: "With this character's death, the thread of
  prophecy is severed. Restore a saved game to restore the weave of fate, OR PERSIST IN THE DOOMED WORLD
  YOU HAVE CREATED." It does NOT stop you. And astonishingly, there's a hidden BACKUP PATH (Yagrum Bagarn +
  Wraithguard) that lets you finish the main quest EVEN IF you murdered a god and broke the prophecy.
  Bethesda let you break the world AND still win (cf. our no-brick-wall + Megaton; the recoverable
  catastrophe).

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- THE POLITICAL MIDDLE IS AN ENORMOUS ERRAND-GRIND: seven factions, each needing their own quest-chains,
  is dozens of hours of fetch-work + persuasion checks + tribal favors. Brilliant in concept; a slog in
  practice for many. LESSON: "earn your legitimacy from every faction" is thematically perfect but
  EXPENSIVE — the connective tissue must stay interesting or it's a checklist (our faction-standing design;
  cf. Ranni's fetch-chain sag Q71).
- YOU CAN SKIP THE ENTIRE THEME: the wiki notes flatly — "It is possible to SKIP these two quests and go
  straight to defeating Dagoth Ur." The whole become-the-Nerevarine political campaign, the game's actual
  thesis, is OPTIONAL. Players can miss the point entirely + still win. LESSON: if your theme lives in
  optional content, most players never receive it (our lesson-under-the-decision must sit on the critical
  path — Dead Money's opposite lesson Q72).
- THE AMBIGUITY IS ARGUABLY UNDERCUT BY THE EVIDENCE: TV Tropes lists the case that it's just PLAYED
  STRAIGHT — the Emperor's prophetic dreams singled you out; Dagoth Ur recognizes you BEFORE you've done
  anything; the Moon-and-Star ring (which kills impostors) doesn't kill you; Azura says you're Nerevar
  reborn; Nerevar's own WIFE calls you Nerevarine. The "maybe you're nobody" reading requires ignoring a
  lot. LESSON: ambiguity needs the evidence to actually BALANCE, or it's just a fig leaf on a chosen-one
  story (our chosen-one skepticism must be real, not decorative).
- THE PLOT DEPENDS ON THE VILLAIN BEING PASSIVE: the guide's own complaint about the genre — "the bad guys
  are aware of said Chosen One yet do nothing to eliminate them until after the Chosen One is strong enough
  to oppose them. And it's like, why, though?" (Morrowind partly answers this — Dagoth Ur WANTS you back,
  and the Tribunal DID try to kill previous Nerevarines — but it's still a strain.) LESSON: if our
  antagonist is competent + aware, it must have a REASON not to crush the dynasty early (our Amalgamation
  needs a motive for restraint).
- IT ASSUMES YOU'LL READ (a lot): the truth lives in in-game BOOKS — suppressed prophecies, conflicting
  histories, the Apographa. Players who don't read never learn the Tribunal are murderers. LESSON: if the
  revelation is in optional text, gate SOME of it into the critical path (our [READ] must have a spine).

===============================================================================

    the game refuses to resolve it (cf. our chosen-one skepticism, our self-made dynasty).

    prophecy has a body count of believers (cf. our fold; the generations who tried before).

    vs-unrecorded/[READ] + the Amalgamation's edited record).
W5. YOU BECOME THE CHOSEN ONE BY CONVINCING PEOPLE: seven factions must each personally NAME you — by
    quests, gifts, bribery, murder or diplomacy. Destiny is a political campaign (cf. our standing-as-
    progression-spine, Gothic/Mount&Blade Q51/Q61).
W6. THE MENTOR IS GUESSING TOO (and then leaves): a skooma-addicted spy investigating whether you're real,
    who gets recalled and hands you the whole operation (cf. our faction handlers).
W7. THE CURSE IS THE CURE (Corprus): you must deliberately catch the villain's divine plague — surviving
    it makes you immortal; you take the enemy's corruption INTO yourself to face him (cf. our Amalgamation-
    bargain/temptation).
W8. THE VILLAIN ASKS IF YOU'RE REAL — AND YOU ANSWER: yes / I'm the Empire's tool / I'm self-made / I don't

    even the "fake" answer produces a real hero (cf. our BECOME finale + upbringing; identity as accumulated action).
W10. THE REWARD IS RELEASE, NOT GLORY + YOU CAN BREAK THE WORLD AND STILL WIN: Azura frees you FROM the
     prophecy; and if you murder a god, the game says "persist in the doomed world you have created" — and
     STILL gives you a hidden path to finish (cf. our finales + no-brick-wall).

## 4. BOHEMIA PORTS (directions, not built) — the anti-chosen-one + destiny as faction politics
===============================================================================
Morrowind's main quest is the best "is the prophecy real or are you being used?" arc ever built, and its
structure — you EARN a destiny by convincing seven factions, in a world whose official history is a
enforced lie — maps almost 1:1 onto our standing-as-spine + our [READ]/recorded-vs-unrecorded + our
Amalgamation's edited record.
- W1/W2/W9 (the prophecy might be fake + "without the hero there is no Event" + mantling — our dynasty's
  legitimacy): bank the anti-chosen-one as our model. Our dynasty should have NO divine right — any claim
  (that they're the ones who can face the Amalgamation, that the bloodline matters) should be ARGUABLE,
  possibly self-serving myth, possibly true, and the game should refuse to settle it. And bank MANTLING for
  our BECOME finale + upbringing: acting the part hard enough MAKES it real — a dynasty that behaves like
  the district's rightful stewards BECOMES them, regardless of where they started (ties our fold +
  upbringing + BECOME finale + standing; destiny as accumulated action, not birthright).
- W5 (you become the chosen one by CONVINCING PEOPLE — our standing-as-spine, realized): the single most
  direct port. Morrowind's middle act IS our standing system: seven factions must each PERSONALLY name you,
  each with its own price (quests, gifts, bribery, blackmail, murder, diplomacy). Bank it as the model for
  our 13 FACTIONS — legitimacy is not granted, it's CAMPAIGNED for, door to door, faction by faction, and
  the Pacifist Law means every door has multiple keys (ties our standing-as-progression-spine Q51/Q61, our
  faction web + Pacifist Law + fold; destiny as a political campaign — a top-tier structural port).
- W4 (contested history + the victors wrote it — our [READ]/Amalgamation): STARTLINGLY on-theme. Bank the
  structure: our world's FOUNDING EVENT (the collapse, the Amalgamation's creation, who did what) should
  have TWO+ irreconcilable accounts — one enforced by a ruling power (the Network/a faction) and one
  outlawed/suppressed — with the truth recoverable only by digging through suppressed records. The
  Amalgamation IS an edited record of the dead; a state built on an enforced lie about the collapse is our
  premise with a spine (ties our recorded-vs-unrecorded + [READ] + Amalgamation + Network + faction web;
  Caves of Qud's biased-history Q56 + Golden Idol's reconstruction Q54 + this = our [READ]'s full blueprint).
- W3 (the failed predecessors): bank the gut-punch — our fold should let a dynast find the ones who TRIED
  BEFORE (previous generations, previous claimants, whole bloodlines that believed and died). Our ~100-year
  arc makes this literal + devastating: the cavern of failed Incarnates is our own family tree (ties our
  fold + generational persistence + [READ]; the body count of believers).
- W7 (the curse is the cure — our Amalgamation temptation): STEAL Corprus — to face the Amalgamation, the
  dynasty may have to take its corruption INTO themselves (interface with it, be recorded by it, accept its
  gift) and SURVIVE it — the poison that makes you able to face the poisoner, with permanent costs +
  benefits (ties our Amalgamation-bargain Q70/Q72, our BECOME finale + conscience system; deeply on-theme).
- W8/W10 (the villain asks if you're real + the reward is RELEASE, not glory): bank both for our finale —
  the AMALGAMATION should ask the dynasty who they actually are, with multiple valid answers (I'm the
  district's rightful heir / I'm a tool of a faction / I'm self-made, your myth has no claim on me / I
  don't know) — the thesis resolved by the PLAYER, with the ending happening regardless. And bank
  RELEASE-as-reward: freedom FROM the burden (of the fold, the prophecy, the dead) is a more interesting
  prize than a throne — that's our Liberate register (ties Mask of the Betrayer/FFX/Ranni Q64/Q68/Q71,
  our Liberate/Respect/Become + Megaton law).
- W6 (the mentor is guessing too): bank the deflating handler — a Bohemia faction contact who is
  INVESTIGATING the dynasty's significance in real time, has no more certainty than the player, and
  eventually LEAVES, handing over the whole operation (ties our faction web + companions; the mentor who's
  guessing).
- FLAWS (bank HARD): (a) "earn legitimacy from every faction" is thematically perfect but EXPENSIVE —
  seven faction-campaigns became a grind; our 13 factions must have INTERESTING connective tissue or it's
  a checklist (Ranni's fetch-sag Q71); (b) DON'T put our theme in optional content — Morrowind lets you
  SKIP the entire become-the-Nerevarine campaign (its own thesis!) and still win; our lesson-under-the-
  decision must sit on the CRITICAL PATH (Dead Money's opposite Q72); (c) our ambiguity must be REAL, not
  decorative — Morrowind stacks so much evidence FOR the prophecy that the "maybe you're nobody" reading
  needs squinting (balance the evidence honestly); (d) our AMALGAMATION must have a REASON not to crush the
  dynasty early (a competent, aware antagonist needs a motive for restraint — O'Dimm's bargain-rules Q70 is
  the model); and (e) our [READ] must have a SPINE — if the revelation is only in optional text, gate some
  of it onto the critical path.
