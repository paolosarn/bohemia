# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK #209 — "DREAM ON (A COUPLE ARE BEING REWRITTEN IN THEIR SLEEP AND THE ONLY WAY TO PROVE IT MIGHT BE TO STOP)"
**Game:** Cyberpunk 2077 (2020)
**Studio:** CD Projekt Red
**Quest:** the Peralez arc (Dream On) — a powerful, idealistic political couple hire the player to investigate strange nightly break-ins with nothing stolen, and the trail leads to the terrifying discovery that they are being systematically kidnapped, mind-wiped, and reprogrammed by a hidden power every single night, so completely that they cannot know it is happening, and the player must decide whether telling them the truth is a rescue or a cruelty that changes nothing.

**Why pulled:** Bohemia needs the paranoia quest where the victims are being rewritten so thoroughly they can't detect it, the puppeteer is invisible and unbeatable, and the only "help" the player can offer is a truth that helps no one and may itself be part of the trap. ## 0 CORE IDEA

**An idealistic, reformist political couple — genuinely trying to do good in a corrupt city — hire the player to investigate a disturbing pattern: intruders enter their heavily secured home at night but steal nothing, and the player, following the trail, uncovers a horror far past burglary: the couple are being abducted, mind-wiped, and reprogrammed every single night by a hidden, faceless power, their memories and even their political will systematically rewritten so seamlessly that they wake believing they are themselves and cannot possibly know it is happening, and the player is left with a truth that has no villain to confront, no cage to break, and no clear mercy in telling.**

Jefferson and Elizabeth Peralez are, unusually for their world, sincere: a councilman and his wife who want to reform Night City, who believe in something. They hire the player because something is wrong in their home — break-ins that take nothing, gaps they can't explain. The investigation descends. The truth is that the couple are not being robbed; they are being edited. Every night, a concealed power extracts them, wipes them, rewrites their memories and their intentions, and returns them, so that the reformist councilman's very will is being authored by the thing that owns him, and neither he nor his wife can detect it, because the rewrite is too complete — the edited self feels exactly like the real self from inside. There is no villain to shoot: the power behind it is faceless, possibly an AI, possibly the city's deepest machinery, unreachable and unbeatable. And the player ends holding the worst kind of knowledge: a truth about two people that they cannot act on to help them, cannot prove to them without being disbelieved, and cannot tell without possibly making things worse, because knowing you are a puppet does nothing to cut the strings.

The machine is that the victims are rewritten so completely that the truth cannot free them, and the puppeteer is so hidden that there is nothing to fight — so the player's only power is a knowledge that helps no one. Every rescue fantasy assumes that exposing the cage frees the captive. This quest denies it on two axes. First, the reprogramming is total: telling the Peralezes they are being rewritten does not un-rewrite them; they will be edited again tomorrow night, and the telling itself might just be overwritten, or worse, might mark the player. Second, the enemy is faceless: there is no boss, no server to blow up, no confrontation available — the power is the city's own hidden hand, and it cannot be reached. So the player is left with the specific despair of true, useless knowledge: they alone see the strings, and seeing them changes nothing. The quest lets the player tell the Peralezes (a truth that terrifies and helps not at all, and puts a target on everyone), or keep it (a mercy that is also abandonment, leaving two people to be authored by their owner forever), and it refuses to provide the catharsis of a fightable villain, because the real horror of being controlled is not a tyrant you can name but a hand you can never find.

**The life lesson underneath, never spoken:** the most complete kind of control is the kind the controlled cannot detect, and knowing you are a puppet does nothing to cut the strings — some cages have no bars to break and no jailer to fight, only a hidden hand, and there is a specific despair in holding a true and useless knowledge about someone you cannot free, where telling them helps nothing and only spreads the target, and the surest sign of total control is that the controlled feel completely free.

## 1 CAST + WHAT EACH ONE WANTS

### THE PERALEZES — the couple rewritten every night
- **What they want (stated):** to find out what's wrong in their home; to keep doing good in the city.
- **What they don't know:** that their memories and their very will are being authored nightly by a hidden power.
- **What they cannot detect:** the rewrite, because the edited self feels exactly like the real self.
- **Function:** THE UNDETECTABLY CONTROLLED: the victims rewritten so completely the truth cannot free them.

### THE HIDDEN POWER — the faceless, unbeatable puppeteer
- The concealed force (possibly an AI, possibly the city's deepest machinery) editing the couple nightly.
- **Function:** THE HAND YOU CAN'T FIND: the enemy with no face, no boss, no confrontation available.

### THE PLAYER (V) — the one holding useless truth
- **What they want:** to help the couple; to find a villain to fight (there is none) or a cage to break (there is none).
- **Function:** THE HOLDER OF USELESS KNOWLEDGE: the one who alone sees the strings, and can't cut them.

### THE TRUTH — the knowledge that helps no one
- The fact of the nightly rewrite; true, unprovable to the victims, dangerous to speak, useless to act on.
- **Function:** THE UNACTIONABLE TRUTH: the knowledge that frees no one and endangers everyone who holds it.

### THE APPEARANCE OF FREEDOM — the sign of total control
- The Peralezes' conviction that they are themselves, acting freely, doing good.
- **Function:** THE PROOF OF THE CAGE: the felt freedom that is the surest evidence of complete control.

## 5 HONEST FLAWS (BANKED)

**F1 — THE FACELESS ENEMY CAN READ AS AN UNFINISHED PLOT RATHER THAN DESIGNED HELPLESSNESS.** The refusal to provide a confrontable villain is the point, but it can feel like the quest just doesn't resolve — a thread left dangling — rather than the deliberate horror of an unbeatable hidden hand, so the intended despair reads as an anticlimax or a sequel hook.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest denies a confrontable villain on purpose, the FACELESSNESS must be framed as the horror, not a gap — the unreachability made legibly the point — so it reads as "you can never find the hand," not "the quest forgot to finish" (#182's the-absence-must-be-owned law).

**F2 — THE PLAYER'S INABILITY TO ACT CAN DRAIN THE QUEST OF STAKES.** Because nothing the player does frees the Peralezes, the quest risks feeling stakes-less — if the player can't affect anything, why care — so the helplessness needs to be made emotionally weighty (the horror of the couple's fate, the burden of the knowledge) rather than just mechanically inert.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's point is the player's helplessness, the EMOTIONAL stakes must carry what the mechanical ones can't — the horror and the burden made felt — so powerlessness is devastating, not empty. Helplessness without weight is just a dead end (#205's the-powerlessness-must-weigh law).

**F3 — "THE TRUTH HELPS NO ONE" CAN BE UNDERCUT IF TELLING SEEMS OBVIOUSLY RIGHT OR WRONG.** The tell-or-keep choice loses its edge if the game frames one option as clearly correct; the power depends on both being genuinely bad (telling is cruel and useless; keeping is abandonment), so if either reads as the "good" choice, the uselessness of the truth is lost.
**LAW FOR BOHEMIA:** if a quest's choice is between a useless truth and a merciful silence, BOTH must be genuinely bad — telling cruel and futile, keeping an abandonment — so neither is the clean answer and the uselessness of the knowledge lands (#181's both-options-must-be-bad law).

## 6 WHY IT WORKS (W1-W10)

W1. THE CONTROL IS UNDETECTABLE. The Peralezes are rewritten so completely they can't know it — the most total control, which is the whole engine.
W2. KNOWING DOESN'T CUT THE STRINGS. The truth frees no one; detecting the cage doesn't open it — the removed "expose them" verb is the spine.
W3. THE ENEMY IS FACELESS. No boss, no server to destroy, no confrontation — the hand you can never find, which denies all catharsis.
W4. THE TRUTH IS USELESS AND DANGEROUS. The player's only power is a knowledge that helps no one and endangers everyone who holds it.
W5. FEELING FREE IS THE PROOF OF THE CAGE. The couple's conviction that they act freely is the surest sign of total control — the horror's cruelest inversion.
W6. THE VICTIMS ARE SINCERE. The Peralezes genuinely want to do good — their idealism, authored by their owner, makes the violation deeper.
W7. THE REWRITE IS NIGHTLY AND TOTAL. Every night undoes any progress — there is no accumulating toward freedom, only endless re-authoring.
W8. TELLING SPREADS THE TARGET. Speaking the truth endangers the teller and the told — knowledge as contagion, not liberation.
W9. THE MERCY IS ABANDONMENT. Keeping the truth leaves them controlled and content — even kindness is a form of leaving them to it.
W10. IT IS REWRITE-THE-MIND WITHOUT A VILLAIN. Total control by a hand you can't find — Bohemia's whole Amalgamation question of the authored self, with no boss to kill.
