# BOHEMIA QUESTBOOK — DEEP DIVE 119: THE TWO-HOUR SHOT, AND THE TAPE THAT SAYS WHY HER FATHER DIED (Assist Mikhaila Ilyushin / Mikhaila's Father, Prey 2017)
Full teardown, the whole enchilada: the two-part side quest where a woman's legs stop working on a two-hour
timer, the medicine is in her own office on the other side of a hull breach, and the reward for saving her
is a second quest where you find the recording of her father's death and decide, alone, whether she gets to
hear it. Complete event flow, the timer, the spacewalk, the ring, the archive terminal, the honest flaws,
and Bohemia ports. STARTLINGLY relevant to our MEDICINE currency, our [READ], our companions, our conscience
system, our survival accounting, + our recorded-vs-unrecorded. Arkane Studios. Reference only; Paolo does
not read it. No Bohemia quest written here.

## 2. THE HONEST FLAWS (banked)
===============================================================================
- "ONLY SLIGHTLY" IS THE WHOLE PROBLEM: two separate guides say the outcome barely matters — "**WHETHER YOU
  SAVE HER OR NOT DOES INFLUENCE THE END OF THE GAME, ONLY SLIGHTLY.**" The after-credits payoff is real,
  but a player who read a guide first is told their choice is nearly cosmetic. LESSON (ours, sharp): if the
  wiki can say "only slightly," your consequence system is a rounding error to everyone who reads before
  playing — our persistent consequence must be **legible enough that a guide has to describe it, not
  dismiss it.**
- THE QUEST IS BUGGED: "**SPEAK TO MIKHAILA MORE TO INITIATE MIKHAILA'S FATHER (**NOTE THE QUEST MAY BE
  BUGGED AND NOT INITIATE UNTIL LATER**)**" and "**INTERACT WITH MIKHAILA (**REPEAT THE ACTION A FEW TIMES IF
  SHE DOESN'T REACT AT FIRST**).**" LESSON (~2nd confirmation with Q116): our best emotional beats live on
  flags, and flags need a **regression gate.**
- THE TIMER IS GENEROUS ENOUGH TO BE INVISIBLE: "**IT'S ON A PRETTY LONG TIMER, SO YOU DON'T HAVE TO COMPLETE
  IT IMMEDIATELY.**" Two hours is long enough that most players never feel the clock. LESSON
  (double-edged): a timer nobody feels is a timer that isn't there — our survival accounting has to make the
  countdown **audible.**
- THE MEDICINE IS BEHIND A MAIN-QUEST GATE: "**BUT YOU CAN'T GO THERE UNTIL YOU PROGRESS FURTHER IN THE STORY
  AND LIFT THE LOCKDOWN.**" The two-hour timer starts and the solution is locked behind Reboot. LESSON: a
  timer you can't act on isn't tension, it's homework — unless the gate IS the point (here it half is).
- THE OPTIMAL PLAY IS TO LOOT HER OFFICE WHILE SHE DIES: "**WHILE YOU'RE IN HERE, CHECK HER EMAIL, AND KEEP
  AN EYE OUT FOR A FREE-FLOATING NEUROMOD... GO OUT INTO THE HALL TO FIND TWO MORE NEUROMODS.**" LESSON
  (ours, careful, ~3rd confirmation): if the moral errand is also the loot run, players optimize the errand
  and the morality becomes scenery.
- SHE'S ALSO YOUR EX AND THE GAME BARELY USES IT: "**AFTER JOINING TALOS I, ILYUSHIN ENTERED INTO A BRIEF
  RELATIONSHIP WITH MORGAN YU.**" A stranger you have amnesia about is your ex-partner, and the quest is
  mostly a fetch. LESSON: our fold can put a relationship in the player's past and let the PLAYER discover
  it — but the discovery needs a scene, not a wiki entry.
