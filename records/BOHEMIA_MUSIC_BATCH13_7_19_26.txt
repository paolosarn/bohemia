THE SIX (each song OWNS its lead voice; support reuses the canon dread set):

1. THE REED THAT HOLDS ITS BREATH  (root45, [0,1,5,7,8], half, kick[0,11])
   pwmreed — TRUE comparator PWM. A saw summed with a slow LFO offset through a
   hard sign curve: the pulse DUTY itself breathes across the note. Not tremolo,
   not vibrato — the waveform's own width is the motion. Catalog first.
   NOTE: deliberately NOT the dead SUNKEN VESPERS fingerprint (kick moved 9->11).
   Dead songs stay dead, their tuples too.

2. SOMETHING SINGS THROUGH THE PIPES  (root43, [0,3,5,6,7,10], drive, kick[0,2,6,8,12])
   filterfmgrowl — audio-rate FILTER-FM. A modulator oscillator wired INTO the
   lowpass cutoff at audio rate; the sidebands come out of the FILTER, not the
   oscillator. The growl is the plumbing itself speaking. Catalog first.

3. WIND THROUGH THE COUNTING HOUSE  (root48, [0,2,5,7,10], half, kick[0,12])
   aeolianharp — aeolian resonator bank. Continuous wind noise blown across five
   Q=30 bandpass 'strings' tuned to the harmonic series; each string's gain
   breathes on its own slow clock, so the WIND chooses which harmonics sing.
   No oscillator carries the pitch. Catalog first (ghostflute swept ONE
   resonator; this is a fixed BANK under continuous excitation).

4. WHAT THE DRAWBARS REMEMBER  (root46, [0,2,4,5,7,11], normal, kick[0,5,8,13])
   drawbarmorph — drawbar crossfade. Additive organ partials 1/2/3/4/5/8, each
   on its OWN setValueCurveAtTime envelope; some drawbars push in while others
   pull out mid-note. The registration itself is the motion. Catalog first
   (distinct from formant morph: no filter; from saltbell: no strike/detune).

5. THE SHAPE THE WATER TAKES  (root41, [0,3,4,7,8,11], half, kick[0,7,11])
   wavemorph — dual-shaper morph. ONE oscillator split through two parallel
   WaveShapers (smooth sine-fold vs hard clip) whose output gains crossfade in
   ANTIPHASE on one slow LFO; the tone rocks between liquid and broken while
   the pitch never moves. Catalog first (wavefolding used one static curve).

6. TWO LIGHTS OVER THE FLOOD  (root47, [0,2,6,7,10], normal, kick[0,6,12])
   harmonictrem — band-split antiphase (harmonic) tremolo. One tone split at
   ~600Hz into lows and highs, each gated by the SAME LFO in OPPOSITE phase;
   the note never drops, its WEIGHT rolls between chest and head. Catalog first
   (tremolo/rotary pumped one gain; this is two bands trading).
