# DESERT SUBURB HOUSES FROM THE 45 - RESEARCH (7/21/26, Paolo's ask)

Paolo: "do my research and look at desert suburb houses from 45 degree angle."
What a real Las Vegas tract house shows the world from our three-quarter view,
distilled into art rules for the house-skin factory. Web-researched 7/21;
sources at the bottom.

## THE HEADLINE FINDING: THE ROOF IS WRONG MATERIAL IN v1
Las Vegas suburbs are NOT asphalt-shingle country. Concrete TILE is the
dominant roof across the master-planned communities (Summerlin, Rhodes
Ranch, Southern Highlands) - Mediterranean/Southwestern S-tile and barrel
profiles, chosen for heat, fire, UV and HOA palettes. The v1 cook's
shingle-course roofs read Midwest, not Mojave. The S-tile look from above:
  - UNDULATING VERTICAL RIBS running DOWN the slope (the barrel crests),
    each crest catching sun on one side with a deep shadow in the trough
    between barrels ("rich shadow lines" is the trade's own language)
  - horizontal COURSE lines where each course overlaps the one below
    (a soft step shadow, much weaker than the vertical ribs)
  - MOTTLED MULTI-TONE "desert blends": a roof is never one flat color,
    individual tiles vary 2-3 tones (terracotta/red-brown blends, browns,
    gray-brown blends)
  - RIDGE AND HIP CAPS: rounded cap tiles ride every ridge/hip line,
    slightly lighter (sky-lit) than the field

## THE SHAPE: HIP ROOFS, LOW PITCH, GARAGE FORWARD
  - Hip roofs dominate the Southwest (wind performance): FOUR sloped
    planes, NO flat gable face. From the 45 you read TWO planes - the
    lit south plane and a shaded side plane - split by a diagonal hip
    line with cap tiles on it.
  - Low pitch. Single story dominates the older fabric; two-story infill
    in the packed 90s+ tracts (our MODELS already carry story 1/2).
  - The GARAGE DOOR is the single biggest feature of the front face
    (2-3 car, sectional panels) with the short apron driveway - our
    canon block already models front-corner garages + car aprons.

## THE WALLS: THE TAN FAMILY IS CONFIRMED
  - Stucco, universally, in HOA earth tones: sandstone, buttercream,
    sand-dune brown, wheat, greige. Light tan to mid-brown range,
    trim a shade off the body. Our TAN WALL 85/15 law IS the real city.
  - Windows are small, recessed (deep top shadow), often with a stucco
    pop-out frame. v1's frame + sill shadow read holds.

## THE GROUND: GRAVEL YARDS, BLOCK WALLS - THE REAL VEGAS IS ALREADY DEAD
  - Front lawns are BANNED in new Summerlin construction since 2003
    (Water Smart); SNWA pays residents to tear grass out. Yards are
    decomposed granite / rock mulch in warm blends - the trade's
    best-sellers are literally named Desert Tan, Mojave Gold, Rebel Red.
    From above a yard reads as a calm speckled tone field, not dirt.
    ACT-1 IMPLICATION: the dead world barely changes the suburb's ground
    read - Vegas yards were already rock. Dead pools and dry planters
    are the decay tells, not dead grass.
  - EVERY yard is walled in CMU block (gray or tan) - lumber warps in
    the heat and blocks are manufactured locally, so block fencing is
    universal. Confirms the canon suburb's walled ring; walls read as
    thin light lines between lots at the 45.
  - Rooftop AC units on older stock, covered patios off the back.
    (Solar panels are a modern tell - skip for the 30-years-dead act 1.)

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE FACTORY (acted on same turn)
  1. COOK S-TILE ROOFS: vertical barrel ribs (crest highlight, trough
     shadow), soft course steps, mottled per-tile tone from a blend,
     lit ridge-cap band - in terracotta, desert-brown, and gray-brown
     blends. v1 shingles stay in the judge for the kill decision.
  2. COOK YARD GRAVEL grounds: Desert Tan / Mojave Gold / Rebel Red
     speckled DG fields (calm, coherent - not confetti) as candidates
     for the ground between houses.
  3. HIP-ROOF READ (city-side, when skins graduate): the roof face of a
     realized house should carry the two-plane hip read (lit plane /
     shaded plane / diagonal cap line) - a render rule, not a tile.
  4. The tan wall family and walled-ring block walls stand as built.
