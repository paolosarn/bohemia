BOHEMIA — HERO BEAT PICKER (7/23/26, character/sound session)
==============================================================================
Paolo's ask: "for each song in the combat pool, sit and listen to it and pick
out BY EAR which specific beat in the bar -- not just always beat 1 -- is the
catchiest, easiest-to-anticipate moment, the one I'd want combat kills
snapping onto. Bake that pick into the song's data as an accent offset, so
combat's existing beat-lock follows MY pick instead of assuming beat 1."

RESOLUTION: locked at quarter-note (1-of-4-beats-per-bar). Confirmed with
Paolo directly: the dial has no code path finer than a whole 120-grid beat
(every dial pattern's cycle is a whole number of beats, phase-shifted so its
kill-moment lands exactly on a beat boundary) -- an 8th/16th pick would just
get rounded away before it reached the dial. 4 slots is the real ceiling.

HOW IT WORKS
------------
No separate audition tool -- the real song IS the audition. In the MUSIC
tab, hit PLAY on any song slot like always; a new HERO BEAT control sits on
that row: 4 tap buttons (1/2/3/4) plus a live 4-dot indicator that lights up
in sync with MUS.uiStep (quarter-beat = Math.floor(uiStep/4)) while that
slot plays. Listen, tap the beat you like. Saves instantly (MUS.hero[key],
key = "SONGNAME#slot", rides MUS.save()/localStorage) and pushes live into
combat immediately via musicPushToCombat() if it's listening. Unset = beat 1
= today's behavior, unchanged -- nothing needs picking before this ships.

THE MECHANISM (why this was safe to build without touching the dial itself)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Combat's own per-pattern phase-lock (PHASE[pat], tickPat) already lands the
kill-moment on A whole 120-grid beat -- that part was already correct and
already shipped ("release on the beat = killshot" already felt right).
What was missing was WHICH beat of the bar that lock targeted -- it was
always effectively beat 1. The fix touches exactly one thing: every place
combat samples the shared clock into its dial clock (`G.beatClock=
beatNow()`, 3 call sites) now subtracts `heroOffset()` beats (0..3, = hero
pick - 1) before sampling. That's it -- shift WHEN the dial starts counting
by (hero-1) beats, and its existing whole-beat-cycle lock recurs on the
picked beat instead of always beat 1. No change to PHASE[], no change to
the audible kick/bass/hat pattern (the song plays its real authored groove
untouched) -- only which beat of that groove the VISUAL kill-lock targets.

DATA FLOW (song 1, song 2, and every category-pool vibe all carry their own
independent pick)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
MUSIC tab (MUS.hero map, keyed "NAME#slot")
  -> musicPushToCombat(): out.factions[nm].hero (song 1), out.song2[nm].hero
     (song 2's own pick), pool song c.hero (every vibe Paolo tagged to a
     faction carries its own pick onto the bus)
  -> combat's bohemiaMusic message handler: f._hero1=e2.hero (song 1's pick
     lives on the faction object, resolved per-slot below)
  -> applySlot(f,slot): resolves f.hero fresh every slot switch -- slot 2
     reads its own override, a pool object reads its own .hero, slot 1
     falls back to f._hero1. (hero is deliberately NOT in POOL_FIELDS: that
     array's restore/backup only makes sense for fields with a canon
     baseline on the hardcoded FACTIONS array; hero has none, so it's
     resolved fresh instead of restored.)
  -> heroOffset(): const f=FAC(); (f.hero||1)-1
  -> the 3 G.beatClock=beatNow() call sites all now read
     G.beatClock=beatNow()-heroOffset()
