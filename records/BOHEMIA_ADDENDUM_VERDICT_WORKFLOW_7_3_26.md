# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM: THE VERDICT WORKFLOW (Paolo 7/3/26, LOCKED)
The production loop Paolo wants for all visual work, and later music,
formalized from the animation-batch system that worked:

## THE LOOP
1. CLAUDE COOKS: batches of concrete options: a street corner in three
   variations, clothing pieces, tile sets, animation clips, layouts,
   eventually musical phrases. Real, rendered, presented for tap-review.
2. PAOLO VERDICTS: thumbs up or thumbs down, fast. He is not nitpicking
   pixels unless he chooses to (pixel-level control stays available, it
   is his art). The verdict is the direction.
3. THUMBS UP: implemented into the build the same turn, filed to canon.
4. THUMBS DOWN: goes to the GRAVEYARD with source preserved AND a
   POST-MORTEM: Claude writes down WHY it likely died (layering issue,
   hand overlap, silhouette mud, off-palette, too metronome) so the same
   mistake is never cooked twice. The graveyard is a lesson catalog, not
   a trash can.

## THE LAWS UNDER IT
- Claude produces VOLUME, Paolo produces DIRECTION. Never inverted.
- Rejected work is never deleted, never re-pitched as-is.
- The post-mortem list gets consulted BEFORE cooking every new batch.
- The export/copy pipe must stay one-tap reliable; when the copy button
  or download pipe breaks, fixing it takes priority over new production
  (a broken pipe kills the whole loop).
