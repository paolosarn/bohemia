=== UPDATE 7/18 (g): HEAD ROTATES IN LOSSLESS 90-DEG SNAPS ===
Paolo: "WHY CANT U ROTATE THE SPRITE WITHOUT IT MORPHING AND RESAMPLING." The
honest answer: rotating raster pixel art to an ARBITRARY angle MUST resample
(the rotated pixels don't fall on the grid), which reshapes a low-res head =
"morph." That's a hard fact of pixel graphics. BUT a rotation by a MULTIPLE OF
90deg is LOSSLESS -- pixels land exactly on the grid, zero resampling. SOLUTION:
the ragdoll head snaps to the nearest 90deg of the real neck bone. It TURNS to
face the way the body fell (fixes 'always facing north') in crisp quarter-turns
and never morphs one pixel. Drawn at exact rest length so it never scales either.
The tradeoff Paolo should know: it steps through 4 crisp orientations, not a
smooth continuous spin (smooth spin is the thing that morphs). If he wants finer
steps with no morph, the next level is pre-drawn head views per angle (authored
art, also crisp). This is the 'rotate without resampling' he asked for.

=== UPDATE 7/18 (k): OMNI PROJECTION — each facing slumps its OWN cardinal way ===
Paolo: "a lot better -- the only thing is they're only laying down east and west,

ROOT CAUSE: the ragdoll ran in a flat side-view plane with gravity always pulling

corpse lay E-W no matter which way it faced.
FIXED (same trick v1 uses): the sim now topples ONE canonical way (+x) and
pancakes flat; rgPose then PROJECTS that horizontal topple onto the compass axis

from the facing). Horizontal topple rides K (points N/S/E/W/diagonal, 3/4
foreshortened); the vertical fall stays vertical. Camera centres on the projected
centroid so a vertical slump doesn't run off-frame.
NO STANDING CORPSES: a straight N/S topple lies dead into/out of the screen,
foreshortens to a narrow vertical blob and reads as STANDING (this is why v1

diagonal cant -- still up/down-biased, but enough spread to read as lying.
