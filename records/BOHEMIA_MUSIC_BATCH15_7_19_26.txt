THE SEVEN (each song OWNS its lead; support reuses the canon dread set):

1. WHAT SPEAKS THROUGH THE GRILLE  (root45, [0,1,4,5,8], half, kick[0,9])
   pulseform — pulse-train FORMANT (VOSIM throat). A comparator turns a saw
   into a click train at f0; the train ring-gates a sine parked on a sweeping
   vowel formant. The pitch is the train, the vowel is the sine: a throat of
   clicks that speaks. Catalog first.

2. THE LIGHT THAT WONT DECIDE  (root47, [0,2,4,6,9], normal, kick[0,3,8,12])
   octgate — OCTAVE-GATE. A slowing square LFO wired into detune flicks the
   voice between its octaves (0 or +1200 cents); a light that cannot decide
   which floor it is on. Pitch-gating, not tremolo. Catalog first.

3. A PROCESSION THAT FOLLOWS ITSELF  (root43, [0,1,3,7,10], half, kick[0,8,12])
   roundchoir — staggered CANON. Five copies of the same swell enter 150ms
   apart: a round sung by one ghost, built entirely from per-copy envelopes.
   No delay node (screech law kept the hard way). Catalog first.

4. IT HAPPENS BACKWARDS HERE  (root46, [0,2,3,7,9], normal, kick[0,7,10])
   reversebloom — tail-attack REVERSE bloom. The note swells from silence to
   FULL at its end and cuts, pitch creeping up as it blooms; the reversed-tape
   shape without any buffer reversal. The envelope IS the reversal. Catalog
   first.

5. THE WINDOW BENDS THE HYMN  (root42, [0,1,4,6,7,10], drive, kick[0,2,6,10,12])
   phasedist — PHASE DISTORTION (the Casio CZ trick). A saw is a phase ramp;
   reading it through a bent-cosine curve resynthesizes a sine whose phase is
   warped, buzzing like a resonant organ. Not Chebyshev, not folding, not
   clipping, not the comparator. Catalog first.

6. EVERYTHING FALLS TO PITCH  (root44, [0,3,5,7,11], half, kick[0,6,11])
   settlebend — SETTLE-BEND. Every note ARRIVES from a fifth above and sinks
   onto its true pitch over a full second: a choir that lands wrong and
   corrects. The bend is the identity. Catalog first.

7. THREE NOTES IN THE DUST  (root48, [0,1,5,8,10], normal, kick[0,4,9,13])
   ghosttriad — NOISE TRIAD. One breath of noise through three razor
   bandpasses tuned to a minor triad, one shared swell: a chord with no
   oscillator in it, sung by static. Distinct from the retired aeolianharp
   (harmonic series of one pitch, per-string clocks): this is a CHORD on one
   breath. Catalog first.
