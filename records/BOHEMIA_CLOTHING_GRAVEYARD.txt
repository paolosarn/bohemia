--- BATCH 1 (7/18/26) — ALL THREE DOWN, no comments ---
RED SHIRT: DOWN
BLUE JEANS: DOWN
WHITE SNEAKERS: DOWN

CLAUDE POST-MORTEM (my honest read, since no note was left):
These were technically "a red shirt / jeans / sneakers," but crude. What's weak:
- MECHANICAL SHADING: a flat 3-tone rule (top=light, bottom/side=dark, else mid).
  No real fabric rendering. Reads as flat colored regions, not cloth.
- ZERO GARMENT DETAIL: no collar, no cuffs, no hem, no denim seams, no belt/loops,
  no pockets, no laces, no tongue, no sole shaping. Real garments have these tells.
- SILHOUETTE JUST TRACES THE BODY: the cloth hugs the region outline exactly, no
  "give" (a shirt bags a little, a sleeve ends in a cuff, jeans stack at the ankle).
- BARE MANNEQUIN PRESENTATION: shown on a plain skin body, so they read clinical /
  half-finished rather than as an outfit in context.
LESSON (pending Paolo's confirm): the factory's SHADING/DETAIL ceiling was too low.
Next batches need real fabric craft (garment-specific detail + richer shading), and
likely to be shown ON the actual Punk, not a mannequin.

from all direction." I loved that he saw the attempt ("I love how you tried to
have a good neckline") but the execution was wrong: my collar band was painted
along the whole TORSO TOP EDGE, which follows the shoulder/collarbone contour and
dips at center -- so it read as a neckline cut down at the collarbone, exposing
the chest, instead of a proper crew collar at the base of the neck.

ROOT CAUSE (confirmed by probing the deformed grid): in front view the neck part
(id 3) is a tiny 4px strip at the very top of the torso; the torso top row IS the
shoulder line. Tracing a dark rim along that whole edge = drawing the collarbone,
not a neckline.

FIX SHIPPED SAME TURN (Paolo explicitly authorized the remake: "if you want try
to remake the things I thumbs down"): genTop rewritten -- the shirt now covers the
WHOLE chest+shoulders, and the collar is only a thin RING hugging the neck's own
columns (id 3), derived per-facing so it's correct front/3-4/side/back. No dark
band on the collarbone, ever. Verified across all 8 directions (neck_sheet).

UI FIX (same note): killed the cardinal-direction button row. The 8-pose strip is
now directly tappable -- tap any pose and it becomes the big live idle view.
