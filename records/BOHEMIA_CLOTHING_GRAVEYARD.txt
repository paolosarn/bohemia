--- BATCH 1 (7/18/26) — ALL THREE DOWN, no comments ---
RED SHIRT: DOWN
BLUE JEANS: DOWN
WHITE SNEAKERS: DOWN

CLAUDE POST-MORTEM (my honest read, since no note was left):
These were technically "a red shirt / jeans / sneakers," but crude. What's weak:
- MECHANICAL SHADING: a flat 3-tone rule (top=light, bottom/side=dark, else mid).
  No real fabric rendering. Reads as flat colored regions, not cloth.
- ZERO GARMENT DETAIL: no collar, no cuffs, no hem, no denim seams, no belt/loops,
  no pockets, no laces, no tongue, no sole shaping. Real garments have these tells.
- SILHOUETTE JUST TRACES THE BODY: the cloth hugs the region outline exactly, no
  "give" (a shirt bags a little, a sleeve ends in a cuff, jeans stack at the ankle).
- BARE MANNEQUIN PRESENTATION: shown on a plain skin body, so they read clinical /
  half-finished rather than as an outfit in context.
LESSON (pending Paolo's confirm): the factory's SHADING/DETAIL ceiling was too low.
Next batches need real fabric craft (garment-specific detail + richer shading), and
likely to be shown ON the actual Punk, not a mannequin.

PAOLO'S NOTE (the reason): the NECKLINE. "You thought a collarbone was a neckline
from all direction." I loved that he saw the attempt ("I love how you tried to
have a good neckline") but the execution was wrong: my collar band was painted
along the whole TORSO TOP EDGE, which follows the shoulder/collarbone contour and
dips at center -- so it read as a neckline cut down at the collarbone, exposing
the chest, instead of a proper crew collar at the base of the neck.

ROOT CAUSE (confirmed by probing the deformed grid): in front view the neck part
(id 3) is a tiny 4px strip at the very top of the torso; the torso top row IS the
shoulder line. Tracing a dark rim along that whole edge = drawing the collarbone,
not a neckline.

FIX SHIPPED SAME TURN (Paolo explicitly authorized the remake: "if you want try
to remake the things I thumbs down"): genTop rewritten -- the shirt now covers the
WHOLE chest+shoulders, and the collar is only a thin RING hugging the neck's own
columns (id 3), derived per-facing so it's correct front/3-4/side/back. No dark
band on the collarbone, ever. Verified across all 8 directions (neck_sheet).

UI FIX (same note): killed the cardinal-direction button row. The 8-pose strip is
now directly tappable -- tap any pose and it becomes the big live idle view.

--- BATCH 2 (7/18/26) — the 4 v1 COATS, all DOWN ---
DUST DUSTER, ASH LONGCOAT, OXBLOOD COAT, ROADWORN JACKET. GRAVEYARD FINAL.
PAOLO: "I really like the IDEA of your long coats, but you made it in a way that
it looks like there's shorts connected to the shirt -- did not look like a long
coat at all. And the long coat belongs in the JACKET LAYER, a layer on top of a
shirt. I hope you're putting things in proper layers."
ROOT CAUSE: genCoat v1 covered each leg (id 9/10) separately down to mid-thigh,
so it read as two leg-tubes = shorts. It also wasn't layered (rendered on bare
skin, not as an outer layer over a shirt).
FIX (fresh cook, new names -- v1 names stay dead): genCoat v2 drapes ONE A-line
skirt panel from the hips to the ankle, FILLING THE GAP between the legs (via a
now-additive buildFrame preview hook) and flaring outward, so it reads as hanging
cloth. Every garment now carries a LAYER (base/legs/feet/outer); coats are 'outer'
and render OVER a base shirt. Fresh coats: WASTELAND DUSTER, SOOT TRENCH,
DRIFTER'S COAT, RANGER LONGCOAT (in COOKING, pending verdict).

--- BATCH 3 (7/18/26) — 4 DOWN: 2 hoodies + 2 v-necks ---
GREY HOODIE, BLACK HOODIE, STEEL V-NECK, OLIVE V-TANK. GRAVEYARD FINAL.
PAOLO (the lesson): a hoodie is NOT texture painted on a shirt. "You have to
understand how a hoodie would be ADDITIONAL PIXELS OUTSIDE OF THE SKELETON. The
hood part actually has to come out the back... same thing with hair -- it's about
the SHAPE and the pixels that go outside the body skeleton."
ROOT CAUSE: my genTop neck:'hood' just recolored the top torso rows into a fake
dark cowl -- paint on the body, no geometry. A hood is a real 3D shape hanging off
the nape, extending beyond the body silhouette.
FIX (fresh cook, new names): genHood ADDS pixels outside the skeleton (via the
additive buildFrame hook, same capability the coat skirt uses) -- a rounded hood
mass off the back of the neck, facing-aware, never covering the face. The v-necks
(STEEL V-NECK, OLIVE V-TANK) also DOWN, no comment -- not remade; fresh v-necks
only if the mechanism improves. This capability is the groundwork for HAIR too.
