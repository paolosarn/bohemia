--- HOOD, take 3 (7/18/26) — Paolo: "whatever you did was dogshit, try again."
    (I looked at the actual rendered pixels this time, zoomed.) ---
DIAGNOSIS: at 56px the head sits right on the shoulders -- there is NO neck gap,
so any hood pixels I forced near the front neck just merged into the shirt collar
as noise. That's WHY it looked like garbage. Reality: a down-hood is not visible
from the front. So:
  front (S/SE/SW): NOTHING (clean -- no noise).
  side (E/W):      a couple px of the hood roll peeking behind the neck.
  back (N/NE/NW):  a small DARK rolled hood collar at the nape (contrast vs the
                   lit shirt is what makes it read) + a hint cresting the shoulders.
Lesson banked: I must LOOK at the rendered pixels (zoomed) before shipping, not
ship blind and let Paolo be the QA. Down-hood is inherently a small back/side
detail at this scale; a hood-UP variant (covers the head) is the option if he
wants it prominent -- offered, not built.

--- THE ACTUAL HOODIE-SKIN BUG (7/18/26) — Paolo: "you KEEP adding skin textures
    to the hoodie, what's wrong with you." ---
It was a RENDER-ORDER BUG, not a drawing problem -- which is why no hood I drew
could fix it. In buildFrame the SKIN_DETAIL pass (which repaints skin, incl. the
neck grid===3) ran AFTER the CLO_PREVIEW garment hook, so it painted skin back
over every hoodie collar, every time. FIX: moved the garment hook to run LAST
(after SKIN_DETAIL). Probe now confirms: neck + torso are skin-free in all 8
facings; the only remaining skin is the FACE/chin (correct -- hood is down).
Lesson: when a symptom survives every content change, suspect the PIPELINE order.

--- HOODIE-SKIN, ROUND 2: THE BACK-VIEW HOLE (7/18/26) — Paolo screenshot: skin
    notch at the collar in the back view, "why can't you have clothing there" ---
The render-order fix killed the NECK repaint leak, but a second bug hid under it:
the hood painter had a blanket "never paint over head pixels" rule. Right for the
FACE; wrong from BEHIND -- a hood drapes OVER the lower skull. So the hood mass
skipped the skull-bottom pixels and left a skin hole in its center. (My probe
missed it because it whitelisted all head skin as legit.)
FIX: in back views (N/NE/NW) the hood covers the lower skull + nape as one solid
bunch (face stays untouchable). Probe now scans the collar band with NO head
whitelist: N/NE/NW CLEAN. E/W show only the visible jaw/cheek -- correct, a
down-hood does not cover the head from the side.

--- HOODIE-SKIN, THE REAL ONE: THE CREW-NECK CARVE (7/18/26) ---
After the render-order fix and the back-hole fix, skin STILL showed. Two lessons:
1. MY PROBES WERE LYING: they called buildFrame directly with a capture-only
   preview hook -- which renders a DIFFERENT image than the preview canvas (no
   garment, PD outfit still equipped). Every "CLEAN" verdict was measured on the
   wrong pixels. New law for me: verify ONLY on the real preview canvas.
2. THE ACTUAL LEAK: genTop carves a small crew-NECK HOLE in the torso top (skin
   at the throat -- intentional for shirts) and the hoodie inherited that carve
   in every facing. Those torso pixels showing skin were the patch in all of
   Paolo's screenshots. FIX: for neck:'hood' the collar FILLS the hole (no carve)
   + the collar covers the whole neck part. Canvas probe (per-row skin counts,
   sandwich detector): ALL 8 facings clean -- head+chin only, then solid fabric.
