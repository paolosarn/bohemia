# BOHEMIA COMBAT NORTH STAR — Fable (2026) x Cyberpunk 2077, on a 120bpm tile dial

**Date:** 7/24/26 · COMBAT session · research + option map, not a build order commitment.
**Paolo's ask:** "big brain research on what combat would be like if you mixed the
roguelite Fable 4 combat with Cyberpunk 2077 ... explore our options and make combat
the funnest we absolutely can."

This is a compass, not a spec. Nothing here is canon until Paolo rules it. Where a
game detail is press-material or early/unconfirmed it is marked. Everything maps back
to what Bohemia already IS: a beat-locked (120 BPM, I-MOVE-YOU-MOVE), tile-based,
occupancy-true, roguelite tactical fight resolved on the **Dead Eye Dial**.

---

## 0. THE ONE-LINE TARGET

> Fable's **style-weaving physicality** (swap melee/ranged/tech inside a single flow,
> zero friction) + Cyberpunk's **systemic build variety** (cyberware, quickhacks, a
> time-slow hero move, enemies with real roles) — expressed as **rhythm** on the tile
> board, where every commit lands on the beat and the Dead Eye Dial is the trigger.

Bohemia is not a shooter and not a card game. It is a **rhythm-tactics roguelite**.
The dial is the katana. The beat is the Sandevistan. The tile is the cover system.

---

## 1. WHAT FABLE (2026 reboot) ACTUALLY DOES  *(grounded, some early/press)*

- **"Style-weaving" combat.** The headline system: melee, ranged, and magic are one
  fluid kit, swappable **within single animation frames**, zero latency. You are never
  "in melee mode" — you flow between a sword strike, a bow shot, and a fireball as one
  sentence.
- **Player identity over rigid classes.** Combat is built so your *mix* is your build.
  No class lock; how you weave IS your character.
- **Three pillars:** melee (swords/hammers), ranged (bows), magic (fireballs and up).
- **Choice-and-consequence framing** wraps the whole game (the series' signature).
- *Unconfirmed / not yet detailed publicly:* exact dodge/parry/flourish frames,
  follower/companion combat, difficulty tuning. Treat as TBD, don't design against
  specifics we can't verify.

**The transferable idea:** *frictionless weaving.* One turn should be able to hold a
pop-shot, a shove, and a tech verb without a mode-switch tax. Our **killshot chain**
is already the seed of this — v53 caps it at 2/turn. Style-weaving says: let the chain
mix VERBS, not just repeat the dial.

---

## 2. WHAT CYBERPUNK 2077 ACTUALLY DOES  *(well-documented)*

- **Cyberware is the foundation** everything else builds on. It is the perk/loadout
  spine, not a side system.
- **Quickhacks + RAM.** Upload "daemons" to enemies: Short Circuit (shock), Contagion
  (poison spread), Reboot Optics (blind), Cyberpsychosis (turn them on their friends).
  RAM is the resource, regens over time, Intelligence makes hacks hit harder. This is
  **pre-fire tactical manipulation** — you soften and reshape the fight before a bullet.
- **Sandevistan** — slow time ~80%, act while the world crawls. The iconic power
  fantasy. Paired with a blade = the Edgerunner moment.
- **Weapon archetypes:** Power (ricochet), Tech (charge, shoot through cover/walls),
  Smart (homing). Each is a different verb, not a stat swap.
- **Build archetypes:** Netrunner (hack the room), Solo (raw gunplay/berserk), Stealth
  (silenced, knives, headshots), Hybrid (weaken by hack → finish by precision).
- **Enemies have ROLES you scan and prioritize:** muscle (rush), shooters (cover +
  **flank** + snipe), netrunners (debuff from range — kill first). Good enemies flank,
  snipe from cover, and hack from distance. Melee types rush straight in.
- **Known weakness to avoid:** at its worst the AI reads as bullet sponges, not a
  thinking challenge. Our enemies must think, or difficulty is just bigger numbers.

**The transferable idea:** *the fight is systemic before it is a gunfight.* You reshape
it (hack/suppress/blind), you have a hero burst (Sandevistan), and enemies play roles.

---

## 3. WHAT ROGUELITES TEACH  *(grounded)*

Four pillars of a great roguelite: **(1)** a satisfying minute-to-minute loop, **(2)**
build variety that creates *"aha" synergies*, **(3)** meta-progression that doesn't
trivialize the run, **(4)** procedural content. And the polish note that matters most
for feel: **Dead Cells / Hades feel incredible because cancel windows and i-frames are
tuned like instruments.** Snappy is not an accident, it is frame tuning.

For Bohemia: the "run" is a night in the valley. Between runs you draft augments (the
cyberware analog). No run is wasted. The **aha** is a weapon + augment + beat synergy,
not a stat bump.

## 4. THE SYNTHESIS — how each idea becomes Bohemia

| Their system | Bohemia form (on the tile + beat + dial) |
|---|---|
| Fable style-weaving | The **turn is a sentence**: pop-shot → shove → tech verb, mixed in one chain, each landing on a beat. Weapon swap costs a beat, not a menu. |
| Cyberware spine | The **RIG**: your augment loadout = the perk system, drafted per run. Structure, not recolor (STRUCTURE-NOT-COLOR law). |
| Quickhacks (pre-fire) | **Tactical verbs** that reshape the enemy cover cycle and the green window BEFORE you shoot: SUPPRESS, BLIND, GLITCH, JAM. (Paolo greenlit suppress this session.) |
| Sandevistan (time-slow) | **DEAD EYE burst** — a meta-resource that slows the dial / widens the window / buys extra chain shots. The name was always waiting for this. Rhythm hero-moment, ties to the hero-beat music picker. |
| Weapon archetypes | Pistol/SMG/rifle/shotgun already differ by lethality + dial. Add **verb identity**: shotgun = suppress cone, rifle = pierce cover, etc. |
| Enemy roles | **Muscle / Shooter / Netrunner-analog / Blade**, each with a SMARTS stat (Paolo's ruling) driving flanking + repositioning + debuffs. |
| Build archetypes | Your RIG + weapon + augments = a run identity (breacher, ghost, brawler, tech). |
| Companions | **Tile allies** that share the 120 beat (I-MOVE-YOU-MOVE), each an archetype, each an occupancy body (OCCUPANCY LAW). None built yet. |
| Meta-progression | Draft augments between nights; the valley remembers. Never trivialize the fight. |

## 5. PAOLO'S RULINGS THIS SESSION *(LOCKED direction, capture per NOTES-ARE-RULINGS)*

1. **FLANKING IS GATED BY ENEMY SMARTS.** Not every enemy flanks. Each archetype carries
   a combat-intelligence rating; only smart-enough enemies reposition to open an exposed
   line on you. A dumb goon holds; a pro flanks and repositions when you camp. Smarts
   drives flank, reposition, and (later) netrunner-style debuffs.
2. **SUPPRESSING FIRE + HAND-PEEK COVER + MICRO-GREEN.** Cover is not all-or-nothing.
   Whether you're in **standing** cover or **ducking** cover, you can **peek just your
   hands and gun over the edge** instead of your whole body — a smaller exposure. And
   **suppressing fire forces enemies' cover fire down**, which can crack open a **tiny
   tiny green window for yourself even when all 8 are firing on you**. This is the answer
   to "too many enemies, I never get a green": you MAKE the green by suppressing. This is
   the single most important new mechanic on the board.
3. **COVER-TO-COVER DASH — greenlit.** Spend a turn to sprint between two pillars.
   Breaks every lock on you, repositions you to a new angle, but you're exposed mid-dash.
4. **VAULT / RETREAT — explained below, pending his pick.**

## 6. VAULT / RETREAT — what it is

Two related "give ground to gain angle" moves, both beat-locked:

- **VAULT** = a short forward/lateral hop OVER a piece of cover (vault the pillar,
  hop the low wall) that lands you on the *far* side in one beat. Use: you're pinned
  behind a pillar with two guns on your open side; vaulting puts the stone between you
  and them and drops you into a fresh firing angle on a third enemy who thought he was
  safe. It trades your current cover for a better one plus surprise. Risk: the airborne
  beat is fully exposed.
- **RETREAT** = a deliberate backward step (1 tile, away from the threat) that does NOT
  cost your whole engagement — it *resets your beads/locks* (breaks line-of-sight the way
  the LOS bead law already rewards) and buys distance, at the cost of ground and tempo.
  Use: you over-extended, three guns have a bead, you retreat one tile behind cover to
  wipe the locks and re-approach on your terms.

Difference from the dash: the **dash** is lateral cover-to-cover (stay in the fight,
change angle). **Vault** goes *over/through* cover for surprise + a new angle. **Retreat**
goes *backward* to wipe locks and reset. Three flavors of movement-as-defense, each a
different rhythm answer to "I'm pinned."

## 7. THE OPTION CATALOG — everything on the table, rough cost/impact

**A. Suppressing fire + hand-peek cover + micro-green** *(Paolo ruled — top priority)*
Impact: enormous (fixes the 8-enemy no-green wall). Cost: medium-high (new verb, new
partial-exposure cover state, cover-cycle manipulation, the micro-green math).

**B. Enemy SMARTS stat → flanking + repositioning** *(Paolo ruled)*
Impact: huge (makes enemies think, not sponge). Cost: high (per-archetype AI, tile
pathing to flank, camp-detection).

**C. Cover-to-cover DASH** *(Paolo greenlit)*
Impact: high (movement-as-fun, escape valve). Cost: medium (dash state, mid-dash
exposure, lock-break).

**D. VAULT / RETREAT** *(pending pick)*
Impact: high. Cost: medium each.

**E. DEAD EYE burst (Sandevistan analog)** *(idea)*
Impact: huge (the hero fantasy, ties to hero-beat music). Cost: high (meta-resource,
dial-slow, chain bonus). The name is already ours.

**F. Tactical verbs — BLIND / GLITCH / JAM (quickhack analogs)** *(idea)*
Impact: high (build variety). Cost: medium each, share the suppress plumbing.

**G. The RIG — draftable augment loadout (cyberware spine)** *(idea)*
Impact: huge (this is the roguelite build engine). Cost: high (draft UI, augment table
— MECHANISM-MINE / CONTENTS-PAOLO'S: build the table empty, he fills the canon).

**H. Weapon VERB identity** *(idea)*
Impact: medium-high. Cost: medium (each weapon gets a verb, not just a lethality number).

**I. Tile COMPANIONS on the shared beat** *(Paolo flagged we have none)*
Impact: huge (whole new axis). Cost: very high (ally AI, occupancy, beat-sharing, the
I-MOVE-YOU-MOVE contract extended to allies).

## 8. PROPOSED BUILD ORDER *(recommendation, Paolo decides)*

1. **Suppressing fire + hand-peek cover + micro-green** — his top ruling, fixes the most
   painful current problem, and it's the keystone the tactical-verb family hangs off.
2. **Cover-to-cover dash** — cheap, high fun, an immediate escape valve.
3. **Enemy SMARTS → flanking** — makes the suppress/dash choices MATTER (camping gets
   punished). Do it right after movement so the two systems teach each other.
4. **Vault / Retreat** — round out the movement verbs.
5. **DEAD EYE burst** — the hero moment, once the beat/music hero-note is wired.
6. **The RIG + tactical verbs** — the roguelite build engine and its content.
7. **Companions** — the biggest new axis, last, when the solo fight is airtight.

Rationale: nail the **solo moment-to-moment feel first** (movement + suppress + smart
enemies), because roguelite pillar #1 is the minute-to-minute loop and pillar #4-polish
is frame tuning. Build variety (RIG) and companions are multipliers — they only pay off
on top of a fight that already feels snappy and fair.
