THE RESEARCH PASS (7/19/26) — what the best beat/tactics combat ever made teaches

Full addendum with sources: laws/BOHEMIA_ADDENDUM_BEAT_TACTICS_RESEARCH_7_19_26.md. The short version:

  • NecroDancer's forgiveness discovery: the dev kept widening the beat window and found it felt best at 100% leeway — the beat is a turn clock, not a timing test. Players are least accurate exactly when most stressed, so strict timing punishes panic, not skill. Missing only resets the groove multiplier. We already live this (SILENT-PLAY LAW); the lab's groove window is a tuning knob, not a wall.
  • Hi-Fi Rush's rule: pressing ALWAYS works; the RESULT snaps to the beat. The beat lives in the WORLD (everything pulses), not in the HUD. Games that hard-gated actions behind timing (BPM, Keylocker) got hammered for it; games that made rhythm a reward multiplier (Hellsinger's 16x unlocks the vocals) are loved.
  • The telegraph consensus: every great in this class is declare-then-answer. Into the Breach shows exact enemy tiles, damage and ORDER before you move — the turn becomes a solvable prevention puzzle. Shogun Showdown makes BOTH sides telegraph. Perfect info about NOW, none about NEXT turn: that boundary is where tactics live.
  • Movement-as-attack: Hoplite kills by footwork (pass between two tiles adjacent to a demon = stab; step toward = lunge) — one action per turn makes offense and defense the same decision. Auro kills only by bumping into water. The move IS the weapon.
  • Displacement beats damage numbers: ITB pushes into walls/water/each other; Fights in Tight Spaces prices it (wall = 4 dmg, enemy-into-enemy hurts both); Tactical Breach Wizards made shove-out-window the signature verb. Spatial outcomes are readable pre-commit; damage rolls are not.
  • Enemy ceiling is cognitive: nothing great in this class exceeds ~6-8 live threats, most stay lower. The cap is what a player can hold in their head, which matches our locked 8 and the 6/27 readability finding.
  • One action per turn is a stable attractor across Hoplite / broughlikes / Shogun Showdown — exactly our locked turn economy. The queue (spend a turn LOADING damage, release later) is the standout post-2023 invention; it is greed, spatialized. Parked as a future option.

THE FOUR OPTIONS (playable, left tabs) — all [PENDING Paolo]

  • A THE CONDUCTOR (NecroDancer): enemies are beat signatures — 1s act every beat, 2s every other, 3s syncopate. The room is a polyrhythm you read. Tests: do enemy rhythms make the 120 grid FELT?
  • B THE PROMISE (Into the Breach): enemies commit attacks to visible red tiles that land next beat, no dice. Your move is prevention. Tests: is declare-then-answer the right pressure?
  • C THE DANCE (Hoplite): sidestep past a guard = he's OPENED (easier dial); backing straight out of reach = he swipes. Footwork manufactures easy dials. Tests: movement-as-attack, Bohemia-flavored.
  • D THE SHOVE (Tight Spaces/ITB): push bodies into walls (stagger), each other (both stagger), burn barrels (cooked+opened). Occupancy law makes every shove readable. Tests: displacement as the off-dial verb.

THE CURRENCY THESIS (the finding underneath all four): in Bohemia the tactical currency is not damage, it is WHICH DIAL YOU EARN. Distance already pulls the package (locked canon). The lab extends it: openings, stagger and stun ease the dial one step; being surrounded (3+ lines on you) hardens it one step. Damage never moves — only odds. Position is the bet, the dial is the payout. No other game can do this because no other game has the dial.

Deliberately absent here (lives in the canon DIAL demo, not re-judged): greed, ammo scarcity, cover pop-timing, faction music, wagers. The lab isolates ONE question: which turn-shell grammar is the most fun to stand in.
