# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM: THE GRASS-TO-FOOD MATH (7/5/26)
Paolo's question: transmute every park, golf course, and real-grass surface
in post-crash Las Vegas into food production. What does it look like, and can
it feed the city? Grounded in real SNWA / Clark County numbers. Lore forks
marked [PENDING].

## THE INVENTORY (what grass actually exists)
Real valley turf at the eve of the crash: ~13,000 acres total historically,
trending down toward ~9,000-10,000 as removal programs bit. Composition:
- ~7,600 acres FUNCTIONAL turf: parks, schools, sports fields (real SNWA
  number). Sunset Park alone is 320 acres.
- ~3,000-4,000 acres GOLF: the courses were exempt from the turf ban.
- ~1,000-4,000 acres decorative scraps (medians, HOA carpets), much of it
  gone or dying by crash time.
WORKING NUMBER: Bohemia inherits ~10,000 acres of irrigated or irrigable
soil. That is the entire arable inventory of the valley. Everything else is
caliche hardpan and rock.

## THE WATER MATH (the part that works FOR us)
- Turf drinks ~55 gal/sq ft/yr = ~7 acre-feet per acre. An average course
  burned ~725 acre-feet a year, and NONE of it returns to Mead (outdoor
  water is consumptive, only indoor water recycles: this is literally why
  the 99% loop favors indoor use).
- Vegetables need 3-4 acre-feet per acre. CONVERTING GRASS TO FOOD USES
  LESS WATER THAN THE GRASS DID. 10,000 acres of crops ≈ 35,000 acre-feet
  a year, a fraction of what the intake can pull. Water is NOT the binding
  constraint. Soil and labor are.

## THE CALORIE MATH (the ceiling)
A human needs ~900,000 kcal a year. Desert double-season intensive farming
(winter greens/roots/grains Oct-Apr, summer heat crops May-Sep) yields:
- Conservative mixed farming: ~3-4M kcal/acre/yr → ~4 people per acre.
- Aggressive biointensive with potatoes/sweet potatoes carrying the calorie
  load: ~8M kcal/acre → ~8-9 people per acre.
THE CEILING: 10,000 acres feeds roughly 40,000-90,000 people. Call the
honest planning band 50,000-80,000 with real losses, seed stock, bad years,
and protein needs. THE GRASS CANNOT FEED 2 MILLION. It feeds a town.
Post-crash Las Vegas's maximum population is written in its dirt.

## THE LABOR MATH
Intensive hand farming runs ~1 worker per 1-2 acres. The full conversion
needs 5,000-10,000 farm workers, a faction-scale labor force. Whoever
organizes farm labor organizes the city.

## WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE
- GOLF COURSES ARE THE CROWN JEWELS: irrigation mains, pump houses, and
  reservoir ponds are ALREADY IN THE GROUND. Fairways become long crooked
  strip-fields that still follow the old dogleg holes. Greens (sand-based,
  best-drained soil in the valley) become seedbeds. Water hazards become
  irrigation tanks. From the city view: green ribbons shaped like a golf
  hole ghost, full of crops.
- PARKS become the commons: Sunset Park (320 real acres, welded to our
  cemetery) is the single biggest farm in the valley, the breadbasket.
  Sports fields = grain plots (a football field is ~1.3 acres of wheat).
- SEASONS INVERT: winter is the growing season. Summer is survival crops
  under shade cloth: melons, okra, sweet potato, tepary beans (the native
  desert bean the Sonoran peoples farmed), sorghum.
- THE SOIL ECONOMY: valley dirt is caliche hardpan. Farms run on compost,
  and the compost comes from two places we already placed: the Apex
  LANDFILL (organics mining) and BIOSOLIDS from the WATER RECLAMATION
  PLANT. The 99% loop literally feeds the fields: sewage in, fertilizer
  out. Every calorie in Bohemia has passed through the reclaim plant twice.
- RECLAIMED WATER PRECEDENT IS REAL: Angel Park golf course already runs
  on reclaimed wastewater today. The farms drink from the purple pipe.
