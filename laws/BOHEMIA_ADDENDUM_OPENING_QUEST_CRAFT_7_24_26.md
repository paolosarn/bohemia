## THE FUSED LAWS FOR BOHEMIA'S OPENING QUEST (both streams agreed on these)
1. OPEN ON A JOB, NOT A SPEECH. Purpose arrives as a small, grounded task from someone who
   genuinely needs you — grief rides UNDER the errand, never a "here's a sad story" cutscene.
   (Bloody Baron W1; KCD Skalitz W4, our declared opening template.)
2. IT'S A HOME YOU REPAY. The opening block is a home that shows you kindness; the debt back,
   and your first real choice, are the hook. (Fallout NV Goodsprings.)
3. VERTICAL-SLICE THE WHOLE GAME IN MINIATURE, LOW STAKES. The first quest should MIRROR
   Bohemia's core loop small — walk, talk, survive, choose, live with it — not teach abstractly.
   (Witcher 3 White Orchard.)
4. TEACH BY DOING; QUICK WIN + HOOK IN THE FIRST ~90 SECONDS; play first, explain later; every
   tutorial beat triggered by a player action, not a text box. "The explanation lands 10x better
   once they've already felt something." (BG3 Nautiloid; onboarding research.)
5. NO FETCH QUESTS — every beat is a story with a beginning/middle/end; pass the "time well
   spent, not just spent" test on every beat. An errand is fine only if it triggers real
   conversation, a choice, or a secret. (CDPR "we don't do fetch quests.")
6. PUT THE MYSTERY IN THE STORY, NEVER THE OBJECTIVE. The player always knows WHAT to do; the
   intrigue is WHY and what it COSTS. (WoW GDC.)
7. BRANCH-AND-BOTTLENECK by default: choices register and are remembered, but branches merge so
   momentum always heads to the next beat. (Ashwell's patterns; our own DIALOGUE_SCENE_SPEC.)
8. SUBTEXT-FIRST DIALOGUE: write the subtext of the scene, then make each choice a MOVE within it
   (accept / reject / deflect). Dialogue structure itself (loops slow, trapdoors speed) is
   characterization. (Jon Ingold, "Sparkling Dialogue.")
9. MAKE EVERY CHOICE INTERESTING OR CUT IT: no clear best answer, competing pulls, informed
   decision — and sell it with DELAYED reactivity that surfaces OUTSIDE the conversation,
   concentrated at gates everyone passes. (Sid Meier; Chris Avellone.)
10. KILL "AS YOU KNOW, BOB." Never have characters state what both already know; wedge any
    exposition between two emotionally charged lines so it feels urgent. (dialogue-craft research.)
11. DELAYED TWO-PART CONSEQUENCE: plant a beat early whose weight is unclear until it pays off
    later. (Whispering Hillock / our A6 — this is EXACTLY S09 boundary -> S06 reveal.)
12. RESTRAINT + END ON A FORK THAT BLOWS THE WORLD OPEN: no timer/nag/score at the emotional
    peak; silence is the premium option; then funnel-to-release — the wall coming down opens the
    world. (Last Light restraint; Skyrim funnel-then-release.)

## THE MARCO FOUNDING QUEST — THE PACING SHAPE (the tension curve to author)
Extends the two BUILT quests (quests/bq/S09_THE_BACK_DOOR = pre-wall, S06_BEHIND_THE_FENCE =
post-wall reveal) with a front half, into one arc. Everything Act-1-clean of the machine
(AMALGAMATION_IS_A_GHOST): pure humanity here; contrast makes the later dread land.

- BEAT 0 — THE COLD OPEN (quick win + hook, ~90s): the worst night just happened (one of the
  10 canon start-options; the loss belongs to the attackers, never the player's hand — F2). Buy
  the warm "before" in ONE compressed scene, not an hour (KCD F1). Grief is the hook. Tone + world
  + protagonist established in the first exchange. LOW-then-SPIKE.
- BEAT 1 — THE KNOCK (job not speech): Marco comes to the fence and hands you WORK he needs, not
  condolence. Bond reciprocal from beat one. Grief rides under the task.
- BEAT 2 — THE ERRAND = THE VERTICAL SLICE: doing Marco's job teaches the whole game small — walk
  the beat-grid world, the living dead-Vegas, the survival economy, a whiff of danger, the cart/
  carry. Teach by doing, quick win. [Its exact content = PENDING Paolo — proposal going to him.]
- BEAT 3 — THE BOUNDARY (S09, the interesting decision): Marco is absolute the back is his. Honor
  it (trust DESERVED, bond up) or pry (FAIL, bond down). NOVERB: you cannot demand the secret. A
  move in the subtext of trust; you don't know why yet. (Plants beat 5.)
- BEAT 4 — THE MILESTONE (funnel -> release): after a couple earned beats, the wall comes down.
  The world opens: your first base/outpost, the pseudo-faction born. The spike.
- BEAT 5 — THE REVEAL (S06, delayed two-part payoff, gut-punch on the peak): post-wall, Marco
  reveals the daughter he hid. Recontextualizes every guarded thing (beat 3 now means something
  new). How you RECEIVE it is the editorial/trust fork; quiet acceptance = deepest bond + almost
  no followers (invisible CLOUT), going public = betrayal. Restraint: silence is the premium
  option, no timer, no score.
- BEAT 6 — THE FORK THAT SELLS THE GAME: you have a home, a found family, a faction seed, and dead
  Vegas ahead — plus one thread pulling you onward. The hook that makes them keep playing.
