## THE MENU

1. **THE EXECUTION** (Gears Tactics lesson): a killshot on an ADJACENT
   stunned or floored man is a brutal finisher that REFUNDS the chain
   slot — it does not count against KILLSHOTS/TURN. Shove a man down,
   step in, execute free, keep the chain rolling. Rewards aggression
   and makes the shove a setup tool, not a stall.
2. **NERVE (morale + the break)** (Battle Brothers lesson): a crew
   watching its brothers drop starts to crack. When most of a crew is
   dead, survivors go erratic (worse aim, frantic cycles), and the LAST
   man can BREAK: drops the gun, hands up (the clip exists) or runs
   into the dark. You choose: let him go, or walk up and decide.
   Surrendered men are future loot/econ/lore hooks. Fights end in fear,
   not always in ten corpses.
3. **SUPPRESSING BURST**: spend your engagement on a covering burst at
   a covered man — no damage, no dial, but his next peek is CANCELLED
   and his bead resets. You buy TEMPO instead of blood. Finally a real
   answer to "they're all cycling and I have nothing to do."
4. **OVERWATCH STANCE** (XCOM, dial-gated): a held stance (spends your
   turn) that triggers YOUR dial the instant a chosen man pops — the
   interrupt shot. The dial opens on their tempo, harder package, but
   you own the window instead of waiting for green.
5. **THE HUMAN SHIELD**: stand directly behind an adjacent stunned man
   and his body eats the first hit of the next volley meant for you.
   Dark, Vegas, occupancy-honest, zero new art (bodies already stagger).
6. **WOUNDED GUNS SHAKE**: bodies under 40% already bleed — now they
   FIGHT hurt: tracers wobble visibly, blades limp (tired-walk exists),
   accuracy honestly degraded. You SEE who is almost done.
7. **FACTION TELLS** (extends the archetype research + kick-lock):
   each faction's gunmen carry a signature rhythm tell — Amalgamation
   crews fire in perfect sync ON the kick (eerie, like their lights),
   cartel guns double-tap, tweaker crews fire off-beat and wild. Enemy
   identity you can HEAR and exploit.
8. **THE PRICE OF FALLING** (Sifu lesson, adapted — NOT permadeath,
   the loop is dropped): going down in a fight leaves a MARK on you —
   a scar on the sprite, a wound that starts the next fight at less
   than full, healed by time/econ later. Losing costs something real
   without resetting anything.

MY PICK IF TOLD "YOU PICK": 1 (execution) + 3 (suppressing burst) first
— they deepen the existing shove/tempo game with almost no new art.
Then 2 (nerve) as the big lore-native one.
