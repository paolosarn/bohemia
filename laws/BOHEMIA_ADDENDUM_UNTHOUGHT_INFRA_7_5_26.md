## 1. THE DATA FORTRESS (the one that matters)
Real Las Vegas contains one of the largest data center complexes on EARTH,
and we never placed it. The facts, all real:
- Switch's Core Campus: ELEVEN data centers, 2+ million square feet, up to
  495 MW at full build-out, in the southwest valley by the 215 Beltway.
- It is SELF-POWERED: its own dedicated solar stations (179 MW) feed it.
- It runs on 100% RECYCLED WATER (it already lives inside the 99% loop).
- Its interior structure is called the BLACK IRON FOREST: a framework of
  extra-thick steel that doubles as thermal mass, keeping the halls cool
  THROUGH POWER OUTAGES. It is built to survive exactly the event that
  ends the world in our game.
- Its origin: the founder bought a former ENRON facility at auction for
  $930k, sole bidder, because Enron kept its fiber plans secret. The
  fortress was born from the corpse of the LAST famous economic collapse.
- Its clients included casino data warehouses and, per press, black-ops
  projects. Locals literally describe the windowless building as
  terrifying to look at.
A self-powered, water-recycling, thermally-armored, windowless fortress
complex designed to keep servers alive through the end of the grid, born
from an economic collapse, sitting in the southwest valley. For a game
whose antagonist is MILLIONS OF UPLOADED DATA PORTRAITS that survived the
crash, this is not set dressing. This is a body.

## 2. THE SPRINGS PRESERVE (the name of the city)
Las Vegas means THE MEADOWS. The city is named for natural springs that
watered a green stopover in the desert for centuries, the reason anyone
ever stopped here. The springs were pumped dry by 1962; the Springs
Preserve now marks the spot near downtown/valley view. Story weight: the
ORIGINAL water, the thing the whole city is named after, died sixty years
before the crash, and the city survived it once already by building the
straws. A small preserve cell near downtown-west is one rect. [PENDING]
