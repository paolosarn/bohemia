## PART 1: HOW LOGISTICS ACTUALLY WORKS (the education)
Modern civilization is a conveyor belt pretending to be a place.
- THE TRUCK IS THE ATOM: about 72% of American freight by weight moves on
  trucks. Vegas specifically imports nearly everything on the 15 from
  Southern California warehouses, plus the Union Pacific rail line.
- JUST-IN-TIME: stores do not store. A grocery store holds roughly 2-3
  DAYS of food. Warehouses are not storage, they are FLOW, goods pause
  there for hours. The shelf is the end of a river, and the river is
  trucks arriving every single night. Stop the trucks and any American
  city starts starving in 72 hours. That is the whole apocalypse right
  there, no bombs required.
- WHAT MAKES THE RIVER FLOW, five invisible systems:
  1. FUEL NETWORK: a loaded semi burns a gallon every 6 miles. Truck
     stops every 40-60 miles are not convenience, they are the road's
     circulatory system.
  2. TRUST + PAYMENT: strangers hand each other $80,000 of cargo because
     the DOLLAR settles it and contracts get enforced. Historically this
     is the LETTER OF CREDIT: born in the medieval world (Templars, then
     the Medici banks) because hauling gold across country got you
     robbed. A letter from a trusted banker saying "pay the bearer" moved
     value without moving treasure. Every wire transfer today is that
     letter's grandchild. Kill the dollar and the letters die with it,
     and suddenly nobody hands cargo to a stranger ever again.
  3. SECURITY: roads only work when robbery is rarer than arrival.
  4. INFORMATION: shippers know prices elsewhere. No radio, no market.
  5. ROADS THEMSELVES: asphalt lasts, bridges are the weak points.

## PART 2: HOW IT DIES (the crash sequence, grounded)
Day 1-3: dollar settlement fails, letters of credit freeze, cargo stops
being handed over. Shelves empty in 72 hours. Day 30: fuel allocation
collapses, tanks drain. Year 1: gasoline chemically dies. Year 2-3:
stabilized diesel dies. The roads are fine. The TRUST is dead. Logistics
does not die of broken machines, it dies of broken promises.

## PART 3: HOW IT RESTARTS, BARELY (history's answer: the caravan)
Every time logistics collapsed in history, it restarted the same shape:
- THE CARAVAN: goods bundle together and move with paid protection.
- THE CARAVANSERAI: the Silk Road built fortified rest stops one day's
  travel apart, water, walls, beds, fodder. Our TRUCK STOP on the 15 is
  the caravanserai reborn. Same building, same job, 900 years apart.
- THE GUARANTOR: someone must play banker, holding escrow, vouching for
  traders, eating the loss when a deal breaks. Whoever plays this role
  accumulates terrifying power. (The quiet life lesson: protection of
  trade routes is how states are born. Tolls become taxes. The escort
  becomes the army. Nobody votes for it, it just happens.)
- THE SPREAD: trade exists because prices differ. Salt Lake has fuel and
  no chips-and-medicine glut; Bohemia has reclaimed water, power, salvage.
  The spread pays for the risk of the road.
