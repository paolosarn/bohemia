# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM: THE SURVIVAL ACCOUNTING (7/5/26)
Paolo's demand: nothing on the map exists without an explanation. Corpse
collection is LOCKED as a natural part of the game (presentation story still
pending from the four options). Clock canon: the number serves the ARC, the
game starts roughly a decade after the crash, after absolute hell broke and
things got a little better; 10 vs 15 is tuning, not canon. This doc is the
full accounting: every system a city needs, how Bohemia answered it in the
hell years, what the map shows, and what is grounded vs pending.

## WATER: ANSWERED (the founding miracle)
Intake 3 works below dead pool. The reclaim plant kept running, which is
THE reason Bohemia exists at all: cities that lose sewage treatment die of
cholera and typhoid within months (every historical siege proves it). The
99% loop is not a nice feature, it is the survival event.
GROUNDED TWIST [PENDING]: post-crash, the giant California and Arizona
pumps (CAP, the aqueducts moving most of the river) likely died with the
grid that ran them. Nobody downstream is drinking the Colorado anymore.
LAKE MEAD REFILLS over the hell years. Vegas draws 300k acre-feet of a
~9M acre-foot river. Ten years after the crash, the lake that was dying is
RISING, and Bohemia is the only city drinking it. The bathtub ring
shrinking year by year is a visible map/lore beat if Paolo wants it.

## POWER: MOSTLY ANSWERED
Solar field + substations + the dam. If Mead rises (above), Hoover makes
power again (2GW nameplate) for whoever can run and hold it. Real Vegas is
ringed by utility-scale solar (the Gemini solar+battery project east of
town is one of the biggest in America). GAP [PENDING]: a BATTERY FARM cell
beside the solar field, because solar without storage means the city dies
every night. One rect, factory pattern.

## FOOD: ANSWERED (the ceiling)
Farms on the plowed courses, ~50-80k population ceiling, compost economy
off the landfill and biosolids. Corpse collection LOCKED as part of the
loop's question (story version pending: compost rows / quiet arrangement /
refusal / faction split).

## FUEL + MOVEMENT: HALF ANSWERED [PENDING]
Gasoline dies in 6-12 months chemically (real: gas degrades, diesel lasts
2-3 years stabilized). The CalNev pipeline from California is dead. What
still moves: BICYCLES (the honest answer of every collapsed economy),
horses (limited, they eat farmland), and the loaded one: ELECTRIC VEHICLES
CHARGED ON SOLAR, which is why the Boring Loop cars matter. A city with
solar power and dead gasoline becomes an EV city by force. [PENDING: how

## MEDICINE: THE BRUTAL LEDGER [PENDING]
The medical cluster stands, but manufactured drugs are the hell-years
killer: insulin needs refrigeration and biomanufacture (type 1 diabetics
do not survive year one without a working cold chain, that is the honest
grounded truth), antibiotics run out, then it is scavenged stock, then
compounding from what grows. Ten years in, Bohemia medicine is: trauma
surgery (skills survive), herbals, hoarded stock as treasure, and whatever
the campus labs restarted. [PENDING: does UNLV's chemistry become the
pharmacy, that is a faction-grade asset.]

## CLIMATE: THE 120-DEGREE QUESTION [PENDING]
Vegas summers kill without AC. Grounded survivals: casino towers are
thermal mass fortresses (the lower floors stay livable), the storm tunnels
are 20 degrees cooler (the Homeless knew first, which is why their
territory mattered before anyone respected it), swamp coolers work where
water is cheap, and desert cultures go NOCTURNAL in summer. [PENDING: does
Bohemia summer flip day/night rhythm, that is a whole gameplay season.]

## MONEY: THE MOST BOHEMIA QUESTION OF ALL [PENDING]
An ECONOMIC apocalypse means the money died first. What replaced it in the
hell years? The grounded candidate sitting in plain sight: CASINO CHIPS.
Counterfeit-resistant, denominated, durable, locally trusted, and the
vaults are full of them. A city that ran on chips before the crash keeps
running on them after, except now the house is whoever holds the cage.
Barter, water-hours, and labor scrip are the alternatives history offers.

## REBUILD MATERIALS: THE MISSING UNIQUE INFRASTRUCTURE (Paolo's question answered)
A city-builder needs an answer for what you BUILD WITH, and the desert
gives a hard one: THERE IS NO LUMBER. No forests for hundreds of miles.
Every rebuild in Bohemia is salvage steel, concrete rubble, block, and
rammed earth (the 1855 Mormon Fort is adobe, the oldest building in the
valley already knew). The real material infrastructure around Vegas:
- SLOAN QUARRY (real, south by the 15): aggregate, the concrete feedstock.
- BLUE DIAMOND GYPSUM MINE (real, southwest by Red Rock): drywall/plaster.
- CONCRETE BATCH PLANTS (real, scattered industrial).
