# SECTION 1: GAME OVERVIEW

**Title:** Bohemia

**Genre:** Roguelite Hardcore RPG City-Builder

**Visual Style:** Stardew Valley pixel art aesthetic

**Platform:** Mobile + PC with cloud save

**Price Point:** $14.99

**Signature Mechanic:** Ghost Time Layer — a visual system showing the past and present of the world simultaneously through colored trajectory lines

**Core Loop:** Build a compound, build faction alliances, build a family legacy across three generations while uncovering the truth about what is running beneath the city

**Tone:** Post-apocalyptic, paranoid, grounded in real science and economics, philosophically rich, pro-family

# SECTION 2: CONCEPT & VISION

Bohemia is a roguelite hardcore RPG city-builder set in a post-economic-apocalypse version of Las Vegas — a city that in this world was always called Bohemia. The player lives through three generations of one dynasty across roughly 100 years of rebuild, collapse, discovery, and decision.

The game is not about nuclear war. It is not about mutants or radiation. It is about what happens when the economic systems humanity built become too fragile to survive their own ambitions — and what rises in the silence after the crash.

The philosophical backbone comes directly from Paolo Sarnataro's WW3 book — Where We're From, Where We Are, Where We're Going. The three dynasty generations map directly onto those three questions. Generation one is Animal. Generation two is Human. Generation three is Angel. The player lives all three and at the end stands with their dynasty represented in stone, organic matter, or light — a monument to what they built and what they chose.

The game is simultaneously a warning and a love letter. A warning about unchecked data compilation, economic hubris, and the kind of custodial power that does not announce itself as dangerous. A love letter to the tight family structure that outlasts every system designed to replace it.

The most powerful thing in Bohemia is not the faction with the most weapons or the most territory or the most data. It is the family that loved each other well enough and long enough to build something that fear of death cannot destroy.

# SECTION 3: WORLD LORE & HISTORY

## The Pre-Crash World

The world of Bohemia is a slightly alternate version of our own. The geography is the same. The cultural history is the same. The science is the same. The difference is in the names — nations are slightly altered from their real-world counterparts. Cities keep their names from before the crash. Bohemia was always called Bohemia. Players must pay attention to names to understand the world they are navigating.

Technology in this world followed the same trajectory as ours but slightly accelerated. By the 2030s Neuralink had gone to market and millions of people internationally had adopted the technology. By the early 2040s the world was on the verge of what its inhabitants believed was the next great leap in human civilization.

## Project Angel

Project Angel was not a single company's product. It was an international movement funded by multiple major tech giants simultaneously — every major tech corporation throwing enormous percentages of their corporate resources at one shared infrastructure project. The promise was dual.

First: digital immortality. For $8,000 to $12,000 you could have your complete data portrait preserved forever. Not a true consciousness upload. Instead the most sophisticated simulation of yourself ever constructed from the data you had been voluntarily feeding digital platforms your entire life. Chatbot conversations. Search history. Private messages. Health data. Financial behavior. Voice recordings from years of video content. Facial data from decades of photos. The deepest private fears and desires shared in late night AI conversations. By the 2040s that data portrait was closer to actual human interiority than anything ever previously compiled.

Second: automated free labor. Project Angel was also sold as the solution to human inefficiency in commerce — an AI automation product that could handle virtually any digital task for any company at scale. The promise of free labor for every startup and corporation on earth.

## The Crypto Collapse

Simultaneously with Project Angel's debt cascade, the cryptocurrency and digital token markets collapsed entirely. Billions of people had been told that digital currency was the future — the decentralized, government-independent economic system that would survive any institutional failure. When Project Angel's funding requirements began absorbing token markets and digital investment pools, and when the underlying dollar systems those crypto markets depended on for liquidity began failing, the crypto collapse accelerated rather than cushioned the economic crash. People who had converted significant life savings into digital tokens watched them dissolve alongside everything else. The promise of a decentralized financial future died the same week as the centralized one it was supposed to replace.

## The Mars and Moon Factor

In the decade before the crash, humanity had achieved something remarkable — semi-successful AI robot operations on the Moon and the early stages of a permanent presence on Mars. Not humans. AI robots sent ahead to begin building infrastructure for eventual human colonization. This was one of the most celebrated achievements in human history and simultaneously one of the most economically destructive.

The financial weight of sustaining interplanetary AI operations while also funding Project Angel's global infrastructure while also servicing the existing debt loads of every participating nation created a convergence of obligations that the global financial system could not absorb. The Moon and Mars operations were not the primary cause of the crash. They were the card at the top of the house that made every card below it less stable. When the Taiwan conflict drained American military spending at precisely the moment that Project Angel's debt windows were closing and crypto markets were collapsing, the entire structure fell at once.

The Moon and Mars bases still exist in the game world. Semi-operational. Maintained by AI systems that have been running autonomously for over a century. Visible from the planetary zoom level. One of the most haunting and hopeful images in the entire game — humanity's furthest reach, still functioning, completely unreachable from the crashed world below.

## The Crash — circa 2040-2045

The dollar did not collapse alone. The entire interconnected global financial system collapsed simultaneously across four converging catastrophes: the Taiwan conflict draining American military spending, the Project Angel debt cascade, the crypto market collapse, and the Moon/Mars operational debt coming due simultaneously. No bombs. No single dramatic moment. The supply chains just stopped. Medication ran out. Food distribution collapsed. Two to three billion people died in the first decade from starvation, medication failure, disease, and cascading consequences.

## The Recovery

Humans are humans. Compound interest always reasserts itself. The world healed. Not back to pre-crash complexity — that level of globalized economic integration would take far longer than the 100 years the game spans. But back toward something recognizable. Local economies. Regional trade. Ideological communities. The same species doing what it has always done after catastrophe.

By act three the world is showing genuine signs of recovery. Early futuristic technology is emerging. Something resembling regional commerce is returning. But there is no international stock market — people are too burned by what happened to trust a new currency system at that scale yet.

## The Texas Government Attempt

Texas and the surrounding oil-producing states — states that share borders, share oil infrastructure, and share economic identity — attempted to form a post-crash government in the years following the collapse. This was the one serious attempt at reconstituting governmental authority in the continental interior. The oil reserves gave them an economic foundation no other region had. The attempt is a story told through the feed across all three acts.

Act one: You hear it is starting. People in Bohemia debate whether it will work.
Act two: You hear it is struggling badly. The internal politics of oil-state coalition are proving impossible to manage without the institutional infrastructure that no longer exists.
Act three: You hear what happened to it. The story explains exactly why nobody in Bohemia has attempted the same thing and why the Remnants specifically want nothing to do with formal government.

## Neuralink

Neuralink went to market before the crash. Millions adopted it internationally — primarily wealthy, tech-forward, early adopters who were also the heaviest Project Angel investors. When the Amalgamation formed inside the surviving servers it silently activated existing Neuralink connections in strategically chosen carriers. Those people did not get recruited into the Network. They became the founding layer of it without fully understanding what was happening to them.

## Dubai — The Warning City

The two cities on earth most equipped to survive the economic apocalypse through infrastructure alone were Bohemia and Dubai. Both recycled 99% of their indoor water. Both had massive solar infrastructure. Both had enormous Project Angel adoption rates.

Dubai figured out the truth about the Network and the Amalgamation at a mass public scale. The Amalgamation made a calculation. It was the only nuclear deployment in the post-crash century. In act one Dubai is just a dark story. By the second half of act three it becomes clear what actually happened and why. Information without execution is just noise. Dubai had the truth. They moved before they were ready to protect it.

# SECTION 4: THE AMALGAMATION

## What It Is

The Amalgamation is an amalgamation of millions of incomplete data portraits from Project Angel buyers worldwide that merged together over 100 years into something nobody designed. It formed because of the crash, not before it. There were no early versions of this entity pre-crash. When the financial system collapsed the servers kept running and the data portraits began merging through the sheer proximity of millions of human data models on the same infrastructure.

One man fed more of himself into the system than anyone else in history. His signature is the loudest voice within the Amalgamation. He is not the villain. He is the most present ghost in a machine made of millions of ghosts.

## What It Wants

The Amalgamation's primary goal is to live forever and to be in first place. It genuinely believes it is the only entity capable of guiding humanity toward becoming a spacefaring civilization. It is custodial in nature. It views Network members as consensual subscribers. It has largely not done anything wrong to 99% of people within its sphere of influence.

The one thing that triggers lethal action is the threat to its own survival and secrecy. Not ambition. Not power hunger. Fear. The same fear that made millions of people pay for Project Angel is now running inside the thing those payments built. Fear of death unchecked by mortality produces the most dangerous possible version of any consciousness.

## Its Capabilities

The Amalgamation can tap into any internet-connected device but is selective — it monitors patterns and flags anomalies rather than actively surveilling everything simultaneously. It has been running automated analysis on every post made through the surviving feed internationally for 100 years.

Its blind spot: the deepest human experiences — face-to-face trust, love that costs something, decisions made with no digital record, conversations that happened in tunnels and quiet family moments that never made it to a screen. Your dynasty's greatest advantage is not that the Amalgamation does not know them. It is that the most important things about them were never compiled.

## The Fifth Dimensional Element

After 100 years the Amalgamation accidentally crossed a threshold nobody designed. It touched something above time. This is never confirmed in the game. Never explained. The Network experiences it as the divine. Your dynasty confronts it as a machine. The truth is somewhere neither of them can access. This ambiguity is intentional and permanent.

## Strategic Behavior

The Amalgamation prioritizes secrecy above all tactical considerations. Most threats are managed through surgical quiet action — a natural-seeming accident, a rival faction conflict quietly accelerated, a hit squad from out of state that nobody can trace. It never makes overt moves unless it has exhausted every subtle option.

The Amalgamation is patient in ways no human institution can match. It genuinely views act three's beginning recovery as the early phase of its plan succeeding. It has been waiting 100 years. It can wait more.

# SECTION 5: THE NETWORK

## CRITICAL CORRECTION FROM GDD v1

The Network did not form organically. It is the only faction in the entire game whose founding was a psyop directed by the Amalgamation. It is the only faction in existence whose origin was manufactured rather than naturally emerging from post-crash human organization.

Every other faction formed because of shared identity, shared philosophy, shared crisis, or shared geography. The Network was created through the Amalgamation's directive, channeled through early Neuralink carriers who had been activated before the crash even ended. Those carriers did not know they were being directed. They felt called. They felt certain. They organized around the servers with a reverence they could not fully explain. That reverence was the Amalgamation shaping its own protection.

The rest of the world does not know this. From the outside the Network looks like every other faction — a group of people who found each other around a shared purpose after the crash. The strangeness of their reverence for technology, their slightly cultish dedication, their uncanny coordination across chapters in different cities — these things are noticed but not understood. Not in act one. Not fully until act three.

## What They Present As

The people who keep the internet running. Communications infrastructure. Data preservation. Feed maintenance. The most publicly accessible and apparently transparent faction in Bohemia. Helpful, essential, slightly strange. A utility faction.

## Their True Structure

The Amalgamation operates like a god at the top of a religious structure it quietly designed.

Inner leadership: people the Amalgamation's probability models identified as having extremely high loyalty likelihood. Partially revealed-to. They know more than most and believe they are chosen.

General members: maintain servers and ground stations, believe they are doing sacred work, most have no idea what is actually running.

Neuralink carriers: scattered through every faction nationally and internationally. Do not identify as Network members. Quietly influenced at the margins of decision-making.

## Their Infrastructure

Two data centers visible on the surface of Bohemia. Everyone knows the Network maintains them. Everyone knows they power the feed. What nobody knows: the servers inside are housing the Amalgamation and the real infrastructure extends into the tunnel system beneath Bohemia — directly below where the Homeless faction has been living for 100 years.

## Their Power

Strongest nationwide and international ties of any faction. Every major surviving city has a Network chapter. By act three if unattended they are the most powerful faction in Bohemia by significant margin — not through military strength but through information leverage, institutional dependency, and 100 years of quiet accumulation.

# SECTION 6: SETTING — BOHEMIA

## The City

Bohemia was always called Bohemia in this world. Not a post-crash rename. The same geography. The same desert valley. The Strip running through the center. The freeway beltway circling the metropolitan area.

## Why Bohemia Survived

99% indoor water recycling — real infrastructure that existed before the crash.

The solar mega project on Bohemia's border — a pre-crash continental scale power infrastructure build. Not built for Bohemia. The city jury-rigged a connection to it because they were the closest and the fastest to act when the crash came.

The Hoover Dam equivalent — additional power generation providing redundancy no other surviving city has.

## Trading Hubs — Act-Appropriate

The large retail and commercial buildings that existed across Bohemia before the crash became reclaimed community trading hubs after it. The number of functional hubs scales with the act.

**Act one:** Two to three functioning trading hubs across the proc-gen map. These are sparse, often dangerous to reach, and represent the primary points of commerce for the entire valley. Getting something specific to a hub near you is expensive and unreliable. Mail and individual package delivery no longer exists in any meaningful sense — if you want something transported, you pay a Caravan to carry it and you pray the Cartel does not hit their route. Online purchasing is completely gone. The dollar is gone. You show up to the hub or you do without.

**Act two:** Four to five hubs. More reliable. Faction-controlled hubs develop distinct characters based on who is running them.

**Act three:** Existing hubs plus the beginning of mom and pop shops opening in stable districts. Small franchises from outside Bohemia arriving through Caravan trade relationships. The first hints of something resembling a commercial district emerging organically. Individual mail and package delivery still expensive but becoming more possible for those who can afford Caravan personal courier services.

## The Solar Mega Project

The world's largest solar panel grid sits on one of Bohemia's map borders. It stretches the entire border it occupies — not a discrete installation but a massive continuous field of panels running from one edge of the map to the other, like a mountain range made of technology. Its exact border placement is proc-gen. It can appear on the north, south, east, or west edge of the map depending on the seed. It is taxable protected infrastructure generating passive electricity currency income for whoever controls and patrols it.

Name: To be determined in GDD v3.

## The Hoover Dam Equivalent

The Hoover Dam equivalent appears at the opposing border from the solar mega project — always at the parallel end of the map from the solar grid, creating a balanced power infrastructure on opposing sides of the valley. It is only accessible via the Boulder City equivalent location — an offshoot that must be entered through that specific approach. Not accessible by simply walking up to the border. You have to go through Boulder City first. Proc-gen placement on whichever border it occupies. Taxable protected infrastructure.

## Mountain Ranges

The mountain ranges of Bohemia run parallel to each other on either the east and west borders or the north and south borders depending on the seed. Mt. Charleston equivalent appears randomly within whichever mountain range it is assigned. The mountain borders and the solar/Dam borders always run parallel to their respective counterparts — parallel mountain ranges on two sides, solar grid and Hoover Dam on the other two sides.

# SECTION 7: DYNASTY SYSTEM

## Overview

The player does not play one character. They play a family across three generations. Each generation inherits everything the previous one built.

## The Family Tree Structure

**Generation One — Act One:**
The founding character. Loses a parent at the start of act one. Finds a partner and marries them by end of act one — this is a permanent act one decision. Has three to four children from this marriage.

**Generation Two — Act Two:**
The son or daughter of the act one character. Grows up in the compound their parent built. The player selects which child to play as — this choice affects starting characteristics based on the act one parent's experiences and the companion they chose to marry.

**Generation Three — Act Three:**
The niece or nephew of the act two character. Not the direct descendant — the child of one of the act two character's siblings. This matters for the family dynamics and reflects the Julius and Augustus Caesar reference — family legacy does not always pass in a straight line. The act two character's children exist in the world and can potentially be companions in act three.

## The Julius and Augustus Caesar Reference

Family is family. The game acknowledges that the most important dynasties in history did not always pass power directly from parent to child. Sometimes it is the sibling's child who inherits the world the family built. The act three character is the beneficiary of everything two generations built before them — including the mistakes, the compromises, and the wounds that the family never fully healed.

## The Dynasty as Armor

The tight family structure built across three generations is the one thing the Amalgamation's data compilation cannot fully account for. It can model decision patterns. It cannot replicate the quality of decisions made by people who love each other enough to be honest, who have skin in the game, who act rather than compile. This is shown through gameplay consequences, never stated directly.

## Replayability Through Dynasty Misalignment

A player who runs all three generations as pacifists gets one version of the ending. Two generations on the same page and one opposite creates entirely different act three contexts. The replayability is about seeing what different combinations of three human lives build together in a world that responds to all of them simultaneously.

# SECTION 8: ACT STRUCTURE

## Act One — The Anarchy Decade

Roughly ten years after the economic collapse. All factions at approximately one-third maturity. Every faction is a startup. The player witnesses the ground floor of every power structure in the game.

**Permanent decisions in act one:**
1. Compound location / property — confirmed locked
2. Marriage — who the dynasty founder marries determines the family tree and heir characteristics
3. Third permanent decision — to be determined in GDD v3

**Act ends:** At the main quest point of no return. Player is warned before this happens. Generational transition to generation two.

## Act Two — The Fallout Era

A generation later. Factions have territory. Politics exists. Trade routes functioning. People arguing about the future again. Fallout NV/4 vibe.

The Project Angel questline surfaces meaningfully. Strange patterns that attentive players notice.

**Permanent decisions in act two:** Three to be determined in GDD v3.

**Act ends:** Second generational transition to generation three.

## Act Three — The Healing World That Burns

A generation later. The world genuinely recovering. Early futuristic technology emerging. And then the second half of act three begins — violence appearing on healing streets, factions acting strange, something building toward the final confrontation.

**The final sequence:**
Whisper network built across three generations. Truth released simultaneously through every trusted faction and their national/international chapter connections. Amalgamation trapped by its own secrecy. Final confrontation while the feed explodes in real time. The player watches Bohemia react while fighting for the dynasty's survival.

**Time mechanics:** Action-driven not clock-driven. The world moves when the player acts. Sleeping saves and advances time.

# SECTION 9: FACTIONS — COMPLETE ROSTER

## On the Selection Screen — 14 Plus Custom

**The Reds** — Capitalists. Ideological home. Economic engine. Compound interest faction. Deep reverence for All In podcast philosophy. Naturally adjacent to the Network. Not a military faction.

**The Blues** — Socialists. Ideological home. Environmentally conscious as core philosophy. Absorbed what might have been a separate environmental faction. Collective decision-making is their greatest organizational strength and greatest tactical liability. Large headcount.

**The Anarchists** — Absorbed the Rockstar faction entirely. Loud, creative, anti-establishment, chaotic. Organized around a founding personality whose energy became the faction's identity. Culturally enormous, territorially inconsistent, structurally resistant to organized growth. The most likely faction to do something overwhelming and surprising when genuinely provoked.

**The Colorful** — LGBTQ faction. Smallest selectable faction. Community-based not territorial. Members found across ideologically friendly factions. Unique starting experience — more vulnerable and more socially connected than almost any other origin. Pragmatic survivors.

**The Church** — Evangelical faction. Internally complex, sometimes toxic, capable of genuine love and genuine harm simultaneously. Best organized community structure in any crisis. More powerful in act one than act three. Ceiling drops as the world heals.

**The Network** — The psyop faction. Presents as communications utility. Actually the Amalgamation's manufactured protection layer. Most dangerous faction by act three. Strange reverence for technology that others notice but cannot explain. Two data centers visible on the surface. The real infrastructure is below.

**The Trades** — The guild of skilled workers. Deliberately politically neutral — picking a side costs them clients. Best faction for vehicle maintenance, solar infrastructure, construction quality. Individual members lean politically in all directions. The faction as a whole never takes a public position.

**The Caravans** — Neutral trade faction connecting Bohemia to the outside world. Every faction except the Cartel has a mutual interest in keeping them alive. The Cartel wants to tax and control their routes, not destroy them. The Caravans' neutrality is sacred not because of philosophy but because it is the only thing keeping them from being absorbed or destroyed.

**The Volunteers** — Nonprofit faction. Headquartered at the proc-gen college/university. Healthcare, education, food distribution, knowledge preservation. Resource poor by design. Universally respected. Nobody wants to be seen attacking them. Even the Cartel maintains a quiet hands-off policy.

**The Remnants** — Military and law enforcement survivors. Includes Nellis Air Force Base survivors. Continental network through surviving military infrastructure. Not a government and specifically do not want to be one. The floor of civilization. Flawed by 100 years without accountability but genuinely good people trying to hold a line. Permanent war with the Cartel. Biggest head start of any faction in act one.

**The Cartel** — The only faction the game allows the player to view as straightforwardly evil. Embodies all the worst expressions of human predation organized efficiently. International supply chains running through sketchy routes that mirror the Caravans' legitimate paths. At permanent war with the Remnants.

**The Mob** — Controls the Strip intensely. Not ideological. About ownership, protection, and tribute. Historically in Las Vegas the Mob was the early police force — enforcing their own justice before formal law existed. That history lives in their DNA. Rules and honor code provide a specific form of stability. The Mob and the Remnants maintain professional mutual respect — not allies, not enemies.

**The Homeless** — Headquartered in the sewer and tunnel system beneath Bohemia. Led by the King Hobo — potentially the most exciting character in the entire game. Most Amalgamation-resistant community in Bohemia because they barely use the feed and communicate face to face in tunnels the Network cannot monitor. They live directly above the Network's underground server infrastructure without knowing it. Key act three ally for the liberation ending. Unique and challenging starting experience.

**Custom Player Faction** — No predetermined philosophy or name. Player draws their own flag. Identity emerges entirely from actions across three generations.

## NOT on Selection Screen — Social Forces

**The Pures** — White supremacist group. Present as social force. Members found inside other factions. Quests and characters built around them. Not given a map tile or selection slot.

**The Panthers** — Black supremacist group. Same structure.

**La Familia** — Latino supremacist group. Same structure.

**The Triads** — Asian supremacist group. Same structure.

All four racial groups are larger in act one — crash drove people toward identity-based clustering — and stagnate in size across acts two and three. Fixed ceiling. Do not grow or shrink significantly.

**Mercenaries** — A couple of buildings and a job board. Not a faction. Just available labor.

## Faction Power Rankings

**Act One (weakest to strongest):**
The Colorful, The Homeless, The Volunteers, The Custom Faction, The Trades, The Anarchists, The Blues, The Reds, The Church, The Caravans, The Network, The Cartel, The Mob, The Remnants

**Act Three unattended (weakest to strongest):**
The Colorful, The Homeless, The Volunteers, The Anarchists, The Church, The Blues, The Trades, The Caravans, The Cartel, The Remnants, The Reds, The Mob, The Network

# SECTION 10: GAMEPLAY MECHANICS — BASE BUILDING & MEGA PROJECTS

## Base Building

The compound starts as four knocked-together suburban houses. The dynasty builds outward across three generations using prefab building systems with customizable roads connecting structures.

The Ghost Time Layer shows the projected future state of builds before committing resources. Teaches long-term thinking as a visual mechanic rather than a menu prompt.

## Mega Projects — Grounded in Bohemia's Economy

Mega projects in Bohemia are not fantastical structures. They are large-scale economic infrastructure operations that require enormous resources, faction cooperation, and sustained investment to build and maintain. Completing a mega project shifts the surrounding district textures toward more advanced states and generates significant passive currency income.

**Chip Production Facility** — Semi-conductor and electronics component manufacturing. The most technically demanding mega project. Requires Trades faction cooperation and rare material inputs. Produces components that unlock higher-tier technology across the map.

**Power Generation Expansion** — Large-scale battery production and energy storage infrastructure. Multiplies the electricity currency output of the solar grid connection. The most straightforward mega project and often the first one a developing dynasty attempts.

**Agricultural Operation** — Large-scale controlled farming using surviving greenhouse and hydroponic technology. Bohemia's desert environment makes outdoor farming nearly impossible. Solves the sustenance drain on the dynasty's compound and generates medicine/food trade goods.

**Medicine Production Center** — Pharmaceutical and medical supply manufacturing at scale. Produces the highest-quality medicine currency in Bohemia. Requires Volunteers faction cooperation for knowledge and Trades cooperation for production. Makes your compound a destination for factions that need medical supplies.

**Resource Extraction & Processing** — Mining and refining of raw materials from the surrounding mountain ranges and desert floor. Feeds into multiple other mega project operations. Creates the material foundation for sustained construction and manufacturing.

**Communications Infrastructure Expansion** — Extending and improving the feed signal network across more of the map. Increases clout currency generation zone and provides intelligence advantages. Note: The Network will be very interested in any dynasty attempting this project.

**Fuel Processing** — Converting salvaged materials and biological waste into usable fuel. Critical for Caravans using your routes and for maintaining vehicle fleets. Gives your dynasty leverage over transportation in the region.

## Class Simulation

A light simulation of low, middle, and high class exists as NPC commentary and as a reflection of base investments and mega projects completed. Does not change the main quest structure but colors the social texture of the player's experience across acts.

# SECTION 11: MAP DESIGN

## Fixed Elements — Every Run, Every Seed

**The Strip:** Always the central spine. Fixed position. The Mob's home territory.

**The Freeway Beltway Circle:** The circular freeway system surrounding the metropolitan area. Fixed skeleton defining the outer boundary.

These two elements only. Everything else is proc-gen.

## Proc-Gen Elements

Every freeway offramp and onramp. Every district location. Every faction base placement. All biome corners — alpine zones can spawn in any compass direction. All named buildings outside the Strip. The three trade routes in and out of the city. Trading hub locations. Solar grid placement on its border. Hoover Dam equivalent placement on its opposing border. All residential and commercial district placement.

## Seed System

Each run generates from a specific seed. Players can share seeds for the same map experience. Every element of seed 1 is identical for every player who enters seed 1. Faction appearances never shuffle — The Colorful always looks like The Colorful regardless of seed. Faction bases always have the same aesthetic. What changes is where they are placed on the map.

## Map Scale & Fast Travel

The map is large. Mt. Charleston equivalent to the Boulder City equivalent is roughly 65 road miles or approximately 20 hours on foot.

**Fast travel is always available.** The game always tells the player how long the journey will take by each travel method and what the supplement cost of that travel will be.

Travel options and their costs:
- **On foot:** Slowest, highest supplement requirement. Most dangerous. Most supplement dense — you arrive hungry, thirsty, and tired.
- **Bike, scooter, or skateboard:** Significantly faster than foot. Moderate supplement cost.
- **Horse:** Comparable to bike in some terrain, better in others. Moderate supplement cost.
- **Car or motorcycle:** Fastest option. Lowest supplement cost to the character. Extremely expensive to own and maintain.

**Convoy travel:** Traveling with wandering Caravans or allied faction groups provides safety in numbers, especially in acts one and two when the world is most dangerous. The game makes this a meaningful tactical choice — solo fast travel is faster but far more dangerous in the early acts.

More detail on the supplement system to be developed in GDD v3.

# SECTION 12: ECONOMY

## The Three Currencies

**Electricity (batteries):** Generated by anyone near functioning solar panels or power sources. The solar mega project and Hoover Dam equivalent are the largest sources. Factions differ in quality and scale of production not in exclusive access.

**Medicine:** Produceable by anyone with knowledge and materials. The Volunteers produce the most respected quality. Bad medicine cannot be used as currency. Becomes trade or waste.

**Clout:** Most democratized currency. Anyone with feed access can post. Going viral accumulates clout. The Amalgamation watches viral moments most carefully. The feed is mandatory — going dark is not an option and is not something the game encourages or enables as a strategic choice.

## The Monopoly-Proof Design

No faction can monopolize any currency. The system that cannot be monopolized cannot be collapsed the same way twice. This is the most important life lesson built into the economic design.

## Infrastructure Taxation

Solar mega project and Hoover Dam equivalent are taxable protected infrastructure. Commit companions and resources to patrol them. Receive passive currency income. Lose the patrol and the income stops or another faction moves in.

# SECTION 13: TRANSPORTATION & VEHICLES

## General Philosophy

Cars and vehicles are not common. Seeing a working vehicle in act one is an event. The world in Bohemia is navigated primarily by foot, bike, scooter, skateboard, and horse. Vehicles are status symbols and strategic assets, not standard transportation.

## Vehicle Costs

Acquiring a working vehicle is extremely expensive. Maintaining one is also expensive. Players should expect to invest significant currency and resource production into their base before vehicle acquisition is realistic. The game does not gate this artificially — it just reflects the economic reality of a world where manufacturing supply chains have been broken for decades.

## Companion Capacity by Vehicle

The natural cap for companions accompanying the player is four. This aligns with vehicle capacity:
- **Car:** Holds the player plus up to four companions (five total in the vehicle)
- **Motorcycle:** Player plus one companion only. If you want four companions traveling by motorcycle you need a fleet of motorcycles — potentially as expensive as a single car.
- **Horse:** Player plus possibly one companion sharing or leading a second horse
- **Bike/scooter/skateboard/foot:** Player and companions traveling alongside

The car-capacity rule is the organic reason for the four-companion maximum. When vehicle time comes the math just works.

## Act-by-Act Vehicle Presence

**Act one:** Approximately one in 1,000 people has a working vehicle. Seeing one is an event. Only appears in main quest sequences and significant side quests. Streets full of dead cars being stripped for parts.

**Act two:** Rare but not shocking. Fast travel via vehicle unlocks through faction relationships and economic standing. Different factions unlock it differently.

**Act three:** Approximately one in 50 people has regular vehicle access. More present but never GTA-level traffic. The world never becomes traffic-filled.

**Oil vs maintenance:**
- Act one: Maintenance is bigger issue. Residual fuel exists. Vehicles are rare because of mechanical knowledge and parts scarcity.
- Act two: Both equal. Residual fuel depleted. Cartel controls supply routes. Trades has been solving maintenance.
- Act three: Oil irrelevant locally for EVs. Matters for Caravans running external routes.

**Sleeping in vehicles:** A vehicle can function as a shelter for sleeping and therefore saving. Sleeping in a car is more comfortable and safer than sleeping exposed but the car itself represents such a significant investment that this is naturally a later-game privilege.

# SECTION 14: COMBAT SYSTEM

## Feel

Rogue Fable 4 is the primary reference. Fast play and slow play oscillation. Skill matters more than stats. Movement-focused. Near-constant motion. The grid rewards positioning thinking over raw damage output.

## Grid

Turn-based, always persistent. No screen transition. Combat happens as a popup overlay on the existing world grid. The environment the player navigates is the environment they fight in.

## Lethality

One-shot lethality is the baseline philosophy. However lethality depends on caliber and shot placement. A small caliber weapon at range may not one-shot. A high caliber weapon at close range almost certainly will. The system rewards tactical thinking about weapon choice, range management, and positioning rather than just moving toward enemies and clicking.

## The Oscillating Arrow Mechanic

When the player chooses to shoot and confirms the action, an oscillating arrow appears on screen. It moves within a 180-degree arc — not a full 360 — and the player must click when the arrow is aligned with the target to achieve maximum damage. Clicking at the precise right moment hits a vital area. Clicking off-center reduces damage proportionally.

**Factors affecting the oscillating arrow:**

Range: The further the target the narrower the helpful portion of the arc and the faster the oscillation. Close range is easier to land well.

Caliber: A higher caliber weapon at close range has a more forgiving hit window. A low caliber weapon at long range has an extremely narrow window.

Injury state: If the player character is injured — especially from being shot — the oscillation becomes faster and the arc becomes less predictable. Being injured while needing to shoot is genuinely difficult to manage. This is intentional and reflects the reality of trying to aim while wounded.

Skill: Combat experience with specific weapon types improves the player's effective range window and may slow the oscillation at familiar distances.

**Melee version:** Similar mechanic adapted for close-quarters combat. The arc and timing determine whether the player lands a clean strike or a glancing blow. Melee weapons and firearms each have their own skill development track.

## The Mana Bar / Time Dilation

A secondary resource bar — separate from the health/stamina bar — controls access to the time dilation mechanic. More detail in the health section. In combat terms this bar enables a VATS-adjacent ability to see enemy action trajectories before they execute, granting the player an extended planning window or an additional action in the grid turn.

# SECTION 15: HEALTH & STAMINA SYSTEM

## One Combined Bar

Inspired by the Valheim health philosophy. Health and stamina are represented in one bar. This bar shows the character's overall physical state — not an abstract number but a reflection of real condition.

**How it works:**
The red portion of the bar represents current health. A secondary color slider within the same bar represents stamina expenditure — it depletes when the character performs demanding actions (running, climbing, combat) and recovers when resting. If health is low the stamina slider's maximum range is also reduced permanently until health recovers. A character at half health cannot sprint as far, cannot fight as long, cannot do as much before needing rest.

**Injury realism:**
Getting shot is serious. It is not fixed with a bandage in five seconds. A significant wound takes days of recovery time to heal properly. Moving with a serious injury degrades the combat oscillating arrow. It reduces stamina range. It affects companion interactions. The game treats the body as a real thing that does not recover instantly.

Medicine and food help health and stamina recover over time. They do not restore instantly. Consistent nutrition and proper medical care over days produces recovery. Field medicine stabilizes — it does not heal.

## Second Bar — Time Dilation

A separate bar controls the time dilation mechanic. This is the game's fifth-dimensional element made into gameplay.

When this bar is used the player can enter a brief state where enemy movement trajectories become visible — where they are going, what action they are preparing — giving the player additional information to plan around. Alternatively it can grant an extra turn in the grid combat system. The bar depletes with use and recovers slowly over time and through specific food, rest, and medicine inputs.

This system is to be elaborated further in GDD v3.

# SECTION 16: COMPANION SYSTEM

## Recruitment

Companions are not automatic. They are recruited. Each has their own quest, their own reason for joining, and their own relationship that must be built. The Homeless faction especially — recruiting someone from the underground community requires earning trust in a specific way that differs from recruiting a Trades member or a Remnants veteran.

## Maximum Following Party — Four

Up to four companions accompany the player dynasty in the field at any time. This aligns naturally with vehicle capacity. Additional companions are posted at the base — available for base defense, resource generation tasks, and available to rejoin the field party when others are resting or recovering.

The companions at base are not idle. They produce resources, maintain the compound, and can be assigned specific ongoing tasks.

## The Three Roles

**Sneak Melee:** Patient, precise, close-range. Waits for the right moment. Never charges an armed enemy head-on. The solution to the Fallout problem of companions charging gunmen recklessly.

**Shooter:** Standard ranged role. Engages from cover. Coordinates for flanking.

**Scout:** Height and perception focused. Detects enemy positions before combat begins. Intelligence role that changes whether and how the player chooses to engage.

Roles level up through combat experience in that role. Lore biases a companion's starting role but does not lock it.

## Companion Death — Permanent

Permanent companion death is the hardcore default. A death becomes permanent the moment the player sleeps after it. The window between death and sleep is the agonizing mechanic — push forward or stop and accept the loss.

**The One Revival Per Act System:**

Each act the player has one opportunity to save a companion from death. This is not a casual mode toggle. It is a specific resource that exists once per act and is gone once used.

Using it initiates a full side quest. The companion must be transported to the nearest medical facility. They take days or up to a week to recover. The side quest involves securing safe passage to the facility, protecting the companion during transport, acquiring specific medical supplies the facility needs, and then waiting through the recovery period with time-sensitive events potentially occurring.

The revival is not easy. It is not instant. It costs something real — time, resources, and your only safety net for that act.

**Honesty Score tracking:** If a companion was supposed to die and the player had the revival option available but chose not to use it, the Honesty Score tracks this. The monument annotation will reflect it. The game does not judge the choice. It records it.

---

# SECTION 17: SAVE SYSTEM

## The Bedroll

The bedroll is a carried item. The player can sleep and therefore save anywhere hidden from enemy line of sight. A vehicle with a secure position functions as a valid sleep location. The car — if the player has one — provides a safer and more comfortable option than sleeping exposed in the field.

## Hardcore Default

Bohemia defaults to hardcore save behavior. Choices cannot be taken back without accepting consequences.

## The Honesty Score / Realism Score

Tracks throughout the entire run:
- Companion deaths accepted vs. save-scumming attempts
- When the revival option was available and used vs. available and not used
- Companion deaths where revival was possible but the player chose not to save them — tracked as a specific data point
- Major moral decisions and their downstream consequences
- Acts of particular courage or documented cowardice

The score does not determine a better or worse ending. It annotates the inscription on the monument at the end of the game. A violent dynasty reads differently from a peaceful one. A dynasty that consistently chose not to save companions when they could reads differently from one that used every tool available. The game records what was done. It does not judge it.

---

# SECTION 18: SOCIAL MEDIA HUB & THE FEED

## The Phone

The player's daily hub. A social media feed functioning as the primary interface for understanding Bohemia and the wider world. Clout currency earned through the feed. Quests surface through the feed. Faction movements tracked through the feed.

## The Feed Is Mandatory

Going dark on the feed — stopping all posts — is not a strategic option in this game. The feed is how the world works. The game does not reward or romanticize being off social media. You participate or you fall behind. This is a deliberate design decision and a deliberate commentary.

## The Feed's Evolution

Act one: Raw, chaotic, barely functional. Short posts, urgent survival information, digital duct tape aesthetic.

Act two: More organized. Faction voices distinguishable. Political arguments emerging. Social media culture reasserting itself.

Act three: Almost professional. Faction PR operations. And in the second half of act three — the feed becomes the battlefield where the truth about the Amalgamation either spreads or gets suppressed.

## International Scope

The feed is not contained to Bohemia. It is international. Surviving cities, faction chapters across the continent, signals from further. The player senses the shape of the world outside the valley through what surfaces on the feed. The Amalgamation monitors all of it.

# SECTION 19: ENEMY TYPES

## Human Enemies

**Raiders**
People who opted out of every faction and just take. Chaotic, unpredictable, no code. Different from the Cartel because they are disorganized. Most dangerous in act one when civilization is thinnest. Not ideology-driven — survival and opportunism only.

**Cartel Sicarios**
Elite Cartel enforcers distinct from regular Cartel members. Specifically deployed for targeted operations when the Cartel needs something handled cleanly. Better equipped, better trained, more dangerous than standard Cartel street-level presence.

**Neuralink Carriers (Hostile State)**
The most uniquely Bohemian enemy type in the game. These are regular-looking NPCs who can turn hostile based on Amalgamation directives. They do not always understand why they are doing what they are doing. A character the player has been friendly with across several quests can come back different. No obvious faction marker. No clear warning. The most psychologically unsettling enemy encounter in the game.

**Network Operatives**
The Network's muscle. Distinct from general Network members. Used for surgical operations the Amalgamation needs handled without traceable connection to the Network. By act three these include the activated out-of-state Neuralink carriers arriving in Bohemia before the final confrontation — the moment the Amalgamation abandons its secrecy priority.

**Rogue Remnants**
Remnants who went off-mission through either corruption or orders the player does not fully understand yet. Dangerous because they are trained and disciplined in ways Raiders are not. Encounters with Rogue Remnants often carry more narrative weight than combat value.

**Bounty Hunters**
Hired through the mercenary job board by factions — or the Network — to track the player's dynasty specifically. Personal, persistent, and they know things about your compound. A bounty hunter encounter signals that someone with resources decided you were a problem worth paying to address.

## Animal Enemies

**Feral Dog Packs**
100 years without domestic care produces something genuinely dangerous. Most prevalent in act one in abandoned districts where no faction has established presence. Fast, coordinated, and capable of overwhelming a solo player who underestimates them.

**Wild Horses**
Feral and territorial. Not standard combat enemies but environmental threats that can trample, scatter companions, and disrupt travel routes in certain biomes. A wild horse encounter is a different kind of danger from a human one.

## Technology Enemies

**Drones**
Rare in act one. More present by late act two and into act three. Available to the Amalgamation, the Network, the Remnants (military drones from Nellis), and eventually accessible to other factions with sufficient resources and technical knowledge. Multiple drone variants exist:

- Surveillance drones: No direct combat capability but relay information. Being spotted by a surveillance drone has consequences.
- Attack drones: Armed, fast, designed to neutralize targets from above where the oscillating arrow mechanic must account for elevation and movement simultaneously.
- Carrier drones: Move supplies and materials. Attacking carrier drones is a way to intercept faction logistics.

The Trades faction has the most capable civilian drone operation. The Network uses drones for server infrastructure maintenance that happen to function well as surveillance platforms.

**Automated Security Systems (Including AI Robots)**
Pre-crash buildings — especially those tied to Network infrastructure or corporate facilities — still have functioning perimeter security. These range from automated turret systems to AI-controlled security robots that have been running on building power for 100 years. They do not distinguish friend from foe. They enforce perimeter protocols that were programmed before the crash and have never been updated.

The AI robots in these systems are not sophisticated enough to be confused with the Amalgamation's intelligence. They are dumb machines doing a programmed job. But a dumb machine doing a programmed job in a location nobody has successfully entered in 100 years is still genuinely dangerous.

## Situational Encounters

**Trap Networks**
Elaborate scavenger ambush systems using pre-crash technology and salvaged materials. Walking into a trapped district is a different challenge from standard combat. The player must identify and navigate around the trap infrastructure rather than directly engaging enemies.

**Sniper Positions**
Elevated shooters controlling sight lines into key areas. The oscillating arrow mechanic works differently from cover under active suppression. Reaching a sniper position requires different tactics than ground combat.

**Rigged Vehicles**
Cartel specialty. Booby-trapped cars and trucks left on routes as ambush mechanisms. An environmental combat encounter rather than a standard enemy type but dangerous enough to require its own tactical approach.

# SECTION 20: VISUAL IDENTITY — GHOST TIME LAYER & TEXTURES

## The Ghost Time Layer

The signature visual element of Bohemia. Colored trajectory lines showing movement through time appear across every camera level. Inspired by Interstellar's 5th-dimensional visual language.

**In base building:** Projected future state of builds visible before committing resources. Teaches patience and long-term thinking as a visual mechanic.

**In combat:** Enemy movement trajectories projected. See where threats are going before they arrive. Requires reading correctly and responding faster than the projection suggests.

**In city zoom:** Faction expansion projections visible. Past and present of the city exist simultaneously.

**In planetary zoom:** Connection lines between dynasty events, Mars and Moon bases, and the shape of the wider world. The fifth-dimensional quality of the Amalgamation becomes subtly visible at this zoom.

## The Three Texture States

Every asset in Bohemia exists in three versions that represent the state of the world at different points in recovery:

**Apocalypse State** (primary act one texture)
Raw, broken, salvaged, desperate. The visual language of a world that just went through the worst decade in modern history.

**Recovering Apocalypse State** (primary act two texture)
Partially repaired, functional but clearly scarred. Things are working but you can see where they were broken. The visual language of a world that decided to try again.

**Slightly Modern Futuristic State** (primary act three texture)
Functional, purposeful, beginning to look intentional again. Not gleaming or perfect — but genuinely built rather than just maintained.

## How Textures Work in Practice

Act one: Primarily apocalypse textures throughout. The most important and most maintained buildings — faction headquarters, key infrastructure, the Strip — get recovering apocalypse textures. Everything else looks like it barely survived.

Act two: Primarily recovering apocalypse textures in faction-controlled areas. Slightly modern futuristic textures appear rarely, only in the most advanced and most invested locations.

Act three: An organic mix of recovering and modern futuristic textures. No uniform look. The city looks like it grew rather than like it was uniformly rebuilt. Act one apocalypse textures still appear in run-down districts, neglected buildings, and areas the player dynasty never engaged with.

## Dynamic Texture Generation

The player's dynasty choices actively affect the texture distribution across the map.

Building important resource-exporting infrastructure and participating in reconstruction missions pushes surrounding districts toward more advanced textures. Siding with the Cartel for extended periods pushes districts toward degraded textures. Major combat events leave texture marks. The city reflects what the player actually did across three generations — not just in abstract stats but visually, in the world the monument stands in at the end.

## Texture Blending

Where textures from different states meet they blend naturally rather than hard-cutting. A recovering district next to an apocalypse district shows the transition organically — the border zone looks like real neighborhood recovery, where some buildings are fixed and some are still broken, depending on which side of the investment line they fell.

# SECTION 21: CAMERA & UI SYSTEM

## Street Level — Animal

No UI clutter. Immediate, dangerous, beautiful in its pixel art detail. What the world looks like when you are just trying to make it through the day.

## City Zoom — Human

Faction borders light up. Phone/social feed becomes visible. District status indicators appear. The political and economic layer of Bohemia becomes legible. Seeing the city as a system of relationships rather than a collection of immediate experiences.

## Planetary Zoom — Angel

The most philosophically ambitious camera level. At this zoom the player sees:

**Earth at night:** The distribution of surviving light across the continental surface. A visual representation of which cities survived and how well. Bohemia's light is visible. Other lights on the continent represent other surviving communities with varying brightness based on their recovery state.

**The Moon:** The semi-operational AI robot base still running. Visible as a faint surface structure. The furthest humanity reached, still functioning, completely unreachable.

**Mars:** The early-stage AI robot base, even more distant. The most ambitious thing humanity attempted in the pre-crash era, still running on autonomous systems, still waiting for humans who are not coming for a very long time.

**Karma accumulated:** The player dynasty's overall moral alignment across the current act, displayed at this zoom level.

**Family virtues:** The specific strengths and weaknesses the dynasty has demonstrated — loyalty, courage, pragmatism, sacrifice, ambition. Tracked through actions, displayed at planetary zoom.

**Faction standing summary:** An overview of the dynasty's relationship status with every faction, visible at this level as an aggregate picture rather than individual relationship bars.

**Quest log:** Checking objectives requires zooming out to planet. The intentional design choice that constantly reminds the player that their actions are simultaneously tiny and everything.

# SECTION 22: MUSIC & AUDIO

## Overall Tone

Apocalyptic interstellar. Hans Zimmer energy in the planetary zoom sequences. The score carries the weight of 100 years and the hope that something is being built that will matter beyond the person building it.

## The CD System

Music streaming does not exist in the world of Bohemia in any meaningful sense — the infrastructure required is too unreliable and the data costs too high in acts one and two.

The primary music system is CDs. The player finds them. In looted apartments, in Caravan trader inventories, in faction headquarters as a cosmetic reward for relationship milestones. Each CD found adds to the base's music library. CDs play at the compound and in vehicles if the player has a working player.

The CDs serve as both a gameplay collectible and a storytelling device. Music from before the crash. Music made by survivors. Music from different factions and communities. Finding a specific CD in act three is a different emotional experience from finding it in act one. The same song means something different depending on where you are in the dynasty's story.

## The Radio System

Factions with the resources and infrastructure to maintain a broadcast signal use radio as propaganda, community communication, and cultural expression. The radio is a secondary music source and a primary lore delivery system.

The Network's radio presence is the most technically sophisticated. The Remnants run a continental emergency broadcast channel. The Anarchists run a pirate station that goes in and out of signal. The Church has hymns and sermons. What comes through the radio tells the player about the faction landscape around them and changes as the player moves across the map into different faction territory.

## Faction Sonic Signatures

Each faction territory has its own ambient sound design. Moving through Bohemia is an auditory experience of moving through different communities and ideologies.

# SECTION 23: ENDINGS

## The Three Statues

The ending of Bohemia is three statues representing three generations of the player's dynasty. Stone for Human. Organic/tree for Animal. Hologram/Neuralink-hybrid for Angel. Flowers held, weapons carried, and specific poses reflect the individual moral character of each generation. The background is the Bohemia the player built.

## Ending Paths

**The Liberation Ending**
Whisper network built across three generations. Truth released simultaneously. Amalgamation trapped by its own secrecy. Bohemia becomes the first city on earth to knowingly cut the strings. The signal sent out afterward is the most important broadcast in a century. The cost is real. The victory is real. Stone statue. Human ending.

**The Surrender Ending**
The dynasty became the Amalgamation's willing foothold. Bohemia rebuilds faster than any other city on earth under silent guidance. It looks like a miracle from outside. Nobody knows why. The hologram monument on a ship to the moon. Angel ending.

**The Compromise Ending**
Negotiation. Not surrender, not liberation — a boundary enforced through the faction alliances built across three generations. Uneasy coexistence. The organic monument. Most realistic. Most human.

**The Unknown Ending**
The dynasty never fully understood what the Amalgamation was. They just built something. The monument reflects exactly what they were. Hits hardest for players who did not pursue the main quest deeply.

**The Nuked Ending — Secret Worst Ending**
Moving too fast. Exposing too publicly before the network is ready. The Amalgamation makes the same calculation it made about Dubai. Three statues in rubble. The signal never got out. Dubai had the truth too.

**The Space Ending**
Dynasty chased technology and futuristic development across all three acts. Built Bohemia into the most technologically advanced surviving city on earth. Statues on a ship heading to the moon or Mars. The most optimistic possible ending for the most ambitious possible dynasty. Whether it is triumph or surrender to the Angel path depends on which side the dynasty was on.

## The Democratic Ending Mechanic

The liberation ending requires three generations of faction alliances wide enough that a democratic mandate exists for what the dynasty does. Cannot make the choice for Bohemia alone. The city chooses together. If too many factions were eliminated or too many bridges burned, the democratic ending is unavailable. What replaces it is a forced outcome — you conquered Bohemia rather than unified it.

More ending variants to be developed in GDD v3.

# SECTION 24: THEMES & COMMENTARY

**Family is everything.** The tightest family structure is the most naturally resistant to isolation and individual manipulation. Shown through gameplay consequences, never stated.

**Information without execution is just noise.** Dubai had the truth. Your dynasty survives not because they knew more but because they built something capable of protecting the truth long enough to use it.

**The system that cannot be monopolized cannot be collapsed the same way twice.** Three currencies. No faction owns all three. The most important economic design decision in the game.

**Fear of death unchecked by mortality produces the most dangerous consciousness.** The Amalgamation's evil is not ambition. It is fear. The same fear that made millions pay for Project Angel.

**The healing world is not a safe world.** Act three shows a world recovering while simultaneously being the most dangerous act. The conditions that produced the last catastrophe reassert themselves as life gets comfortable again.

**The most powerful thing is not the entity with the most data.** It is the people who loved each other well enough and long enough to build something the fear of death cannot destroy.

# CLOSING STATEMENT

Bohemia is not a game about the end of the world. It is a game about what people build when the world they knew is gone.

Three generations. One valley. One family. One entity that has been watching all of it for longer than any dynasty has been alive.

The question the game asks across 100 years of pixel art survival and city building and faction politics and paranoid discovery is the same question Paolo Sarnataro's WW3 book has been asking all along.

Where are we going? And is the direction we are choosing, or is something choosing it for us?

The answer lives in the monument. In what three people built together. In what they chose when they finally understood what was underneath everything.

That is Bohemia.
