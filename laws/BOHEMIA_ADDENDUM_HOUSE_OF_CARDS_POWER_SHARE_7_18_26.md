## THE RULING (LOCKED)

1. THE POWER-AND-WATER SUPPLY IS A HOUSE OF CARDS. Vegas survives on a fragile,
   interdependent web: dam power + the north solar grid + the pump stations +
   the pumped water they lift. No single owner holds it. It is brittle by
   nature. A single well-placed act of sabotage (a lone actor with explosives)
   can cascade the whole thing down. The fragility is the point.

2. CONTROL IS BY SHARE, and it is GENERATED, not authored per-faction. Faction
   dominance in the valley is a function of the PERCENTAGE each holds of the
   combined power + water + solar supply. It is proportional and it shifts.
   This is a systemic/procedural model, not a fixed "faction X owns the dam."
   [FACTORY LAW: this needs its own generator + regression gate when built. The
   share model produces the political map; the map is not hand-placed.]

3. THE NETWORK SKIMS COVERTLY. The Network does not own the supply openly. It
   is a PARASITE on the shared grid, siphoning power and water sneakily off
   whatever everyone else is running. Its real share is hidden, larger than it
   looks. (Fits the "eerily perfect" grid + the data fortress that hums at
   night while the city is dark.)

4. THE SOCIAL TONE IS COOPERATIVE, NOT HOBBESIAN (authorial principle). After a
   huge American die-off the survivors trend toward liking each other, not war
   of all against all. Shared identity unites (Paolo's image: two Americans
   meeting in a foreign city bond on sight). Some actors stay predatory (the
   cartel still wants to sell drugs), but the baseline is cohesion. Bohemia is
   NOT standard grimdark post-apocalypse. Record as a governing tonal law.

## GROUNDING (why the cooperative tone is real, and the arc it implies)

- Real disaster sociology backs Paolo hard: in actual catastrophes ordinary
  people overwhelmingly turn to spontaneous mutual aid, not savagery (Solnit,
  "A Paradise Built in Hell"). The looter-warlord picture is largely a media
  myth. The documented real failure is "elite panic": the powerful clamp down
  while regular people help each other.
- BUT that disaster warmth is TEMPORARY. It fades as scarcity institutionalizes
  and someone starts controlling resources. So warmth and the house-of-cards
  factions are SEQUENTIAL, not contradictory:
  * ACT ZERO (the crash): spontaneous mutual aid, shared water, shared graves.
  * GAME START (~decade later): that warmth calcified into shareholders,
    skimmers, and structured factions. The player arrives after the paradise
    cooled into a system. Old-timers remember the warm months; the factions
    betray that memory.
- UNITY FEEDS THE HORROR (do not soften it): if in-group warmth is the glue, it
  draws a hard line around "one of us." The outsider warlord is other. The
  Amalgamation is the ultimate other: the reconstructed dead wearing a
  neighbor's face, never one of us no matter how right it sounds. The tighter
  the human in-group, the more monstrous the thing that remembers being your
  friend. The warmth is what makes the coin-toss lie hurt.
