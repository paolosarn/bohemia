# BOHEMIA — ADDENDUM: SURVIVING THE AFTERMATH RESEARCH (swipe file, not canon)
### 7.21.26 — Paolo asked for a dedicated file on Surviving the Aftermath, a colony-survival city-builder he loves, to see what it does right and steal swagger from. This is a RESEARCH REFERENCE, not a locked design decision — nothing here is canon until Paolo picks a piece and confirms it.

---

## CORRECTION FIRST (read-before-speak)
Paolo said "the same people who made Surviving Mars made this." Not quite: **Surviving the Aftermath was made by Iceflake Studios** (Tampere, Finland, founded 2007), which Paradox Interactive acquired in 2020 as its ninth internal studio. **Surviving Mars was made by Haemimont Games** (Bulgaria), a different studio. Both are Paradox-published "Surviving ___" city-builders and clearly share a design lineage/branding, but they are not the same team. Worth knowing so we cite it right if it ever comes up.

## WHAT THE GAME IS
A post-apocalyptic colony-management city-builder: you land survivors in a procedurally generated world after a civilization-ending event and manage a colony's production, defense, and expansion while sending specialists out to explore, trade, and scavenge a broader world map.

## THE MECHANICS WORTH STEALING FROM

### 1. Reputation is the trade currency, not a spent resource
"Trade allows the player to leverage their colony's **reputation** in exchange for favors and resources." You don't hand over a stack of reputation points to buy something — reputation is a STANDING that unlocks better deals, alliances, and eventually federations with other colonies. This is the single most useful precedent for Bohemia's CLOUT problem (see the CLOUT addendum, 7/21): reputation as a lever/gate, not a spendable token. STA proves this works in a game about literal post-collapse survival economics, not just narrative games.

### 2. Multiple resource TIERS with different failure modes
Resources aren't one undifferentiated pile. Metal/Concrete/Plastic are finite and extractor-dependent (run out if you don't invest in extraction infrastructure). Food is a composite (vegetables + meat + insects, refined into meals to meet nutrition, not just a raw number). Water does DOUBLE DUTY — drinking water for colonists AND power for certain buildings. Fuel is a separate critical resource for operations. The lesson: distinct resource categories should have distinct FAILURE SHAPES (some scarce and extraction-gated, some dual-purpose, some composite from sub-ingredients) rather than one uniform "resource point" currency. This maps well onto Bohemia's existing three-tier idea (resources / electricity / clout) — each should fail differently, not just have a different name on the same bar.

### 3. Specialists are a separate unit class from colonists
Specialists are directly-controllable named units with 5 skills rated 1-4 (barely competent to world-class); they do NOT need to be fed/resourced like regular colonists, and vehicles let you move squads of them fast across the world map. This is a clean pattern for a small hero/companion roster distinct from a faceless population — relevant to Bohemia's companion system (the FFXV-style camp/hangout companions already discussed) more than to the economy directly, but worth remembering.

### 4. Trade caravans require pre-positioned supply
"Trading convoys establish trade routes between colonies; depending on the arrangement, players need enough supplies stocked up before the convoy's arrival." Trade isn't instant/on-demand — it's a scheduled, prep-dependent event. This is a real mechanic precedent for the "meet at the trading hub, bring your goods" barter idea Paolo floated in the same conversation (see the CLOUT addendum's ECONOMY DISCUSSION section) — STA already proves out a version of exactly that.

### 5. Biomes create asymmetric scarcity, not just reskins
Six biomes, each with different environmental hazards, soil types (three main types affecting building placement and crop viability), and available resources. The world map isn't just re-painted — different regions are actually good/bad at different things, forcing trade and expansion decisions. Bohemia's districts already have a texture system (apocalypse/recovering/modern) that could carry this same asymmetric-scarcity idea if it doesn't already (worldgen question, not economy, flagging for later).

### 6. Calamities are named, mechanical, and resource-generating
Disasters aren't just punishment — the "Shattered Moon" calamity rains destructive fragments AND deposits valuable Moon Rock resources. A disaster that also drops a reward resource is a good pattern for turning hardship into an active choice (do you brave the disaster for the loot) rather than pure attrition.

## WHAT DOESN'T APPLY / WHERE BOHEMIA IS DIFFERENT
STA is third-person-camera colony management with no single embodied player character walking the world; Bohemia is a first-person-lived, embodied RPG where the player IS a person with a phone, not an overseer. So STA's UI patterns (top-down base management, specialist rosters as a menu) don't port directly — what ports is the ECONOMIC LOGIC (reputation-as-lever, resource-tier asymmetry, scheduled trade, biome asymmetry), not the interface.

## PENDING — Paolo's calls
- Whether Bohemia's districts should carry STA-style biome asymmetry (some districts genuinely better at food, others at scavenge, forcing real trade) — a worldgen/economy question, not decided here.
- Whether disasters/calamities should drop a resource reward the way Shattered Moon does — ties into the Act 1 procedural climax and combat/camp systems already in flight.
