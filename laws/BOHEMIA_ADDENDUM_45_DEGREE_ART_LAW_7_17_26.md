# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM — THE 45 DEGREE ART LAW (7/17/26)

Ruled by Paolo 7/17/26 night, judging the first commissioned original art
(the traffic signals, v4) against his lamp at zoom. Verbatim:

> "every art that I have you make it has to be viewed from like a 45 degree
> angle and you have to understand that I need you to look at the other lamp
> light post and I need you to look at yours. Yours is like a flat 90 like
> it's a 2D scroller"

## THE LAW

Every original piece of art Claude draws for the world is seen from the
world's THREE-QUARTER 45 VIEW, the same camera the corpus was painted from.
Never flat side-on (a "2D scroller" billboard), never top-down flat.

What the 45 view means at the pixel level (the blessed lamp bank is the
reference standard):

1. **HORIZONTAL CROSS-SECTIONS ARE ELLIPSES.** A pole base is a stack of
   ellipse discs, not a rectangle. A ring around a cylinder is a bowed band,
   not a straight line. Rims, flanges, caps, plates: all elliptical.
2. **TOPS ARE VISIBLE AND SKY-LIT.** From 45 above you can SEE the top face
   of every disc, box, arm, and visor, and the sky lights it: top faces run
   brighter than walls, walls brighter than undersides.
3. **HORIZONTAL EDGES BOW TOWARD THE VIEWER.** A band wrapping a cylinder
   rides high at the silhouette edges and dips at the center (the near side
   of the ring is closer to the camera).
4. **BOXES SHOW TWO FACES.** A housing seen from the world's 45 shows its
   front AND a sliver of top and one side, not a flat front alone.

## SCOPE

All original prop/object art Claude produces from 7/17/26 on. Existing
corpus art already obeys (it is the standard). Paolo's painted regions are
untouched by this law (RIG LAW: sacrosanct).

## THE GATE (same turn, per the law of laws)

`gates/art_45_gate.py`, wired into the suite as ART 45. Machine checks per
registered commissioned-art bank: the perspective declaration, the ellipse
signature of the ground hardware (widest row above the bottom row, bottom
row narrower), and sky-lit top faces (top-face rows brighter than wall
rows). The registry grows with every commission.
