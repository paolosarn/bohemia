## THE REFERENCE (LOCKED): Pocket City 2 as the construction base
Paolo locked Pocket City 2 as the model for the city-building layer. What Bohemia adopts from it:

- **Zone, don't hand-place.** The player designates zones (residential / commercial / industrial equivalents in Bohemia's terms — [PENDING Paolo's naming]). The SOCIETY auto-constructs the actual buildings on zoned land. This is Paolo's explicit "not necessarily yourself, kinda like Fallout 4 but the society does it" instinct. You are the planner; the population is the labor.
- **Everything needs road + power + water access to function.** A zone or building with no road connection, no power, or no water is dead/darkened. (Pocket City 2 abstracts power lines/sewer into "connected by road" — [PENDING Paolo: does Bohemia keep that abstraction or simulate the grid? note: the solar mega-project + Hoover-dam-equivalent already make power a real geographic thing in canon, leaning toward a light real grid].)
- **Daily upkeep on everything.** Zones, roads, most buildings cost currency per period to maintain. Overbuilding past your income bankrupts you. This is the discipline that makes the city-builder a real economy and not a paint tool. Ties directly to the three-currency system (electricity/medicine/clout).
- **Demand signals guide growth.** Pocket City 2 shows demand bars telling you what to build next. Bohemia equivalent [PENDING Paolo: keep explicit demand bars, or read demand diegetically through NPC need / district texture?].
- **Zones/buildings upgrade to higher levels** once the upgrade is unlocked; upgraded = improved effects. Maps onto Bohemia's texture escalation.

## THE BOHEMIA TWISTS (LOCKED, Paolo 7.1.26)

### Three cities = three generations, each an upgrade tier of the last
The same valley, rebuilt across ~100 years. Gen 2's city is literally built on gen 1's bones; gen 3 on gen 2's. Not three maps — one map at three escalating construction tiers, matching the three texture states (apocalypse → recovering → modern) already locked in GDD v2 §20.

### Buildings are gated by ACT / tier (LOCKED)
- **Act 1 buildings:** the baseline survivable set. What a scrappy post-crash settlement can throw up.
- **Act 2 buildings:** special / genuinely hard to build. Things Act 1 simply cannot support (needs more infrastructure, materials, faction cooperation).
- **Act 3 buildings:** the hardest. You CAN build them, but they're the top of the mountain — the grand, modern, city-defining structures.
This mirrors Pocket City 2's unlock model (buildings unlock by leveling / completing quests / clearing harder difficulty), remapped onto the three-act dynasty spine. Progression is generational, not just XP.

### The shipping/logistics spine (LOCKED intent)
The story is being built around **setting up train stations / shipping infrastructure** to handle goods movement. Logistics is how the city graduates tiers: Act 1 can barely move goods (foot, bike, horse, rare vehicle — per GDD v2 §transport), Act 3 runs real rail. Shipping capacity is a gate on how big the city can grow. Ties to the already-locked Caravan trade-route economy. **[PENDING Paolo: the specific logistics tech ladder — what unlocks rail, what the intermediate steps are.]**

### Everything can genuinely be rubble (LOCKED)
If the player doesn't invest, the city stays ruined, for real. Degradation is not cosmetic. This is the persistent-consequence rule (Mayor addendum §1) applied to construction: neglect and destruction persist the same way violence does. The apocalypse texture is the DEFAULT, not a starting cutscene.

### The player becomes pseudo-mayor (ALREADY LOCKED — Mayor addendum §2)
This construction layer is the SURFACE the Mayor Arc gates. Territory → Popular Mandate (~49% factions FWU) → Mayor governs. Those rungs control WHERE you may zone/build and how cheaply; THIS addendum controls the mechanics OF zoning/building. The two lock together: mayor status makes the Pocket-City construction loop cheaper/broader across the map.

## THE LIFE-LESSON TWANG (underneath, never preached)
Civilization isn't built by one hero — it's zoned, connected, and MAINTAINED across generations by people who mostly won't live to see it finished. The upkeep never stops. You can raise a cathedral and still go bankrupt if you stop tending the water lines. Augustus was greater than Julius because he inherited unfinished work and consequences and didn't squander them. Gen 2 building on gen 1's mess is the whole game.

## THE OPEN GUT-PUNCH FORK (Paolo's call, blocks the economy sim shape)
**Do zoned buildings appear INSTANTLY once paid + requirements met (Pocket City 2 style), or are they CONSTRUCTED OVER TIME by the society (turns/days pass, Caravan hauls materials in, you watch it rise frame by frame)?**
- Instant = simple, clean, the proven mobile-friendly loop.
- Over-time = grounded and gorgeous, fits the world, makes every building a mini supply-chain project — but it's a much heavier economic simulation (materials, haulers, build queues, interruption if a caravan is raided).
This single answer defines whether the economy is a light city-builder or a logistics sim. **[PENDING, Paolo's call.]**
