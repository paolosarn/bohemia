# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM — ACT 3 MOONSHOT + THE NETWORK'S CRUMBLING (7/19/26, Paolo, LOCKED)

The big structural turn of the 7/18-19 lore session. The old "Act 3 confrontation"
moves down, and Act 3 becomes the journey off-world. Newest date wins. Updates the
finale section of BOHEMIA_STORY_MASTER_7_18_26.md.

## LOCKED

1. ACT RESTRUCTURE. The local Amalgamation reckoning (discovering it, the whisper
   broadcast, stopping the EARTH-side nuke, freeing Vegas) moves to the END OF
   ACT 2. ACT 3 becomes the journey to the MOON to end the Amalgamation at its
   off-world source. This is the good-ending path (many ways to play the game).
   Grounded in canon already written: the generations are Animal / Human / Angel
   and the camera levels are street / city / planetary zoom. Act 3 was always
   Angel, always the planetary level. The structure pointed here the whole time.

2. THE MOONSHOT IS ONE-WAY (locked "in the law"). The gen-3 (Angel) heir goes and
   does not come back. The dynasty ends looking down at the planet. One-way also
   makes it logistically buildable by a city-state: no return stage, no orbital
   refueling, you only have to GET there.

3. ACT 3 IS THE CRUMBLING OF THE NETWORK, for better or worse. Which way it
   crumbles is set by what the player did in Act 2:
   - WAR PATH: if you did not win the Network over, Act 3 is a war. You ally the
     other factions and take the Network's industrial base by force (the forge:
     the robotics factory, the data fortress compute, the hoarded chips and
     knowledge). You build the spear in the dragon's own forge, by siege.
   - CONVERSION PATH: if you did the right things in Act 2, you convert the
     Network. You show them the error of their ways and they HELP you build the
     rocket. Either way the Network falls apart in Act 3.

4. THE CONVERTIBILITY SPLIT (the moral line through the faction). Network members
   WITHOUT NeuroLink implants still have free will and can be turned. NeuroLink
   CARRIERS are the Amalgamation's puppets and cannot be converted. The
   conversion path saves the believers and loses the wired.

5. WHY IT MUST BE THE MOON, AND ONE-WAY. You cannot nuke the Moon (real physics:
   a ballistic weapon lacks the energy to leave Earth, and the crashed world has
   no launch capability lying around). The Moon/Mars is canon "completely
   unreachable." So ending the off-world Amalgamation requires physically GOING,
   and the crashed world can muster that as a one-shot, one-way capability at
   most. The impossibility is the point of Act 3.

## THE LINCHPIN [PENDING Paolo, the 7/19 question]
For the rocket to ever get built, the Amalgamation must NOT be able to strike the
launch site. By its own locked kill-rule it destroys anything that threatens its
survival, and building an anti-Amalgamation rocket in the Network's own wired,
monitored forge is the single most survival-threatening act possible. So Act 2
likely has to END with the Amalgamation losing its power to hit Earth (its local
node severed, the Earth nuke spent and stopped), leaving it able only to WATCH
from the Moon, unable to reach you. That severed Earth-arm is what buys Act 3 the
room to build. To confirm with Paolo.
