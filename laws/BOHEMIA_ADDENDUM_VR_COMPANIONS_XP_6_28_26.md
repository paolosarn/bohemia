## 1. VR / HEADSETS — WORLD + MECHANIC  [LOCKED INTENT]

**The world has VR headsets.** The real-world lineage (Oculus/Meta, Apple Vision-class wearables)
exists in Bohemia's pre-crash tech stack alongside Neuralink and Project Angel. VR is **part of the
story and a gameplay mechanic**, surfacing through side and/or main quests.

**Base mechanic — the VR station.** The dynasty can build a **VR station in the compound**. It lets
the player **simulate things and gain real experience without risking death in the real world** — a
safe training/sim space. (Natural fit with the XP/growth thread below: the VR station is where you
grind a skill without bleeding for it.)

**In the world.** Some enemies and some NPCs **wear VR headsets**. Not everywhere — **scalable to
how many people own a headset** in that area/era. **The Network has notably higher headset adoption**
than the general population.

**Why this is based (grounded, fits existing lore):** the headset is one more **voluntary data-feed
and immersion vector** — the same pattern as Project Angel (people pay to pour themselves into a
system). A population wearing headsets is a population partially living inside something the
Amalgamation/Network can reach, shape, and harvest. The Network's high adoption rate is a quiet tell,
like the rain/cooling tell and the homeless-come-up tell already in GDD v3 — noticed, not understood.

## 2. COMPANION COMBAT THRESHOLDS — SELF-PRESERVATION  [LOCKED INTENT]

Companions should **not just suicide into fights.** The player can **talk to a companion and set a
health threshold** at which that companion **pulls out of combat** rather than keep fighting.

- Example thresholds the player chooses per companion: "after you take a hit and you're under **70%**,
  stop engaging," or a more aggressive "**under 40%**, you're out." The number is the player's call.
- Below the set threshold the companion **stops participating in combat** (falls back / goes to cover
  / disengages) instead of dying for no reason.
- **Why it's rewarding:** it gives the player real control over companion risk and rewards the
  relationship/management layer. Pairs with the GDD §16 companion philosophy (the sneak-melee that
  *never* charges a gunman) and the permanent-death + one-revival-per-act stakes — a threshold is how
  you keep a companion alive *before* you ever need the revival.

**[OPEN]**
- Set per-companion (talk to each) vs a global party default you can override per companion. (Leaning
  per-companion via dialogue, since "talk to them" is how Paolo framed it.)
- What "pulls out" looks like on the combat grid (retreat to cover, leave the engagement, go passive).
- Does a companion's own courage/loyalty trait nudge their willingness to hold past the threshold?

## 3. XP / CHARACTER GROWTH WITHIN AN ACT  [PROPOSED — awaiting greenlight]

Context: Paolo asked whether the dial's hit/vital/kill **arc widths** should scale with distance.
Direction he gave: **don't just hardcode that — tie growth to the character's earned experience
within an act** (gunslinger, talker, etc.), so over time you get better-this, better-that.

**Pitch (lean, builds on what's already in the combat prototype):**
- The greed meter already mints an **XP multiplier** on kills — that's the seed. Route kill quality
  (clean kill > vital > hit) and greed into a **per-skill XP pool**, e.g. **Gunslinger** (the dial),
  with room for **Talker**, etc., as other systems come online.
- Leveling a skill **within an act** subtly improves it: a higher Gunslinger level **widens your
  effective kill/vital window** and/or **eases the dial a touch at range** — so the distance arc-scaling
  Paolo mentioned becomes **earned through play, not handed out.** Point-blank stays easy; long range
  starts brutal and *becomes* makeable as your gunslinger grows across the act. That's the staircase.
- The **VR station (§1)** is the safe place to grind this XP without real-world death.
- Generational fit: skills are an **Act-level arc** (you feel yourself grow Gen 1 → end of act), and
  what each generation built/learned can flavor the heir (ties to the dynasty system, GDD §7).

  a visible level, window widens slightly per level), or keep it on paper until the act structure is
  being built?
- Do skills **reset/carry** across the generational handoff, and how much does a parent's mastery
  pass down?
