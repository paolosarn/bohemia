## THE LAW
Each act (1/2/3) has its OWN graphic asset era - a distinct visual tier
(Act 1 = dead world, 30-years-worn, no vegetation, tan/dust-worn corpus;
later acts get their own tiers as they're built). BUT assets are NOT
strictly zone-locked to their act's chronological gate. Placement follows
NARRATIVE STATE, not calendar:
  - A neglected, poor, or forgotten part of a LATER act's town can still
    wear ACT 1 textures - it never got rebuilt, nobody invested in it.
  - A wealthy, cared-for, or renewed space WITHIN Act 1's world (a boujie
    interior, someone's kept room, a faction stronghold) can wear a LATER
    act's tier of asset - money and control buy the nicer texture early.
Mixing is a DELIBERATE narrative choice about who has resources and care,
not a default or an accident. Each act's tier stays real and distinct; the
choice of which tier a given room/block wears is content, not chronology.

## THE REUSE-FIRST LAW (same conversation, same ruling)
"i want you to use these assets as much as you can before u create your own
and shit." Before cooking new original art for any need for an act's
world, sweep that act's OWN approved + candidate corpus first (banks/) for
material that already fits. Only cook fresh art for genuine gaps the
corpus doesn't cover. This applies within an act's tier - reaching into a
LATER act's corpus for an EARLIER act's default fill is the zone-lock
violation the law above exists to prevent; reaching for it as a DELIBERATE
narrative placement (the boujie Act-1 interior) is exactly what's allowed.
