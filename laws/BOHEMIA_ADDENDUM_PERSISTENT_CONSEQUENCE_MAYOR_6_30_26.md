# BOHEMIA — ADDENDUM: PERSISTENT CONSEQUENCE & THE MAYOR ARC
*6.30.26 — locks two pieces of canon Paolo raised: (1) your violence permanently changes the world within an act (no Skyrim-style respawn), threats only reset at generational handoff, and (2) the city-building escalation from friendly territory → popular mandate → mayor. Extends the Entities/Spawning addendum and GDD v2 §7 (dynasty), §8 (acts), §10 (base building). Builds on the delta model.*

---

## 1. PERSISTENT CONSEQUENCE — YOUR VIOLENCE STAYS (LOCKED)

**Paolo's complaint, verbatim intent:** Skyrim/Fallout flood cleared dungeons and raid camps back in after ~a week. You never feel the weight of the death and war you caused because the world just refills it. That is unacceptable for Bohemia.

**The rule (LOCKED):**
- **Cleared is permanently cleared, within an act.** A camp you wipe, a hostile spawn you end, a raid you break, it STAYS ended for the rest of that generation. The delta model already makes this free: clearing logs a "cleared" delta that overrides the seed-generated spawn, forever, within the act. Your massacres leave permanent holes in the world.
- **No timer-based respawn. Ever.** Nothing hostile refills a location because N days passed. The thing that made Skyrim feel insulting is banned at the architecture level.
- **You feel the map empty as you carve through it.** Because kills persist, a violent playthrough visibly depopulates its region. The world remembers what you did to it. That IS the feedback the respawn model destroys.

**Why this is a flex, not a limitation:** it's the entire payoff of the delta/seed model (Entities addendum). Skyrim respawns because it's too lazy to track that you cleared a place. Bohemia tracks it as one small delta, so permanence costs nothing and buys everything.

### The generational threat reset (LOCKED — this is the release valve, and it's NOT a respawn)

Threats DO return, but only at the **act / generational handoff** (Act 1→2→3, ~30 years and a new dynasty character each time). Critically, this is **not** the same enemies refilling the same holes. It is a NEW threat map:
- New camps in **new locations**, new hostile groups, new configurations, generated fresh for the new act from the seed + world state.
- Never a refill of what the player personally ended. What you cleared as gen-1 stays cleared *for gen-1's whole life*.

**The feel, and the life-lesson twang:** in Skyrim the same bandits refill the same cave on a clock and it's insulting. In Bohemia you clear the east camps as your gen-1 character and they stay clear your entire life; thirty years later your son inherits a world where *new* threats have emerged in *new* places, because that's what three decades do, not because a timer ticked. **Your father's war is over. You can win a fight for good, but you can't win it forever for your children.** The world moving on across generations is the point of the dynasty, and the threat reset is that truth expressed as a mechanic.

## 2. THE MAYOR ARC — CITY-BUILDING ESCALATION (LOCKED shape, tuning PENDING)

**Paolo:** the shining jewel is customizing the city in your friendly territory, and at some point you become mayor when you've done so much that damn near everyone loves you. The more the city backs you, the easier building becomes, even in areas whose local faction doesn't love you, because the whole city has your back.

**The escalation ladder (LOCKED as three rungs; maps onto the three acts):**

1. **TERRITORY — build where you're loved.** Baseline. In a faction's territory that FWU (friendly), you can customize and build. This is the starting city-building surface.

2. **POPULAR MANDATE — cross the threshold, the city itself backs you.** When enough of the city is behind you (Paolo's number: **~49% of factions FWU**), you gain the ability to build in districts **even where the local faction doesn't love you.** Popular support overrides local resistance. You're no longer negotiating plot by plot; the city's goodwill is your permit. Building gets easier broadly, including in neutral/cool areas, because "you got the whole city behind your back."

3. **MAYOR — governance, the map opens.** The top of the arc. Enough done, enough love, and you become mayor of Bohemia. Building becomes markedly easier, cheaper, faster, fewer restrictions across the map, because you're not negotiating anymore, you're governing. This is the full expression of the jewel.

**The through-line:** Territory → Popular Mandate → Governance. Negotiation gives way to mandate gives way to rule. This political economy is legible and it escalates the city-builder alongside the dynasty. **[PENDING]** exact numbers/curves (the 49% is Paolo's starting instinct, not final), what specifically "easier" grants at each rung (cost multipliers? unlock tiers? restriction removal?), and how losing faction favor can knock you back down a rung.

### "MAYOR" = PSEUDO-MAYOR / CITY-STATE STRONGMAN (LOCKED — this resolves a real lore tension)

**The contradiction, named honestly:** core canon (GDD v2) establishes that FORMAL government failed everywhere and is a cautionary tale, not an aspiration. Texas tried to reconstitute a real multi-state government and it's a story told across all three acts of why that fails (§Texas/oil-states). The Remnants "specifically do not want to be" a government. "Nobody in Bohemia has attempted the same thing" as Texas, for a reason revealed in Act 3. The word "mayor" appears NOWHERE in core canon. So making the player a literal elected Mayor of a restored municipal government would directly contradict the world's political backstory.

**The resolution (Paolo, 7.1.26):** it's a **pseudo-mayor**, not a restored office. After the crash, cities didn't rebuild federal-style government, they collapsed into **city-state structure** — the oldest thing humans default to when the large system dies. You are not elected. There is no municipal institution, no seal, no restored bureaucracy. You are the **dynasty everyone in the city defers to** because you hold the territory, the trade, the defense, and the loyalty. "Mayor" is just what people naturally call the family at the center of gravity — the same "something people would say in conversation without sounding like a government program" instinct the GDD already uses for the solar-project name.

**Why this makes the arc REINFORCE the lore instead of breaking it:**
- Texas failed trying to **rebuild the old thing** (formal authority on paper). City-states are what emerge **instead** of that. Different mechanism, opposite category.
- The Remnants refuse formal government because institutions-without-accountability burned people for 100 years. A beloved family that IS the city's spine isn't an institution; it's the pre-institutional structure that precedes and outlasts formal states (Renaissance Italian communes, Sumerian city-states, post-Roman merchant-families). Grounded, real, "based."
- It fits the dynasty spine exactly: you don't inherit an OFFICE across three generations (that's the Julius→Augustus straight-line succession the lore explicitly avoids), you inherit a FAMILY'S standing — the accreted loyalty and holdings that make the city rally to whoever the family puts forward. Power passes the way it really passes in a city-state: through the house, not the title.
- The life-lesson twang: legitimate power after collapse isn't granted by a system, it's **earned by being the one who fed, built, and defended the city** until everyone just… looks to you. No ballot. No decree. You became load-bearing.

**What this locks:** the top rung is de-facto rule of a city-state, "mayor" as an affectionate/colloquial title for the dynasty at the city's center of gravity, NOT a restored municipal government. This keeps the Texas cautionary tale, the Remnants' anti-government stance, and "nobody attempts what Texas attempted" all intact — because the player never attempts formal government either. They become the city-state's first family.

**[PENDING]** what the world CALLS this in-game (does the game ever surface the word "mayor," or is it purely colloquial NPC speech? is there a diegetic city-state name for the role — "the House," "the Family," "the Seat"?), and whether rival factions running the same play means competing pseudo-mayors / rival houses exist as an Act-2/3 tension.

## 3. ACT 2 — "SHIT HAS CHANGED OVER THE YEARS" (LOCKED intent, one fork PENDING)

When the player becomes the gen-2 character (the son/daughter of the Act 1 founder), the city and world should visibly reflect that a generation passed. Not a reset, a weathering. New threats (see §1), shifted factions, the texture-state drift the world model already produces from accumulated pressures.

### The dad's-book mechanic (LOCKED — beautiful, keep it)
The player can **read their father's book** (a diegetic in-world object) to revisit the Act 1 city-building state / the gen-1 city as it was. Nostalgia as a literal object. A way to revisit "Act 1's city-building shit" without breaking the "shit has changed" forward progression. Diegetic, optional, emotional. This is exactly the kind of touch that makes people love a game and it costs little (the Act 1 city state is deterministically reconstructable from the choice log, per the save model, so the book is a "view an earlier reconstructed state" window).

### The one open fork [PENDING — Paolo leaning "most things stay," wants a lore reason]
Do the buildings the gen-1 character constructed **still stand** in Act 2 (you walk your father's city and feel the weight of what he built), or does Act 2 partially **decay/wipe** gen-1 construction so you rebuild on the bones of the old?
- Paolo's lean (6.30.26): **most things can stay**, ideally justified with a lore reason rather than a mechanical wipe.
- Candidate lore hooks for "most stays" (not chosen, options for later): the dynasty compound and its works are maintained by the family and allied factions across the gap; the whole point of the dynasty-as-armor (GDD v2 §7) is continuity the Amalgamation can't erode; the brownout/recovery arc (GDD v3) means infrastructure the family invested in is precisely what stays lit.
- Candidate lore hooks for "some decays" (if partial weathering is wanted for gameplay): neglected districts the player never engaged slide back (the world model already degrades un-tended tiles); a specific Act 1→2 event (a raid, a blackout year, a faction betrayal) damages a defined area, giving a rebuild hook without insulting the player's Act 1 work.
- **Decision deferred.** Lock the feel ("most stays, lore-justified"); pick the exact rule when Act 2 content is designed.

## HOW THIS SITS ON THE ENGINE (already supported)

- **Permanent clears** = "cleared" deltas in the save (Entities addendum). Free, already modeled.
- **Generational threat reset** = new act regenerates the threat map from seed + new world state; the delta log's clears apply only within their act. The act boundary is where the seed re-rolls the hostile layer.
- **Mayor escalation** = reads faction-standing meta (already in the save's meta block) and gates city-building permissions on it. A threshold check on aggregate faction love.
- **Dad's book** = a read-only reconstruction of an earlier `beat`/act state via the forward-compute (save model), rendered as a viewer. No new persistence needed.

Nothing here requires new foundation. It's all expression of the delta model + the deterministic reconstruction + the faction meta that already exist.
