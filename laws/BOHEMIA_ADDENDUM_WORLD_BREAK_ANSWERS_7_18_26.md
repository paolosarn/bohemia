# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM — WORLD-BREAK ANSWERS (7/18/26, Paolo, LOCKED)

Paolo answered six adversarial "break the world" questions in one pass. His
answers lock and correct several things, including a few of Claude's own recent
proposals. Newest date wins on any conflict.

## LOCKED

1. CRASH DATE ~2050. The economic crash happens around 2050, not the older
   "circa 2040-2045" band. Game start is still almost 10 years later (~2060).
   This matters as a rule: 25 years of tech advancement past 2026 answers the
   "hardware rots in 3-5 years" break. By 2050 storage lifespan and maintenance
   knowledge are far past today's, so the Amalgamation surviving on maintained
   hardware is plausible. Do not argue 2026 tech limits against a 2050 world.

2. CIVILIZATION HELD ON BY A THREAD (corrects Claude's over-apocalyptic
   framing). There was NEVER a total blackout. Paolo never said the power went
   out for ten years and never would. What happened: most industries and
   companies collapsed, stores barely alive, a lot of people died, but power,
   technology, and civilization persisted, thin and strained. BROWNOUTS are the
   texture (already in the documentation; Claude misread it), not apocalypse
   darkness. THE DOLLAR died (currency collapse, consistent with the
   medicine/electricity/resources economy). "Held on by a thread," not "the
   lights went out."

3. MORE HYDROPONICS THAN PREVIOUSLY CREDITED. Desert soil is terrible for
   growing food and a huge number of people die of that, but Vegas endures
   because what people love is the POWER and the WATER (that it has power at
   all, brownouts included). FOOD is the questionable leg, and it is answered by
   HYDROPONICS at a larger scale than earlier canon assumed. Power and water are
   the draw; food is the weakness hydroponics patches.

4. DUBAI STAYS UNEXPLAINED, AND THAT IS THE POINT (supersedes Claude's
   cover-story proposal). Do NOT over-engineer how the nuke launched. The horror
   is precisely that the Amalgamation got away scott free and NOBODY knows how
   the nuke went up. It is unattributed, unsolved, clean. The earlier [PENDING]
   "blamed on a state / spoofed launch cover story" is dropped. No one knows how
   it happened, and that unknowing is the fear.

5. THE GRID IS NOT ONE-BOMB FRAGILE (corrects the house-of-cards "a single
   sabotage cascades the whole thing" line). It would not take one C4 charge to
   drop the whole infrastructure grid; the grid is more robust than that.
   Preventing a terrorist action against infrastructure is good QUEST material,
   one story among many, not the existential spine of the game. Soften the
   house-of-cards fragility accordingly: interdependent and precious, not
   one-match-from-collapse.

6. KINDNESS IS GLIMPSES, NOT A PERMANENT STATE (nuances the "cooperative, not
   Hobbesian" tone). It is definitely not perfect and definitely not forever. In
   the immediate fallout there are glimpses of people being good to each other,
   and survivors carry stories ("one reason I'm still alive is someone was kind
   to me two years after the crash"). It is not sustained utopia. Human,
   scattered, remembered, not idealized. Keep the warmth as moments and
   testimony, not as a governing peace.
