# BOHEMIA ADDENDUM: THE LAST LIFE-SUPPORT BUILDINGS (7/6/26)
Paolo's ask: what structural buildings that kept civilization alive are
still missing from the procedural city. After the full sweep, three remain,
each grounded, each carrying its weight. Proposals [PENDING verdicts].
Plus the three-currency canon at the bottom.

## A. THE GRANARY (the oldest building in the world)
The first permanent buildings humans ever made were not homes, they were
GRANARIES. Storage is what turns harvest into civilization: without it,
one bad season kills the city, and whoever guards the grain rules the
town. Every early city was a granary with a wall around it.
Bohemia's farms currently have NO storage on the map. The real seed:
North Las Vegas has actual grocery distribution centers (the regional DCs
that fed the valley), giant cold-storage warehouses that went silent when
the trucks stopped. Post-crash one gets recommissioned as THE GRANARY:
harvest storage, seed stock, and the cold rooms (which also make it the
medicine vault, the cold chain reborn, tying straight to the medicine
currency). One cell, north fabric near the warehouse district.
The life lesson under it: surplus, not wealth, is what civilization
actually is.

## B. THE LIBRARY (the analog memory)
The Amalgamation is the city's DIGITAL memory, millions of portraits in a
humming fortress. The LIBRARY is the other memory: paper, which needs no
power, no network, no permission. How to farm, fix, wire, midwife,
doctor. When the cold chain and the internet died, the only surviving
copy of civilization's skills was print. Clark County's library district
is real and everywhere. One cell downtown near the civic block.
The quiet counterpoint is the whole game in one building: the fortress
remembers PEOPLE and demands power to do it; the library remembers HOW TO
LIVE and asks for nothing. One of them is the antagonist.

## C. THE RADIO STATION (the information layer)
The logistics doc locked that markets need information: no radio, no
market. The antenna farm on the rim is the transmitter, but the STATION,
the studio where someone actually speaks to the valley, is a building
downtown. Convoy news, price calls, water schedules, the voice of
Bohemia. Whoever holds the microphone shapes the truth, which makes it
faction-grade real estate. One cell, downtown.
