## THE DEATH MATH
Pre-crash valley: ~2.3 million people. Food ceiling from the grass math:
~50,000-80,000. The map must account for roughly 2.2 MILLION people who are
not here anymore. History says the split is never all death:
- EXODUS (the biggest number, historically): when cities die, most people
  LEAVE. Detroit lost 65% of its people over decades without a single
  famine. Post-crash Vegas has one direction to walk: the 15 south to
  California, through 40 miles of open Mojave. The CA APPROACH desert we
  already generate is the exodus road. What the walkers left along it, and
  how many made it, is story ground.
- DIE-OFF (the brutal months): historical siege/famine cities (Leningrad
  lost ~30% in two years) suggest the ones who stayed past the trucks
  stopping faced the worst arithmetic in the first 18 months: heat, water
  pressure loss on the towers, medication runouts, violence. A plausible
  grounded split: ~70-80% exodus, ~15-25% early die-off, ~3% remain.
  [PENDING: Paolo picks the split; it sets the tone of every ruin.]

## THE FERTILIZER QUESTION (Paolo raised it; here is the grounded ground)
- HISTORY SAYS YES, HUMANS HAVE DONE THIS: after Waterloo and Leipzig,
  battlefield bones were collected by the ton, shipped to England, ground
  in bone mills, and sold as crop fertilizer. Documented trade, 1820s.
- MODERN PRECEDENT: human composting (terramation) is LEGAL today in
  Washington, Colorado, California and more: a body becomes about a cubic
  yard of clean soil in 60 days. The science is settled and safe.
- THE PRACTICAL POST-CRASH LOGIC: cremation costs fuel nobody has; burial
  in caliche needs machinery; and the farms are starving for organics
  (valley dirt is dead, the compost economy runs on the landfill and the
  reclaim plant's biosolids). A city that recycles 99% of its water
  deciding whether it recycles its dead is the exact moral weight Bohemia
  carries.
- MAP-TELLABLE OPTIONS [PENDING, Paolo picks what is true]:
  a) THE COMPOST ROWS: a terramation yard beside the reclaim plant or the
     cemetery; the dead feed the fields openly; the culture made peace.
  b) THE QUIET ARRANGEMENT: officially the cemetery grew; everyone knows
     the fields nearest it grow strangest and best; nobody says it.
  c) THE REFUSAL: Bohemia buried or burned its dead at terrible cost, and
     the mass grave sites (real famine-history pattern: trenches at the
     city edge) scar the map; the soil stayed poor and the ceiling lower.
  d) FACTION SPLIT: different factions chose differently, and you can read
     each territory's choice in its dirt.
- Whatever is picked, the map evidence follows: mass grave trenches,
  a terramation yard, expanded cemetery rings, or ash grounds. One rect
  each, factory pattern, ready when the story locks.
