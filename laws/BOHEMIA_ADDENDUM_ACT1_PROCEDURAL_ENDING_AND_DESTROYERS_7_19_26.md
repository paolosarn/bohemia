## LOCKED — ACT 1 EXPLOSIVE ENDING IS PROCEDURAL / EMERGENT
The Act 1 climax is NOT one fixed scripted scene. It is a COMBINATION of approved
climax-elements, generated and shaped by HOW the player played Act 1:
- whether they built any of the city / stood up any industry,
- whether they raced toward becoming the pseudo-mayor or not,
- their faction choices and questline path.
Elements can also fire THROUGHOUT the act, not only at the very end. (FACTORY LAW:
the ending is a generator over the element set plus the playthrough state, the
same emergent spirit as the seed-driven overmap and the fold.) A WAR is wanted as
a common thread through most combinations.

## LOCKED — THE DESTROYERS (a FORCE, not a faction) [name PENDING]
A new hostile group, distinct from every faction:
- NOT joinable. They are NOT a faction, because a faction implies you could join
  or ally with them. They are a hazard / a force, closer to weather or wildlife.
- Purely destructive. They ruin for the sake of ruin. No plan to rebuild anything,
  no territory to govern, no ideology to sell.
- UNIVERSALLY HATED, even by the worst factions. The Cartel at least needs some
  civilization standing to restart the drug trade; the Destroyers need nothing at
  all, so even the Cartel opposes them. Every faction in the game hates them.
- MECHANICAL ROLE: the common enemy. The one threat that can make rival factions
  temporarily cooperate. A natural pressure for the Act 1 war.
- NOT "the Raiders" (too lazy / generic). Vibe reference: "Pillager"-ish, plain
  evil.
- NAMING [PENDING Paolo]: leaning either NAMELESS BY DESIGN (too chaotic to have
  named themselves; survivors and factions each have their own word for them, none
  official, which reinforces that they are chaos not an organization) OR a single
  folk-handle. Claude candidates for a handle: "the Locusts" (a swarm that strips
  everything and moves on, hated even by other predators), the Wreckers, the
  Scourge, the Ferals. Paolo's call.
